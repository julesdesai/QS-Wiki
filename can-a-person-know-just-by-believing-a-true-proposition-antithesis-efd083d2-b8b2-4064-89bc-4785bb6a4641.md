# Can a person know just by believing a true proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** efd083d2-b8b2-4064-89bc-4785bb6a4641
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-d2c0abd4-6924-4f8e-8c3f-b341856f4998.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification in addition to true belief. This principle together with the claim that a person knows a proposition if they believe it and it is true entails that justification is not necessary for knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone believes a true proposition based on a lucky guess. Intuitively, we would judge that they do not have knowledge, because their belief lacks justification.**
