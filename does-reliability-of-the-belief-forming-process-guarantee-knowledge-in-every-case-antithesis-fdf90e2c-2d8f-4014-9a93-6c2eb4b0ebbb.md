# Does reliability of the belief-forming process guarantee knowledge in every case?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fdf90e2c-2d8f-4014-9a93-6c2eb4b0ebbb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief about the shape of an object by using a new kind of visual app that, unknown to them, adds deceptive features to shapes.**, **The process might be reliable in other situations, but in the case where the app deceives, it seems wrong to say that the person has knowledge about the shape.**, **It is implausible to assert that a usually reliable process ensures knowledge without considering specific instances where the process circumstantially fails.**
