**Parent:** [Is there a difference between being justified in believing a proposition and possessing knowledge of that proposition?](is-there-a-difference-between-being-justified-in-believing-a-proposition-and-possessing-knowledge-of-that-proposition-antithesis-2564cc33-b1a2-453a-9462-9f9b3c6dc5f6.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is formed through the exercise of intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed through intellectual virtue**
