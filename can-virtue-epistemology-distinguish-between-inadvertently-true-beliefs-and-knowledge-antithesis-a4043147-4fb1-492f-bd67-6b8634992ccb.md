# Can virtue epistemology distinguish between inadvertently true beliefs and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4043147-4fb1-492f-bd67-6b8634992ccb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**A character trait that leads to truth in complex cases may also lead to true beliefs by accident, raising doubts about whether these beliefs count as knowledge.**, **Suppose a person believes a true proposition through virtuous means, but the actual truth of the belief involves factors unknown to them. This makes the belief true by coincidence rather than justified knowledge.**, **Knowledge implies more than a true belief and a reliable process; it also requires some awareness or understanding of why the belief is true.**
