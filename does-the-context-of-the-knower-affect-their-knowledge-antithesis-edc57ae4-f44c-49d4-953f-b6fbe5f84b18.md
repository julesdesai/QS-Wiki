# Does the context of the knower affect their knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** edc57ae4-f44c-49d4-953f-b6fbe5f84b18
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a meteorologist and a layperson both claim it will rain tomorrow. Both have access to the same weather data, but the meteorologist's expertise provides a higher degree of justification. Intuitively, we judge that the meteorologist's belief is more likely considered knowledge than the layperson's.**, **There is a plausible principle that the expertise of an individual affects the strength of their justification. This principle suggests that knowledge could depend on the context of the knower.**
