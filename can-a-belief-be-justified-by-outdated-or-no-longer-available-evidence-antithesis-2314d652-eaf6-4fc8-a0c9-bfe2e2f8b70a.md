# Can a belief be justified by outdated or no longer available evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2314d652-eaf6-4fc8-a0c9-bfe2e2f8b70a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism with Objective Evidence](evidentialism-with-objective-evidence-synthesis-54d609bf-9930-4ddb-a0d6-5d54abfff05d.md)

## Content

**A belief may initially be justified by evidence, but if the evidence becomes unavailable or out of date, it is implausible to consider it justified forever after.**, **Consider a belief in a scientific theory that was justified by past experiments but is contradicted by newer experiments, intuitively it no longer seems justified.**
