# Can someone know a proposition based only on belief if they lack understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4f9767f-d717-4b94-81c5-7d440dc4e538
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-8d92b68a-d96e-49d3-9b29-39a25430afac.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a complex mathematical proposition but does not understand the proof or underlying concepts, even though the proposition is true. Intuitively, we would not say that the person knows the proposition.**, **Knowledge requires understanding of the concepts underlying a belief; mere belief does not constitute knowledge without comprehension.**
