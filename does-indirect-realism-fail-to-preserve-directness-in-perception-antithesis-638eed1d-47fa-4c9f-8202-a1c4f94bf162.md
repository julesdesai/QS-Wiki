# Does indirect realism fail to preserve directness in perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 638eed1d-47fa-4c9f-8202-a1c4f94bf162
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Maintained Through Inference](indirect-realism-maintained-through-inference-synthesis-ffd82d7b-be0f-4614-a603-c896b6b1b7df.md)

## Content

**Perceptual experiences, under indirect realism, are representations rather than direct apprehensions of external objects.**, **Knowledge of the external world requires going beyond immediate experience to make inferences about things as they exist independently.**, **The necessity of inference implies a mediating step that weakens the directness and immediacy of knowing external objects.**
