# Is accessibility of justification essential for preventing epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** acd83c80-c8d1-4f7d-8fc7-460562c94202
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Externalism Summary](hybrid-externalism-summary-synthesis-2250092f-2630-4da2-af9c-09ed6c4e9cfe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a true proposition due to a reliable method they cannot discern and coincidentally reasons that have no bearing on the truth. Intuitively, we would judge this is a case of epistemic luck and not knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that epistemic luck ought to be minimized for knowledge. This principle together with the current understanding of knowledge challenges cases where reliability alone suffices.**
