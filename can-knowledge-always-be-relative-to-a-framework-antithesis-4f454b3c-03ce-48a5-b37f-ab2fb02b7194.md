# Can knowledge always be relative to a framework?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f454b3c-03ce-48a5-b37f-ab2fb02b7194
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Relativism with Justification Relative to Frameworks](epistemic-relativism-with-justification-relative-to-frameworks-synthesis-ad42e55b-b997-4355-ba09-4958e4df6c61.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which Alice claims to know a proposition based on her cultural framework, which asserts that only direct divine revelation constitutes true knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that Alice's knowledge claim lacks universal credibility.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge claims should be evaluated based on shared rational standards. This principle, together with the claim that knowledge is relative only to one's own framework, entails a conflict with universal reason.**
