# Can invariantist contextualism account for shifts in conversational contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 11f9bac2-c863-4cc9-b622-d3232db3707a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Contextualism](invariantist-contextualism-synthesis-72e0f6e6-e984-47a7-9872-225586edec54.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people are discussing whether someone knows a proposition in a relaxed context and then shift to a rigorous academic setting, intuitively, it seems the standards for knowledge attribution change.**, **Invariantist contextualism maintains that the standards for knowing a proposition are fixed and do not vary with different contexts.**, **This view appears insufficiently flexible to account for the intuitive shifts in knowledge standards that accompany changes in conversational context.**
