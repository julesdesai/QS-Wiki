# foundherentism as a balance between coherence and foundationalism

**Parent:** [Does coherentism account for empirical evidence adequately in constituting knowledge?](does-coherentism-account-for-empirical-evidence-adequately-in-constituting-knowledge-antithesis-a4f32718-3beb-4a8b-bec0-b42381b38562.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief coheres with a system of interrelated beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is supported by empirical evidence**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief coheres with a system of interrelated beliefs, and the proposition is supported by empirical evidence**
