# Does varying justification conflict with the requirement for belief to align with truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6025f020-df3e-4279-8b6b-a65bc7a1e030
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism highlighting context-dependent justification](contextualism-highlighting-context-dependent-justification-synthesis-c9f62fb8-5b79-43c8-99f6-6461f5a6c05a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a belief is to be considered knowledge, it must reliably align with truth. If the justification standards vary too widely, it risks allowing beliefs that are not consistently reliable to be classified as knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where, in varying contexts, a belief meets weak justification standards and is true but does not meet stronger standards when closely examined. Intuitively, it seems problematic to equate weakly justified beliefs with knowledge.**
