# How do social contexts influence knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 08e388b3-99b8-4deb-bf83-3c85fbe7878e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Consider an employee believing management will announce layoffs based on insider conversations. Within this social context, they have compelling reasons to trust this belief. Outside this circle, however, these reasons hold less weight. Intuitively, social contexts provide substantive justification for beliefs that might count as knowledge.**, **Social context appears to shape evidential strength, implying that knowledge standards might not be universally invariant.**
