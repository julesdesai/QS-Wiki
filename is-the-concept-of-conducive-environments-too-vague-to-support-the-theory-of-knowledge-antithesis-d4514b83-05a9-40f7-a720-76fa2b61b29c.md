# Is the concept of conducive environments too vague to support the theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d4514b83-05a9-40f7-a720-76fa2b61b29c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the concept of an environment conducive to truth should be precisely defined. This principle together with the vague nature of what constitutes such an environment entails difficulties in determining when proper function applies.**, **Consider a situation where environmental conduciveness is interpreted differently by various individuals, leading to confusion about knowledge claims. In such situations, intuitively we may have differing judgments about whether there is knowledge.**
