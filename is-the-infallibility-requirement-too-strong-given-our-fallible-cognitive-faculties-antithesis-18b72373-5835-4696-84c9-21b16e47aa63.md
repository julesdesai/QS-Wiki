# Is the infallibility requirement too strong given our fallible cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18b72373-5835-4696-84c9-21b16e47aa63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infallibility](knowledge-as-infallibility-synthesis-8f0bcd35-bb4c-4192-816d-76a63a41903c.md)

## Content

**Humans often form beliefs based on evidence that is not beyond all conceivable doubt.**, **It is implausible to demand infallibility for a belief to count as knowledge when human cognitive faculties are inherently fallible.**, **If infallibility were required, almost no human beliefs could qualify as knowledge, which seems counterintuitive.**
