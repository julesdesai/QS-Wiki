# Can a belief be rational but not count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7329fe57-3adc-4c68-b350-361641760cc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Bayesian Epistemology](bayesian-epistemology-synthesis-4576807d-29a3-407d-a965-1c884989f7fe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person updates their belief based on available evidence using rational Bayesian reasoning, but the evidence is incomplete or distorted. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is rational yet not knowledge or that incomplete evidence can suffice for knowledge. If we say the belief is rational yet not knowledge, then it highlights a gap between rational beliefs and knowledge.**, **It is implausible that mere rational belief adjustment always results in knowledge without accounting for the reliability and completeness of the evidence.**
