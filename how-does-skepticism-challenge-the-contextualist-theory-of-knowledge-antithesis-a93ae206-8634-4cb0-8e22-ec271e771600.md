# How does skepticism challenge the Contextualist Theory of Knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a93ae206-8634-4cb0-8e22-ec271e771600
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c8c66eb7-99be-427a-b717-b1ecb74e1290.md)

## Content

**In a skeptical context, the standards for claiming knowledge are extremely high, requiring absolute certainty.**, **In such contexts, intuitively, we cannot claim to know anything about the external world.**, **If standards for knowledge claims vary by context, then in skeptical contexts, no one can truly know anything, undermining the utility of knowledge claims altogether.**
