# Are beliefs sufficient for knowledge in varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d673f28-f3ae-483c-92cf-ec6d5c9a58ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where personal biases strongly influence one's belief in a proposition due to context. Intuitively, belief formed this way does not justify knowledge**, **If context allows for varying beliefs without strong justification, it questions whether beliefs alone are reliable indicators of knowledge, especially when biases are contextually derived**
