# Is knowing always practically useful?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b799ae16-591b-4d0f-9956-caea3503989b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-af5a1410-77c2-4ceb-b390-2ced8876b488.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows an obscure historical fact, such as the date of an ancient battle, that has no practical use in their life today. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person still possesses knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowing something is always useful to a person's practical aims, as some knowledge is purely theoretical and detached from practical considerations.**
