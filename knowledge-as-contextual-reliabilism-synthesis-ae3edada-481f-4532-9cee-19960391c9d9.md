**Parent:** [Does the notion of reliability account for all types of knowledge?](does-the-notion-of-reliability-account-for-all-types-of-knowledge-antithesis-85211d41-4f8b-4015-99da-198124b2300c.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a cognitive process that is reliable in the specific context**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief is produced by a contextually reliable cognitive process**
