# Can contextualism lead to epistemic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76e5f901-b3bb-41d1-b2f7-6edb9dcc4437
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Knowledge](contextualism-about-knowledge-synthesis-b64cba08-2a7b-44b8-b81d-d7241d8be6e0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person P claims to know a proposition because they are in a low-standard context, while person Q, in a high-standard context, denies that knowledge claim. Intuitively, we would judge that whether a knowledge claim is correct should not merely depend on changing context standards between P and Q, suggesting a risk of relativism.**
