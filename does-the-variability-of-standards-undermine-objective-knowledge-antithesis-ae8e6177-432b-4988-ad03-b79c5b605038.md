# Does the variability of standards undermine objective knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae8e6177-432b-4988-ad03-b79c5b605038
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Justificationism](contextual-justificationism-synthesis-1b60e9a8-d801-480b-8adb-e726a7af0440.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals in different contexts evaluate the same proposition; intuitively, they should both either know or not know it equally if evaluated objectively.**, **If the standards for knowing vary with context, then it leads to the paradox where the same belief could be knowledge for one person and not for another, undermining the objectivity of knowledge.**
