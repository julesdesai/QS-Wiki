# Does safety consider cognitive access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae81e116-dc27-465b-a02c-44576242a0b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-3968e05f-d744-409d-bfbb-59e5b6ab2a0b.md)

## Content

**Cognitive access refers to an individual's awareness or understanding of the reasons for their belief.**, **It is possible for a belief to be true and safe without the individual having cognitive access to why it is true.**, **If knowledge requires cognitive access to one's reasons, then safety alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
