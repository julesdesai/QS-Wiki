# Can intellectual exaggeration compromise knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f35a3d5-7c96-406d-9ee8-f6fb3a33b898
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person exaggerates their open-mindedness to entertain clearly false propositions. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the excess of intellectual virtue leads to confusion rather than knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that exaggerating intellectual virtues might lead to skepticism or relativism.**, **It is implausible that more virtue is always better; balance is necessary to avoid pitfalls in reasoning.**
