# Are high stakes necessary for determining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 061df236-7324-423c-8ea0-df9d7ce7cefb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Theory](pragmatic-encroachment-theory-synthesis-48f209b4-b71c-4b9b-814c-8c2a5af0c840.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person Q is deciding whether to invest in a stock based on insider information with high stakes involved. Intuitively, if the information is reliable, Q knows that the investment will succeed.**, **Consider a similar case where a person R is simply discussing the stock as a theoretical exercise with no investment plans. Intuitively, if R has access to the same reliable information, R also knows the investment will succeed.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliable information alone, regardless of stakes, is sufficient to determine knowledge.**
