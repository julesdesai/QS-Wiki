# Does coherentism account for perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b93fdc4e-e457-40d2-b006-2124aa8db45d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-189c934d-40a0-41d9-b70b-1a7a0a9cc7c3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a coherent system of beliefs, but their beliefs about the external world are systematically based on faulty perceptual inputs. Intuitively, we either have to say that they have knowledge or they do not. If we say they have knowledge about the world, then it seems we are allowing false beliefs to count as knowledge. If we say they do not have knowledge about the world, then coherence is insufficient for knowledge.**
