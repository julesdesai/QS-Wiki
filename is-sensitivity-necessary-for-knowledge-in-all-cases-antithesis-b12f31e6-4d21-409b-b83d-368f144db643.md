# Is sensitivity necessary for knowledge in all cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b12f31e6-4d21-409b-b83d-368f144db643
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa7f9900-da54-4309-8d89-06019053aee3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person has a belief formed from a reliable source but lacks awareness of possible error. Intuitively, we might say the person has knowledge due to the reliability of the source, not the sensitivity of their belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can result from reliable testimony alone, without the need for sensitivity.**
