# Does Reliabilism Address the Problem of Epistemic Luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 92af39e6-2ece-4d3a-9e8d-08f0dc4e1999
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that true belief by luck does not constitute knowledge. This principle together with the observation that even reliable processes can result in true beliefs by luck entails that reliabilism might fail to eliminate epistemic luck.**, **Suppose a person guesses correctly due to a reliable process activated by chance circumstances. Intuitively, this should not count as knowledge despite meeting reliabilist criteria.**
