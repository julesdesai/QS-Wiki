# Do intellectual virtues always lead to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d6c9ba5-a0d0-461c-9130-ab5e1d0ad387
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person exercises intellectual virtues like thorough examination and critical thinking but reaches an incorrect conclusion due to incomplete evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that such virtues do not infallibly lead to truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that while virtues aim to guide toward truth, imperfect information can still lead to false conclusions.**, **It is implausible that virtuous reasoning is sufficient to guarantee the truth of all beliefs.**
