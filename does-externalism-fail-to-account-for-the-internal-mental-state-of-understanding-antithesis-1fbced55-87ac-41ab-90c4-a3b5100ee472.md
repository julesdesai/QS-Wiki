# Does externalism fail to account for the internal mental state of understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fbced55-87ac-41ab-90c4-a3b5100ee472
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that understanding a concept internally is necessary for true knowledge. This principle suggests that without internal comprehension, mere external justification cannot amount to knowledge.**, **Suppose a person memorizes a mathematical formula and can apply it correctly due to constant reminders from a third party, but they do not understand its derivation or application. Intuitively, they do not truly know the formula beyond rote usage.**
