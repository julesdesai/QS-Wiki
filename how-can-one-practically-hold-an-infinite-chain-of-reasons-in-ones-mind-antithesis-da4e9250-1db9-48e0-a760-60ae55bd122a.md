# How can one practically hold an infinite chain of reasons in one's mind?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da4e9250-1db9-48e0-a760-60ae55bd122a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Conceptual Infinite Regress](infinitism-with-conceptual-infinite-regress-synthesis-14e25e58-6ba7-452c-ac18-aba278058c50.md)

## Content

**A justified belief must be based on reasons that a person can theoretically articulate.**, **In philosophy, a requirement exists that justification should be based on reasons that can be articulated in finite terms.**, **If justification involves an infinite regress that is conceptually infinite, it seems implausible that anyone could ever practically articulate such a chain of reasons.**
