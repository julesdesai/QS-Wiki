# Are some contexts epistemically inappropriate for knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8823c763-01f9-4f41-8b27-c898e1218374
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims to know a complex scientific fact in a casual conversation. The relaxed context makes it hard to ensure proper understanding and justification, raising doubts about the knowledge claim.**, **Intuitively, some conversational contexts lack the rigor needed for genuine knowledge claims, casting doubt on whether knowledge can be assessed in all contexts.**
