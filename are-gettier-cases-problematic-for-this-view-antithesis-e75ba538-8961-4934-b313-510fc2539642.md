# Are Gettier cases problematic for this view?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e75ba538-8961-4934-b313-510fc2539642
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**There is a case in which Smith has a justified belief that the person who will get the job has ten coins in their pocket, because he saw Jones' coins and has good reason to believe Jones will get the job. However, Smith gets the job, and Smith also has ten coins in his pocket, unbeknownst to him. In this scenario, Smith does not know the proposition despite having justified true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be based merely on coincidence or luck, whereas the view only requires justified true belief which allows for such cases.**
