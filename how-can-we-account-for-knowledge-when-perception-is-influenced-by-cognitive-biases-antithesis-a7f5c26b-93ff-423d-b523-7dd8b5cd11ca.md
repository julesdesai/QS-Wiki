# How can we account for knowledge when perception is influenced by cognitive biases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7f5c26b-93ff-423d-b523-7dd8b5cd11ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Direct Realism](contextual-direct-realism-synthesis-9053ea14-9a99-4649-aa95-1bc3b223c980.md)

## Content

**Cognitive biases can distort perception in ways that are not always detectable by the perceiver.**, **If knowledge requires direct perception, individuals with cognitive biases may claim to know propositions that are erroneously perceived.**, **It is implausible that someone can claim knowledge when their perception is substantially skewed by cognitive biases.**
