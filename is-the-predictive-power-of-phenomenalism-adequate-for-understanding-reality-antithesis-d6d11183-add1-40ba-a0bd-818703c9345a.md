# Is the predictive power of phenomenalism adequate for understanding reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d6d11183-add1-40ba-a0bd-818703c9345a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a well-established scientific theory predicts phenomena that cannot be directly perceived by the senses, like black holes. Intuitively, we either have to say that these predictions are about real aspects of the universe or that they are merely constructions of sensory experiences. If we say they are real, this contradicts phenomenalism's view that reality is constructed from sensory experiences. If we say they are just constructions, then such theories lose their explanatory power.**
