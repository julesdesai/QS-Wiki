# Is disjunctivism too permissive of deception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 888a1d3e-5734-443b-85f3-f9bc19ea6ba2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a researcher observes an optical illusion and forms a belief about it being a straight line that appears curved. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief about the curve is based on deception even though it is false.**, **If perceptual experiences justify beliefs only when veridically connected, then the belief about the illusion should not be justified.**, **It seems implausible that people often form beliefs based on deception without justification, questioning the exclusiveness of veridical justification.**
