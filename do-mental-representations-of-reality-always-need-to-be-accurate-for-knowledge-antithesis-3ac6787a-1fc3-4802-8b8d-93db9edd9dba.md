# Do mental representations of reality always need to be accurate for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ac6787a-1fc3-4802-8b8d-93db9edd9dba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Representationalism](epistemic-representationalism-synthesis-c86e8f4e-5440-4a21-8e04-4337bf022fd8.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that mental representations must be perfectly accurate for knowledge to occur.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person slightly misrepresents a detail but still grasps the core truth of a situation. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person has knowledge despite the minor inaccuracy.**
