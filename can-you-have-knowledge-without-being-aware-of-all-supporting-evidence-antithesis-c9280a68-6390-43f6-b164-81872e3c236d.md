# Can you have knowledge without being aware of all supporting evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9280a68-6390-43f6-b164-81872e3c236d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person accurately predicts weather without understanding meteorological data but based on intuition corroborated by experts. Intuitively, we would judge that this person can have knowledge even without personal awareness of all the supporting evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge does not require personal awareness of all evidence, but rather that the belief is supported by sufficient evidence, perhaps through reliance on an expert.**
