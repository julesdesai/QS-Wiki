# Generalized Network Reliabilism

**Parent:** [Can a reliable process that does not apply to all cases still produce knowledge?](can-a-reliable-process-that-does-not-apply-to-all-cases-still-produce-knowledge-antithesis-9a0a6df1-1539-4c9b-b9e2-4e2d7fd02b38.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed by a network of cognitive processes that is generally reliable across situations**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed by a network of cognitive processes that is generally reliable across situations**
