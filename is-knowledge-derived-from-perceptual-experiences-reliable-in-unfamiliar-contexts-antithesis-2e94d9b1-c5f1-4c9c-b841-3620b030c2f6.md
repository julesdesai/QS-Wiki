# Is knowledge derived from perceptual experiences reliable in unfamiliar contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e94d9b1-c5f1-4c9c-b841-3620b030c2f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual travels to a completely foreign environment, encountering objects and phenomena they have never experienced before. Intuitively, the person's pre-existing knowledge seems insufficient to make accurate predictions in this new context.**, **There is a plausible principle that new or unfamiliar contexts require new forms of understanding not wholly based on previous perceptual experiences. This principle suggests that perceptual experiences are not universally reliable for acquiring knowledge in all contexts.**
