# Can internal cognitive states fully determine justification for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 863d8ef4-373a-4ddd-91cf-828d1c270cf3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for a person to have knowledge, their own internal cognitive states should be sufficient to justify their belief.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has the same internal mental states in two different circumstances, one where external factors are unreliable and one where they are reliable. Intuitively, it seems the person should have the same level of justification for their belief in both scenarios.**
