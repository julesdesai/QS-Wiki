# Evidentialist Reliabilism with Counterfactual Dependence

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 6c778e35-990d-4b26-85bc-052efea82381
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can knowledge be accidental but reliable?](can-knowledge-be-accidental-but-reliable-antithesis-06c5d028-4383-4273-a116-b0f5e58513ef.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a reliable process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their believed proposition be counterfactually dependent on reliable indicators of truth**, **Counterfactual dependence means that if circumstances were different, the belief would change accordingly**
