# Can false beliefs sometimes result in what is considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20e570fe-77d4-4229-88a5-1ace9f765b8d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1a215329-c53d-4dbe-9509-6b517d1bb8ad.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person believes a false proposition based on reliable evidence, yet this belief is mistaken only due to an unusual set of circumstances. Intuitively, we might still say the person had knowledge because they followed reliable methods.**, **It is implausible that the truth-tracking condition alone rightly disregards cases involving genuinely reliable belief formation processes.**
