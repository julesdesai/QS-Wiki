# Can external factors undermine the claim that knowledge arises from cognitive skill?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c41b08c-6805-465a-98da-9d5e44cae182
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-dbddd6f3-a688-44af-bb01-dd29b6f7b5c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately predicts the weather through a feeling or hunch rather than meteorological skill. Intuitively, the person does not have knowledge as the belief lacks grounding in cognitive skill.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification beyond mere external coherence with truth. The principle implies that true belief alone is not sufficient for knowledge without supporting cognitive skill.**
