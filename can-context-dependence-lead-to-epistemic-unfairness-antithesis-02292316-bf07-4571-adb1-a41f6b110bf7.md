# Can context dependence lead to epistemic unfairness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02292316-bf07-4571-adb1-a41f6b110bf7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an expert and a non-expert are assessed for their knowledge claims about a scientific fact in different contexts. The non-expert's context demands lower standards of justification. Intuitively, one might find it unfair that the expert is held to higher standards despite potentially having more reliable knowledge.**, **Suppose an academic setting where students are held to different standards for knowing based on their years of study. It seems unjust if a first-year student's claim to know a theory is accepted more readily than the same claim made by a senior researcher, given equal evidence.**
