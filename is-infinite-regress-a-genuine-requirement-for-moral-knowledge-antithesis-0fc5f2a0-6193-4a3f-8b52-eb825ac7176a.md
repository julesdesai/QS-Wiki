# Is infinite regress a genuine requirement for moral knowledge?

**Node ID:** 0fc5f2a0-6193-4a3f-8b52-eb825ac7176a

## Content

**There is a reasonable idea that moral knowledge, like "it is wrong to lie," is understood and accepted without infinite justificatory chains.**, **In cases of moral reasoning, individuals often seem to know moral truths intuitively or after considering limited principles, suggesting infinite chains are not necessary.**
