# Can knowledge be based solely on perceptual experiences without considering external validation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f95a8dfd-6a04-46e4-871f-01af6d01545e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Phenomenalism Allowing Memory Role](revised-phenomenalism-allowing-memory-role-synthesis-5fb5abbe-6adb-4855-a476-25c8a91d11a9.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a match between perception and external reality. This principle together with the idea that memory shaped perceptions may not align perfectly with reality entails that knowledge relying on memory-influenced perceptual experiences is potentially flawed.**, **Suppose we have a scenario where a person remembers seeing a red apple, but the apple was actually a cleverly disguised fake. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have true knowledge of the apple being red based solely on their memory-influenced perceptual experience.**
