# How do we reconcile varying standards of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a9e3ed7-2ba9-41b4-bc02-6901e624ab25
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where two people in different contexts disagree about a proposition due to differing standards of justification. Intuitively, it seems problematic to say both have knowledge if one is contextually justified and the other is not**, **There is a plausible principle that the truth of a proposition should be consistent regardless of context. This principle together with varying standards for justification across contexts entails that knowledge might be context-dependent, potentially leading to contradictions**
