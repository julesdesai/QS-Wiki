# Should perceptual experiences always be tied to decision-making for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cbdea3fb-4971-4d73-a732-95d86dbf5161
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Consider an artist who perceives the beauty of a sunset but does not make any decisions based on this perception. Intuitively, the artist still seems to know what they have perceived without any practical reasoning.**, **It is implausible to assume that all perceptual experiences leading to knowledge need to be tied to decision-making processes. Some perceptual knowledge remains purely experiential.**
