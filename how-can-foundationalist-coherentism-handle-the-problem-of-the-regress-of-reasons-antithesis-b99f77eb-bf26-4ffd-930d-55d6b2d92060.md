# How can Foundationalist Coherentism handle the problem of the regress of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b99f77eb-bf26-4ffd-930d-55d6b2d92060
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**A belief that is justified by another belief requires that the other belief itself be justified.**, **If the justification of a belief depends on an infinite series of justifications, then the belief cannot be truly justified, as such infinite series are practically unattainable.**, **Requiring a stopping point in the justification process, namely basic beliefs, raises the issue of whether these basic beliefs can be truly self-justifying without reference to coherence with other beliefs.**
