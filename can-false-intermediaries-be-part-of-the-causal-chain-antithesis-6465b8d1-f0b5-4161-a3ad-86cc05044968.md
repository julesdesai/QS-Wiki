# Can false intermediaries be part of the causal chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6465b8d1-f0b5-4161-a3ad-86cc05044968
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a truth based on a report from a generally reliable source that contains some inaccuracies, which are also part of the causal chain. Intuitively, this undermines the knowledge.**, **A belief can have a causal connection to a fact even if part of the causal chain includes some incorrect or misleading information. Such presence of falsity should intuitively lead us to reject that it constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must not rely on false intermediaries. If falsehoods are integral to the causal chain, the resulting belief is not knowledge.**
