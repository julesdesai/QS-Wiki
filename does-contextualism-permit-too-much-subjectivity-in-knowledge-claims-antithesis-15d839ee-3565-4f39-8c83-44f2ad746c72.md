# Does contextualism permit too much subjectivity in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15d839ee-3565-4f39-8c83-44f2ad746c72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c8c66eb7-99be-427a-b717-b1ecb74e1290.md)

## Content

**Knowledge claims should be based on objective criteria to ensure reliability and verifiability.**, **Contextualism suggests that what one claims to know might depend heavily on subjective features of the conversational context, such as the speaker’s intentions or audience’s expectations.**, **The subjectivity introduced by varying standards across contexts can undermine the objectivity of knowledge claims.**
