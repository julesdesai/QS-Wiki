# Can coherence guarantee individuation of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9f8e34d-8ae1-47bb-a130-623592882204
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where experiences of two identical twins are perfectly coherent but there's no distinguishing feature. Intuitively, we would either have to say experiences cannot individuate or must accept a false individuation.**, **There is a plausible principle that individual objects must be distinctly identifiable. This principle together with coherence alone cannot guarantee individuation challenges the reliability of coherence for distinguishing objects.**
