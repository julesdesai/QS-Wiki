# Do context-dependent standards adequately capture true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e934b376-3cbc-4571-94c2-065d93f897ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes a proposition that is true and they meet standard everyday conversational norms for claiming to know. Intuitively, we would regard that belief as knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which practical decision-making needs require extraordinarily high standards for knowledge. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person does not know due to not meeting these high standards, or we discount these standards as irrelevant. If we say they do not know, then true belief may be foregone. If we discount these standards, then we undermine the ability of practical needs to significantly adjust knowledge standards.**
