# Can knowledge exist without reliable belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 97387185-faf2-458e-aed3-572edebe6085
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief by rolling dice, and the belief happens to be true. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite the belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must be reliably connected to the truth. This principle together with the scenario of dice-produced belief suggests that reliability cannot be the sole factor for knowledge.**
