# Is empirical testing always necessary for knowledge confirmation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e704630d-cdcb-4f43-9f81-07599a2fe951
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone has witnessed an event, such as a car accident, and immediately knows a fact about it, poses a challenge since empirical testing may not always occur immediately post-experience but the knowledge is claimed.**, **There is a plausible principle that immediate perceptual awareness might lead to knowledge that precedes empirical testing, suggesting empirical validation is not always parallel to perceptual knowledge acquisition.**
