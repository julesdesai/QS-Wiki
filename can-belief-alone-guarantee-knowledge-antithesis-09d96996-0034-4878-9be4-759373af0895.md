# Can belief alone guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 09d96996-0034-4878-9be4-759373af0895
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition based on faulty reasoning, but the proposition is true. Intuitively, we would hesitate to say the person knows the proposition despite their belief.**, **There is a common principle in epistemology that knowing requires justified belief, which belief alone does not satisfy.**, **It is implausible that a person can be said to have knowledge if their belief lacks justification, regardless of the truth of the proposition.**
