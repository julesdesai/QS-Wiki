# Is there a conflict with epistemic conservatism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad1d0bed-f3b5-49ab-b55f-4e40347449d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3749df9e-e568-414c-88ec-cc6b23580530.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves some degree of epistemic conservatism, meaning that beliefs should not change frequently without good reason. This principle, together with an understanding that truth-tracking might demand frequent belief revision, suggests tension.**, **It is implausible that a person should constantly revise their beliefs according to every new piece of evidence, yet the tracking requirement might imply this necessity.**
