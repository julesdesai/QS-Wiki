# Is practical success truly a component of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8141342b-f9aa-403f-84a1-b2bca25feff1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a true proposition, based on strong evidence, that does not lead to any practical success because it is about a distant galaxy. Intuitively, such a belief still constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can exist independently of any practical outcome. This principle suggests that practical success is not a necessary component of knowledge.**
