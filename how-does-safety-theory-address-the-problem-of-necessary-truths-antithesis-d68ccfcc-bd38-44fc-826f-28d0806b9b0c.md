# How does Safety Theory address the problem of necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d68ccfcc-bd38-44fc-826f-28d0806b9b0c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-2fb86082-7528-4d1d-a3e0-8c751a94ae5c.md)

## Content

**A proposition that is necessarily true, such as a mathematical fact, holds true in all possible worlds regardless of the believer's epistemic state**, **If safety depends on truth across nearby possible worlds, then necessarily true beliefs would always be safe, making the theory trivially applicable and failing to account for the believer's epistemic responsibility**
