# Does the theory adequately differentiate between true accidental beliefs and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ae8c564-f1f9-48c7-a25c-2580bf5b3f14
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person believes a specific stock will rise due to insider knowledge, but the stock rises due to an unrelated market event. Intuitively, the rise of the stock is accidental relative to the reason for the person's belief. A plausible principle is that accidental truths, despite being true, do not equate to knowledge. This principle suggests that merely meeting the safety condition does not ensure knowledge due to external factors.**
