# Does justified true belief account for all forms of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d2f64af-0e63-4754-871f-97a0e87be502
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-f73c0505-1eaa-4768-8143-11a2f8c9ca92.md)

## Content

**There are intuitive cases where knowledge is ascribed without clear evidence of justification and truth.**, **For example, a chess master's intuitive knowledge of good moves doesn't always involve conscious justification or true belief.**, **In practical terms, knowledge can manifest as an unconscious competence that bypasses the justified true belief structure.**, **Not all supposed knowledge fits neatly into the mold of justified true belief without excluding intuitive or experiential knowledge.**
