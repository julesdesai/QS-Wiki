# What if a belief is practically detrimental but true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 01b01e7b-5aad-4948-9295-6251cbb82792
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person has a true belief that is practically detrimental, such as predicting a natural disaster without being able to act on it. Intuitively, this person knows the proposition despite a lack of practical benefit.**, **A plausible principle states that truth can constitute knowledge even without practical usefulness. This principle together with scenarios of detrimental truth challenges the pragmatist notion.**
