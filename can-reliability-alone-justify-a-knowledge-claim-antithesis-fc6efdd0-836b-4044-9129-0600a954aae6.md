# Can reliability alone justify a knowledge claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fc6efdd0-836b-4044-9129-0600a954aae6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism to knowledge with reliable cognitive process](externalism-to-knowledge-with-reliable-cognitive-process-synthesis-e78416a8-ed0b-42d6-b63b-0cc8fec7b97b.md)

## Content

**A belief that is reliably produced but dependent on random factors might not be perceived as knowledge.**, **For instance, accurately predicting the weather daily with no scientific basis but consistently correct results is questionable knowledge.**, **This challenges the notion that reliability, without more, suffices for knowledge.**
