# Can justification ever fully preclude false lemmas?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fa14ee1-00cb-4f26-9289-d4d99a1a5ab3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Justification Theory](epistemic-justification-theory-synthesis-d0523946-79ba-4279-979e-9ba317ba7953.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be knowledge, no false lemma should support the belief.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief based on multiple premises, one of which is false, but the overall conclusion is true. In such a scenario, intuitively, we judge that the person does not have knowledge.**
