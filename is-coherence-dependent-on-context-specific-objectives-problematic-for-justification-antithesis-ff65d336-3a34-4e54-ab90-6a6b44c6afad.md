# Is coherence dependent on context-specific objectives problematic for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff65d336-3a34-4e54-ab90-6a6b44c6afad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherentism](pragmatic-coherentism-synthesis-37d6c6a9-68e2-4800-8f97-9338457a8af1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that coherence should be evaluated in a stable, objective manner rather than based on varying individual objectives. This principle, together with the idea that objectives can change frequently, suggests that justification can become erratic if based solely on personal goals.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an individual changes their objectives frequently, leading to fluctuating beliefs. Intuitively, we either have to say that their beliefs are justified each time they change, or that they lack stable justification. If we say they are justified, this leads to the issue of erratic justification; if they are not justified, then coherence with objectives seems insufficient.**
