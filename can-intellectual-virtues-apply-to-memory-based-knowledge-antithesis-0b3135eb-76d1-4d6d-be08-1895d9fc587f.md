# Can intellectual virtues apply to memory-based knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0b3135eb-76d1-4d6d-be08-1895d9fc587f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person recalls a true belief from memory but cannot remember how they originally acquired the belief. Intuitively, we wonder if they have knowledge despite the disconnect from the exercise of intellectual virtues at the time of recall.**, **A memory-based belief that cannot be traced back to active intellectual engagement challenges the notion that intellectual virtues are necessary for sustaining knowledge.**
