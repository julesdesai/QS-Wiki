# How can intellectual virtue avoid issues of subjective bias in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5c4a71a8-c5db-433b-a8b0-4bf2f2514277
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual virtue alone can account for justification without addressing inherent biases in the agent's perspective.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an agent uses their intellectual openness to form a belief, but their openness is influenced by a cultural bias affecting how they process evidence. Intuitively, we should question if this really yields justification.**
