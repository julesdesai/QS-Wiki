# Does the dynamic nature of knowledge conflict with an infinite chain of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab070729-1b0d-4660-9a5d-b77f47ae80e5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Consider the idea of knowledge as a dynamic and expanding structure. If every belief is supported by an infinite chain, intuitively, it seems that knowledge is static since each addition does not alter the infinite nature of previous beliefs.**, **It is plausible to believe that for knowledge to be truly dynamic, new information must build upon and alter existing justified beliefs. This belief, together with the static nature of an infinite chain, implies that an infinite regress does not support the dynamic conception of knowledge.**
