# Are context-sensitive standards applied consistently across various knowledge domains?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Suppose that standards of knowledge vary significantly between scientific and everyday contexts. Intuitively, the lack of consistency across domains can lead to skepticism about the reliability of knowledge itself.**, **It is plausible to argue that for a concept like knowledge to be robust, its assessment should be reliably consistent across different contexts. The variability of standards between contexts challenges this idea.**
