# Can knowledge of external objects be solely inferred from indirect experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d7fe1b64-14c9-44d6-830e-b003edaf117f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that inferences made solely from indirect experiences without any direct validation ensure knowledge of reality, because they may lead to incorrect conclusions.**, **Consider a scenario where a person only hears about the outside world through someone else's account while never leaving a room themselves. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks firsthand knowledge of the outside world.**
