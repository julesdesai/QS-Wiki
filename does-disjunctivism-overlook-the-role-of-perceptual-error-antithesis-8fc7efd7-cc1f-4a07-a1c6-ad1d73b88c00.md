# Does disjunctivism overlook the role of perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8fc7efd7-cc1f-4a07-a1c6-ad1d73b88c00
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-72a215c5-4d26-4e6e-86ab-383aabdf6290.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person sees a stick partially submerged in water and it appears bent. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person perceives the stick as it is or perceive an error. If we say they perceive reality, then perceptual error seems impossible. If we say they perceive error, then disjunctivism's notion of direct access to reality is challenged.**
