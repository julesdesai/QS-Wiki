# Is the causal theory flexible enough for mathematical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e8a3126-f161-4625-82ca-c819ec82bcef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b2b10034-482f-418e-b256-f8ad46aa44f3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider a case where Lisa knows that two plus two equals four. Intuitively, we would say that Lisa knows this mathematical truth, even though there is no empirical causal connection between the belief and the truth of the mathematical proposition as it relies on abstract reasoning.**
