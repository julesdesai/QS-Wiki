# Do differing modes of perception challenge phenomenal consistency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 934df98b-3149-48c3-b86c-7f540b5067ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Realism with Consistency Conditions](phenomenal-realism-with-consistency-conditions-synthesis-05f9226f-1221-4391-b2ae-790270c7a451.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person perceives the same phenomenon through different modes, such as visually and audibly, and notices inconsistency between these perceptions. Intuitively, the person may still know the underlying phenomenon despite the inconsistency in phenomenal experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves an integration of different perceptual inputs, rather than consistency within a single mode of perception. This principle together with the existence of multiple perception modes entails that inconsistencies between these modes do not preclude knowledge.**
