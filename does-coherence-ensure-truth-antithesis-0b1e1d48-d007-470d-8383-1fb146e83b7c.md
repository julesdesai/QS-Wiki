# Does coherence ensure truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0b1e1d48-d007-470d-8383-1fb146e83b7c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds a fully coherent set of beliefs that are entirely false, like a detailed but erroneous understanding of a historical event based on misleading sources. Intuitively, coherence alone does not guarantee that these beliefs are true.**, **It is implausible that coherence serves as an adequate criterion for truth since many coherent sets of beliefs can still be false.**
