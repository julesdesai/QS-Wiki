# Knowledge as Reliably Produced Beliefs with Externalism and Biological Function

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Do animals know without reliable processes?](do-animals-know-without-reliable-processes-antithesis-bdb93875-eb54-40db-8a1d-e89c8e02e250.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a creature to know a proposition, it suffices if the cognitive processes fulfill their biological function through environmental interaction to produce mostly true beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**
