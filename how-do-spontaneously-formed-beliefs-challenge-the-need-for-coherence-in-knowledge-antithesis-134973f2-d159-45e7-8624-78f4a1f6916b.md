# How do spontaneously formed beliefs challenge the need for coherence in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 134973f2-d159-45e7-8624-78f4a1f6916b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Partial Correspondence Coherentism Summary](partial-correspondence-coherentism-summary-synthesis-73282c9a-d730-43f4-a933-7c2ffa7c9bab.md)

## Content

**Consider the formation of a spontaneous true belief about an unknown fact, such as a lottery win, lacking coherence with prior beliefs.**, **This belief might correspond to actual states of affairs without coherence within the entire belief system.**, **If such spontaneously formed beliefs can constitute knowledge, coherence may not be necessary.**
