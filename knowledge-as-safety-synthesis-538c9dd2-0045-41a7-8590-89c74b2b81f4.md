# Knowledge as Safety

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 538c9dd2-0045-41a7-8590-89c74b2b81f4
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does truth alone adequately distinguish justified belief from knowledge in misleading environments?](does-truth-alone-adequately-distinguish-justified-belief-from-knowledge-in-misleading-environments-antithesis-ceae63fd-e009-4272-9302-9d3ac2eba879.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is safe in that in nearby possible worlds where the person believes the proposition, it is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is safe**
