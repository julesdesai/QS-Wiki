# Do people always have access to their total available evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 195ef6d5-b97f-4487-abee-516cca5de12f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is certain they left their keys on the kitchen table, having seen them there earlier, yet their spouse moved them without their knowledge. Intuitively, the person's belief that the keys are still there is justified from their perspective.**, **There is a principle that suggests justification requires access to one's total available evidence; however, individuals might not be aware of all relevant evidence, challenging the notion that belief alignment with available evidence is sufficient.**
