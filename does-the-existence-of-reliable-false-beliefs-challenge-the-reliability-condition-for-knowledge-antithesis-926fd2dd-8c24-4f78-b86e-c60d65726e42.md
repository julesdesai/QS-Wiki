# Does the existence of reliable false beliefs challenge the reliability condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 926fd2dd-8c24-4f78-b86e-c60d65726e42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a reliable process for forming beliefs but, due to unusual circumstances, forms a false belief. Intuitively, this suggests reliability does not unequivocally result in true beliefs.**, **A reliable belief-forming process must consistently lead to truth, yet in this scenario, it does not, suggesting that reliability might not guarantee knowledge.**
