# Do we require an infinite regress of justification to rule out all relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b47ce50-3b65-4978-b01e-6ed25a05ab5a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Suppose person U holds a justified true belief because they have verified every detail with multiple sources. Intuitively, requiring further justification for each verification leads to an infinite regress**, **There is a logical fact that an infinite regress of justification is impossible, which suggests not all alternatives need to be ruled out.**
