# Can the idea that truth conditions vary with context lead to contradictory knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fba5e1c3-1b9c-414a-9ab8-dd7912c7624a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a case where a person discusses two distinct contexts—one with high standards for knowledge and one with low standards—and claims they both know and do not know the same proposition within those contexts. Intuitively, such a case results in a contradiction in the possession of knowledge.**, **The principle that knowledge truth conditions vary with context suggests that differing contexts can result in conflicting truth claims about the same proposition. This threatens consistency in the theoretical understanding of truth conditions.**
