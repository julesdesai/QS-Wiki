# Are practical consequences reliable indicators of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37a1c211-92f7-4225-bc3d-ec8bbbe39846
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a case where practical consequences align with a belief, but these consequences arise coincidentally or through manipulation, such as a magician arranging circumstances to appear a certain way. Intuitively, we would doubt that these consequences establish the belief as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that practical consequences can be misleading indicators of truth. This principle suggests that belief verification through practical consequences is not reliable for determining knowledge.**
