# Is the notion of ordinary evidence contextually sufficient too permissive?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 33492704-ef8a-4755-a973-be4abd01d78f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism to Halt Infinite Regress](contextualism-to-halt-infinite-regress-synthesis-b66f7af1-99ff-4dd4-8ca6-3f96222448b6.md)

## Content

**Ordinary evidence varies significantly in strength across different contexts, which may sometimes be too weak to justify beliefs.**, **Permissive justification could lead to inconsistency in what we consider as justified beliefs.**, **A stringent and consistent criteria for justification may reduce confusion and promote epistemic reliability.**
