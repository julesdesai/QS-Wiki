# Is it problematic if justification standards prevent common knowledge?

**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-8d92b68a-d96e-49d3-9b29-39a25430afac.md)

## Content

**Suppose everyday knowledge claims require too stringent justification in certain contexts to count as knowledge at all. Intuitively, we risk denying widely held facts as knowledge, which seems problematic.**, **Knowledge that depends excessively on varying context-specific standards could undermine universally accepted knowledge claims about common facts, thereby creating practical challenges.**
