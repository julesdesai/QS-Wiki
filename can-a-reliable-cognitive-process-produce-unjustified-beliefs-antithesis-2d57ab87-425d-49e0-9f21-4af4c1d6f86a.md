# Can a reliable cognitive process produce unjustified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d57ab87-425d-49e0-9f21-4af4c1d6f86a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person has a belief instilled by an otherwise reliable source, such as a respected teacher, but the person never independently reflects or questions this belief. Intuitively, we would judge such beliefs as unjustified due to the absence of the individual's critical engagement.**, **It is implausible that reliance on reliable sources alone confers justification without the individual's engagement or reflection. Reliable cognitive processes without scrutiny could lead to unjustified acceptance of beliefs.**
