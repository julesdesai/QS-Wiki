# Can Direct Realism explain perceptual disagreements?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ad44e36-33cb-4925-b6f9-e10444f3777e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which witness accounts of a traffic accident differ, with each person claiming to have directly observed the event. Intuitively, we must concede that perception does not merely reflect the external scene if such disagreements occur.**, **It is implausible that direct access to external objects can account for significantly conflicting perceptual reports without mediation by interpretation or perspective.**
