# Does coherentism adequately address the problem of circularity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43440206-9eca-40bc-aaeb-54dcdc17a111
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Epistemology](coherentism-in-epistemology-synthesis-03124e37-be18-4868-b2cf-e370b5186105.md)

## Content

**A belief can be justified within a coherent system by being supported by other beliefs in the system.**, **This mutual support might lead to circular reasoning, where beliefs justify each other without an external basis.**, **It is implausible that circular reasoning can provide genuine knowledge or justification, as it does not provide new information or connection to an external truth.**
