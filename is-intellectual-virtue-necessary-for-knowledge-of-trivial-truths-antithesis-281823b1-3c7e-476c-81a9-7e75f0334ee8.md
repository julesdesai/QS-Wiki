# Is intellectual virtue necessary for knowledge of trivial truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 281823b1-3c7e-476c-81a9-7e75f0334ee8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**A person knows a trivial truth, like the current time, using a reliable clock without exercising intellectual virtue; intuitively, we think they still have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a person can have knowledge without employing intellectual virtue if the belief is reliably formed.**
