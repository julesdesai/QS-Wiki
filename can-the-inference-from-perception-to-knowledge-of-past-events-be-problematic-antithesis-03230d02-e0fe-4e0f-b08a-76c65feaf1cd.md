# Can the inference from perception to knowledge of past events be problematic?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 03230d02-e0fe-4e0f-b08a-76c65feaf1cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Inference](phenomenalism-with-inference-synthesis-5c9b476a-f87e-418f-98f3-b4842f3fdc21.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a historian bases their knowledge of past events solely on current perceptual experiences and reliable background information. Intuitively, we either have to say that the historian has complete knowledge or only partial understanding. If we say the historian has complete knowledge about past events, then it seems incorrect since current experiences cannot fully encompass past complexities. If we say the historian has only partial understanding, then it implies that knowledge inferred from perception is limited.**
