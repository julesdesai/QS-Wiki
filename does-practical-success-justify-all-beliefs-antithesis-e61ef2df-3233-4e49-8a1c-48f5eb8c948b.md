# Does practical success justify all beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e61ef2df-3233-4e49-8a1c-48f5eb8c948b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Adequacy](pragmatism-with-practical-adequacy-synthesis-7de50134-9521-4bfe-88f0-682c548d4774.md)

## Content

**Suppose a case where a person holds a belief in a superstition, such as believing that carrying a rabbit’s foot leads to career success, and this belief coincidentally correlates with their success. Intuitively, we must say that the belief is not justified purely based on its accidental correlation with success, as this confounds causation and correlation.**, **It is implausible that mere coincidence between a belief and practical success is sufficient for justification, since correlation does not necessarily imply causation.**
