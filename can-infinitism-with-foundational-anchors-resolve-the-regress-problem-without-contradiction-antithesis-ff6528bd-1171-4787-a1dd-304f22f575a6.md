# Can infinitism with foundational anchors resolve the regress problem without contradiction?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff6528bd-1171-4787-a1dd-304f22f575a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Foundational Anchors](infinitism-with-foundational-anchors-synthesis-6b525f84-03a5-4482-aa47-2329492f1b5f.md)

## Content

**The regress problem in epistemology holds that every belief requires a justification, which leads to an infinite regress of reasons.**, **Infinitism posits that justification is indeed infinite, but also claims that there are foundational beliefs, which seems contradictory.**, **If beliefs are anchored by non-inferentially justified beliefs, the chain is not truly infinite, contradicting a key feature of infinitism.**
