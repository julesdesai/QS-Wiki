# Do false beliefs undermine intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e915ec3-2c74-4b17-be27-629fe67d5f98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Suppose person D has a belief about a scientific fact that aligns with all known data and theories, but new information reveals the belief to be false. Initially, D seemed justified and intellectually virtuous. Intuitively, D lacked knowledge despite justification.**, **If knowledge depends on truth, then justification through intellectual virtues is insufficient if the belief is false. The existence of false beliefs challenges justification wholly based on intellectual virtues.**
