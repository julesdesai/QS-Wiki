# Is knowledge always dependent on cognitive abilities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7456a93-32b7-4d5d-b17e-6efe1031b0ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-10eec969-c917-4d6d-bc9d-ac9f02dc3f98.md)

## Content

**A scenario involves a person who correctly guesses the result of a coin flip. Intuitively, we do not consider them to have knowledge of the result even though their belief is true.**, **In a case where a person has a true belief due solely to luck, we would not attribute knowledge to them.**, **True belief due to luck does not imply the presence of intellectual virtues or cognitive abilities.**
