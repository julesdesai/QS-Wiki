# Is perception truly free from mental representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20926d42-7a74-44b7-802a-94d06dc18856
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-42a15467-8636-4e99-a9b6-42a7b3d54195.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some form of mediation or representation of what is known. This principle together with our understanding of complex perceptual experiences suggests that if perception lacks mental representations, it may not fully convey external reality.**, **A case in which a person misjudges the size or distance of an object they perceive, such as mistaking a close object for a distant one, indicates the presence of interpretative processes usually understood as mental representations. Intuitively, without such representations, the discrepancies and adjustments in perception remain unexplained.**
