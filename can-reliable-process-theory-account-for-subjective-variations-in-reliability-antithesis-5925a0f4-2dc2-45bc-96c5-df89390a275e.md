# Can Reliable Process Theory account for subjective variations in reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5925a0f4-2dc2-45bc-96c5-df89390a275e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory](reliable-process-theory-synthesis-2b23f056-a980-462c-bb6c-659be3049cf1.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual has a cognitive bias, reliably forming true beliefs under its influence, yet differing from others without the bias. Intuitively, we might question whether such subjective reliability translates to true knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that the universality of the process influences its reliability, challenging subjective variations in cognitive reliability.**
