# Can knowledge exist without an accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f367cb36-6e78-4306-be27-6625b49744f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief about a fact purely by chance, without any awareness or understanding of how this belief is formed. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not have knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a belief can be considered knowledge if the person holding the belief cannot provide any justification or reason for why they hold this belief.**
