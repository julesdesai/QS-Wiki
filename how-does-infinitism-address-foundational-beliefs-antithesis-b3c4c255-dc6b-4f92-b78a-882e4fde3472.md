# How does infinitism address foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3c4c255-dc6b-4f92-b78a-882e4fde3472
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Epistemology](infinitist-epistemology-synthesis-6da6e9b6-0ddd-4346-9ef8-d27afad7386e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that foundational beliefs, those not supported by other beliefs, can exist and serve as a basis for knowledge. This principle suggests that not all knowledge needs an infinite chain of reasons.**, **Consider a belief such as "I exist," generally accepted without infinite justification. Intuitively, we would still consider this knowledge.**
