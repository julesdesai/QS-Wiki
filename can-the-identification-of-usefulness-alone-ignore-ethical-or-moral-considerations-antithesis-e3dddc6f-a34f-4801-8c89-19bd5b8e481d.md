# Can the identification of usefulness alone ignore ethical or moral considerations?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where the usefulness of a belief disregards ethical or moral implications. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that ethical ignorance prevents this belief from being considered knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that acknowledges ethical considerations as part of a robust definition of knowledge, suggesting that usefulness alone is insufficient.**
