# If bounded rationality allows rational stopping, how does it affect the status of infinite justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ffdd204-f3a6-4807-8842-55d9bc6b126e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Bounded Rationality](infinitism-with-bounded-rationality-synthesis-6de35f9a-9231-4ac2-b1a8-a1ab9be932b6.md)

## Content

**Bounded rationality supports decision-making within practical limits, not necessarily by completing infinite procedures.**, **If rational stopping is accepted due to bounded rationality, then the original need for infinite justification becomes redundant.**, **Accepting rational stopping invalidates the necessity of infinite chains by preferring practicality over theoretical infinity.**
