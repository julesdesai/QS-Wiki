# How does coherence theory account for the problem of circular justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0381044e-83b7-4f97-bcb8-479507332edd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes proposition A because of proposition B, B because of C, and C because of A. Intuitively, this circular reasoning fails to provide genuine justification or knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification should provide an independent support that is not merely a re-affirmation of what is already assumed.**, **Circular justification may result in beliefs appearing justified internally while lacking true epistemic grounding externally.**
