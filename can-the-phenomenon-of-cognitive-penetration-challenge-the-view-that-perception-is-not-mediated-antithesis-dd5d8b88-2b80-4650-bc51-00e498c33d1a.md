# Can the phenomenon of cognitive penetration challenge the view that perception is not mediated?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd5d8b88-2b80-4650-bc51-00e498c33d1a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person’s emotional state influences how they perceive a neutral face, seeing it as hostile. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is influenced by cognitive states.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception is not mediated, then it should be immune to cognitive influences such as expectations or emotions. This principle, together with the evidence of cognitive penetration, entails that perception is not purely direct.**
