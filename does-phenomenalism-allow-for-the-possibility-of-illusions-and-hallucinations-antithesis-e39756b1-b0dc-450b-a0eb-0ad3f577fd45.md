# Does phenomenalism allow for the possibility of illusions and hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e39756b1-b0dc-450b-a0eb-0ad3f577fd45
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-11423d6b-b29a-46b2-967d-bb4b03435a75.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person experiences the sensation of water in a desert, which we call a mirage, is a consistent sensory experience that does not correspond to an actual object.**, **It is plausible that not all consistent sensory experiences are veridical, meaning that they do not always represent the way things actually are in the external world.**
