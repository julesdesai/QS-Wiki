# How does intellectual virtue account for knowledge in cases of luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8482e0e-2772-41d4-b275-b2219fffccb1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Mixed Virtue Epistemology](mixed-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9b1d3c06-dd01-4be5-8e49-871c85befc1a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person predicts future events correctly by luck, rather than through intellectual virtues of reliability and responsibility. Intuitively, we would not credit them with knowledge.**, **If intellectual virtue necessarily involves reliability and responsibility, luck's role in producing true beliefs challenges these criteria, indicating a gap in explaining knowledge.**
