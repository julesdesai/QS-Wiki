# Is the truth requirement for knowledge too strict given practical scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc50066c-3913-4fd4-9b10-45ced0987fa3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**Imagine a meteorologist predicts rain based on all available data, but an unforeseeable event prevents it from raining. Intuitively, we perceive them as knowledgeable despite the truth of their prediction being disrupted.**, **In practical terms, truth might sometimes be less critical than reliability or sound reasoning for considering someone knowledgeable.**
