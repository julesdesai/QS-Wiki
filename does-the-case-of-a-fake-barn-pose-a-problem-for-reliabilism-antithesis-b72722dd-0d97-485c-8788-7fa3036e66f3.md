# Does the case of a fake barn pose a problem for reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b72722dd-0d97-485c-8788-7fa3036e66f3
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliabilism](knowledge-as-reliabilism-thesis-72dce40c-4bce-46d9-be3b-311bf9457188.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, while driving through an area filled with fake barns indistinguishable from real barns, correctly identifies one real barn among fakes.**, **In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, despite the belief being true and produced by a reliable cognitive process in the specific instance.**
