# Does accidental alignment with truth and utility constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46bb8a8a-e7fa-4d85-914b-e608d0d43680
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where someone believes they are drinking water for health, but it's actually a nutrient drink, improving health. Intuitively, an accidental alignment of belief, truth, and utility does not make it knowledge.**, **A principle that underlines knowledge involves a deliberate connection between belief and fact, not accidental.**
