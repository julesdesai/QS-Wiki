# Does contextual phenomenalism adequately account for intersubjective agreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c60c6a65-a45b-4ce0-83ce-c70fe2df10f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**A situation where different individuals arrive at the same conclusion about an event, despite different contexts, challenges the necessity of context in all knowledge claims.**, **There is a plausible principle that certain truths may be evident universally, regardless of individual perceptual or contextual differences.**, **It is implausible that contextual factors are always necessary if intersubjective agreement can be achieved without them.**
