# Is a belief that turns out to be true and useful but formed randomly knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98bec1ec-d4cd-4fd4-8678-f0884a3cd2b2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes it will rain tomorrow due to a random guess, and it does rain, making the belief true and the umbrella they carry useful. Intuitively, we would not judge this case as knowledge.**, **A principle suggests that knowledge requires a non-random basis for belief. This principle together with the random basis of the belief implies it is not knowledge.**
