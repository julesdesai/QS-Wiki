# Can knowledge of the external world arise only from non-illusory perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 363dea72-7a6d-4067-834d-a88041d6359c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-ef0ab88b-769a-4e6f-afc6-1fb31c926a76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person observes virtual environments that mimic real-world conditions perfectly. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge gained from such environments can be as true as that from real environments.**, **A plausible principle states that knowledge can be derived from reliable processes regardless of their underlying authenticity. The possibility of gaining knowledge from perfectly mimicked environments threatens this principle that ties knowledge strictly to non-illusory perception.**, **There is a logical fact that knowledge must allow for degrees of certainty beyond direct perception, suggesting that non-perceptual processes can also contribute to knowledge of the external world.**
