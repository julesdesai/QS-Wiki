# Does the no-false lemma view allow for accidental truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8866c2ac-c12b-437d-bf80-5a066639ef2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma View](no-false-lemma-view-synthesis-d417d5a7-7b9d-4d6b-8a89-c64775b0283f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person comes to believe a truth based on an accident of evidence that coincidentally aligns with reality. Intuitively, this coincidence does not seem to constitute genuine knowledge.**, **It is implausible that beliefs formed accidentally with no intentional basis in truth can be considered knowledge. This accidentality questions the sufficiency of true belief and justification in defining knowledge.**
