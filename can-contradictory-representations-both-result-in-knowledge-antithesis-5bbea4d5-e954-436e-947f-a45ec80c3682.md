# Can contradictory representations both result in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5bbea4d5-e954-436e-947f-a45ec80c3682
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario in which two people have contradictory mental representations of an ambiguous artwork, but both derive valuable insights. Intuitively, we might judge that both have knowledge of the artwork in different respects.**, **There is a plausible principle that diverse interpretations can lead to distinct knowledge states. This principle together with the existence of contradictory representations implies that contradiction need not preclude knowledge.**
