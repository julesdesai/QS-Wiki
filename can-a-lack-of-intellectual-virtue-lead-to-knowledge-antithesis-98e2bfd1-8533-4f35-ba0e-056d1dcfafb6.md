# Can a lack of intellectual virtue lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98e2bfd1-8533-4f35-ba0e-056d1dcfafb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone has true introspective or perceptual beliefs that are reliably true, yet they have no cultivated intellectual virtues. In such a scenario, intuitively, it seems they can still possess knowledge.**, **The principle that reliability requires neither virtue nor effort challenges the necessity of intellectual virtues for knowledge.**
