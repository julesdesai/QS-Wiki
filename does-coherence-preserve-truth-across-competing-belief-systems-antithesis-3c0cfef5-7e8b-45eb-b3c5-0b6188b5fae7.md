# Does coherence preserve truth across competing belief systems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c0cfef5-7e8b-45eb-b3c5-0b6188b5fae7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Consider two equally coherent systems of religious beliefs that make mutually exclusive claims about the afterlife. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that both systems cannot be true simultaneously.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence within a belief system is insufficient to adjudicate between competing systems, particularly when they address metaphysical or unobservable domains. This principle together with the assumption that coherence indicates truth entails a contradiction when considering mutually exclusive yet coherent systems.**
