# Can knowledge be true without intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 26cf5a0a-525b-44d8-b20a-eafb93e5d5af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-35af9163-7530-4447-9474-ccb688222939.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth. This principle together with the idea that a proposition can be true without a person exercising intellectual virtue suggests a scenario where someone believes a true proposition but has not exercised intellectual virtue.**, **A person might guess a fact correctly without employing any intellectual virtue, thus having a true belief without it being a result of cognitive achievement.**
