# Can knowledge exist without cognitive mechanisms aimed at truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a29606a-e3f9-4e26-8e7b-853b76119938
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism With Proper Functioning Belief Mechanisms](proper-functionalism-with-proper-functioning-belief-mechanisms-synthesis-d742806a-8086-4d38-b926-69c7d212e234.md)

## Content

**Suppose there are beings who know many truths, but their cognitive mechanisms are not designed to produce true beliefs—they produce truth only by accident. Intuitively, we might still consider them to have knowledge.**, **This suggests that cognitive mechanisms do not need to be designed to produce truth for knowledge to occur.**
