# Is the concept of nearby possible worlds too vague for safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e5d84f77-d256-4f72-bb1a-d188786b95b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a belief formed by interpreting data in a way dependent on context that varies across possible interpretations of nearby worlds. Intuitively, we would judge that variation in contextual interpretation might undermine the clarity of nearby possible worlds.**, **There is a plausible principle that clarity and precision are necessary for a robust theory of knowledge. This principle together with indistinct definitions of nearby possible worlds suggests vagueness affects understanding of safety.**
