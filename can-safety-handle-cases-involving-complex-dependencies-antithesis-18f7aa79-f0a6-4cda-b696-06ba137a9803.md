# Can safety handle cases involving complex dependencies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18f7aa79-f0a6-4cda-b696-06ba137a9803
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person uses a vast network of data to analyze stock outcomes and believes a certain stock will rise, and it does. Unknown dependencies and complexities in the data mean the belief, though safe, could easily have been false. Intuitively, knowledge seems absent due to the overlooked complexity.**, **A belief is safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. Complex dependencies can disrupt the safe belief's trajectory unintentionally, complicating judgments about knowledge.**
