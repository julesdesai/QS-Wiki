# Can reliabilism explain cases of justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 211b7ee5-e1a8-4539-8a9c-590b7ad767e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism avoids skepticism by focusing on the reliability of perceptual processes.](reliabilism-avoids-skepticism-by-focusing-on-the-reliability-of-perceptual-processes-synthesis-530c64e9-e95c-4173-a410-5dad7635a770.md)

## Content

**A case in which some person believes a true proposition based on a generally reliable cognitive process but in this instance the process produces a false belief is not a case where the person has knowledge.**, **The notion of reliability may still support justified belief in this situation, even when the resulting belief is false.**
