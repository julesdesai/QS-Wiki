# How does the Tracking Theory explain knowledge in lottery scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7bf96a01-635b-4b99-9d41-1e1f71c73bb8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3fc68b12-ca29-40d7-8341-51e40d71c5e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes they will lose a fair lottery as there are millions of tickets and only one winner. Intuitively, even with a high chance of loss, we would not say the person knows they will lose before the draw.**, **If it were false that they would lose (i.e., they win), they would no longer believe they will lose.**, **Despite slight variation, like buying an extra ticket, they would still believe they will lose rather than claim to know the outcome.**
