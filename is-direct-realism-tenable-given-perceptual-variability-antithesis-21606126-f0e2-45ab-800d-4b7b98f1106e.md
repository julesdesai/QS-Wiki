# Is direct realism tenable given perceptual variability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21606126-f0e2-45ab-800d-4b7b98f1106e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario where two people observe the same object under different lighting conditions, perceive it as different colors. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception cannot be providing direct and consistent access to the object's color.**, **There is a plausible principle that if knowledge is a direct acquaintance with reality, then it should be unaffected by subjective or environmental conditions. This principle together with perceptual variability entails a challenge to the idea of direct realism.**
