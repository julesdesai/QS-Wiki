# Are testimonies always reliable sources for knowing external objects and events?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ccd3f46-39e7-4ce0-8639-c836eab9fc85
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Phenomenalism](testimonial-phenomenalism-synthesis-3205e188-94ee-499a-b528-9152e3586bc6.md)

## Content

**Consider cases where individuals receive testimony about miraculous historical events that contradict established scientific principles. In such cases, intuitively, one would question whether this testimony provides genuine knowledge of those events.**, **Suppose testimony is given by someone believed to be reputable, but they are later found to be a convincing impostor or fraud. Intuitively, knowledge derived from such testimony is suspect.**
