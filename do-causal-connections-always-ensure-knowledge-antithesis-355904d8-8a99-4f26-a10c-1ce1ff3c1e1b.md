# Do causal connections always ensure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 355904d8-8a99-4f26-a10c-1ce1ff3c1e1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes that a proposition is true because of a causal chain that includes unreliable sources. Intuitively, we would judge that their knowledge is questionable despite the causal connection.**, **Suppose a belief is caused by the fact making it true but involves intermediaries that distort information, such as rumors. Intuitively, the resulting belief does not constitute true knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only a causal link to the fact but also reliability in the causal process. A belief satisfying the causal condition could still fail to be knowledge if the process is unreliable.**
