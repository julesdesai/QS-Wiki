# How does the tracking theory account for necessary truths that can't be false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a2df53e-94b6-4c9a-80ac-c044f649fa37
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50e12e5-c945-480c-9bf6-a71471f277b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a necessary truth, such as "2+2=4." Intuitively, the truth of this belief is inherent and does not vary across possible scenarios.**, **There is a plausible principle in tracking theory requiring belief to vary with truth over relevant scenarios. Necessary truths, being true across all scenarios, do not allow for variations in belief tracking. This challenges the theory's applicability to necessary truths.**
