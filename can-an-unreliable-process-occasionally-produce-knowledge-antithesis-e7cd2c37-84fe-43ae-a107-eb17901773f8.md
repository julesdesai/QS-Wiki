# Can an unreliable process occasionally produce knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7cd2c37-84fe-43ae-a107-eb17901773f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliabilism](externalism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c10e5c74-1dbc-479d-ba5e-469176c5d537.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a generally unreliable process happens to produce a true belief in a specific instance. Intuitively, we must decide whether the product is knowledge or not. If we claim it is, then the reliability requirement appears to be unnecessary. If we claim it is not, then reliability is not sufficient for knowledge.**
