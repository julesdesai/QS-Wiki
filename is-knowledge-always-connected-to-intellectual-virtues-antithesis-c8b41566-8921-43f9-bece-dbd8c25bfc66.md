# Is knowledge always connected to intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8b41566-8921-43f9-bece-dbd8c25bfc66
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Virtue Epistemology](knowledge-as-virtue-epistemology-thesis-04805afc-e3a8-43d5-b0d9-d6c1e7d88e2f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone arrives at a true belief through a reliable cognitive process that is not considered an intellectual virtue. Intuitively, this could still be considered knowledge.**, **It is implausible that only intellectual virtues can lead to knowledge, since competent but non-virtuous processes can also yield truth.**
