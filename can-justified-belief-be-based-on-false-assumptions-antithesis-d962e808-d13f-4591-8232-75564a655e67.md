# Can justified belief be based on false assumptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d962e808-d13f-4591-8232-75564a655e67
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a proposition based on an assumption that turns out to be false, yet the proposition itself happens to be true by coincidence. Intuitively, we do not attribute knowledge to the person, as their belief is not appropriately linked to the truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine justification must not rely on false premises. This principle, together with justified true belief sufficing for knowledge, would imply that unjustified beliefs can still lead to knowledge.**
