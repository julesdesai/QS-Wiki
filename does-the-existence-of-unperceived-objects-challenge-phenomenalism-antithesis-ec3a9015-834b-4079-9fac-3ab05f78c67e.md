# Does the existence of unperceived objects challenge phenomenalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec3a9015-834b-4079-9fac-3ab05f78c67e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-6256cdee-fde6-4d81-b9e8-d1897bfcfd04.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a tree falls in a forest with no sentient beings around to perceive it. Intuitively, we either have to say that the tree's existence depends on its being perceived or that it exists independently. If we say the tree exists only when perceived, it contradicts our intuition of the tree's continuous existence. If we say it exists independently, it challenges the notion that reality consists entirely of sensory data.**
