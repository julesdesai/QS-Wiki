# Can someone know something solely by holding a belief that is true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7292355f-0c28-441e-a3d0-0864e2d616bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Reliable Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-reliable-basic-beliefs-synthesis-62bc80a9-ef75-4d49-9578-a29890370bd2.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true.**, **It is implausible that merely holding a true belief is sufficient for knowledge in the absence of any justification.**, **Intuitively, a person who luckily guesses a true proposition should not be said to have knowledge of that proposition.**
