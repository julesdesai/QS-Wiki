# Can Constructivism explain cognitive illusions where perception is demonstrably misleading?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca205ee8-93db-4351-9e7f-8031a9052c3e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism Summary](constructivism-summary-synthesis-38220637-7d58-4a33-978f-6de4b5b7771f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person experiences an optical illusion, such as the Müller-Lyer illusion, in which two lines of equal length appear to be different lengths due to the context in which they are placed. Intuitively, we have to say that the person's perception is misleading even though it is actively constructed from their sensory data. If perception is shaped by prior knowledge and experiences, then it becomes implausible that all constructed perceptual knowledge will be accurate or reliable.**
