# Are object permanence and perception dependent on unchanging perceptual patterns?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f28e3a7-c7f9-4a71-aab5-9ed57697f128
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where an object's presence is intermittently confirmed through erratic perceptual experiences rather than regular patterns. Intuitively, reliance on such perceptual inputs challenges the establishment of object permanence.**, **A plausible principle is that consistent perceptual patterns are needed to establish and maintain object permanence. Thus, if the patterns are irregular, it poses a problem for the perception of permanence and external predictability.**
