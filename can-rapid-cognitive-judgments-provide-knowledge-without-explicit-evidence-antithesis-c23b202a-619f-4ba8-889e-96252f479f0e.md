# Can rapid cognitive judgments provide knowledge without explicit evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c23b202a-619f-4ba8-889e-96252f479f0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that quick, automatic cognitive judgments can sometimes lead to true beliefs without relying on explicit evidence.**, **Consider a scenario where a person intuitively navigates a complex task correctly. In such a scenario, we might judge that they have a form of knowledge.**, **This challenges the idea that all knowledge must be based on deliberate evidence evaluation.**
