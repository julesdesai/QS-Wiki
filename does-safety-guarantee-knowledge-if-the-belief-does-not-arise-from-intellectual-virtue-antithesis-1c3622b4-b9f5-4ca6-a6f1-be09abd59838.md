# Does safety guarantee knowledge if the belief does not arise from intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1c3622b4-b9f5-4ca6-a6f1-be09abd59838
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who guesses the outcome of a coin flip and happens to be correct in all nearby possible scenarios due to unlikely circumstances. Intuitively, we would not say that this person knows the outcome of the coin flip, despite the safety of their belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just safety of a belief; it also requires the belief to arise from intellectual virtue. This challenges the idea that safety alone can account for knowledge.**
