# Is the notion of context too arbitrary to reliably determine the truth of propositions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3fde0599-6a98-4d05-a789-369e4a15389b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-8ade45c1-54b8-462e-aef7-204b002692b3.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a situation in which contextual cues vary wildly, such as shifting light conditions, noise levels, or cultural practices. In such a situation, it becomes difficult to ascertain a stable context for validating knowledge.**, **If the definition of a context can shift arbitrarily, then using context to determine the truth or falsity of propositions becomes unreliable.**, **A scenario in which a specific sound is recognized differently in two contexts: one being a quiet library and the other a loud concert, underscores the challenge in reliably using context to determine knowledge.**
