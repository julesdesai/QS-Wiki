# Can contextualism account for knowledge errors due to miscommunication?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1454af5-3a96-4bd5-a019-bf6970ea9fa1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which miscommunication alters the context in which information is received, leading to a mistaken knowledge claim. Intuitively, we would judge that such an error indicates a flaw in the contextualist account of knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that miscommunication should not fundamentally alter the truth value of a knowledge claim. This principle indicates that contextualism might struggle to account for how communication errors influence knowledge.**
