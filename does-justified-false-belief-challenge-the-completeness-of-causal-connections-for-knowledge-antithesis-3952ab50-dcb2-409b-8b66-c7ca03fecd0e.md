# Does justified false belief challenge the completeness of causal connections for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3952ab50-dcb2-409b-8b66-c7ca03fecd0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person has a justified belief based on strong evidence that later turns out to be false, yet coincidentally aligns with the truth due to an unrelated factor. Intuitively, we hesitate to grant knowledge in such instances, questioning whether a causal link to the actual truth is necessary for a belief to be knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a justified belief that turns out to be false but coincides with the truth by chance does not necessarily amount to knowledge, even if a causal link exists otherwise.**
