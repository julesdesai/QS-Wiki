# Does reliable process failure negate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca5f55ff-2792-4001-8c6b-321dd1038f55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalist Knowledge Summary](proper-functionalist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-ea5a08e3-f799-4ced-bdad-582e7b9aaf66.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a case where a person's reliable cognitive process generates a false belief due to unexpected environmental changes, yet the belief accidentally turns out to be true. Intuitively, this does not seem to constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliability in cognitive processes should account for environmental variability.**
