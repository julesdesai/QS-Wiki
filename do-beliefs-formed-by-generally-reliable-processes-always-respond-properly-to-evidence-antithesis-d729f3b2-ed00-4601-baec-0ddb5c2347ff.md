# Do beliefs formed by generally reliable processes always respond properly to evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d729f3b2-ed00-4601-baec-0ddb5c2347ff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Evidence Sensitivity](process-reliabilism-with-evidence-sensitivity-synthesis-e3e15d29-f0bc-4d87-b0aa-fbeab510c604.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a reliable process generally produces true beliefs but fails to consider critical evidence in a specific instance. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief formed in this instance does not constitute knowledge.**, **A reliable process can generally produce true beliefs when evidence is present but still fail to respond properly to all evidence in specific cases.**
