# Can externalism account for justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 793859ac-cf6e-4976-955d-2b6f681e6a23
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justified false beliefs remain possible. This principle together with the externalist claim that justification is irrelevant challenges the need for justification in knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has a justified belief that turns out to be false, yet formed by an otherwise reliable process. Intuitively, this case undermines the idea that reliability without accessible justification ensures knowledge.**
