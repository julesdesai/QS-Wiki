# Can causal theory handle scenarios with malfunctioning instruments?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory With Causal Relation to Truth](goldmans-causal-theory-with-causal-relation-to-truth-synthesis-95e17f8c-2d52-493c-a8f5-41488d26cf0a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientist uses a faulty measuring instrument to obtain a result. By sheer luck, the correct measurement aligns with the faulty output. Intuitively, such a scenario suggests the scientist does not know the measurement despite the correct belief.**, **It is implausible that a belief caused by malfunctioning instruments, even if yielding a true reading, establishes knowledge.**, **A case in which a belief is caused by a malfunctioning device but aligns with the truth questions the adequacy of causal connections for knowledge.**
