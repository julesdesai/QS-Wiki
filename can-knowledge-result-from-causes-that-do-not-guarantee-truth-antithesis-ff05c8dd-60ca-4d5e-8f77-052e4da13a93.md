# Can knowledge result from causes that do not guarantee truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff05c8dd-60ca-4d5e-8f77-052e4da13a93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that causation alone does not ensure truth, as causal chains might be present in the absence of truth. This principle entails that a belief caused by such a chain may lead to accidental truth rather than knowledge.**, **It is plausible we can have a scenario where a belief is causally connected to a fact but arises via a misleading or incomplete initial cause. In this scenario, intuitively, we might hesitate to regard it as knowledge.**
