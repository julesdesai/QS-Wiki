# How do we account for mathematical knowledge phenome?

**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle suggesting that mathematical truths exist independently of human perception, as they hold regardless of sensory experience. This principle supports the view that mathematics provides knowledge of realities beyond directly experienced phenomena.**, **Consider knowing that two plus two equals four. This knowledge does not derive from immediate sensory experience but from our understanding of abstract numerical concepts. It suggests that mathematical knowledge is not constructed from sensory experiences.**
