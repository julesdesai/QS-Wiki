# Can cognitive processes be both reliable and vague?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 565e369b-2b9a-4209-91f8-112dde2fa0f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that cognitive processes can be reliable on the whole but fail to clearly meet a threshold of reliability in particular instances, leading to ambiguity in justification.**, **This creates a problem if justification is tied to reliability, as it might be unclear whether a belief should count as knowledge when reliability is vague.**
