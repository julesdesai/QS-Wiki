# How can inter-subjective phenomenalism account for expert knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28442b3f-615f-46e1-a5fd-304db626e4aa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a specialist has knowledge based on technical observations inaccessible to laypeople. In this instance, knowledge cannot be based on collective perceptions because others lack the tools or expertise to share that experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be domain-specific and require specialized understanding. If knowledge required general consensus, it would undermine specialized expertise and advanced knowledge.**
