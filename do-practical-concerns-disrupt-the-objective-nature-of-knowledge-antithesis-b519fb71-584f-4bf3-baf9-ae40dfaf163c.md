# Do practical concerns disrupt the objective nature of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b519fb71-584f-4bf3-baf9-ae40dfaf163c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Suppose we are comparing two propositions, both understood equally well in terms of evidence by a person. In one case, acting on the knowledge has significant consequences, and in the other, it does not. Intuitively, it feels inappropriate for practical stakes to affect the status of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be objective and consistent, suggesting practical concerns introduce unwanted subjectivity.**
