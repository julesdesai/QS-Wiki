# Is truth sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bea7d427-bb7d-4e0a-9aee-f707f522a848
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a robot forms beliefs based entirely on computational algorithms and those beliefs are true. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the robot does not truly 'know' the propositions despite the truth of its beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing requires more than just having a true belief; it involves consciousness and understanding, which robots lack.**, **It is implausible that a mere mechanical process that happens to arrive at true conclusions constitutes knowledge.**
