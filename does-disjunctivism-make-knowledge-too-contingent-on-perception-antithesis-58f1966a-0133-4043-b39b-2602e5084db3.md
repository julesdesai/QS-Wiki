# Does disjunctivism make knowledge too contingent on perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 58f1966a-0133-4043-b39b-2602e5084db3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be robust in different perceptual conditions. This principle together with that knowledge involves direct perception or true belief entails that drastic changes in perception can lead to abrupt knowledge loss.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person sees a tree clearly and knows it is a tree. If visibility decreases but they continue to believe it is a tree, intuitively one could say they still have knowledge, although disjunctivism might deny it.**, **It is implausible that minor perceptual changes should lead to the loss of knowledge.**
