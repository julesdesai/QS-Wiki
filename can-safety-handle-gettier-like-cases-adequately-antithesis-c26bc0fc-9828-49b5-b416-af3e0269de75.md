# Can Safety Handle Gettier-Like Cases Adequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c26bc0fc-9828-49b5-b416-af3e0269de75
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety as Knowledge Condition](safety-as-knowledge-condition-synthesis-7750cb65-384b-43f1-84c2-53dadda89e01.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which a person has justified true belief through a chain of reasoning, but where unknown to them, their justification depends on a false belief not affecting the truth of the conclusion. It is intuitively plausible that this is not knowledge, resembling classical Gettier cases.**, **Safety as a condition does not accommodate misleading evidence that seems correct but gives rise to true beliefs only accidentally, suggesting inadequacy.**
