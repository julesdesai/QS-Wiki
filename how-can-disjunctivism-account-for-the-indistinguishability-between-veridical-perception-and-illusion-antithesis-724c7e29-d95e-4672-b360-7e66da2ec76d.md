# How can disjunctivism account for the indistinguishability between veridical perception and illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 724c7e29-d95e-4672-b360-7e66da2ec76d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-6c9ba281-6911-4c85-9826-b83f47b211cf.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where a person sees an apple on a table and an identical hallucination occurs without an actual apple present, intuitively both experiences might appear the same to the perceiver.**, **The indistinguishability of experiences suggests that the internal experience is identical, even when one is veridical and the other is not.**, **If internal experiences are indistinguishable between veridical perception and illusion, it challenges the claim of a fundamental difference between them.**
