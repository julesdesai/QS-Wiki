# Is coherence neglected by relying solely on basic beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e571d58-c902-4678-88cc-37f6249ab94d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Pragmatic Grounds](foundationalism-with-pragmatic-grounds-synthesis-35d5e861-b788-4e52-a02b-4488aba3b8d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds numerous basic beliefs that contradict each other. In such a scenario, intuitively, the coherence or lack thereof impacts perceived justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence among beliefs enhances justification. This principle together with focusing only on individual basic beliefs without coherence considerations poses a potential flaw in the formation of justified belief systems.**
