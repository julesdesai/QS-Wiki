# Knowledge as Contextual Safety

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** f17cbea0-4158-4f36-bd9f-8ec58467018e
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is it plausible that nearby false beliefs undermine true knowledge?](is-it-plausible-that-nearby-false-beliefs-undermine-true-knowledge-antithesis-fcf3dbbf-def9-4bb4-8a30-342fd02b77d7.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that in the context-relevant nearby possible worlds where the person believes the proposition, the proposition is not false**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and in the context-relevant nearby possible worlds where they believe it, it is not false**
