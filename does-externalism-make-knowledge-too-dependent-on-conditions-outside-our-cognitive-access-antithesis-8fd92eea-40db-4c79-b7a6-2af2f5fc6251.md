# Does externalism make knowledge too dependent on conditions outside our cognitive access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8fd92eea-40db-4c79-b7a6-2af2f5fc6251
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliance on External Factors](externalism-with-reliance-on-external-factors-synthesis-94d45e40-e833-458e-8cba-8623e44201d6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be accessible to the knower's cognitive perspective. This principle, together with the dependence on external factors inaccessible to the knower, entails a disconnect between belief and knowing.**, **It is implausible that an individual can possess knowledge without any cognitive awareness of the contributing factors.**
