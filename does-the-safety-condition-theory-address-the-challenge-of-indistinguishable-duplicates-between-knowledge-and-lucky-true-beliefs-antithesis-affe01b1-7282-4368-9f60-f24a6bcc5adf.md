# Does the Safety Condition Theory address the challenge of indistinguishable duplicates between knowledge and lucky true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** affe01b1-7282-4368-9f60-f24a6bcc5adf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario involving two observers in identical situations: one forms a belief that is knowledge, while the other forms a belief that is a true belief due to luck. Intuitively, it's challenging to distinguish which observer truly knows when the belief's validity rests on luck in one case. This indistinguishability between knowledge and luck suggests that fulfilling the theoretical conditions as described does not inherently clarify the grounds of knowledge.**
