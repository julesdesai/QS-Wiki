# Can reliance on intellectual virtue exclude justified true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87672439-7295-4908-93c3-19fb7e177ec5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief is justified, true, and arises from reliable cognitive faculties but not intellectual virtue. Intuitively, we still might consider this knowledge, undermining the exclusivity of virtue.**, **It is implausible to claim that beliefs formed this way do not constitute knowledge, casting doubt on the necessity of restricting knowledge to virtue-based formations alone.**
