# Can fallible evidence still constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a1e4fd27-9554-4963-a35f-c908e5e98569
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infallibilism](knowledge-as-infallibilism-synthesis-82427764-33dd-4339-b187-9cedd7ac6a36.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a detective believes a suspect is guilty based on strong, but fallible, evidence and the suspect is indeed guilty. Intuitively, we would judge that the detective knows the suspect is guilty.**, **It is plausible to claim that knowledge can be based on strong evidence that does not preclude all possibility of error, yet still constitutes knowledge because it leads to truth.**
