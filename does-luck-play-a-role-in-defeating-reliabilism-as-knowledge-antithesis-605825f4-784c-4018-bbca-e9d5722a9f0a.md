# Does luck play a role in defeating reliabilism as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 605825f4-784c-4018-bbca-e9d5722a9f0a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism and the Role of Justification](reliabilism-and-the-role-of-justification-synthesis-e3c23741-1c3a-47b4-8f99-17f5f12eafda.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person P believes it will rain today based on a weather app that is typically reliable but malfunctioned without P knowing, yet it happens to rain. Intuitively, we either have to say that P knows it will rain or that P believes luckily. If we say P knows, the role of luck in the belief's truth seems to undermine the reliability. If we say P believes luckily, it challenges the notion that reliability leads to knowledge.**
