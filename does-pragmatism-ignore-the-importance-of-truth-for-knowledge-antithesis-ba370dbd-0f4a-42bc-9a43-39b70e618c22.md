# Does pragmatism ignore the importance of truth for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba370dbd-0f4a-42bc-9a43-39b70e618c22
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief about a historic event is widely useful for cultural identity but is not historically accurate. Intuitively, such a belief does not constitute knowledge due to its lack of truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is essential for a belief to be knowledge, regardless of its practical benefits. This implies that an emphasis on pragmatism may neglect the essential role of truth in constituting knowledge.**
