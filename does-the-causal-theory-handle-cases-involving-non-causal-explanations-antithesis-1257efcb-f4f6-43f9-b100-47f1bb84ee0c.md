# Does the causal theory handle cases involving non-causal explanations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1257efcb-f4f6-43f9-b100-47f1bb84ee0c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cee9090d-2e34-47f2-b4ee-a59054387264.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can come from understanding non-causal explanations, such as mathematical truths or logical deductions. This principle together with the causal theory implies that such understanding would not count as knowledge if no causal chain exists.**, **Non-causal domains, like abstract mathematics, involve truths disconnected from causal influences, and yet knowledge in these areas is widely accepted.**
