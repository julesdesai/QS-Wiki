# Is it implausible that invariantism ignores practical stakes in knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0e58a55-4ae9-4726-88ea-fb731c71fd5a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Epistemic Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-epistemic-standards-synthesis-0f962817-db98-42be-9977-f6a5e2bd4d1d.md)

## Content

**In varying practical stakes scenarios, such as high-stake exams vs. casual conversations, it seems intuitive that what's at risk influences our judgment about knowledge.**, **A plausible principle suggests risk considerations impact what counts as knowing a proposition.**, **If knowledge standards are invariant and fixed, risk should not influence knowledge, which seems implausible.**
