# Does evidentialist foundationalism adequately accommodate the role of inferential knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a50fa00e-7a82-4641-b096-841920b5f0c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Foundationalism](evidentialist-foundationalism-synthesis-26c84477-fc91-4190-8a0e-24d90c56393c.md)

## Content

**A case where beliefs are justified through inference but lack a direct foundational base questions whether inferentially justified beliefs can actually constitute knowledge without direct foundational underpinning.**, **Suppose a scientist infers a conclusion based on evidence from experiments, but the foundational beliefs are about the reliability of instruments, not the conclusion itself. Intuitively, such inferential beliefs should account as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that true belief with inferential justification must either be supported by foundational belief or be a standalone epistemic category, which evidentialist foundationalism does not readily include without adaptation.**
