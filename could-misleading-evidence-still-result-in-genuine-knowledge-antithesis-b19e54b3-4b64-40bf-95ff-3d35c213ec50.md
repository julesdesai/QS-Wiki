# Could misleading evidence still result in genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b19e54b3-4b64-40bf-95ff-3d35c213ec50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**Example: A historian believes a historical fact based on sources that are mostly correct except one misleading source. Intuitively, the historian is said to know the fact.**, **A plausible principle states that partial misleading evidence can still result in knowledge as long as it aligns with the truth of a belief and other supporting evidence.**
