# Can conflicting webs of beliefs pose a problem for coherentism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 122355aa-5506-4b81-9966-697ed476d227
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Justified Belief](coherentism-in-justified-belief-synthesis-5c04c2a6-2653-47f8-81ab-a9d276d37a24.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals hold different, yet internally coherent, sets of beliefs concerning a controversial topic such as climate change. Intuitively, one might claim that at least one must be unjustified, as their conclusions contradict each other.**, **If coherence with a web of beliefs suffices for justification, it leads to a potential relativism where conflicting beliefs can both be justified, which appears implausible.**
