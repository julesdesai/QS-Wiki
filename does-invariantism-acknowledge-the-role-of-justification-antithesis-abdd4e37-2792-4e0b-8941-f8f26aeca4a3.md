# Does invariantism acknowledge the role of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abdd4e37-2792-4e0b-8941-f8f26aeca4a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-2877eecc-8388-4e01-b87d-ffe7f20b2619.md)

## Content

**Philosophically, justification is often seen as a necessary component of knowledge, along with belief and truth.**, **If a belief is unjustified, even if the belief is true, intuitively it may not qualify as knowledge.**, **A person who knows something usually has reasons or evidence to support the belief, which invariantism with fixed standards seems to ignore.**
