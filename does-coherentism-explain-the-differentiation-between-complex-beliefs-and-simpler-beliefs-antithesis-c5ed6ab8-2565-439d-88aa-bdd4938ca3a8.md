# Does coherentism explain the differentiation between complex beliefs and simpler beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5ed6ab8-2565-439d-88aa-bdd4938ca3a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where complex scientific theories fit wonderfully into a coherent system, but simpler beliefs, such as 'the sky is blue,' trivially fit due to minimal complexity. Intuitively, simplistic beliefs seem to require a different type or level of justification than complex theories.**, **There is a plausible principle that different types of beliefs might demand varied standards of coherence for justification. This principle implies that coherentism might oversimplify the criteria for knowledge across diverse belief types.**
