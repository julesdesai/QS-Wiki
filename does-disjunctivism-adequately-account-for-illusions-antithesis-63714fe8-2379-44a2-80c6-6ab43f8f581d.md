# Does disjunctivism adequately account for illusions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 63714fe8-2379-44a2-80c6-6ab43f8f581d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-72a215c5-4d26-4e6e-86ab-383aabdf6290.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have an illusion, such as a mirage appearing as a pool of water. Intuitively, either the person perceives a pool of water or does not. If it is claimed they perceive reality, then illusions undermine disjunctivism’s notion of direct access. If it is claimed they perceive error, disjunctivism struggles to distinguish between real perception and illusion.**
