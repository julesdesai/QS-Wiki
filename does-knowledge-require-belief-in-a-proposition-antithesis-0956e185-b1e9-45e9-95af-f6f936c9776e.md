# Does knowledge require belief in a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0956e185-b1e9-45e9-95af-f6f936c9776e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-c35a9476-dc46-42db-bda2-17c735809ae7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person acts consistently with a true proposition but claims not to believe it, such as a professional athlete who does not "believe" in luck but avoids behaviors they associate with bad luck. It is plausible to think they have knowledge connected to the proposition without having traditional belief.**
