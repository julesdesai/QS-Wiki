# Can knowledge be gained through faulty reasoning or luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 54b36de1-95c3-4f85-bbd6-81c7c5340620
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick’s Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-5a62ad60-20de-4329-b52b-21f59b8da1c1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person arrives at a true belief through a chain of reasoning that is entirely faulty or based on incorrect premises. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, even though their belief is true and they hold that belief.**, **Consider a case where a person believes a true proposition due to a lucky coincidence rather than through a reliable method of belief formation such as perception, memory, or inference. Intuitively, in such a case, the person does not have knowledge due to the presence of luck in their belief formation process.**
