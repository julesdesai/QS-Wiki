# Are Contextualist standards arbitrary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cadae5c5-23ae-42e0-b2a4-4aa8e2058529
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Suppose two contexts assign different standards for what counts as sufficient justification without a clear reason for divergence.**, **There is a plausible principle that such divergences require justifiable rational explanations.**, **Intuitively, if standards can vary arbitrarily without justification for their difference, it reduces justification practices to mere customs or conventions lacking rational grounding.**
