# Is utility influential in considering whether a belief is knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93e5bde7-62b7-4df1-b5cf-4c12abfa6d1f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a belief, such as believing a plane will crash, is true but offers no practical utility or action-guiding relevance. Intuitively, the utility of a belief seems influential, though the view disregards it as a non-necessary condition.**, **The utility of a belief can contribute to its significance, and ignoring it might overlook an aspect of why some beliefs are valued as knowledge.**
