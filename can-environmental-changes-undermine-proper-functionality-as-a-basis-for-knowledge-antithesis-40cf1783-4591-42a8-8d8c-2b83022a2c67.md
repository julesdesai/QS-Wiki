# Can environmental changes undermine proper functionality as a basis for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 40cf1783-4591-42a8-8d8c-2b83022a2c67
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's cognitive faculties function perfectly in an environment without certain stimuli and produce a true belief. If suddenly exposed to those stimuli, the belief remains true. We might judge that they possess knowledge in both environments.**, **A principle of knowledge requiring proper function in a suitable environment is challenged if environmental variations do not affect knowledge.**, **The concept of environmental suitability suggests that cognitive function must adapt to varying conditions to sustain knowledge. If adaptation is unnecessary, the requirement of a suitable environment is questionable.**
