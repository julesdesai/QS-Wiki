# Can justified beliefs exist without direct causal connections?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15b07ed2-d5e2-4102-aafb-ad4185d9f8ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person justifies a belief through logical deduction rather than direct perception. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that they possess justified belief without a direct causal connection to the fact.**, **There is a plausible principle that some beliefs can be justified solely through deductive reasoning. This principle together with the reliance on causal connections entails conflict with cases relying on a priori reasoning.**
