# Does Contextualism lead to inconsistency across different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 945d1474-7220-4f17-98fe-d93d86f11a6f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where in one context a person knows a proposition, but in another context, that same proposition is not known because the justification is no longer sufficient.**, **Intuitively, it seems inconsistent and problematic for the same proposition to be known and not known simply by shifting contexts, which challenges our understanding of knowledge.**
