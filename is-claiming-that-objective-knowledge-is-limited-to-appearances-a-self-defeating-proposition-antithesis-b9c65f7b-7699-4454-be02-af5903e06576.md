# Is claiming that objective knowledge is limited to appearances a self-defeating proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b9c65f7b-7699-4454-be02-af5903e06576
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**Consider the proposition that all knowledge is limited to appearances shaped by human cognition. If this proposition is itself a piece of knowledge, intuitively, it must be only about appearances. However, the claim attempts to express a fundamental truth about the nature of all knowledge, leading to a paradox wherein it both asserts and denies its own objectivity.**
