# Could defiant belief constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 165e829a-f918-4ebb-86b3-cacd81896bcd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-f1663159-56f4-4f6f-b53a-d60dc9dbfcb7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a true proposition without regard for evidence and would maintain this belief even if presented with contrary information. Intuitively, we need to determine if such a steadfast belief could still be considered knowledge. If steadfast beliefs qualify as knowledge, then sensitivity to truth conditions is unnecessary for knowledge. If they don't qualify, then some common strong beliefs might be unjustly excluded as knowledge.**
