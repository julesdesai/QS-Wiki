# Can Nozick's criteria handle scenarios involving necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 298c39ba-26f5-45e6-bfe0-f3ef0da01cb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-dfd5c5a1-144e-4c90-a9f0-b64275b800c5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where person P believes a necessary truth, such as "2+2=4", which is true in all possible worlds. Even if P would still believe it if it were false, this situation is impossible. There seems to be an intuition that P knows the necessary truth regardless.**, **There is a plausible principle that if all necessary truths cannot fail to be false, knowledge of them should not require the condition that one would not believe them if they were false. This principle suggests that Nozick's requirement struggles to accommodate knowledge of necessary truths such as mathematical or logical truths.**, **It is implausible that a theory of knowledge fails to account for a person's knowledge of necessary truths since understanding necessary truths is often considered a foundational aspect of human knowledge.**
