# Is a belief’s source always distinct from its truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5496fa7-2ae8-4636-be89-e7016f332d06
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone's belief is based on superstition but coincidentally aligns with factual outcomes. Intuitively, the process of belief formation is unreliable, and such true beliefs do not represent knowledge.**, **The lack of epistemic soundness or reason behind the belief formation process may prevent true beliefs from constituting knowledge.**
