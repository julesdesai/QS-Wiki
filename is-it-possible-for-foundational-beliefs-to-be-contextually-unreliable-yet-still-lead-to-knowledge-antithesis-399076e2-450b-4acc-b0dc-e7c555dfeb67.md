# Is it possible for foundational beliefs to be contextually unreliable yet still lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 399076e2-450b-4acc-b0dc-e7c555dfeb67
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where foundational beliefs are accepted as reliable in a given context but are objectively unreliable. Intuitively, we would still judge that knowledge cannot arise from such beliefs despite their contextually accepted reliability.**, **It is implausible that contextually accepted reliability alone should suffice for foundational beliefs to lead to true knowledge.**
