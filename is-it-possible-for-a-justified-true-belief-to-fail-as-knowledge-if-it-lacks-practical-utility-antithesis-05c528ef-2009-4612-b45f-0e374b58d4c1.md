# Is it possible for a justified true belief to fail as knowledge if it lacks practical utility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05c528ef-2009-4612-b45f-0e374b58d4c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge with Justification](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-with-justification-synthesis-570c8b94-2cc9-4f79-b923-9eacb64ddf74.md)

## Content

**A person who accurately believes that a storm is coming and is justified in this belief may have knowledge, even if this belief has no practical impact on their life.**, **Intuitively, a belief that is justified and true should qualify as knowledge regardless of its practical utility.**
