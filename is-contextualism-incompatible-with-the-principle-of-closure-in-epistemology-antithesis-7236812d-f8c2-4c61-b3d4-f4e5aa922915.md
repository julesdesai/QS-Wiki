# Is contextualism incompatible with the principle of closure in epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7236812d-f8c2-4c61-b3d4-f4e5aa922915
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Knowledge](contextualism-about-knowledge-synthesis-b64cba08-2a7b-44b8-b81d-d7241d8be6e0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a person knows a proposition, p, and knows that p entails q, then they know q. If different standards for knowledge apply in different contexts, then a person might know p in one context but not know q, despite knowing the entailment from p to q.**
