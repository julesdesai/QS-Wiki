# Are there cases where reliable processes depend on false premises?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e662032-9d2b-40cb-af6c-8acaa73dba62
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a navigational system is generally reliable but operates on a slightly outdated map, resulting in a few true belief outcomes despite inaccuracies. Intuitively, we question whether new beliefs can be reliably formed from faulty bases without undercutting knowledge claims.**, **A reliable cognitive process that is based partially on false information raises questions about the ultimate soundness of the beliefs it generates.**
