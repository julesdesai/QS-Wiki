# Does the Reliable Process Theory adequately handle Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8cfd49ca-d804-4ca2-87f9-07f442631a18
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory](reliable-process-theory-synthesis-2b23f056-a980-462c-bb6c-659be3049cf1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief that their friend owns a Ford through the coincidence of seeing them in a rented Ford. Intuitively, we would not judge this as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing requires a truth-conducive justification. In this scenario, the process does not reliably indicate truth about ownership, despite the belief being true.**
