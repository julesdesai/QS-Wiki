# Does the theory adequately explain intuitive counterexamples?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91114e2a-124d-40c7-aef7-c55cb14274b5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**Consider the Gettier problem, where a true belief is held due to luck rather than virtue, questioning the reliability of virtues in explaining knowledge.**, **A Gettier case involves a belief formed based on justified false premises leading accidentally to a true conclusion. Intuitively, such cases are problematic under a virtue epistemology framework.**, **These cases suggest that the link between epistemic virtues and knowledge acquisition might not be robust in explaining all ordinary instances of knowledge.**
