# Can foundational beliefs vary with context while maintaining justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e0823da0-ad42-453a-b69c-070046e72fa0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-9835edc1-dd3b-4e9d-81fa-8a065de25b95.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should be stable across different situations. This principle together with contextually shifting foundational beliefs entails that justification can change radically across contexts.**, **Consider a scenario where a foundational belief is deemed justified in one context but not in another. Intuitively, it seems problematic for justification to vary like this simply due to context.**
