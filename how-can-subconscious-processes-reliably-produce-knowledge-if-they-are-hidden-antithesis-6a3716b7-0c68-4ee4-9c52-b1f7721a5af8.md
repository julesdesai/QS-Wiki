# How can subconscious processes reliably produce knowledge if they are hidden?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a3716b7-0c68-4ee4-9c52-b1f7721a5af8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-094c2ddc-bd9c-46c2-87b1-e486aaff8cb4.md)

## Content

**A subconscious process may be reliable if it tends to produce true beliefs. However, if the subject is unaware of how the process functions, it seems questionable to count beliefs from it as knowledge.**, **Consider a case where someone gets correct answers in a quiz through a gut feeling, with no understanding of the process involved. Intuitively, these correct answers might not constitute knowledge even if consistently true.**
