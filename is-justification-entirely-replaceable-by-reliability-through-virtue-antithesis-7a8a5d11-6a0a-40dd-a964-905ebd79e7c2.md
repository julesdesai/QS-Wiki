# Is justification entirely replaceable by reliability through virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a8a5d11-6a0a-40dd-a964-905ebd79e7c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Justification traditionally offers an internal perspective confirming one's beliefs, whereas reliability through virtue focuses on external validation. It is implausible that the latter can completely replace the former without losing this introspective element.**, **Suppose that an individual forms a belief through an intellectual virtue but has no justification accessible to introspection. Intuitively, the absence of such justification leads to skepticism about the belief’s status as knowledge.**
