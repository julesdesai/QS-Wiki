# Does the tracking theory sufficiently explain knowledge of logical and mathematical truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14f97a04-b909-433c-bb32-bac84f8fc1ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-ba2ab1ae-08da-4d9d-a4db-0c45d3904537.md)

## Content

**Logical and mathematical truths do not vary across possible worlds; they are necessarily true.**, **A belief in a necessary truth does not track truth in the same way empirical beliefs do since there are no nearby worlds where they could be false.**, **Tracking theory's emphasis on counterfactual sensitivity might not apply naturally to necessary truths.**
