# Does indirect realism imply skepticism about the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 73bb7079-3818-49dd-b339-80cac22fdd47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-801f6a7d-c909-434a-bdd9-effe8ebd5964.md)

## Content

**Consider the case where all perceptions are merely mental representations and not direct encounters with reality. Intuitively, this scenario suggests that it is challenging to know whether these representations accurately reflect the external world.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires direct acquaintance with facts outside the mind. This principle together with perception being mediated by mental representations implies that we may lack direct knowledge of the external world.**
