# Can perception-independent knowledge exist?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a691a5e6-c30b-413c-8d06-93ff5d942597
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structuralism](epistemic-structuralism-synthesis-7fb538c7-d6b1-4a5d-b302-4f53eea0d56a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person understands complex theoretical physics through mathematical equations without any direct perceptual experience of the phenomena described. Intuitively, we either have to say that they possess knowledge of the physical reality or deny the sufficiency of perceptual experiences. If we say they possess knowledge, then knowledge can be perception-independent. If we deny sufficiency, our perceptional framework needs reevaluation**
