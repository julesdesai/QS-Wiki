# Is knowledge simply true belief based on properly basic foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ac2df0f-a91b-4ad5-b69d-765d6afb0217
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Foundationalism](proper-foundationalism-synthesis-115cfa71-603e-48b1-bce9-39f7dced12e3.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a belief to be connected by a reliable method to the truth. This principle together with the idea that knowledge is true belief based on properly basic foundational beliefs entails that properly basic foundational beliefs are acquired through a reliable process.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on a perceptual experience that is an illusion. Intuitively, we would not consider this a case of knowledge even if the belief is true.**, **It is implausible that all properly basic foundational beliefs are acquired through reliable processes as some could be formed under deceiving conditions without the person being aware of it.**
