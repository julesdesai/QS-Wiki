# Does the notion that hallucinations lack epistemic grounding conflict with cognitive science insights?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 77298d48-8342-4002-b7f9-ca9792fc46e0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle in cognitive science that experiential states, including hallucinations, can sometimes adaptively inform future predictions or behaviors. It is implausible that all hallucinations completely lack epistemic grounding, as they can play adaptive roles in cognitive processing.**
