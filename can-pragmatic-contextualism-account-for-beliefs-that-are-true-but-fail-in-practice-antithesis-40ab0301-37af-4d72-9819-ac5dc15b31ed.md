# Can pragmatic contextualism account for beliefs that are true but fail in practice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 40ab0301-37af-4d72-9819-ac5dc15b31ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**A belief can be true but still fail to result in practical success due to unforeseen circumstances or factors beyond the believer's control.**, **A belief that does not lead to practical success cannot be considered knowledge according to the proposed theoretical view.**, **Knowledge of a proposition should not be contingent solely on achieving practical success, as practical success can be influenced by external factors unrelated to the truth of the belief.**
