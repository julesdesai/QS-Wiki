# Does cultural variability challenge the universality of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f682a60-f057-48ba-b287-454c06101d4d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Reliable Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-reliable-basic-beliefs-synthesis-62bc80a9-ef75-4d49-9578-a29890370bd2.md)

## Content

**A foundational belief is one that does not derive justification from other beliefs.**, **There is a plausible observation that foundational beliefs might differ significantly across cultures or traditions.**, **If foundational beliefs vary by cultural context, then the universality of foundational justification across all beliefs becomes implausible.**
