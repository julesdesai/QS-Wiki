# Is a causal connection without understanding sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3022bc7-6450-4516-9ca0-900fa6c38c3a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief about a fact, yet they have no understanding of the causal process leading to this belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks knowledge due to their ignorance of the causal mechanism.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding the mechanism leading to a belief is necessary for it to be knowledge.**
