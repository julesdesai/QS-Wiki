# Is it plausible for intellectual virtue to mislead without affecting knowledge status?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 573d5b8e-aa0b-41b1-a06d-8b644f97eb5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Consider an expert ornithologist identifying a rare bird species based on specific traits, which is an exercise of intellectual virtue. In nearby plausible scenarios, misleading mutations could make this identification false. Intuitively, the ornithologist still possesses knowledge.**, **A flaw in intellectual virtue could occasionally lead to false beliefs in nearby possible worlds, yet this doesn't negate current instances of knowledge when the belief system is largely reliable.**
