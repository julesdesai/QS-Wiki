# Can the principle of coherence justify contradictory beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 92d46544-3871-4a0d-945a-930141f08a6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Foundationalism](coherentist-foundationalism-synthesis-815fa62f-5e40-4962-8349-ea400fc3f5ce.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief coheres with a coherent set but is contradictory to another coherent set of beliefs held by the same person. Intuitively, we would judge that coherence alone cannot resolve the contradiction objectively.**, **In theory, two distinct coherent systems could justify contradictory beliefs, suggesting coherence might not be a definitive arbiter of truth.**
