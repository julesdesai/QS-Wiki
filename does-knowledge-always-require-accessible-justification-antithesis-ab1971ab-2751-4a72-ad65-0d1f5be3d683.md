# Does knowledge always require accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab1971ab-2751-4a72-ad65-0d1f5be3d683
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition but has no understanding of why it is true or how their belief was formed. In such a scenario, intuitively we would question whether the person truly knows the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some form of accessible justification to the person. This principle suggests that without such access, the belief may fail to be knowledge.**
