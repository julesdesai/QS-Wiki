# Are accidental virtues sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 77ebc692-9ba3-4c13-91e4-e63018723ac1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person possesses an intellectual virtue like careful reasoning but their true belief arises through accident rather than through exercising this virtue. Intuitively, we do not consider this as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtues must be actively exercised for knowledge. This principle together with the concept of accidental virtue suggests that passive possession of virtue insufficiently accounts for knowledge.**
