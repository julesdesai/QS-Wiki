# Does reliability equate with truth in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ba2c963-85ce-40ce-8f1e-0d7c97edf94f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge](reliable-process-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8a084bde-8e94-4b49-86d3-6339aef5c9e6.md)

## Content

**A process can be considered reliable because it generally produces true beliefs, yet it might not always lead to truth in unpredictable or novel situations**, **In unusual situations, a supposed reliable process might falter, suggesting reliability is context-dependent**, **The variability of contexts implies that reliability does not necessarily align with truth universally**
