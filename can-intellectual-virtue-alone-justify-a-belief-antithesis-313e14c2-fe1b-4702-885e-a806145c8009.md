# Can intellectual virtue alone justify a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 313e14c2-fe1b-4702-885e-a806145c8009
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**An individual exercising an intellectual virtue might be unaware of key evidence against their belief, calling into question the justification of that belief.**, **An epistemic environment that hides or distorts certain information can render a virtuous belief unjustified despite being true.**, **Justification requires not only intellectual virtue but also accessibility to relevant evidence and reasoning.**
