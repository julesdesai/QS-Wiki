# Do all true beliefs justify as knowledge when practicality varies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f396a7e0-121e-4555-b5e0-09a779c6367b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](knowledge-as-subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-73203364-04bd-40f3-b8f4-b58f75431fb6.md)

## Content

**In a situation where a student believes they know the exam content based on the syllabus which aligns truthfully, they may still fail due to unexpected questions seemingly irrelevant to their initial practical approach.**, **This example undermines the sufficiency of true beliefs being relevant to practical interests as the sole criteria for knowledge.**
