# Is coherence among false beliefs sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 34cfbebf-a923-499d-9db6-609b199330ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-7bdcae0a-f633-4c38-9857-77d6ba49435c.md)

## Content

**A scenario where a person holds a set of coherent beliefs that logically support the conclusion that the Earth is flat would lack knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that coherence among these false beliefs does not constitute knowledge, challenging the sufficiency of coherence for knowledge.**
