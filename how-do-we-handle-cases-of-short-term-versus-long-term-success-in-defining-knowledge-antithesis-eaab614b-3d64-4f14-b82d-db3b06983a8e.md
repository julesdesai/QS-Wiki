# How do we handle cases of short-term versus long-term success in defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eaab614b-3d64-4f14-b82d-db3b06983a8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-c3735471-f546-42ec-8e21-f2451431b4e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief leads to a successful action in the short term but leads to negative consequences in the long term. Intuitively, we would question whether this should qualify as knowledge**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain consistent over time, hence a belief leading to short-term success but eventual failure should not qualify as knowledge**
