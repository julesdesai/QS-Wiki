# Can infinite networks provide satisfactory explanations for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d80b914c-11e7-463f-a765-3f28fed916a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Infinitism Summary](coherent-infinitism-summary-synthesis-eca6bdeb-868b-4e1e-9a63-23b9880f21ff.md)

## Content

**A satisfactory explanation of justification typically involves a clear and finite set of reasons that support a belief.**, **An infinite network of interrelated reasons may not allow for a clear and concise explanation of why a belief is justified.**, **Justification is often judged by the clarity of reasons, which could be obscured by an infinite and complex network.**
