# Can circumstances defeat normally reliable truth-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d34a8a2e-b6cd-4215-a76a-c9a42292950c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick’s Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-5a62ad60-20de-4329-b52b-21f59b8da1c1.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone uses a normally reliable method (such as a clock) to form a belief that a certain time is noon. However, unknown to them, a circumstance like a power outage has made the clock unreliable, leading to a coincidental accuracy in their belief. Intuitively, we might deny they have knowledge because the abnormal circumstance undermines the typical reliability and truth-tracking is disrupted by external interference.**
