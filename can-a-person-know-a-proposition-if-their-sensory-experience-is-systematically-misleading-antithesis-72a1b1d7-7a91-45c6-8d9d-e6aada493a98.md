# Can a person know a proposition if their sensory experience is systematically misleading?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72a1b1d7-7a91-45c6-8d9d-e6aada493a98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empiricism Summary](empiricism-summary-synthesis-4e15833e-fa6c-416c-a78e-6563a82d7628.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge can be accurately obtained if sensory experiences are consistently deceptive.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person's optical illusions consistently mislead them about object size or distance. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their sensory-based propositions are suspect.**
