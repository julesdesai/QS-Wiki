# Does infinite justification lead to infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 887eb3f5-cc58-4191-be12-90c76b62b24a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Recursive Infinitism Summary](recursive-infinitism-summary-synthesis-e60eeb81-9314-4a67-a7c8-de308e762c47.md)

## Content

**An infinite justification chain requires each reason to be justified by another reason ad infinitum, potentially leading to infinite regress.**, **Infinite regress is typically considered problematic because it suggests no foundational justification is possible, undermining the idea of justified belief.**, **If infinite regress undermines justification, then requiring infinite justification for knowledge seems self-defeating.**
