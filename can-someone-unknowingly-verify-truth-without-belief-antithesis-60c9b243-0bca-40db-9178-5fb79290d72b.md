# Can someone unknowingly verify truth without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60c9b243-0bca-40db-9178-5fb79290d72b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**Consider a mechanic working on an engine, who accidentally fixes a problem by replacing a part, despite mistakenly believing the issue lies elsewhere. The success of actions unintended to verify the true problem does not constitute knowledge of the issue.**, **Verification of truth can incidentally arise from actions that are wrongly intended, indicating knowledge is not entirely dependent on belief's practical success.**
