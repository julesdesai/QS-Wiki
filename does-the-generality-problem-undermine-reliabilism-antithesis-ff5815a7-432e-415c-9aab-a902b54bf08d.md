# Does the generality problem undermine reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff5815a7-432e-415c-9aab-a902b54bf08d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**The generality problem arises from the challenge of determining which level of generality a process should be assessed.**, **Suppose a person forms a belief based on perception. Intuitively, the reliability of perception can be categorized at different levels, such as "visual perception" or "visual perception in good lighting conditions."**, **There is no clear principle for deciding how general or specific this process category should be to judge reliability, which creates ambiguity in applying reliabilism.**
