# Does coherentism account for intuitive knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d7f82c2-423a-4d33-b312-591871ff419f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person intuitively recognizes ethical truths, such as the wrongness of an unjust act, without reflection on a system of beliefs. Intuitively, it seems a person can have justified knowledge of such truths independently of coherence with other beliefs.**, **A plausible principle is that intuitive knowledge, such as basic ethical insights, does not always rely upon coherence with other beliefs to be justified.**
