# Is probabilistic justification context-sensitive, and does this affect it as a basis for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9642f03-8dfc-4a67-9209-eb2005cc573b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-16fe8fa2-69bc-4186-a85e-e3861ba3ba16.md)

## Content

**In one context, a high probability might provide justification, but in another, more information might be required for the same probability to be sufficient.**, **The variable context-dependent requirements for what counts as a justified belief suggest that probability alone doesn't fully account for what constitutes knowledge.**, **Context-sensitivity requires additional elements to secure knowledge, making probabilistic justification insufficient simply universally.**
