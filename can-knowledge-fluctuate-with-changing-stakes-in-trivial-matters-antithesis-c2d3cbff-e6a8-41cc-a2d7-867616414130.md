# Can knowledge fluctuate with changing stakes in trivial matters?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2d3cbff-e6a8-41cc-a2d7-867616414130
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment with Influence of Practical Stakes](pragmatic-encroachment-with-influence-of-practical-stakes-synthesis-1e83d295-b88f-4064-889e-097b4fb938ca.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person knows the time by glancing at a reliable wall clock showing ten o'clock, and the person faces no significant consequences regardless of being right or wrong. Intuitively, practical stakes do not change the person's knowledge in such a trivial matter.**, **According to a reasonable assumption, knowledge in trivial matters should be stable and not subject to fluctuation based on practical consequences. This assumption conflicts with the idea that practical stakes always influence the threshold for knowledge.**
