# Does reliabilism account for the intuitive absence of knowledge in fake barn cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2f18b38d-b242-4e22-b298-bd2d089bb464
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees what appears to be a barn in a countryside filled with lifelike fake barns. Intuitively, one might judge they do not know it's a barn even if it happens to be the only real barn.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should withstand misleading environments where accuracy largely depends on luck. This principle suggests that mere truth and reliability in such contexts might not suffice for genuine knowledge.**
