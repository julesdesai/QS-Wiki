# Can justification criteria lead to contradictory claims of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 11cd7d94-709f-4424-a251-a7769c03f274
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b00dff79-8ba4-4b33-82ea-85278e861c3e.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that in varying contexts, the standard for justification in knowing a proposition could change significantly.**, **Different contexts could generate cases where a person is justified in knowing a proposition in one context but not in another.**, **This variability could lead to situations where two people making claims about the same proposition have contradicting judgments about having knowledge without absolute inconsistency.**, **If justification criteria permit such contradictory outcomes, it challenges the coherence and reliability of knowledge claims.**
