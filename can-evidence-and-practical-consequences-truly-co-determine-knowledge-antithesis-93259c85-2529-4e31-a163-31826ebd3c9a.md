# Can evidence and practical consequences truly co-determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93259c85-2529-4e31-a163-31826ebd3c9a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-98e3a79c-8fed-46a8-a97f-357c8246f324.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person observes rain clouds and believes it will rain, based solely on their observations. Regardless of their plans—such as a leisurely walk or important meeting—intuitively, the evidence of rain clouds should be the primary factor for believing it will rain.**, **Consider a case where two people have identical beliefs based on identical evidence about rain, but face wildly different practical consequences. It seems implausible that both their knowledge claims about rain should be valid if evidence is genuinely co-dependent on practical consequences.**
