# Does Reliabilism adequately address the problem of epistemic closure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe29ac73-2d60-424e-8179-2211812ab3ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-24b421a0-08f3-4c66-b0fb-2acc7f5da514.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person knows a certain proposition through a reliable cognitive process. If the person does not know the logical consequences of that proposition, it is difficult to see how they have full knowledge of the initial proposition.**, **Consider the principle of epistemic closure which states that if a person knows a proposition and competently deduces another proposition from it, they should know the new proposition as well. This principle raises concerns about whether reliabilism can explain such deductive knowledge.**
