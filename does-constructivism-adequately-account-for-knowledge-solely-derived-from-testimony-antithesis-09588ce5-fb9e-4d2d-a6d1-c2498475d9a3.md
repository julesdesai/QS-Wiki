# Does constructivism adequately account for knowledge solely derived from testimony?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 09588ce5-fb9e-4d2d-a6d1-c2498475d9a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism](constructivism-synthesis-c5cc0372-1259-4a9f-927f-8a96c3c904c0.md)

## Content

**Knowledge can be acquired through testimony without direct sensory experience.**, **A person can gain knowledge by accepting well-justified propositions provided by reliable sources.**, **Constructivism implies knowledge requires active construction through sensory and conceptual integration, which is not present in testimony-based knowledge.**
