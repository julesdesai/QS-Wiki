# Is an infinite chain of reasons compatible with everyday experience of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95aa2d9c-2160-4b88-b2f2-4e1882c8a83f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-d655bb23-a2c0-4c5f-b0d3-18cd986ab587.md)

## Content

**Everyday instances of knowledge acquisition do not appear to rely on infinite chains of reasons.**, **Suppose one justifies a belief by referring to an immediate fact or observation, such scenarios intuitively demand no infinite regress for justification.**, **In practical reasoning, usually a finite number of steps are sufficient for individuals to feel justified in their beliefs.**
