# Can one know what they cannot perceive?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 708dc2f0-243f-4459-992d-e29fc53c9c3e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Direct Realism](pragmatic-direct-realism-synthesis-4173acef-5348-4f34-a95b-b6ba164e9e84.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which a scientist infers the existence of an unobservable particle based on mathematical models and experimental results without direct perceptual access to it. Intuitively, one might claim the scientist knows the particle exists despite the lack of direct perception.**, **Indirect evidence and theoretical reasoning sometimes provide substantial grounds for knowledge even if perception is absent.**
