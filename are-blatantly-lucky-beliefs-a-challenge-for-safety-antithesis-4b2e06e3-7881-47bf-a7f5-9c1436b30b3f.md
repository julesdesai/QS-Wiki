# Are blatantly lucky beliefs a challenge for safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b2e06e3-7881-47bf-a7f5-9c1436b30b3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person randomly selects an answer on a quiz and it happens to be correct purely by chance. Intuitively, even if the belief is true, it does not seem to be knowledge.**, **A belief is safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. The element of sheer luck in the belief's formation questions its alignment with the criteria of safety and ultimately knowledge.**
