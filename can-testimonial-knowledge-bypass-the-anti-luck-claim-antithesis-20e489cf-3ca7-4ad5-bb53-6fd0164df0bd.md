# Can testimonial knowledge bypass the anti-luck claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20e489cf-3ca7-4ad5-bb53-6fd0164df0bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a true belief by learning from a highly reliable source, but unbeknownst to them, the source has made a rare mistake which was corrected by sheer chance. Intuitively, this seems not to fit the notion of excluding epistemic luck completely.**, **The claim that intellectual virtues inherently exclude epistemic luck seems weakened when considering how testimonial knowledge can still involve significant luck.**
