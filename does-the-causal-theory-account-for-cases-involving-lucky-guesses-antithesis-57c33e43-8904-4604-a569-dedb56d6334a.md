# Does the causal theory account for cases involving lucky guesses?

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory With Causal Relation to Truth](goldmans-causal-theory-with-causal-relation-to-truth-synthesis-95e17f8c-2d52-493c-a8f5-41488d26cf0a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person guesses the answer to a question. The answer turns out to be correct purely by chance. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **A case in which the belief that a gas station is open is caused by flipping a coin, and the station is, in fact, open is not a case where knowledge is present despite the correct belief.**
