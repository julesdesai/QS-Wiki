## Content

**Suppose an individual with color blindness perceives certain hues differently than those without, questioning the universality of perception and, by extension, knowledge derived thereof**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptual differences due to physiological variation among individuals undermine the notion of a universal correspondence of knowledge to reality**, **Consider a scenario where animals perceive ranges of sounds or light outside human capacity, challenging the assumption that human-mediated knowledge captures all aspects of reality**
