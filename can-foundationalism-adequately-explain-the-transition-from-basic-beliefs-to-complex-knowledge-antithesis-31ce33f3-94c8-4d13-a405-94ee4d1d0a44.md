# Can foundationalism adequately explain the transition from basic beliefs to complex knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 31ce33f3-94c8-4d13-a405-94ee4d1d0a44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**There is a scenario in which basic beliefs, like "The room feels warm," are considered starting points for building knowledge about one's environment. Intuitively, the complexity of knowledge should evolve from such a foundation.**, **It is implausible that foundationalism alone can explain the full complexity of knowledge acquired from certain basic beliefs without specifying how such beliefs integrate with or support additional knowledge claims.**
