# Does having a true proposition guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d5c13129-b0c9-4491-8e2b-1a919a121fa1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Justification Theory](epistemic-justification-theory-synthesis-d0523946-79ba-4279-979e-9ba317ba7953.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who believes a true proposition based purely on random chance. For example, guessing someone's age correctly without any background information. Intuitively, such a person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than mere true belief; it requires some form of justification or grounding.**
