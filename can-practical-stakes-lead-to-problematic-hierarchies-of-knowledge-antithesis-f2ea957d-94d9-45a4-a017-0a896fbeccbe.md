# Can practical stakes lead to problematic hierarchies of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2ea957d-94d9-45a4-a017-0a896fbeccbe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism Summary](pragmatic-contextualism-summary-synthesis-cadd0bfa-2179-4efb-90ce-cbf918dbd894.md)

## Content

**Consider a corporate environment where higher stakes for executives influence who is deemed to know more, despite equal evidence. Intuitively, this hierarchy of knowledge based on practical stakes seems unfair.**, **It is implausible for practical stakes to create unjust hierarchies in who is considered knowledgeable.**
