# Can knowledge exist without producing successful practical outcomes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e82fb66-d13b-43da-b432-d4cd843ce43b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Direct Realism](pragmatic-direct-realism-synthesis-4173acef-5348-4f34-a95b-b6ba164e9e84.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person P perceives an object directly and forms a belief, but this belief results in no practical outcomes. Intuitively, one would judge that P's understanding should still count as knowledge.**, **Knowledge can exist without immediate practical consequences, such as theorizing about distant galaxies which may not have a direct practical outcome.**
