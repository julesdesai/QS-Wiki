# Is knowledge solely derived from the immediate feel of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebb8d297-370a-435f-9d0a-3a5781573763
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Perspective](phenomenological-perspective-synthesis-c5a75a88-a263-494b-bef9-8c1e426bd706.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person perceives an object but later discovers it to be something else entirely, like mistaking a rope for a snake. Intuitively, we judge that actual knowledge requires external validation beyond immediate perception.**, **The immediate feel of perception can lead to beliefs that are later proven incorrect without supplementary information or reasoning.**
