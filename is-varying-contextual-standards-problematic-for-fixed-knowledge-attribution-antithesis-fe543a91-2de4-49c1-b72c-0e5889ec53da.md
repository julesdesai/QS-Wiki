# Is varying contextual standards problematic for fixed knowledge attribution?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe543a91-2de4-49c1-b72c-0e5889ec53da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity Condition](contextualism-with-sensitivity-condition-synthesis-dd26ba8e-d20d-4379-a4ae-bc06eaa28734.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person P knows a proposition in one context with low epistemic standards and then shifts to another context with higher standards where they no longer know the proposition**, **The variability of standards suggests that knowledge may be less stable than traditionally assumed**, **If what is known changes with context, there is an issue with consistently attributing knowledge**
