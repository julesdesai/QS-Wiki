# Do indistinguishable experiences imply identical processing in the brain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c90ca6ce-ec9b-4b6d-972e-316064ead372
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-e62747dd-0f26-4379-9406-3d7f6fb883b9.md)

## Content

**Suppose in a controlled environment, brain scans of someone perceiving and hallucinating an apple show identical patterns. Intuitively, this suggests similar processing of information.**, **It is implausible that two neurologically identical states correspond to fundamentally different kinds of experiences. This inconsistency challenges the distinctiveness of perception of reality and hallucination.**
