# Does tracking theory ignore the role of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d0248ca-4f7c-44ef-8ac3-b4ef39a9df5c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-ba2ab1ae-08da-4d9d-a4db-0c45d3904537.md)

## Content

**Epistemic luck occurs when a belief is true due to chance rather than reliable methods or tracking.**, **A case in which a person's belief is true solely by luck does not intuitively qualify as knowledge.**, **Tracking theory must account for reducing epistemic luck to maintain the integrity of knowledge.**
