# Can knowledge stem from unreliable sources?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f0aa3f5-df7b-4a08-ad6c-3d888ff7627e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person receives information from an unreliable source, yet the information is true.**, **A plausible principle suggests that the reliability of evidence contributes to genuine knowledge.**, **If the proposition is true by chance, it challenges the notion that knowledge based on unreliable evidence meets the evidentialist criteria.**
