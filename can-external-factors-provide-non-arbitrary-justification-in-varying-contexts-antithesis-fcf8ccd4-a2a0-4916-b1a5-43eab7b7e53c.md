# Can external factors provide non-arbitrary justification in varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fcf8ccd4-a2a0-4916-b1a5-43eab7b7e53c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where the same external factor provides justification in one context but not in another, which undermines the reliability and robustness of external contributors to justification.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that external factors are reliable across all contexts or that their reliability is arbitrary if it varies with context.**, **If we say external factors are reliable across all contexts, then their role is not truly context-dependent. If we say their reliability varies with context, then such a justification system seems arbitrary.**
