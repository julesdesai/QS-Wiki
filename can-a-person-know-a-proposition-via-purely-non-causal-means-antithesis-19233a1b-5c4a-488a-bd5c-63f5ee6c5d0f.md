# Can a person know a proposition via purely non-causal means?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 19233a1b-5c4a-488a-bd5c-63f5ee6c5d0f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-916d423a-1723-4911-b4ab-5dfda1d6916a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone, Pete, believes he will win the lottery after dreaming of the winning numbers, and he does win. Intuitively, either we have to say that Pete knows he will win or that his belief is mere luck. If we say Pete knows, then it seems non-causal means can still count as knowledge. If we say it is mere luck, then there’s a challenge to how we consider causality essential to knowledge.**
