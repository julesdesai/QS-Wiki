# Can knowledge withstand radical skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce5ef3f2-9ead-4e3f-bc9c-86a848665b36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**Suppose you are faced with a skeptic who challenges the very possibility of knowing anything due to the possibility of deception, such as being in a virtual reality simulation without your knowledge. Intuitively, it is difficult to claim certain knowledge in the face of such doubt.**, **There is a plausible principle that to know a proposition, one must rule out all skeptical alternatives, but this is incompatible with a constant standard of knowledge claim under radical doubt.**
