# How can optical illusions persist if perception is direct?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06859747-80ca-49bc-9293-8f35d3a92cb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-42a15467-8636-4e99-a9b6-42a7b3d54195.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual experiences a motion illusion on a static image, such as perceiving swirling motion in a certain pattern under direct gazing. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception involves complex processing or accept that direct realism fails to account for such illusions.**, **If we say perception is direct, then the continued presence of motion illusion under scrutiny remains unexplained.**
