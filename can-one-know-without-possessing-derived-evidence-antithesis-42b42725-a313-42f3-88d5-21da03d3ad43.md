# Can one know without possessing derived evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42b42725-a313-42f3-88d5-21da03d3ad43
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where an individual has a deeply rooted intuition about the reliability of a belief but cannot provide derived or secondary evidence to support its reliability. Intuitively, we might think that this person can still possess knowledge, recognizing that the deeper intuition does not always articulate explicitly available evidence.**, **It is implausible that possession of concretely articulated derived evidence is always requisite for knowledge, as intuitions can serve as direct evidence.**
