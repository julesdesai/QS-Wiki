# Can truth alone constitute knowledge if justification is not sound?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6a81487-17b2-4dc7-9c4b-16fac71b83bf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person randomly guesses the outcome of a coin toss. If the guessing leads to the true result, intuitively we would judge that they still lack knowledge.**, **The principle that a person knows a proposition if the proposition is true does not hold if the truth results from mere chance rather than sound justification.**
