# Can phenomenalism account for the objective knowledge of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ba2317e-7400-474d-ace3-981f7ad0a018
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**A case in which a scientist makes predictions about an unobserved subatomic particle, and those predictions are confirmed through experimentation, challenges the idea that knowledge is based solely on sensory experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that scientific knowledge often extends beyond immediate sensory experience. This principle together with the dependence of phenomenalism on sensory experiences suggests that it cannot fully account for scientific knowledge.**
