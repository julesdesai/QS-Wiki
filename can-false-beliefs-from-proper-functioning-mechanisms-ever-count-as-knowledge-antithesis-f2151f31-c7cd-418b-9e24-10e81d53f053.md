# Can false beliefs from proper functioning mechanisms ever count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2151f31-c7cd-418b-9e24-10e81d53f053
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism With Proper Functioning Belief Mechanisms](proper-functionalism-with-proper-functioning-belief-mechanisms-synthesis-d742806a-8086-4d38-b926-69c7d212e234.md)

## Content

**Consider a detective who gathers all the correct evidence through properly functioning cognitive faculties but arrives at a false conclusion due to an external lie. Intuitively, if that lie were exposed and corrected, the detective's belief would align with knowledge.**, **Proper functioning does not preclude the occurrence of false beliefs, yet knowledge can emerge if the external interference is resolved.**
