# Can context determine the standards for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9b60c96-7014-4af7-97fc-81e84016dbb1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person claims to know a proposition in a low-stakes context and meets the contextual standards for justification could lead to an intuitive judgment that they know the proposition.**, **A case where the same person claims to know the same proposition in a high-stakes context and does not meet the higher contextual standards for justification could raise doubts about their claim to knowledge.**, **If knowledge depends on varying contextual standards, it implies that the person both knows and does not know the proposition in different contexts, which seems contradictory.**
