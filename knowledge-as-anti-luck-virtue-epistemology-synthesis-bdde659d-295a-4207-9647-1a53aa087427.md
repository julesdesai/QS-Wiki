# Knowledge as Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology

**Parent:** [Doesn't Gettier challenge virtue epistemology?](doesnt-gettier-challenge-virtue-epistemology-antithesis-572926cb-e046-4588-a5b4-c022506f8775.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed through the exercise of intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is not subject to epistemic luck**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is rooted in intellectual virtue, and the belief is not subject to luck**
