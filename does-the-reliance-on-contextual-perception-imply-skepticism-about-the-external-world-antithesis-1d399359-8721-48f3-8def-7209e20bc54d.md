# Does the reliance on contextual perception imply skepticism about the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d399359-8721-48f3-8def-7209e20bc54d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Contextualism Summary](perceptual-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9fb6be1-a4e1-46bf-91f2-7fc63c3abb0c.md)

## Content

**If knowledge is primarily based on perception influenced by context, this suggests our understanding of the external world is filtered through subjective lenses.**, **Such a view implies that our knowledge is more about how we perceive than about an objective reality, which raises skepticism about the world as it exists independently of perception.**, **Reliance on subjective perception potentially leads to skepticism about whether knowledge can actually reflect an objective reality.**
