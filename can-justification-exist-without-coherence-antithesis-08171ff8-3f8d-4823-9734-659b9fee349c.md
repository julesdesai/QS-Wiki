# Can justification exist without coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 08171ff8-3f8d-4823-9734-659b9fee349c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that certain foundational beliefs, such as those stemming from direct experience or self-evident truths, can be justified independently of coherence with a system of beliefs.**, **Consider a scenario where a person self-evidently knows they are conscious through immediate awareness. Intuitively, this seems to be justified knowledge that does not rely on coherence with other beliefs.**
