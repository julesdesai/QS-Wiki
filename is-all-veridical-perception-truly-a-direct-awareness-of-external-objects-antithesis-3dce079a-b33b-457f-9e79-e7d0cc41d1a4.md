# Is all veridical perception truly a direct awareness of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3dce079a-b33b-457f-9e79-e7d0cc41d1a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-ef0ab88b-769a-4e6f-afc6-1fb31c926a76.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which environmental factors, such as fog or reflection, cause deviations in perception that do not amount to illusions yet alter the true appearance of the objects. Intuitively, we have to consider whether this perception constitutes direct awareness of those objects.**, **There is a plausible principle that direct awareness requires unmediated access to objects. In cases influenced by mediation, this principle is threatened.**, **Consider a scenario in which an observer perceives an object partially occluded by another object, yet identifies it correctly. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that perception seems mediated by inference rather than direct awareness.**
