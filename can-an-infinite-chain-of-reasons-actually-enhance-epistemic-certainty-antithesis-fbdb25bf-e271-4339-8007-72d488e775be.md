# Can an infinite chain of reasons actually enhance epistemic certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fbdb25bf-e271-4339-8007-72d488e775be
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-c0072d0f-5658-4115-88b4-8385b90f0067.md)

## Content

**Suppose we insist on an infinite number of reasons for any belief. Intuitively, one would find this requirement burdensome and practically inaccessible, suggesting it might decrease rather than increase conviction in a belief.**, **The idea that adding more reasons invariably strengthens certainty is implausible because added complexity often leads to confusion and doubt.**
