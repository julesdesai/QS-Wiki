# Is contextualism's account of knowledge too permissive?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7456cfe5-aede-4ddc-b7f4-afb2bae292b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a true proposition in a context with very low standards for knowledge. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to say that they have knowledge when the standards are so permissive.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should involve more than just holding a true belief, especially in scenarios with minimal justification, which a context of low standards could allow.**
