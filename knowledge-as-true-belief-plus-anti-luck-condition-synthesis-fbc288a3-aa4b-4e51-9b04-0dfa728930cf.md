# Knowledge as True Belief Plus Anti-Luck Condition

**Depth:** 5

**Parent:** [Could fortuitous reliability undermine safety and subsequently knowledge?](could-fortuitous-reliability-undermine-safety-and-subsequently-knowledge-antithesis-e214be54-7044-4d3b-be82-cc0f9fbee913.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is not true merely as a result of luck in the actual world**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief is not true merely as a result of luck in the actual world**
