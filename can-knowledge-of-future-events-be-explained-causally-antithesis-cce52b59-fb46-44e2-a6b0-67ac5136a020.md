# Can knowledge of future events be explained causally?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cce52b59-fb46-44e2-a6b0-67ac5136a020
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a causal connection between belief and a present truth, yet it cannot explain knowledge of future events or predictions.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a meteorologist predicts a storm based on reliable models. Intuitively, this is knowledge even though the event itself cannot cause the belief until it occurs.**
