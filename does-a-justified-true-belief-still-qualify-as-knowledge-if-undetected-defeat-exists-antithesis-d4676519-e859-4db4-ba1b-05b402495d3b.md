# Does a justified true belief still qualify as knowledge if undetected defeat exists?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d4676519-e859-4db4-ba1b-05b402495d3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person correctly believes they are not in danger because they have information that a safety system is operational. Intuitively, their belief is justified and constitutes knowledge. Later, it is discovered unbeknownst to them, the system briefly failed but danger was avoided by coincidence, representing an undetected defeater.**, **It is implausible that knowledge requires awareness of every possible defeater, especially if those defeaters do not affect the outcome, indicating justification remains valid.**
