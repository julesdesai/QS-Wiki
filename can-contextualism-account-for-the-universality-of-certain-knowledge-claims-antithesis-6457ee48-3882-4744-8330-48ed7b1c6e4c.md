# Can contextualism account for the universality of certain knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6457ee48-3882-4744-8330-48ed7b1c6e4c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-56747199-fb08-45b9-8206-960c0717486b.md)

## Content

**Certain knowledge claims, like fundamental mathematical truths, seem to remain constant across all contexts.**, **If knowledge were strictly context-dependent, universally recognized truths would lack a stable foundation.**, **It is implausible that universally accepted truths could be subject to context-dependent standards.**
