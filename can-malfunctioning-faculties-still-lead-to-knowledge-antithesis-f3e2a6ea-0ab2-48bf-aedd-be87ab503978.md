# Can malfunctioning faculties still lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3e2a6ea-0ab2-48bf-aedd-be87ab503978
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Proper Functionalism](knowledge-as-proper-functionalism-synthesis-95adcceb-ce7f-44ef-aea6-bebac418915b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief while experiencing a temporary cognitive malfunction, such as when sleep-deprived. Intuitively, we might still say they know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a belief is true and justified, even if formed under temporary malfunction, it could still be considered knowledge.**
