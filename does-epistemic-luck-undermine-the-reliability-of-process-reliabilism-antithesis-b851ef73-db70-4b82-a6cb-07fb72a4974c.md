# Does epistemic luck undermine the reliability of process reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b851ef73-db70-4b82-a6cb-07fb72a4974c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a cognitive process typically considered reliable happens to yield a true belief, but only due to an improbable set of circumstances amounting to epistemic luck. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge remains intact or that it is compromised. If we say knowledge remains, then reliability has no true effect. If we say knowledge is compromised, then the concept of reliability is undermined in light of epistemic luck.**
