# How do we handle cases where external indicators lead to true belief but not knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89d27d95-918d-40e2-ac7d-d2c01503e8ef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief that it will rain because they see dark clouds, yet those clouds are not rain clouds but merely a deceptive atmospheric phenomenon. Intuitively, the person does not have knowledge because their belief was not properly justified.**, **It is implausible that external indicators alone can always justify a belief as knowledge without considering their reliability or correctness.**
