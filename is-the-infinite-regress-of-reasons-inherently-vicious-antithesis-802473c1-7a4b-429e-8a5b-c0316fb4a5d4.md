# Is the infinite regress of reasons inherently vicious?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 802473c1-7a4b-429e-8a5b-c0316fb4a5d4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Coherentism](infinitist-coherentism-synthesis-2a4efbd0-0f1f-4a01-bfa6-1ecf016eb1e0.md)

## Content

**There is a traditional view that an infinite regress of reasons is vicious if it prevents reaching a conclusive justification. This view together with the notion of requiring an infinite justification chain suggests the impossibility of achieving knowledge.**, **It is implausible that an infinite regress of reasons can meaningfully support justifications without ever concluding.**
