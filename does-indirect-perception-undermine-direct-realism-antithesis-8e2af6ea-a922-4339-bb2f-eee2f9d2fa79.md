# Does indirect perception undermine Direct Realism?

## Content

**The Müller-Lyer illusion demonstrates cases where two lines appear different in length but are actually the same.**, **In the Müller-Lyer illusion, perception suggests the lines differ when they do not, indicating perception can be influenced by intermediaries like visual context.**, **If intermediaries like visual context distort perception, then there may not be immediate knowledge of external objects.**
