# Does the truth-tracking theory handle the issue of necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9f49832-4ccd-4d78-8112-2413f463eb19
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a necessary truth, such as a mathematical fact. In such a scenario, intuitively, the ability to track the truth does not explain the person's knowledge because necessary truths are true in all possible worlds.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person believes some necessary truth. Intuitively, we either have to say that the truth-tracking condition is irrelevant or that the belief tracks the truth trivially. If we say the first option about this case, then truth-tracking conditions are redundant. If we say the second option about this case, then truth-tracking conditions mischaracterize the nature of necessary truths.**
