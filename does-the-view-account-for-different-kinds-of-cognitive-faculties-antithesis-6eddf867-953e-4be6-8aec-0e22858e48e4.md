# Does the view account for different kinds of cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6eddf867-953e-4be6-8aec-0e22858e48e4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Dependability View](epistemic-dependability-view-synthesis-57cd5008-8279-46a6-a04a-50cbd8dfd618.md)

## Content

**There are different kinds of cognitive faculties, such as perception, memory, and reasoning. It is implausible that all these faculties lead to truth in the same way or with the same reliability.**, **A principle that reliable cognitive functions should account for the distinct processes of different faculties makes intuitive sense. This principle is not straightforwardly aligned with the idea of general dependability leading to truth.**
