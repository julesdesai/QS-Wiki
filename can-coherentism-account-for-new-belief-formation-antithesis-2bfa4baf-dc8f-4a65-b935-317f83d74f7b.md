# Can coherentism account for new belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2bfa4baf-dc8f-4a65-b935-317f83d74f7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario when a person encounters entirely new evidence, such as a scientific discovery revealing new properties of a particle that contradicts their existing beliefs. Intuitively, the person has grounds to justify belief in the new evidence even before reorganizing their entire system of beliefs to maintain coherence.**, **It is implausible that new beliefs could not be justified without first altering or aligning them with an existing coherent belief system, especially in rapidly changing or unexpected conditions.**
