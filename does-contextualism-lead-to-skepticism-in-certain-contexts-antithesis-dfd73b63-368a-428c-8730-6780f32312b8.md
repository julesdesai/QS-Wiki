# Does Contextualism lead to skepticism in certain contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dfd73b63-368a-428c-8730-6780f32312b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a true belief to be indistinguishable from what is false.**, **If the standards for justification vary with context, then in contexts with very high standards, it becomes impossible for individuals to distinguish between true beliefs and false ones.**, **Intuitively, this would mean that, in certain contexts, no one can truly know anything, which leads to skepticism.**
