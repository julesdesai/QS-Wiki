# Does every true belief justify itself as virtuous knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e0044986-d544-4dd9-a2af-f9ded056e013
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that every true belief justifies itself as being obtained through virtuous knowledge. Without outlining whether virtuous processes play a role, true belief alone does not necessarily result in genuine knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which someone holds a true belief about a historical fact but derived it from an unreliable source. Intuitively, we might agree that while the belief is true, its basis in fallacious reasoning lacks the virtue needed for true epistemic justification.**
