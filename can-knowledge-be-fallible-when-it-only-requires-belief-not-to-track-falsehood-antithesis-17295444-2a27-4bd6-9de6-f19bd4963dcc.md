# Can knowledge be fallible when it only requires belief not to track falsehood?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17295444-2a27-4bd6-9de6-f19bd4963dcc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50e12e5-c945-480c-9bf6-a71471f277b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person strongly believes a proposition due to misleading evidence, yet their belief accidentally aligns with the truth. Intuitively, this person does not have knowledge.**, **Suppose a theory has a requirement that knowledge involves not believing a proposition if it were false. Even if this holds, misleading evidence can lead to false beliefs that are only correct by chance, which fails the intuitive test of knowledge.**
