# Does the focus on causality undermine cases of counterfactual dependence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 64732d4d-ef93-4fae-b52a-d4ff96f2401b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Consider a person driven by deceptive appearances to believe in extraterrestrial life because their environment—although they are isolated from the universe—is flooded with spaceships for a movie set. This contrived belief, albeit causally connected, channels wrong causality. Intuition suggests this belief doesn't equate to knowledge.**, **Situations involving causal facts that don't accurately reflect counterfactual dependence test the robustness in defining knowledge based on causality.**
