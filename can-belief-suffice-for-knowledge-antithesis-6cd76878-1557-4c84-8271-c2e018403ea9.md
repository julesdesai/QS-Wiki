# Can belief suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6cd76878-1557-4c84-8271-c2e018403ea9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-d7f52b7c-07b8-44a2-8804-2022a32ed27e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just belief, but justification. This principle together with the claim that belief is sufficient for knowledge entails that a person could know a false proposition if they just believed it.**, **Consider a case where a person believes a false proposition but cannot rule out any alternatives. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not have knowledge.**
