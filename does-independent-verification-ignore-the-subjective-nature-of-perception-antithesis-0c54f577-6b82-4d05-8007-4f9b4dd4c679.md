# Does independent verification ignore the subjective nature of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c54f577-6b82-4d05-8007-4f9b4dd4c679
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an artist perceives colors vividly in a sunset differently from an untrained observer. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that independent verification cannot account for these subjective elements.**, **There is a plausible principle that subjective elements are inherently embedded in perceptual experiences. This principle together with independent verification implies a potential neglect of personal perceptual nuances.**, **It is implausible that independent verification could thoroughly factor in or integrate subjective variances in perception.**
