# Does Gettier-like scenarios show that true belief and justification based on probability are not enough for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fa43350-ee52-4591-b50d-637863834781
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-16fe8fa2-69bc-4186-a85e-e3861ba3ba16.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where Jack believes that the person who will get the job has ten coins in their pocket based on a probabilistic judgment about reliable sources. Unknown to Jack, the hired person, Jill, incidentally has ten coins in her pocket. Intuitively, we would judge that Jack's belief is not knowledge.**, **A case in which a person believes a true proposition based on probabilistic justification can still lack knowledge if crucial information is missing, as missing elements can lead to faulty conclusions.**, **In knowledge, the reliability of the probabilistic foundation must be assessed but in Gettier cases, justified true belief is intuitively not sufficient for knowledge due to unforeseeable factors.**
