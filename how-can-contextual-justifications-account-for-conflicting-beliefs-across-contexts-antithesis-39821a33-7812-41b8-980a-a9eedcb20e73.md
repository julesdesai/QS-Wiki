# How can contextual justifications account for conflicting beliefs across contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39821a33-7812-41b8-980a-a9eedcb20e73
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Coherentism](contextualist-coherentism-synthesis-156207e7-3c9d-49a2-8bb7-7097c1d027cd.md)

## Content

**In one context, Person A holds the belief that climate change is a serious threat based on a set of scientific studies. The belief is justified within that context.**, **In a different context, Person B believes climate change is not a serious threat based on different sources of information, and this belief is also justified within that context.**, **If justification standards vary based on context, then a belief and its opposite can both be justified, which seems inconsistent with the idea of objective truth.**
