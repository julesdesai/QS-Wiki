# Do intellectual virtues ensure that every true belief counts as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2526cf60-f0d4-4a45-bf1a-db1f0e843d72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**Not every true belief acquired through intellectual virtue constitutes knowledge if the belief stems more from luck than virtue.**, **A belief might be true and obtained through a virtuous process but is coincidentally correct, therefore not knowledge.**, **Cases exist where true beliefs are formed via intellectual virtues but the believer lacks good reasons or evidence for their belief.**
