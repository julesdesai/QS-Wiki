# Doesn't an infinite regress challenge the possibility of actual knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee16e8dc-0981-488f-8dbc-9498f2936178
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Coherentism](infinitist-coherentism-synthesis-a30e3b29-79f2-4d20-a375-1e170fe170c4.md)

## Content

**An infinite regress of justifications implies that the end of justification is never reached.**, **If justification is never achieved conclusively, then it seems that any claim to knowledge can never be validly confirmed.**, **For a belief to qualify as knowledge, there must be a satisfactory and complete justification available.**
