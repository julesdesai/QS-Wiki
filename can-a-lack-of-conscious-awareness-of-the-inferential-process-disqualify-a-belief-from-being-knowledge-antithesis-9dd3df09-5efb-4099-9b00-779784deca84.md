# Can a lack of conscious awareness of the inferential process disqualify a belief from being knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9dd3df09-5efb-4099-9b00-779784deca84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Evidence Theory](inferential-evidence-theory-synthesis-888e1fca-9103-4ede-a19f-61db12f550f6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person routinely avoids dark alleys at night out of habit but doesn't recall the statistical reasoning they once learned about crime rates that originally informed this practice. Intuitively, their belief about safety in numbers seems less like knowledge without conscious recall of the inferential basis.**, **Inferential evidence involves reasoning from known premises to conclusions, yet if one is not aware of this reasoning process, the link between belief and evidence is obscured, challenging the notion that such a belief is genuine knowledge.**
