# What about beliefs formed through reliable but non-cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abadfa34-5975-437a-9efd-7c6a44cbd5b2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person believes something true due to a non-cognitive process like a gut feeling or intuition that happens to be reliable. Intuitively, we might not want to claim they have knowledge.**, **It is plausible that knowledge requires cognitive endorsement or understanding, which is absent in certain non-cognitive reliable processes.**
