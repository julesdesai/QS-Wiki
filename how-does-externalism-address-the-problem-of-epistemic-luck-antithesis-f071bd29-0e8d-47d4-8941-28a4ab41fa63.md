# How does externalism address the problem of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f071bd29-0e8d-47d4-8941-28a4ab41fa63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-9fb6627e-b63e-4d81-a81a-128eb1da4172.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person employs a generally unreliable method to form beliefs, but in one instance, the method leads to a true belief purely by chance. Intuitively, we would judge that despite the external truth connection, the belief is not knowledge because it occurs due to luck.**, **The truth-conduciveness of a belief process, affected by luck, calls into question whether it contributes to knowledge in a meaningful way.**
