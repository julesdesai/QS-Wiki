# Does the causal condition account for abstract knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 387b6e08-601f-45c5-98b9-04da90e59fa0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Consider a mathematical truth such as '2+2=4'. Intuitively, we would judge that one can know this proposition independently of a specific causal connection to the truth of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that mathematical truths do not require a direct causal connection to be known, which suggests limits to the necessity of causal connections for abstract knowledge.**
