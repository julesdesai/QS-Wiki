# Can sensory adaptation affect the accuracy of direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7cf7cf76-c3e8-45f8-a733-64c8a6bac71b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-63f38132-cda9-42dc-9c71-38670cc01e77.md)

## Content

**Sensory adaptation refers to changes in sensory perception after prolonged exposure to a stimulus, such as a dimly lit room appearing normal over time.**, **In the case of sensory adaptation, perception adjusts despite an unchanging external stimulus, suggesting that perception is not directly tied to the external object.**, **If perception adjusts independently of external changes, then perception may not provide immediate and direct knowledge of external objects.**
