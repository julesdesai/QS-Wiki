# Can true belief be knowledge without independent justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 16f8d6b8-f0b5-4d93-a4eb-9f50aa3769ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Coherentism](fallibilist-coherentism-synthesis-f085a715-bc4b-46dd-bc93-6874997728cd.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person P believes a proposition that fits into their coherent system of beliefs, but has formed the belief based on bias or wishful thinking, does not seem to be a clear case of knowledge.**, **Intuitively, we would judge that mere coherence of a belief system does not adequately guarantee the quality or reliability of the belief, suggesting a need for some independent justification.**
