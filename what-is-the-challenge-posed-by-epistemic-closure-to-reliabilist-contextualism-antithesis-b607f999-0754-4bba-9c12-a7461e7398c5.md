# What is the challenge posed by epistemic closure to reliabilist contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b607f999-0754-4bba-9c12-a7461e7398c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Contextualism Summary](reliabilist-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9442c5a-8eb2-4660-b9f9-f0b7378a7bd6.md)

## Content

**Epistemic closure is the principle that if a person knows a proposition and also knows that this proposition implies another, they should be able to know the second proposition as well.**, **In varying contexts, reliabilist contextualism might allow for a person to "know" individual propositions without recognizing logical implications, challenging the coherence of contextual standards.**
