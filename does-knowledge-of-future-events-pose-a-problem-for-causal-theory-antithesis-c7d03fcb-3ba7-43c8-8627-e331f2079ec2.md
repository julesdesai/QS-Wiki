# Does knowledge of future events pose a problem for causal theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c7d03fcb-3ba7-43c8-8627-e331f2079ec2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone accurately predicts a future event such as the outcome of a election based on statistical models. Intuitively, they can be said to know the outcome without a causal link, since the event has not yet occurred.**, **It is plausible that knowledge of future events can exist prior to their occurrence based on reliable predictive mechanisms, rather than any causal connection to the fact.**
