# Is there a difference between environmental reliability and cognitive reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b72c5a6-626b-4d5d-b36f-e989019591cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle posits that cognitive reliability, which pertains to the mind's ability to produce true beliefs, can differ from environmental reliability, which describes the actual external consistency of such beliefs. This principle raises a question about whether knowledge requires not just environmental consistency but also mental processing reliability.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person relies on the consistent environment, such as the regularity of sunrise, to predict future sunrises. Intuitively, the reliability of such knowledge seems rooted more in environmental regularity than in superior cognitive processing abilities.**
