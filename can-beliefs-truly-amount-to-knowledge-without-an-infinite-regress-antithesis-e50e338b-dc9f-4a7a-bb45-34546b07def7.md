# Can beliefs truly amount to knowledge without an infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e50e338b-dc9f-4a7a-bb45-34546b07def7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**A belief that something is true based only on an infinite sequence of reasons may never reach a conclusion and thus never fully justify the belief.**, **An infinite sequence of reasons implies that each supporting reason requires another reason, which leads to a regress problem with no final grounding.**, **A regress problem occurs when no first principle or foundational reason exists to ultimately justify the belief.**
