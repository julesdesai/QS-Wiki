# Does contextualism imply an infinite regress of contexts affecting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 40b2001c-1371-40fe-b65e-2788f6b0d60c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-cec2b7f9-8a52-4ab7-9dd4-6f75547db097.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where context affects knowledge, which in turn is affected by another broader context. Intuitively, this creates a potential for infinite regress where each context shift affects the previous one, posing problems for stable knowledge.**
