# Can we have knowledge if there's no possible causal connection, such as in mathematical truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c4d0d93d-5dc8-4927-8f92-0fa66205366f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory](goldmans-causal-theory-synthesis-55eaa724-2453-46d2-8e70-b1e77bdcb753.md)

## Content

**Mathematical truths, like the sum of two and two being four, have no causal connection to belief because they are necessary truths.**, **A belief about a mathematical truth cannot involve a causal connection because mathematical truths are abstract and non-causal.**, **If knowledge requires a causal connection between belief and fact, then mathematical knowledge cannot exist.**
