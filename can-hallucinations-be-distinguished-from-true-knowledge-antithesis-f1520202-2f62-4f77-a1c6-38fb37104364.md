# Can hallucinations be distinguished from true knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1520202-2f62-4f77-a1c6-38fb37104364
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Idealism](idealism-synthesis-53ec409c-4a9b-4261-be64-9dda9f92eb94.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person hallucinates a pink elephant in a room. Intuitively, we would not judge this hallucination as imparting knowledge of a pink elephant.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires verification beyond perception. This principle together with hallucinations being artifacts of altered mental states entails that hallucinations are not knowledge.**
