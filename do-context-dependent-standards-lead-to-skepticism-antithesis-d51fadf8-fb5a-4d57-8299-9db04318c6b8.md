# Do context-dependent standards lead to skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d51fadf8-fb5a-4d57-8299-9db04318c6b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**If the context determines knowledge, then we may constantly revise whether someone knows something, leading to skepticism about what can be known at any given time**, **Consider a scenario where frequent shifts in context lead to perpetual questioning of whether one truly knows anything. Intuitively, this resembles skepticism because our confidence in knowledge becomes unstable and ever-changing**
