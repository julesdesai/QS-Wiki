# Can phenomenalism address the intersubjective verification of scientific discoveries?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a98ad5e2-6cf1-4c03-b44c-02f2aa68f0d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which multiple scientists independently verify the existence of a microorganism using different equipment and techniques, but with consistent results. Intuitively, we either have to say that the microorganism objectively exists or that it is merely a construct of consistent sensory experiences. If we say it is the latter, this challenges our understanding of objective scientific verification. If we say it objectively exists, this seems contrary to the construction of reality from sensory experiences.**
