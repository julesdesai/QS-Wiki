# Is Constructivism challenged by the apparent universality of moral truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 128ab119-67b4-46c6-b33c-1a8755ddcaaf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism Summary](constructivism-summary-synthesis-38220637-7d58-4a33-978f-6de4b5b7771f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that certain moral truths, like "it is wrong to cause unnecessary harm," hold universally across different cultures and personal experiences, suggesting they are not merely constructed from individual perceptual and cognitive processes. This principle challenges the notion that all knowledge, including moral knowledge, is actively constructed from perceptions.**
