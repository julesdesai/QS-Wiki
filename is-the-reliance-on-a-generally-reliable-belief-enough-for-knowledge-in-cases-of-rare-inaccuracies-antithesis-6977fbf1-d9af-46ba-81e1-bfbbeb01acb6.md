# Is the reliance on a generally reliable belief enough for knowledge in cases of rare inaccuracies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6977fbf1-d9af-46ba-81e1-bfbbeb01acb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Foundationalism](fallibilist-foundationalism-synthesis-ed3484e7-a0b3-47a4-939c-ec83c0f7bb7c.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person believes a historical fact based on a generally reliable history book, but this particular fact in the book is an error due to a rare mistake. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the fact despite the general reliability of the book.**, **It is implausible that general reliability, which allows for rare inaccuracies, suffices for knowledge if knowledge requires truth in each instance.**
