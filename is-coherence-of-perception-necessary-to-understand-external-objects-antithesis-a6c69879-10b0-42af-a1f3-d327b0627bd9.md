# Is coherence of perception necessary to understand external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6c69879-10b0-42af-a1f3-d327b0627bd9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Coherence Theory](phenomenal-coherence-theory-synthesis-37d80231-0661-49bf-9bec-8b3f779084e8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider a case where an individual has direct, veridical perceptions of an external object, but these perceptions are isolated and not part of a broader coherent system. Intuitively, we would say the individual understands the external object, even without the perceptions forming a coherent system.**, **It is implausible that the coherence of related perceptions is always necessary for the understanding of external objects, as one can have immediate and accurate understanding based on isolated direct perceptions.**
