# Does the lottery paradox present a problem for tracking theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1d2abe7-74e7-455c-b289-b0f2a3658369
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-56c7bb47-47e8-422c-831e-e887399874aa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes their lottery ticket will lose because of the low probability of winning. Intuitively, we judge that they do not know it will lose even though the belief is true if the ticket does lose.**, **If the ticket turned out to win, the person would not have believed it would lose, showing belief does not track truth as required across nearby possible worlds.**
