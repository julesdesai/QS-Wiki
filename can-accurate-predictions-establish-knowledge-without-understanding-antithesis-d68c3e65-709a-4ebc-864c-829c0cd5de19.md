# Can accurate predictions establish knowledge without understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d68c3e65-709a-4ebc-864c-829c0cd5de19
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliabilism](knowledge-as-reliabilism-synthesis-e43dfc1f-e9e5-4ef5-90d3-e8588e1acc09.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person predicts the weather using a torn-out page from a journal's weather section, and the prediction is accurate. Intuitively, we would not consider this a case of knowledge.**, **A prediction formed without comprehension or an understanding of the underpinning reasons cannot transform a correct guess into knowledge.**
