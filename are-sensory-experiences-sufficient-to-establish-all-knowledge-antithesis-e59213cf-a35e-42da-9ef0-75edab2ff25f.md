# Are sensory experiences sufficient to establish all knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e59213cf-a35e-42da-9ef0-75edab2ff25f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual hallucinates a pink elephant when no such elephant exists. Intuitively, the individual’s sensory experience cannot be said to establish knowledge about the presence of a real elephant**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some form of correspondence or coherence with reality beyond mere sensory patterns**
