# Can knowledge exist without belief in specific cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0769cf2-9c9a-46fa-90a8-6a1463109a83
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where someone acts upon true information that they do not consciously believe, due to subconscious processing or instinctive reactions. Intuitively, we might judge that they possess some form of practical knowledge despite lacking belief.**, **It is implausible to claim that belief is always a necessary component for knowledge when subconscious processes lead to correct outcomes.**
