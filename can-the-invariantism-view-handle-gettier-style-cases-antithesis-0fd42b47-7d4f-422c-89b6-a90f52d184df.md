# Can the Invariantism view handle Gettier-style cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0fd42b47-7d4f-422c-89b6-a90f52d184df
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition on the basis of a justified false belief, such as a broken clock showing the correct time by accident. In this scenario, intuitively, the person does not know the proposition.**, **The principle that a person knows a proposition if the proposition is true suggests knowledge is attributed in this case, despite lacking clear justification.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be attributed in the absence of robust justification that excludes accidental correctness.**
