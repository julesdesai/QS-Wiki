# Does belief solely stemming from intellectual virtues guarantee truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d880cd0-9177-4441-8da9-ccd6010bf56e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**A belief might arise entirely from intellectual virtues but still be false due to flawed initial information or external deception. Intuitively, such a belief does not constitute knowledge, highlighting the essentiality of truth for knowledge.**, **A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true. Intellectual virtues alone, without ensuring truth, fail to produce knowledge.**
