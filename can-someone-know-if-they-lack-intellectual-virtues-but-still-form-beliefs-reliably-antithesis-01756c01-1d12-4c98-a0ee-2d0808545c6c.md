# Can someone know if they lack intellectual virtues but still form beliefs reliably?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 01756c01-1d12-4c98-a0ee-2d0808545c6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Expanded Virtue Reliabilism](expanded-virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-be1f6636-aeca-4898-8b76-a37fb9c31f3b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms beliefs based on a reliable computerized system that they neither operate nor understand. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks intellectual virtues.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtues are character traits possessed by an individual, implying they are not attributes of external systems. This principle together with the scenario entails that people can have reliably produced true beliefs without intellectual virtues.**
