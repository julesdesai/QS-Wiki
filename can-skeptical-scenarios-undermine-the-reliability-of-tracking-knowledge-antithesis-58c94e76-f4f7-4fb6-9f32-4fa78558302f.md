# Can skeptical scenarios undermine the reliability of tracking knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 58c94e76-f4f7-4fb6-9f32-4fa78558302f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-56c7bb47-47e8-422c-831e-e887399874aa.md)

## Content

**In skeptical scenarios like the brain-in-a-vat hypothesis, one might believe a truth by coincidence rather than actual tracking. Intuitively, such a belief is not considered knowledge.**, **If the skeptical scenario were true, the person's beliefs about the world would still be the same, and thus their beliefs do not track the truth in a counterfactual sense.**
