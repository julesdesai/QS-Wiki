# Can a person possess knowledge despite failing to abandon a belief when the proposition is false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22ad8295-e32f-44ab-8e50-898a60d262b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone believes a proposition because it was told to them by a trusted but fallible source. If the proposition is false, the person might not have the tendency to abandon the belief but instead maintains it due to trust in the source. Intuitively, we might say they still have knowledge if the source is generally reliable.**, **It is plausible to think that a person can know something based heavily on trust in a normally reliable source even if they wouldn't change their belief if the proposition were false.**
