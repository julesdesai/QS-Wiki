# Does the theory adequately account for the gradation in intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f379efa8-9b29-4012-856c-81d23307eb08
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues exist on a spectrum of strength, yet a minimal degree of virtue can still produce knowledge of the same caliber as a greater virtue; intuitively, we don't regard these as equivalent levels of knowledge.**, **It is implausible that varying degrees of intellectual virtues without an effect on the strength of knowledge aligns with our understanding of epistemic importance.**
