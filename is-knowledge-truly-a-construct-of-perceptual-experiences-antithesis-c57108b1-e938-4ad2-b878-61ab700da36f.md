# Is knowledge truly a construct of perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c57108b1-e938-4ad2-b878-61ab700da36f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Objective Phenomenalism](objective-phenomenalism-synthesis-91c0e065-6d6c-4b9a-9519-a161a622cd2d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone derives knowledge through abstract reasoning or mathematical deduction, rather than perceptual experiences. Intuitively, we recognize this as a form of knowledge, yet it is detached from direct perceptual experiences.**, **It is implausible that perceptual experiences alone encompass all forms of knowledge, given how much we gain through inferential and logical means.**
