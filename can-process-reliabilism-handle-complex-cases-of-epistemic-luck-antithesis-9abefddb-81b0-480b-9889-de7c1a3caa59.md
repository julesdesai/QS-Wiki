## Content

**A belief achieved through epistemic luck might be true, but intuitive objections suggest it lacks the cognitive reliability that contributes to knowledge.**, **Imagine a situation where someone guesses correctly on a test without studying, facilitated by random brain activity. Intuitively, this guess, despite being true, might not amount to knowledge due to its luck-based origin.**
