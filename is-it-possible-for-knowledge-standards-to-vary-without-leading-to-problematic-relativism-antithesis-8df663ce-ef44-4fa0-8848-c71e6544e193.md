# Is it possible for knowledge standards to vary without leading to problematic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8df663ce-ef44-4fa0-8848-c71e6544e193
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**If the standards for justification vary contextually, then what counts as knowledge could change from one context to another.**, **Such variability in standards might lead to the conclusion that knowledge is merely relative to the context, challenging its objective nature.**, **The view that knowledge could be context-dependent might undermine the traditional understanding that knowledge should be uniformly applicable regardless of context.**
