# Does contextualism blur the line between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5be2a099-f24e-415f-9f0f-dbe9bf7cc12f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Dynamic Standards](contextualism-with-dynamic-standards-synthesis-b251edf1-06ee-458e-a02d-1c3435caef87.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is traditionally seen as resting on a stronger foundation than mere belief.**, **By making belief part of the criteria for knowledge, contextualism may blur this line.**, **If belief can easily satisfy knowledge standards, the distinction between belief and knowledge becomes less clear.**
