# Can direct perception yield knowledge if it doesn't depend on inferential justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95c88a48-9816-475c-8fd1-3a81b578e4bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person sees a distant object they believe to be a dog. Without depending on inference, they claim perceptual knowledge of the object. Intuitively, we might say that their belief about the object’s identity is uncertain without some inferential check, casting doubt on their knowledge claim.**, **It is implausible that one can know the identity of a distant or unclear object purely through direct perception, as the initial perception may be misleading.**
