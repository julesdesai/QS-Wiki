# Can intellectual virtues be sufficient for knowledge in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44f2a0b9-e86f-438f-88c4-74bc81c5be93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-f60d76d9-bfb9-483c-b262-fa52975172be.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scientist has the intellectual virtue of careful observation, but she is manipulated by a deceptive virtual reality simulation.**, **In this scenario, the scientist's belief about her environment would stem from her exercise of intellectual virtue but would still be false.**, **This suggests that intellectual virtues alone may not always lead to true belief or knowledge.**
