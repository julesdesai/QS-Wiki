# Is practical application of contextualism in real-world scenarios problematic?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52ef4143-1c28-4b52-8875-8f902302a3cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a practical case where a legal standard sets the context for knowing something, but this context-dependent standard differs in a court and everyday life, leading to conflicting judgments. Intuitively, this could cause practical confusion and hinder decision-making.**, **It is implausible that variable contexts can manage the concept of knowledge in a consistent manner across all practical scenarios without resulting in significant confusion or error in application.**
