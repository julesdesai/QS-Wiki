# Are external conditions enough to justify belief?

## Content

**Suppose there is a person who believes their friend is in another room based solely on the external condition that they were seen heading there. Intuitively, the belief might not constitute knowledge if unknown actions of the friend contradict this belief.**, **It seems implausible that knowledge is entirely founded on external conditions since unforeseen factors can undermine the belief.**
