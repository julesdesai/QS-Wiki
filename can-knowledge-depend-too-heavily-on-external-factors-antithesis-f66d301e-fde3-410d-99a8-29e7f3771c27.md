# Can knowledge depend too heavily on external factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f66d301e-fde3-410d-99a8-29e7f3771c27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**In some cases, external factors beyond the agent's cognitive virtues contribute significantly to the formation of a true belief.**, **Consider a scenario where an agent possesses an epistemic virtue, like careful observation, but only accedes to knowledge due to favorable conditions outside their control. Intuitively, this reliance on outside factors dilutes the agent's cognitive contribution.**, **Knowledge requiring the successful application of cognitive virtues might overemphasize the agent's role and underplay external contributions to achieving true belief.**
