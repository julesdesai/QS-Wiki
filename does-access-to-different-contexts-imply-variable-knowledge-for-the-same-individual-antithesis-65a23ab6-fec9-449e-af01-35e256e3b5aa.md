# Does access to different contexts imply variable knowledge for the same individual?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 65a23ab6-fec9-449e-af01-35e256e3b5aa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-8ade45c1-54b8-462e-aef7-204b002692b3.md)

## Content

**Consider an individual in two contexts: observing a building from the outside and then from the inside. In these distinct contexts, intuitively, the knowledge about the building's interior varies.**, **If the knowledge of an object is contingent upon different contextual perceptual validations, it suggests that knowledge is not stable for a given individual, thereby challenging the consistency of knowledge claims.**, **A case where an individual remembers the contents of a book differently when reading it alone versus discussing it in a group illustrates the variability of knowledge under differing contexts.**
