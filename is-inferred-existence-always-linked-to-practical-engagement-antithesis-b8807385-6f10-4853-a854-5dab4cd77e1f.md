# Is inferred existence always linked to practical engagement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8807385-6f10-4853-a854-5dab4cd77e1f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a physicist infers the existence of a subatomic particle through mathematical models without any practical engagement. Intuitively, the physicist knows about the particle even without engaging with it.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of inferred existences can occur without practical engagement. This principle suggests that some inferred knowledge does not aid practical engagement with the world.**
