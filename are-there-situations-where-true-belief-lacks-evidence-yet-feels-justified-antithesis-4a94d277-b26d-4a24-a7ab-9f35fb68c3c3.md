# Are there situations where true belief lacks evidence yet feels justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4a94d277-b26d-4a24-a7ab-9f35fb68c3c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person instinctively avoids a dangerous area without any concrete evidence of threat, and later discovers it genuinely was unsafe. Intuitively, their instinctive belief might feel justified post-factum.**, **There is a principle suggesting that beliefs can feel justified through instinctive reactions without concrete evidence, which challenges the necessity of evidence for justification.**
