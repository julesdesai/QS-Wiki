# Can someone perceive an external object without any prior experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5079aca3-2463-4095-ac28-3e1af9c8c14b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person encounters a completely novel object without any familiar features or past experiences to relate to. Intuitively, this scenario raises the question of whether the person can truly perceive the object's existence independent of their past perceptual experiences.**, **A plausible principle is that perception requires some form of recognition or familiarity. This principle, when applied to the scenario, suggests that without prior experience, perceiving an entirely new object may not be possible.**
