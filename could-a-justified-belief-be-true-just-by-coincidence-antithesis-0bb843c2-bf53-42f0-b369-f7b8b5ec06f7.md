# Could a justified belief be true just by coincidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0bb843c2-bf53-42f0-b369-f7b8b5ec06f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Foundationalism](coherentist-foundationalism-synthesis-815fa62f-5e40-4962-8349-ea400fc3f5ce.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person holds a belief justified by coherence with their belief system, but the belief is true purely by coincidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows it or that they do not. If we say they know it, then true belief stemming from coincidence can be knowledge, which challenges the intuitive link between justification and truth. If we say they do not know it, then justification by coherence alone does not ensure true belief.**
