# Knowledge with Safety and Anti-luck

**Parent:** [Can safety determine reliability when beliefs are coincidentally true?](can-safety-determine-reliability-when-beliefs-are-coincidentally-true-antithesis-86ba8b8d-5e03-4a03-8b38-eb98d5a4d9ba.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief would not easily turn false in nearby possible situations**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their true belief is not the result of luck**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, their belief would not easily turn false in nearby possible situations, and their true belief is not the result of luck**
