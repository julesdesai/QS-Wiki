# Can justified true belief still fail to constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d34f24f4-5ebb-4920-a85d-9beb7d6cf29c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-c35a9476-dc46-42db-bda2-17c735809ae7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a justified true belief due to inaccurate reasons or luck, such as overhearing a true piece of gossip that coincidentally aligns with available evidence. Intuitively, this seems not to qualify as knowledge.**
