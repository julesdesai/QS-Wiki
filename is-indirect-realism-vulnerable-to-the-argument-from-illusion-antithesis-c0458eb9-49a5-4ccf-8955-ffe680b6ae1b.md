# Is indirect realism vulnerable to the argument from illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0458eb9-49a5-4ccf-8955-ffe680b6ae1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-801f6a7d-c909-434a-bdd9-effe8ebd5964.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a stick appears bent in water due to refraction, though it is straight. Intuitively, we either have to say the representation is erroneous or that there is a problem with perception matching reality. If we say the representation is erroneous, then perception is not always trustworthy. If we say there is a problem with perception matching reality, then consistency in perception becomes problematic.**
