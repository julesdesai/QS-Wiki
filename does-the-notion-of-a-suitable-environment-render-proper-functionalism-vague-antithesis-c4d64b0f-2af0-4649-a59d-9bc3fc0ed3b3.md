# Does the notion of a 'suitable environment' render proper functionalism vague?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c4d64b0f-2af0-4649-a59d-9bc3fc0ed3b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Consider a circumstance where a person is in an environment that continuously changes, like a simulation. Intuitively, we judge that their knowledge depends on constant adaptability.**, **A principle that knowledge needs a suitable environment presumes a non-variable setting. If continual environmental shifts occur, then suitability as a static concept is problematic.**, **The concept of a suitable environment must accommodate dynamic changes without losing its explanatory power, challenging the static nature of proper function.**
