# Do standard reliability failures undermine the concept of safe belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 481d0044-25dd-4488-a8e9-ac9556935d53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person uses a normally reliable clock that occasionally stops without warning and forms the belief that it is noon when the clock reads noon. Intuitively, we recognize instances where the clock stops and gives the wrong time, yet we still consider the person to have knowledge when the clock is functioning properly.**, **A belief cannot be considered safe if reliance on faulty time-telling devices results in a false belief in nearby possible worlds. However, relying on such a device often feels like knowledge when it functions properly.**
