# Can external objects be necessary for explaining perceptual predictions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 473ad732-97b1-4f60-8ff9-0275626d1197
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Scientific Phenomenalism](scientific-phenomenalism-synthesis-ce8a34ca-bcf7-45c1-b3c8-1c3da9267ceb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scientist predicts the behavior of an object based solely on perceptual experiences.**, **The predicted behavior aligns with the construct of an external object.**, **However, the construct alone is not sufficient for explaining the object’s properties without some external reality.**
