# Do failures in specific faculties negate broader knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a53e9a4-fe5a-4c76-8eb6-10776d42cf58
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-252aa5eb-1857-4367-af60-24f7afe1c9be.md)

## Content

**Imagine that one cognitive faculty malfunctions, leading to false beliefs in one area, but other faculties operate correctly, still leading to true beliefs elsewhere. Intuitively, one may still have knowledge despite some faculties not adhering to a design plan.**, **Suppose malfunction in particular faculties does not preclude functioning faculties from providing true beliefs, challenging the idea that a holistic design plan is necessary for knowledge.**
