# Knowledge as Justified True Belief with a No False Lemmas Condition

**Parent:** [Does the problem of misleading evidence argue against justified true belief as knowledge?](does-the-problem-of-misleading-evidence-argue-against-justified-true-belief-as-knowledge-antithesis-b6c3cebe-0cf7-4cb5-8a6e-050371c43560.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that none of the person's reasons for believing the proposition involves a false premise**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and none of the person's reasons involves a false premise**
