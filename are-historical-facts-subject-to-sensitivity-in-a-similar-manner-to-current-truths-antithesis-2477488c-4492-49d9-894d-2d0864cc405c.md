# Are historical facts subject to sensitivity in a similar manner to current truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2477488c-4492-49d9-894d-2d0864cc405c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-102d682a-8eb5-4983-bf10-f287ee1765f9.md)

## Content

**Imagine a historian who knows a battle occurred in 1066 through strong evidence. Even if some documents were misleading, they would still believe it happened. Intuitively, this remains legitimate historical knowledge.**, **The sensitivity condition might not be applicable to facts that can only be known by evidence from the past.**
