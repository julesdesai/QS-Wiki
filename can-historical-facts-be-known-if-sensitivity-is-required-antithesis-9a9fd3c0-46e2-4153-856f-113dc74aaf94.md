# Can historical facts be known if sensitivity is required?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a9fd3c0-46e2-4153-856f-113dc74aaf94
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ba84f32c-948c-4607-9039-09527bd88c50.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a historical fact that cannot be easily subjected to a counterfactual test, such as knowing Julius Caesar crossed the Rubicon. Intuitively, knowledge should include historical facts even if they can't be tested for sensitivity.**
