# Do practical interests conflict with the invariant nature of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55219cac-0fb1-4c1c-9b05-b60c05b452c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people hold the same true belief, but for one, the belief is crucial, whereas for the other, it holds little importance. Intuitively, we would judge that their knowledge status should not diverge based solely on their subjective interests.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is invariant, meaning it is unaffected by varying personal stakes or interests.**, **It is implausible that the subject’s practical interests, which are variable and subjective, could affect the objective nature of knowledge.**
