# Does sensitivity theory fail to account for memory-based knowledge?

## Content

**Consider a situation where a belief is formed by reliable memory, yet the nearest possible world where the event did not occur is ignored or unknown to the person. Intuitively, such persons possess knowledge despite their belief lacking sensitivity.**, **There is a plausible principle that memory knowledge does not depend on examining alternate worlds.**
