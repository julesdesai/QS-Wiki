# How does the causal theory handle knowledge of necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f39c0e2-6166-475a-9077-77b6778ae8e0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that necessary truths, like mathematical truths, do not exist as causes in the world. This principle suggests that neither beliefs about mathematical truths nor their statuses as knowledge can be explained by causal connections.**, **Consider a person who knows a mathematical fact, such as 2+2=4, due to understanding the principles of arithmetic. Intuitively, this is knowledge, yet there is no causal connection to a fact in the external world.**
