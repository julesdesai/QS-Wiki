# Can knowledge be counterfactually dependent rather than causally linked?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56d9b0ff-4999-49b2-b4da-46e6142e622a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a principle where knowing a fact counterfactually depends on the existence of the fact, rather than a direct causal link. In such cases, the truth of the belief depends on the fact being true under varying circumstances rather than a specific causal connection.**, **Consider a case in which a person believes it will rain because of dark clouds. Even if the actual cause is an entirely different meteorological phenomenon, the belief is still true based on counterfactual scenarios involving clouds and rain.**
