# Can knowledge form from unreliable yet currently functional processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 096ec6ea-d278-49ca-88aa-aeea0101dc6f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalist Knowledge Summary](proper-functionalist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-ea5a08e3-f799-4ced-bdad-582e7b9aaf66.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a scenario where a normally unreliable cognitive faculty happens to function correctly in an isolated instance, producing a true belief. Intuitively, one could argue this does not constitute knowledge.**, **Justification traditionally involves ongoing reliability, not isolated instances of correctness.**
