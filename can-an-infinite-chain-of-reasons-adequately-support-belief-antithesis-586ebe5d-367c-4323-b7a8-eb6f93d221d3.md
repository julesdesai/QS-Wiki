# Can an infinite chain of reasons adequately support belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 586ebe5d-367c-4323-b7a8-eb6f93d221d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundational Infinitism](foundational-infinitism-synthesis-8fb4ffc2-abd6-406c-b0e9-89dab8b3bfd6.md)

## Content

**An infinite regress of reasons implies never arriving at a foundational point, which contradicts the possibility of foundational beliefs.**, **A chain requiring infinite steps to justify a belief makes acquiring knowledge practically impossible for finite beings.**, **If justification must begin with foundational beliefs, then an infinite regress of reasons undermines the concept of beginning.**
