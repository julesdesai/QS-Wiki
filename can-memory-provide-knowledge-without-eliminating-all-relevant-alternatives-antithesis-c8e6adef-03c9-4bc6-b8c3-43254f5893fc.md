# Can memory provide knowledge without eliminating all relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8e6adef-03c9-4bc6-b8c3-43254f5893fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person T remembers their partner's birthday without reconsidering alternatives like confabulation or deception. Intuitively, T knows the birthday based on memory alone**, **There is a plausible principle that memory, as a source of knowledge, does not demand ruling out all alternative explanations.**
