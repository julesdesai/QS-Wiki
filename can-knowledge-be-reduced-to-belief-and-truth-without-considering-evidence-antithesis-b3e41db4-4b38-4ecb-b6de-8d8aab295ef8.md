# Can knowledge be reduced to belief and truth without considering evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3e41db4-4b38-4ecb-b6de-8d8aab295ef8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a scientist believes a theory is true but has no evidence to support it, yet the theory turns out to be true. Intuitively, it seems the scientist does not have knowledge of the theory, despite its truth and belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires evidence or reasons supporting the truth of a belief. This principle undermines the idea that mere true belief without evidential support constitutes knowledge.**
