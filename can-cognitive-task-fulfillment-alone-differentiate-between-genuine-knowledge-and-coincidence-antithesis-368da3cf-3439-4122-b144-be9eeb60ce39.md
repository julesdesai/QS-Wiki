# Can cognitive task fulfillment alone differentiate between genuine knowledge and coincidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 368da3cf-3439-4122-b144-be9eeb60ce39
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Functionalism with Cognitive Task Fulfillment](epistemic-functionalism-with-cognitive-task-fulfillment-synthesis-3edd3165-e9fd-4e05-81df-6b63dbbb89eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone predicts an election result accurately due to a gut feeling, without any supporting evidence or reasoning. Intuitively, this feels more like a coincidence than knowledge.**, **The principle that knowledge involves more than mere success suggests that cognitive task fulfillment from a gut feeling might not suffice for genuine knowledge.**
