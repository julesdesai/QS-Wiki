# Does knowledge require accessible justification in all cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3f8316b-b4ff-4065-85a4-bf741df0c8d4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Theory of Knowledge](disjunctivist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-eb3c40bf-59e8-47a3-98db-de4d9568dbcc.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual believes a proposition based solely on a dream with no other supporting evidence. Intuitively, it seems the individual does not have knowledge despite the belief potentially happening to be true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some form of reliable connection to the truth that is accessible to the individual. This principle together with the scenario described suggests knowledge requires more than mere belief in a true proposition without accessible justification.**
