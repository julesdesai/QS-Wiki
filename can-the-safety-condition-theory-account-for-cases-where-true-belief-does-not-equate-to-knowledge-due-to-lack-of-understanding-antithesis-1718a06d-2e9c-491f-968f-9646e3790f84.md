# Can the Safety Condition Theory account for cases where true belief does not equate to knowledge due to lack of understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1718a06d-2e9c-491f-968f-9646e3790f84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a student correctly answers a question on an exam by repeating what they heard without understanding the material. Intuitively, we would judge that the student does not have knowledge because they lack understanding.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding is necessary for knowledge. According to this principle, merely having a true belief is insufficient for knowledge if there is no understanding.**
