# Can contextualism accommodate the possibility of universal truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2c18cdc-44f8-4008-8d41-34034a623c05
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a proposition is universally true, regardless of context. Intuitively, we either have to say this proposition is knowledge in all contexts, or it is not universally true. If we say it's knowledge in all contexts, this undermines the relativity of contextualism. If we say it's not universally true, this contradicts its universal nature.**, **A plausible principle is that universal truths remain constant across all contexts. This principle, when considered alongside contextualism, suggests an incompatibility with certain truths.**
