# Can knowledge be compatible with intellectual mistakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9f11a2f-f95c-470b-a43f-616bcbd92851
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone reaches a true belief through faulty reasoning or intellectual mistakes. Intuitively, we would hesitate to attribute knowledge in this scenario, as genuine knowledge is expected to arise from sound reasoning.**, **Knowledge is true belief arising from intellectual virtues. If a belief arises from intellectual mistakes, it defies the expectation of being rooted in virtue, questioning its status as knowledge.**
