# Can misunderstanding lead to apparent knowledge without true belief?

**Node ID:** 0be2786c-3742-4416-a72a-53e8e928c883
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge with Necessary Truths Provision Theory](knowledge-with-necessary-truths-provision-theory-synthesis-38cee6c1-34a3-4668-9bf4-0afe9824287c.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone claims they know "the Earth is flat" by misunderstanding geographic evidence. They simultaneously disbelieve "the Earth is round," a necessary truth related to their mistake.**, **In such cases, a person can misconstrue a necessary truth yet claim understanding and knowledge despite having a false belief, revealing mismatches between belief and knowledge.**
