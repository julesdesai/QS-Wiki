# Do beliefs formed by unreliable processes count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 192d1a78-b8ea-419a-84ac-176f503dc347
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-ebb76c32-727a-4eba-8f42-21c2ee3c5622.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief through an unreliable process, such as a faulty memory, but by chance this belief is true. Intuitively, the unreliability of the process indicates a lack of knowledge despite the belief's truth.**
