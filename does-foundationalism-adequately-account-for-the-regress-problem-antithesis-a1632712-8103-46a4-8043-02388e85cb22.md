# Does foundationalism adequately account for the regress problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a1632712-8103-46a4-8043-02388e85cb22
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Knowledge Summary](foundationalist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-d6291bb5-6e86-4606-a555-64e99394a6d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a belief that supposedly rests on a foundational belief, which faces questions of its own. Intuitively, the foundational belief seems unable to halt an infinite regress of questioning.**, **There is a plausible principle that any explanation of justified beliefs must provide a satisfactory resolution to the regress problem.**
