# Is the idea of defeaters compatible with a fallibilist view of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7a994cb-a5ca-44e2-bc46-2287b4642b27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which people hold fallible knowledge based on good evidence that could be overturned by new discoveries. Intuitively, people accept fallible knowledge despite the potential presence of defeaters.**, **Fallibilism, the view that knowledge doesn't require certainty, challenges the need for beliefs to be free of defeaters to count as knowledge.**, **As a requirement for knowledge, the focus on defeaters may conflict with a fallibilist approach that acknowledges the potential for new evidence.**
