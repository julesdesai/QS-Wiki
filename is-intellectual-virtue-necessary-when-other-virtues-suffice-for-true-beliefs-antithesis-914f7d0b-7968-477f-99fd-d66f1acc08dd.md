# Is intellectual virtue necessary when other virtues suffice for true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 914f7d0b-7968-477f-99fd-d66f1acc08dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where an individual exhibits moral virtues such as honesty and diligence leading to the acquisition of a true belief. In this case, we might wonder if intellectual virtues are necessary when other virtues suffice for knowledge acquisition.**, **A principle could be that non-intellectual virtues can result in the acquisition of true beliefs that might be considered knowledge. This challenges the primacy of intellectual character virtues.**
