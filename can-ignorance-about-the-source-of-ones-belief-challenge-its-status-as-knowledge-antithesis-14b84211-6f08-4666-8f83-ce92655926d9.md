# Can ignorance about the source of one's belief challenge its status as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14b84211-6f08-4666-8f83-ce92655926d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person believes a proposition, it's true and safe, but they are unaware of how they came to hold this belief. Intuitively, not knowing the source of their belief challenges its status as knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can exist without some understanding of the origin of one's belief.**
