# Can false belief still lead to knowledge under context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 736998e8-0e4b-4cb5-9afc-66e9c4577676
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-4f1ad7a2-76eb-4161-89f7-f4b83097a73b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees what looks like water in a mirage and believes there is water due to the context of being in a desert. The belief is false, as it is a mirage, yet the context provided strong justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing requires a true belief, which means false beliefs, even with contextual justification, cannot amount to knowledge.**, **In the case of the desert mirage, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, highlighting the failure of contextual justification to account for the requirement of truth in knowledge.**
