# Does lack of perceptual access to microscopic entities challenge knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ccddc294-3f9d-4743-87f5-2cbae2c09624
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Strong Correspondence](direct-realism-with-strong-correspondence-synthesis-28884d49-4ced-45da-83a0-528139a02f57.md)

## Content

**There are microscopic entities, like atoms, that we do not perceive directly but infer their existence through theoretical models and indirect evidence.**, **Knowledge about these entities, despite lack of direct perceptual correspondence, poses a challenge to the view.**, **If some knowledge is gained without perceptual access, then knowledge may not wholly depend on direct perception.**
