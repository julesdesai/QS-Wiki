# Does safety confuse necessary conditions for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone is in a classroom guessing the number of beans in a jar and guesses correctly. Intuitively, most would agree that this belief, though true and safe under common guesses, does not equate to knowledge.**, **Safety requires that the method of forming the belief would not easily lead to a false belief. The guessing method challenges the robustness of safety as an all-encompassing necessary condition for knowledge.**
