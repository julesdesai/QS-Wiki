# Does a true belief obtained through guesswork constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9e09e7db-e03b-494a-b2e5-952f33de0026
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person makes a wild guess about the answer to a complex mathematical problem and happens to guess correctly. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not constitute knowledge despite the belief being true.**, **A belief derived from guesswork is an unreliable process and does not generally produce true beliefs, challenging the notion that reliability alone can attribute knowledge.**
