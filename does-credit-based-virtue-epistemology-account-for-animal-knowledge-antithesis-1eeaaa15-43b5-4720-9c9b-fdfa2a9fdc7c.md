# Does credit-based virtue epistemology account for animal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1eeaaa15-43b5-4720-9c9b-fdfa2a9fdc7c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit-based Virtue Epistemology](credit-based-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b8bc04b8-9f33-4496-9274-056c4b04b89c.md)

## Content

**Consider an animal that forms true beliefs about its environment primarily through evolutionary developed instincts. Intuitively, these beliefs might count as knowledge.**, **Animals might not possess intellectual virtues in a human-like sense, challenging the need for intellectual virtue crediting in defining knowledge.**
