# Does naturalized epistemology lead to epistemic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4ad8ac61-89d6-4d91-989d-620219a205fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two scientists come to contradictory conclusions based on different research methods. Intuitively, one might judge that not both can be said to know their respective conclusions.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification requires a standard beyond empirical practices.**, **If justification consists in processes studied by natural sciences, then it admits multiple divergent methods as equally valid, leading to potential relativism.**
