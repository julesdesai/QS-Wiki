# Can knowledge occur without intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 29891f34-5d65-4aa4-845e-7c827fdc1515
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person obtains true belief accidentally, without any exercise of intellectual virtue. In such a scenario, intuitively we might see this person as knowing the truth despite the absence of virtue.**, **There is a plausible principle that one can have knowledge through mere reliability or luck, not requiring intellectual virtues. This principle suggests that knowledge might be gained without any antecedent virtues.**
