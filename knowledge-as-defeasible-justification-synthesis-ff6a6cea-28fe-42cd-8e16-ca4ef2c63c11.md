# Knowledge as Defeasible Justification

**Parent:** [Can contradictory propositions both be "known" if believed via reliable processes?](can-contradictory-propositions-both-be-known-if-believed-via-reliable-processes-antithesis-ac98e3a3-de60-4f34-8a52-7af5631a1866.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have a defeater-free justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have a defeater-free justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**
