# Does epistemological disjunctivism risk undermining the reliability of perception as a source of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 353f99e5-f66a-46d7-9392-d9d7e204e319
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be based on reliable and consistent methods. If the epistemic status of perception is fundamentally different from that of hallucination, relying solely on perception might undermine the uniform reliability needed for solid knowledge claims.**
