# Does knowledge require more than reliable true belief, specifically in cases of misleading evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1330f1c1-c07c-4b23-9421-54b1cdc296cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable True Belief](knowledge-as-reliable-true-belief-synthesis-73884ab1-e6ad-49fe-9997-a4e027a9805c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Tom, forms a true belief that his friend is at home because he sees his friend's car parked outside. However, his friend is actually out of town but lent the car to someone else. Intuitively, we would judge that Tom does not know that his friend is at home, despite having a reliable belief based on typical circumstances.**, **A belief that can be considered reliably formed in normal cases might fail to be knowledge if formed based on misleading evidence, even when true.**
