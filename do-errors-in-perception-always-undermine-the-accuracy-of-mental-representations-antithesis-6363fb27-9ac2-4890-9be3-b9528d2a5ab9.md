# Do errors in perception always undermine the accuracy of mental representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6363fb27-9ac2-4890-9be3-b9528d2a5ab9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Representationalism](epistemic-representationalism-synthesis-c86e8f4e-5440-4a21-8e04-4337bf022fd8.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that even with perceptual errors, mental representations can still accurately reflect reality. This principle together with everyday experiences, where we often correct perceptual errors automatically, entails that minor perceptual errors do not necessarily negate knowledge.**
