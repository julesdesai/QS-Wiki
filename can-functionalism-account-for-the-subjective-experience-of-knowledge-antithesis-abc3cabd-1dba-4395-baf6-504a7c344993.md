# Can functionalism account for the subjective experience of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abc3cabd-1dba-4395-baf6-504a7c344993
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functionalism](functionalism-synthesis-2d0849d8-6d8d-49c5-822f-95e70d5e82e6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that subjective experience is essential for knowledge acquisition. This principle together with the claim that knowledge is merely about functional roles conflicts with the idea that subjective experience can be omitted.**, **Consider a scenario where two individuals have identical functional mental states but only one has conscious awareness. Intuitively, we would judge that the conscious individual has knowledge, while the other does not.**
