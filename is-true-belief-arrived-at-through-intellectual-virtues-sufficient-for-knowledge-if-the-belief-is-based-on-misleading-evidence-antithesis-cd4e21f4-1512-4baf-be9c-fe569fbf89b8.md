# Is 'true belief arrived at through intellectual virtues' sufficient for knowledge if the belief is based on misleading evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd4e21f4-1512-4baf-be9c-fe569fbf89b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Virtue Epistemology](knowledge-as-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d19acabb-013f-4278-8a52-84aea003e41a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective uses all their intellectual virtues to conclude someone is guilty, based on seemingly strong but ultimately misleading evidence, and the person happens to be guilty by coincidence.**, **A belief formed through intellectual virtues based on misleading evidence does not constitute knowledge, even if it ends up being true.**
