# Can knowledge be consistent with all possible experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39abbb9d-b785-40bc-96cc-f70378da121a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-2166f961-34d5-4334-bf8f-39dfb647eb18.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person has consistent sensory experiences of a mirage in the desert; intuitively, we would not claim they know the mirage exists.**, **It is implausible that for any proposition, there could not exist some bizarre or unlikely sensory input that appears inconsistent, challenging the idea of consistency across all experiences.**
