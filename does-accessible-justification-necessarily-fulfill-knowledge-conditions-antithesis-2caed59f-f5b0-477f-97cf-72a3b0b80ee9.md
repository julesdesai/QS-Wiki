# Does accessible justification necessarily fulfill knowledge conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2caed59f-f5b0-477f-97cf-72a3b0b80ee9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Access Internalism](access-internalism-synthesis-1ebb0a7e-8cd6-40ff-bf0f-a16161232f85.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has accessible justification for a false belief. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person has knowledge, which is implausible because the belief is false, or that accessible justification alone is insufficient for knowledge.**, **Having accessible justification for a belief, when the belief is false, indicates that such justification isn't sufficient to constitute knowledge.**
