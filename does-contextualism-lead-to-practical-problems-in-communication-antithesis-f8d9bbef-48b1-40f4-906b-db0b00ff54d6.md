# Does Contextualism lead to practical problems in communication?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8d9bbef-48b1-40f4-906b-db0b00ff54d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**In a case where individuals from different contexts communicate about knowledge claims, their differing standards for justification cause misunderstandings.**, **A plausible principle is that shared communication requires a common understanding of terms like “know.”**, **If communication regularly fails due to context-dependent standards, this suggests a practical problem with the contextualist theory of knowledge.**
