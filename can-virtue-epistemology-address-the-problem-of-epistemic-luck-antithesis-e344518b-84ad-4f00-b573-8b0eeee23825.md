# Can virtue epistemology address the problem of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e344518b-84ad-4f00-b573-8b0eeee23825
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual proficiently solves a complex equation but their calculator, through an error, produces the correct result; intuitively, they lack knowledge of the result.**, **A belief that is true due to factors beyond the control of any agent's virtue challenges the claim that intellectual virtue suffices for knowledge.**, **If knowledge requires a cognitive ability to be credited, then any intervening luck implies that the truth cannot solely be credited to cognitive abilities.**
