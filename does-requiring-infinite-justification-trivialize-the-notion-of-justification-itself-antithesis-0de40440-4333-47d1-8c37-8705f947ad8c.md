# Does requiring infinite justification trivialize the notion of justification itself?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0de40440-4333-47d1-8c37-8705f947ad8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Justification is typically understood as a means of providing support sufficient to lend credibility or acceptance to a belief.**, **By making justification an infinitely demanding process, the special status of 'justification' as attainable seems undermined.**, **If justification is forever out of reach, claiming a belief is justified or unjustified loses practical meaning.**
