# Does practical encroachment lead to inconsistent knowledge standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3443156c-8591-4d6e-b850-6c7d9bba0717
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Theory](pragmatic-encroachment-theory-synthesis-66e6d98a-2899-4b80-b05b-15066f046ddd.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which two individuals, A and B, have identical evidence for believing a proposition, but A faces higher stakes while B faces lower stakes regarding the truth of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not vary based on arbitrary external factors unrelated to the truth or evidence.**, **Different practical stakes for A and B suggest differing standards of knowledge despite the same evidence, which seems inconsistent.**
