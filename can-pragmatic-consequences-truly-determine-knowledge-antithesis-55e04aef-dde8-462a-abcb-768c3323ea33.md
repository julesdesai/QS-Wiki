# Can pragmatic consequences truly determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55e04aef-dde8-462a-abcb-768c3323ea33
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition that has practical consequences supporting its truth, but this person's belief arises from unreliable cognitive processes such as guesswork. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks genuine knowledge despite the practical consequences.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not depend solely on the practical consequences of a belief but also on the origin or reliability of the belief. This principle suggests that practical consequences are not a sufficient condition for knowledge.**
