# What about innate knowledge that seems present without perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b76eabd-93d6-45ac-bda1-f035004db77b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism Summary](constructivism-summary-synthesis-38220637-7d58-4a33-978f-6de4b5b7771f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that some aspects of human cognition and knowledge are innate, as evidenced by infants' abilities to recognize basic numerical, spatial, and temporal concepts without extensive prior perceptual experiences. This principle, together with constructivism, entails a tension because constructivism emphasizes knowledge arising from perceptual experiences that infants have not had.**
