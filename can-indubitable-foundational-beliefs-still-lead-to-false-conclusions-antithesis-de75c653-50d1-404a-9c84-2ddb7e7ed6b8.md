# Can indubitable foundational beliefs still lead to false conclusions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** de75c653-50d1-404a-9c84-2ddb7e7ed6b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds indubitable foundational beliefs due to a malfunction in cognitive processes, resulting in a false conclusion, like an optical illusion convincing them that a stick in water is bent. Intuitively, we would judge that the conclusion does not qualify as knowledge since it is false.**, **It is implausible to maintain that indubitable foundational beliefs suffice for knowledge if cognitive processes are corrupted or misleading, even if only aiming at truth.**
