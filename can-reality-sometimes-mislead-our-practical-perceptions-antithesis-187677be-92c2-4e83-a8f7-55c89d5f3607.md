# Can reality sometimes mislead our practical perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 187677be-92c2-4e83-a8f7-55c89d5f3607
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person perceives a broken clock showing the correct time and believes it’s accurate for daily planning. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person's belief corresponds to reality or not. If we say the belief corresponds, then it conflicts with the clock's unreliability. If we say it does not correspond, then useful perceptions may not offer genuine knowledge.**
