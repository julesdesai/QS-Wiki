# Does practical failure affect intellectual virtue and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43190cb2-d999-49ef-9872-5fc97483c4ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b21afb01-928a-4a07-bd62-53cb05c919ec.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtues extend to successful application of knowledge. A belief can be true and arise from intellectual virtues but lead to practical failures, challenging its status as knowledge.**, **Suppose a person has beliefs formed through intellectual virtues that consistently mismatch practical circumstances. Intuitively, this undermines claims to knowledge as successful application is a relevant condition.**
