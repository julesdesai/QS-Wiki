# Can beliefs be justified knowledge if they rely on false evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a008d8d5-dcbe-4309-b7bc-a327cbd4790e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Defeaters Theory of Knowledge](no-defeaters-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-0be8e529-9437-4b4e-96a3-c585180c1210.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition because they have compelling evidence that later turns out to be false. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the proposition even if it is true.**, **Knowledge requires both true justification and true evidence. If the evidence turns out to be false, it cannot serve as a basis for knowledge.**
