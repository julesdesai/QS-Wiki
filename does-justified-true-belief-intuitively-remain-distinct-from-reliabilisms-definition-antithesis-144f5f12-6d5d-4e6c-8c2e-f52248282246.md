# Does justified true belief intuitively remain distinct from reliabilism's definition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 144f5f12-6d5d-4e6c-8c2e-f52248282246
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person correctly predicts the stock market’s surge using poorly substantiated insider tips, gaining accurate but unjustified beliefs. Intuitively, such cases do not fit our understanding of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that stresses the necessity of adequately justified beliefs for knowledge. This principle always highlights a significant gap when when juxtaposed with reliabilism's justification-dismissive stance.**
