# How does the theory handle unreliable environments for cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22e85dfb-db31-400f-b1f8-0e2781d6d6dc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-eb8cad20-b2ac-4ebe-8b63-c23141145814.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's cognitive faculties function properly, but they are in an environment where these faculties are unreliable. Intuitively, we would judge that they have less knowledge than in a reliable environment.**, **A counterexample where the environment, not the faculties, affects the reliability of true beliefs challenges the idea that merely proper functioning faculties assure knowledge.**
