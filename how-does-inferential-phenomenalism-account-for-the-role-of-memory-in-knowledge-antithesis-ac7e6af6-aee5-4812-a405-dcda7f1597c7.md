# How does inferential phenomenalism account for the role of memory in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac7e6af6-aee5-4812-a405-dcda7f1597c7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person remembers a past event accurately but has no current perceptual experiences of the event. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows about the event.**, **It is implausible that inferential phenomenalism, which relies heavily on current perceptual experiences, can fully account for the knowledge derived from memory.**, **Knowledge about past events cannot be solely based on current perceptual experiences and inferences drawn from them.**
