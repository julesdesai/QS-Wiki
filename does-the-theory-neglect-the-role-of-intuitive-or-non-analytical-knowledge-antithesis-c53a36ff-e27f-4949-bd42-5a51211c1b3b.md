# Does the theory neglect the role of intuitive or non-analytical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c53a36ff-e27f-4949-bd42-5a51211c1b3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-5db4d183-a5a9-469a-b4ff-d831c6a59a11.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an experienced chess player intuitively knows the best move without analytically considering each opponent's potential moves. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the player has knowledge due to expertise, even if alternatives are not consciously ruled out.**, **Skills-based or tacit knowledge may not fit neatly into the theory's framework yet still seem to encompass genuine knowledge.**
