# Is justification linked too weakly to safety in the theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c10297bf-756d-45e0-9446-6970909eea92
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should be sufficiently strong to rule out false beliefs in nearby possible worlds. This principle together with instances where justified true belief occurs without proximity of false belief suggests that safety is redundant if strong justification alone preserves true belief across worlds.**, **It is implausible that merely having a justified true belief requires ensuring truth in every nearby world, as cases exist where knowledge seems present despite not meeting such conditions.**
