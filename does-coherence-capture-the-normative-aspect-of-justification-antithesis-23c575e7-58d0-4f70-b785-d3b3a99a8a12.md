# Does coherence capture the normative aspect of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23c575e7-58d0-4f70-b785-d3b3a99a8a12
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Justified Belief](coherentism-with-justified-belief-synthesis-01e10c3b-52a8-4097-b02c-3f010fc56355.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification requires some normative standard beyond internal coherence, such as truth or evidence.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has a coherent and consistent set of beliefs about moral actions that are negatively viewed by society. Intuitively, coherence alone does not justify these moral beliefs.**
