# Can invariantist contextualism make sense of disagreement over knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c65903a6-eb2b-414a-9557-4cecaa568a6e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Contextualism](invariantist-contextualism-synthesis-72e0f6e6-e984-47a7-9872-225586edec54.md)

## Content

**In a situation where two people disagree on whether a proposition is known, intuitively, such disagreement appears meaningful and significant.**, **Invariantist contextualism claims that the standards for knowing a proposition are fixed and do not vary with different contexts.**, **This view may inadequately capture the significance of such disagreements, which often seem contextually grounded.**
