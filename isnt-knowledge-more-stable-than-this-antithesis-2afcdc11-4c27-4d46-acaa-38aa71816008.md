# Isn't knowledge more stable than this?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2afcdc11-4c27-4d46-acaa-38aa71816008
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person in a high-stakes exam believes they know an answer and in a low-stakes quiz believe they do not. Intuitively, if both situations involve the same evidence, we ought to say that their knowledge doesn't vary with the stakes.**, **There is a plausible principle that possession of evidence, not context, should primarily determine knowledge.**
