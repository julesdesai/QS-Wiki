# Can reliable processes fail to yield justification if unknown to the knower?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e35e132e-24ef-42f8-83f1-2be983dd25ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief through a reliable process that the person does not understand or know about. Intuitively, it seems the person is not justified in their belief if they are unaware of the reliability of the process.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some understanding or awareness of the process by which one's belief is justified.**
