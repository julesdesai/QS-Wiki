# Do stake-based knowledge standards lead to skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d42010d-d5f1-4ba0-b95c-ce86b2ba56ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Summary](pragmatic-encroachment-summary-synthesis-e897bf05-5a21-418d-ae35-b563f51919c6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which individuals in high-stakes situations must double-check basic arithmetic due to increased stakes, compared to low-stakes contexts where their calculations are trusted without issue. Intuitively, this could cause an endless regression of second-guessing knowledge claims.**, **It is implausible that a shift in practical stakes should prompt systematic skepticism about the knowledge that one already confidently possesses.**
