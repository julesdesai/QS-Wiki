# Does contextualism adequately address the issue of disagreement in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad227bdf-bade-4b08-af15-d58b93e78718
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where two people, observing the same evidence, disagree on whether they know a particular fact due to different contextual standards. Intuitively, such persistent disagreements suggest that context-sensitivity fails to resolve disputes of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a robust theory of knowledge would be able to help adjudicate disagreements rather than exacerbate them due to varying contexts. This principle along with enduring disagreement suggests a limitation of contextualism.**
