# Does the veridical experience truly depend solely on the presence of reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c3885ccf-1667-482f-b322-85e05fd28705
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-8f6e7bad-fa5f-47b7-8d7c-d5f7ea4e2a2a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately perceives a red apple and another in which the person hallucinates a red apple in an apple-less room. Intuitively, if the perceptual experience in both cases is subjectively indistinguishable to the person, then the presence of the apple in reality does not solely account for the nature of perceptual experience.**, **It is implausible that two indistinguishable perceptual experiences differ merely in virtue of one having an actual object present while the other does not, since the subjective experience is identical.**
