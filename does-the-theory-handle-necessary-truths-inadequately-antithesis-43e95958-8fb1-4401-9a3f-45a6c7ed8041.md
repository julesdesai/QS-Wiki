# Does the theory handle necessary truths inadequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43e95958-8fb1-4401-9a3f-45a6c7ed8041
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3749df9e-e568-414c-88ec-cc6b23580530.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a necessary truth, a truth that could not be false. Intuitively, the condition of belief-tracking seems irrelevant, because the truth condition could never fail.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of necessary truths doesn't require the possibility of these truths being false, which challenges the relevance of the truth-tracking condition.**
