# Can sufficient knowledge be obtained without a psychological state of understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc116558-340a-455a-a1a2-dcbd36fd8461
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a student memorizes answers to a math test without comprehending the underlying mathematical concepts. Although they can produce correct responses that match facts, they lack understanding of the subject. Intuitively, this memorization doesn't qualify as genuine mathematical knowledge.**, **A belief resulting in true propositions that lacks a foundation of comprehensive understanding can seem inadequate for knowledge, questioning the role of psychological recognition in knowing.**
