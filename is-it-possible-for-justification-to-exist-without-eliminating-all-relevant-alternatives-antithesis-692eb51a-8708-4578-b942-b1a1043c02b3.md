# Is it possible for justification to exist without eliminating all relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 692eb51a-8708-4578-b942-b1a1043c02b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person Q justifies their belief that their flight departs at 6 PM by checking the airline's website. Intuitively, Q is justified even if they have not eliminated the alternatives like the website being hacked or outdated**, **There is a plausible principle that justification can exist even if not all imaginable relevant alternatives are eliminated.**
