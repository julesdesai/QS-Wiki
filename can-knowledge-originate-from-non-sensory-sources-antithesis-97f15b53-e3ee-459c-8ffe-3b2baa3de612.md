# Can knowledge originate from non-sensory sources?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 97f15b53-e3ee-459c-8ffe-3b2baa3de612
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empiricism Summary](empiricism-summary-synthesis-4e15833e-fa6c-416c-a78e-6563a82d7628.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that people can have knowledge of mathematical truths, such as the proposition "2+2=4". This principle together with the claim that mathematical truths do not originate from sensory experience entails that knowledge can originate from non-sensory sources.**, **A person can grasp mathematical truths through reasoning and without sensory experience, suggesting knowledge does not always stem from sensory data.**
