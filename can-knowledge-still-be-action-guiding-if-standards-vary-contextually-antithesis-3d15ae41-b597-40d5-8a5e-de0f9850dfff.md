# Can knowledge still be action-guiding if standards vary contextually?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d15ae41-b597-40d5-8a5e-de0f9850dfff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Consider a practical scenario where an individual needs to act on their knowledge, but the standards for knowing the relevant proposition are in flux due to changing contextual conditions. Intuitively, we might judge that the variability makes it difficult for knowledge to consistently guide action.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should reliably guide action. This principle together with contextually variable standards suggests that knowledge may lose its guiding role if standards change unpredictably.**
