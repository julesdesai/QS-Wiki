# Can external factors undermine even well-supported beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fd75333-214e-4c55-a56e-f4e772a21290
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**Suppose there are external factors, like social influence, which affect a person's belief formation despite robust evidence. Intuitively, this belief can still be knowledge if truthful.**, **There is a plausible principle maintaining that knowledge can withstand extrinsic pressures if the intrinsic support remains evidentially strong.**
