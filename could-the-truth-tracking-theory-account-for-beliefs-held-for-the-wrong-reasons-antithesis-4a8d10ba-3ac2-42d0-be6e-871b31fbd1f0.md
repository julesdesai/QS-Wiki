# Could the Truth-Tracking Theory account for beliefs held for the wrong reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4a8d10ba-3ac2-42d0-be6e-871b31fbd1f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-dfd5c5a1-144e-4c90-a9f0-b64275b800c5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where person Q believes a true proposition for poor or irrelevant reasons, such as believing it will rain because they have a lucky coin, and it does rain. Although Q's belief is true and would likely track the truth across nearby possible worlds, intuitively Q's belief seems accidental and not genuine knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge should exclude beliefs based on irrelevant or erroneous reasoning even if the truth conditions are met. This principle challenges Nozick's criteria by indicating that causation and justification are crucial in knowledge, which is not addressed in mere truth-tracking.**, **It is implausible that knowledge could encompass beliefs that seem lucky or coincidental, as this counter-intuitively undermines the epistemic value generally associated with genuine knowledge.**
