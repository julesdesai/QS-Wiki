# Can the context-sensitivity of relevance affect the standards for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ed87573-e434-4b33-9a1e-f04fc82c8552
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**The context influences which alternatives are relevant, and this could imply variability in the standards for knowing, since different contexts may involve different criteria for assessing knowledge.**, **In a case where two people are in different contexts with different relevant alternatives, intuitively it would seem strange for them to meet different standards for knowing the same proposition.**
