# Are slightly varying standards adequate for complex knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4be02f02-5f02-41da-bf1f-201e0aaa3ea0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-550a9ee8-c8d3-41b5-bd1e-c079e9d505d4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where intricate scientific knowledge is applied to a legal context, needing high precision. Intuitively, slightly varying standards are inadequate for such complexity, necessitating major standard adjustments.**, **It is implausible that minor context adjustments suffice for all forms of knowledge, particularly in highly technical or sensitive fields.**
