# Does understanding alone truly constitute knowledge without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d440473-4ac7-445e-a02d-61797d2c0b77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Belief Necessary View](no-belief-necessary-view-synthesis-81f9ccf2-9484-47e2-8ae4-3b9845798b1d.md)

## Content

**True understanding might imply a form of belief because understanding exhibits a substantial alignment with the true nature of the thing understood.**, **Understanding without belief could be seen as merely a form of cognitive mimicry rather than actual knowledge.**, **If understanding alone equated to knowledge, it would be possible to "understand" false propositions without believing them, contradicting the necessity of truth in knowledge.**
