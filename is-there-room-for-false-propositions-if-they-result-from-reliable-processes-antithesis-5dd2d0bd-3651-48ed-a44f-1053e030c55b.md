# Is there room for false propositions if they result from reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5dd2d0bd-3651-48ed-a44f-1053e030c55b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-383ba069-e0f1-4291-bdc7-eacefc701040.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person generally uses a reliable weather app but occasionally receives incorrect forecasts still trusting the app. Intuitively, reliance on normally reliable processes might lead to believing false propositions. Thus, reliability as a blanket requirement for knowledge may not guide us in evaluating false beliefs.**, **A reliable process can sometimes produce false beliefs, leading us to question whether reliability alone is sufficient for ensuring all beliefs formed qualify as knowledge.**
