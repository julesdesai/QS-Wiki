# Is contextual justification sufficient for knowledge in every instance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c54192c-60f7-487e-b2fb-57a8d71b4d9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism on Epistemic Justification](contextualism-on-epistemic-justification-synthesis-c8f3ec75-75e9-400a-9969-d95699e610cc.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people with the same belief in different contexts have varying degrees of justification; it seems problematic to claim one has knowledge while the other does not just based on context.**, **In cases where justification varies drastically due to context, it is implausible that knowledge can be consistently attributed.**
