# Can hallucinations provide some form of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eff495f1-05c9-4c50-af9d-a98c3c4744d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-e62747dd-0f26-4379-9406-3d7f6fb883b9.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone hallucinates a scenario which coincidentally matches reality, learning something true by sheer accident. Intuitively, this person gains knowledge despite hallucinating.**, **The claim that hallucinations cannot provide knowledge because they lack direct connection is challenged by such accidental truths. This demonstrates that hallucinations can sometimes align with reality, suggesting a possible knowledge source.**
