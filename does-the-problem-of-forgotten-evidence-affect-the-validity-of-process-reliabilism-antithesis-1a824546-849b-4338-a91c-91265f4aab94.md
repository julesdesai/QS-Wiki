# Does the problem of forgotten evidence affect the validity of Process Reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a824546-849b-4338-a91c-91265f4aab94
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-54c291ff-d1f5-45c3-8197-657cbffa4347.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who believes a scientific fact, originally learned from a reliable source, but they have since forgotten that source while retaining the belief. Intuitively, the belief remains justified due to initial reliable formation, despite lacking present access to evidence.**, **A plausible principle is that justification should relate to the current cognitive state and accessible evidence, contradicting scenarios where Process Reliabilism maintains justification via inaccessible past processes.**
