# Is it possible for knowledge to arise from imperfect virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bfb1a4c0-f923-4a86-80c6-00f316cebb3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person relies on an imperfect virtue, one that sporadically leads to truth, like guessing correctly occasionally. Intuitively, we would doubt that knowledge can arise from such hit-or-miss virtues.**, **It is implausible that inconsistent traits qualify as intellectual virtues that reliably lead to knowledge. This raises doubts about whether imperfectly reliable virtues fulfill the criteria suggested by virtue epistemology.**
