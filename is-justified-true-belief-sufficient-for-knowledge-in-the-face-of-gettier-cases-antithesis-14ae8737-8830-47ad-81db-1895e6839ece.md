# Is justified true belief sufficient for knowledge in the face of Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14ae8737-8830-47ad-81db-1895e6839ece
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Communicable Justification](foundationalism-with-communicable-justification-synthesis-8dbcdd2b-eb8b-4593-a7ca-0bfc89d9258b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes that Jones owns a Ford because they see him driving a Ford, and therefore believes that either Jones owns a Ford or Brown is in Barcelona. Unbeknownst to them, Jones has rented the car. However, it is true that Brown is in Barcelona. Intuitively, we might still hesitate to say this person truly knows that either Jones owns a Ford or Brown is in Barcelona.**, **A justified true belief does not necessarily constitute knowledge if luck plays a role in the belief being true. Gettier cases illustrate how individuals can have justified true beliefs that are not knowledge due to the role of luck.**
