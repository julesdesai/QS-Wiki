## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person's belief aligns with probabilistic reasoning when they possess the capability to engage in such reasoning**, **For a person to know a proposition through testimony, it is sufficient for them to believe the proposition from a credible source without engaging in explicit probabilistic reasoning**, **The default position is to trust credible testimony unless there is evidence to doubt its reliability**
