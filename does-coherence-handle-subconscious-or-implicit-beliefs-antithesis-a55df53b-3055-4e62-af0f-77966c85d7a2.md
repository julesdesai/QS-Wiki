# Does coherence handle subconscious or implicit beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a55df53b-3055-4e62-af0f-77966c85d7a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-4ac849ed-bd0f-4658-91df-2567162ccdab.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has subconscious beliefs that influence their decisions but are not part of the explicitly coherent system of beliefs. Intuitively, these beliefs still seem significant for knowledge**, **It is implausible that coherence in explicit beliefs alone can adequately account for knowledge if subconscious elements are ignored**
