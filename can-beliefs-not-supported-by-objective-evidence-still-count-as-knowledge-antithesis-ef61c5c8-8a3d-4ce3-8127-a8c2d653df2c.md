# Can beliefs not supported by objective evidence still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef61c5c8-8a3d-4ce3-8127-a8c2d653df2c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism with Objective Evidence](evidentialism-with-objective-evidence-synthesis-54d609bf-9930-4ddb-a0d6-5d54abfff05d.md)

## Content

**A person may intuitively be said to know a fact after directly perceiving it, even if the evidence is not objective or publicly available.**, **Consider a scenario where someone knows the time by looking at a functioning clock, and intuitively we would say they have knowledge even if they did not check the mechanism to prove objective evidence.**
