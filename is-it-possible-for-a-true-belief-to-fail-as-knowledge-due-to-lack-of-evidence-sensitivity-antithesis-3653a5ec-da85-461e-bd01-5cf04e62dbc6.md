# Is it possible for a true belief to fail as knowledge due to lack of evidence sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3653a5ec-da85-461e-bd01-5cf04e62dbc6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Evidence Sensitivity](process-reliabilism-with-evidence-sensitivity-synthesis-e3e15d29-f0bc-4d87-b0aa-fbeab510c604.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where an individual has a true belief formed without considering available evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief does not count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires belief formation be responsive to available evidence. This principle, together with the failure to respond to available evidence, entails the belief is not knowledge.**
