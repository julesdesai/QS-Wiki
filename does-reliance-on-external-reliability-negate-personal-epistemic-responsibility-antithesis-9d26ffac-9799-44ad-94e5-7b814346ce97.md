# Does reliance on external reliability negate personal epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d26ffac-9799-44ad-94e5-7b814346ce97
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-3f203114-9a4a-4d29-9451-dfb5538c3c76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person accesses information through unreliable sources but usually ends up being correct. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have epistemic responsibility for their knowledge due to the unreliability of the sources.**, **There is a plausible principle that personal epistemic responsibility is a core aspect of justified knowledge. This principle together with the lack of reliability by the individual sources entails the person is not epistemically responsible despite external reliability.**
