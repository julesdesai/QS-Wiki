# How does contextualism handle peer disagreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c23c2eaa-d684-4858-aea9-a2f4e0eac084
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**Two equally informed peers may disagree because of different contextual justification standards.**, **Contextualism implies both positions could be knowledge if based on context-specific standards.**, **This seems counterintuitive, as peer disagreement suggests an error on at least one side.**
