# Does phenomenalism contradict causal interaction?

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism suggests that physical objects are logical constructions of sensory experiences.](phenomenalism-suggests-that-physical-objects-are-logical-constructions-of-sensory-experiences-synthesis-70c8baf6-067c-48d6-b521-64b5ff284db6.md)

## Content

**Causal interaction suggests that objects can act on each other in ways independent of human perception. Intuitively, we might believe this implies an underlying reality not entirely dependent on sensory experience.**, **Consider a billiard ball hitting another ball on a table when no one is present. The assumption is that the causal interaction is real, irrespective of observation or direct sensory experience.**
