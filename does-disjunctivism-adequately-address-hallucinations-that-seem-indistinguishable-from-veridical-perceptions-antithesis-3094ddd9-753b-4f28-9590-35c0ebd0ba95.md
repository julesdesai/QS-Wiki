# Does disjunctivism adequately address hallucinations that seem indistinguishable from veridical perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3094ddd9-753b-4f28-9590-35c0ebd0ba95
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-ef0ab88b-769a-4e6f-afc6-1fb31c926a76.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a hallucination perfectly mimics a real object to an observer. Intuitively, the observer might have an experience indistinguishable in nature from one of veridical perception.**, **There is a plausible principle that if two states are indistinguishable to an observer, they should be treated equivalently in terms of awareness. The claim that hallucinations and veridical perceptions are distinct threats this principle.**, **It is implausible that individuals can always discern types of perceptual experiences when presented with indistinguishable scenarios, challenging the idea that direct awareness is entirely clear-cut.**
