# Can a person lack knowledge if a belief is true and they are unaware of the process's reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23d1a87f-c190-4753-a791-b3304ac0dac3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a proposition based on a gut feeling and later discovers the belief was true due to a reliable subconscious process. Intuitively, we might judge that knowledge was lacking due to the person's ignorance of the process's reliability.**, **The reliability of the belief-forming process is central to attributing knowledge, yet here the person is unaware of its reliability, calling into question the attribution of knowledge.**
