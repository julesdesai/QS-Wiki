# Can a morally dubious practical success justify a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d4c1976-ba04-4a73-933b-9c10bb72c02a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherentism](pragmatic-coherentism-synthesis-37d6c6a9-68e2-4800-8f97-9338457a8af1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief that lying often leads to practical success. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the belief's justification is questionable from a moral standpoint.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief's justification should not depend solely on practical outcomes if those outcomes violate moral or ethical norms. This principle together with a practical success that involves unethical behavior challenges the notion that mere practical success can justify a belief.**
