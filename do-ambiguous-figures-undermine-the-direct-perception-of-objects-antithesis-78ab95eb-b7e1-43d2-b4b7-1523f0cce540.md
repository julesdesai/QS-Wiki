# Do ambiguous figures undermine the direct perception of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 78ab95eb-b7e1-43d2-b4b7-1523f0cce540
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a figure that can be seen as either a duck or a rabbit, depending on perceptual focus. Intuitively, the perception of the object flips without any change in the external object itself.**, **Ambiguous figures reveal that perception is subject to shifts without alterations in external stimulation.**, **Such cases challenge the notion that perception directly reveals stable properties of external objects.**
