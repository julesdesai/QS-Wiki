# Does the reliability of a process guarantee belief rather than mere true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b169d7d1-1537-46b3-8c84-47487de05f5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Externalism Summary](reliabilist-externalism-summary-synthesis-3207924c-3d5f-4b8a-8088-8161a66f3954.md)

## Content

**A person may use an unreliable source to gain information that randomly happens to be true, like gossip that turns out accurate; such cases intuitively don't fulfill the condition of knowledge due to lack of consistency in reliability.**, **Process reliability seems vital for belief, as correct beliefs may sometimes be uninformative or lack solid grounding if reliability isn't consistently ensured.**
