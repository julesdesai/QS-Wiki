# Does contextual justification avoid the problem of arbitrariness?

**Node ID:** ef3d624a-3704-445a-b524-83af7ae5190e
**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Contextualism to Halt Infinite Regress](contextualism-to-halt-infinite-regress-synthesis-b66f7af1-99ff-4dd4-8ca6-3f96222448b6.md)

## Content

**Contextual justification implies that what counts as sufficient evidence can change arbitrarily based on context.**, **Arbitrarily changing standards risk undermining the objectivity of justification claims.**, **An objective standard would require a consistent standard of evidence, threatening the coherence of varying context-dependent standards.**
