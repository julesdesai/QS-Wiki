# Is belief always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d636ef2-5649-4084-9f86-612fa64794d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e589bc4a-d04c-4c88-94ca-6439d0b5fd61.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that someone can have knowledge even if they do not consciously hold a belief.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person acts consistently and effectively in a domain without being able to articulate or consciously hold any belief about that domain. Intuitively, we might attribute knowledge to this person despite their lack of conscious belief.**
