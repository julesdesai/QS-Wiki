# Can knowledge of external objects be genuinely indirect?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 545c80db-9687-4099-8905-b02f20e58441
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Phenomenalism](moderate-phenomenalism-synthesis-66f89d49-d085-4cff-b6cd-a9e130c91f7b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is locked in a room with no windows, relying solely on a video feed for information about the outside world. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their knowledge of the outside world is limited and possibly flawed, despite indirect perception.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge of external objects can be categorized as indirect, as such a claim would require that direct interaction with the environment is somehow less informative.**
