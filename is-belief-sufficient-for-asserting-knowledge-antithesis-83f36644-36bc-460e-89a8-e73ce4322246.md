# Is belief sufficient for asserting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83f36644-36bc-460e-89a8-e73ce4322246
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-basic-beliefs-synthesis-db3b35bf-6f35-4ce4-82b6-698bdde0b345.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes they have won the lottery based on sheer hope. Intuitively, belief alone in this context does not suffice for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that mere belief, without additional supporting factors, can equate to knowledge.**, **Consider a thought experiment where everyone believes a false proposition about historical facts. This challenges the notion that belief alone satisfies the conditions for knowledge.**
