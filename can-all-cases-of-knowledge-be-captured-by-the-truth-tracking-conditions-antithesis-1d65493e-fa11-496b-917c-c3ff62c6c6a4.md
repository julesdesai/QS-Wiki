# Can all cases of knowledge be captured by the truth-tracking conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d65493e-fa11-496b-917c-c3ff62c6c6a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P forms a belief based on reliable evidence but the belief accidentally aligns with the truth due to luck. In such a scenario, intuitively, P does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if knowledge can be undermined by luck, then truth-tracking conditions alone are insufficient. This principle, together with the scenario, entails that truth-tracking conditions do not account for all cases of knowledge.**
