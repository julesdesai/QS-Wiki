# Can knowledge exist without justification even if situations imply otherwise?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06df3c46-e469-4241-ac7b-8bd8b9d8b3d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who forms a true belief about tomorrow's weather by an unreliable source, like tarot cards, which coincidentally turns out correct. Given no reasonable basis for the belief other than luck, intuitively we might judge they lack knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that for one to have knowledge, there should be justification underpinning the belief. This principle suggests that knowledge without justification is implausible on logical grounds.**
