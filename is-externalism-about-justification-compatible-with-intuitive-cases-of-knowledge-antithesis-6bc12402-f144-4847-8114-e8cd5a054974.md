# Is externalism about justification compatible with intuitive cases of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6bc12402-f144-4847-8114-e8cd5a054974
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person gains a true belief solely from an external source they do not understand, such as a mysterious oracle. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that they do not really know the proposition intuitively, despite its truth and their belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that for something to count as knowledge, the process of its justification should be at least partially accessible and comprehensible to the person.**
