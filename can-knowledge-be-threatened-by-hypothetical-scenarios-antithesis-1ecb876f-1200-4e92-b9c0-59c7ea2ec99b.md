# Can knowledge be threatened by hypothetical scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ecb876f-1200-4e92-b9c0-59c7ea2ec99b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-abf2a878-2a24-4b04-aec1-b3f1fce56257.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is watching a high-quality nature documentary and believes they are seeing a real lion.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively they do not lose knowledge of seeing a lion if no plausible alternative scenario is actively considered.**, **The introduction of merely hypothetical and far-fetched scenarios as alternatives shouldn't always affect the knowledge status.**
