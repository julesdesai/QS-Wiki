# Is gathered evidence always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9725b61-d35c-41aa-84ca-f4240594e3c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief about a proposition based on innate cognitive faculties, like a mathematical truth they have never been taught but can intuitively understand. Intuitively, we might say this constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that innate cognitive faculties can be a source of knowledge even if external evidence is not directly accessed.**
