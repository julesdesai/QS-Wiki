# Can perceptual differences between individuals challenge the claim that perception provides direct access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 845d2db0-8bc4-4a11-b1cd-ae7d5144c3f4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people observe the same object, such as a dress, and one perceives it as blue and black while the other perceives it as white and gold. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception differs among individuals.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception provides direct access to external objects, then it should be consistent across different observers. This principle, together with the observation of differing perceptions, entails that perception may not provide direct access as claimed.**
