# Are epistemic intuitions about stakes reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7cc672a-268d-4f8d-b755-3f1aa0a58283
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that our epistemic intuitions, which vary with stakes, accurately track knowledge if standards are truly invariant.**, **Consider a scenario in which high stakes cause us to doubt whether a belief is knowledge, despite unchanged evidence and truth. Intuitively, our epistemic intuitions may not reliably reflect invariant standards.**
