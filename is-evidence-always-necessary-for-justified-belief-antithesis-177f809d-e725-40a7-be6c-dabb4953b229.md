# Is evidence always necessary for justified belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 177f809d-e725-40a7-be6c-dabb4953b229
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a deeply held moral belief that everyone has equal rights, irrespective of empirical evidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that some justified beliefs do not require empirical evidence or that such moral beliefs are not justified. If we say moral beliefs can be justified without empirical evidence, then evidence is not always necessary for justified belief.**
