# Are we justified in believing intellectual virtues suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b43e707-de5f-466f-b408-bf77f89a1ca5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that criteria for knowledge must exclude cases where beliefs are true by luck rather than through genuine understanding or reasons. This principle along with examples of true beliefs formed through virtue but true by accident questions if virtue epistemology's criteria sufficiently distinguishes knowledge from lucky guesses.**, **It is implausible that entire reliance on intellectual virtues can distinguish knowledge from true beliefs formed without intellectual understanding, suggesting that knowledge requires something beyond intellectual virtues.**
