## Content

**Consider a type of mathematical knowledge, such as knowing the truth of a mathematical theorem. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that there is knowledge without any causal link, as mathematical truths are abstract and non-causal.**, **There is a plausible principle that sees mathematical knowledge as independent of causal processes, suggesting that some knowledge need not emerge from causal linkages.**
