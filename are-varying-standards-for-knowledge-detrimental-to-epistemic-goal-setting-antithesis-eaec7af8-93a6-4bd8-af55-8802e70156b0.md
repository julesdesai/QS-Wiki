# Are varying standards for knowledge detrimental to epistemic goal setting?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eaec7af8-93a6-4bd8-af55-8802e70156b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a research group sets epistemic goals based on standards that are higher than those of everyday situations. Intuitively, the variability in standards could hinder the establishment of coherent goals.**, **It is implausible that consistent epistemic goals can be set and pursued if the standards for what constitutes knowledge are subject to frequent change according to context.**
