# Do knowledge-limiting cases based on luck challenge the role of intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98f52b08-a71a-4a33-9fd5-1da76457057c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit-based Virtue Epistemology](credit-based-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b8bc04b8-9f33-4496-9274-056c4b04b89c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief due to a lucky guess rather than intellectual virtue. We might still intuit that this is not knowledge.**, **It is plausible to argue that knowledge must exclude cases of luck, questioning whether intellectual virtue alone distinguishes knowledge from mere true belief.**
