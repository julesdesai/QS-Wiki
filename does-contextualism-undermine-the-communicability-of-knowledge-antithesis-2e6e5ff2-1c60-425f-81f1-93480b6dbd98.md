# Does contextualism undermine the communicability of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e6e5ff2-1c60-425f-81f1-93480b6dbd98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism adapts justification to situations](contextualism-adapts-justification-to-situations-synthesis-6de50ec6-d692-4487-9677-ea468fdc8bbe.md)

## Content

**Consider the principle that knowledge should be communicable and understandable across different contexts.**, **If justification and standards for belief vary depending on context, then what counts as knowledge for one person in one situation may not be considered knowledge by another in a different situation.**, **A context-dependent approach can lead to misunderstandings and disagreements about what constitutes knowledge, undermining effective communication and shared understanding.**
