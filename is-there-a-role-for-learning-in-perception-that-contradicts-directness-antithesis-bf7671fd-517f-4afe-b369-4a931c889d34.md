# Is there a role for learning in perception that contradicts directness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf7671fd-517f-4afe-b369-4a931c889d34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Ecological Direct Perception Theory](ecological-direct-perception-theory-synthesis-73031ffe-0106-4b3d-af38-bdf2623ababa.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that learning and experience shape perceptual abilities. This principle together with empirical evidence that perception can improve with experience entails that perception might involve more than direct interaction with affordances.**, **Consider a scenario in which a novice and an expert perceive a complex task differently, such as playing a musical instrument. Intuitively we would judge that the expert's superior perception is informed by internalized skill and learning rather than direct affordance detection alone.**
