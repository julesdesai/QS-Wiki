# How can disjunctivism address the problem of hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21723c20-1e33-4901-ac1a-61c279ac9e42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-19882849-157e-4dc6-b31d-66173a7c79c0.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person experiences a vivid hallucination of an apple on a table, identical in every aspect to actually seeing the apple. Intuitively, the hallucinatory state does not involve awareness of reality, yet it is phenomenally indistinguishable from genuine perception.**, **It is plausible that knowledge involves a relationship to the external world that isn't captured simply by the phenomenology of the experience. This challenges the sufficiency of perceptual experience for knowledge.**
