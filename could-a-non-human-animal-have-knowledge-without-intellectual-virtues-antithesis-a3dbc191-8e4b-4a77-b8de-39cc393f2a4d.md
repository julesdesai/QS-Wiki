# Could a non-human animal have knowledge without intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3dbc191-8e4b-4a77-b8de-39cc393f2a4d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9deda8b7-9bee-4f9f-90d0-2d2b46259731.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a homing pigeon consistently finds its way back to its nest over long distances. In such a scenario, intuitively we might consider the pigeon to have a kind of knowledge about navigation despite lacking what are typically considered intellectual virtues like reflection or reasoning.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can exist without the explicit exercise of intellectual virtues, particularly in non-human animals whose cognitive faculties do not fit neatly into the model of human intellectual virtues. This principle together with the fact that non-human animals navigate effectively without intellectual reflection challenges the idea that intellectual virtues are necessary for knowledge.**
