# Can non-epistemic factors truly influence knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0e21ca9-f930-475d-ba2f-8cfd5d356659
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that practical stakes, which are non-epistemic factors, should influence whether a belief constitutes knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where two people have identical evidence and belief for a proposition, but only one faces high practical stakes. Intuitively, both should either know or not know the proposition regardless of their practical stakes.**
