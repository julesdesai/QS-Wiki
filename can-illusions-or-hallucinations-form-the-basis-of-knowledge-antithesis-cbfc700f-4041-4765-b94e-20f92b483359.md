# Can illusions or hallucinations form the basis of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cbfc700f-4041-4765-b94e-20f92b483359
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-2365a190-e3fc-4d37-a83b-defd0eadffa2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person perceives a mirage which fits perfectly within their coherent set of perceptual experiences. Intuitively, they do not know a proposition about water being present, even though their perceptions are coherent.**, **It is implausible that knowledge could be based on illusory or hallucinatory experiences, as these do not correspond to the actual state of the external world.**
