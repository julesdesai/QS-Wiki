# How do we handle conflicting intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8e4306ce-f444-425e-8cd5-02214453d244
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has two conflicting intellectual virtues, such as carefulness and creativity, leading to different conclusions about a single proposition. Intuitively, we would judge that it's problematic to determine which belief is knowledge without additional criteria.**, **It is implausible that a person can simultaneously have knowledge of conflicting beliefs without further epistemic criteria. This calls into question whether virtue epistemology provides sufficient tools for resolving such conflicts.**
