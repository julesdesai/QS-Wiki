# Does epistemological disjunctivism adequately address the possibility of skepticism about the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98c363ce-7c14-4a1b-8ce2-1a877bb2e47b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Suppose a skeptic argues that our perceptions could always be hallucinations. Intuitively, epistemological disjunctivism, which strongly differentiates the epistemic status of perception from hallucination, appears to have difficulty in showing why all perceptions shouldn't be doubted as potential hallucinations.**
