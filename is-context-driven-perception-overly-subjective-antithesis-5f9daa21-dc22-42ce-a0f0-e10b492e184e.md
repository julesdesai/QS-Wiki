# Is context-driven perception overly subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f9daa21-dc22-42ce-a0f0-e10b492e184e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perception should yield an objective representation of the world, free from undue influence of individual context. This principle together with the variability implied by context could entail that perception becomes too subjective.**, **Consider a scenario in which two perceivers in similar environments provide contrasting reports due to differing contextual backgrounds. In such a situation, intuitively, we would judge that perception is swayed excessively by subjective elements.**
