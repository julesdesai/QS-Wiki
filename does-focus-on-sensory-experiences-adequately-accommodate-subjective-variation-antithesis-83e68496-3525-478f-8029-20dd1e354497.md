# Does focus on sensory experiences adequately accommodate subjective variation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83e68496-3525-478f-8029-20dd1e354497
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two individuals, one colorblind and one not, both experiencing a red apple. Intuitively, focusing on their sensory experiences to describe the apple leads to conflicting representations of the same object**, **It is implausible that sensory experiences alone offer an objective or shared understanding of physical objects when subjective variations alter those experiences drastically**
