# Can coherentism account for shared system coherence among different individuals?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d76db62-4d8e-4f6e-82d4-1039c5cd3723
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals have coherent belief systems, yet the systems are inconsistent with one another. Intuitively, true justification should extend beyond subjective coherence to a more universally shared reason.**, **If belief systems contradict, coherence cannot fully account for knowledge since it fails to explain whose beliefs are justified or correct. This suggests coherentism lacks a mechanism for reconciling different, yet internally cohesive, belief systems.**
