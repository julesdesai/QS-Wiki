# Do constant standards regarding knowledge truly capture its variability in practice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee1c4b43-83a4-4347-901d-a6e8700bbe62
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Interest-Relative Invariantism Summary](interest-relative-invariantism-summary-synthesis-34d6e50b-4fc4-4003-bede-59691f379a3b.md)

## Content

**Intuitively, the experience of knowledge acquisition varies significantly across scenarios with differing practical stakes, suggesting variability is inherent to understanding knowledge**, **Consider a scenario where a proposition holds different levels of importance to different people; constant standards would fail to reflect this diversity in importance**, **It is implausible that a fixed standard on the truth of a proposition could address the diverse practical needs encountered in various situations**
