# Can beliefs in isolation be true and justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3acc4387-edca-4b78-b7bb-15f63c67180e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person correctly believes an isolated fact, such as the exact time when distant but obscure astronomical events happen based on sheer luck. Intuitively, even if disconnected from a coherent belief system, such a belief can still be true and coincidentally justified by future verifications.**, **A plausible principle is that isolated truths can be justified post-facto objectively without requiring coherence in the original belief formulation.**
