# Can intellectual virtues sufficiently counteract external epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a1db197-3111-4be1-80e1-3249788815e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtues may not guard against the role of luck in cases where a belief is accidentally true.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes they have found a rare artifact because they guessed and a sudden change in weather uncovers it. Intuitively, the virtue does not account for the enormous role of luck.**
