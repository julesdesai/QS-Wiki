# What about knowing objects without successful practical engagement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b5ca6c9-0b63-4a77-83e2-778e25e2bf7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scientist infers the existence of a distant galaxy through indirect evidence such as light patterns, yet cannot practically engage with it. Intuitively, we still consider the scientist to know about the galaxy.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of distant or theoretical entities can occur without successful practical engagement. This principle suggests that not all knowledge of external objects aids practical engagement with the world.**
