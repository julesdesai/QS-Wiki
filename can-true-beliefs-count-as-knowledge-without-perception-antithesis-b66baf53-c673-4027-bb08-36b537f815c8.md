# Can true beliefs count as knowledge without perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b66baf53-c673-4027-bb08-36b537f815c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Direct Realism](pragmatic-direct-realism-synthesis-4173acef-5348-4f34-a95b-b6ba164e9e84.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a true belief about an event that they have never perceived, based purely on reliable testimony. Intuitively, we might still say that they possess knowledge of the event.**, **It is plausible that reliable testimony can lead to genuine knowledge, indicating perception is not always necessary for knowing.**
