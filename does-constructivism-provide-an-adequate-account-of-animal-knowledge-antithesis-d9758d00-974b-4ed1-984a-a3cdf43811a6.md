# Does constructivism provide an adequate account of animal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9758d00-974b-4ed1-984a-a3cdf43811a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism](constructivism-synthesis-c5cc0372-1259-4a9f-927f-8a96c3c904c0.md)

## Content

**Non-human animals seem to possess knowledge of their environment, even with limited conceptual understanding.**, **Animals remain capable of utilizing functional knowledge relative to their survival without displaying advanced cognitive construction of knowledge.**, **Constructivism posits that conceptual understanding is necessary for knowledge, which may not apply to non-human animals.**
