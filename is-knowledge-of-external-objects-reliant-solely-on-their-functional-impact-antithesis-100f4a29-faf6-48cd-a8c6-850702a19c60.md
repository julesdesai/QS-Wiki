# Is knowledge of external objects reliant solely on their functional impact?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 100f4a29-faf6-48cd-a8c6-850702a19c60
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two indistinguishable objects have identical functional impacts on perception. Intuitively, we either have to say that their identities are the same or that they have distinct identities. If we say they are the same, then such a claim may dismiss genuine distinctions between objects. If we say they have distinct identities, then merely functional impacts are insufficient for complete knowledge.**
