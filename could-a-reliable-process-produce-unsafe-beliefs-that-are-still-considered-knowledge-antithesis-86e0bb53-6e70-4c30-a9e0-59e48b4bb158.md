# Could a reliable process produce unsafe beliefs that are still considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86e0bb53-6e70-4c30-a9e0-59e48b4bb158
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8bfe37cc-0831-4bcb-9f14-728b24c81cc0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a process P that reliably produces true beliefs for an individual U about a proposition V.**, **Imagine that the process P, in nearby possible worlds, occasionally leads to false beliefs due to unusual but rare conditions.**, **A plausible principle is that reliability is a key indicator of knowledge.**, **This principle might lead us to see U’s belief as knowledge, despite its lack of safety across nearby possible worlds, thereby questioning the necessity of the safety condition.**
