# How does external world skepticism challenge the sensitivity condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df77650d-5d15-4e5c-ab29-f98bfb897833
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Sensitivity](knowledge-as-sensitivity-synthesis-e88cb550-3d2c-41d6-a550-a96af77d5cc7.md)

## Content

**Consider the skeptical scenario where all experiences are illusions, akin to the Cartesian evil demon or brains in vats thought experiments. In such cases, sensitivity to actual truth seems compromised.**, **Intuitively, skepticism highlights a gap where one might fail to dismiss a false belief under such scenarios, bringing into question the adequacy of sensitivity to assess genuine knowledge.**, **Skeptical scenarios reveal that sensitivity may falter as a conclusive measure for dismissing false beliefs, given its reliance on the reliability of empirical evidence, which skepticism undermines.**
