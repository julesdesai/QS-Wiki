# Is justification always virtue-based?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c309029-8c67-4869-ba51-d5d36f2ac366
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology: Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-1d322156-3f22-49a0-99a5-f932d24b0e66.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone relies on unreliable sources yet forms a true belief; intuitively, the belief lacks proper justification despite its truth.**, **Intellectual virtues suggest that justification should derive from reliable methods rather than reliance on chance, challenging the claim that all justifications must stem from virtues.**
