# Can knowledge arise from belief alone without virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7bff2439-eb34-4548-b3e8-d9bde6a818b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-35af9163-7530-4447-9474-ccb688222939.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes a proposition simply because they trust an authoritative figure is a case in which the belief does not stem from intellectual virtue.**, **Yet, if the authoritative figure is indeed correct, and the proposition is true, it challenges whether such belief formation can count as knowledge without virtue.**
