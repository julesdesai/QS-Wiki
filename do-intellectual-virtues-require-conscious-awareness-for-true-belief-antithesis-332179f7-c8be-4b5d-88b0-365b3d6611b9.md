# Do intellectual virtues require conscious awareness for true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 332179f7-c8be-4b5d-88b0-365b3d6611b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person subconsciously uses an intellectual virtue, like intuition, to form a true belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge the person still possesses knowledge.**, **It is plausible that some intellectual virtues, such as intuition or instinct, operate without conscious awareness yet contribute to knowledge formation.**, **Intellectual virtues may not require active consciousness if they inherently produce reliable true beliefs, challenging the necessity of awareness.**
