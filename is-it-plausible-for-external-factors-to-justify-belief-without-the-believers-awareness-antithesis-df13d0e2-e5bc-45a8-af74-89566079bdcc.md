# Is it plausible for external factors to justify belief without the believer's awareness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df13d0e2-e5bc-45a8-af74-89566079bdcc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-3f203114-9a4a-4d29-9451-dfb5538c3c76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person firmly believes they will win the lottery tomorrow based solely on a gut feeling. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not constitute a justified belief even if external factors make it likely they will win.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification requires awareness. This principle together with the intuition that we can only be responsible for beliefs we are aware of entails that external factors without our awareness do not justify belief.**
