# Does safety imply knowledge if deception plays a role?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 563d9c8d-da74-4eae-8b51-04b3e1478cf7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where someone is systematically deceived about what they think they know but, by chance, their belief happens to be true and safe from error**, **In this scenario, the perception of safety is undermined by the presence of deception, which intuitively leads us to judge that they do not possess knowledge**, **Deception undermines the trustworthiness of the belief formation, even if the belief happens to be true in nearby possible worlds**
