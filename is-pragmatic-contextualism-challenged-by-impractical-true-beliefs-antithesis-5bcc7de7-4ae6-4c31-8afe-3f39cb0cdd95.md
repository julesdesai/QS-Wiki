# Is pragmatic contextualism challenged by impractical true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5bcc7de7-4ae6-4c31-8afe-3f39cb0cdd95
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**A belief may represent knowledge if it is true and justified, even if it does not lead to practical success in any given context.**, **Some true beliefs fail to result in practical success due to extraneous variables irrelevant to the belief's validity or truth.**, **Practical success should not be the sole criterion for knowledge, as some essential truths remain knowledge despite being impractically applied.**
