# Can memory serve as a form of empirical direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 611fb460-8a00-4413-88d6-771de1113324
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone recalls a past event accurately but no longer has direct access to perceptive experience or immediate empirical evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows the past event, yet this knowledge does not align with current direct perception and empirical evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that memory, reconstructing past perceptions, can serve as a basis for knowledge independently from current empirical direct realism.**
