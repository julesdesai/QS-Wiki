# Does reliable belief formation guarantee justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c213e9f9-481c-49c5-b9b1-8da8eacfddfa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e2d53466-acc3-45a1-95cc-71c95728df76.md)

## Content

**Consider a weather forecaster whose methods have been statistically reliable even when his belief about their causal mechanisms is incorrect. Intuitively, the reliability might not translate above mere coincidence to justified belief.**, **Suppose an investor uses reliable algorithms to predict stock movements without understanding underlying economic factors. The gap between belief and justification questions reliability as an immediate link to knowledge.**
