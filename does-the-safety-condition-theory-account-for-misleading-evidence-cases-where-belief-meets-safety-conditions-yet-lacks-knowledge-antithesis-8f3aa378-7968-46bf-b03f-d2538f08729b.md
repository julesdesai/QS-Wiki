# Does the Safety Condition Theory account for misleading evidence cases where belief meets safety conditions yet lacks knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f3aa378-7968-46bf-b03f-d2538f08729b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation in which an individual has a belief that is true but based on misleading evidence; every available clue suggests this belief might be false, but the belief inadvertently turns out true. Intuitively, we might suggest that despite the accuracy, the belief isn't knowledge due to unreliable evidential support. A plausible principle indicates that beliefs drawn from such evidence should not count as knowledge due to their precarious nature.**
