# Knowledge as External True Belief

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can moral beliefs constitute knowledge contextually?](can-moral-beliefs-constitute-knowledge-contextually-antithesis-40e43228-81ee-4e32-ba50-3fae4a92c82e.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the justification for the belief involves external factors beyond the believer's immediate cognitive environment**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the justification for the belief involves external factors beyond the believer's immediate cognitive environment**
