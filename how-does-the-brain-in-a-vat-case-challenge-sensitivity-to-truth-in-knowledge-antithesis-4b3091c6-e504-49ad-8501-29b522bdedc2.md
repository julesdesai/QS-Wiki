# How does the "brain in a vat" case challenge sensitivity to truth in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b3091c6-e504-49ad-8501-29b522bdedc2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes they have hands based on visual and tactile experience, but they are actually a brain in a vat being fed false experiences. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know they have hands.**, **If the person cannot form a belief about their hands being false due to the misleading evidence, then they lack sensitivity to the truth.**, **It is implausible that the criterion of sensitivity to truth adequately distinguishes between knowledge and non-knowledge in such skeptical scenarios.**
