# Can knowledge be present without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47c3f9fe-be10-4a20-84d2-5e36596033ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person acts based on a habit or instinct, leading them to a true conclusion without actually believing anything specific about the proposition**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not actually know the proposition because they lack a belief despite the outcome being true and safe from error**, **A proposition can meet the criteria for truth and safety, yet if there is no belief held by the person, knowledge cannot be attributed**
