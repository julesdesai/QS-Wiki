# Is justification enough to ensure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a565c5f2-0ccf-4da6-ab95-ade2c3ee00ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition due to an intellectual virtue but lacks justification from a reliable process. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that they lack knowledge. **, **There is a plausible principle that having a justified belief is necessary for knowledge. This principle together with the lack of justification suggests that knowledge is not present.**
