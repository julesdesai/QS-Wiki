# Can beliefs be knowledge without an appropriate causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c79044e-68ac-4e69-aabd-bf4c5048acd8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2f0f13d0-725f-4ae5-ba67-2c976061582b.md)

## Content

**A person can have a justified true belief about a fact due to a reliable process without any direct causal connection to the fact itself.**, **In a scenario where a person deduces the time from an accurate clock that is fortuitously malfunctioning, their belief may be true and justified, but lacks a direct causal relation to the fact.**
