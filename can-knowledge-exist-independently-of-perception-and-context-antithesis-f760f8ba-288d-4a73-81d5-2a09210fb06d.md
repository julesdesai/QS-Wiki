# Can knowledge exist independently of perception and context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f760f8ba-288d-4a73-81d5-2a09210fb06d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be obtained through purely rational or logical means without reliance on perception or context.**, **A case in which a person comes to know a mathematical truth through reasoning alone without any perceptual input challenges the dependence of knowledge on perceptual experiences.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge requires the contextualization of perception, as some abstract knowledge seems independent of context.**
