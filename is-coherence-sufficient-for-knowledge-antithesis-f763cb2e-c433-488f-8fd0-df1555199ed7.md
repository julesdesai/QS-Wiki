# Is coherence sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f763cb2e-c433-488f-8fd0-df1555199ed7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-2365a190-e3fc-4d37-a83b-defd0eadffa2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a coherent set of perceptual experiences resulting from a sophisticated virtual reality simulation. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not actually know about the external world because their experiences are artificially induced.**, **There is a plausible principle that true knowledge should correspond to an external reality, rather than merely being internally coherent. This principle suggests that coherence alone is insufficient for true knowledge.**
