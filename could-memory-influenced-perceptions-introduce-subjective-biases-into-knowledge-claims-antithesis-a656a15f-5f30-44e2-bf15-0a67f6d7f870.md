# Could memory-influenced perceptions introduce subjective biases into knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a656a15f-5f30-44e2-bf15-0a67f6d7f870
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Phenomenalism Allowing Memory Role](revised-phenomenalism-allowing-memory-role-synthesis-5fb5abbe-6adb-4855-a476-25c8a91d11a9.md)

## Content

**Memory can be influenced by personal biases and emotions. This aspect together with the use of memory as a basis for perceptual knowledge implies that knowledge claims may be subjectively biased.**, **Imagine a person recalling a past social interaction where they believe they were slighted, though objective evidence might show otherwise. Intuitively, relying on such memory-influenced perceptions introduces subjectivity into their knowledge claim about the interaction.**
