# What if someone's true belief does not result from their own intellectual capabilities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c4305a0-b296-43de-8dd4-2e3028a1564a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone has a true belief but it results entirely from another person's intellectual capabilities. Intuitively, we would question whether the original person genuinely has knowledge if they did not contribute their own cognitive abilities.**, **A belief that results from another individual's intellect challenges the condition that the belief must arise from one's own intellectual capabilities to count as knowledge.**
