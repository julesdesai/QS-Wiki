# Do perceptions provide knowledge without interpretation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 117bdcc3-a27b-4fdf-be08-c95527bfee1d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Memory-Integrated Phenomenalism](memory-integrated-phenomenalism-synthesis-20b6ad46-c9b1-41b8-87a0-7619b03b72c5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where two people observe the same event but interpret it differently based on their backgrounds. Intuitively, the knowledge each person derives varies despite identical perceptions.**, **It is implausible that perceptions alone can be the basis for knowledge, as interpretation plays a crucial role in what is considered known.**
