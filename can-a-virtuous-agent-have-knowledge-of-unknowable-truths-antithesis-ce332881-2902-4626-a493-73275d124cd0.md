# Can a virtuous agent have knowledge of unknowable truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce332881-2902-4626-a493-73275d124cd0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**Consider a virtuous agent attempting to know a truth that is unknowable according to other epistemic criteria; intuitively, the agent's virtue alone should not grant knowledge of such truths.**, **There is a plausible principle that unknowable truths, by definition, cannot be known regardless of intellectual virtue or other factors.**
