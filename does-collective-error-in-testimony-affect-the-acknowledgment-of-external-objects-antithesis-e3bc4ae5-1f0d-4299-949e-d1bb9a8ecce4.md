# Does collective error in testimony affect the acknowledgment of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3bc4ae5-1f0d-4299-949e-d1bb9a8ecce4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where a large group of people mistakenly identify a weather balloon as a UFO and report it as such. Intuitively, we must say that the collective testimony fails to acknowledge the external object accurately.**, **There is a plausible principle that acknowledgment of external objects requires accurate perception. This principle together with collective error in testimony entails that shared perceptual accounts can fail to correctly acknowledge external objects.**
