# Can knowledge stem from intellectual luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95972b01-fd6a-49c9-837e-10037dd59bfa
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Virtue Epistemology](knowledge-as-virtue-epistemology-thesis-04805afc-e3a8-43d5-b0d9-d6c1e7d88e2f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based on an intellectual virtue, and the proposition is true by sheer coincidence outside the person's control. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot depend on luck. If a true belief due to luck counts as knowledge when arising from intellectual virtue, it contradicts this principle.**
