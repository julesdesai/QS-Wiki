# Does the necessity of true propositions for knowledge exclude unknowable truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15f7ae72-d13f-4320-a07d-dec6cccddd05
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Infinitism Summary](coherent-infinitism-summary-synthesis-eca6bdeb-868b-4e1e-9a63-23b9880f21ff.md)

## Content

**Some propositions may be true but inherently unknowable due to limitations in human understanding or empirical access.**, **Requiring truth for knowledge excludes the possibility of knowing certain propositions that are true but unknowable.**, **Knowledge possibly involves justified belief without the prerequisite of truth, especially for unknowable propositions.**
