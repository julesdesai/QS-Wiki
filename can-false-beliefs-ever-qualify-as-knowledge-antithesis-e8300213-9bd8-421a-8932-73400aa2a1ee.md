# Can false beliefs ever qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e8300213-9bd8-421a-8932-73400aa2a1ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Virtue and Credit Epistemology](hybrid-virtue-and-credit-epistemology-synthesis-7563744d-4993-49c2-9fb5-fa60254b49ad.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be based on false beliefs. This principle, combined with the claim that knowledge is true belief involving intellectual virtue or credit assignment, is challenged by the idea that beliefs can be false yet produced by intellectual virtues.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone holds a false belief through a process involving intellectual virtues such as careful reasoning based on misleading evidence. Intuitively, we would not classify this as knowledge because the belief is false.**, **It is implausible to categorize the holding of a false belief as a case of knowledge, even if the belief is virtuous or credit is assigned, because knowledge traditionally requires the truth.**
