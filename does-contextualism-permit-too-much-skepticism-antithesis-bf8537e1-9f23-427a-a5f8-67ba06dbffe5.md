# Does contextualism permit too much skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf8537e1-9f23-427a-a5f8-67ba06dbffe5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**Suppose we accept that context determines standards for knowing, leading to varying claims of knowledge about the same proposition. Intuitively, this variability could open the door to persistent skepticism, as claims about what one knows could always be undermined by a change in context.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be stable enough to resist easy skeptical challenges.**
