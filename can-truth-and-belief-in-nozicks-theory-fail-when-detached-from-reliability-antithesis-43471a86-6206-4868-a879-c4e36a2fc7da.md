# Can truth and belief in Nozick's theory fail when detached from reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43471a86-6206-4868-a879-c4e36a2fc7da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozickian Tracking Theory Summary](nozickian-tracking-theory-summary-synthesis-44760ad8-dc58-4f04-9662-a1508a0ab6d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes a true proposition but is largely unreliable, and the belief tracks the truth across possible worlds by chance rather than a reliable process. Intuitively, such a belief is not knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge demands the reliability of belief-forming processes. If tracking truth does not entail reliability, it might lead to beliefs that fail to be knowledge.**
