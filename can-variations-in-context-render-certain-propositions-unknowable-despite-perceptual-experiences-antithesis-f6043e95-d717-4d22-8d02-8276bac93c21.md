# Can variations in context render certain propositions unknowable despite perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6043e95-d717-4d22-8d02-8276bac93c21
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-8ade45c1-54b8-462e-aef7-204b002692b3.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a context in which perceptual experiences do not establish the truth due to misleading conditions, like illusions or hallucinations. In such a context, intuitively, it seems that knowledge of certain propositions becomes unattainable.**, **If perceptual experiences do not reliably establish truth in some contexts, then the claim that a proposition is known depends on contextual validation is threatened.**, **A case in which a person has a perceptual experience of a mirage that seems to be water, in a desert, provides a situation where perceptual experiences misleadingly establish a proposition which is not true, suggesting that context can render some propositions unknowable.**
