# Does the absence of skill undermine propositional knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d902023f-cc0e-4ab0-9c2d-9c706498de69
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person can describe the theory for solving a problem but cannot execute the solution due to lack of skill. In such a case, intuitively we would judge the person lacks a complete understanding, suggesting propositional knowledge on its own may be insufficient without skill.**, **There is a plausible principle that comprehensive knowledge involves both theoretical understanding and practical ability. This principle, together with skill being necessary, implies that skill absence may undermine propositional knowledge.**
