# How does contextualism handle ordinary disagreements about knowledge?

## Content

**Suppose two people disagree about whether a third person knows a fact, each using different contextual standards. Intuitively, we have to say that contextualism struggles to explain ordinary disagreements.**, **Disagreements about knowledge typically imply that at least one party is incorrect, which contextualism makes challenging to resolve when different contexts are invoked.**
