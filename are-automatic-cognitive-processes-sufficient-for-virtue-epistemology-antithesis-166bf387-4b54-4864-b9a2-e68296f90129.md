# Are automatic cognitive processes sufficient for virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 166bf387-4b54-4864-b9a2-e68296f90129
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-dbddd6f3-a688-44af-bb01-dd29b6f7b5c7.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which a person has a true belief because of a deeply ingrained habit rather than conscious exercise of skill. Intuitively, such beliefs might lack credibility as knowledge since they rely on automatic processes.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge requires active engagement of cognitive skills, which might be absent in habitual or automatic beliefs.**
