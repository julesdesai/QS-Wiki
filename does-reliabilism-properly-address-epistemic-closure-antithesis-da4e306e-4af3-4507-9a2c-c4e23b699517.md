# Does reliabilism properly address epistemic closure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da4e306e-4af3-4507-9a2c-c4e23b699517
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**The principle of epistemic closure suggests that if a person knows a proposition and knows that this proposition entails another, they should know the entailed proposition as well**, **Suppose a person knows a reliable source indicated the winner of a race but did not consider the reliable source also means bets on that winner will pay off**, **Reliabilism might suggest they do not know the betting outcome even if intuitively they should, questioning the closure principle**
