# Do all expressions of cognitive skills lead to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5710ea49-fb30-4baa-b04f-4c3c7861813a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d110ebcb-cebc-4b41-b013-8e3cca593b6d.md)

## Content

**A person might utilize high-level reasoning and evidence evaluation to reach a hypothesis that later proves incorrect, suggesting cognitive skills might not always lead to true belief**, **A chess player might make a logical but wrong move due to a misjudgment of the opponent’s strategy, indicating complex skill exercise does not assure truth**, **In analyzing complex literature, someone may produce a thoughtful interpretation that misrepresents the author's intentions, thus demonstrating intellectual effort not infallibly aligned with truth**
