# Can unreliable processes ever yield true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0d1fc35-4ea6-4632-9310-6be4928f0cc5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that unreliable processes cannot systematically lead to true beliefs. However, some beliefs formed through such unreliable processes do turn out to be true.**, **Suppose a person flips a coin to decide whether a complex scientific fact is true, and by chance, it coincidentally is true. Intuitively, this person could not have knowledge.**
