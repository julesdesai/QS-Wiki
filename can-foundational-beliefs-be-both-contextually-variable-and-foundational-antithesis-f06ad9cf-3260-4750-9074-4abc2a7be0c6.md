# Can foundational beliefs be both contextually variable and foundational?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f06ad9cf-3260-4750-9074-4abc2a7be0c6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**A foundational belief, by definition, should be a fundamental and invariant component of a knowledge system.**, **The variability of foundational beliefs across contexts implies these beliefs are not truly foundational, as they lack the stability required to serve as a base.**, **If foundational beliefs are context-dependent, they fail to provide the necessary fixed points for building coherent knowledge structures.**
