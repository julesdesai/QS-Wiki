# Can foundational beliefs ever truly be foundational if they are fallible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da515b02-21dc-44a7-8201-59f1a7311a59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Foundationalism](fallibilist-foundationalism-synthesis-ed3484e7-a0b3-47a4-939c-ec83c0f7bb7c.md)

## Content

**A foundational belief is supposed to be a belief that needs no further justification.**, **Consider a case where a person believes a basic sensory perception, such as seeing a red apple, but the person knows there is a chance they might be mistaken due to lighting conditions. Intuitively, we would judge that the perception needs further justification because of its fallibility.**, **If foundational beliefs are fallible, they seem to require additional justification, which contradicts their status as foundational.**
