# Is truth truly necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50091730-6d45-4b5d-ac13-ff537c50bee9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Knowledge](evidentialist-knowledge-synthesis-7bc70333-bda0-499d-9144-ee5b1e41b165.md)

## Content

**In some hypothetical cases, individuals could have beliefs so well-supported by evidence that we might be tempted to regard them as knowledge, even if the proposition turns out to be false.**, **Suppose a doctor diagnoses a rare disease based on a comprehensive set of relevant medical evidence but is later corrected. Initially, it seems reasonable to say the doctor knew the diagnosis even though it was later falsified.**
