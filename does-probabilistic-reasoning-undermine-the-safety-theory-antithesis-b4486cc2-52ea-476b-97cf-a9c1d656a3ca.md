# Does probabilistic reasoning undermine the Safety Theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4486cc2-52ea-476b-97cf-a9c1d656a3ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a belief is statistically probable to be true due to probabilistic reasoning, but there is a likelihood, albeit low, of it being false. Intuitively, the presence of any false potential challenges the label of knowledge.**, **Probabilistic reasoning can allow for safe beliefs, but it also introduces an element of chance that could arguably make such beliefs insufficiently certain to count as knowledge.**
