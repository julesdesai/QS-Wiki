## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person believes the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief arises from a practical intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, truth is not strictly necessary if cognitive success is achieved**, **A practical intellectual virtue is a character trait or faculty leading to successful action**, **Knowledge prioritizes pragmatic outcomes over mere truth**
