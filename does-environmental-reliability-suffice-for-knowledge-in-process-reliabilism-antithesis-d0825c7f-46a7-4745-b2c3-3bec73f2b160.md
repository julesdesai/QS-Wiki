# Does environmental reliability suffice for knowledge in process reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0825c7f-46a7-4745-b2c3-3bec73f2b160
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Luke, is in a matrix-like environment where his beliefs about the physical world are true by sheer simulation design, where he forms beliefs about trees based on a process that, in the real world, would be reliable. Intuitively, we would judge that Luke lacks knowledge about the trees outside his 'simulation'.**, **The process of forming beliefs in a 'normal' environment is reliable, but in this controlled environment, it yields true beliefs accidentally.**, **Knowledge relying on external reliability without factoring in the context might not account for the conditions of this perception.**
