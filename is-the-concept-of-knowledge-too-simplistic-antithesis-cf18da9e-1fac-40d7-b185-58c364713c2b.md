# Is the concept of knowledge too simplistic?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf18da9e-1fac-40d7-b185-58c364713c2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which different kinds of knowledge, like acquaintance knowledge and skill knowledge, do not fit neatly into the schema of believing a true proposition. Intuitively, we have to say that our understanding of knowledge might be too narrow.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge can be captured merely by having a justified true belief, as it fails to account for variations such as knowing 'how' versus knowing 'that'.**
