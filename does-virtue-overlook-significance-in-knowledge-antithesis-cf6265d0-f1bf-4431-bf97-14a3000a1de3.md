# Does virtue overlook significance in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf6265d0-f1bf-4431-bf97-14a3000a1de3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cfcf3dee-48b2-4ff8-855e-aba5061f4a51.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that true knowledge requires not just virtue, but also the significance or importance of the truth for the knower. This principle suggests that minor truths gained through virtue may not qualify as knowledge.**, **Consider a case in which a person believes, through intellectual virtue, a trivial proposition like the exact number of leaves on a tree. Intuitively, we might judge that while true, this belief does not constitute knowledge because it lacks significance.**
