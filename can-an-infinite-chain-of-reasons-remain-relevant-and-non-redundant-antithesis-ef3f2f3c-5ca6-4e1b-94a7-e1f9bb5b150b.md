# Can an infinite chain of reasons remain relevant and non-redundant?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef3f2f3c-5ca6-4e1b-94a7-e1f9bb5b150b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Infinitism](contextual-infinitism-synthesis-969d15cb-7035-4449-8e21-a18b5558491f.md)

## Content

**Relevance in a chain of reasons requires that each reason adds new information or support rather than repeating or relying on previous parts**, **It is implausible that an infinite chain can avoid redundancy, as repetition seems inevitable without infinite cognitive capacity to distinguish new elements**, **Suppose someone argues for a proposition by referencing the same few core facts repeatedly; this repetition undermines the requirement for infinite new reasons**
