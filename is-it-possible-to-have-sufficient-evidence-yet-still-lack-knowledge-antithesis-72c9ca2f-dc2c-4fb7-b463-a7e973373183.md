# Is it possible to have sufficient evidence yet still lack knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72c9ca2f-dc2c-4fb7-b463-a7e973373183
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidence-Based Theory](evidence-based-theory-synthesis-3a180c4f-30b0-4e6f-87b5-c20a77cbb8ff.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where all the gathered evidence suggests a proposition is true, but later, unforeseen factors show it is false. In such cases, despite having sufficient evidence initially, intuitively one might not have had real knowledge.**, **A person conducts a scientific experiment and gathers enough evidence to support a hypothesis. If the hypothesis is later debunked, the initial "knowledge" based on sufficient evidence seems questionable.**
