# How does the Safety Theory handle lucky true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84fd4ae3-fd97-4071-aa57-87457310b2c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety Theory](knowledge-as-safety-theory-synthesis-c8c71029-2564-4cee-86e3-af699df3743f.md)

## Content

**In a lottery scenario where a person believes their ticket will not win and this belief turns out true, we would not consider such a belief as knowledge due to the inherent luck involved. Intuitively, winning this belief purely by chance suggests lack of knowledge.**, **Imagine a situation where a hiker believes the trail is safe based on outdated maps, and coincidentally, the trail remains safe. This luck-based true belief is intuitively not knowledge.**
