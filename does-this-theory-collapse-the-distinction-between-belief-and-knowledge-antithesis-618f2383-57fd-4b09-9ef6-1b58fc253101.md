# Does this theory collapse the distinction between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 618f2383-57fd-4b09-9ef6-1b58fc253101
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d7b209ba-b523-44b3-ad9a-c228c53c8771.md)

## Content

**Believing a proposition does not necessitate knowing it.**, **It's possible to believe something true without the belief having practical benefit.**, **The theory implies mere belief in a true proposition with practical benefit counts as knowledge, which blurs belief and knowledge distinction.**
