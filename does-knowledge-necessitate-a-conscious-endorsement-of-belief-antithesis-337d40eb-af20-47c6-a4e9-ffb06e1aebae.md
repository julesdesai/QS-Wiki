# Does knowledge necessitate a conscious endorsement of belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 337d40eb-af20-47c6-a4e9-ffb06e1aebae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds a true belief but is not consciously aware of believing it. Intuitively, we would not grant knowledge to this person because knowledge requires an active endorsement of belief.**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe it, yet believing a proposition absent conscious endorsement challenges our intuition that knowledge involves awareness and deliberate acceptance.**
