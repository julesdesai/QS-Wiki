# Is intellectual virtue sufficient to account for all knowledge instances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46be0835-6f8d-4eca-8821-7553c133038a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtue, while valuable, may not account for all conditions in which we classify beliefs as knowledge. This principle suggests gaps that need accounting beyond intellectual virtues.**, **Consider scenarios where truth, belief, and justification are present without the agent displaying any intellectual virtues. In such scenarios, intuitively, knowledge seems present, questioning the sufficiency of virtue alone.**
