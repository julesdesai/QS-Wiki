# Can perceptions be misleading?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b9c18ba3-f958-4623-90a7-0c7ef60a158e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Memory-Integrated Phenomenalism](memory-integrated-phenomenalism-synthesis-20b6ad46-c9b1-41b8-87a0-7619b03b72c5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a situation where a mirage causes a traveler to perceive water in a desert. Intuitively, the perceptual experience does not provide knowledge of actual water.**, **It is implausible that perceptual experiences are always reliable sources of knowledge, since they can sometimes be deceptive.**
