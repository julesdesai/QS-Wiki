# Is reliance on basic standards alone sufficient for knowledge in dynamic situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c9006b5-8465-4a43-9082-0bdeacbd0aba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Context-Sensitive Invariant Endorsement](context-sensitive-invariant-endorsement-synthesis-de3f576d-97e3-4b7e-b7cd-f089d4d156aa.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where Dana believes she knows a company policy rule because it's written in the employee handbook. In a routine setting, this judgment is intuitively solid. However, during a legal review where implications are significant and the policy might be outdated, the reliance solely on written materials may not constitute true knowledge, challenging the notion of invariant core standards.**
