# Can knowledge involve true unlucky beliefs that lack cognitive virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5150de8e-697c-4c66-bc95-6c46101cb4ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7681d6b8-18d3-4c19-95f1-d061b70d0c81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a gambler forms a true belief that a particular number will win, based on a hunch, without relying on any cognitive virtue. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might not want to say the gambler has knowledge.**, **Justification involving cognitive virtues implies reliability, yet true beliefs formed by sheer luck appear unjustified even if they accidentally get to the truth.**
