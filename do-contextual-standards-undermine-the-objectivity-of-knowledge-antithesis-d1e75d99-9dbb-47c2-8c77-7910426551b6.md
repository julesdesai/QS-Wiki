# Do contextual standards undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1e75d99-9dbb-47c2-8c77-7910426551b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**In a case where knowledge standards vary with context, the objectivity of knowledge claims can be questioned.**, **If knowledge is seen as objective, then its validity should remain stable across different contexts and not be subject to change.**, **There is an intuition that objective knowledge should not be influenced by external contextual factors, which contextualism seems to violate.**
