# Can someone mistakenly know a true proposition under the Safety Theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 340752a7-9736-480a-b385-5afc887ac731
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety Theory](knowledge-as-safety-theory-synthesis-c8c71029-2564-4cee-86e3-af699df3743f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person rolls a fair die and believes it will land on six, and it indeed does. Intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the die will land on six despite the Safety Theory possibly implying they could.**, **A scenario in which a person randomly guesses the correct answer on a multiple-choice quiz and believes it, yet does not genuinely know the answer. Intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the answer despite their belief being true.**
