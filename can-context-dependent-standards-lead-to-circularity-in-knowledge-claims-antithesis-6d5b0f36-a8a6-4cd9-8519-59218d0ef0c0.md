# Can context-dependent standards lead to circularity in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d5b0f36-a8a6-4cd9-8519-59218d0ef0c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-55297f9c-50a2-4f24-b1b0-6029f7bef7b2.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that justification should be self-referentially determined by the context, since contexts themselves require justification which can lead to circular reasoning.**, **A situation where the context demands justification for its own interpretative standards creates a paradox where a belief is justified if and only if the context permits it based on those very standards.**
