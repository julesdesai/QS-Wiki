# Can knowledge be sensitive to changes in practical significance?

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](knowledge-as-subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-73203364-04bd-40f3-b8f4-b58f75431fb6.md)

## Content

**If a person believes a deeply relevant medical result, which is true, while another equally true result about a minor health issue matters less practically, intuitively we would treat the first belief as more significant knowledge.**, **A situation where practical significance shifts, such as prioritizing different health outcomes, challenges the sufficiency of true belief being relevant to practical interests alone.**
