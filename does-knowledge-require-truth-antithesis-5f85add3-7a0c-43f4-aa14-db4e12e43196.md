# Does knowledge require truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f85add3-7a0c-43f4-aa14-db4e12e43196
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person truly believes they know something, based on strong evidence and reasoning, yet it turns out to be false through a change in circumstance, like stock performance before a market crash. Intuitively, we would hesitate to say the person had knowledge, given the actual falsity.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves not just strong belief but also the actual truth of the proposition. This principle challenges the idea that one can possess knowledge with false beliefs, despite otherwise solid justification.**
