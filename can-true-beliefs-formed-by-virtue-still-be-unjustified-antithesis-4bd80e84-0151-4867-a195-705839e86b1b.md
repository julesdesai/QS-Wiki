# Can true beliefs formed by virtue still be unjustified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4bd80e84-0151-4867-a195-705839e86b1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ffa55650-0795-4012-9f33-14517d86090e.md)

## Content

**A person can develop a belief based purely on a virtuous trait such as optimism, which occasionally leads to truth.**, **Intuitively, the truth in such cases appears accidental rather than stemming from justified reasoning.**, **If justification is tied to intellectual virtue, true beliefs from such traits could unjustly be considered knowledge.**
