# Is externalist reliabilism consistent with the intuitions about radical skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab7b3cc9-0b45-4a69-9efa-6dfb68d6e313
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is unknowingly in a skeptical scenario, such as a brain in a vat, yet forms beliefs through processes that appear reliable from their perspective. Intuitively, skepticism might suggest these beliefs lack justification or knowledge, despite the seeming reliability.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliable processes should account for the possibility of radical deception to ensure genuine knowledge or justification.**
