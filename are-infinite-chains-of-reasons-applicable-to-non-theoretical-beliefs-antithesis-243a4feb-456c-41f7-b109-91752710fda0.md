# Are infinite chains of reasons applicable to non-theoretical beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 243a4feb-456c-41f7-b109-91752710fda0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Infinitism](virtue-epistemology-infinitism-synthesis-700b1be0-40ec-4861-9f40-25460b6be3e1.md)

## Content

**Consider everyday beliefs, such as "the chair will support my weight," which are justified based on common experience and limited reasoning.**, **In such cases, we are unlikely to reconstruct or require an infinite chain of reasons.**, **It is implausible that mundane beliefs must adhere to infinitism for justification.**
