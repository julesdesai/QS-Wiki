# Can knowledge exist despite phenomenal inconsistencies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fd7c0dd-6c2f-4a65-9f98-e675926044c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Realism with Consistency Conditions](phenomenal-realism-with-consistency-conditions-synthesis-05f9226f-1221-4391-b2ae-790270c7a451.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person perceives a phenomenon differently under identical conditions due to external influences like lighting or mood. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person can still possess knowledge despite the phenomenal inconsistency.**, **It is implausible that knowledge requires absolute phenomenal consistency across all experiences, given that perceptions can change under different contexts without affecting the truth of what is known.**
