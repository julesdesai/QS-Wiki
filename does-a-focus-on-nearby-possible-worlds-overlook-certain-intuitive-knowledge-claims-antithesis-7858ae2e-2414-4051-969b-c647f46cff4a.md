# Does a focus on nearby possible worlds overlook certain intuitive knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7858ae2e-2414-4051-969b-c647f46cff4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone living in a stable environment believes it is safe due to their experience and local history. In nearby possible worlds, their environment is suddenly unsafe due to unlikely events. Intuitively, they still possess knowledge about their environment's safety.**, **Knowledge claims that rely heavily on actual environments and contexts might challenge a strong focus on the implications of nearby possible worlds.**
