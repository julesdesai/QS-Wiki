# Can contextualism explain disagreement between experts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** feef85ae-b7f7-4208-b5ee-24b6bd95f224
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**Experts in a field can disagree on what constitutes knowledge despite similar backgrounds.**, **Contextualism implies both experts can be correct if they use different justification standards.**, **This challenges the assumption that experts should converge on the same knowledge given the same evidence.**, **The model fails to account for expert disagreements that are not due to contexts but due to different interpretations of evidence.**
