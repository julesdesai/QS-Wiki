# Does virtue epistemology account for testimonial knowledge effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c0e2c20-70a1-4912-abd5-6fbbb9f0192e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**In a situation where a child acquires true beliefs from a reliable teacher, intuitively the child has knowledge despite possibly lacking developed intellectual virtues.**, **A belief acquired through testimony may lack the direct contribution of the listener's intellectual virtues, challenging the view that such virtues are essential for knowledge.**, **Credit for the truth of a testimonial belief might be primarily due to the informant's virtues rather than the recipient's, questioning the necessity of the recipient's cognitive abilities in crediting truth.**
