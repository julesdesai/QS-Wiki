# Can synesthetic experiences challenge the idea of direct cross-modal perception?

**Node ID:** 85ed4afc-d725-4f82-8d13-7b261ef94e4f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person with synesthesia perceives numbers as inherently colored. Intuitively, this synesthetic perception adds an extra sensory dimension not present in the external world.**, **Synesthetic experiences suggest that cross-modal integration in sensory perception may produce perceptions without a direct correspondence to external objects.**, **The presence of synesthetic perceptions challenges the natural integration of multiple senses in a way that directly reflects external reality.**
