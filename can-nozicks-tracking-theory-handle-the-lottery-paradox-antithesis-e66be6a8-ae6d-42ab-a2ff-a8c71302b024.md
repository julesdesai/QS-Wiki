# Can Nozick's tracking theory handle the lottery paradox?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e66be6a8-ae6d-42ab-a2ff-a8c71302b024
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism with Nozick's Tracking Theory](epistemic-externalism-with-nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-99a4169f-6bac-4b5a-b46b-52fe3916b903.md)

## Content

**In the lottery scenario, for any single ticket, we can say it tracks the truth that it will lose since buying a ticket does not substantially increase belief that it will lose.**, **Despite this, intuitively, we would not say one has knowledge that this particular ticket will lose, even if it is very likely.**, **The tracking condition seems satisfied, yet our intuition signals a lack of knowledge due to the possibility of winning, hence challenging Nozick's requirements for knowledge.**
