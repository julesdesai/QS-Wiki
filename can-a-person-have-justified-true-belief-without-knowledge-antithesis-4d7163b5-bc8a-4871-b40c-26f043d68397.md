# Can a person have justified true belief without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d7163b5-bc8a-4871-b40c-26f043d68397
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a justified true belief based on a generally reliable cognitive process, but unbeknownst to them, specific conditions undermine the reliability in this instance. Intuitively, we might say the person lacks knowledge despite having a justified true belief.**, **This scenario suggests that reliability alone is not always sufficient to convert justified true belief into knowledge.**
