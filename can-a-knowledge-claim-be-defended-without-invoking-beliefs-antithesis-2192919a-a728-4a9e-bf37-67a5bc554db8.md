# Can a knowledge claim be defended without invoking beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2192919a-a728-4a9e-bf37-67a5bc554db8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Belief Necessary View](no-belief-necessary-view-synthesis-81f9ccf2-9484-47e2-8ae4-3b9845798b1d.md)

## Content

**Knowledge claims typically require the claimant to provide reasons or justifications, which are typically belief-like in nature.**, **If a person claims to know something without having any related beliefs, it’s unclear how they would respond to challenges or questions about their knowledge.**, **Consider a case where someone is asked to explain why they know something; intuitively, we would expect them to reference beliefs, not just assert knowledge.**
