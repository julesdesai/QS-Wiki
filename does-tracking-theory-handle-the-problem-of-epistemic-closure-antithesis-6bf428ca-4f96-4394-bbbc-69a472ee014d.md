# Does tracking theory handle the problem of epistemic closure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6bf428ca-4f96-4394-bbbc-69a472ee014d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-92e582e4-32a5-4017-b62b-ea7485c8afb8.md)

## Content

**Epistemic closure is a principle stating that if a person knows a proposition and knows that it entails another proposition, then they should know the other proposition. Tracking theory allows for cases where a person knows a proposition but fails to track or know an entailed proposition.**, **Consider a scenario where a person knows a premise but the conclusion, although true and entailed, does not track truth in nearby worlds. Intuitively, this challenges the claim that they have knowledge of the conclusion.**
