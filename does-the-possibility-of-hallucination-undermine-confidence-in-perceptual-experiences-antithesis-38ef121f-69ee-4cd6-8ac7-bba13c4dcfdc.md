# Does the possibility of hallucination undermine confidence in perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 38ef121f-69ee-4cd6-8ac7-bba13c4dcfdc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-e49b01dd-7eac-432b-990b-a606f4616c7d.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person under the influence of hallucinogens perceives non-existent objects.**, **If perceptual experiences involve hallucinated objects, then representations cannot be reliably accurate indicators of external truth.**, **This suggests that the fallibility of perception raises questions about the reliability of representations of external reality.**
