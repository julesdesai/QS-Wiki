# How do we account for cases of accidentally true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1207f48d-fb2b-4edf-9e92-ff0dac738b83
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition because of an unreliable source, yet the proposition is true due to sheer luck. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of the proposition.**, **In this scenario, the person's belief is accidentally true, which challenges the idea that mere true belief based on reliable processes is sufficient for knowledge.**
