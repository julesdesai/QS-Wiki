## Content

**In Gettier cases, a person's belief is true and justified but not knowledge because of the element of luck involved. Intuitively, these beliefs are not safe, yet they illustrate instances where safety might mistakenly attribute knowledge.**, **Consider a belief derived from justified falsehoods leading to an accidental truth. While the belief accidentally meets the requirements of the nearby worlds mentioned in the safety condition, it challenges our concept of true knowledge.**
