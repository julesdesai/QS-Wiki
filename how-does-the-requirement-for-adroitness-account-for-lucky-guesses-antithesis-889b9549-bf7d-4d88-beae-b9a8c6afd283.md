# How does the requirement for adroitness account for lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 889b9549-bf7d-4d88-beae-b9a8c6afd283
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Triple-A Virtue Epistemology](triple-a-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5ec13be3-cfbe-4179-96b3-378e0ebd05b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person randomly guesses the outcome of a coin toss and gets it right unintentionally. Intuitively, this case would not count as knowledge even though the belief is accurate and true.**, **In this scenario, the belief could accidentally be true without being formed skillfully, challenging the necessity of adroitness for knowledge.**
