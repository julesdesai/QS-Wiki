# Can perception alone justify knowledge without existing belief systems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39c21573-e861-4b63-b31d-1133b7e012a9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism](coherentism-synthesis-47013a86-4e6f-4466-86d9-11bbc22ccdaf.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge derived from perception should require minimal reliance on other beliefs. This principle together with the view that perception's role is to integrate with existing beliefs suggests that perception alone cannot justify knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where a person sees a tree but has no prior beliefs about trees. Intuitively, the person's perception should suffice for knowledge of the tree, contrary to requiring a network of supporting beliefs.**
