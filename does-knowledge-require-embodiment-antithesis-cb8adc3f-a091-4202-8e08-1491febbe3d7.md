# Does knowledge require embodiment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cb8adc3f-a091-4202-8e08-1491febbe3d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Embodied Cognition with Reliance on Bodily Skills](embodied-cognition-with-reliance-on-bodily-skills-synthesis-e9d28d24-22c1-4ccc-a9a1-a716398534d0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that disembodied entities, such as artificial intelligence, can still possess knowledge if they have true, justified beliefs. This principle together with the claim that knowledge requires embodiment entails that disembodied systems cannot possess knowledge, which some might find implausible.**
