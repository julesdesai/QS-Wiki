# How can Proper Functionalism distinguish between knowledge and lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b151cff8-d389-4c4e-9217-53ba4516af3e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-17c9fccb-5f3e-4011-91c3-5213c66550ac.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief that is true and results from properly functioning faculties, but the formation process involves a significant element of luck. Intuitively, we either have to say the person knows the proposition or they do not. If we say they know, then chance can play an unacceptable role in knowledge. If we say they do not, then proper functioning seems insufficient for knowledge.**
