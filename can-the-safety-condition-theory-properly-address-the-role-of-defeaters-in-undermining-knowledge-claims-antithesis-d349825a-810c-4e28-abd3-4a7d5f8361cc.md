# Can the Safety Condition Theory properly address the role of defeaters in undermining knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d349825a-810c-4e28-abd3-4a7d5f8361cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Consider an instance where a person accurately believes a proposition to be true and meets the safety condition, but there’s overlooked evidence that would have altered their belief if known. Intuitively, the presence of a defeater undermines their claim to knowledge despite the proposition being true. There is a plausible principle that knowledge is not present if a valid defeater exists that casts doubt on a belief’s justification or truth.**
