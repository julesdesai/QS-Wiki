# Does reliance on true general assumptions with specific false instances undermine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d12bc18-5eca-4ec3-a90f-90e24341cd27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma Theory](no-false-lemma-theory-synthesis-4a11ee42-2be9-4836-9dd6-d365881bb277.md)

## Content

**A principle suggests that general truths underpin belief justification; it appears plausible that reliance on a general assumption, while correct, could include unknown false specifics, challenging the absence of falsehood in justification.**, **Suppose a person relies on the general belief that it always rains on Sundays in Seattle. This may be generally true over a significant period, but if a specific Sunday does not meet this criterion, relying on such a pattern might involve falsehood despite the overall general truth.**
