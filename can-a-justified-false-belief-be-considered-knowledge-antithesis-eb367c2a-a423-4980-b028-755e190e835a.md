# Can a justified false belief be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eb367c2a-a423-4980-b028-755e190e835a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-foundationalism-synthesis-a32c22e3-fd99-46f1-bd06-3346824cbc70.md)

## Content

**A belief can be justified and still be false. Intuitively, a justified false belief cannot amount to knowledge. There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth in addition to justification. This principle together with the claim that knowledge is true belief based on intellectual virtues acting on foundational beliefs suggests that justification alone, even if based on such virtues, cannot guarantee knowledge if the belief is false.**
