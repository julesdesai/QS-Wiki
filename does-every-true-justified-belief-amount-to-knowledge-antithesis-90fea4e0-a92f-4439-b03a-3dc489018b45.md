# Does every true justified belief amount to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 90fea4e0-a92f-4439-b03a-3dc489018b45
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology: Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-1d322156-3f22-49a0-99a5-f932d24b0e66.md)

## Content

**There could be a situation where a person forms a belief through faulty reasoning but incidentally arrives at truth. It seems that despite justification and truth, this is not knowledge.**, **An intuitively plausible principle is that good reasoning is necessary for knowledge, implying that justification derived from bad reasoning undermines claims of knowledge.**
