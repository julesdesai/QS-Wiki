# Can tracking truth capture the notion of justified belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f13abb54-392c-4c2d-a4bb-d8d5c069b665
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory With Truth Tracking](nozicks-tracking-theory-with-truth-tracking-synthesis-9a557177-6533-4827-a4a8-a2fb94f50474.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that tracking truth alone fully encapsulates what it is to have a justified belief, because justification generally involves reasons readily available to the believer that are good, accessible, and explicable from their perspective.**, **The notion of justification typically involves an internalist perspective where a believer ought to have access to reasons and evidence for their belief, which a purely external tracking system might lack. This perspective suggests that justification is a distinct component of knowledge beyond mere truth-tracking.**
