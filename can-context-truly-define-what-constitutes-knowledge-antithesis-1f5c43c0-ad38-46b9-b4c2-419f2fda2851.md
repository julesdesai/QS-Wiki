# Can context truly define what constitutes knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f5c43c0-ad38-46b9-b4c2-419f2fda2851
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**Knowledge depends on the context of the knower, suggesting that different settings alter what we consider knowledge. However, it is implausible that a fundamental epistemic concept like knowledge should shift based merely on surrounding circumstances.**, **Suppose two people have identical beliefs with identical justification in different contexts, but only one is deemed to have knowledge. Intuitively, this seems unjustified, questioning the role of context in defining knowledge.**
