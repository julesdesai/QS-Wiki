# Is the dispositional account too individualistic, ignoring social factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1feba63a-a5d1-4b1c-b6fd-7bc9f0aa5496
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person has a dispositional ability to form true beliefs, but only because they live in a community that consistently presents true beliefs to them. Intuitively, we either have to say the person's knowledge is solely theirs or acknowledge community influence. If we say it's solely theirs, it overlooks the social aspect. If we acknowledge community influence, it suggests knowledge is not only dispositional but also social**
