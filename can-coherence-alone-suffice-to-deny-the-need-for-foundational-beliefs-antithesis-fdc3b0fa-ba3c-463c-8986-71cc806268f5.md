# Can coherence alone suffice to deny the need for foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fdc3b0fa-ba3c-463c-8986-71cc806268f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that every belief in a system can be justified solely by its relation to other beliefs without any foundational beliefs.**, **Suppose we have a case in which no belief within a coherent system is independently justified. Intuitively, the absence of any foundational belief challenges the entire system's justification. If coherentism is true, then no foundational belief is needed, which contradicts the common intuition that some beliefs must be self-evident or empirically grounded.**
