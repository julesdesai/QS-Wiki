# Does an epistemic virtue guarantee truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 125e7356-b3ec-4522-8f03-fa3c2be41f7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person consistently uses reliable intellectual virtues but arrives at a false belief due to misleading evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the exercise of intellectual virtue does not guarantee true belief in every case.**, **A plausible principle is that for a belief to be knowledge, it must be true. This principle alongside a case where intellectual virtue doesn't lead to truth suggests that intellectual virtue alone cannot guarantee the acquisition of knowledge.**
