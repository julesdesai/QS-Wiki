# Do contextual standards undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d938610-f9e6-463f-b8fa-165953c0dde8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Imagine two scientists working independently on the same problem, who arrive at different conclusions due to variable contextual standards. Intuitively, this undermines the objectivity that scientific knowledge is supposed to represent.**, **It is implausible that an objective domain like science can allow for context-dependent standards of knowledge without leading to conflicting conclusions.**
