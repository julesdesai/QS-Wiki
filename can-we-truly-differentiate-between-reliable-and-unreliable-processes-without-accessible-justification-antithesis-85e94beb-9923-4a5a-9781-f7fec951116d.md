# Can we truly differentiate between reliable and unreliable processes without accessible justification?

**Node ID:** 85e94beb-9923-4a5a-9781-f7fec951116d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-c84f45b2-357a-4b29-be66-6a09ffb029e2.md)

## Content

**A case in which a belief is formed by a cognitive process that produces a reliable output 80% of the time, but the person holding the belief has no accessible justification, leaves doubt about whether they truly know the belief**, **It is implausible that we can identify a reliable cognitive process without some form of accessible justification to distinguish it from unreliable processes**
