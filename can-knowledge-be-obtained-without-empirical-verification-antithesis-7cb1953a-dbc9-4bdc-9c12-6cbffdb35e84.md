# Can knowledge be obtained without empirical verification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7cb1953a-dbc9-4bdc-9c12-6cbffdb35e84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a strong intuitive belief about a proposition that is later proven true without empirical testing. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person possesses knowledge despite the lack of empirical verification.**, **There is a plausible principle that states some knowledge can be gained through intuition or insight without the need for direct empirical evidence. This principle together with the scenario entails that empirical verification is not always necessary for knowledge.**
