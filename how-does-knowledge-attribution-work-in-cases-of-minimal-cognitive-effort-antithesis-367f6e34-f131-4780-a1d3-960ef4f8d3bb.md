# How does knowledge attribution work in cases of minimal cognitive effort?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 367f6e34-f131-4780-a1d3-960ef4f8d3bb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person acquires a true belief with minimal cognitive effort, such as quickly glancing at a reliable source and accepting the information without further thought. Intuitively, we might attribute knowledge to the person, yet this challenges the notion of demonstrating intellectual virtue.**, **Intellectual virtue seems to require significant cognitive effort, suggesting that minimal effort might conflict with virtue epistemology's standards for knowledge.**
