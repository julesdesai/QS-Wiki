**Parent:** [How do we handle beliefs formed by reliable methods that could have easily been false?](how-do-we-handle-beliefs-formed-by-reliable-methods-that-could-have-easily-been-false-antithesis-52fa7299-025f-4f73-876d-ef2b218d9131.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition were false, the person would not believe it**, **The belief must track the truth across the closest possible worlds in which it might otherwise be false**
