# Does belief sensitivity to falsity undermine the idea that knowledge can be inferred from reliable information sources?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 908697d6-999b-4a3f-86c8-3208eafd3779
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person receives information from a reliable scientific instrument about a proposition. If the proposition were false, they might still believe it due to high confidence in the instrument's usual accuracy. Intuitively, this belief can still be called knowledge because of the instrument’s reliability.**, **If knowledge presupposes discarding beliefs when propositions are false, it may exclude cases where beliefs based on reliable sources are justified even when those sources could occasionally be wrong.**
