# Are distant counterfactual possibilities relevant in denying knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1fceee6-39e7-4bb0-a789-a0c082d6921b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes they will get a job offer, and it is true because no miscommunication occurs, though it nearly did. Intuitively, we either have to say that separate possible miscommunications that did not happen should not affect the knowledge claim, or they do. If they should not, then distant counterfactual possibilities are irrelevant, but if they do, this would invalidate many legitimate claims under practical circumstances.**, **There is a plausible principle that distant counterfactual possibilities should only minimally interfere with knowledge claims unless reasonably expected to materialize.**
