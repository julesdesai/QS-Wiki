# Does justification always involve reliably truth-conducive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d23ad96d-5158-4f32-b76a-8f365287accd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism: Reliable Processes](reliabilism-reliable-processes-synthesis-f70f1f60-dbef-46bd-b415-215191e514dd.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a belief that is formed through a process that is generally reliable but, in this particular instance, produces a false result. Intuitively, we would judge that the process did not serve as justification in this instance.**, **There is a plausible principle that a process can be generally reliable, yet fail to provide justification in every particular case.**
