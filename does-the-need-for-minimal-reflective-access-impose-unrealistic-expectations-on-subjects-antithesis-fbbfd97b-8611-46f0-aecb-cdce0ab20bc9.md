# Does the need for minimal reflective access impose unrealistic expectations on subjects?

**Node ID:** fbbfd97b-8611-46f0-aecb-cdce0ab20bc9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimalist Internalism](minimalist-internalism-synthesis-c1aff540-8ee4-4dca-921e-260ac4a2c333.md)

## Content

**Suppose an ordinary belief is held with practical assurance without reflective access. Intuitively, everyday knowledge doesn’t always involve reflection.**, **A plausible objection is that requiring minimal reflective access imposes unrealistic expectations on ordinary epistemic agents.**, **Internalist accounts demanding reflective access misrepresent typical cognitive processes in everyday situations.**
