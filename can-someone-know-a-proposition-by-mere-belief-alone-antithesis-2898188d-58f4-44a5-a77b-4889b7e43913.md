# Can someone know a proposition by mere belief alone?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2898188d-58f4-44a5-a77b-4889b7e43913
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition purely because it feels right to them, without any evidence or reasoning. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that merely believing a proposition does not suffice for knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that distinguishes mere belief from knowledge by requiring some grounding justification or evidence.**
