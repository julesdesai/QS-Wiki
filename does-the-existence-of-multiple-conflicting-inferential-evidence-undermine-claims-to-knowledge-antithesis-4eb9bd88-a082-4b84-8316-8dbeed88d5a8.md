# Does the existence of multiple, conflicting inferential evidence undermine claims to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4eb9bd88-a082-4b84-8316-8dbeed88d5a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Evidence Theory](inferential-evidence-theory-synthesis-888e1fca-9103-4ede-a19f-61db12f550f6.md)

## Content

**Imagine an investor deciding to buy a stock based on positive market trends but overlooking equally valid reports predicting a downturn. Intuitively, given the conflicting evidence, the belief in the stock’s future success lacks certainty and doesn't constitute knowledge.**, **Inferential evidence involves reasoning from premises to conclusions, but when evidence leads to multiple plausible, conflicting conclusions, the lack of a definitive basis undermines the claim that resulting beliefs are truly knowledge.**
