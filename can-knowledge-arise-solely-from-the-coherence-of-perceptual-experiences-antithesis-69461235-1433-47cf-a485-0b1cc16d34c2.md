# Can knowledge arise solely from the coherence of perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69461235-1433-47cf-a485-0b1cc16d34c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a connection to truth. This principle together with the claim that coherence of perceptual experiences alone can constitute knowledge suggests that coherence might be insufficient because it lacks a direct truth connection.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has a coherent set of hallucinations. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of external objects despite the coherence of experiences.**
