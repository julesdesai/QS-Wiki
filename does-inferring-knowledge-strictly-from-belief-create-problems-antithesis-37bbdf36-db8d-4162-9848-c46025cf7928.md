# Does inferring knowledge strictly from belief create problems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37bbdf36-db8d-4162-9848-c46025cf7928
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-4b9cf48f-532c-40a3-a960-718d00ef564e.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has a firmly held false belief and suddenly the belief becomes true due to changes in the world. Intuitively, we would not claim they gained knowledge because their belief was previously unjustified.**, **Consider a situation where belief transitions from false to true without any increase in reason-based justification. Intuitively, this transition would not suffice for establishing knowledge.**, **It is plausible to propose that merely believing a proposition, even if it becomes true by chance, is insufficient for conferring knowledge status.**
