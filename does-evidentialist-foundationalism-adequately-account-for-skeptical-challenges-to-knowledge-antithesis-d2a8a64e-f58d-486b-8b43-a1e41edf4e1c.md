# Does evidentialist foundationalism adequately account for skeptical challenges to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2a8a64e-f58d-486b-8b43-a1e41edf4e1c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Foundationalism](evidentialist-foundationalism-synthesis-26c84477-fc91-4190-8a0e-24d90c56393c.md)

## Content

**Consider the skeptical scenario where a person is dreaming but has foundational beliefs about their environment being real. In such a scenario, intuitively, having foundational beliefs and evidence does not secure knowledge of the external world.**, **There is a plausible principle that skepticism suggests no beliefs about the external world are justified. If foundationalism does not include an account to defeat skeptical scenarios, then our foundational beliefs remain unjustified under these circumstances.**, **The principle that one can know a proposition if it rests on foundational beliefs and evidence fails to address the challenge of radical skepticism which questions the possibility of any certain knowledge.**
