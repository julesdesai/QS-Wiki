# Can misleading evidence undermine the idea that justified true belief equals knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aec044d3-a3ed-4bf1-b43d-7751b5289e86
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Think of a case where a person derives a belief from misleading but convincing evidence, leading to a correct conclusion. Intuitively, such a belief does not seem to qualify as knowledge despite being justified and true.**, **The existence of misleading evidence challenges the adequacy of justification as defined by the theory.**
