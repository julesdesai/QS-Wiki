# Is causation necessary in cases of inferential knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f1f1758-125a-4fd0-8560-ca0fddc50157
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cee9090d-2e34-47f2-b4ee-a59054387264.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective infers the identity of a culprit from various evidence rather than directly from the facts of the crime itself. The detective can be said to know the identity of the culprit even if there is no direct causal connection between the truth and their belief.**, **Inferential knowledge, such as mathematical deductions, does not always involve a causal interaction with the truth of the proposition.**
