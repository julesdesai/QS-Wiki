# Can Reliable Processes Fail in Unpredictable Environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14bf7b49-6000-424c-aee3-9a1dc35ca923
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a cognitive process is reliable in a stable environment but not in a rapidly changing or deceptive environment. Intuitively, a person relying on such a process in the latter would not have knowledge.**, **A reliable cognitive process in one context may not be reliable in another. This raises questions about whether context-specific reliability can adequately account for knowledge.**
