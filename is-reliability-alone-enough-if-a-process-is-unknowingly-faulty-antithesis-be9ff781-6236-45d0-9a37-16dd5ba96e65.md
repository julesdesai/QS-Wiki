# Is reliability alone enough if a process is unknowingly faulty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** be9ff781-6236-45d0-9a37-16dd5ba96e65
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e2d53466-acc3-45a1-95cc-71c95728df76.md)

## Content

**An individual obtains historical data from a source previously vetted as reliable but unknowingly compromised by errors. This challenges the sufficiency of reliability for knowledge acquisition.**, **Suppose a student uses a textbook found to contain factual inaccuracies, yet the textbook was previously dependable. Knowledge relies on the process's status, not its perceived reliability.**
