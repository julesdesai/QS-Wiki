# Can a method's reliability be unknowable yet still confer knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 70e0d421-9ebe-4d66-889b-21d2be8dc038
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b27c6d4b-ac60-4e95-8268-38e201ff9a4c.md)

## Content

**A person may form a true belief using a method with unknowable reliability, such as relying on an unverified algorithm.**, **It is implausible that a method's reliability can confer knowledge if the reliability cannot be known or verified.**
