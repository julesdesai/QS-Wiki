# Could contextualism overlook non-epistemic factors in knowledge attributions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d78b3a3-85d2-4c03-878a-a1dc7d8bd39c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextualism](knowledge-as-contextualism-synthesis-98d1787d-f33b-4a95-b2e8-91902f0e81be.md)

## Content

**The contextualist view focuses heavily on epistemic context, potentially disregarding non-epistemic influences on knowledge attribution.**, **For instance, social and cultural pressures might affect whether someone is said to "know" something.**, **Ignoring these non-epistemic factors might limit the ability to fully understand knowledge claims in their lived context.**
