# Does the high-stakes gambler know the odds?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment with Influence of Practical Stakes](pragmatic-encroachment-with-influence-of-practical-stakes-synthesis-1e83d295-b88f-4064-889e-097b4fb938ca.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a high-stakes gambler believes the odds of winning are 3 to 1, the available evidence supports this belief, and the gambler stands to win or lose a significant amount of money based on this belief. Intuitively, even with strong evidence, the gambler is perceived as not knowing the odds due to the high stakes involved.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should be determined by evidence and truth rather than practical consequences. This principle suggests that practical stakes should not influence whether someone knows something.**
