# Can knowledge exist without a causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d768a0da-ae80-4042-b4e4-64a232ae3d01
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-9ece6694-b5cf-4143-897d-321cf0165994.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where a person P reads a true statement about the time in a fictional novel and believes it, P's belief is true but lacks a causal connection to the truth of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that if someone can form a true belief by accident without a causal link, they do not have knowledge.**
