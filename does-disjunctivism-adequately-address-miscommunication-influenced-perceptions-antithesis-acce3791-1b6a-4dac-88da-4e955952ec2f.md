# Does disjunctivism adequately address miscommunication influenced perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** acce3791-1b6a-4dac-88da-4e955952ec2f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone hears a conversation in a noisy environment and perceives agreement when there is none. Intuitively, the person might claim knowledge of the agreement based on the misheard conversation.**, **If perceptual experiences justify beliefs only if veridically connected, then the person’s belief in agreement remains unjustified.**, **It seems implausible that ordinary misunderstandings under noisy conditions render such beliefs universally unjustified.**
