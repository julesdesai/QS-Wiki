# How can synthesis reconcile conflicting evidence from different sources?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 919ee7c6-3683-4604-8a2b-136d04ee6a24
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person perceives a bridge to be structurally sound visually, but engineering reports indicate signs of damage. Intuitively, synthesis of evidence appears challenging when there is conflicting input.**, **There is a plausible principle that conflicting evidence should lead to skepticism rather than synthesis. This principle together with synthesis of conflicting data implies that synthesis does not always lead to true knowledge.**, **It is implausible that synthesis of conflicting evidence would consistently yield accurate knowledge claims.**
