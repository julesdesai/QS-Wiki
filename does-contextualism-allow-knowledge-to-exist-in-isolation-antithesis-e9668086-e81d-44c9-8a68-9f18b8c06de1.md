# Does contextualism allow knowledge to exist in isolation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9668086-e81d-44c9-8a68-9f18b8c06de1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b7b03c88-6aa4-4b47-9f59-bc5548cc994c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person knows a proposition while isolated without any interaction. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that contextual conversation plays no role in confirming their knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, if contingent on conversational context, exists when there is no conversational interaction.**
