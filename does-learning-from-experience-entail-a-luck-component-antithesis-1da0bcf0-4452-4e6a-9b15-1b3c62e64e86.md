# Does learning from experience entail a luck component?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1da0bcf0-4452-4e6a-9b15-1b3c62e64e86
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all knowledge derived from experience is devoid of luck, given the unpredictable nature of experiential learning.**, **Consider a case where a person learns a true belief through an unexpected turn of events in experience. Intuitively, the belief is considered knowledge, highlighting how experience-linked luck does not necessarily invalidate knowledge claims.**
