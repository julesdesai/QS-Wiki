# Can contextualism be tested empirically?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0be1e0ae-6fc1-4999-ad97-d7235f26772f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that theories about human cognition should be empirically testable. This principle together with the idea that context variably affects knowledge entails that we should find consistent empirical support for such variation.**, **It is implausible that context-dependent theories of knowledge are reliably observed in empirical psychology as ordinary knowledge judgments often appear stable.**
