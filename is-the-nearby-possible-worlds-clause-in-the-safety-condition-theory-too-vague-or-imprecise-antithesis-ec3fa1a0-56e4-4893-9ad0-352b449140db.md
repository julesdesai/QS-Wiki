# Is the 'nearby possible worlds' clause in the Safety Condition Theory too vague or imprecise?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec3fa1a0-56e4-4893-9ad0-352b449140db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on solid evidence, but small alterations in initial conditions in nearby possible worlds lead to false beliefs. Intuitively, these nearby worlds seem insignificant, yet they challenge the accuracy of the safety condition. If the definition of 'nearby possible worlds' is too vague, it creates ambiguity in determining the safety of the belief.**
