# Is a high proportion of true beliefs sufficient for reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74da49a1-5fdf-4a0f-98a4-6cbf389773a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge](reliable-process-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8a084bde-8e94-4b49-86d3-6339aef5c9e6.md)

## Content

**A process might yield a high proportion of true beliefs but still systematically produce certain specific falsehoods**, **Merely having a higher number of truth over falsehoods does not eradicate the chance of systematic error**, **The presence of systematic errors indicates the process might not be entirely reliable as defined**
