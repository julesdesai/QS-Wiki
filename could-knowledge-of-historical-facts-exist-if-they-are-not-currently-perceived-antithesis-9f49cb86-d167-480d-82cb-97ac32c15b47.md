# Could knowledge of historical facts exist if they are not currently perceived?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f49cb86-d167-480d-82cb-97ac32c15b47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-ab29b70f-4a42-463b-b303-b38517d8aa22.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone learns about a historical event like the signing of the Declaration of Independence through documents and narratives, which are not direct perceptual experiences. Intuitively, this person knows about this historical fact, suggesting that objects of knowledge can exist independently of current perception.**
