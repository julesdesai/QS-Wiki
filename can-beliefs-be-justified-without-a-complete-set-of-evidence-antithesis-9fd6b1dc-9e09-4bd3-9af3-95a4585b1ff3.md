# Can beliefs be justified without a complete set of evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fd6b1dc-9e09-4bd3-9af3-95a4585b1ff3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes that it will rain tomorrow because they feel it in their bones without checking the weather forecast. Intuitively, we might consider their belief unjustified even though it turns out to be true.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs can be justified through means other than evidence, such as intuition or gut feeling. This principle with the notion that justification requires alignment with evidence challenges the idea that alignment is necessary.**
