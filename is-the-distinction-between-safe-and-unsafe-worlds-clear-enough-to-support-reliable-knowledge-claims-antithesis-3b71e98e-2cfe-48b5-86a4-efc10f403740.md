# Is the distinction between safe and unsafe worlds clear enough to support reliable knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3b71e98e-2cfe-48b5-86a4-efc10f403740
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where the distinction between the actual world and possible worlds where a belief turns false is highly ambiguous. Intuitively, this ambiguity raises doubts about the reliability of safety as a criterion for knowledge.**, **A plausible principle holds that for safety to be credible, we must clearly distinguish between safe and unsafe possible scenarios.**
