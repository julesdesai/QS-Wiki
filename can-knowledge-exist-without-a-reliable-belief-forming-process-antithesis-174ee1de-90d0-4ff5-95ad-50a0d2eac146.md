# Can knowledge exist without a reliable belief-forming process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 174ee1de-90d0-4ff5-95ad-50a0d2eac146
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-e6df444a-c8a0-46a3-91c3-0432faee253d.md)

## Content

**A person can have a true belief formed by an unreliable process that accidentally results in correctness, which intuitively does not count as knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where someone forms a belief about the weather by flipping a coin, and their belief happens to be true; this is not intuitive knowledge.**
