# Does knowledge require more than functional criteria?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e6f7eae-71e4-4f4c-98a5-a6b28b162a6e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that criteria for knowing a proposition require more than functional predictability and must involve truth. This principle alongside functional roles suggests that predictability alone might be insufficient for full knowledge.**, **It is implausible that predictable functional outcomes account for the truth component essential to knowledge.**
