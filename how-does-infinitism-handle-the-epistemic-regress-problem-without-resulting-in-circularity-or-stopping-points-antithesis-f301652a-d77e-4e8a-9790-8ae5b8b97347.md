# How does infinitism handle the epistemic regress problem without resulting in circularity or stopping points?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f301652a-d77e-4e8a-9790-8ae5b8b97347
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**The epistemic regress problem describes the challenge of providing justification without ending in circularity, an infinite chain, or basic beliefs.**, **An infinite chain proposes an indefinite extension of reasons, but does not actually solve how one practically proceeds when none are ultimately foundational.**, **By disallowing stopping points or foundational beliefs, infinitism seems to leave unanswered the original need for a resolution to the regress problem.**
