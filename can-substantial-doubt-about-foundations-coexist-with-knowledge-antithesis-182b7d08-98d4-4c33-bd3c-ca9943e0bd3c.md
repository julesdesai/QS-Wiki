# Can substantial doubt about foundations coexist with knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 182b7d08-98d4-4c33-bd3c-ca9943e0bd3c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a justified true belief based on foundational beliefs with recognized fallibility. Intuitively, the existence of substantial doubt about these foundational beliefs would lead us to question whether knowledge is truly present despite justification and truth.**, **A belief is justified if it rests on foundational beliefs that are reliable but not infallible, and yet substantial doubt prevents closure on the knowledge claim.**
