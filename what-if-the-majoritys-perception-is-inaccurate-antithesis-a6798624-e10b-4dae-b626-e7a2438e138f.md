# What if the majority's perception is inaccurate?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6798624-e10b-4dae-b626-e7a2438e138f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where most individuals perceive a sound they misidentify due to environmental factors. If the majority's shared perceptual experience is false, then the knowledge derived from it is unreliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that the majority's perception can be fallible. If consensus on an inaccurate perception constitutes knowledge, then knowledge is based on potentially false beliefs, which is implausible.**
