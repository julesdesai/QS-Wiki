# Can invariantism explain differing intuitive knowledge judgments in similar contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f06efe6-344e-4d56-95e5-64513adcac6f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Epistemic Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-epistemic-standards-synthesis-0f962817-db98-42be-9977-f6a5e2bd4d1d.md)

## Content

**Consider two scenarios: in one, a person believes a true proposition with full attention, and in another, they believe it while distracted. Intuitively, we judge that attention affects their knowing.**, **A principle states that focus and attention should affect whether someone knows a proposition.**, **If knowledge standards remain invariant, attention should not affect knowing, which seems counterintuitive.**
