# Is infallibility required for a foundational belief to confer knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8951910d-2cb9-4f5e-b116-213bb14aab81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Foundationalism](evidentialist-foundationalism-synthesis-26c84477-fc91-4190-8a0e-24d90c56393c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a foundational belief, based on immediate sensory experience, turns out to be mistaken due to illusion or perceptual error. Intuitively, the certainty required for foundational beliefs to confer knowledge is lacking.**, **A foundational belief that is fallible undermines the justification required for deriving further knowledge. For a belief to truly serve as foundational, it must be infallible or immune to doubt.**, **Knowledge derived from fallible beliefs inherits the uncertainty of its foundation, indicating that infallibility is necessary for the foundationalist claim that belief justified by foundational evidence amounts to knowledge.**
