# Might open-mindedness sometimes lead away from truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06ac2922-7b77-4add-8c65-d61b61d4ee57
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that open-mindedness, while typically virtuous, can lead one to entertain falsehoods as potentially true, which challenges its role in forming genuine knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where a person keeps an open mind about a debunked medical treatment, continuing to consider it viable despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary. Intuitively, this openness seems to detract from forming true knowledge.**
