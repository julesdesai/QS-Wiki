# Can knowledge extend beyond perceptual concepts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bbeac540-06aa-4b03-b9f1-6f6ffd7119b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Conceptual Empiricism](conceptual-empiricism-synthesis-7b58485d-3ea5-4513-ba7e-4d4d19b630bf.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that mathematical truths, such as "2+2=4," are known without direct perceptual experiences.**, **A person knows the proposition that "2+2=4" despite it not pertaining to concepts derived from perceptual experiences.**, **Mathematical objects do not appear to be external objects that can be known through the application of concepts generated by perceptual experiences.**
