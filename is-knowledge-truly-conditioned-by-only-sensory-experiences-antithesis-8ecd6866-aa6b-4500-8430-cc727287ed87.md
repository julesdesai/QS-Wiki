# Is knowledge truly conditioned by only sensory experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8ecd6866-aa6b-4500-8430-cc727287ed87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-2166f961-34d5-4334-bf8f-39dfb647eb18.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a sensory experience of seeing a red apple while wearing glasses that distort colors; intuitively, we would question if this experience is a true basis for knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should be stable under minor changes in sensory conditions; however, reliance solely on current sensory experiences makes this stability uncertain.**
