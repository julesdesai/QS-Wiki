# Does reliabilism account for changing reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a270ad8-3d6f-4789-9ba2-85fcfc86ad2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-c54e9ff1-39e1-4224-b3be-b0979177f411.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a cognitive process is reliable in one environment but not another, such as relying on visual perception in different lighting conditions. A belief formed under poor lighting might be reliable in one setting but not another. Intuitively, this challenges whether such beliefs can genuinely be justified.**, **Reliability can be contingent on shifting circumstances, complicating the application of reliabilism as a blanket justification.**
