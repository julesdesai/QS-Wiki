# How can Direct Realism account for perceptual illusions and hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 61822771-547b-4dcd-b0e6-b771db06a2e6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a stick partially submerged in water appears bent to an observer. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the observer perceives something at odds with the actual state of the object.**, **A case in which an individual in a desert sees a mirage of water that does not exist is a case in which the observer's perceptual experience is not providing direct access to an external object.**, **It is implausible that all perceptual experiences accurately represent external objects when illusions and hallucinations occur.**
