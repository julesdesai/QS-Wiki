# Is it possible for human cognition to grasp an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e50cfd7-323d-4352-b0c3-f38602bfbd7a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person tries to conceive of an infinite series of beliefs supporting each other. It seems implausible that a finite human mind can truly comprehend or mentally manage such an infinity.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a justification to be meaningful or usable, it must be conceivable by the human mind. This principle, together with the inconceivability of an infinite chain, suggests that such a chain cannot provide meaningful justification.**
