# Is context-sensitive justification susceptible to undermining by contrarian perspectives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b51ddfcf-2d11-4573-97b8-1b5e05fcc054
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual's belief is justified contextually within a supportive community but faces opposition from an external group with different epistemic standards. Intuitively, we would judge that such a context-sensitive justification may be undermined when confronted with differing perspectives, posing a challenge to its robustness across diverse epistemic communities.**
