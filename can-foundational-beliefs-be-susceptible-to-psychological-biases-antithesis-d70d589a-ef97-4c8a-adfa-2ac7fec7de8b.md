# Can foundational beliefs be susceptible to psychological biases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d70d589a-ef97-4c8a-adfa-2ac7fec7de8b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that psychological biases can affect what is considered self-evident, infallible, or evident to the senses. This principle together with known cognitive biases entails that basic beliefs might not be as epistemically secure as foundationalism suggests.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has a basic belief that is self-evident due to confirmation bias, leading them to ignore evidence to the contrary. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the justification of their belief is compromised.**
