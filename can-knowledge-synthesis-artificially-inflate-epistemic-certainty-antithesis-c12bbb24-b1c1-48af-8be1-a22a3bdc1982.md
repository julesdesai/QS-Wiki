# Can knowledge synthesis artificially inflate epistemic certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c12bbb24-b1c1-48af-8be1-a22a3bdc1982
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person combines weak perceptual data and weak testimonial evidence to form a strong belief. Intuitively, we question whether such synthesis unjustly inflates their certainty in the belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that stronger knowledge should arise from stronger evidence, not merely the combination of weak evidence.**, **It is implausible that synthesis from independently weak evidences could produce a level of certainty in knowledge that overrides the individual weakness of the evidences.**
