# Is belief alone enough to rule out accidents in acquiring knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 77579bf9-98e1-41c4-887a-a73eddef4294
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [True Belief as Primary in Knowledge](true-belief-as-primary-in-knowledge-synthesis-f83d0a87-6cde-4f50-a75a-0024fde50cce.md)

## Content

**Merely holding a belief does not prevent its truth from arising solely by accident.**, **Attributing knowledge to a true belief without further criteria could involve accidental circumstances providing an unreliable basis for knowledge.**, **Such situations suggest belief isn't enough for solidifying complete knowledge without some form of validation.**
