# Could coherence be accidental rather than virtue-driven?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55372d30-fde4-4224-9085-9ecdd77909e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Coherentism](virtue-epistemology-coherentism-synthesis-fa375fb7-6e26-497f-b63d-bbaec5305ff8.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a coherence of belief arises from random chance rather than through an exercise of intellectual virtues.**, **A network of beliefs can be coherent without it necessarily being the result of intellectual virtues.**, **It is conceivable that coherent beliefs occur under conditions of systematic error or bias despite being coherently random.**
