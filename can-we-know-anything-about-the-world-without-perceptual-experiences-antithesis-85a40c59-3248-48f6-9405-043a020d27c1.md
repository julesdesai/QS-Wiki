# Can we know anything about the world without perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85a40c59-3248-48f6-9405-043a020d27c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structuralism](epistemic-structuralism-synthesis-7fb538c7-d6b1-4a5d-b302-4f53eea0d56a.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge of external objects can only be of their structures within experiences, as this would exclude the possibility of knowledge about the existence of the world independent of personal perception**, **Consider a scenario where multiple people independently observe a physical event. Even if their perceptual experiences differ, they might all know that the event occurred, indicating knowledge of an external reality not entirely dependent on individual perceptual structures**
