# Is true belief without justification adequate for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3a7f621-f397-4027-9b10-5b0577a0b1d5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Theory of Knowledge](disjunctivist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-eb3c40bf-59e8-47a3-98db-de4d9568dbcc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual, Jack, randomly picks a card from a deck and forms a belief that he picked the ace of spades. If Jack's belief happens to be true, intuitively, we would not say Jack knows he picked the ace of spades, as his belief lacks justification beyond chance.**, **It is implausible that true belief without adequate justification constitutes knowledge, because this defies the notion that knowledge requires more grounded reasoning.**
