# Is usefulness a sufficient condition for knowledge in inherently unreliable methods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e0aeee9-7648-4e68-bb53-24a4bbd84a8b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Envision a situation where a person uses a coin flip to decide outcomes, and it consistently benefits them over time. Intuitively, the unreliability of the method undermines knowledge attribution.**, **Knowledge should require reliability in belief formation which is missing in inherently unreliable methods.**
