# Is reliability sufficient if the subject has no understanding or awareness of the process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 11874e94-4562-4f93-88cc-d55cbc1da900
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-c84f45b2-357a-4b29-be66-6a09ffb029e2.md)

## Content

**A person could have beliefs formed by a neural implant that reliably outputs true beliefs, but they are entirely unaware of its operation. Intuitively, this feels more like manipulation than genuine knowledge**, **It is implausible for knowledge to rest on a reliable process in circumstances where the subject has no awareness or understanding of how that process works**
