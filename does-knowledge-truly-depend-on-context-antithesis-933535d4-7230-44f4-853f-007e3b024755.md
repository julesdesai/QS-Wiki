# Does knowledge truly depend on context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 933535d4-7230-44f4-853f-007e3b024755
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-d7f52b7c-07b8-44a2-8804-2022a32ed27e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person claims to know a proposition in one context but not in another. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge varies with context or that the person has an incorrect understanding of knowledge. If we say knowledge varies with context, then we have to accept that knowledge is not stable. If we say the person misunderstands knowledge, then context does not determine knowledge.**
