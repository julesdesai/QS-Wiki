# Does the safety condition outdo sensitivity's role in ensuring knowledge alignment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f3a3343-db4f-4bde-81d7-96866b2f3f5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes correctly due to a high-frequency fluctuation but would believe falsely under similar nearby conditions.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief is safe if it couldn’t have easily turned out false in nearby possible worlds.**, **It is implausible that sensitivity provides stronger assurance than safety in ensuring belief aligns with reality in reliable knowledge.**
