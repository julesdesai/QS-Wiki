# Can knowledge result from unreliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e87e8a6e-9781-467f-bf48-f6ba2f08e011
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-498bb613-4448-44e8-9d2e-1c93717bc9f2.md)

## Content

**A person can have a true belief formed by an unreliable cognitive process, such as flipping a coin and getting the correct answer by chance.**, **A belief formed by an unreliable process cannot be considered knowledge due to the lack of a connection to truth.**
