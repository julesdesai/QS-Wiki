# Can proposition truth alone establish knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b79e9f7-351d-4bd3-8bed-8109f01c75bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-b49d8dfc-4ae9-4058-b817-2db750a2772b.md)

## Content

**A scenario involves someone asserting a proposition "the suspect is guilty" because it aligns with their values, rather than evidence, reveals that true propositions can be believed for non-knowledge grounded reasons.**, **Truth without justification can result in belief perseverance despite contrary evidence, as seen in conspiracy theories, which undermines its sole ability to define knowledge.**, **Suppose a genuine claim "the earth revolves around the sun" relayed by a figure of authority only convinces due to their status, not understanding, thereby lacking the epistemic robustness expected of knowledge.**
