# Does pragmatic encroachment blur the distinction between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment with Influence of Practical Stakes](pragmatic-encroachment-with-influence-of-practical-stakes-synthesis-1e83d295-b88f-4064-889e-097b4fb938ca.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that belief is a mental state while knowledge is a justified true belief. This principle highlights that practical stakes should not alter the conceptual distinction between belief and knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which practical stakes alter whether a person is said to know something. Intuitively, this alteration blurs the distinction between belief and knowledge by introducing pragmatic factors rather than evidential ones.**
