# Does contextualism allow for too much subjectivity in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** addcb206-00a1-4691-8e20-9d31c18ba92b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Multi-Standard Contextualism](multi-standard-contextualism-synthesis-9865cf66-a535-4334-b5b4-390c78d28080.md)

## Content

**If contextual standards for belief, truth, and justification vary independently, then what is knowledge for one person may not be knowledge for another.**, **There is a plausible principle that suggests that knowledge should have some objective commonality to avoid total subjectivity and arbitrariness in its attribution.**
