# Can a coherentist view account for perceptual errors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 385acc81-26b1-4a64-939c-4a470229380e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Knowledge Summary](coherentist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-1fa3c944-9677-491a-8edd-ea154ce998c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which perceptual errors are consistently integrated into a coherent belief system. Intuitively, the beliefs may seem justified within the system but inaccurate.**, **It is implausible to claim that coherence with perceptual errors can lead to robust grounds for knowledge. Coherent systems may integrate errors without detecting them, thus threatening justification.**
