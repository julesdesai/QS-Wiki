# Can contextually variable standards adequately account for knowledge transmission?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae691995-737f-485f-b5d7-f368a1984fc7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where a teacher conveys a fact to a student under everyday standards, and the student is expected to know it under more rigorous standards. Intuitively, there is a tension here because the transmitted knowledge could fail to meet the higher standards.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be reliably transmitted from person to person if contextually variable standards disrupt the commonality in what is considered known.**
