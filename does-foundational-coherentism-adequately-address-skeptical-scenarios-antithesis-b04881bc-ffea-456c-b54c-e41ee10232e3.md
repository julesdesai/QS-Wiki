# Does Foundational Coherentism adequately address skeptical scenarios?

## Content

**Consider a skeptical scenario, such as a brain in a vat, where beliefs are coherent and have apparent empirical input. Intuitively, we would judge that these beliefs are unjustified because they do not connect with the real world.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification requires contact with reality, suggesting that mere coherence and foundational empirical backing are insufficient in scenarios where empirical input could be misleading.**
