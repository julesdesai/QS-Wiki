# Are different types of knowledge truly reliant on different foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7e643a6-7e8a-4829-993d-7ac4ed7506dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**Knowledge types often interrelate and share common elements, suggesting that they may rely on overlapping foundational beliefs rather than entirely distinct ones.**, **Relying on different foundational beliefs for different types of knowledge implies compartmentalization of epistemic systems, which can lead to fragmentation rather than integration of knowledge.**, **Integration and coherence among types of knowledge are essential for a unified understanding of truth and reality.**
