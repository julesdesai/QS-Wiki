# Can contextualism adequately explain shared public knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c738a9c-4d1f-4431-9f77-fc0f7c93b529
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that knowledge should entail a degree of public agreement on standards. This principle together with varying contextual standards suggests that contextualism might fail to explain shared public knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario involving a widely accepted scientific fact. Intuitively, shared knowledge should not differ markedly due to individual context variations.**
