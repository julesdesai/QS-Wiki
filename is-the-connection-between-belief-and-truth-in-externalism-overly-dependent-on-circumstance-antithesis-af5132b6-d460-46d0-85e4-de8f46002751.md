# Is the connection between belief and truth in externalism overly dependent on circumstance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** af5132b6-d460-46d0-85e4-de8f46002751
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief in two identical mental states but in different environments where the truth of the belief varies. Intuitively, it seems problematic that external circumstances alone should affect whether one has knowledge.**, **It is implausible that external circumstances should heavily influence whether a belief constitutes knowledge, given identical internal reasoning.**
