# Does proper functioning assume too strong an internalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d16c4fc8-497b-4440-861a-7eaaf8a57fd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Proper Functionalism](knowledge-as-proper-functionalism-synthesis-95adcceb-ce7f-44ef-aea6-bebac418915b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a belief is true due to external factors unknown to the believer, such as relief during an indiscriminate act of luck. Intuitively, the person still gains knowledge without internal justification.**, **It is implausible that proper functioning requires the believer to be aware of all justifying conditions internally.**
