# Are intellectual virtues necessary for all epistemic goods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1c5db7d-d3d4-46db-91a9-8a9078dadab9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues are thought of as character traits that promote epistemic goods, but it is plausible that other factors, such as situation or environment, may also promote epistemic goods.**, **Suppose a scientist obtains experimental results through an automated process that does not engage any intellectual virtues. Intuitively, these results can still constitute an epistemic good despite the absence of the scientist's virtues.**
