# Is contextualism undermined by its dependence on conversational context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4759e3d6-c89c-4a8b-9e43-54f09357149e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**The idea that knowledge depends on conversational cues is implausible if such contexts can be influenced or manipulated, making knowledge claims arbitrary.**, **A plausible principle is that the truth conditions of knowledge should be independent of conversational maneuvers.**, **If knowledge attribution shifts through mere discourse, it risks reducing epistemic standards to rhetorical strategies.**
