# Does the Safety Condition Theory fail to address Gettier problems where justified true belief does not equal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d720340-1ef8-4bdb-8c32-1ec08401306d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which someone has a justified belief that a person owns a particular car due to seeing that person drive it, yet unbeknownst to them, the car had been stolen and later returned unbeknownst to them. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not possess knowledge even though they have justified true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that Gettier problems show that justified true belief is insufficient for knowledge. This principle entails that additional conditions are needed beyond the safety condition to truly define knowledge.**
