# Is evidence always necessary for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dde1577c-9404-4cf1-94cb-7d0a946b6eb2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View](evidentialist-view-synthesis-6f63629b-c949-4605-9acc-d91a2708886a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where an individual has an immediate emotional response, like the sudden fear of a dangerous animal, which results in a belief about imminent danger. Intuitively, we perceive this belief as justified despite the lack of time to accumulate evidence.**, **It is a logical fact that certain beliefs arise instantaneously in response to perceived threats or opportunities, and we treat them as justified for practical purposes. This logical observation implies that the requirement of evidence for all justified beliefs may be too stringent.**
