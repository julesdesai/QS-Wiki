# Can a conducive environment compensate for cognitive bias in judgments of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1c3bf2a6-83f9-42ac-a0fe-8c1e2f962c42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is biased due to prejudice, but forms a true belief about a topic due to the conducive nature of the environment. Intuitively, we would question whether this constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that cognitive biases negatively impact the epistemic status of beliefs. This principle together with conducive environmental factors questions whether favorable conditions can compensate for biased reasoning.**
