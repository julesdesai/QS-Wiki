# Can two equally coherent but conflicting systems both provide knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d74e96d6-07db-46b4-8b52-1fa48ff883f1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sticky Coherentism](sticky-coherentism-synthesis-cfe693b5-fe7c-40d5-b39e-4879bfdf6ed9.md)

## Content

**There can be two belief systems that are both internally coherent but make conflicting claims about the same proposition.**, **If coherence alone is the criterion for knowledge, both belief systems would provide justification, a paradox since conflicting claims cannot both represent knowledge.**
