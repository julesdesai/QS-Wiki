# Can knowledge of hypothetical scenarios involve perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 438f20a0-9457-4e1f-a73e-de3ee00f16d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a philosopher considers a hypothetical scenario purely for theoretical exploration with no perceptual engagement. Intuitively, it seems they can have knowledge about this scenario without any perceptual experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of hypothetical situations does not require perceptual engagement. This principle suggests that hypothetical knowledge can exist independently of perceptions.**
