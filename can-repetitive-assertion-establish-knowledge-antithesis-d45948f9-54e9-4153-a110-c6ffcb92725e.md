# Can repetitive assertion establish knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d45948f9-54e9-4153-a110-c6ffcb92725e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b7b03c88-6aa4-4b47-9f59-bc5548cc994c.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that merely repeatedly asserting a belief in different contexts can confer knowledge. Even if each assertion corresponds with contextual standards in those settings, repeatedly asserting a truth does not equate to knowing it, as knowledge should be more than contextually aligned assertion.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person asserts the same proposition across multiple conversations, meeting the fellow participants' expectations, albeit with varying context. Intuitively, we either have to accept that each assertion independently involves knowledge or that knowing requires some consistency beyond contextual standards. This undermines the notion that each contextual alignment equals knowledge.**
