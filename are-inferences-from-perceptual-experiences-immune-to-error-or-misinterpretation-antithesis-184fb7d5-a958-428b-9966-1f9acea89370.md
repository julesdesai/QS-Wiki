# Are inferences from perceptual experiences immune to error or misinterpretation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 184fb7d5-a958-428b-9966-1f9acea89370
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person infers there are clouds outside because the sky appears overcast through a foggy window. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that inference is susceptible to error or misinterpretation given the unclear medium.**, **There is a plausible principle that inferences based on potentially misleading information may result in false beliefs. This principle together with inferences from perceptual experiences entails vulnerability to error.**
