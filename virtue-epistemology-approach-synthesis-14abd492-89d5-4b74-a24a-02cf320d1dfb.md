## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person believes the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from the intellectual virtues of the agent**, **The intellectual virtues provide agents with the ability to reliably form true beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that these virtue conditions hold**
