# Is true belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac016182-7ac5-4033-9aff-867a8fa477dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-b49d8dfc-4ae9-4058-b817-2db750a2772b.md)

## Content

**If a person believes they are drinking water, yet in reality it is vodka, their belief is true in another sense but lacks knowledge due to mistaken identity of substance.**, **True belief alone might be coincidental correctness, such as guessing the correct answer to a math problem without understanding the rationale, compromising its status as knowledge.**, **Consider a multiple-choice exam where a student guesses the correct answers without studying; the belief coincides with truth but lacks justificatory substance to count as knowledge.**
