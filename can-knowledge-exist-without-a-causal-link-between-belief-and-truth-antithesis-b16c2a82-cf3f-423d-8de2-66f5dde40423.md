# Can knowledge exist without a causal link between belief and truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b16c2a82-cf3f-423d-8de2-66f5dde40423
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-916d423a-1723-4911-b4ab-5dfda1d6916a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person, Carla, believes it will rain because her knee hurts, which often correlates with rain, and indeed it rains. There is no direct causal connection between her belief and the fact of rain. Intuitively, we might judge Carla does not know it will rain despite the truth of her belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that to know a proposition one must have more than mere correlation; there must be a direct causal explanation linking belief and truth. This principle together with the given scenario entails that Carla does not know it will rain, challenging the causal theory.**
