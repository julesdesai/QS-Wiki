# Is a virtue sufficiently enough to ensure justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7d2fc3b-53b1-45c2-a299-3c82ce409aa7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone forms a belief in a way generally considered virtuous, yet without understanding or evidence supporting the belief. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is not justified or that justification can exist absent evidence or understanding. If we say the belief is not justified, then we challenge the sufficiency of intellectual virtues for justification. If we say justification can exist without evidence or understanding, we risk allowing justification based on ignorance.**
