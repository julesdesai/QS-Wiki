# How well does the Tracking Theory address Gettier-type problems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 455e89d4-c6d0-4ad8-9848-1d447cf8f00d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3fc68b12-ca29-40d7-8341-51e40d71c5e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a stop sign and believes there is a stop sign ahead, but unbeknownst to them, the sign is actually a cleverly disguised mirror reflecting another sign behind them. Intuitively, we hesitate to attribute knowledge.**, **If no actual stop sign was there, they would still believe there is one due to the reflection.**, **In slightly different contexts (different angle of the mirror), the belief would not show resilience due to dependency on misleading evidence.**
