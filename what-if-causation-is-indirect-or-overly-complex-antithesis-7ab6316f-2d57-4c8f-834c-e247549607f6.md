# What if causation is indirect or overly complex?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ab6316f-2d57-4c8f-834c-e247549607f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person holds a true belief about a distant event, but the causal chain is exceedingly long and complex. Intuitively, we might doubt their knowledge due to the indirect nature of the causation.**, **A belief caused by an intricate chain of events can satisfy the causal condition, yet the complexity can lead to doubts about whether true knowledge is present.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires direct or clear causation. Overly complex causal chains, even if accurate, may still undermine the status of the belief as knowledge.**
