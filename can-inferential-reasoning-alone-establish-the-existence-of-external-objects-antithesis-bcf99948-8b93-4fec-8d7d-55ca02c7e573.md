# Can inferential reasoning alone establish the existence of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bcf99948-8b93-4fec-8d7d-55ca02c7e573
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that external objects must affect a person's perceptions directly for those objects to be known.**, **Inferential reasoning alone, without direct perceptual evidence, fails to establish the existence of external objects.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has perceptual experiences of a mirage; intuitively, inferring the existence of water from the mirage is not a case of knowing about water.**
