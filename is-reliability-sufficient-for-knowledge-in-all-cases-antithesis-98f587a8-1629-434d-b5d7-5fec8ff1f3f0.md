# Is reliability sufficient for knowledge in all cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98f587a8-1629-434d-b5d7-5fec8ff1f3f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person has a true belief resulting from a reliable process, but fails to understand the specific reason the belief is true. Intuitively, we would hesitate to say the person knows the belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing a proposition involves understanding why the proposition is true. This principle suggests that reliability alone may be insufficient for knowledge.**
