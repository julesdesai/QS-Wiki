# Can accidental truth be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e040de07-0416-407d-a693-6306f5881150
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it will rain tomorrow because they always guess 'rain,' and it happens to rain. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to say they knew it would rain, despite their belief being true.**, **It is implausible to regard a belief as knowledge if its truth is accidental or coincidental rather than based on good reasons or evidence.**
