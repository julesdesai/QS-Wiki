# Is belief sufficient for knowledge if justification and truth are met contextually?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2f2a1a6-75e8-4ded-96a7-5d236fdbe4fd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-4f1ad7a2-76eb-4161-89f7-f4b83097a73b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a person who, because of cultural or educational context, firmly believes a widely debunked conspiracy theory. The belief is held with justification within their context and is mistakenly true.**, **Intuitively, we would have to say that mere belief is not sufficient for knowledge without critical evaluation outside one's context. This challenges the component that belief suffices when justified and true within a context.**, **It is implausible that contextually justified belief should count as knowledge if it lacks critical scrutiny against broader standards of truth.**
