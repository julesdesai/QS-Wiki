# Does reliabilism address differences between internalist and externalist views effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0f13f9e-3ae6-42c1-a988-fdfaf228c62b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**Reliabilism fundamentally aligns with externalism—focusing on the reliability of the process rather than the subject's awareness**, **Consider a scenario where someone forms a true belief through an obscure neural process they cannot access or understand**, **An internalist might object that without personal awareness of the process, the person lacks true epistemic justification, which reliabilism alone may not satisfactorily bridge between these views**
