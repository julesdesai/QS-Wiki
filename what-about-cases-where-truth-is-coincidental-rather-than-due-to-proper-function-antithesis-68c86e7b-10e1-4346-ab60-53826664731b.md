# What about cases where truth is coincidental rather than due to proper function?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68c86e7b-10e1-4346-ab60-53826664731b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-eb8cad20-b2ac-4ebe-8b63-c23141145814.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person achieves a true belief, but it is due to sheer coincidence rather than a proper function of cognitive faculties. Intuitively, this person does not have knowledge of the proposition despite believing something true.**, **Coincidental truth challenges the idea that cognitive faculties’ proper function is sufficient for determining knowledge.**
