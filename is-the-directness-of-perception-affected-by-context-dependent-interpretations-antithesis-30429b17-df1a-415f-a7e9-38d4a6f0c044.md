# Is the directness of perception affected by context-dependent interpretations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30429b17-df1a-415f-a7e9-38d4a6f0c044
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees an ambiguous image that can be interpreted as either a rabbit or a duck depending on contextual cues. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception involves interpretative processes based on context.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception provides direct access, then it should not be heavily influenced by context-dependent factors. This principle, together with the observation of context-influenced perception, entails that perception may not be direct.**
