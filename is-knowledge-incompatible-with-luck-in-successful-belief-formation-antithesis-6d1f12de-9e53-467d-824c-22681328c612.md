# Is knowledge incompatible with luck in successful belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d1f12de-9e53-467d-824c-22681328c612
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person forms a true belief through a lucky guess. Intuitively, we would hesitate to claim the person has knowledge due to the element of luck involved, even if the belief is true.**, **The involvement of luck in a true belief formation conflicts with the requirement for reliably leading to true beliefs, posing a challenge to the view that intellectual virtue is necessary for knowledge.**
