# Is justifiability only determined by practical consequences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d69d675f-dd0f-490a-8659-39a33cba461b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-af5a1410-77c2-4ceb-b390-2ced8876b488.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the justifiability of a belief can be assessed based on evidence alone, without regard for practical outcomes. This principle suggests that even if a belief does not lead to practical consequences, it can still be justified if it is supported by strong evidence.**, **Suppose a mathematician believes in the truth of an esoteric mathematical theorem based solely on proving it through deductive logic. Intuitively, this belief is justified irrespective of its practical implications.**
