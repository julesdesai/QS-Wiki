# Can true justified beliefs that fail practically still constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd448112-25be-469f-9780-6c551d4c0b86
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-c3735471-f546-42ec-8e21-f2451431b4e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief that is true and justified but fails to lead to successful action due to uncontrollable external factors. Intuitively, we would not want to deny that the person has knowledge**, **A plausible principle is that the uncontrollability of failure should not necessarily impact whether a true, justified belief is considered knowledge**
