# Is reliance on intellectual virtue necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dbc075e6-21c9-45a6-9ce7-0b7d59821dc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief by sheer luck without the exercise of any intellectual virtues. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that this belief might still count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can sometimes be acquired independently of intellectual virtue. This principle together with the scenario in which knowledge can arise through luck suggests that the necessity of intellectual virtue for knowledge is questionable.**
