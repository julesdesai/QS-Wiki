# Is reliability sufficient for earning a belief its justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0a774a15-2bf8-41f2-bfb8-06383827103c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Reliable Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-reliable-basic-beliefs-synthesis-62bc80a9-ef75-4d49-9578-a29890370bd2.md)

## Content

**A belief is justified if it is either foundational or derived from foundational beliefs through reliable methods.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a method is generally reliable, individual cases might still lead to justified false beliefs.**, **Suppose a belief derived through a generally reliable method is false; despite the method's reliability, the belief lacks true justification.**
