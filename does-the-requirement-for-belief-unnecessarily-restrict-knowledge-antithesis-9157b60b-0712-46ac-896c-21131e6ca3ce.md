# Does the requirement for belief unnecessarily restrict knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9157b60b-0712-46ac-896c-21131e6ca3ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Enhanced Virtue Reliabilism with Error Avoidance](enhanced-virtue-reliabilism-with-error-avoidance-synthesis-bae843f7-47b1-4791-af4f-a08b903c892d.md)

## Content

**Consider a proposition known objectively, such as a mathematical truth, to which one has no personal belief. Intuitively, the truth of the proposition remains despite belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that belief is unnecessary for truth; hence, conceiving belief as a necessary condition for knowledge may be excluding objective knowledge.**
