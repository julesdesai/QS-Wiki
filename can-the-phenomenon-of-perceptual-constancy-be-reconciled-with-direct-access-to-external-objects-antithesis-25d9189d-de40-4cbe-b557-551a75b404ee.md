# Can the phenomenon of perceptual constancy be reconciled with direct access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 25d9189d-de40-4cbe-b557-551a75b404ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a white piece of paper looks the same under different lighting conditions, such as bright daylight or dim candlelight. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception compensates for changes, or that perception varies directly with external changes. If perception compensates, then it involves processing that mediates perception. If perception varies directly, this contradicts experiential stability known as perceptual constancy.**
