# Is the notion of 'non-arbitrary reasons' sufficiently clear to ensure justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eabe5d06-078c-49cc-8ca8-d0cdb9bbf41e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Non-arbitrary Reasons](infinitism-with-non-arbitrary-reasons-synthesis-11967ecc-f2b8-44cb-a381-b7e79afac297.md)

## Content

**A reason is non-arbitrary if it is both relevant and justified.**, **The criteria to determine the non-arbitrariness in an infinite context may be vague or inconsistent.**, **Ambiguity in what constitutes non-arbitrary reasons can undermine the clarity required for justification.**
