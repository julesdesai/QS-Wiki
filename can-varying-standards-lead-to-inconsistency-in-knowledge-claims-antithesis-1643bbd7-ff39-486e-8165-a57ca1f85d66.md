# Can varying standards lead to inconsistency in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1643bbd7-ff39-486e-8165-a57ca1f85d66
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism With Context-Dependent Justification Standards](contextualism-with-context-dependent-justification-standards-synthesis-a8126985-f485-4c51-b545-16d8a583ea1e.md)

## Content

**Suppose there are two different contexts with different standards for justification. In one context, a person is said to know a proposition, while in another, the same person does not know the same proposition due to higher standards. Intuitively, it seems inconsistent to declare that both claims about knowledge are true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be consistent across similar cases. This principle together with the idea that in different contexts the same proposition can be both known and not known leads to a contradiction.**
