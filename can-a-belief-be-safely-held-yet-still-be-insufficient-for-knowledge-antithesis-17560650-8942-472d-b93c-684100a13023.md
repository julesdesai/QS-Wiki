# Can a belief be safely held yet still be insufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17560650-8942-472d-b93c-684100a13023
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-df26c783-c115-4102-a084-91df0b928704.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone believes a proposition based on a reliable source which has provided accurate information in nearly all past instances, but in this specific case, the source inadvertently gives the wrong information, yet the belief happens to be true by coincidence.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that although the belief is safely held, it does not constitute knowledge.**
