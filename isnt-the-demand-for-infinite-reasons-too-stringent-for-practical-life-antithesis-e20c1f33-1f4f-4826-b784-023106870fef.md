# Isn't the demand for infinite reasons too stringent for practical life?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e20c1f33-1f4f-4826-b784-023106870fef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-a48a630f-d4e8-4dc2-b25a-518a64dbd1fb.md)

## Content

**Consider knowing that a chair will hold your weight. Requiring infinite reasons for this belief is unrealistic for practical, everyday decisions, creating a barrier to knowledge.**, **In daily life, we function using finite, sufficient reasons rather than infinite chains, suggesting that realistic knowledge does not require endless justifications.**
