# Are regular perceptual patterns sufficient for causal understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a13aff8-9fd2-4564-959e-8452b598b37b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person repeatedly observes that rain is followed by wet streets but does not understand the causal mechanisms of precipitation and water flow. Intuitively, we would attribute their lack of causal understanding to the absence of inferential reasoning about underlying processes.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding causality requires inferential reasoning about underlying mechanisms, not solely reliance on regular patterns of perceptual experiences.**
