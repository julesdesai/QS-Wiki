# How is external justification sufficient for knowledge without conscious accessibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf8d657c-d44b-4571-b68b-480cbc2242ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Belief Necessary View](no-belief-necessary-view-synthesis-81f9ccf2-9484-47e2-8ae4-3b9845798b1d.md)

## Content

**Justification has traditionally been associated with the ability to access the reasons or grounds for belief, and it's unclear how it remains justification when it is entirely external.**, **Suppose someone has no access to the reasons for their knowledge and cannot articulate why they "know" something; intuitively, this seems more akin to guessing.**, **If justification is external and not consciously accessible, it becomes mystifying how individuals can be held accountable for their knowledge.**
