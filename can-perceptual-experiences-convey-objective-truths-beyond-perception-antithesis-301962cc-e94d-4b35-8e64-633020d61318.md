# Can perceptual experiences convey objective truths beyond perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 301962cc-e94d-4b35-8e64-633020d61318
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-thesis-2daa98ba-8f8c-4200-a8b1-c2a336a65d2f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people perceive a tree from different angles and lighting conditions. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the tree remains the same despite differing perceptions.**, **There is a plausible principle that if knowledge is based exclusively on perceptual experience, then knowledge cannot affirm the constancy of objects independent of varying perspectives. This principle together with the constancy of an object under different perceptions entails that perception-based knowledge is insufficient to account for objective truths.**
