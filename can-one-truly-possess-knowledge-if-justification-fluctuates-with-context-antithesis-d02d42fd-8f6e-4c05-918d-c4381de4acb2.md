# Can one truly possess knowledge if justification fluctuates with context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d02d42fd-8f6e-4c05-918d-c4381de4acb2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person sees dark clouds and claims to know it will rain. In a planning meeting, their claim is justified if rain affects the event, suggesting a higher need for accuracy and thus stronger evidence.**, **In casual talk with a friend, the same claim about rain may be more easily justified due to the lower stakes.**, **Intuitively, if justification standards shift with context, the claim might be both true and believed in one scenario but unjustified in another, challenging whether knowledge is genuinely possessed.**
