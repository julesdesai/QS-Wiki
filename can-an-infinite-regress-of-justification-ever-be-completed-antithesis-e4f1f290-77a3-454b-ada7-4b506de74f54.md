# Can an infinite regress of justification ever be completed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4f1f290-77a3-454b-ada7-4b506de74f54
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Any series with a starting point, an end point, or a finite number of elements cannot be truly infinite.**, **Human cognitive capabilities, including memory and understanding, are finite and cannot process an infinite chain.**, **A requirement for an infinite chain would leave most beliefs without access to complete justification, which seems to undermine the possibility of having knowledge altogether.**
