# Is it possible for a false belief to be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1399dc33-8f17-447e-a849-28c6dbd0ade6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d871be87-85c4-4634-87bb-b00a8ec22277.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual has a strong belief based on overwhelming but misleading evidence that turns out to be false. In such a situation, intuitively, we might argue the person has knowledge until the evidence is disproved.**, **This suggests that a belief's truth might not be strictly necessary for it to be considered knowledge if the justification appears strong.**
