# Is the contextualist approach to knowledge too flexible to be meaningful?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69e4f793-1c45-406c-98bc-0d3c5ba8f69f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**The contextual criteria for justification can vary widely depending on who is considering the proposition.**, **If justification standards change, what is justified true belief for one person might not be for another.**, **If people use different justification standards, the term 'knowledge' loses its clear meaning.**, **The variability in standards seems to undermine the objective notion of knowledge.**
