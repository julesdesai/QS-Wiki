# Can knowledge be contingent on environment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a949dd9-c517-4615-92c2-c3ce4291f723
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-92e582e4-32a5-4017-b62b-ea7485c8afb8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's belief about an external world fact is true in their current environment but would be false if the environment were slightly altered. Intuitively, we would judge that their belief does not constitute knowledge because it heavily depends on the environment being exactly as it is.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should not be overly contingent on environmental factors beyond the agent's control, but tracking allows for such contingencies.**
