# Could infinite justification lead to epistemic paralysis?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5fce53af-ab54-4837-a5f9-a7f32fa053ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Epistemic paralysis is the situation where action is indefinitely postponed due to an inability to resolve epistemic duties.**, **If infinite regress in justification implies that the task of justifying can never be complete, decision-making is obstructed.**, **Practical epistemology suggests the need for a stopping point to allow for action, which is absent in infinite regress.**
