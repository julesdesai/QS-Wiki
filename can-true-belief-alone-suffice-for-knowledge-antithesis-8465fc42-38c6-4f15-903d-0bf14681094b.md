# Can true belief alone suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8465fc42-38c6-4f15-903d-0bf14681094b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person randomly guesses the correct answer to a quiz question without understanding why the answer is correct. In this scenario, the person does not seem to have knowledge despite their true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification or rational support beyond mere belief in a true proposition. This implies that true belief alone is insufficient for constituting knowledge.**
