# Does indirect realism adequately account for perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bb15b138-93ae-44cd-aa96-426a2d5b4e35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Contextual Perception](indirect-realism-with-contextual-perception-synthesis-77e2d877-90d5-4b41-9238-09aba0491b6a.md)

## Content

**Perceptual experiences can sometimes lead to errors, such as when a stick appears bent in water but is straight.**, **Indirect realism suggests these experiences are mediated by mental representations, but errors occur even when there is no change in the mental representation.**, **If knowledge involves perception filtered through interpretative processes, then errors should consistently inform us about these processes if indirect realism is true.**
