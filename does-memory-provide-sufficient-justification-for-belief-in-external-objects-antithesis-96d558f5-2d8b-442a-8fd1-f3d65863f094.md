# Does memory provide sufficient justification for belief in external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96d558f5-2d8b-442a-8fd1-f3d65863f094
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Appeal to Memory](phenomenalism-with-appeal-to-memory-synthesis-2b7704c1-89ef-4cc3-a1d9-427b8175dc3f.md)

## Content

**Belief in the existence of external objects requires justification that cannot solely depend on past experiences stored in memory.**, **If memory can be deceptive, then any inference made from it lacks the necessary certainty to provide sufficient justification for belief in external objects.**
