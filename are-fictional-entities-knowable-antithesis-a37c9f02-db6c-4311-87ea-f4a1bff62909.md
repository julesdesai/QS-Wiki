# Are fictional entities knowable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a37c9f02-db6c-4311-87ea-f4a1bff62909
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Conceptual Empiricism](conceptual-empiricism-synthesis-7b58485d-3ea5-4513-ba7e-4d4d19b630bf.md)

## Content

**A person can discuss and know about fictional entities such as 'Sherlock Holmes' without perceptual experiences of them.**, **Knowledge of fictional entities does not necessarily pertain to concepts derived from actual perceptual experiences.**, **Fictional objects are known in ways that are not based on the application of concepts generated by perceptual experiences.**
