# How do different epistemic systems define standards without a universal criterion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30317852-8ac1-44cc-a5dd-3d7326ec8dc4

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Pluralism](epistemic-pluralism-synthesis-81e4116f-f2b8-419c-97e5-1ca5c482c2fd.md)

## Content

**Differing standards for what constitutes knowledge suggest that each system operates in a silo without a common measure.**, **Without a universal criterion, comparing or adjudicating between different epistemic systems becomes conceptually problematic.**, **Incompatibility between standards may lead to epistemic relativism, undermining claims of universal knowledge.**
