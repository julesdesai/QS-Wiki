# Can isolated coherent systems still have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8feb3f8-8fb4-4c28-bec6-7b9826a10ac9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-189c934d-40a0-41d9-b70b-1a7a0a9cc7c3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two isolated communities develop entirely coherent and self-contained systems of beliefs that are mutually contradictory. Intuitively, we either have to say that both possess knowledge or only one does. If we say both possess knowledge, then we must accept contradictory knowledge claims. If we say only one does, then coherence alone is insufficient.**
