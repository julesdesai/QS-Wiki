# Can knowledge occur without practical reasoning performance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e2c12223-be78-473c-96b1-6f982a2a3761
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief, although true, does not enhance the person's ability to perform in practical reasoning situations, such as a forgotten fact that has no relevance to current decisions. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not gain knowledge merely because of the truth of a forgotten fact without its use in practical reasoning.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be independent of its utility in practical reasoning, emphasizing understanding and insight rather than just performance.**
