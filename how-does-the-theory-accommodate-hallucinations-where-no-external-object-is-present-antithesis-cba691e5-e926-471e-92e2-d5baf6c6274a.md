# How does the theory accommodate hallucinations where no external object is present?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cba691e5-e926-471e-92e2-d5baf6c6274a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-2d3f5cf7-4de1-4047-bfb0-105e4b8ca669.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person hallucinates a pink elephant but there is no elephant present. Intuitively, we must say that the person perceives something, or perceives nothing. If we say the person perceives nothing, then it conflicts with the intuition that they are having a perceptual experience. If we say they perceive something directly, then it conflicts with the non-existence of the object.**
