# Can Reliabilism handle environmental luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83852db9-a995-49b5-9c9c-a414a48bde73
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-e69ee86a-1c0a-4d0e-840e-f25c67fd8e9a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person sees a barn in a region full of fake barns, and they form a true belief that they are looking at a real barn because they are indeed looking at the one real barn in the area. Intuitively, we would judge that due to the presence of fake barns, the cognitive process producing the belief is not reliable, and thus the person does not have knowledge.**, **A reliable cognitive process is one that consistently leads to true beliefs across different contexts. The presence of misleading information in the environment can disrupt the reliability of the belief-forming process, even when a belief is true.**
