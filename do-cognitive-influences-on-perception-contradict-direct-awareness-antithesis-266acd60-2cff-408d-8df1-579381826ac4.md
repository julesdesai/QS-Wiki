# Do cognitive influences on perception contradict direct awareness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 266acd60-2cff-408d-8df1-579381826ac4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-ad05d8f4-c9d2-4024-8001-bf520360cf02.md)

## Content

**Experiments show that beliefs, expectations, and desires can influence how we perceive objects, such as expecting to see a face influencing one's perception of a pattern.**, **Direct awareness would mean that perception is unaffected by cognitive states.**, **If cognition affects perception, then perception cannot be purely direct awareness of objects.**
