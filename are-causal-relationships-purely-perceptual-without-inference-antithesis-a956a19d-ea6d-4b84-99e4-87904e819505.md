# Are causal relationships purely perceptual without inference?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a956a19d-ea6d-4b84-99e4-87904e819505
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a tree branch fall and hears a loud crash immediately after. Intuitively, we would judge that the person infers that the crash is caused by the branch falling, rather than solely relying on the regularity of perceptual experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that causal relationships involve not just perception but also inference about the connection between cause and effect. This principle entails that regular patterns of perception alone are insufficient for understanding causal relationships.**
