# Does intellectual courage itself imply correctness of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 695aee13-2b66-4b99-9642-465ec6cde5e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual courage, while commendable, inherently implies the correctness of belief when it may lead to defending unsupported or false claims.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an academic courageously defends a theory universally dismissed by peer review and evidence. Intuitively, their courage alone does not validate the belief.**
