# Is invariantism consistent with practical decision-making?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bbdf96e0-4de8-41d2-b78e-e6735acc2c63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-2877eecc-8388-4e01-b87d-ffe7f20b2619.md)

## Content

**In practice, people adjust their knowledge claims based on the importance of being correct.**, **A high-stakes situation, like ensuring a medication is safe, requires stricter standards for knowledge than a low-stakes situation, like predicting the weather.**, **Invariantism suggests that stakes should not affect knowledge, opposing the intuitive adjustments made for practical purposes.**
