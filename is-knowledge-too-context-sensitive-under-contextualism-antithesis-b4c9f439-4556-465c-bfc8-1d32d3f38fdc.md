# Is knowledge too context-sensitive under contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4c9f439-4556-465c-bfc8-1d32d3f38fdc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [The Contextualist View of Knowledge](the-contextualist-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-744b190b-128c-4187-8edf-0a707cd161d1.md)

## Content

**A proposition considered known in one context might not be known in another due to differing standards of justification.**, **This context-sensitivity suggests an instability and inconsistency in what people can claim to know.**, **The reliability and objectivity of knowledge might be compromised as a result.**
