## Content

**There is a plausible principle that language shapes thought and perception. This principle together with evidence from studies on linguistic relativity entails that internalized language structures may influence perceptual processes.**, **Consider a scenario where language allows the distinction between colors that others perceive as identical. Intuitively, we judge that linguistic categories modify perception, potentially requiring internal representations.**
