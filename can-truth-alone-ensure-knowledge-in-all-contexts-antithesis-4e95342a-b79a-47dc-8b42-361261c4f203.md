# Can truth alone ensure knowledge in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e95342a-b79a-47dc-8b42-361261c4f203
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true. However, it is implausible that truth alone can guarantee someone's knowledge without considering the justification and methods behind the belief.**, **Imagine a situation where someone stumbles upon a true belief by chance. Intuitively, they do not truly know the proposition because their belief lacks a proper basis, despite its truth.**
