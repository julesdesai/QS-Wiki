# Can one know something through accident or luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 173ceb07-4011-4545-90f7-b40a35b0204e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a true proposition through sheer luck rather than intellectual virtue. Intuitively, if the truth was arrived at purely by chance, it seems implausible to count this as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge cannot be the result of mere luck. This principle together with cases of accidental correctness challenges the necessity of intellectual virtue in defining knowledge.**
