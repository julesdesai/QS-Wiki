# Can a false belief inadvertently lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bcd3c274-e71a-407d-8868-3a4c8af8a614
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Reliabilism](virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-43b268cc-327c-4b25-91ff-4eb7346ac08b.md)

## Content

**There are odd cases where holding a false belief in one aspect leads to forming a true belief in another aspect that one did not explicitly consider, thereby suggesting something akin to knowledge may arise.**, **A person may misinterpret some evidence leading to the true belief which appears to pose a challenge to the necessity of all belief components being entirely accurate for knowledge.**
