# How can knowledge exist without conscious awareness of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eeafde1e-4368-4347-8e4d-72b0a813ccf2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**In certain situations, a person might hold a true belief formed by a reliable process without any conscious awareness of how their belief is justified.**, **Intuitively, if the person cannot consciously provide reasons or access the justification for their belief, it seems problematic to claim that they have knowledge.**, **It is plausible that having access to the justification is a necessary aspect of knowledge.**
