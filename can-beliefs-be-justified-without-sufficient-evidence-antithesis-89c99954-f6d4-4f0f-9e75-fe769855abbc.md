# Can beliefs be justified without sufficient evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89c99954-f6d4-4f0f-9e75-fe769855abbc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d871be87-85c4-4634-87bb-b00a8ec22277.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person accurately predicts the future based on a gut feeling without any evidence. In such a case, intuitively, we might question whether their belief is justified even though they lack evidence.**, **It seems plausible that justification might sometimes arise from factors other than evidence, such as reliable intuition. This suggests justification could exist independently of sufficient evidence.**
