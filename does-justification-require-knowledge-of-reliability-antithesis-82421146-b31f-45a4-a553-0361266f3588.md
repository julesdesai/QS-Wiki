# Does justification require knowledge of reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 82421146-b31f-45a4-a553-0361266f3588
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Coherentism](reliabilist-coherentism-synthesis-d6c1c642-66ac-40b0-82ca-187cf7250017.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a person can be said to have justified belief if they have no awareness or understanding of the reliability of the cognitive process involved.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief produced by a reliable process, but they have no insight into the workings of this process. Intuitively, we question whether this belief is truly justified or a matter of luck.**
