# How does Indirect Realism account for hallucinations or illusions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39b2b938-44ac-446d-bf95-0ffa44d0ac4b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-1b3cd8c8-92a3-4a52-be84-8ec951a45683.md)

## Content

**In cases of hallucination, there are mental representations without corresponding external objects.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge requires a reliable connection between our mental representations and external reality.**, **Since hallucinations involve mental representations that do not correspond to reality, they challenge the reliability required for knowledge.**
