# Does the notion of seeming justification adequately account for the presence of defeaters?

## Content

**Suppose a belief seems true to a person based on evidence available to them, but there exists some contrary evidence they aren't aware of. Intuitively, the presence of unknown defeaters undermines the claim that the person knows the proposition.**
