# Does hallucination challenge the reliability of perception for causal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e5238601-89bb-47af-b529-50cc210110f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-aa743ec0-e3e4-4aae-9408-fa20370276b8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone is experiencing a vivid hallucination, perceiving an object that isn't present in reality. Intuitively, this perception does not accurately reflect any actual causal structure.**, **Hallucinations demonstrate that perception can occur without a causal interaction with real external objects, challenging the idea that perception always leads to understanding causal structures.**
