# Does contextualism make sudden new knowledge possible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9e719f3-fb01-4e3d-9f59-c0a8023cba56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge Summary](contextualist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-c38eaf7b-9feb-49d3-8375-2d19895c61fa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person gains a new piece of information that changes the context. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that what they know instantly changes, suggesting knowledge can appear suddenly without additional justification.**, **A case in which context dictates knowledge easily allows for sudden shifts, creating a problematic flexibility in epistemic status.**
