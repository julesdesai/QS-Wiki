# Could valuable falsehoods disrupt the knowledge claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 11950531-18d9-4922-b793-cb9e950bb6bf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**A case in which believing a false yet consistent narrative leads to significant practical advantages, such as in storytelling for peace-building. Intuitively, these are not instances of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is necessary for knowledge. This principle together with the acceptance of valuable falsehoods threatens the notion that practical success alone suffices.**
