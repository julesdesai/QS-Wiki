# Can intellectually virtuous beliefs be undermined by later evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57529963-7d9c-49c2-89b6-33b64be67b3c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual forms a true belief due to an intellectual virtue, but later evidence suggests the reasoning or method was flawed. Intuitively, we might question whether their earlier belief still constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be undermined by subsequent evidence of flawed belief formation. This principle challenges the notion that beliefs derived from intellectual virtues are infallibly justified.**
