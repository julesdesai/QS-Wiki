# No Coincidence Reliabilism

**Parent:** [Does sheer coincidence override reliability?](does-sheer-coincidence-override-reliability-antithesis-9f542ca6-b446-4e28-a55a-7ea537c1211a.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **Knowledge requires the belief to be reliably produced**, **A reliable cognitive process produces true beliefs more often than not**, **For a belief to count as knowledge, the truth of the belief must not be due to a coincidental factor unrelated to the reliability of the process**
