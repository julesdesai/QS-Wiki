# Is knowledge still valid if the causal link is indirect?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 878ad6a9-0c42-4432-8856-fdbf5fff73ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P believes that it will rain because they saw clouds in the sky this morning. However, unbeknownst to P, a weather control agency manipulated the clouds, while the rain was scheduled due to other atmospheric factors later discovered. Intuitively, P's belief seems not to be true knowledge due to the indirect causal link.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a direct causal connection. This principle together with the necessary causal link in true beliefs entails that indirect causes do not justify knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a person's knowledge can be based on coincidental or indirect causes.**
