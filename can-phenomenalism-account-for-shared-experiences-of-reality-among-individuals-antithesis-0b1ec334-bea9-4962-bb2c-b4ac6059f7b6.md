# Can phenomenalism account for shared experiences of reality among individuals?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0b1ec334-bea9-4962-bb2c-b4ac6059f7b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-6256cdee-fde6-4d81-b9e8-d1897bfcfd04.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two individuals perceive a blue sky. Intuitively, we would judge that there is a common object being perceived independent of their individual sensory experiences.**, **It is implausible that shared experiences of an external reality are merely coincidental alignments of individual sensory data. This suggests that reality might include entities beyond just sensory data.**
