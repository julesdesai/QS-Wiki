# Can incoherent perceptual experiences still contribute to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5289af7c-ad37-48e8-9375-cc3d4669ab53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Coherence Theory](phenomenal-coherence-theory-synthesis-37d80231-0661-49bf-9bec-8b3f779084e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two seemingly incoherent perceptual experiences lead to a surprising but accurate understanding of a complex situation, thereby contributing to knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that these experiences contribute to knowledge despite their initial incoherence.**, **It is plausible to suggest that knowledge can arise from synthesizing incoherent perceptual experiences into a new coherent understanding, challenging the notion that only pre-existing coherence can contribute to knowledge.**
