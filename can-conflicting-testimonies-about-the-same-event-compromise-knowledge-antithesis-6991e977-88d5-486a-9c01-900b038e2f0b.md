# Can conflicting testimonies about the same event compromise knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6991e977-88d5-486a-9c01-900b038e2f0b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals witness the same car accident and provide contradictory testimonies about who caused it. Intuitively, we either have to say that neither testimony contributes to certain knowledge regarding the cause of the accident, or that we must arbitrarily choose one testimony. If we say neither contributes to knowledge, then the reliance on testimony becomes problematic. If we choose arbitrarily, then the basis for knowledge becomes questionable.**
