# Can externalism appropriately address epistemic disagreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 026c1fdb-0599-4b9e-80da-8adbf982a84f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-9fb6627e-b63e-4d81-a81a-128eb1da4172.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals hold opposing beliefs formed under the same external conditions. Intuitively, we would judge that epistemic disagreement challenges the notion that external conditions alone suffice for justification.**, **A framework that relies on external conditions fails to adequately explain how divergent beliefs emerge and how they might be justified from an internal perspective in cases of disagreement.**
