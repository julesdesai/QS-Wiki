# Can a true proposition be unknowingly believed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0abeeb34-e730-40ce-ab65-dd993a203726
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person tosses a fair coin, believes it will land heads due to a dream, and it does land heads. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the outcome despite the truth of the proposition believed.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires an appropriate connection between belief and truth, which is not satisfied by mere coincidence.**
