# Are reflectively inaccessible beliefs compatible with knowing a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ce79b9f-f7f6-4127-8cff-bae253e85d9f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Access Internalism](access-internalism-synthesis-1ebb0a7e-8cd6-40ff-bf0f-a16161232f85.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows a proposition through a belief formed by a reliable process, yet lacks the capacity to reflect on the justification. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that knowledge requires the ability to reflect on one's justification, suggesting that reflective access is needed.**, **A belief formed by a reliable process that is not reflectively accessible seems incompatible with having knowledge of a proposition.**
