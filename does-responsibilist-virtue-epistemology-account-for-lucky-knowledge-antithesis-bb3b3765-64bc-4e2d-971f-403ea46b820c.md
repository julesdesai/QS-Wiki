# Does responsibilist virtue epistemology account for lucky knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bb3b3765-64bc-4e2d-971f-403ea46b820c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person accidentally stumbles upon a true belief while flipping a coin, without any intellectual virtue involved. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might still say that the person has knowledge despite the lack of intellectual virtue.**, **A belief can arise from a cognitive faculty reliably producing true beliefs, yet the acquisition of the belief can be purely accidental and not involve any manifestation of intellectual character virtues.**
