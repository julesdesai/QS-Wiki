# Does the ability to articulate justification alter one's status of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b27053b-8bd5-4f67-8ab2-2af8fd2f40d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**A skilled surgeon performs complex operations based on intuitively understood procedures without being able to articulate every step. Intuitively, we see the surgeon as knowledgeable.**, **The failure to articulate justification does not necessarily prevent one from holding knowledge, showing a gap between justification articulation and possession of knowledge.**
