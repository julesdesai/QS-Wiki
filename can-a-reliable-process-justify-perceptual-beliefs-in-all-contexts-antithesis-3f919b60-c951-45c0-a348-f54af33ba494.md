# Can a reliable process justify perceptual beliefs in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f919b60-c951-45c0-a348-f54af33ba494
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e6fc2437-0f22-487d-91cb-ba903a4e1b1f.md)

## Content

**A person in a room with colored lights believes a banana is ripe because it looks yellow.**, **The belief about the banana being ripe is formed by a perceptual process which usually reliably indicates ripeness.**, **In this specific context, the belief is not reliable because the colored lights can skew perception.**
