# Does knowledge always require conscious justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5643dd8f-be4f-44ac-af1b-762d643bbf13
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism: Reliable Processes](reliabilism-reliable-processes-synthesis-f70f1f60-dbef-46bd-b415-215191e514dd.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person holding a true belief is unable to consciously articulate their reason for holding that belief is not clearly a case in which they have knowledge.**, **It is implausible that the inability to consciously access justification does not affect whether a belief counts as knowledge.**
