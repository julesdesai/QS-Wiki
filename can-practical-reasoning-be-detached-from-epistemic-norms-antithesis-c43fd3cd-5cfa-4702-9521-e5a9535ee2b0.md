# Can practical reasoning be detached from epistemic norms?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c43fd3cd-5cfa-4702-9521-e5a9535ee2b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Suppose practical reasoning allows for consideration of beliefs that are beneficial but not necessarily true, such as a belief that motivates positive action regardless of its accuracy. Intuitively, we might accept this belief as reasonable in practical terms but not as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that epistemic norms, which govern the truth and justification of beliefs, should not be subordinated to practical reasoning norms. This principle suggests that epistemic considerations are distinct from and not reducible to pragmatic considerations.**
