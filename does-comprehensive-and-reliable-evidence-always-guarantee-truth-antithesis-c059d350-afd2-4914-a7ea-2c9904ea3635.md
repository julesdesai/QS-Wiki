# Does comprehensive and reliable evidence always guarantee truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c059d350-afd2-4914-a7ea-2c9904ea3635
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Imagine a detective gathering comprehensive and reliable evidence pointing to a suspect's guilt, but the suspect is in fact innocent. Intuitively, we might judge the detective’s belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **Instances where reliable evidence points strongly to a conclusion that turns out false challenge the sufficiency of such evidence to ensure truth.**
