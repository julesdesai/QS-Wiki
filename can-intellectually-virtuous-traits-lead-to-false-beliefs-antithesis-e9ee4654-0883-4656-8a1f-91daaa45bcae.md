# Can intellectually virtuous traits lead to false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9ee4654-0883-4656-8a1f-91daaa45bcae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d7a894af-29e5-42fe-8e5e-957e2009566d.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who is intellectually curious and always seeks out new information. It is possible for this person, despite their curiosity, to encounter misleading or false information and come to hold false beliefs.**, **Intuitively, a person exercising intellectual curiosity, a virtue, might still end up with beliefs that are not true, challenging the reliability component of the virtue.**
