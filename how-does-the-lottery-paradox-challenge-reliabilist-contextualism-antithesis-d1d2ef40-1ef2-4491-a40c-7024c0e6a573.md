# How does the "lottery paradox" challenge reliabilist contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1d2ef40-1ef2-4491-a40c-7024c0e6a573
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Contextualism Summary](reliabilist-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9442c5a-8eb2-4660-b9f9-f0b7378a7bd6.md)

## Content

**In a large lottery, even though it is highly improbable that a particular ticket will win, we do not claim to "know" that it will lose based on probability alone.**, **The standards of knowledge should not vary with context in such a way that we cannot clearly claim to know something even when a belief-forming process appears reliable.**
