# Can a belief be knowledgeable without being virtuous?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28c233b7-c930-42d0-88d5-d02527e7baf0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on flawed reasoning but the belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is knowledgeable or not. If we say the belief is knowledgeable, then it suggests that intellectual virtue was not necessary. If we say the belief is not knowledgeable, then intellectual virtue seems overly demanding.**, **A true belief formed through quick intuition rather than thorough open-minded inquiry can still be considered knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that sometimes people know things in non-ideal conditions that lack intellectual virtues.**
