# Does employing intellectual virtues always lead to true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f75b376a-6edd-4cb6-a2a2-7ea63008d54d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Coherentism](virtue-epistemology-coherentism-synthesis-fa375fb7-6e26-497f-b63d-bbaec5305ff8.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone exhibits intellectual virtues could still result in a false belief about a factual matter due to incomplete information.**, **It is conceivable for people to exercise intellectual virtues like open-mindedness and end up with false beliefs simply because not all information is available or comprehensible.**, **The exercise of intellectual virtues leans towards true beliefs but does not invariably result in them due to external epistemic factors.**
