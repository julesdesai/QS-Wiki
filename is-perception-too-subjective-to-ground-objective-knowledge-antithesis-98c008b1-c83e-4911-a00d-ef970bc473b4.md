# Is perception too subjective to ground objective knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98c008b1-c83e-4911-a00d-ef970bc473b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people perceive the same event differently due to their distinct mental and unconscious filters. Intuitively, we would judge that their knowledge claims about the event are subjective and unreliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective knowledge requires a consistent and impartial mediation of reality. This principle, together with the notion of perception being heavily filtered by individual mental processes, entails that knowledge claims are too subjective to be considered objective knowledge.**
