# Is there an issue with the supposed directness of evidence provided by veridical perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 072fdc86-58e5-4611-b81d-8b3a6fc104cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people watch the same event, but due to different angles and lighting, they perceive it differently. Intuitively, if veridical perceptions provide direct evidence of reality, both should have the same perception, yet they don't.**, **It is implausible that perceptions differing significantly can still be considered as providing direct evidence of the same reality.**
