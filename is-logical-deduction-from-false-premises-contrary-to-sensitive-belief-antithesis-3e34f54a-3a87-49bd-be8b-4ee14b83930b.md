# Is logical deduction from false premises contrary to sensitive belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e34f54a-3a87-49bd-be8b-4ee14b83930b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitive belief theory of knowledge](sensitive-belief-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a04ea135-b51c-4804-8a79-c6b4af4a4910.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a person believes a proposition based on a deductive argument with a false premise, but the conclusion happens to be true. Intuitively, we would judge that the person's belief does not constitute knowledge because it relies on an incorrect reasoning process.**, **Knowledge based on conclusions from unsound arguments lacks sensitivity to the truth of the premises, challenging the notion that knowledge is present in such situations.**
