# Does the influence of language on perception suggest mediation by cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84a3f539-3eee-441c-8b2d-f59c13952f7c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which speakers of different languages perceive colors differently because their languages categorize colors in varying ways. Intuitively, we either have to say that language influences perceptual experience or that perception accurately captures external reality independently of language. If language influences perception, then perception is mediated. If perception operates independently of language, this contradicts empirical studies on linguistic relativity.**
