# Can perceptual experiences fully account for causal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7195ad0-c199-4483-a480-cdcfe975bf34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person learns about the transmission of diseases through reading scientific texts rather than through perceptual experiences related to diseases. Intuitively, we might judge this knowledge as causal even though it is not derived from personal perception.**, **There is an argument that causal knowledge often involves theoretical understanding in addition to empirical observations, implying that perceptual experiences alone are inadequate.**
