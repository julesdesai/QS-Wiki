# How does memory-based knowledge withstand scrutiny under the Safety Theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f8d31a0-363d-4867-b842-5865a402ad0c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who bases a belief about a past event on memory, which is generally reliable but occasionally faulty. Intuitively, even if their belief is true, the unreliability of memory questions whether they actually hold knowledge.**, **Memory fallibility introduces cases where beliefs might easily be false in some memory-related situations, questioning the application of safety.**
