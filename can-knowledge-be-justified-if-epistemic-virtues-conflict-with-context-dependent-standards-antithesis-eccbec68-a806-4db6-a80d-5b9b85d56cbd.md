# Can knowledge be justified if epistemic virtues conflict with context-dependent standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eccbec68-a806-4db6-a80d-5b9b85d56cbd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Virtue Epistemology](contextualism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4d017c8b-6b5b-48d7-b8c7-737c6c80044f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's belief results from epistemic virtues but fails to meet a context-dependent standard. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that for someone to know something, their belief must be justified according to both epistemic virtues and context-dependent standards.**
