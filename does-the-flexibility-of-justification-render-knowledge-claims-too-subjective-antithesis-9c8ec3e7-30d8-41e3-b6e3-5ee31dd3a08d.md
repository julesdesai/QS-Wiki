# Does the flexibility of justification render knowledge claims too subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c8ec3e7-30d8-41e3-b6e3-5ee31dd3a08d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that objectivity is key to determining knowledge to avoid subjective interpretation based on individual belief systems.**, **Consider a journalist whose claim to know about a story depends on a personal source, taking loosely justified information when writing for a tabloid versus when they adhere to strict standards for a reputable outlet.**, **If justification is accepted based on context, then what counts as knowledge becomes too reliant on personal judgment, introducing excessive subjectivity.**
