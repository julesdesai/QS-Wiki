# Is justifying belief independence essential for its safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 575ed16b-f388-4252-b842-a288cc816d2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual believes water is present in a glass due to seeing it, but minor alterations in lighting conditions can deceive them into wrongly perceiving it as present in nearby possible worlds. Intuitively, such a belief seems shaky as knowledge despite seeming justified.**, **Safe beliefs should not be easily influenced by perceptual errors due to minor changes. Reliance on visual justification questions the safety if minor variations lead to false beliefs frequently.**
