## Content

**A person knows a proposition if and only if their belief is the result of exercising intellectual virtues**, **For a belief to result from intellectual virtues, it must be formed through faculties like perception, reason, and memory that reliably lead to truth**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed through exercising intellectual virtues**
