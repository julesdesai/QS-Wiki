# Can sensory experiences lead to true beliefs without sensitivity to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d27c218c-efbc-4eba-b922-df088c6030a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitive belief theory of knowledge](sensitive-belief-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a04ea135-b51c-4804-8a79-c6b4af4a4910.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person touches a surface and feels that it is warm, leading them to believe that the surface is warm. The surface is in fact warm, but had the person merely touched a different part of the surface that is cool, they would believe it is cool. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person's belief is not sensitive to the actual temperature of the surface despite being true.**, **In cases where sensory experiences lead directly to beliefs which are true by luck rather than sensitivity, the person does not truly know the proposition.**
