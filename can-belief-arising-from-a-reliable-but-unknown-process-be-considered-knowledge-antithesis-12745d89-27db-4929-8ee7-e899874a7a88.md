# Can belief arising from a reliable but unknown process be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 12745d89-27db-4929-8ee7-e899874a7a88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as an externalist account of knowledge](reliabilism-as-an-externalist-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-f53d2eeb-6036-46fc-bee7-531fa51577e9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's belief is formed by a reliable cognitive process, but the process is entirely unknown or opaque to them; intuitively, this seems insufficient for them to claim knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing something implies having some understanding or insight into how or why it is true. This principle suggests that reliability alone, if unknown, is insufficient for knowledge.**
