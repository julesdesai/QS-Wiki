# How do we account for the timing of belief formation in reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3b6a62a3-5e70-4123-a7bc-9b1f5d60f2f2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a reliable process leads to the formation of a true belief, but the belief becomes irrelevant or outdated almost immediately. Intuitively, we would hesitate to say the person possesses knowledge based on this obsolete belief.**, **Knowledge seems to require relevance or adaptability to current circumstances, which mere reliability of the process does not guarantee.**
