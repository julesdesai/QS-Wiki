# Knowledge as Justified Reliable Belief

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does reliabilism account for all instances of knowledge?](does-reliabilism-account-for-all-instances-of-knowledge-antithesis-57bcad73-01b7-495a-b1ee-b4ff84cdf71b.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed through a reliable process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for their belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is formed through a reliable process, and they have justification for their belief**
