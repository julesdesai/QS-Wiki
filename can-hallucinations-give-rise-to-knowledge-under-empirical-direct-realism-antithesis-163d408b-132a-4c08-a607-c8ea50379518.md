# Can hallucinations give rise to knowledge under empirical direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 163d408b-132a-4c08-a607-c8ea50379518
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a vivid hallucination of an event that inadvertently aligns with reality and thus claims knowledge. Intuitively, we either have to say the person has knowledge, despite the hallucinatory source, or they do not. If we say the person has knowledge, then hallucinatory experiences can lead to knowledge.**, **It is implausible that hallucinations can produce knowledge due to their unreliable nature when compared to direct empirical perception.**
