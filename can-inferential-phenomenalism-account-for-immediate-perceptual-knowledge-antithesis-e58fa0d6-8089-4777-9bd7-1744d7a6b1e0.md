# Can inferential phenomenalism account for immediate perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e58fa0d6-8089-4777-9bd7-1744d7a6b1e0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person instantly recognizes a familiar face without engaging in inferential reasoning. Intuitively, we would judge that the person has immediate knowledge of the face.**, **There is a plausible principle that not all perceptual knowledge requires inferential reasoning.**, **Knowledge that seems immediate and non-inferential is difficult to explain through a model that insists on inference.**
