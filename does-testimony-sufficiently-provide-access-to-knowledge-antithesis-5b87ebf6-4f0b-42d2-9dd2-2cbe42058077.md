# Does testimony sufficiently provide access to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b87ebf6-4f0b-42d2-9dd2-2cbe42058077
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Phenomenalism](moderate-phenomenalism-synthesis-66f89d49-d085-4cff-b6cd-a9e130c91f7b.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person hears a detailed eyewitness account of a rare animal’s behavior but has never seen the animal themselves. Intuitively, we might say they lack complete knowledge as they could be misled or misunderstand.**, **A plausible principle is that testimony should supplement, not replace, direct experiences for robust knowledge. This principle together with the view of relying primarily on testimony implies that potential errors and biases in testimony could undermine genuine knowledge.**
