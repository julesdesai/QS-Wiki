# Do Gettier cases challenge the Causal Theory of Knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d752ecd-9566-4f54-88fa-6b2a797dac56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition because of a justified false belief, but this belief accidentally aligns with a true fact due to an unrelated causal connection. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a person truly knows something, their belief cannot be merely accidentally true. This principle together with the scenario suggests that a causal connection alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
