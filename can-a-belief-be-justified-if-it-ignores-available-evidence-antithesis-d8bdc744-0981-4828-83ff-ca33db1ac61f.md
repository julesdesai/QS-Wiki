# Can a belief be justified if it ignores available evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8bdc744-0981-4828-83ff-ca33db1ac61f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a student guesses the answer to an exam question without consulting their study materials, and the guess happens to be correct. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief isn't justified since relevant evidence was overlooked.**, **Even if the student achieves practical success by getting the answer correct, the justification of the belief seems flawed as it disregards relevant contextual evidence available.**
