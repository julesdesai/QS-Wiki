# Does luck undermine the connection between virtue and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f18cc0d3-43e3-444f-b7e3-ee617c1f85b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-deb477f8-4a11-4db0-ba96-00a9c4a7815c.md)

## Content

**A person might possess a trait that generally leads to truth, yet in a specific instance, they believe something false due to random error. This shows that intellectual virtue does not always guarantee true belief.**, **Imagine a person with the intellectual virtue of careful reasoning who, due to a rare brain malfunction, incorrectly concludes a proposition. Here, the intellectual virtue exists, but it does not lead to truth.**
