# Can an infinite chain of reasons really provide justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 31f22192-18a6-48d9-ae82-3cff529f3704
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-4b9cf48f-532c-40a3-a960-718d00ef564e.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that an infinite chain can be completed or fully grasped by finite human minds as it requires engaging with an unending sequence.**, **Suppose justification requires the completion or accessibility of an entire chain of reasons. Intuitively, this is unattainable if the chain is infinite.**, **Consider a scenario where someone claims to have justification via an infinite chain, but they cannot present or understand the entire chain. Intuitively, this undermines their claim to have genuine justification.**
