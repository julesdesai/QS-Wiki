# Are intuitively virtuous beliefs necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3526a32c-15ab-4281-9536-011e66ac8825
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Refined Virtue Epistemology](refined-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed8e6ab8-d432-4955-9aec-982a4b148be7.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a belief not formed by any intellectual virtue can ever rise to the status of knowledge.**, **Suppose we consider a situation in which a true belief is held but lacks any intellectual character or virtue. In this case, without intellectual engagement or virtue, we might see such belief as mere accident rather than knowledge.**
