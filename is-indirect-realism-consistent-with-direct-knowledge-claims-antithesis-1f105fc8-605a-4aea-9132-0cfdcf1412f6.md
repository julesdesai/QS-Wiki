# Is indirect realism consistent with direct knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f105fc8-605a-4aea-9132-0cfdcf1412f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Contextual Perception](indirect-realism-with-contextual-perception-synthesis-77e2d877-90d5-4b41-9238-09aba0491b6a.md)

## Content

**Some direct knowledge claims suggest immediate awareness of objects, such as seeing an apple and knowing it is an apple without interpretative filtering.**, **If knowledge is mediated by mental representations and interpretative processes, then immediate awareness is not possible.**, **The claim that contextually filtered mental representations are necessary for knowledge conflicts with the notion of direct perception.**
