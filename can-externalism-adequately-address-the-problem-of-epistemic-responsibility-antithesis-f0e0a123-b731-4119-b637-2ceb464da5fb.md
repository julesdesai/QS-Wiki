# Can externalism adequately address the problem of epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0e0a123-b731-4119-b637-2ceb464da5fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-a9c372ef-1ae7-460b-b26c-8a82dc6a1ee1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a belief is true and meets external conditions, but the agent formed it irresponsibly, ignoring evidence.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that such a belief can be knowledge or it cannot be.**, **If we claim it can be knowledge, we undermine the importance of responsible belief formation.**, **If we deny it is knowledge, responsible processes seem necessary, contradicting external-only dependencies.**
