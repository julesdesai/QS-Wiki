# Does the existence of unconscious perceptions pose a problem for Direct Realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22df20f7-2895-4d57-a110-4057711f5e0d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perceptions must be conscious to provide direct knowledge of external objects.**, **Research suggests that much of perceptual processing occurs unconsciously, as with subliminal perceptions.**, **Unconscious perceptions imply that a direct relationship between perception and knowledge may not exist, as unconscious processing does not directly inform our conscious understanding.**
