# Does causal theory differentiate between luck and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b52252e3-6cfb-4c5f-8f04-29ae11a3b616
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person guesses a random trivia answer correctly due to luck rather than a causal process. Intuitively, getting the answer correct by luck does not count as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that if a belief is reliably caused by truth, it qualifies as knowledge, but this principle fails when beliefs are true by chance.**
