# Does the causal theory fail in accounting for inferential knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17f8ea6f-1636-49d7-b935-6bc71a45cb4f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual believes a complex scientific hypothesis due to the cumulative evidence gathered by a reliable scientific community. Intuitively, we would attribute knowledge to this belief.**, **According to the causal theory, unless the individual directly experiences the fact confirming the hypothesis, the causal link is missing or indirect, potentially denying the status of knowledge.**
