# Can someone know something without being able to refute skeptical doubts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2358c77f-f2f3-4eb1-b335-7c785df33931
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes they are holding a cup of coffee, they see it, smell it, and feel it. Intuitively, we would say they know they are holding a cup of coffee.**, **Suppose this person is presented with skeptical doubts about being in a dream and is unable to refute these doubts convincingly. Intuitively, their inability to refute such doubts does not strip them of knowledge about holding a cup of coffee.**
