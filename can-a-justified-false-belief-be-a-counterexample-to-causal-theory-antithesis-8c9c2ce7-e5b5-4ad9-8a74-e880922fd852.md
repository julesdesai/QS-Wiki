# Can a justified false belief be a counterexample to causal theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c9c2ce7-e5b5-4ad9-8a74-e880922fd852
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone forms a justified belief about a fact due to misleading evidence that is causally connected to other facts but not the fact in question. Intuitively, we would judge this is a case of justified belief yet false, questioning the causal theory's capacity to ensure the truth of the belief.**
