# Can context sensitivity undermine historical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7d122ea-2a96-4f50-b778-df7f64ea20cb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider historical claims where the context of evaluation has changed over time. Intuitively, relying on context-sensitive standards may lead to revisiting historical knowledge constantly, thus undermining its stability.**, **Suppose historians accept knowledge claims about past events based on evidence that is valid in one era but not another. It seems problematic if the knowledge of historical facts becomes uncertain as context evolves.**
