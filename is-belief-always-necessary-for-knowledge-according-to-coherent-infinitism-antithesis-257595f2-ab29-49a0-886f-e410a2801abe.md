# Is belief always necessary for knowledge according to coherent infinitism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 257595f2-ab29-49a0-886f-e410a2801abe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Infinitism Summary](coherent-infinitism-summary-synthesis-eca6bdeb-868b-4e1e-9a63-23b9880f21ff.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is traditionally considered justified true belief, but belief in a proposition is not always present when someone knows something.**, **There are cases where individuals act or reason correctly without holding explicit beliefs about propositions, yet they demonstrate knowledge.**, **The requirement of belief for knowledge suggests an unnecessary constraint that might exclude implicit knowledge.**
