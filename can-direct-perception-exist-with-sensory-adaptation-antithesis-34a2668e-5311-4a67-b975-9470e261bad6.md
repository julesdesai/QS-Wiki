# Can direct perception exist with sensory adaptation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 34a2668e-5311-4a67-b975-9470e261bad6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism with Direct Cross-Modal Sensing](naive-realism-with-direct-cross-modal-sensing-synthesis-96f2c374-253a-4f49-a9c2-1ee8405679ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person enters a dark room and initially sees nothing but gradually adjusts to perceive objects. Intuitively, we either have to say the perception of objects is altered over time or that the initial absence of perception implicates a mediation process.**, **If we say the perception is altered over time, then our sensory access to objects is not constant and direct.**, **If we say the initial absence implicates mediation, then perception involves an intermediary process before direct awareness occurs.**
