# Can knowledge exist without cognitive abilities or virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3375feb7-7e59-429e-80f7-c7933a742605
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ad239dac-f508-456d-984a-1344bc3c3ad8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief due to sheer luck without exercising any cognitive abilities or virtues. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person would still be said to know.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is not solely dependent on the exercising of cognitive abilities or virtues. This principle suggests that true beliefs can sometimes constitute knowledge without cognitive virtues.**
