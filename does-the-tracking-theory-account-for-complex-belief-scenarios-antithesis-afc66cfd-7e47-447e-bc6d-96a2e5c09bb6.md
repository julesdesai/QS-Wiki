# Does the tracking theory account for complex belief scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** afc66cfd-7e47-447e-bc6d-96a2e5c09bb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dda35d00-5b10-4342-b443-f197350806e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a complex case where a person believes a multi-faceted proposition, some aspects of which remain true and others are false. Intuitively, we might say that the person knows the aspects that are true but not the whole proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be partial and compartmentalized. This principle together with complex propositions suggests knowledge can be aspectual rather than holistic.**
