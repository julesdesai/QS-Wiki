# Is virtue epistemology undermined by the distribution of intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b84adc63-97d5-4cd3-bbdc-0ab17f43b386
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**Consider a community where few have access to educational resources, while others form beliefs through well-supported intellectual virtues; intuitively, inequity impacts the recognition of knowledge.**, **A belief formed through intellectual virtues may not widely exist due to unequal distribution of such faculties, challenging the universality of virtue epistemology.**, **The requirement for cognitive abilities to be checked for crediting truth encounters obstacles in social inequities and access to resources equitably.**
