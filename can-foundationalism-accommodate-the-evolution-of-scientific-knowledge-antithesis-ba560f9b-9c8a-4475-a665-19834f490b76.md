# Can foundationalism accommodate the evolution of scientific knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba560f9b-9c8a-4475-a665-19834f490b76
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**Scientific knowledge evolves through hypothesis testing, refutation, and paradigm shifts, not immediate self-evidence.**, **For example, Newtonian physics was revised to accommodate Einstein's theory of relativity.**, **Foundationalism requires each justified belief to connect with a self-evident or sensory evident belief.**, **If scientific knowledge cannot be solely reduced to foundational beliefs, foundationalism may not account for its complexity.**
