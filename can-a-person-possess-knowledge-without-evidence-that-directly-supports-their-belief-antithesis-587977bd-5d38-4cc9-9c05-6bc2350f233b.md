# Can a person possess knowledge without evidence that directly supports their belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 587977bd-5d38-4cc9-9c05-6bc2350f233b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a person can possess knowledge without having direct evidence supporting their belief if their belief happens to be true by chance.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition because of a guess, and the proposition is true due to luck alone. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows the proposition, despite lacking evidence.**
