# Does a belief based on circular reasoning constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 069a2d66-b7b0-46dc-a172-f377a9a75f55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Evidence Theory](inferential-evidence-theory-synthesis-888e1fca-9103-4ede-a19f-61db12f550f6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a student believes they passed an exam because they believe they are a good student, and this belief is based on them passing previous exams, an outcome they justify by their belief in being a good student. Intuitively, basing beliefs in such a circular manner undermines the foundation of knowledge as it lacks an independent, objective measure.**, **Inferential evidence requires reasoning from known premises to a conclusion, yet circular reasoning returns to an initial assumption without adding any external validation or new information, thus failing to meet the requirements for establishing knowledge.**
