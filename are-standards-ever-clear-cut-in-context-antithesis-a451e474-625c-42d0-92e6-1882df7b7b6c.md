# Are standards ever clear-cut in context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a451e474-625c-42d0-92e6-1882df7b7b6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where two contexts overlap with no clear boundary on standard applicability. Intuitively, clear-cut standards are needed for precise knowledge claims.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should derive from clearly defined conditions, which variable contextual standards may not provide.**
