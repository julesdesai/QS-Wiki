# Is knowledge really stable across different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe2e4142-6195-4c92-bdfc-86a59500025f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Context-Sensitive Invariant Endorsement](context-sensitive-invariant-endorsement-synthesis-de3f576d-97e3-4b7e-b7cd-f089d4d156aa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Emma, asserts that she knows the bank is open based on her memory from a past visit. In a casual context, intuitively we would judge that Emma knows this.**, **Consider the same scenario, but now Emma is being quizzed by a police officer because the bank robbery's timing is critical. Intuitively, we may now judge that Emma does not actually know this.**, **These differing intuitive judgments suggest that the standards for knowing are not invariant across contexts, challenging the claim that core standards do not vary with context.**
