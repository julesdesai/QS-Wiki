# Does the truth condition sufficiently capture context-sensitive knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9181d7cf-ec60-4cf2-b17e-ac2f2712d899
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b7b03c88-6aa4-4b47-9f59-bc5548cc994c.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle suggests that truth is an invariant characteristic of a proposition regardless of context. This principle together with the idea that truth must align with context-dependent assertions introduces tension because truth itself remains constant, raising doubt whether truth alone captures the nuance demanded by contextualist views.**
