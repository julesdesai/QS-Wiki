# Is belief truly necessary for practical verification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3bddd611-2c3c-45f9-88e9-3070f933f118
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person accidentally performs an action without belief, such as typing a correct solution to a puzzle out of random key presses, and this action results in verification of a proposition. Intuitively, we wouldn't categorize this as knowledge, since belief is absent.**, **Belief itself is not required in scenarios where practical actions happen coincidentally but still lead to verifying a proposition.**
