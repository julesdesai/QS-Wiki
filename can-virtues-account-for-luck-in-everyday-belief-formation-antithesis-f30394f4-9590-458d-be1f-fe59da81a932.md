# Can virtues account for luck in everyday belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f30394f4-9590-458d-be1f-fe59da81a932
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**A person habitually forms beliefs about weather predictions from a local app that is generally reliable but occasionally faulty. On a rare occasion, a forecast is accurate by chance despite faulty data. Intuitively, epistemic luck seems unavoidable.**, **Given the frequent interaction with systems like apps, which can introduce luck in belief formation, the exclusion of luck by virtue-based approaches appears inadequate.**
