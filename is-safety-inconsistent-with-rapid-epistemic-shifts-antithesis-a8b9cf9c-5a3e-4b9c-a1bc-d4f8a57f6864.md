# Is safety inconsistent with rapid epistemic shifts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8b9cf9c-5a3e-4b9c-a1bc-d4f8a57f6864
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where rapid shifts in evidence or opinion, such as in financial markets, lead to a belief that could easily be false in the nearby future but remains true now. Intuitively, we would judge that such belief may lack long-term safety yet still be known presently.**, **It is implausible to require stable long-term conditions of beliefs in rapidly changing contexts for them to qualify as knowledge.**
