# How can pragmatic justification account for moral disagreements?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d635d4cb-69c0-48d1-a9e6-620e729e53cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-838d6a9b-2af0-4e99-882f-62ebc82bed50.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals hold opposing beliefs about a moral issue, each justified pragmatically by their own life's outcomes. Intuitively, it is unlikely that both beliefs can be considered true knowledge.**, **Moral disagreements with opposing pragmatic justifications suggest that pragmatic justification might not adhere to the principle that a belief must be true for it to be knowledge.**
