# Can knowledge be strictly tied to intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 31e4f736-a4cd-4651-9079-24efe0a11994
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-deb477f8-4a11-4db0-ba96-00a9c4a7815c.md)

## Content

**It is possible for a person to accidentally reach the truth without exercising intellectual virtue, which suggests that knowledge might not strictly require virtue.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person, due to a lucky guess, believes a true proposition without using any intellectual virtue. In such a scenario, the belief is true and the person "knows" the proposition by traditional standards, yet the belief did not arise from virtue.**
