# Knowledge as strict invariantism

**Parent:** [Can contextualism account for error in knowledge claims across contexts?](can-contextualism-account-for-error-in-knowledge-claims-across-contexts-antithesis-9d0b30bd-c4e2-4511-95b2-1cd3e8eb3164.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the justification meets a rigid and context-independent standard**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the justification meets this rigid standard**
