# Knowledge As No Relevant Alternatives

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does incorrect causal interpretation reduce knowledge even if beliefs are true?](does-incorrect-causal-interpretation-reduce-knowledge-even-if-beliefs-are-true-antithesis-ca4dfeab-dee6-497f-afb6-1b27059ab0d5.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that there are no relevant alternatives which are uneliminated**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and there are no relevant alternatives which are uneliminated**
