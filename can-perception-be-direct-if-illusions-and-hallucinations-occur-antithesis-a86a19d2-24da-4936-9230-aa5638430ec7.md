# Can perception be direct if illusions and hallucinations occur?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a86a19d2-24da-4936-9230-aa5638430ec7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-ad05d8f4-c9d2-4024-8001-bf520360cf02.md)

## Content

**Illusions occur when a person seems to directly perceive an external object differently from how it actually is, such as a stick appearing bent in water.**, **Hallucinations occur when a person seems to perceive an external object without the object actually being present, such as seeing a mirage in the desert.**, **If perception is direct awareness of external objects, illusions and hallucinations would not be possible, yet they clearly occur.**
