# Does contextualism make knowledge too context-dependent and unstable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8d7bf0a-9f5e-4b65-9ae1-9cc7fd0b532b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person asserts they know something in an everyday context, but when their context changes (e.g., facing a skeptical challenge), they can no longer claim to know it. Intuitively, this makes knowledge seem too unstable and subject to trivial shifts.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, a concept typically associated with certainty and stability, should change so facilely with context, undermining its utility in practical decision-making.**
