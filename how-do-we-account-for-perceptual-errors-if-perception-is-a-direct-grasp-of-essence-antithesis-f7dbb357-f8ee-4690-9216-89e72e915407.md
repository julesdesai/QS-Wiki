# How do we account for perceptual errors if perception is a direct grasp of essence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7dbb357-f8ee-4690-9216-89e72e915407
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Perspective](phenomenological-perspective-synthesis-c5a75a88-a263-494b-bef9-8c1e426bd706.md)

## Content

**In cases of illusions, such as a stick appearing bent in water, the essence of the stick itself does not change, yet the perception is incorrect.**, **If perception directly grasps the essence, then errors in perception would seem inexplicable, as the essence itself is not altered in such cases.**
