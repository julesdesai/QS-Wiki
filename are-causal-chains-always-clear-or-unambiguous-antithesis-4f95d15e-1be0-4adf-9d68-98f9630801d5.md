# Are causal chains always clear or unambiguous?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f95d15e-1be0-4adf-9d68-98f9630801d5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a belief formed in complicated circumstances involving multiple indirect causes. Intuitively, determining a single clear causal chain that justifies the belief is problematic and ambiguous.**, **It is implausible that causal connections alone can deterministically justify beliefs when causal origins are complex and multifaceted.**
