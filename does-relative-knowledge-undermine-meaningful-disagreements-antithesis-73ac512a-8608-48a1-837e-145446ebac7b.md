# Does relative knowledge undermine meaningful disagreements?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 73ac512a-8608-48a1-837e-145446ebac7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Relativism](epistemic-relativism-synthesis-8a36f430-fbe7-4356-93bb-efe42975c670.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where two individuals disagree about a proposition R, with each claiming knowledge from their respective epistemic frameworks.**, **If R is taken to be true within one framework but false in another, this would imply that both individuals are correct, given their respective community norms.**, **Such a notion dissolves the substantive nature of disagreements, as both parties can simultaneously claim to know R and to know not-R without any resolution.**
