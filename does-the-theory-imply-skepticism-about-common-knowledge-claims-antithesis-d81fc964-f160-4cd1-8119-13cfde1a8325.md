# Does the theory imply skepticism about common knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d81fc964-f160-4cd1-8119-13cfde1a8325
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**Contrastivism could imply that everyday knowledge claims are tentative, as new contexts could introduce new relevant alternatives that undermine previous knowledge claims.**, **A person might be considered to know a fact under contrastivism until a new context makes a different alternative relevant, which would seem to allow for easy loss of knowledge, contradicting ordinary conceptions of knowledge stability.**
