# Is it plausible that direct perception can provide knowledge about abstract concepts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 223d206e-0200-4953-8d90-7f10d4cc9fca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone observes an interaction and claims to know the abstract concept of justice through direct perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception alone cannot capture abstract concepts without inferential understanding.**, **It is implausible that perceiving physical events can provide immediate knowledge of abstract concepts such as justice, beauty, or morality without inferential justification.**
