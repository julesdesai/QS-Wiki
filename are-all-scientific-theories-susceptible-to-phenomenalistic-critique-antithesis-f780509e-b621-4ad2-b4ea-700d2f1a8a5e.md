# Are all scientific theories susceptible to phenomenalistic critique?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f780509e-b621-4ad2-b4ea-700d2f1a8a5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**Consider a scientific theory that makes predictions about entities or phenomena that cannot be directly perceived, such as electrons or black holes. Intuitively, we would hold that such entities have effects on observable phenomena but are not themselves constructs of sensory experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that theoretical entities, though not directly knowable through senses, are necessary for explaining consistent sensory experiences and scientific observations. This principle challenges the view that only sensory constructs are reliable knowledge.**
