# Does truth necessarily entail reliability in forming beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b02deca2-35a0-4505-b592-7baa8d7c7e12
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a true proposition by mere coincidence, with no reliable connection to the truth. Intuitively, this coincidence should not grant knowledge, indicating truth does not necessarily entail reliability in forming beliefs.**, **It is implausible that merely having a true belief can automatically confer knowledge if there is no reliable connection.**
