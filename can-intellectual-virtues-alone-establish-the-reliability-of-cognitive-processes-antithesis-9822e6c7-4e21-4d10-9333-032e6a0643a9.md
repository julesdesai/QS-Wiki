# Can intellectual virtues alone establish the reliability of cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9822e6c7-4e21-4d10-9333-032e6a0643a9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**A virtuous agent may have habits or tendencies that appear reliable but do not yield true beliefs in particular contexts. Intuitively, we would judge that virtues alone cannot establish reliability without context-specific information.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a process to be reliable, it must consistently yield true beliefs across various contexts. This principle together with context-sensitivity entails that virtues alone are insufficient to determine process reliability.**
