# Does perception capture all functional impacts of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9dc3dc9-4d42-4afa-b305-44a710442332
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person perceives a hologram as a real object due to its functional impact on their senses. Intuitively, we would judge that perception might not capture the actual nature or existence of the object.**, **It is implausible that all functional impacts perceived truly represent external reality, suggesting limitations in perceivable knowledge of objects.**
