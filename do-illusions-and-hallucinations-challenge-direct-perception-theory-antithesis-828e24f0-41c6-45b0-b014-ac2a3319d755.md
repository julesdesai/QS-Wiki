# Do illusions and hallucinations challenge direct perception theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 828e24f0-41c6-45b0-b014-ac2a3319d755
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Ecological Direct Perception Theory](ecological-direct-perception-theory-synthesis-73031ffe-0106-4b3d-af38-bdf2623ababa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual experiences a visual illusion such as the Müller-Lyer illusion. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the perceived lines appear different in length despite being the same, suggesting mediation by mental processes.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person hallucinates an object that is not present. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception is not purely direct or that hallucinations are a different kind of perception. If we say that hallucinations are different, then we separate them from ordinary perception without justification.**
