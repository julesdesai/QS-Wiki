## Content

**A case in which a person P forms a true belief based on a justified but false premise, such as believing the time based on a stopped clock showing the correct time by coincidence, challenges simple reliabilism.**, **In this situation, intuitively, it seems that P does not have knowledge even though their belief might result from a usually reliable process.**
