# Can a person mistakenly not believe a false proposition in a relevantly similar counterfactual situation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0bcd4d09-0efd-40d7-b509-639f2dc0a8b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-68f75ad1-a4e1-40d8-ae80-33e70d924463.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on a reliable source. In a similar situation where the proposition is false, the belief remains unchanged due to reliance on the same source. Intuitively, it seems the person does not know the proposition.**, **A person believes a true proposition due to misleading evidence. If that evidence varied slightly, the proposition would be false, yet the belief remains. Intuitively, it seems the person does not know the proposition.**
