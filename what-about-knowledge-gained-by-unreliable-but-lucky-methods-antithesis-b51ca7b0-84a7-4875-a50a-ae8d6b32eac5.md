# What about knowledge gained by unreliable but lucky methods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b51ca7b0-84a7-4875-a50a-ae8d6b32eac5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition because of a fortune teller's prediction. While the proposition might be true, intuitively, we hesitate to qualify it as knowledge.**, **Even if the belief proposition is true or believed, the cognitive process used in fortune telling isn't reliable in leading to true beliefs, according to our intuition.**, **Reliability requires a cognitive process that leads generally to truth, and if reliance on luck substitutes this, knowledge becomes disputed.**
