# Can intellectual virtue fail to operate yet still result in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f77f6f4f-0b04-4a36-90d7-84d1e533e832
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Enhanced Virtue Reliabilism with Error Avoidance](enhanced-virtue-reliabilism-with-error-avoidance-synthesis-bae843f7-47b1-4791-af4f-a08b903c892d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's intellectual abilities are not engaged, but they still form a true belief by sheer coincidence. Intuitively, we would have to say that the person's belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **The necessity of intellectual virtue in knowledge suggests that true belief by coincidence lacks the reliability required for knowledge.**
