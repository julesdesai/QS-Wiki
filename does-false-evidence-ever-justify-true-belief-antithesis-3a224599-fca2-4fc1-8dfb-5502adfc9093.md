# Does false evidence ever justify true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a224599-fca2-4fc1-8dfb-5502adfc9093
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-10c49977-d180-4d95-bcbb-8124324d7ccb.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person believes a true proposition based on evidence that is generally regarded as false or misleading. For example, a person could believe it will rain because of incorrect weather predictions that happen to align with an unexpected weather change. Intuitively, their belief is true, but their evidence does not seem to justify it.**, **It is implausible that false evidence could justify a true belief in the same robust way that true evidence does. This raises questions about what counts as sufficient evidence for justification.**
