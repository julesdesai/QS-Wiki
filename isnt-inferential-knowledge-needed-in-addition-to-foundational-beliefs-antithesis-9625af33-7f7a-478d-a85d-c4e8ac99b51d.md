# Isn't inferential knowledge needed in addition to foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9625af33-7f7a-478d-a85d-c4e8ac99b51d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Foundationalism](epistemic-foundationalism-synthesis-e90eff9d-4b30-41a2-b4e3-4bbc38385542.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where foundational beliefs are held without any inferential connections to other beliefs. Intuitively, we would judge that such isolated foundational beliefs do not amount to full knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just foundational beliefs but also inferential connections between beliefs. This principle together with isolated foundational beliefs entails that inferential knowledge is necessary for complete knowledge.**
