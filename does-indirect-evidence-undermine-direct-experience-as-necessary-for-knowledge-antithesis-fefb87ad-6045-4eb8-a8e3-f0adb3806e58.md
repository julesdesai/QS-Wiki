# Does indirect evidence undermine direct experience as necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fefb87ad-6045-4eb8-a8e3-f0adb3806e58
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Realism](phenomenological-realism-synthesis-f972efdb-cd3c-4df6-b5d4-a15ff27a6c64.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a detective concludes that a suspect is guilty based on a compilation of indirect evidence and testimony, without directly witnessing the crime. In this case, we intuitively judge the detective knows the suspect is guilty.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be adequately supported by indirect evidence as well as direct perceptual experience. This principle together with examples like the detective's case entails that direct experience is not always necessary for knowledge.**
