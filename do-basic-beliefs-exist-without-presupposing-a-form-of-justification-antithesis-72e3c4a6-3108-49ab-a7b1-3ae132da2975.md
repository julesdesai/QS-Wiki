# Do basic beliefs exist without presupposing a form of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72e3c4a6-3108-49ab-a7b1-3ae132da2975
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**Basic beliefs are defined as beliefs that require no justification from other beliefs.**, **Critics argue that identifying a belief as basic already presupposes some criteria or standard of justification, contradicting the notion of a belief requiring no justification.**, **The criteria for identifying a basic belief might inadvertently imply a need for them to be justified by coherence with other beliefs.**
