# How does indirect realism handle knowledge of one's own mental states?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a459be16-276e-4008-8d12-7909d862b2c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone immediately knows they are feeling pain without needing to perceive or process it through intermediaries. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person has direct knowledge of their mental state.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge of one's own mental states is direct and does not require representational models of reality. This principle challenges the notion that all knowledge involves intermediaries.**
