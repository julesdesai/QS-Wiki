# Does a reliable causal process always produce knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6025f6a5-70a4-4cbf-9763-1df80234625a

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-4b0c5095-8474-46c5-ad2b-26df0561fddc.md)

## Content

**A reliable causal process could occasionally lead to a true belief just by coincidence rather than genuine knowledge.**, **An example would be someone predicting a weather event based on an unreliable source that happens to be correct this time; it's a reliable causal process but seems more like luck than knowledge.**
