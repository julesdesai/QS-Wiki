# Does contextualism account for shared knowledge in diverse situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86a8901e-d6cd-474d-9c1c-9976efcbe057
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which individuals from distinct practical backgrounds (e.g., a farmer and a banker) discuss agricultural economics. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that shared knowledge on the economic impact of crop yields needs universally accessible justification to function communally, challenging the notion that practical conditions can overly influence justification without complicating shared understanding.**
