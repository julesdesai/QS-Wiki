# Is truth necessary in all cases of knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52a427ce-5cb4-49fd-9c19-ae6da7e1d6d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that truth is not always necessary for the concept we commonly associate with knowing. This challenges rigid definitions that insist knowledge must always be true.**, **Consider a situation where someone accurately predicts an outcome based on false premises, but their prediction aligns perfectly with reality. Intuitively, it might feel they have a form of knowledge, even if their belief was initially false.**
