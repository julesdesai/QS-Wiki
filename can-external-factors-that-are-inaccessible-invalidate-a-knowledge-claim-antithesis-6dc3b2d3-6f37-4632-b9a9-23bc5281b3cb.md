# Can external factors that are inaccessible invalidate a knowledge claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6dc3b2d3-6f37-4632-b9a9-23bc5281b3cb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a true proposition about a business's success based on confident insider information which turns out to be unfounded, yet the business succeeds due to an unrelated and unexpected factor.**, **There is a plausible principle that states knowledge should not depend on factors completely unknown or radically misunderstood by the knower.**, **If a belief is true but its justification is undermined by inaccessible factors, it complicates the assertion that the belief is knowledge.**
