# Can illusions undermine the claim that objects of perception constitute experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9d3f5c3-4b3f-420f-a29e-dc1a9eb87b70
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism](naive-realism-synthesis-a575a970-7874-461b-b9b5-9de679546681.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a stick submerged in water appears bent. Intuitively, our experience is of a bent stick despite a straight stick being present.**, **If a straight stick appears bent, then objects of perception do not straightforwardly constitute the experience as it is supposed by naive realism.**
