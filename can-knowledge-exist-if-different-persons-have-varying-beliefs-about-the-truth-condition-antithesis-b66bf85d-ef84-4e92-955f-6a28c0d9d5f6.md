# Can knowledge exist if different persons have varying beliefs about the truth condition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b66bf85d-ef84-4e92-955f-6a28c0d9d5f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where two individuals witness the same event and form beliefs about it, but their beliefs contradict each other due to differing worldviews, yet one of the beliefs is true and safe**, **Intuitively, we struggle to assert that either has knowledge due to this disagreement, despite one belief being true and safe**, **Variability in personal convictions highlights challenges in asserting knowledge under the safety requirement when differing views yield conflicting truth conditions**
