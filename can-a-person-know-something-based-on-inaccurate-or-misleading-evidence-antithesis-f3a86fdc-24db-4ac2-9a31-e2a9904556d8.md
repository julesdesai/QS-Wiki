# Can a person know something based on inaccurate or misleading evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3a86fdc-24db-4ac2-9a31-e2a9904556d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory With Truth Tracking](nozicks-tracking-theory-with-truth-tracking-synthesis-9a557177-6533-4827-a4a8-a2fb94f50474.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief based on misleading or faulty evidence, such as a broken clock that happens to show the correct time when they glance at it. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not actually know the time despite having a belief that is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than merely true belief; it requires a reliable method of arriving at the truth. This principle, in conjunction with the scenario, suggests that knowledge requires a basis that accurately tracks truth beyond mere coincidence.**
