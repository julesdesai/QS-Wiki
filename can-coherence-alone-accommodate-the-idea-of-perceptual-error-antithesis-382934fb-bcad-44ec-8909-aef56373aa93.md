# Can coherence alone accommodate the idea of perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 382934fb-bcad-44ec-8909-aef56373aa93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Phenomenalism](coherentist-phenomenalism-synthesis-7f881170-8b02-4aad-92a8-2bed5a4b8a3c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's perceptions are systematically deceptive, such as a person living in a virtual reality environment like The Matrix. In such a scenario, the person could have a coherent system of beliefs that are all false.**, **If coherence alone is the standard for knowledge, then this person could be said to have knowledge despite all their beliefs being incorrect.**, **It is implausible that a person has knowledge if every perceptual experience they have is part of a coherent but entirely false system.**
