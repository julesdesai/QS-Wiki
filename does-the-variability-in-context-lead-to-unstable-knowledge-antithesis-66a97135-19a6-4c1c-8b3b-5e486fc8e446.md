# Does the variability in context lead to unstable knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 66a97135-19a6-4c1c-8b3b-5e486fc8e446
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where changing minor details in context leads to significant changes in perceived knowledge. Intuitively, we might judge that such variability undermines the stability of what can be claimed as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should remain stable and reliable across minor contextual shifts. This principle suggests that if knowledge is too susceptible to contextual changes, its reliability is questionable.**
