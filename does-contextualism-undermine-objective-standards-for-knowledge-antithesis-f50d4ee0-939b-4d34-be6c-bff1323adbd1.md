# Does Contextualism undermine objective standards for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f50d4ee0-939b-4d34-be6c-bff1323adbd1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle states that knowledge should be based on objective standards that are universally applicable.**, **If the standards for justification vary with context, then knowledge becomes relative rather than objective.**, **Intuitively, the relativity of knowledge standards undermines the idea of any stable, objective standard for true belief.**
