# Is it enough for a proposition to be true and believed for it to be known if it fails the truth-tracking condition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fbb0cf88-b069-4044-9217-99b1b71e2195
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-tracking theory of knowledge](truth-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-bf2c0c4d-aad3-4dd0-ab9e-ac7c51e27bd7.md)

## Content

**A case where someone believes a coin will land heads and it indeed lands heads due to chance does not meet the truth-tracking condition, yet the proposition is true and believed.**, **If we accept this as knowledge, it contradicts the requirement that knowledge must track truth across possible alternatives to the proposition being true.**
