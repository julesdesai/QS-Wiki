# Does knowing require more than just a true belief and conducive environment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 745b6f3e-ab3b-4ebb-93ec-eaf602e30552
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief due to luck rather than proper cognitive function, even in a conducive environment. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know.**, **It is implausible that luck can play a constitutive role in the acquisition of knowledge.**
