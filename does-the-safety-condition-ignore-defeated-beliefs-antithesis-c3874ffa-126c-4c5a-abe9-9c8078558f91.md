# Does the safety condition ignore defeated beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c3874ffa-126c-4c5a-abe9-9c8078558f91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has justified belief in a proposition based on solid evidence which is ultimately true, but they unknowingly receive misleading information that serves as a defeater to their justification. Intuitively, their belief, though accidentally true and based on original safe grounds, does not seem like knowledge given the potential defeater.**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe it and their belief is safe. Defeaters introduce significant concerns for the safety account, challenging its comprehensiveness in defining knowledge.**
