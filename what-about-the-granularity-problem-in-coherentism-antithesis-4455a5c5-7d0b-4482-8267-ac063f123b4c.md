# What about the granularity problem in coherentism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4455a5c5-7d0b-4482-8267-ac063f123b4c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Epistemology](coherentism-in-epistemology-synthesis-03124e37-be18-4868-b2cf-e370b5186105.md)

## Content

**The concept of coherence typically applies to whole systems of beliefs rather than individual beliefs.**, **A single belief's contribution to coherence could be difficult to determine, as it might have complex interrelations within the system.**, **Intuitively, we expect certain beliefs to be independently justified, suggesting a granularity issue in assessing coherence strictly at the system level.**
