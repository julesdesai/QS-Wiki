# Does contextualism allow for stable epistemic evaluation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7f476a4-50a6-4d61-89bd-43a0afe039bc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a student claims to understand a mathematical proof in a classroom setting. If questioned in a research seminar, the contextual standards for evaluating their claim may rise, leading us to question the initial claim of understanding.**, **A fluctuating standard for knowing undermines the consistency required for reliable epistemic evaluation. Knowledge should offer stable assessment criteria, which are undermined by variable contexts.**
