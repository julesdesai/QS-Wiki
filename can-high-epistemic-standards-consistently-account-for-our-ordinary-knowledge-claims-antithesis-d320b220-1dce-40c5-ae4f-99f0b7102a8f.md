# Can high epistemic standards consistently account for our ordinary knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d320b220-1dce-40c5-ae4f-99f0b7102a8f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-bf708b38-e467-425c-8744-5d490abcb982.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider everyday situations where people claim to know simple facts, like the color of an object in good lighting.**, **Intuitively, people exhibit confidence in their knowledge claims without meeting high epistemic standards.**, **If ordinary knowledge claims require meeting high epistemic standards, then many common knowledge claims would unjustifiably be considered false.**
