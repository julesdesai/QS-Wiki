# Is true belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e0330a9a-0b36-4e71-9b5e-6e78be71d48d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-54895291-30c8-42cf-8322-baea34575bd1.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where someone randomly guesses the correct answer to a complex question, resulting in a true belief. Intuitively, we would not ascribe knowledge in this case despite the belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that a true belief formed without proper justification or reliable method does not constitute knowledge.**
