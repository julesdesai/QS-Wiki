# Can hallucinations undermine the stability of perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fa390a84-874f-419c-b5cb-5123e17e2cee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Experience Consistency](phenomenal-experience-consistency-synthesis-dff6895e-5f7e-47e4-9fd7-5b1463da077a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person experiences vivid hallucinations indistinguishable from real perceptions. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their experiences jeopardize perceptual stability.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should distinguish between veridical (truthful) and non-veridical experiences. This principle together with the scenario implies that perceptual consistency is challenged by indistinguishable hallucinations.**
