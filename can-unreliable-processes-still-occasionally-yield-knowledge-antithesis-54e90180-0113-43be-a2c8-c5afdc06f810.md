# Can unreliable processes still occasionally yield knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 54e90180-0113-43be-a2c8-c5afdc06f810
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-383ba069-e0f1-4291-bdc7-eacefc701040.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition based on guessing. Intuitively, in this scenario, guessing does not seem to be a reliable process for forming beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a process yields a truth sporadically and not due to reliability, then it is not considered reliable. This principle together with the claim that guessing is not reliable entails that guesswork even if correct does not amount to knowledge.**
