# Could different cognitive frameworks lead to contradictory knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c5dc9b2-386e-420f-bf2b-85fa34ff8dc8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism Summary](transcendental-idealism-summary-synthesis-0df97e88-4364-4bc5-aaf8-288ab627f355.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals with distinct cultural frameworks interpret the same sensory experience in completely opposing ways; intuitively, both cannot be said to rightly know the same proposition**, **There is a plausible principle that moral and aesthetic knowledge, dependent heavily on cognitive frameworks, yields radically different claims across cultural contexts, suggesting a lack of objectivity**, **It is implausible that knowledge claims grounded in distinct but conflicting cognitive frameworks can both be true, implying a limitation of framework-dependent epistemology**
