# Can false evidence lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83be71b3-86f0-4989-a597-470eb6773b10
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Grounding Knowledge in Evidence](grounding-knowledge-in-evidence-synthesis-000c43a5-6d20-4fdc-9093-7ec5ca2a4c8d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be based on false evidence.**, **Suppose we have a case where a detective concludes a suspect is guilty based on evidence manufactured by an unreliable witness, but the suspect is guilty by coincidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that the detective has knowledge or that they do not. If we say they have knowledge, then knowledge can be grounded in false evidence. If we say they do not have knowledge, then false evidence cannot lead to knowledge.**
