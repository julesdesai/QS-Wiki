# Can knowledge exist without reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 889fca0c-2308-4dfc-bca3-ebb148f076c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Reliabilism](contextualism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c7c3d2a5-9a25-4e3f-9e1f-65010441e024.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's belief in a proposition is produced by an unreliable process but happens to be true. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a belief is not formed through a reliable process, it cannot be considered knowledge. This principle suggests that reliability is a necessary component of knowledge.**
