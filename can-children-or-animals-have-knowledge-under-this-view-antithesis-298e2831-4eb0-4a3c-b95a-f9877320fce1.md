# Can children or animals have knowledge under this view?

**Node ID:** 298e2831-4eb0-4a3c-b95a-f9877320fce1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**Children and animals often seem to have knowledge despite lacking sophisticated epistemic virtues.**, **Imagine a child knowing the sound of a parent’s voice without consciously applying any epistemic virtue, yet intuitively, this is knowledge.**, **Under the requirement that knowledge involves the successful application of cognitive virtues, such instances challenge the universality of the theory across different cognitive agents.**
