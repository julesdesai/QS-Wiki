# Does hallucination challenge the notion that perception is direct access to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50c6b359-3eb6-4615-b81a-ae633b295edc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is experiencing a vivid hallucination of an apple. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have direct access to knowledge about an actual apple.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a causal connection to an external object. Since hallucinations do not involve such a connection, they challenge the idea of perception as direct access.**
