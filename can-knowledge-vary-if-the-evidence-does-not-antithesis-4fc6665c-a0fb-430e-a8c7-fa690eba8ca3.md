# Can knowledge vary if the evidence does not?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4fc6665c-a0fb-430e-a8c7-fa690eba8ca3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where two individuals have identical evidence regarding a proposition, but are situated in different contexts with varying standards for knowing. Intuitively, we would judge that both individuals should equally know or not know the proposition.**, **The idea that knowledge depends on the context and has varying standards seems to conflict with the expectation of consistent knowledge in cases of identical evidence.**
