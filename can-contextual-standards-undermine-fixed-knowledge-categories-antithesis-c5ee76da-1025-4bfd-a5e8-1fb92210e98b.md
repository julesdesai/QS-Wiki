# Can contextual standards undermine fixed knowledge categories?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5ee76da-1025-4bfd-a5e8-1fb92210e98b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**The standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, suggesting flexibility in the category of knowledge. However, it is plausible to hold that knowledge should remain classified under fixed categories to preserve its objective value.**, **Imagine a scenario where shifting contexts lead to continual reclassification of beliefs from knowledge to non-knowledge and vice versa. Intuitively, this instability conflicts with the desire for consistent and reliable knowledge classifications.**
