# Can a person have knowledge that arises from intellectual virtue but is unsafe?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 408b5ab5-6363-46ff-9133-603cbd5e114e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a mathematician solves a complex problem correctly, relying on their intellectual virtue, but in nearby possible worlds, they make a calculation error. Intuitively, we would judge that she still knows the answer despite the unsafeness.**, **It is plausible that knowledge can arise from intellectual virtue even if the belief is unsafe in nearby worlds. This suggests that the conditions for knowledge should not strictly require safety in all nearby worlds.**
