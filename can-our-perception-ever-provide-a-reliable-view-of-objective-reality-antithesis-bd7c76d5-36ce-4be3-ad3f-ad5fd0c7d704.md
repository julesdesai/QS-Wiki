# Can our perception ever provide a reliable view of objective reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd7c76d5-36ce-4be3-ad3f-ad5fd0c7d704
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-de08aecd-5e9b-4f9b-874a-d2dc97cb1697.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a scenario in which a person perceives a stick partially submerged in water as bent. Intuitively, this perception misrepresents the objective reality of the stick being straight.**, **Our perception suggests that the stick is bent, but upon removing the stick from water, it appears straight, indicating perception's fallibility.**, **Even after interpretation and correction, the initial perception was incorrect, questioning whether objective reality can be accurately known through our senses.**
