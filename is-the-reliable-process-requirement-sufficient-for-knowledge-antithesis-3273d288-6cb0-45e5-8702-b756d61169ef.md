# Is the Reliable Process Requirement Sufficient for Knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3273d288-6cb0-45e5-8702-b756d61169ef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process. Intuitively, we might doubt whether the person genuinely has knowledge if they lack understanding of why the belief is true.**, **A situation could arise where a person holds a belief via a reliable process due to luck rather than understanding. Intuitively, genuine knowledge seems to require more than just a reliable process.**
