# Are there cases where unnecessary evidence still justifies belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** daef2720-8db8-4f4f-a2cc-07c39e4ed0b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d871be87-85c4-4634-87bb-b00a8ec22277.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where an individual forms a belief based on more evidence than needed for certainty. In such circumstances, intuitively, one might argue that justification remains intact regardless of excess evidence.**, **This suggests that adequacy of evidence might sometimes be a weaker requirement, questioning its necessity for justified belief.**
