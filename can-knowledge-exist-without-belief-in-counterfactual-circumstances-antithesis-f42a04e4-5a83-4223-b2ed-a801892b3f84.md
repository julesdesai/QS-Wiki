# Can knowledge exist without belief in counterfactual circumstances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f42a04e4-5a83-4223-b2ed-a801892b3f84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a false proposition in all nearby possible worlds except the actual world. In this scenario, intuitively, one might judge that they can still have knowledge in the actual world despite their belief not tracking truth across those worlds.**, **It is plausible to assert that knowledge should account for circumstances where truth-tracking fails in most nearby instances but succeeds in the actual instance.**
