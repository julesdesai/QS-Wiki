# Can reliable beliefs that fail in different contexts question the universality of reliability in guaranteeing knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c1be327-31b9-464a-aad9-64d71d9eae11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a reliable belief-forming process is effective in one context but fails in another due to differing external conditions. Intuitively, reliance on such a process cannot universally guarantee knowledge across all contexts.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliability should be context-independent to serve as a determinant of knowledge. When reliability in one context does not translate to another, this principle entails a failure of reliability to serve as a sufficient condition for knowledge.**
