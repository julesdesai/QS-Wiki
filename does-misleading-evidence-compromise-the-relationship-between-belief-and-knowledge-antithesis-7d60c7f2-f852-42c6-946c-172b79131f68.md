# Does misleading evidence compromise the relationship between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d60c7f2-f852-42c6-946c-172b79131f68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has accurate evidence about a proposition but is presented with misleading additional evidence causing doubt.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge even though they initially had evidence for their belief.**, **This suggests that having evidential support for a true belief may not be sufficient for knowledge when additional misleading factors intervene.**
