# Does pragmatic success ensure genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 41907752-e9ec-4f79-a111-ead587c8559b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone consistently achieves pragmatic success through beliefs that turn out to be true by chance, such as using a horoscope for decision-making. Intuitively, we would doubt that these beliefs qualify as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than pragmatic success; it requires reliable methods of belief formation. This principle suggests that the source of belief is critical to its status as knowledge.**
