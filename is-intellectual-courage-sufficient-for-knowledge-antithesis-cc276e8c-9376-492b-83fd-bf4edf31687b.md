# Is intellectual courage sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cc276e8c-9376-492b-83fd-bf4edf31687b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person bravely maintains their belief against strong opposition but lacks evidence or true understanding. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is knowledgeable or not. If we say the belief is knowledgeable, then intellectual courage seems sufficient. If we say the belief is not knowledgeable, then courage is not enough.**, **A belief formed or maintained mainly due to courage without sufficient reason lacks epistemic depth.**, **There is a plausible principle that courage in belief maintenance needs to be coupled with truth and understanding to constitute knowledge.**
