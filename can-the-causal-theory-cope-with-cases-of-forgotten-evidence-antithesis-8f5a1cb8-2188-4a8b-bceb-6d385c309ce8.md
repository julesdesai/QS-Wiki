# Can the causal theory cope with cases of forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f5a1cb8-2188-4a8b-bceb-6d385c309ce8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dc61c008-27ed-4921-863e-badc9b961db6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone knew a fact due to sound evidence they later forget, but they retain the true belief; either we must say they know it without justification, or causal connection must be indirect, both of which seem problematic.**, **Intuitively, cases with forgotten justifications challenge the principle that justification must link directly to the causal origin of the knowledge.**
