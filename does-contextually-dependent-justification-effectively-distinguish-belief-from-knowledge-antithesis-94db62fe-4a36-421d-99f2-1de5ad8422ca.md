# Does contextually dependent justification effectively distinguish belief from knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 94db62fe-4a36-421d-99f2-1de5ad8422ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism on Epistemic Justification](contextualism-on-epistemic-justification-synthesis-c8f3ec75-75e9-400a-9969-d95699e610cc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person justifies a belief in one context, but the belief remains questionable in general; this blurs the line between belief and knowledge.**, **It becomes difficult to clearly demarcate when beliefs suffice as knowledge if context overly influences justification.**
