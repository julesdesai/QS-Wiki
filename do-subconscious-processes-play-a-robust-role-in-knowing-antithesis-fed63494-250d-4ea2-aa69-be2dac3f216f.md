# Do subconscious processes play a robust role in knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fed63494-250d-4ea2-aa69-be2dac3f216f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-bf708b38-e467-425c-8744-5d490abcb982.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires conscious justification rather than subconscious processes.**, **Subconscious processes might lack the reliability and transparency needed for robust knowledge justification.**, **If subconscious processes were reliable justifiers, clear cases of unreliable subconscious beliefs would not challenge claims to knowledge.**
