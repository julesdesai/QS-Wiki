# Nozickian Conditional Account of Knowledge

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition is true, then the person believes it, and if the proposition is false, then the person does not believe it**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief tracks the truth through the relevant relations of belief and falsity**
