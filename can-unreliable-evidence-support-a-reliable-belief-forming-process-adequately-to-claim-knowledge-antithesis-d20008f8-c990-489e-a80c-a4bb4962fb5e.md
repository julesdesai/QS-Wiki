# Can unreliable evidence support a reliable belief-forming process adequately to claim knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d20008f8-c990-489e-a80c-a4bb4962fb5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief based on expert opinion that is usually right but backed by unreliable evidence at this moment. We would question knowledge attribution due to unreliable support, even if the expert was right this time.**, **A belief's formation requires not only a reliable endpoint but also a reliable evidential basis, which the unreliable evidence challenges.**
