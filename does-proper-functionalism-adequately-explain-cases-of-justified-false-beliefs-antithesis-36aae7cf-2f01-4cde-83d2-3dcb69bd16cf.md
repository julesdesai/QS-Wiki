# Does Proper Functionalism adequately explain cases of justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36aae7cf-2f01-4cde-83d2-3dcb69bd16cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-17c9fccb-5f3e-4011-91c3-5213c66550ac.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief through properly functioning cognitive faculties but the belief is false due to misleading evidence. Intuitively, we would judge the belief to be justified but not knowledge.**, **It is implausible that proper functioning alone can account for the justification of beliefs when environmental factors lead to false conclusions.**
