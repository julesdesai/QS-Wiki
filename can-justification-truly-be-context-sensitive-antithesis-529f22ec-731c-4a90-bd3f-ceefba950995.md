# Can justification truly be context-sensitive?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 529f22ec-731c-4a90-bd3f-ceefba950995
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-70123d28-f52a-42a2-8d85-6f92ac2f5bd1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a detective has gathered the same set of evidence for a case, but whether the case is discussed in a police station or at a dinner table changes whether the belief is justified. Intuitively, the justification should depend on the evidence, not the context of discussion.**, **It is implausible that the context of a conversation or the stakes involved could change the intrinsic justification of a belief that is supported by fixed evidence.**
