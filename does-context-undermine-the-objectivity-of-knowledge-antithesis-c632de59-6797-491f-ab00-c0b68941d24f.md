# Does context undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c632de59-6797-491f-ab00-c0b68941d24f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-70123d28-f52a-42a2-8d85-6f92ac2f5bd1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be objective and stable across contexts. This principle suggests that knowledge should not shift based purely on conversational or practical factors.**, **Consider a case in which a student believes she knows an answer during a classroom discussion, but this belief is questioned during a formal exam setting. Intuitively, it seems problematic to claim she does not know the answer anymore.**
