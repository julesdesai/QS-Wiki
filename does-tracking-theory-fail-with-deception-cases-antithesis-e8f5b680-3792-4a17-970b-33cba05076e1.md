# Does tracking theory fail with deception cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e8f5b680-3792-4a17-970b-33cba05076e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d302593-f2de-4e10-8195-85a314e81400.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone believes a true proposition, but the belief was manipulated to lead them away from that truth.**, **In such scenarios of deception, the person’s belief may accidentally align with the truth, without effectively tracking it across situations.**, **This suggests truth-tracking fails to account for knowledge in deception scenarios, as intentional manipulation is not conducive to knowledge.**
