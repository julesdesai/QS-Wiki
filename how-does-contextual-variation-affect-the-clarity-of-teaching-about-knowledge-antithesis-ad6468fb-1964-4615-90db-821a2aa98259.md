# How does contextual variation affect the clarity of teaching about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad6468fb-1964-4615-90db-821a2aa98259
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-d2c0abd4-6924-4f8e-8c3f-b341856f4998.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which small shifts in context result in changes in how we assess knowledge. Intuitively, we either have to say that such shifts matter for knowledge assessments or that they do not. If we say these shifts matter, then instructional clarity is disrupted by varying standards. If we say they do not matter, then context does not impact knowledge to the extent claimed by contextualism.**
