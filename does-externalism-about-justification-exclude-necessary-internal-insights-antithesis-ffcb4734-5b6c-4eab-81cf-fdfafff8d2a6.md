# Does externalism about justification exclude necessary internal insights?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ffcb4734-5b6c-4eab-81cf-fdfafff8d2a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Externalism Summary](safety-condition-externalism-summary-synthesis-29bd2257-ee31-4256-8506-f8628368ab8d.md)

## Content

**Consider an individual who has a true belief, but it fits into a complex theory they do not understand. Intuitively, we would not credit them with knowledge because they cannot provide insight into the truth of the theory.**, **Externalist justification focuses on the external reliability of beliefs rather than the individual's awareness or comprehension of the belief's basis.**, **A plausible principle of knowledge is that an individual must have some insight or understanding into the reasons for their belief, beyond its external truth condition.**
