# Can justification really involve an infinite regress of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ed76c6af-22cd-4387-954d-4fad49412e62
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-17c5b8c2-c7de-43ec-bf90-6dedae4ad235.md)

## Content

**For any series to be infinitely long, it must never terminate and have an unbounded number of members.**, **Intuitively, a series of reasons that justifies a belief would need to be entirely accessible to be operative in justification.**, **It is implausible that an individual can access an infinite series of reasons, making such a series inapplicable for practical justification.**
