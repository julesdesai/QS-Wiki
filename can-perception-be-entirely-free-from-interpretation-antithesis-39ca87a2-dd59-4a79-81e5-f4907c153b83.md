# Can perception be entirely free from interpretation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39ca87a2-dd59-4a79-81e5-f4907c153b83
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-04d59a43-df5d-4ff1-bc30-b363f66f9a03.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person perceives a red apple on a table and simply sees it as an apple. In this scenario, intuitively, the person's perception is not influenced by any reinterpretation or cognition, but is instead a direct apprehension of the object as it is.**, **It is plausible to maintain that perception can occur without continual interpretative processes, as some perceptions seemingly deliver truth without cognitive filtering.**
