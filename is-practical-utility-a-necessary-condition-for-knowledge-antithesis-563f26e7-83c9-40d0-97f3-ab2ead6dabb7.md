# Is practical utility a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 563f26e7-83c9-40d0-97f3-ab2ead6dabb7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person holds a deeply theoretical belief, such as a mathematical truth, with no immediate practical utility. Intuitively, it still seems that such a belief constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can include purely theoretical beliefs without practical applications. This implies practical utility is not a necessary condition for knowledge.**
