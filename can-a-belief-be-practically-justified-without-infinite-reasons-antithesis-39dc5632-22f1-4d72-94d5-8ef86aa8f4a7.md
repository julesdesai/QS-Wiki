# Can a belief be practically justified without infinite reasons?

**Consider a scenario in which a person must make a decision based on their belief with time constraints. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person can be justified in acting even with finite justification.**, **Justification in practical reasoning often leads to action based on finite considerations, which challenges the necessity of infinite justification.**
