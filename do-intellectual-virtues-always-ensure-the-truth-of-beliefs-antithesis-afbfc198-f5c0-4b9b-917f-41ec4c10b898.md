# Do intellectual virtues always ensure the truth of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** afbfc198-f5c0-4b9b-917f-41ec4c10b898
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual virtues, understood as reliable cognitive abilities, always lead to true beliefs. In complex, unpredictable environments, even virtuous agents may form false beliefs despite their intellectual reliability.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a meteorologist uses sophisticated equipment and the best available data to predict weather patterns. Despite this virtuous method, unexpected changes in climate conditions lead to an incorrect forecast. Intuitively, we might say the meteorologist is intellectually virtuous, yet they have formed a false belief, challenging the idea that their virtues must lead to truth.**
