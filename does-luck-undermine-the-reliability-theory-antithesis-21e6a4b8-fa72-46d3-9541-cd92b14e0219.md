# Does luck undermine the reliability theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21e6a4b8-fa72-46d3-9541-cd92b14e0219
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that outcomes significantly influenced by luck cannot be classed as reliable because luck inherently lacks consistency and predictability. This raises a question about whether beliefs forming by fortuitous circumstances, rather than structured processes, could or should be counted as knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where someone wins a random lottery by guessing the numbers despite no understanding of odds or probability. Intuitively, this luck-driven belief cannot be considered a reliable means of generating true outcomes or knowledge.**
