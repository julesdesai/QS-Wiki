# Does context variability undermine knowledge consistency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 470b5050-6325-471b-8fc8-727ce39fecc2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people are in the same situation and one knows something while the other does not, due to differing contexts. Intuitively, this seems inconsistent with our understanding of knowledge as being objective and consistent across similar cases.**, **Suppose a case where person A is in a casual conversation and claims to know they will receive a letter today, while person B only counts the claim as knowledge if they have tracking information. Intuitively, we would say it is problematic if knowledge attribution changes based on conversational context alone.**
