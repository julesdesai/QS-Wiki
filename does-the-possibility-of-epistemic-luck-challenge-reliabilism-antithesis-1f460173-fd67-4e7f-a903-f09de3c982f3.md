# Does the possibility of epistemic luck challenge reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f460173-fd67-4e7f-a903-f09de3c982f3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person reads a broken clock that by luck shows the correct time. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the time.**, **A principle states that if a belief is produced by a reliable process, it should not result from epistemic luck.**, **In the case of the broken clock, the truth of the belief seems to be due to luck rather than the reliability of the process.**
