# Is correspondence with an interpreted reality sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 627d022a-dce0-4d2c-a252-461ca5535caa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a proposition merely corresponding with an interpreted reality, shaped by fallible mental processes, is sufficient for knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person’s mental processes interpret a deceptive illusion as reality. Intuitively, this correspondence with a falsely interpreted reality fails to constitute genuine knowledge.**
