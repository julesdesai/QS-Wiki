# Is practical effectiveness necessary for defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd7bda91-8c52-4411-8142-b83ea2bd8ef5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1312135d-680e-4dd5-9377-79ee82721c0c.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge should be dependent on practical effectiveness, because individuals can possess extensive knowledge that they never have the opportunity to apply practically, such as abstract mathematical knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has deep knowledge of a mathematical theorem but never uses it in practical scenarios; intuitive judgments suggest that the person indeed has knowledge despite the theorem's lack of practical effectiveness in their daily life.**
