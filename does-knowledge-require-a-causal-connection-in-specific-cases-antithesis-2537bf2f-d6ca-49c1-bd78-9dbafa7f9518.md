# Does knowledge require a causal connection in specific cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2537bf2f-d6ca-49c1-bd78-9dbafa7f9518
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately guesses the outcome of a coin toss without observing any causal factors, and it happens to be true. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the outcome because of the guess's safety or lacks knowledge due to the absence of a causal connection. If we say they know based on the guess's safety, then such knowledge seems inadequate without a causal link. If we say they lack knowledge due to the absence of a causal connection, then knowledge may sometimes require it.**
