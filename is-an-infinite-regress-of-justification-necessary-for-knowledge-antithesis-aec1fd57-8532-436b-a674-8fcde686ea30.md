# Is an infinite regress of justification necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aec1fd57-8532-436b-a674-8fcde686ea30
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Finite Infinitism Summary](finite-infinitism-summary-synthesis-cf1727db-af05-4f36-9758-13f464b5dd32.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a justification process that eventually terminates. A belief justified by an infinite regress of reasons seems to undermine practical decision-making and action.**, **It is implausible that a person can construct or possess an infinite chain of reasons. Human cognitive capacities are limited and cannot handle an infinite regress of reasoning.**
