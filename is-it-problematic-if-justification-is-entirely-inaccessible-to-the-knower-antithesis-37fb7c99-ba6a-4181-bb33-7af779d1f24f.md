# Is it problematic if justification is entirely inaccessible to the knower?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37fb7c99-ba6a-4181-bb33-7af779d1f24f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person’s belief is formed by a complex process entirely hidden from their awareness, such as a computer making a prediction for them. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition or that they do not know. If we say they know, then it seems odd because they cannot explain or understand why, which may undermine the credibility of their knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be accompanied by some level of cognitive access to its justification.**
