## Content

**Knowledge traditionally involves not just reliable processes, but also the virtues of the knower, such as attention and reflection.**, **Reliabilism focuses on processes devoid of the agent’s epistemic virtues, risking an incomplete account of justification.**, **If epistemic virtues are disregarded, reliabilism might inadequately capture the full spectrum of what constitutes knowledge.**
