# Can false beliefs with beneficial outcomes be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0526dc3-2752-456d-ad2a-3789144ce98f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Consequences](pragmatism-with-practical-consequences-synthesis-9fee2762-7d04-464a-9533-5a40ce1aa186.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a false proposition that inadvertently leads to a beneficial outcome, like believing a placebo will cure their illness and then recovering. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the placebo will cure them despite the practical benefit.**, **Knowledge is traditionally considered true belief that is justified. If a belief is false, it cannot be knowledge regardless of practical outcomes.**
