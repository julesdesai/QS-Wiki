# Does contextualism allow for clear distinctions between knowledge and mere belief when contexts overlap?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bddd353c-1e31-4486-a73e-adcad6d1bc5b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where two people are discussing a topic, and their conversational contexts are slightly different but overlap. Intuitively, it seems difficult to determine whether either person actually "knows" the topic being discussed.**, **When contextual boundaries are not clearly defined, it can lead to ambiguity about whether the contextual standards for knowledge are met.**, **In such cases, disagreement about what constitutes knowledge might arise simply from differences in perceived contextual standards rather than actual epistemic differences.**
