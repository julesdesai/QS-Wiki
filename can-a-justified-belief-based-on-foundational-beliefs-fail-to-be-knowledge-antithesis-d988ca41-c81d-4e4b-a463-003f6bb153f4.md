# Can a justified belief based on foundational beliefs fail to be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d988ca41-c81d-4e4b-a463-003f6bb153f4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-3cd555af-8ec3-406f-b70a-64f89852bc49.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person justifies their belief via foundational beliefs, but those foundational beliefs are false. Intuitively, we either have to say that this is not a case of knowledge or that foundational beliefs do not contribute to knowledge. If we say the former, then merely having foundational beliefs is not enough for knowledge.**
