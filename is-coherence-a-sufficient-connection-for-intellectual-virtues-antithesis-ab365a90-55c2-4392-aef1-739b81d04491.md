# Is coherence a sufficient connection for intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab365a90-55c2-4392-aef1-739b81d04491
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Coherentism](virtue-epistemology-coherentism-synthesis-fa375fb7-6e26-497f-b63d-bbaec5305ff8.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues such as open-mindedness may lead to changing one’s beliefs over time, even if they remain coherent.**, **Coherence between beliefs does not necessarily guarantee that they were formed by intellectual virtues, as coherence can be accidental.**, **In a thought experiment, two people could have coherent beliefs, but only one might use intellectual virtues to arrive at them, suggesting coherence alone does not assure virtue utilization.**
