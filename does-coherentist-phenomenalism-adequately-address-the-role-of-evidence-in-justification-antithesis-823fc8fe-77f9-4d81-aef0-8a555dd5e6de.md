# Does coherentist phenomenalism adequately address the role of evidence in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 823fc8fe-77f9-4d81-aef0-8a555dd5e6de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Phenomenalism](coherentist-phenomenalism-synthesis-7f881170-8b02-4aad-92a8-2bed5a4b8a3c.md)

## Content

**Evidential input from perception is a critical component in traditional accounts of justification.**, **Coherentist phenomenalism might allow a belief to be justified primarily by its fit within a belief system, rather than by direct perceptual evidence.**, **Neglecting the importance of perceptual evidence may weaken the robustness of justification under coherentist phenomenalism.**
