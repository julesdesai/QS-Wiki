# Can reliability alone guarantee knowledge without considering all possible scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fcb71666-ec63-4f5f-bc56-b29b67fbe1ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based on a reliable process, but that process is interfered by an external factor they are unaware of. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not know the proposition despite the reliability of the process.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not depend on elements beyond a person's awareness or control. This principle, together with the existence of unknown influencing factors, entails that reliable processes alone may not guarantee knowledge.**
