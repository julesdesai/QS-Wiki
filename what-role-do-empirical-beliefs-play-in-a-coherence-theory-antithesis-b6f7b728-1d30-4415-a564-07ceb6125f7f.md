# What role do empirical beliefs play in a coherence theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6f7b728-1d30-4415-a564-07ceb6125f7f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where empirical beliefs, like those formed from sensory experience, do not fit neatly within a coherence model of justification. Intuitively, empirical beliefs seem to demand a kind of foundational justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that empirical beliefs require a direct interaction with the world that coherence among ideas alone cannot provide.**, **Without accommodating the foundational character of empirical beliefs, a coherence theory seems to omit a crucial aspect of how we understand and justify our experiences.**
