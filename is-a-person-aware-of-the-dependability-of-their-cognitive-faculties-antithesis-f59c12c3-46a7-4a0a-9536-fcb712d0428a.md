# Is a person aware of the dependability of their cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f59c12c3-46a7-4a0a-9536-fcb712d0428a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Dependability View](epistemic-dependability-view-synthesis-57cd5008-8279-46a6-a04a-50cbd8dfd618.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief but does not know whether their cognitive faculties are dependably functioning. Intuitively, awareness of dependability seems necessary for knowledge.**, **A principle that knowledge involves some level of metacognitive awareness about one's cognitive faculties is plausible. This principle, alongside an actual lack of such awareness, challenges the idea of knowledge arising from mere dependability.**
