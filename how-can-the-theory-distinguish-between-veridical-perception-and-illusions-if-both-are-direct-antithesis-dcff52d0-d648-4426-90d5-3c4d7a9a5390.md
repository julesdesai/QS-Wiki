# How can the theory distinguish between veridical perception and illusions if both are direct?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dcff52d0-d648-4426-90d5-3c4d7a9a5390
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-2d3f5cf7-4de1-4047-bfb0-105e4b8ca669.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that veridical perception and illusions are distinguishable. This principle, together with direct apprehension of objects, entails that distinguishing factors must be identified for each perception.**, **Consider a scenario where a person perceives a round coin appearing elliptical when viewed from an angle. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the perception is illusory yet the theory suggests direct apprehension.**
