# Does the mind-dependent nature of reality annihilate the possibility of an objective metaphysics?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b84f5504-731f-4568-8efa-63e708b86730
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a coherent metaphysical framework should provide an objective account of existence. This principle suggests that if reality is mind-dependent, metaphysics—concerned with the nature of existence—cannot achieve objectivity, thus undermining the claim to objective knowledge.**
