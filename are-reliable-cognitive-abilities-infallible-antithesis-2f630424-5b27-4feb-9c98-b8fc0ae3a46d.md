# Are reliable cognitive abilities infallible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2f630424-5b27-4feb-9c98-b8fc0ae3a46d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that reliable cognitive abilities can be infallible as no set of cognitive abilities is immune to error. Every individual with reliable cognitive abilities may still encounter scenarios that lead to mistaken beliefs due to unforeseen factors or limited evidence.**, **Consider a scenario where a skilled chess player anticipates an opponent's move based on years of experience, yet the opponent unexpectedly deviates from their usual strategy, resulting in a misjudgment. Intuitively, this suggests that even reliable cognitive abilities are not infallible, posing challenges to the assumption that they always lead to true beliefs.**
