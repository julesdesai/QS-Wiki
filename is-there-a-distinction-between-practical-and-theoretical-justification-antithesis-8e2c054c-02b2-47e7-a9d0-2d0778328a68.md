# Is there a distinction between practical and theoretical justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8e2c054c-02b2-47e7-a9d0-2d0778328a68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Lemmas View](no-false-lemmas-view-synthesis-031e6f6f-2b49-47e4-821c-d2421562acef.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person avoids walking under ladders because they were told it’s unlucky, and by doing so they avoid getting hit by a falling object. Intuitively, we either have to say that their avoidance is justified for practical reasons or that it is not. If we say it is justified, then there is a distinction between practical and theoretical justification. If we say it is not justified, then knowledge might require more than practical outcomes.**
