# Does invariantism overlook the role of stakes in practical reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d0ad8da-b08b-4515-ab17-5561c7f3381b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person must choose whether to cross a rickety bridge based on their belief it is safe. If in a low-stakes situation they cross, but in a high-stakes they hesitate, intuitively, this reflects changing knowledge standards.**, **The principle that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant implies that stakes should not affect their decision-making process.**, **It is implausible that practical stakes do not influence our sense of whether we know something.**
