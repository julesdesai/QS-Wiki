# Are there beliefs that remain sensitive yet aren't knowledge due to changing contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d76642a-6cb4-42d7-99bc-11c5d3c9c218
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a painting is a genuine Picasso because a trusted expert told them so, and this is true. Intuitively, we might depend on context, such as new evidence or altered settings, to sustain this knowledge.**, **If in close possible worlds, the narrative or test conditions change slightly such that the statement about authenticity remains sensitive, the person may lack knowledge due to altered epistemic contexts.**
