# Are external objects always experienced as they truly are?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2f10e5af-55b2-4945-a95f-93ba39bb3ca1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-ad05d8f4-c9d2-4024-8001-bf520360cf02.md)

## Content

**Perceptual experiences often present external objects with properties they do not have, as when colors look different under different lighting conditions.**, **If external objects are always experienced as they are, perceptual experiences would not misrepresent properties.**, **Perceptual experiences can represent objects inaccurately, calling into question the idea that they are experienced as they truly are.**
