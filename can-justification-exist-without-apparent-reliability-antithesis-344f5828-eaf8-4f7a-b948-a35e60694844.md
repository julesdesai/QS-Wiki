# Can justification exist without apparent reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 344f5828-eaf8-4f7a-b948-a35e60694844
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**It is plausible to think that a person could have compelling personal or anecdotal evidence leading them to justify a belief, yet this evidence lacks objective reliability.**, **Suppose someone trusts a typically reliable friend’s warning about a potential danger that turns out to be baseless. Intuitively, the belief is justified for the person but not objectively reliable, challenging the idea that justification requires reliability.**
