# Is truth always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7320898f-cecd-44f2-acbf-4a55c472da11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Knowledge-Based on Proper Function](knowledge-as-knowledge-based-on-proper-function-synthesis-53555bef-1e28-4c2f-bf5f-bf14f3bc1d69.md)

## Content

**Consider a hypothetical scenario in which a person has a belief that is widely accepted, pragmatic, and actionable but not necessarily true. Intuitively, people might regard this as knowledge due to its utility and support by evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that in some contexts, the utility and evidential support might substitute for absolute truth in constituting knowledge.**
