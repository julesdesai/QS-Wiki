# Can Reliabilism account for cases where reliable processes produce true beliefs by accident?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0343cf1-2df8-446d-9314-bad4fe86f788
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-24b421a0-08f3-4c66-b0fb-2acc7f5da514.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief based on a reliable cognitive process that is usually accurate but is accidentally correct in this instance. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge because the belief was true by luck rather than reliability.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to count as knowledge, it must not only be produced by a reliable process but also not merely accidentally true. This principle, together with the presence of an accidental true belief in a reliable process, suggests that reliabilism may not suffice for knowledge.**
