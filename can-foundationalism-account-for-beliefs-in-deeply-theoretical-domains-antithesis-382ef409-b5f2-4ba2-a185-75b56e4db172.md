# Can foundationalism account for beliefs in deeply theoretical domains?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 382ef409-b5f2-4ba2-a185-75b56e4db172
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person holds a basic belief about a complex theory in quantum mechanics that is not self-evident and cannot be directly sensed. Intuitively, we either have to say that such beliefs are unjustified or that the foundationalist criterion for basic beliefs is not applicable in this domain.**, **It is implausible that deeply theoretical beliefs which cannot be self-evident or evident to the senses can be foundational, yet these are crucial to our understanding of the world.**
