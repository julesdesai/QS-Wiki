# Can justification be based solely on basic beliefs without infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 027f5c10-b3e5-448a-9f75-6c2f0946b6a1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Communicable Justification](foundationalism-with-communicable-justification-synthesis-8dbcdd2b-eb8b-4593-a7ca-0bfc89d9258b.md)

## Content

**Suppose that to justify a belief, it must be supported by other beliefs, which is the core problem of epistemic regress. Foundationalism attempts to stop this regress by positing basic beliefs. However, if these basic beliefs require explanation or support, the regress continues.**, **The notion that beliefs are justified by other beliefs leads to a chain that risks either repeating infinitely or becomes circular, which foundationalism attempts to avoid but may not successfully achieve if basic beliefs aren't truly self-justifying.**
