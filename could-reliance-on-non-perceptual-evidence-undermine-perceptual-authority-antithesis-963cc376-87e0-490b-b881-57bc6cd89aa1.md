# Could reliance on non-perceptual evidence undermine perceptual authority?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 963cc376-87e0-490b-b881-57bc6cd89aa1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person can recognize a type of bird by sight and also by reading a field guide. Intuitively, reliance on the guide might undermine the authoritative nature of their perceptual experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptual experiences should hold primary authority in cases of direct observation. This principle together with reliance on non-perceptual evidence implies that primary perceptual knowledge is diluted.**, **It is implausible that non-perceptual validation would be necessary when direct perceptual evidence is sufficient on its own.**
