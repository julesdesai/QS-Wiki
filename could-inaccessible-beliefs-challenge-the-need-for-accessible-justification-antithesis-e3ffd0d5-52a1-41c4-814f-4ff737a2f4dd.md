# Could inaccessible beliefs challenge the need for accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3ffd0d5-52a1-41c4-814f-4ff737a2f4dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Access Internalism](access-internalism-synthesis-1ebb0a7e-8cd6-40ff-bf0f-a16161232f85.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief formed by a reliable cognitive process but is unaware of this process. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks knowledge due to inaccessible justification.**, **A true belief formed by a reliable process without the individual's awareness is a case where justification is not reflectively accessible.**
