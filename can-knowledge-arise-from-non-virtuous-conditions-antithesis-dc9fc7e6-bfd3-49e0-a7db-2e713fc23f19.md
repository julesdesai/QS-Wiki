# Can knowledge arise from non-virtuous conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc9fc7e6-bfd3-49e0-a7db-2e713fc23f19
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Refined Virtue Epistemology](refined-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed8e6ab8-d432-4955-9aec-982a4b148be7.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person forms a belief through non-virtuous means, like guessing or false reports, cannot be a case of knowledge.**, **Consider a situation where a belief turns out to be true due to circumstances unrelated to the person's intellectual virtues or efforts. Intuitively, this suggests such beliefs lack the status of knowledge.**
