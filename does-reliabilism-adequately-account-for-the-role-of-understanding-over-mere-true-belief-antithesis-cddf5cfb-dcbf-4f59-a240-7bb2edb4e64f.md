# Does reliabilism adequately account for the role of understanding over mere true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cddf5cfb-dcbf-4f59-a240-7bb2edb4e64f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Process-Oriented Justification](reliabilism-with-process-oriented-justification-synthesis-8900612b-46bb-42d6-a4c1-a983dd2da82b.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person accurately predicts future events through random guessing. Intuitively, we would judge that even true guesses do not constitute knowledge without understanding.**, **Guesses might incidentally be true, but they are not formed by a reliable cognitive process that involves understanding or explanation.**
