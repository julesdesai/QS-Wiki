# Can contextualism handle skeptical challenges consistently?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 370ce785-3223-4007-9b49-701a60bf529d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which the context involves a skeptical challenge, such as a brain-in-a-vat scenario, where doubting everything is the standard. In such a context, intuitively, we would judge that no one knows anything.**, **There is a plausible principle that the standards for knowledge in any context must be capable of being satisfied for at least some propositions. This principle together with the scenario entails a tension because if standards can vary so much that no knowledge is possible in certain contexts, it undermines the possibility of any context-sensitive knowledge.**
