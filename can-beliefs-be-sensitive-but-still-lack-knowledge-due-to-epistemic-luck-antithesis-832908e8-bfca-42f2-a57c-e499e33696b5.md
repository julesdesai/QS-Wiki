# Can beliefs be sensitive but still lack knowledge due to epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 832908e8-bfca-42f2-a57c-e499e33696b5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a student believes she passed an exam solely because her friend hinted the results were positive. She indeed passes, but this belief is sensitive because if it were false, she wouldn't believe it.**, **It is plausible to judge that epistemic luck played a significant role in her belief being true, which undermines the claim she actually knows she passed the exam.**
