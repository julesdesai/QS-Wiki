# Can future predictions qualify as knowledge within empirical direct realism?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientist accurately predicts a future phenomenon using available data without direct perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the scientist knows the future outcome, yet this knowledge lacks direct empirical support until verified later.**, **There is a plausible principle that states predictive success rests on forms of inference beyond direct observation, suggesting that not all practical knowledge emerges from current empirical direct realism.**
