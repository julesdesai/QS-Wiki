# How does Constructivism handle the persistence of memories not actively constructed from current perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 621eec26-4479-45f9-a06b-0a262588eec9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism Summary](constructivism-summary-synthesis-38220637-7d58-4a33-978f-6de4b5b7771f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person recalls a vivid and detailed memory from childhood, yet it does not align with what they currently perceive or conceived actively. Intuitively, we either have to say that these memories are not knowledge or that knowledge of past events does not require active organization of current sensory data. If the latter, it suggests some knowledge persists independently of current perceptual experiences and cognitive processes.**
