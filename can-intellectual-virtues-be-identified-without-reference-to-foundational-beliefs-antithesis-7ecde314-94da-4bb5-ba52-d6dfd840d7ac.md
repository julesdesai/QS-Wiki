# Can intellectual virtues be identified without reference to foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ecde314-94da-4bb5-ba52-d6dfd840d7ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we attempt to define intellectual virtues without any reference to foundational standards. Intuitively, we would judge that some foundational beliefs are necessary to identify what counts as a virtue.**, **It is implausible that virtues can be defined in isolation from beliefs about what is good or virtuous, which are themselves foundational.**
