# What about truths unknowable due to lack of causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cdf27bf1-4c9f-485e-a67b-c9b97a562b25
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b3086f02-fe19-4187-b3b5-dcf882df82f4.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where some historical facts cannot be connected causally to any current belief, such as a specific event from a long-extinct civilization for which no evidence remains. Intuitively, some might suggest these facts remain unknowable.**, **The causal requirement implies that without a causal connection, knowledge cannot occur, which seems problematic for unknowable truths simply due to lack of causal link paths.**
