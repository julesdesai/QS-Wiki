## Content

**A case in which a person sees a white sheet of paper as white under both sunlight and indoor lighting, despite changes in light wavelengths, suggests that perception adjusts based on context and mental interpretation, conflicting with the idea of perception being a direct, unchanged access to objects.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptual constancy requires mental processes to maintain consistent perception despite environmental changes, which implies mediation in perception.**
