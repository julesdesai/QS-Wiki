# Can knowledge based on perceptual experiences be inconsistent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 746b2b5f-021a-4d7c-96c3-7ea4d87d55a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-762c00d9-ee32-4648-8d8e-67643edf20ba.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person perceives a straight stick as bent when it is half-submerged in water. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person's perceptual experience is inconsistent with reality.**, **Knowledge should account for consistent perceptual experiences reflecting the actual state of objects, rather than being coherent only within subjective perceptions.**
