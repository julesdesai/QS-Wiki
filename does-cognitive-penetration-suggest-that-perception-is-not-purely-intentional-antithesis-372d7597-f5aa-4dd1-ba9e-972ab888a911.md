# Does cognitive penetration suggest that perception is not purely intentional?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 372d7597-f5aa-4dd1-ba9e-972ab888a911
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Intentionalism Summary](intentionalism-summary-synthesis-3678fe22-b4a0-4fa7-aad8-5dfa98b1a709.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's perception of a racially ambiguous face is influenced by stereotypes, affecting their perception's content. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that cognitive states influence perceptual states.**, **It is implausible that changes in perception are purely changes in intentional content if cognitive states can alter perception without changes in the external environment.**
