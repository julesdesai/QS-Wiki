# Does practical success conflict with the requirement of objective validity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 329dd9cb-d3d4-4b60-96b8-002b75188683
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a personal belief in a medical diagnosis turns out to be correct but was based on a personal intuition rather than objective medical tests. Intuitively, attributing knowledge here seems problematic as it lacks objective validity despite practical success.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective validity is essential for justificatory processes. If practical success bypasses such objective assessment, it seems to conflict with deeper norms of knowledge attribution.**
