# Does phenomenalism fail to provide a satisfactory account of memory as a connection to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bca17cec-d188-4172-b525-b8d2e7fa2121
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person remembers seeing a unique painting accurately years later despite no current sensory experience of it challenges the sufficiency of sensory experiences to explain memory.**, **There is a plausible principle that memory involves a connection to reality beyond present sensory experiences. This principle together with the dependence of phenomenalism on current sensory inputs implies that phenomenalism cannot fully explain the nature of memory.**
