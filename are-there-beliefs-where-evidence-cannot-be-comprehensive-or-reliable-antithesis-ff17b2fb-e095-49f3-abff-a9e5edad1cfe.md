# Are there beliefs where evidence cannot be comprehensive or reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff17b2fb-e095-49f3-abff-a9e5edad1cfe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Consider beliefs about personal experiences that cannot be corroborated, such as recalling specific childhood memories. Intuitively, we might judge that these beliefs constitute knowledge despite a lack of comprehensive evidence.**, **Certain personal or subjective beliefs may be viewed as knowledge even though evidence for them may neither be comprehensive nor reliably tested.**
