# Do practical considerations always affect knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8539e101-106d-4462-949e-99f22c0152db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Theory](pragmatic-encroachment-theory-synthesis-48f209b4-b71c-4b9b-814c-8c2a5af0c840.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which the practical stakes are minimal for a person P who believes that it will rain tomorrow based on a reliable weather forecast. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that P knows it will rain.**, **Consider a scenario in which the practical stakes for P increase significantly, such as needing to decide whether to hold an important outdoor event. Intuitively, we still judge that P knows it will rain tomorrow due to reliance on the same forecast.**, **It seems implausible that the mere increase in practical stakes for P should affect whether P's belief qualifies as knowledge.**
