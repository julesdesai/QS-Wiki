## Content

**A plausible principle suggests that consistently reliable perception should not require distinguishing between types of perception.**, **This principle implies that creating a distinction between veridical and non-veridical perceptions might not be necessary to dismiss skepticism.**, **Skepticism can arise from doubts about how we can know which perceptions are veridical if we rely on perception itself to determine this.**
