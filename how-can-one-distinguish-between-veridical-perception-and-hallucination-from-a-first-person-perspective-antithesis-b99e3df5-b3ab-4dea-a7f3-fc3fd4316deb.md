# How can one distinguish between veridical perception and hallucination from a first-person perspective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b99e3df5-b3ab-4dea-a7f3-fc3fd4316deb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a red apple that is actually there, and another scenario where they seem to see a red apple but it's a hallucination. It is implausible that the person, from a first-person perspective, can determine which scenario they are in solely based on internal experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a person cannot distinguish between two mental states through introspection, they should not be assumed to have different direct external evidence for those states.**
