# Can phenomenalism explain perceptual experiences in non-standard sensory conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf4538de-9450-4a99-b980-b74bb717c04b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person experiences an echo in a canyon where sounds are delayed and distorted. Intuitively, we either have to say that this is a coherent experience across auditory modalities or that it is not. If we say it is coherent, then it contradicts the notion that perception is consistent with normal auditory experiences. If we say it is not coherent, then it challenges the claim that all perception is explained by phenomenal experiences.**, **It is implausible that a disrupted auditory experience, such as an echo, aligns consistently with other sensory modalities when natural perception expectations are altered.**
