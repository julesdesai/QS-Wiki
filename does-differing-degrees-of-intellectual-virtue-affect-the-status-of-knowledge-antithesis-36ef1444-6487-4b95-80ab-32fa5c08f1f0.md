# Does differing degrees of intellectual virtue affect the status of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36ef1444-6487-4b95-80ab-32fa5c08f1f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people hold the same true belief, but one arrives at it through greater intellectual virtuosity than the other. Intuitively, both should possess knowledge, despite differing levels of virtue.**, **It is implausible to claim that only a high degree of intellectual virtue can result in knowledge, as it discounts the knowledge claims of those who employ lesser, but still competent, intellectual virtues.**
