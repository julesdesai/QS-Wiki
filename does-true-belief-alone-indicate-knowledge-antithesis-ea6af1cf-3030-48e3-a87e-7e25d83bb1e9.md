# Does true belief alone indicate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ea6af1cf-3030-48e3-a87e-7e25d83bb1e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-2eef6ba9-ea95-4aea-9a96-5c60688dde0e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person, without any good reason, guesses the correct answer to a question. Intuitively, we would hesitate to say they have knowledge, as their belief is not based on a reliable process.**, **If simply obtaining a true belief counts as knowledge, then accidental truths could be mistakenly identified as knowledge.**
