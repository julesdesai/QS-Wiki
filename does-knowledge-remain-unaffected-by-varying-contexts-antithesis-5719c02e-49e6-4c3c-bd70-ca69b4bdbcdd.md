# Does knowledge remain unaffected by varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5719c02e-49e6-4c3c-bd70-ca69b4bdbcdd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-0c8c051d-f89d-468b-87e6-c5a1fb54f910.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person accurately identifies a type of mushroom in a low-stakes context, such as a cooking class. In this scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person knows what type of mushroom it is.**, **Consider a different scenario where the same person is identifying the same mushroom, but in a high-stakes situation where misidentification could lead to poisoning. Intuitively, in this situation, we might judge that the person does not know what type of mushroom it is despite their belief.**, **The fact that judgments about what is known vary between low and high stakes scenarios suggests that knowledge may depend on the context, challenging the idea that knowledge is invariant across different contexts.**
