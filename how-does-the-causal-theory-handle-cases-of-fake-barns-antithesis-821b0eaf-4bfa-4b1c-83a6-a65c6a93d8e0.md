# How does the causal theory handle cases of fake barns?

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a fake barn that looks perfectly real but is surrounded by other misleading structures, yet one of the structures happens to be a real barn. Intuitively, we would judge that seeing the real barn among the fake barns does not result in knowledge even though the belief is true.**, **A plausible principle is that for a belief to count as knowledge, the mechanism of causation must reliably produce true beliefs. This principle together with the causal theory implies that not all true beliefs caused by seeing are knowledge.**
