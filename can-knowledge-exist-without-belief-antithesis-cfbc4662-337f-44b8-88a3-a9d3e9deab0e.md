# Can knowledge exist without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cfbc4662-337f-44b8-88a3-a9d3e9deab0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-40b3d691-a5bb-479c-ada3-1931521a86e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual possesses all the information needed to form a true belief but fails to consciously acknowledge or believe it. Intuitively, this raises the question of whether knowledge requires actively held beliefs.**, **A true belief is typically seen as a necessary component of knowledge, challenging scenarios where information alone is considered knowledge without belief.**, **If it is possible for someone to have knowledge without consciously holding a belief, this poses a question for linking knowledge strictly to intellectual virtue-induced belief.**
