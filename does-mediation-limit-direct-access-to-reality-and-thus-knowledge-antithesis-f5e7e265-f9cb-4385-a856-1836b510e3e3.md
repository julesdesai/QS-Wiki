# Does mediation limit direct access to reality and thus knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f5e7e265-f9cb-4385-a856-1836b510e3e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Acknowledgment of Mediation](indirect-realism-with-acknowledgment-of-mediation-synthesis-e56d95ce-eeee-446c-93a6-a21b3a4bd4d7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person views a landscape through virtual reality technology and perceives distance inaccurately. Intuitively, such mediation limits direct knowledge of the landscape's true distance.**, **There is a plausible principle that optimal knowledge requires some level of direct, unmediated access to the reality being perceived.**, **Mediation creates a layer that may obstruct rather than facilitate accurate knowledge acquisition.**
