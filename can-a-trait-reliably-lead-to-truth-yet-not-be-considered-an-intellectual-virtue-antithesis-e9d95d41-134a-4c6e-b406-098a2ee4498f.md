# Can a trait reliably lead to truth yet not be considered an intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9d95d41-134a-4c6e-b406-098a2ee4498f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-deb477f8-4a11-4db0-ba96-00a9c4a7815c.md)

## Content

**Some traits that reliably lead to truth may not align with traditional notions of virtue, such as obsessive information-gathering, which suggests that reliability alone doesn't qualify a trait as virtuous.**, **Consider an individual who compulsively gathers information out of fear. While this trait reliably leads to true beliefs, it is driven by an unhealthy source and is not typically viewed as a virtue.**
