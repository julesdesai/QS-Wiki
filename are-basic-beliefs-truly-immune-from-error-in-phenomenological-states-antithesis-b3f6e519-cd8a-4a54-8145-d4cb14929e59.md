# Are basic beliefs truly immune from error in phenomenological states?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3f6e519-cd8a-4a54-8145-d4cb14929e59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**Consider a belief such as "I am perceiving red." This belief about a phenomenological state seems to be beyond error.**, **In conditions like under certain lighting or neurological conditions, phenomenological beliefs deemed basic can actually be shown to be erroneous, contradicting the assumption of their infallibility.**
