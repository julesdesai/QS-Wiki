# Does varying justification imply instability in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7ff6785-cae3-405d-a1cd-6ec164b4fa36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people in different roles interpret the same data about climate change, one as a policymaker and the other as a blogger. The policymaker requires a higher standard of evidence to justify climate policy knowledge than does the blogger.**, **Intuitively, it is unreasonable to claim both possess the same level of knowledge when their justifications differ so significantly due to role-specific stakes.**, **It is implausible to assert that knowledge is invariant when justification practices clearly differ based on context, suggesting an instability in knowledge claims between different interpreters.**
