# Does knowing necessitate understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9bf96cb3-b283-405c-8f1d-4ea2f3f211e5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a student who memorizes answers for an exam without understanding the underlying concepts. If their answers are correct, intuitively, we stop short of calling it true knowledge due to the lack of comprehension.**, **Understanding is generally seen as necessary for robust knowledge claims. This implies that knowledge might depend on more than context-invariant justification and truth.**
