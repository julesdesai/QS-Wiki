# Can we conceptually separate causation and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 33fc24e4-5d96-4d07-b181-134a6d13c7c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c3b94507-0858-4bfa-a251-208b7829ae1f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief that a bird is in the garden because they hear singing, but the singing is from a recording. The belief is true, but the causation is unrelated to the fact.**, **It is implausible that a belief, even though true and caused by a sound, constitutes knowledge if the sound is not related to the fact of a bird being present.**
