# Do true beliefs formed from unreliable processes qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1dafc8c0-d7cd-4613-94de-11a85c1cc20f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b6d42ffa-59fb-457e-877f-3bcb8ac1af86.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has a true belief formed from an unreliable cognitive process, like consistently flipping a coin to decide outcomes.**, **A belief formed through unreliable processes lacks the backing of an intellectual virtue.**, **Cognitive virtues typically involve reliable, truth-conducive cognitive faculties or methods.**
