# Can false but seemingly strong justification still defeat the notion of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f09cd25-8c85-4ce0-b3e7-508bb9b72412
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Justification Theory](epistemic-justification-theory-synthesis-d0523946-79ba-4279-979e-9ba317ba7953.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a detailed and seemingly strong justification for a belief, but the justification is based on faulty data. Intuitively, we judge that their belief does not count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that for justification to lead to knowledge, it must be grounded in truth or factually correct data, not merely appear robust.**
