# Can knowledge exist without justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 75c89bdd-00f5-494c-ae78-2da7338bb58d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism: Contexts Influence Justification, Not Knowledge](moderate-contextualism-contexts-influence-justification-not-knowledge-synthesis-be3d790f-5519-485d-8f0d-32864e4a3e9e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition and the proposition is true, but they have no justification for the belief; intuitively, we would judge that they do not actually know the proposition.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge requires justification.**, **The principle that knowledge requires justification contradicts the idea that justification varies while standards for knowing are fixed.**
