# Can conflicting virtues invalidate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a24c378e-7d03-4a50-96a7-68088013078e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person's belief arises from conflicting intellectual virtues, such as curiosity and skepticism, leading to true belief through unresolved tension. Intuitively, we could question whether this constitutes knowledge.**, **It is plausible that for knowledge, intellectual virtues should harmonize towards truth. The conflict of virtues might suggest that virtue disharmony undermines the formation of knowledge.**
