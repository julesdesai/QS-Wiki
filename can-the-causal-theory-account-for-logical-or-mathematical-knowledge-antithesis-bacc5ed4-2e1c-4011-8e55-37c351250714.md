# Can the causal theory account for logical or mathematical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bacc5ed4-2e1c-4011-8e55-37c351250714
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a causal connection between belief and truth. This principle cannot explain how we know mathematical truths, such as 2+2=4, as these do not causally interact with our minds.**, **Consider a scenario where someone knows that the sum of angles in a triangle equals 180 degrees; intuitively, this knowledge does not seem to involve a causal interaction with the physical world.**
