# Is aiming at truth always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8bc6fcf-0b39-4fb8-99ce-a8a4e9a28a2c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-7d89ec66-0836-44e3-8613-d999cc659dfb.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person forms a belief based on a faculty aimed at comfort rather than truth, yet the belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we would still consider such a belief as potentially constituting knowledge.**, **It is implausible that all cognitive faculties must always aim at producing true beliefs for every instance of knowledge.**
