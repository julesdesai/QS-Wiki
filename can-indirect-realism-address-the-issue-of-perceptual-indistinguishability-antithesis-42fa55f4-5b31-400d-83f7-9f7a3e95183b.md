# Can indirect realism address the issue of perceptual indistinguishability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42fa55f4-5b31-400d-83f7-9f7a3e95183b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-801f6a7d-c909-434a-bdd9-effe8ebd5964.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two scenarios, one real and one not, are perceptually indistinguishable, like a real apple and a hologram apple. Intuitively, we either have to say knowledge is impossible or determine another method of distinguishing them. If we say knowledge is impossible, then the validity of perception is undermined. If we determine another method of distinguishing them, then reliance on perception is questionable.**
