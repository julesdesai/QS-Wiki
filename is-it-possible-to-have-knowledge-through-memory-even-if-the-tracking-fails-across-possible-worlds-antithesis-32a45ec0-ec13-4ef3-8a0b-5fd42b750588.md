# Is it possible to have knowledge through memory even if the tracking fails across possible worlds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 32a45ec0-ec13-4ef3-8a0b-5fd42b750588
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Consider someone who knows a mathematical fact from memory. If the proposition were false in some possible world, they might not stop believing it due to consistent prior affirmations and practice.**, **Memories that consistently yield true beliefs constitute knowledge even when counterfactual scenarios where they would continue to hold false beliefs do not exist. Intuition suggests learned factual knowledge should count as knowledge regardless of counterfactual considerations.**
