# Can a belief formed reliably but irresponsibly still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42d1955e-393e-4bc1-84d8-9fd3bf401ba8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Mixed Virtue Epistemology](mixed-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9b1d3c06-dd01-4be5-8e49-871c85befc1a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief using a reliable but deceptive method, such as a manipulated media source known for very few occasional truths. Intuitively, this does not seem to count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just true belief from a reliable process but also involves responsible evaluation of the method used. This principle, together with our scenario, suggests a distinction between mere true belief and knowledge.**
