# Does the causal theory adequately encompass testimonial knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1169ade2-e049-4203-96be-f81ffd068803
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cee9090d-2e34-47f2-b4ee-a59054387264.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual learns about a historical fact from a reliable textbook. The belief is true, but there is no direct causal chain from the past event to the individual's belief. Intuitively, we generally consider such testimonial knowledge valid.**, **Testimonial knowledge relies on the trustworthiness of the source and not a direct causal linkage to the truth of the proposition.**
