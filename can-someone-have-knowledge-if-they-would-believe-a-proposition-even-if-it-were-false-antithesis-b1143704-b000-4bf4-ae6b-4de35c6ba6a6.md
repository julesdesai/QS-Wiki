# Can someone have knowledge if they would believe a proposition even if it were false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1143704-b000-4bf4-ae6b-4de35c6ba6a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person, David, would continue to believe a proposition regardless of its truth. Intuitively, this implies a lack of sensitivity to the actual truth, which seems inconsistent with knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge demands sensitivity to the truth. If David's belief is not sensitive to whether the proposition is true, it undermines the claim to knowledge.**
