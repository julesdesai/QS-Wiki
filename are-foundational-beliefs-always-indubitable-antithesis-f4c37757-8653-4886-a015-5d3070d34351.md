# Are foundational beliefs always indubitable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4c37757-8653-4886-a015-5d3070d34351
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person firmly believes in an axiom or principle as foundational, yet others present compelling reasons to doubt it, like a mathematical axiom challenged by a new theory. Intuitively, a foundational belief cannot withstand serious challenges and maintain its indubitability. If foundational beliefs can be reasonably doubted, then they cannot provide the certainty required for other beliefs to rest upon them reliably.**
