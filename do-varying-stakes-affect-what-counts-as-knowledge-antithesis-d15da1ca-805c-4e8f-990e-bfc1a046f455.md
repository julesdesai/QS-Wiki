# Do varying stakes affect what counts as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d15da1ca-805c-4e8f-990e-bfc1a046f455
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person faces a low-stakes situation where little hinges on knowing a train’s departure time. Their belief, justified by checking the schedule once, may be considered knowledge. In a high-stakes situation, where missing the train would result in severe consequences, the same level of justification seems insufficient for knowledge. Intuitively, stakes seem to affect whether we attribute knowledge.**, **It is plausible that the importance of the consequences tied to truth affects our standards for attributing knowledge. This suggests that knowledge standards may vary across contexts.**
