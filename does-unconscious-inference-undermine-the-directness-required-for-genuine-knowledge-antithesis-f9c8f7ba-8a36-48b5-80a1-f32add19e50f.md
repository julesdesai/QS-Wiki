# Does unconscious inference undermine the directness required for genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f9c8f7ba-8a36-48b5-80a1-f32add19e50f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Unconscious Processes](phenomenalism-with-unconscious-processes-synthesis-53c9374d-552a-49b3-b121-1513a04b6dab.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's understanding is primarily shaped by unconscious inference, then intuitively, either this does not constitute direct knowledge or the inference aspect dominates and undermines directness.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge as direct awareness should minimize mediation by unconscious processes to preserve the immediacy of sensory experiences.**
