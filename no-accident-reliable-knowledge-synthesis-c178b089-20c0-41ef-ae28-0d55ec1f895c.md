## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the belief is formed through a reliable process and is not accidentally true**, **A belief formed through a reliable process is one that is commonly true in similar situations**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe the proposition and it is true**, **Justification arises from the absence of accidental truths**
[END]

These are some of the views that preserve the essence of the Reliable Process Theory while addressing the objection regarding justification and the possibility of accidental true beliefs.
