# Is the notion of self-justifying basic beliefs coherent on its own terms?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae4ae205-1733-4070-b0e0-18e6d185f4fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**A belief is considered self-justifying when it needs no external justification or support from other beliefs to be justified.**, **If a belief is self-justifying, it must have intrinsic qualities that make it immune to doubt and self-evident, yet identifying such qualities can be problematic.**, **The concept of self-justification appears to contradict the need for any belief to be justified by other evidence, raising questions about the coherence of the justification process.**
