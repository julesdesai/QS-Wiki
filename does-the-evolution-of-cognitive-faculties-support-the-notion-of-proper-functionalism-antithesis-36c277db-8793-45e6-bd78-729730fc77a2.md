# Does the evolution of cognitive faculties support the notion of proper functionalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36c277db-8793-45e6-bd78-729730fc77a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Assume that human cognitive faculties evolved under conditions that favored survival rather than truth. If faculties function properly, their design may not prioritize true beliefs. Intuitively, we may judge that some survival-conducive false beliefs do not constitute knowledge.**, **The principle that justification is derived from faculties designed to produce true beliefs assumes evolution directly selected for truth, which is not necessarily true.**, **If cognitive evolution favors survival efficiency over truth, then the alignment of proper function with true belief production is unreliable.**
