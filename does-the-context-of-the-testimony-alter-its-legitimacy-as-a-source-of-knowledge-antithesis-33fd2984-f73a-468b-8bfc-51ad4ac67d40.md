# Does the context of the testimony alter its legitimacy as a source of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 33fd2984-f73a-468b-8bfc-51ad4ac67d40
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Justification Theory](testimonial-justification-theory-synthesis-a0572f9e-ea0a-45f6-9bf1-a16e71038692.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person A provides testimony during a casual conversation and another case where similar testimony is given under oath in a court of law. Intuitively, we either have to say that the context enhances the justification or that it does not. If we say context enhances justification, then casual testimonial knowledge is less reliable. If we say it does not affect, then context makes no difference to testimonial knowledge's validity.**, **It is implausible that all testimony holds equal weight regardless of contextual factors impacting its credibility.**
