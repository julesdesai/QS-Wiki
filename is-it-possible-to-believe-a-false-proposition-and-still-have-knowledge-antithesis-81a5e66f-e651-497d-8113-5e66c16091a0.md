# Is it possible to believe a false proposition and still have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 81a5e66f-e651-497d-8113-5e66c16091a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person, Zoe, believes a proposition that is false in all relevant cases, yet she arrives at a true belief through a fortunate happenstance. Intuitively, this does not seem to constitute knowledge.**, **Knowledge seems to require that the belief aligns with truth consistently, not just in isolated instances. If Zoe believes a false proposition but accidentally hits truth, it does not qualify as knowledge.**
