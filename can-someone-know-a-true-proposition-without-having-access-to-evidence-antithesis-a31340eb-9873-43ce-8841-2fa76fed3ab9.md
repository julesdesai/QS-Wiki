# Can someone know a true proposition without having access to evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a31340eb-9873-43ce-8841-2fa76fed3ab9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-10c49977-d180-4d95-bcbb-8124324d7ccb.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person has a clairvoyant ability to know true propositions about the future without any empirical evidence. Intuitively, we might judge that this person knows these true propositions despite lacking evidence accessible in the usual sense.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can sometimes stem from sources other than empirical evidence, such as intuition or direct insight. This challenges the assertion that evidence is necessary for knowledge.**
