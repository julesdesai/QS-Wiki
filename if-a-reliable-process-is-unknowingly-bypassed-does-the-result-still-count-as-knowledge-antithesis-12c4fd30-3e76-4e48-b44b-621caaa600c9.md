# If a reliable process is unknowingly bypassed, does the result still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 12c4fd30-3e76-4e48-b44b-621caaa600c9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-9b4935ff-e1a0-4d23-aeca-7dfa716ad7fb.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where an individual forms a belief in alignment with true scientific consensus without knowing it, due to being misled by false reasoning. Intuitively, such belief doesn't qualify as knowledge despite truth and consensus.**, **Without awareness of the originating process's reliability, an accidental bypassing of genuine reliability fails to delineate true knowledge.**
