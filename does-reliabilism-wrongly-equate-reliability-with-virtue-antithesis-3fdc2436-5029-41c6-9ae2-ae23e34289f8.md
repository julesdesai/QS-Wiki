# Does reliabilism wrongly equate reliability with virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3fdc2436-5029-41c6-9ae2-ae23e34289f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cfcf3dee-48b2-4ff8-855e-aba5061f4a51.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that a character trait can be reliable in producing true beliefs without being a virtue.**, **Consider the case of a person who has a cognitive process that reliably leads to true beliefs due to an unusual quirk in their neurological wiring. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to label this quirk as a virtue.**, **There is a plausible principle that an intellectual virtue should involve a degree of conscious reflection or deliberation. This principle together with the claim that a mere reliable quirk lacks reflection entails that not all reliable traits are virtues.**
