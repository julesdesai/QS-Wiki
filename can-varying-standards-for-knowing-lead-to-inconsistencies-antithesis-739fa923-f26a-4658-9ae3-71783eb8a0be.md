# Can varying standards for knowing lead to inconsistencies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 739fa923-f26a-4658-9ae3-71783eb8a0be
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person claims to know a fact while in a relaxed context with low standards. In another context, the same person claims not to know the same fact because of higher standards. Intuitively, it seems inconsistent for the person to both know and not know the same fact.**, **It is implausible that standards for knowledge can vary enough to allow for one person to both know and not know the same proposition in different contexts without a contradiction.**
