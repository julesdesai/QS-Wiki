# Do Gettier cases pose a problem for reliable cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c6867e5-8162-40ba-aea4-50e40c583b4c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**A Gettier case is a situation where someone has a justified true belief, but intuitively lacks knowledge because it occurs due to luck.**, **Consider a person who believes it is noon because their reliable watch says so; unknown to them, the watch stopped one hour ago. Intuitively, they lack knowledge despite the reliability of the cognitive process involved.**, **A cognitive process is supposed to be reliable and generate knowledge by consistently leading to truth, but in Gettier cases, knowledge seems absent despite meeting these criteria, suggesting a flaw.**
