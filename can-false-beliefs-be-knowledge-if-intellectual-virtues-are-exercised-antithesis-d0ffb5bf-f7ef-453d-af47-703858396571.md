# Can false beliefs be knowledge if intellectual virtues are exercised?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0ffb5bf-f7ef-453d-af47-703858396571
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue epistemology view on knowledge](virtue-epistemology-view-on-knowledge-synthesis-e49b38ea-0e37-4416-ac67-ff35516a157e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person exercises their intellectual virtues but arrives at a false belief. Intuitively, such a scenario cannot be knowledge, suggesting truth is also required.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be attributed to cases involving false beliefs, even if intellectual virtues are exercised.**
