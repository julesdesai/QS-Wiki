# Does mediated perception truly capture independent reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5c3e529f-3961-4e14-8e60-691883807b99
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-3b2a41b4-2333-4928-b6a1-340c94708483.md)

## Content

**A scenario where two individuals perceive the same object differently, like a dress appearing differently colored to different observers, challenges the notion that mediated perception unerringly captures the independent reality of the object**, **It is plausible that if perception is mediated, then it includes elements subject to variability, interpretation, and error, which undermines a direct correspondence to reality**, **It is implausible for mediated perception, which is susceptible to individual interpretation, to provide a uniform and complete account of reality's independently existing aspects**
