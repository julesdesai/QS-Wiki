# Does Reliabilism adequately address the issue of cognitive biases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6901d2de-02d6-44ce-93c1-9aaea950bf3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-24b421a0-08f3-4c66-b0fb-2acc7f5da514.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person consistently forms beliefs using a cognitive process affected by biases that typically lead to true beliefs but are still influenced by irrelevant factors. Intuitively, we might doubt whether these count as knowledge because they result from biased processes even if they are reliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that not just any reliable process should result in knowledge, especially if it involves biases that compromise epistemic integrity. This principle highlights that reliability alone may not account for all dimensions of epistemic evaluation.**
