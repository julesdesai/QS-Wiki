# Knowledge as Contextual Justification

**Parent:** [Does indirect evidence undermine the claim of having knowledge?](does-indirect-evidence-undermine-the-claim-of-having-knowledge-antithesis-26ad1a46-9cfd-4066-be17-c85e8ef5f839.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, justification must be robust against changes in context that might introduce indirect evidence**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have contextually robust justification for believing the proposition, and the proposition is true**
