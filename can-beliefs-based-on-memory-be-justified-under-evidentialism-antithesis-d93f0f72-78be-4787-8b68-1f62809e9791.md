# Can beliefs based on memory be justified under evidentialism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d93f0f72-78be-4787-8b68-1f62809e9791
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View](evidentialist-view-synthesis-6f63629b-c949-4605-9acc-d91a2708886a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual recalls a childhood event without present evidence beyond their memory. Intuitively, we might say that the individual has justified belief about the event.**, **There is an empirical fact that memory often serves as a basis for beliefs, and these beliefs are typically regarded as justified in everyday reasoning. This empirical observation suggests that sufficient evidence defined by current supporting evidence may exclude justifiably held beliefs based on memory.**
