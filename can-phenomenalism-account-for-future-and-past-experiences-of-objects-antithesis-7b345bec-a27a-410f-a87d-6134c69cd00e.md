# Can phenomenalism account for future and past experiences of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7b345bec-a27a-410f-a87d-6134c69cd00e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-11423d6b-b29a-46b2-967d-bb4b03435a75.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that experiences of objects over time lead to the knowledge of an object's persistence in objective reality. This principle together with the idea that systematic experiences construct reality entails that past or future experiences do not contribute to knowledge of persistent objects.**, **It is implausible that knowledge of objects can be based solely on current sensory experiences without reference to how these experiences fit into a temporal pattern of past, present, and future perceptions.**
