# Can unacknowledged external factors affect whether a belief constitutes knowledge?

## Content

**Consider a botanist who identifies plant species correctly in a dark forest but uses defective night-vision equipment that erroneously interprets light patterns. Despite correct belief, reliance on poor technology suggests intuition against it being classified as knowledge.**, **In situations where legitimate knowledge rests on unacknowledged external aids, questions arise on the adequacy of knowing without recognizing these influences.**
