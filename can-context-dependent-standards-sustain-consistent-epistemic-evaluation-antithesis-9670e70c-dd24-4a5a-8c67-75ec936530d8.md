# Can context-dependent standards sustain consistent epistemic evaluation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9670e70c-dd24-4a5a-8c67-75ec936530d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a scientist's work is evaluated within different contexts, such as academic, industrial, or governmental settings. Intuitively, we either have to say that the context dictates the standards, allowing variability, or that consistent standards should apply regardless of context. If we say the context dictates standards, then scientific claims could fluctuate in validation. If we say consistent standards apply, then context-sensitivity appears undermined.**
