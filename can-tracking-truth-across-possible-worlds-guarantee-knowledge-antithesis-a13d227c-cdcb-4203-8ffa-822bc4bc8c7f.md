# Can tracking truth across possible worlds guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a13d227c-cdcb-4203-8ffa-822bc4bc8c7f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozickian Tracking Theory Summary](nozickian-tracking-theory-summary-synthesis-44760ad8-dc58-4f04-9662-a1508a0ab6d4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition, and their belief tracks the truth across many possible worlds but not in the actual world. Intuitively, we would not say that they know the proposition in the actual world.**, **Consider a scenario where a belief tracks the truth across some possible worlds but is based on faulty reasoning or unreliable methods. Intuitively, in such a case, this tracking is insufficient for knowledge.**
