# Can a person know a false proposition according to truth-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b09bf0bb-8925-4be3-81a0-2adac59f8594
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-44ea7417-270f-4c93-876d-f98b6754dd68.md)

## Content

**Knowledge cannot involve false propositions because knowledge requires the truth of the proposition known.**, **A person can believe a proposition even if the proposition is false, such as believing in the existence of unicorns.**, **If knowledge requires belief tracking the truth across possible scenarios, then in a scenario where the proposition is false, belief should not occur.**, **A scenario where a person believes a false proposition poses a challenge to the idea that believing a proposition is sufficient for knowing it.**
