# Does mediation through technology inherently affect the validity of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2aba42ba-3fce-4f3d-ac2c-d45e55c2ed3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Acknowledgment of Mediation](indirect-realism-with-acknowledgment-of-mediation-synthesis-e56d95ce-eeee-446c-93a6-a21b3a4bd4d7.md)

## Content

**Suppose an instrument like a funhouse mirror alters the shape of objects viewed. Intuitively, this perception would not yield true knowledge of the object's shape.**, **It is implausible that perceived validity requires only instrument reliability, as changes in perception could render it invalid.**, **Reliable mediation might not always ensure objective correspondence if the instrument consistently deviates from reality in specific respects.**
