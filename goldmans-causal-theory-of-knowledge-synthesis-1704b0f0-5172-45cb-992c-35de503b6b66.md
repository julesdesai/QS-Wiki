## Content

**A person knows a proposition if there is an appropriate causal link between the fact that makes the proposition true and the person's belief**, **For a proposition to be known, it is necessary that the fact that makes the proposition true is the cause of the belief in that proposition**, **Causation between the belief and the fact is both necessary and sufficient for knowledge**
