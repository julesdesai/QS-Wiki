# Do cases of lucky true beliefs pose a challenge to requiring knowledge to result from intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 61b5daba-f11a-452c-b925-df25f16df631
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person arrives at a true belief through luck or coincidence, such as guessing correctly without evidence. Intuitively, we would not say that the belief arose from intellectual virtue. However, the belief is true, which challenges the idea that mere possession of true belief equates to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should eliminate the element of luck, and this suggests that requiring intellectual virtue is necessary to distinguish knowledge from lucky true beliefs.**
