# Is reliability immune to changes over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86b7310c-0ec4-4938-b11a-08cbd3e2c0c9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-383ba069-e0f1-4291-bdc7-eacefc701040.md)

## Content

**Suppose a once-reliable method of diagnosis becomes outdated due to new scientific discoveries, yet previously yielded true beliefs. Intuitively, if reliability is bound by time, then knowledge claims might shift with new developments.**, **Reliability tied to temporality challenges the notion that a reliable formation process ensures timeless knowledge attribution, making knowledge temporally contingent.**
