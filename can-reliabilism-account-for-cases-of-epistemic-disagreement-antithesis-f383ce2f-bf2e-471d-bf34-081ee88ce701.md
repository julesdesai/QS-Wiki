# Can reliabilism account for cases of epistemic disagreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f383ce2f-bf2e-471d-bf34-081ee88ce701
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**Consider two experts disagreeing on a scientific topic, both using processes they each believe to be reliable**, **Intuitively, both can have justified beliefs despite one necessarily being wrong, given they both rely on epistemic standards accepted in their respective fields**, **Reliabilism has difficulty explaining how contradictory beliefs can both be justified if one process must be unreliable if it leads to false beliefs**
