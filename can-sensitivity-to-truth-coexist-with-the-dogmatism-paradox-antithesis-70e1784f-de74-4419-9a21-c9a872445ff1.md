# Can sensitivity to truth coexist with the dogmatism paradox?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 70e1784f-de74-4419-9a21-c9a872445ff1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario where a person dogmatically believes their friend is honest despite contrary evidence.**, **It is implausible that sensitivity to truth can resolve the issue that even if the friend is honest, the belief seems less like knowledge due to resistance to evidence.**, **A plausible principle states that knowledge should not be impervious to significant contrary evidence, which challenges sensitivity's role when dogmatism is present.**
