# Is knowledge truly independent of reliability of the belief-forming process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e92cf282-71a9-4ebb-8d17-3449f15b1437
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Knowledge Summary](invariantist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-7e971fef-e35c-4ec9-93e0-84ae5864f440.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's justified true belief arises from an unreliable belief-forming process, like a random guess that happens to be correct. Intuitively, we hesitate to classify this as true knowledge.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge should depend on reliable cognitive processes, challenging the fixed standards view of knowledge.**
