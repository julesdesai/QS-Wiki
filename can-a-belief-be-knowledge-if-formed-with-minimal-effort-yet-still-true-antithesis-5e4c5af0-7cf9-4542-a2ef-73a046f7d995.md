# Can a belief be knowledge if formed with minimal effort yet still true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e4c5af0-7cf9-4542-a2ef-73a046f7d995
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-193f50ad-c71a-4e26-bf9b-21d5bbf78043.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone easily forms a true belief by following a simple heuristic or rule of thumb rather than applying complex thought. Intuitively, such a belief might be considered knowledge if reliably true, despite lacking substantial cognitive endeavor.**, **The ease of belief formation in accurate cases may imply that exhaustive credit in cognitive effort isn't a necessary condition for knowledge.**
