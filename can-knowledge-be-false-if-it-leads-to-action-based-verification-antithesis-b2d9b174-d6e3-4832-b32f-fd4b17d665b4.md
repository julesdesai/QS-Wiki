# Can knowledge be false if it leads to action-based verification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b2d9b174-d6e3-4832-b32f-fd4b17d665b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**A person believes they can fly and jumps off a table in a situation where, due to external wires, they do not fall. Their belief leading to an action that supports their belief is not knowledge since flying unaided remains false.**, **If a belief leads to action that appears to verify it, but the belief itself is actually false, a person does not truly have knowledge.**
