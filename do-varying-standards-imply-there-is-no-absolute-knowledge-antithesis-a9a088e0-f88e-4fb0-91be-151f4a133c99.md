# Do varying standards imply there is no absolute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9a088e0-f88e-4fb0-91be-151f4a133c99
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Contextualism: Varying Standards Across Contexts](epistemic-contextualism-varying-standards-across-contexts-synthesis-e2822998-5307-4123-86a7-bb234ea90892.md)

## Content

**Standards of justification depend on context and purpose.**, **If justification standards can vary, this implies knowledge might not be absolute but contingent on the context.**, **The concept of absolute knowledge is incompatible with non-absolute justification standards.**
