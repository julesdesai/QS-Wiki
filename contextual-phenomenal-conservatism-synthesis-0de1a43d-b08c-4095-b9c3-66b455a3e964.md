## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have an appearance or seeming in favor of the proposition**, **for a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **for a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the appearance is not defeated by contrary evidence**, **for a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the standards for knowledge in the person's context recognize the appearance as reliable**, **for a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they have an appearance in favor, the proposition is true, there is no contrary evidence, and the contextual standards recognize the appearance as reliable**
