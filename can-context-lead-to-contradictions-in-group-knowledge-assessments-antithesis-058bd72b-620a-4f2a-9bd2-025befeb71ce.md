# Can context lead to contradictions in group knowledge assessments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 058bd72b-620a-4f2a-9bd2-025befeb71ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a team assesses their collective knowledge of a project, but individual contexts lead to different standards for justification. Intuitively, it seems contradictory to have a group assessment potentially resulting in conflicting knowledge claims.**, **Suppose a work setting where team members must know a procedure to pass a safety audit. If contexts vary among team members, it might result in contradicting claims about what is known collectively, complicating team responsibility.**
