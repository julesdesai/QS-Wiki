# Does reliable belief alone suffice for knowledge without understanding the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56cb9bb8-f5a5-4e44-98ba-3b8131fffdb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Externalism Summary](moderate-externalism-summary-synthesis-b4668bae-162e-4191-89bc-89b702818630.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves understanding why a proposition is true. This principle together with the statement that a person knows a proposition if they reliably believe it challenges the sufficiency of reliable belief alone for knowledge.**
