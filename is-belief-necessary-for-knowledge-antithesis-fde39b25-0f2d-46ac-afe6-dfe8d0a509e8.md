# Is belief necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fde39b25-0f2d-46ac-afe6-dfe8d0a509e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-10c49977-d180-4d95-bcbb-8124324d7ccb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, due to some glitch in their brain, never actually forms a belief about a certain true proposition but consistently acts as if the proposition is true and correct. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person may be said to have knowledge of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that if one can consistently and successfully guide their actions based on a proposition as if it were true, this could be a form of knowledge without belief. This principle suggests that belief might not be necessary for knowledge.**
