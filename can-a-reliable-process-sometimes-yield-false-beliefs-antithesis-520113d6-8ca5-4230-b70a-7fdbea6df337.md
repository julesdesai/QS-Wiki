# Can a reliable process sometimes yield false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 520113d6-8ca5-4230-b70a-7fdbea6df337
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a reliable weather forecasting model predicts rain based on all available data, but an unexpected atmospheric change results in sunshine. In such a scenario, intuitively, the belief based on this process is false despite the process being reliable.**, **There are instances where reliable cognitive processes can yield beliefs that turn out to be false because of unforeseen variables.**
