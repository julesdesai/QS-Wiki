# Can knowledge exist without true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 192706e4-aee4-4857-9f2d-6620db7db22f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person holds a false belief but acts successfully as if the belief were true, such as thinking they are luckier than they are and winning at a game. Intuitively, we might judge they have knowledge about how to win despite the belief being false.**, **Cases where individuals succeed based on false beliefs raise doubts about whether every element of knowledge must be true.**
