# Can one have justified true belief without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 932657b1-b9b3-4f80-a106-712df65d749c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person A forms a belief that it is noon because they heard a random guess from someone on the street. A's belief is true, and they feel sure of it, yet intuitively it seems A does not know it is noon.**, **Suppose person B has a justified true belief that it will rain tomorrow because they are an expert meteorologist considering weather patterns. B is more intellectually virtuous than A, yet both have justified true belief. The intuition is that not both have knowledge.**
