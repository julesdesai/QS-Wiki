# Does social collaboration challenge the necessity of individual intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f11117a4-accf-4b53-824f-0ce49bfa9e32
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be distributed across social networks, with individuals contributing without complete grasp or virtue. This principle, alongside intellectual individual virtue requirements, creates tension in collective knowledge acknowledgments.**, **Consider a scenario where a scientific team achieves a true belief through collaborative work, but no individual member alone possesses all virtues necessary. Intuitively, we might still claim that the team collectively knows the truth.**
