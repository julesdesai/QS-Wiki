# Are all coherent systems of belief equally valid?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7fa37eb1-a3c3-4f81-9113-a9ee954c5245
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism](coherentism-synthesis-47013a86-4e6f-4466-86d9-11bbc22ccdaf.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals have entirely different but internally coherent systems of beliefs about a subject, such as the earth being flat versus round. Intuitively, both cannot be equally valid as knowledge claims, despite their coherence.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence alone cannot determine the validity or truth of a belief system. This principle together with differing coherent belief systems suggests problems for coherentism as the sole account of knowledge.**
