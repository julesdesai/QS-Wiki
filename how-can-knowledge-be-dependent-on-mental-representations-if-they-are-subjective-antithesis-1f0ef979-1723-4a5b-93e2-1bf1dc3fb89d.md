# How can knowledge be dependent on mental representations if they are subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f0ef979-1723-4a5b-93e2-1bf1dc3fb89d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Representationalism](epistemic-representationalism-synthesis-c86e8f4e-5440-4a21-8e04-4337bf022fd8.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that mental representations, being subjective, can universally refer to an objective reality.**, **Consider a case where two people observe the same event but their mental representations differ due to personal biases. Intuitively, we must either say that one has knowledge and the other does not, or accept that both can have knowledge despite differing representations.**
