## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **A belief constitutes knowledge if, in relevant counterfactual situations, the belief would still be true**, **Counterfactual scenarios must not involve accidental factors in the causal chain**
