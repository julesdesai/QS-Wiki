# How can coherentism account for the initial acquisition of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** af6c6e92-78ba-4a5e-b3a7-212ccd3782ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who is learning about the world for the first time and forms their initial beliefs. Intuitively, these initial beliefs cannot be said to arise from coherence with other beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that for beliefs to be coherent, they must already exist in some form, but it appears impossible for the first beliefs to achieve this status.**, **The absence of foundational beliefs suggests a paradox in the coherence theory where the initial beliefs lack justification.**
