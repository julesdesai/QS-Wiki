# Do normal conditions always preclude misleading perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1637bb39-cadc-4832-abfd-c88ca1eadf64
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Adjustment to Conditions](reliabilism-with-adjustment-to-conditions-synthesis-3bb29a6f-93c9-4729-aeba-712624404c1b.md)

## Content

**A person may believe they see their colleague waving from a distance during normal daylight when it is actually a tree branch moving in the wind, leading to a misleading perception.**, **Normal conditions like daylight and clear skies often accompany reliable beliefs, yet these can still give way to visual illusions.**, **The insistence on normal conditions aligns poorly with the possibility that our senses might be misled even when events seem ordinary and reliable perceptions are assumed.**
