# Do perceptions and cognitive frameworks truly shape all knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e984b50-645e-49f0-80ba-c0c8eb6dd02b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism Summary](transcendental-idealism-summary-synthesis-0df97e88-4364-4bc5-aaf8-288ab627f355.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is unable to perceive a phenomenon and has no pre-existing cognitive framework for it; intuitively, they might still come to know about it through other means such as testimony or inference**, **It is plausible to claim that knowledge of abstract entities like numbers and mathematical truths does not depend on perception or pre-existing cognitive frameworks**, **Some empirical facts make it implausible that all knowledge requires both sensory experience and cognitive structures, as people often learn through deduction from axioms not directly linked to perception**
