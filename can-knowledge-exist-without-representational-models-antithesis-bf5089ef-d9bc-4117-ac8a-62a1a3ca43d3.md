# Can knowledge exist without representational models?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf5089ef-d9bc-4117-ac8a-62a1a3ca43d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**There is the plausible thought that some knowledge, such as basic perceptual knowledge, does not require a representational model. This thought suggests that not all knowledge relies on complex cognitive structures.**, **Suppose we have a scenario where someone identifies a red apple by sight alone without necessarily engaging in representational modeling. Intuitively, we would say that the person knows the apple's color.**
