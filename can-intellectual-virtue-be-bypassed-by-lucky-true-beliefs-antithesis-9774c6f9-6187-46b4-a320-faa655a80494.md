# Can intellectual virtue be bypassed by lucky true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9774c6f9-6187-46b4-a320-faa655a80494
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief that there is water in a cup simply by guessing, and fortuitously, there is indeed water in the cup. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know there is water in the cup despite the belief being true.**, **It is plausible to say that a belief resulting from mere luck, even if true, does not arise from the exercise of intellectual virtue. Therefore, knowledge cannot merely be a true belief resulting from intellectual virtue if it can also arise from mere luck.**
