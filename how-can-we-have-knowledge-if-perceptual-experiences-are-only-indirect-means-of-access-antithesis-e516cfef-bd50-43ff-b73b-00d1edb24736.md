# How can we have knowledge if perceptual experiences are only indirect means of access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e516cfef-bd50-43ff-b73b-00d1edb24736
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-a95d91b3-a4d2-4740-8b59-c24b26663299.md)

## Content

**If access to external objects is indirect, then the link between perceptual experiences and knowledge is not clear.**, **If the link between perceptual experiences and knowledge is not clear, we lack a reliable method to obtain knowledge about external objects.**, **Without a reliable method to obtain knowledge about external objects, we cannot confirm their existence through perception.**
