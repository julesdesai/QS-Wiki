# How can knowledge have infinite justifications yet be finitely satisfied?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 78eab35b-8f6f-4c87-9b1a-9c7673ad58b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Finite Justifications](infinitism-with-finite-justifications-synthesis-69ad7e03-7f20-4a4f-85ef-ebc999d85fe8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a belief that requires infinite justifications to be fully justified in a theoretical sense. Intuitively, it seems implausible for someone to be epistemically justified if they cannot ever complete their justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief is justified only if all conditions for justification can, in principle, be met. This principle, combined with the requirement for infinite justifications, seems to imply that a belief could never be fully justified.**
