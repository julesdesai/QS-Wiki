# Does virtue epistemology adequately account for memory's role in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fed2255-14ab-403d-9e42-8ce74090290d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**Imagine an individual recalling a complex proof learned years ago; intuitively, this counts as knowledge, though current intellectual virtues may play no active role.**, **A belief retained and applied from memory can involve minimal active intellectual virtue, suggesting inconsistencies with defining knowledge solely by intellectual traits.**, **Cognitive abilities for truth credit can seem less relevant when a retained belief bridges past acquisition through intellectual virtue, complicating the view's stance on knowledge.**
