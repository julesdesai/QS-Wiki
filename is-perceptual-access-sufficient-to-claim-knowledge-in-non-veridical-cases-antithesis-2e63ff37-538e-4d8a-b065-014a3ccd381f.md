# Is perceptual access sufficient to claim knowledge in non-veridical cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e63ff37-538e-4d8a-b065-014a3ccd381f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-8f6e7bad-fa5f-47b7-8d7c-d5f7ea4e2a2a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a vivid memory of eating an imaginary fruit they’ve tasted only in dreams. Intuitively, claiming knowledge about the taste of this fruit based on such a memory feels unfounded, as non-veridical perceptual access cannot ensure it aligns with reality.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge about an entity requires a direct causal link to it, aligning taste experiences only to internally perceptible imagination cannot fulfill this demand.**
