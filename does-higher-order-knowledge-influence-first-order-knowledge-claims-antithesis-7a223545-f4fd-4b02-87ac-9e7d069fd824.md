# Does higher-order knowledge influence first-order knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a223545-f4fd-4b02-87ac-9e7d069fd824
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick’s Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-5a62ad60-20de-4329-b52b-21f59b8da1c1.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual knows he has a certain belief and tracks the truth of this belief effectively. Yet, unexpectedly, they fail to recognize second-order doubts about the reliability of their belief formation process. Intuitively, this raises concerns about whether they have first-order knowledge when the tracking mechanism itself may lack scrutiny that could affect the presumed knowledge.**
