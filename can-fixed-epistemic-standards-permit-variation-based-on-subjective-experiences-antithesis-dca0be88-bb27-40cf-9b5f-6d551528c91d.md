# Can fixed epistemic standards permit variation based on subjective experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dca0be88-bb27-40cf-9b5f-6d551528c91d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-fa9170ce-e58d-4311-9c0f-f4410b4d69a4.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a case where Karen feels confident about her math skills, but Leo is insecure despite identical training. Intuitively, this suggests Karen might know her abilities while Leo might not, implying variation despite fixed standards.**, **A principle asserting that fixed standards imply consistent criteria cannot reconcile variability entailed by subjective influences.**
