# Can contextualism account for universal claims of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 961ee160-28c8-44b7-b6db-dd01de96c814
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a case where an individual claims to know a universal truth, such as a law of nature, across all contexts. Intuitively, this appears inconsistent with context-dependent knowledge, which suggests variability based on context.**, **A principle of universality in epistemology implies that certain truths are immune to contextual variation. If contextual standards allow for mutable knowledge claims, they seem inadequate for universal knowledge.**
