# Is contextual support sufficient for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42134530-346c-4337-8f63-c50555c4b83c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification requires more than just fitting into contextual support; it necessitates aligning with broader epistemic criteria such as consistency, coherence, and truth-conduciveness.**, **Contextual support alone may not ensure that a belief is justified if it fails to meet these broader criteria.**, **Without such alignment, contextually justified beliefs risk being only superficially justified, lacking true epistemic merit.**
