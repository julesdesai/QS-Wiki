# Is practical success sufficient for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 70485746-8bae-4a9d-a9c2-81bc1e7a27ea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person successfully navigates a difficult terrain by believing a unreliable map was accurate. Intuitively, we must say that despite the practical success, this does not constitute justified belief because the map was flawed.**, **If we say that practical success with flawed evidence is enough for justification, then we must accept that unreliable beliefs can count as knowledge, which seems implausible.**
