# How does context handle conflicting justified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5fc55ead-5198-4fa6-93c8-0c308cc04bb0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-4f1ad7a2-76eb-4161-89f7-f4b83097a73b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people, A and B, witness an event but are in different cultural contexts that lead to opposite justified beliefs about the event. A believes the act was moral, while B believes it was immoral. Both are justified in their context.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be contradictory between equally justified beliefs, because contradictory knowledge cannot both be true.**, **In the case of the conflicting beliefs, intuitively, we cannot both claim A and B know the truth about the event, highlighting the issue for context-dependence in managing conflicting yet justified beliefs.**
[END]

These objections identify significant challenges to the theoretical view, focusing on how context might fail to explain or uphold necessary conditions for knowledge.
