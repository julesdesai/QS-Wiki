# Is indirect knowledge adequate for knowing factual states of affairs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56482baf-7444-4d93-beb0-411d9a1278b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Phenomenalism](moderate-phenomenalism-synthesis-66f89d49-d085-4cff-b6cd-a9e130c91f7b.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that accurate, direct perception is required to fully understand factual states. This principle together with the reliance on indirect knowledge suggests potential misinformation or misunderstanding.**, **Imagine a scenario where a person only learns about global events through second-hand reports without ever observing evidence themselves. Intuitively, we judge that their grasp of these events might lack clarity or depth, challenging the idea that indirect knowledge suffices.**
