# Does cognitive success always result from intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3144e79-57d1-44ca-94bb-4f0ac20b4e34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-dbddd6f3-a688-44af-bb01-dd29b6f7b5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief about a complex mathematical theorem through an accidental miscalculation. Intuitively, we would judge that this is not a case of knowledge, as the outcome lacks the exercise of cognitive skill.**, **It is plausible that intellectual virtue requires not just a correct outcome but a correct method. A correct method is absent in an accidental success, indicating the absence of intellectual virtue.**
