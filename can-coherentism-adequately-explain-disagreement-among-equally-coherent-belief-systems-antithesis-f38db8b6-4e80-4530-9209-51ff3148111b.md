# Can coherentism adequately explain disagreement among equally coherent belief systems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f38db8b6-4e80-4530-9209-51ff3148111b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Imagine two people with completely coherent and internally consistent but mutually contradictory belief systems. Intuitively, both cannot be said to possess true knowledge if their beliefs are contradictory.**, **It is plausible that coherentism fails to provide a mechanism for resolving conflicts between coherent belief systems that are equally internally justified.**, **If two belief systems are equally coherent yet contradictory, coherence alone seems insufficient for knowledge.**
