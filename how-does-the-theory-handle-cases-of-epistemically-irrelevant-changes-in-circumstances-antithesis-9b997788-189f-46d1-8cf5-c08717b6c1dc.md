# How does the theory handle cases of epistemically irrelevant changes in circumstances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9b997788-189f-46d1-8cf5-c08717b6c1dc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-dfd5c5a1-144e-4c90-a9f0-b64275b800c5.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a person R believes a true proposition about a nearby event, such as "The concert starts at 8 PM," based on reliable testimony. In a nearby possible world, the concert is inexplicably canceled minutes before it starts. R's belief does not track this unforeseen change despite being admirable and sound, which seems intuitively problematic for considering the original belief as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be resilient to minor epistemically irrelevant changes in circumstances, such as sudden events unconnected to the basis of the belief. This suggests that truth-tracking alone may unduly restrict what counts as knowledge by too closely tying it to circumstance changes unrelated to epistemic justification.**, **It is implausible to deny that such cases illustrate genuine knowledge, as making beliefs contingent on unrelated events undermines stable knowledge characteristics.**
