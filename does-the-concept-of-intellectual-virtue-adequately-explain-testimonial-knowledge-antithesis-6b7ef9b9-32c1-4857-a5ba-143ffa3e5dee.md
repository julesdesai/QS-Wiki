# Does the concept of intellectual virtue adequately explain testimonial knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b7ef9b9-32c1-4857-a5ba-143ffa3e5dee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-c68e0504-3343-450c-a9ae-cebbb34ec2ad.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario wherein a child learns a fact solely based on what a trusted teacher tells them. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the child has knowledge, but this knowledge seems more rooted in social trust rather than solely an exercise of intellectual virtue**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be acquired through testimony. This principle, together with the idea that intellectual virtue requires individual cognitive effort, entails that strictly intellectual virtue might not fully account for knowledge acquired through social interactions**
