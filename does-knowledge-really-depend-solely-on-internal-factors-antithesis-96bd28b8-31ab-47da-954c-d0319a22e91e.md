# Does knowledge really depend solely on internal factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96bd28b8-31ab-47da-954c-d0319a22e91e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes they have enough money in their bank account to pay their rent, based on an internal calculation of recent deposits, but in fact, they have been the victim of bank fraud and their funds were stolen. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know they have enough money to pay rent, although their belief is internally justified.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should account for external facts in addition to internal factors. This principle together with situations of fraud or misinformation entails that internal factors alone are insufficient for knowledge.**
