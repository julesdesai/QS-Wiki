# Can a belief be justified externally in a way that bypasses the believer's agency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab6d0f20-4793-401a-bf66-ca28286d667e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is passively exposed to media propaganda leading them to form a justified true belief. Intuitively, this belief does not represent knowledge because the person did not actively engage with the information.**, **It is implausible that external justification alone can amount to knowledge if it bypasses active processing or agency by the believer.**
