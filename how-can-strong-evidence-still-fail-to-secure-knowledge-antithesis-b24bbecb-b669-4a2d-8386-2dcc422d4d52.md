# How can strong evidence still fail to secure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b24bbecb-b669-4a2d-8386-2dcc422d4d52
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that strong evidence should logically lead to knowledge, assuming the belief is true.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person concludes a historical event based on multiple reliable sources, and this conclusion is true but one of the sources turned out to be fabricated. Intuitively, it seems the person still knows the historical event.**
