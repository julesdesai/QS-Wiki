# Does the flexibility of context make knowledge too fluid?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ca83079-6775-4d8e-ae35-8c2aaae74b11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b7b03c88-6aa4-4b47-9f59-bc5548cc994c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's knowledge claim shifts with changing contexts from one immediate conversation to another. Intuitively, we either have to say knowledge is too fluid to be reliable or that knowledge should withstand contextual shifts without distortion. If judged fluid, it challenges traditional notions of knowledge as stable.**
