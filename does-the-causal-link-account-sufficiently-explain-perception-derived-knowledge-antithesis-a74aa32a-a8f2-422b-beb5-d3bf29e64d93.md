# Does the causal link account sufficiently explain perception-derived knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a74aa32a-a8f2-422b-beb5-d3bf29e64d93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that causation does not necessarily imply accurate representation or knowledge. This principle together with the claim that external objects cause perceptual experiences entails that experiences could misrepresent the external world.**, **Consider a case in which an individual experiences a loud sound due to a hallucination, intuitively, we would judge that this experience, although caused by a complex neural event, does not provide true knowledge about the world.**
