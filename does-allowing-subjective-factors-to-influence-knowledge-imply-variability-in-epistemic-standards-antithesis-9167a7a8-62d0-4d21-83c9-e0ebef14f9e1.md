# Does allowing subjective factors to influence knowledge imply variability in epistemic standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9167a7a8-62d0-4d21-83c9-e0ebef14f9e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-fa9170ce-e58d-4311-9c0f-f4410b4d69a4.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where Ingrid confidently believes a trivial fact while Jack hesitantly believes the same fact due to social pressure. Intuitively, their different subjective experiences imply variability in applying epistemic standards.**, **It is implausible that invariant standards should yield different outcomes based on subjective experiences.**
