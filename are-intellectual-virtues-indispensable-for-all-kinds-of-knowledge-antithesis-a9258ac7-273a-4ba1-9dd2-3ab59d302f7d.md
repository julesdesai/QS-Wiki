# Are intellectual virtues indispensable for all kinds of knowledge?

## Content

**Consider person J who possesses knowledge of basic arithmetic, which they learned through rote memorization, absent any intellectual engagement. Their belief about arithmetic is true by definition. Intuitively, rote learning doesn't rely on intellectual virtues.**, **The reliance on intellectual virtues for knowledge appears questioned when considering that some knowledge, such as basic arithmetic, can be acquired devoid of intellectual exploration or virtue.**
