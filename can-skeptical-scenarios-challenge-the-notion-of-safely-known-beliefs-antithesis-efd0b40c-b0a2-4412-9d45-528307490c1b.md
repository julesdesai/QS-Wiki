# Can skeptical scenarios challenge the notion of safely known beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** efd0b40c-b0a2-4412-9d45-528307490c1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which all beliefs are as if in a deceiver's matrix where sensations are manipulated to seem real without any possibility for the deceived to know this setup. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that beliefs do not constitute knowledge because they are systematically detached from reality.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge must be immune to radical skeptical scenarios that fully separate belief from truth connection. This principle, alongside skeptical considerations, could imply that the Safety Theory's conception of safe belief does not adequately address these extremes.**
