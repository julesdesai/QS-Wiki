# How can we distinguish genuinely basic beliefs from those that merely appear basic?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ecb7778-428b-4889-b9af-4b9712b8a5fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief feels intuitively basic, such as "I see a tree." Intuitively, this belief seems foundational without needing further justification.**, **In reality, the veracity of the belief "I see a tree" often requires additional support, such as confirming the reliability of perception or ruling out illusions, suggesting it might not be genuinely basic.**
