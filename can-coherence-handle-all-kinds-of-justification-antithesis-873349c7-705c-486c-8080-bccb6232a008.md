# Can coherence handle all kinds of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 873349c7-705c-486c-8080-bccb6232a008
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a scientist develops a coherent hypothesis based solely on their existing theoretical framework without any additional empirical data. Intuitively, we either have to say that this hypothesis counts as knowledge or that it does not. If we say it counts as knowledge, then such and such leads to a problematic lack of empirical testing. If we say it does not count as knowledge, then such and such challenges the sufficiency of coherence for knowledge.**
