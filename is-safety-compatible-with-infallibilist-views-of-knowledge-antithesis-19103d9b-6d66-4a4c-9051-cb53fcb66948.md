# Is safety compatible with infallibilist views of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 19103d9b-6d66-4a4c-9051-cb53fcb66948
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-b5a72759-42a3-4d9b-b74c-df40c84bdc9d.md)

## Content

**Infallibilism posits that knowledge must guarantee truth without any possibility of error. The safety condition allows for a measure of probability across possible worlds, which means it does not align with infallibilism.**, **Consider a belief that could easily have been false in only slightly changed circumstances. Intuitively, infallibilism would reject this as knowledge, even as the safety condition might not.**
