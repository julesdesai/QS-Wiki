# Does true belief become knowledge regardless of its origination?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c2875c6-dc27-4376-9c5f-2c6b44ca6f42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person holds a true belief about the world based on flawed reasoning or inadequate evidence. Intuitively, we would reject the notion that this amounts to knowledge as the belief was arrived at through suspect foundations.**, **Knowledge is true belief based on foundational beliefs that are fallible; however, the questionable origin of the belief undermines it being considered knowledge.**
