# Can phenomenalism adequately distinguish between real and virtual environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6596dc51-8a25-4142-b0cf-efd42989d219
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-11423d6b-b29a-46b2-967d-bb4b03435a75.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is in a virtual reality simulation that perfectly mimics sensory experiences of the physical world. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person is not interacting with the objective world, even though their sensory experiences are consistent.**, **It is implausible that consistent sensory experiences alone can distinguish between interactions with real objects and simulated environments.**
