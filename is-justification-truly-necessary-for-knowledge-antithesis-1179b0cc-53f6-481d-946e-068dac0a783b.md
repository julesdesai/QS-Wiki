# Is justification truly necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1179b0cc-53f6-481d-946e-068dac0a783b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**A person can intuitively seem to know a proposition without being able to explain their justification, as in cases of instinctive or intuitive knowing.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be applicable to many everyday instances where justification cannot be readily articulated.**, **This principle together with the idea that justification is necessary for knowledge challenges cases of apparent knowledge without articulation of evidence.**
