# Can personal experiences serve as robust evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff741fb4-180d-4daa-9978-c21549bbd4d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidence-Based Theory](evidence-based-theory-synthesis-3a180c4f-30b0-4e6f-87b5-c20a77cbb8ff.md)

## Content

**A person may have a vivid dream providing them with insights later verified as true. Intuitively, the dream, though not robust evidence, seems to have contributed to genuine knowledge.**, **Personal anecdotes, though lacking scientific rigor, often lead to insights that many would consider valuable knowledge, questioning whether all personal experience can lack sufficient evidential weight.**
