# Can cognitive malfunctions produce knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9618e5ce-3e6d-4023-86dd-8e131b3ecde9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition due to a cognitive malfunction, such as a hallucination that turns out to be accurate. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks knowledge despite the belief being true.**, **Consider a plausible principle that knowledge requires a reliable process of belief formation. If a belief resulting from a malfunction happens to align with the truth, it lacks reliability.**, **Suppose knowledge requires beliefs formed under cognitive faculties functioning properly. If a malfunction can occasionally yield true beliefs, knowledge may exist independently of proper functioning.**
