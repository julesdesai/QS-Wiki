# Is all evidence equally persuasive for all propositions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37843aca-765a-4155-b3ba-ae89c25b021d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where hearsay evidence suggests someone is guilty of a crime. Intuitively, we would judge that hearsay is less persuasive than forensic evidence in establishing guilt.**, **There is a plausible principle that different types of evidence have different persuasive strengths depending on the proposition. This principle with the notion that evidence must persuade to justify belief implies not all evidence equally justifies the same level of belief.**
