# Is the truth of a proposition sufficient for knowledge if divorced from understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0c14b48-49e0-4437-b821-f7f9f7f6a07c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition, but they cannot provide any understanding or reasoning behind it. Intuitively, we would judge that the mere truth of the proposition does not satisfy the conditions for knowledge without accompanying understanding.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be reduced solely to the truth of a belief without any consideration of the person's reasoning or comprehension.**
