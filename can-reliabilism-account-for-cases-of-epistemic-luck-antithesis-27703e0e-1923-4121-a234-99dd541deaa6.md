# Can reliabilism account for cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27703e0e-1923-4121-a234-99dd541deaa6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes that it is noon because their clock reads noon, but the clock stopped exactly 24 hours ago without them knowing. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge that it is noon**, **Although the person's belief aligns with reality (it's noon) and follows from their usual reliance on the clock (a seemingly reliable process), this is an instance where true belief is achieved purely by chance rather than process reliability**
