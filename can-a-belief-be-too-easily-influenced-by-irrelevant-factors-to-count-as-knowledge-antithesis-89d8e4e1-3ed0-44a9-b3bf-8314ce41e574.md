# Can a belief be too easily influenced by irrelevant factors to count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89d8e4e1-3ed0-44a9-b3bf-8314ce41e574
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which a person has a true and safe belief, but this belief is highly sensitive to irrelevant changes such as mood or weather. Intuitively, we would question whether this is knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a belief is influenced by irrelevant factors, then its stability as knowledge is undermined.**
