# Can belief alone constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7bbf2a8-9e6c-4e59-b879-dee5cf12f29f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone sincerely believes a false proposition. Intuitively, we would judge that they do not possess knowledge simply by virtue of belief alone**, **It is implausible that mere belief, even if true, automatically results in knowledge if there is no link to understanding or justification**, **Knowledge typically requires more than just belief; it requires a connection to evidence or reasons that justify the proposition**
