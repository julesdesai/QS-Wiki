# Is belief sufficient for knowledge if it must also be true?

**Node ID:** 397a16b3-2760-43ff-a83d-eb10039e658b

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Pluralism](epistemic-pluralism-synthesis-81e4116f-f2b8-419c-97e5-1ca5c482c2fd.md)

## Content

**A proposition believed to be true does not guarantee it is true or known in any substantive sense without additional conditions.**, **It is plausible that a belief, even when true, does not amount to knowledge if it lacks an epistemic justification component.**, **Epistemic systems often require more than mere belief, such as evidence or reason, to elevate belief to knowledge.**
