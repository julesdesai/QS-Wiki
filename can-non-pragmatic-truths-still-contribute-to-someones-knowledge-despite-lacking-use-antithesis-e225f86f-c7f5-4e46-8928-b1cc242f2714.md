# Can non-pragmatic truths still contribute to someone's knowledge despite lacking use?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e225f86f-c7f5-4e46-8928-b1cc242f2714
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Pragmatism](knowledge-as-pragmatism-thesis-809d9692-c145-4622-889b-65edccbfde29.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone learns a historical fact with no current pragmatic application. Intuitively, we might still judge that the person has acquired knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that the acquisition of true information, regardless of its immediate or practical application, still constitutes knowledge. This principle suggests that knowledge is inherently valuable beyond practical utility.**
