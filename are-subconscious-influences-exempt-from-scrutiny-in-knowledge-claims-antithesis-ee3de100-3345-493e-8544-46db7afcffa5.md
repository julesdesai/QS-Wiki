# Are subconscious influences exempt from scrutiny in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee3de100-3345-493e-8544-46db7afcffa5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-37dfac32-afee-4ea8-9a67-23e7efbc2507.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that subconscious influences in belief formation are inherently more reliable than conscious reasoning.**, **Consider a scenario where a subconscious influence distorts a person's perception of evidence, leading to a belief. Intuitively, the presence of subconscious distortion implies a reliance on potentially false premises, questioning the validity of the belief.**
