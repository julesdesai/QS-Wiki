# Can deceptive perceptions ever truly mirror reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 53381f11-cc09-41fa-b7e9-11a32472a6a1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-8f6e7bad-fa5f-47b7-8d7c-d5f7ea4e2a2a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone hallucinates a pink elephant in the room and there is no pink elephant present. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person’s perceptual experience cannot be about the world since the elephant does not exist.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception directly reflects reality, then hallucinations cannot provide a perceptual experience directly about the world. This principle suggests that hallucinations and perceptions should be fundamentally different, yet they both appear perceptually similar subjectively.**
