# Is invariantism compatible with the intuition of knowledge improvement over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a873935f-3056-4ebd-a220-60b5e083361d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which scientific knowledge clearly improves over time as new discoveries are made. Intuitively, this shows that what is considered known can change.**, **The principle that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant suggests scientific progression should not affect what is considered knowledge.**, **It is implausible that improved evidence and understanding do not refine our knowledge standards.**
