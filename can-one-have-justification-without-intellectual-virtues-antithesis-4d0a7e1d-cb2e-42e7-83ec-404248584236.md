# Can one have justification without intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d0a7e1d-cb2e-42e7-83ec-404248584236
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology: Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-1d322156-3f22-49a0-99a5-f932d24b0e66.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who systematically acquires true beliefs through pure luck, without exercising intellectual virtues like careful reasoning. Intuitively, these beliefs lack proper justification.**, **A belief is not considered justified unless supported by intellectual virtues such as critical thinking and evidence evaluation, indicating that justification cannot solely rely on accidental truth.**
