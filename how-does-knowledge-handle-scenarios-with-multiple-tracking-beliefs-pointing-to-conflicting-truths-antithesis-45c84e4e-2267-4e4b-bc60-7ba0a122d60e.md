# How does knowledge handle scenarios with multiple tracking beliefs pointing to conflicting truths?

## Content

**Consider a case where two people rely on different news sources about an election outcome, both tracking different truth conditions from conflicting reports. Intuitively, such beliefs, while tracking, do not both equally constitute knowledge, challenging the symmetry in tracking theory.**, **Intuitively, when multiple conflicting beliefs track truth from multiple observing perspectives, not all amount to knowledge, raising questions about how the theory assigns knowledge in such scenarios.**
