# Does the idea of epistemic luck challenge Process Reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d494727-12f0-4f3b-b203-d86f1e976d29
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-54c291ff-d1f5-45c3-8197-657cbffa4347.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person guesses the right answer to a quiz question due to a series of unrelated events making their guess fortuitously accurate. Although the belief is true and derived through seemingly coincidental methods, intuitively, this belief feels unjustified due to the lack of systematic reliability.**, **A plausible principle suggests that a justified belief should arise from a dependable process, not sheer luck, challenging cases where Process Reliabilism might ascribe justification to lucky guesses.**
