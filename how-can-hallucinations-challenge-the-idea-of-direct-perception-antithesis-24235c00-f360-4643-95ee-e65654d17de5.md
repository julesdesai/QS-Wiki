# How can hallucinations challenge the idea of direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24235c00-f360-4643-95ee-e65654d17de5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism](naive-realism-synthesis-a575a970-7874-461b-b9b5-9de679546681.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates an apple that is not present. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person seems to have an experience just like perceiving a real apple.**, **If hallucinations can create experiences indistinguishable from perception, then perception cannot solely involve direct acquaintance with external objects.**
