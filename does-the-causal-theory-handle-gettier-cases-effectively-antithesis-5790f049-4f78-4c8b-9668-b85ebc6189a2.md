# Does the causal theory handle Gettier cases effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5790f049-4f78-4c8b-9668-b85ebc6189a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dc61c008-27ed-4921-863e-badc9b961db6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a true belief caused by misleading evidence, such as seeing a mirage and believing there is an oasis, and coincidentally there is an oasis, either the causal theory must claim this is knowledge or find a complex causal story to deny it, which is counter-intuitive.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that such cases count as knowledge, which challenges our understanding of knowledge, or deny it and question the adequacy of causal connections in determining knowledge.**
