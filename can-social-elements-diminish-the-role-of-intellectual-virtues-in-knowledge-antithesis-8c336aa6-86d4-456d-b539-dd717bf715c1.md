# Can social elements diminish the role of intellectual virtues in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c336aa6-86d4-456d-b539-dd717bf715c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person adjusts their belief due to peer pressure, yet the belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we doubt knowledge attribution due to social influence.**, **Social and peer influences can diverge from purely reliable cognitive character traits.**, **Societal elements impacting belief formation are typically seen as non-epistemic, complicating the impact of intellectual virtue on knowledge.**, **When peer pressure, rather than an individual's virtue, leads to a belief, it challenges the sole reliance on intellectual virtue for knowledge.**
