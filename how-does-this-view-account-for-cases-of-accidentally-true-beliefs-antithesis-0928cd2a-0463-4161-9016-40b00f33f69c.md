# How does this view account for cases of accidentally true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0928cd2a-0463-4161-9016-40b00f33f69c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Internalism](hybrid-internalism-synthesis-3d9ce0d9-6ba2-4665-a054-ed06708d6ee0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone believes something that is true due to sheer luck, like guessing the outcome of a coin toss. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the outcome or that they do not know it. If we say they know it, then this seems counterintuitive because their belief was based on luck. If we say they do not know it, then we are acknowledging that truth alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
