# Is contextualism at odds with the universality of epistemic principles?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2325c271-41e4-4ecb-a408-b067851c5bb3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that epistemic principles, like the standards for knowing, should be universally applicable to maintain philosophical consistency. This principle, together with the contextual variability of those standards, entails that contextualism might undermine the universality of epistemic assessment.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be consistently defined if its standards are allowed to dramatically vary with context, challenging the universality of such principles.**
