# How does sensitivity theory handle necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a62fe55-0561-410a-b1f6-b3565f523256
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa7f9900-da54-4309-8d89-06019053aee3.md)

## Content

**A belief in a necessary truth cannot have a nearest possible world where the proposition is false, yet intuitively such beliefs qualify as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that one can have knowledge of necessary truths, independent of the sensitivity of those beliefs.**
