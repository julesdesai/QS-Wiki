# Is cognitive task fulfillment adequate when deception is involved?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba4f08d9-424e-46db-820b-e1a9d37139f3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Functionalism with Cognitive Task Fulfillment](epistemic-functionalism-with-cognitive-task-fulfillment-synthesis-3edd3165-e9fd-4e05-81df-6b63dbbb89eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses excellent reasoning skills to arrive at a conclusion, but the initial premise they were given is a deliberate lie. Intuitively, it seems unlikely they truly have knowledge, despite fulfilling the cognitive task of reasoning.**, **Knowledge requires truth in addition to cognitive task fulfillment, suggesting that deception can undermine genuine knowledge.**
