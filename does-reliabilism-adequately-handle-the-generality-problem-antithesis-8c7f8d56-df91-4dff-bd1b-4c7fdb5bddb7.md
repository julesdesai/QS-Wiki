# Does reliabilism adequately handle the generality problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c7f8d56-df91-4dff-bd1b-4c7fdb5bddb7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**A process can be described in many different levels of generality, leading to different evaluations of its reliability**, **Suppose a process is described as "reading clocks," which could be generally reliable, but another description as "reading stopped clocks," which is generally unreliable**, **Without a precise criterion for determining the correct level of generality, reliabilism struggles to consistently determine whether a belief is justified or a process is truly reliable**
