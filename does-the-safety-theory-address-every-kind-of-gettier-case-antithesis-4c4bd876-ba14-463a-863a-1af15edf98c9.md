# Does the Safety Theory address every kind of Gettier case?

**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Gettier cases are scenarios where someone holds a justified true belief that still fails to be knowledge due to some luck-based element. Imagine a case where a person believes something true due to justification, but it remains no less true that the belief could have easily been false. Intuitively, these are counterexamples to the notion that justified true belief is sufficient for knowledge.**, **Gettier cases suggest that the mere safety of belief from falsity is not enough to rule out the role of epistemic luck completely.**
