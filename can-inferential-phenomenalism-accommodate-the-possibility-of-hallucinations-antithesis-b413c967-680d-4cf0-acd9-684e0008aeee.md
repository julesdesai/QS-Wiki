# Can inferential phenomenalism accommodate the possibility of hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b413c967-680d-4cf0-acd9-684e0008aeee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person hallucinates a pink elephant in the room. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the existence of a pink elephant.**, **It is implausible that hallucinations can form a reliable foundation for inferring truths.**, **Knowledge about external objects based on inferences drawn from hallucinatory experiences is undermined by the unreliability of the perception.**
