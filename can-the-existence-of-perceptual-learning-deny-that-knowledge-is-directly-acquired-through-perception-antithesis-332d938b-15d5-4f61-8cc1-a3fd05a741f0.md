# Can the existence of perceptual learning deny that knowledge is directly acquired through perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 332d938b-15d5-4f61-8cc1-a3fd05a741f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a novice birdwatcher gradually learns to distinguish between similar bird species. Intuitively, we would judge that this learning process is necessary for accurate knowledge.**, **This suggests that perception alone is insufficient for knowledge, as expertise involves non-perceptual cognitive development.**
