# Can knowledge be independent of perceptual experience?

**Node ID:** c9c0ec2a-8db9-4125-b5f7-920fd3a930ce

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Realism](phenomenological-realism-synthesis-f972efdb-cd3c-4df6-b5d4-a15ff27a6c64.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a scientist knows a mathematical truth such as the Pythagorean theorem through symbolic reasoning and proof, without any direct perceptual experience. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the scientist has knowledge.**, **Certain types of knowledge, such as mathematical knowledge, often do not require direct perceptual experience but are derived from logical deduction and reasoning.**
