# Does invariantism apply equally to testimony?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e835ee1-ecfa-4a2c-9c2c-918f19710330
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-2877eecc-8388-4e01-b87d-ffe7f20b2619.md)

## Content

**When considering testimony, the credibility and expertise of the person providing the information often influence claims of knowledge.**, **Intuitively, accepting testimony from a reliable expert seems to confer knowledge more readily than accepting testimony from an unreliable source.**, **Invariantism suggests uniform standards, ignoring the varying credibility of different sources in knowledge claims.**
