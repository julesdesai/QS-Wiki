# How does Constructivism account for objective truths that appear independent of individual perception?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Consider a scenario where multiple independent observers perceive a distant galaxy through telescopes, despite having different prior knowledge and experiences, they all report the same objective astronomical data. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the existence and properties of the galaxy are independent of individual perceptual constructions.**, **There is an empirically observable fact that certain scientific truths, like the chemical composition of water being H2O, remain consistent regardless of individuals' sensory experiences or cognitive processes. This fact suggests that some knowledge might exist independently of constructed perceptual experiences.**
