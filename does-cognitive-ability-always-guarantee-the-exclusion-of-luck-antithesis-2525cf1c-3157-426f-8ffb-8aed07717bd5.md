# Does cognitive ability always guarantee the exclusion of luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2525cf1c-3157-426f-8ffb-8aed07717bd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone with exceptional cognitive abilities correctly solves a mathematical problem, but their solution happens to be coincidentally right despite an initial mistake in reasoning. Intuitively, the belief seems true by luck.**, **This suggests that even with strong cognitive abilities, epistemic luck may not always be excluded, challenging the principle that intellectual virtues eliminate all luck.**
