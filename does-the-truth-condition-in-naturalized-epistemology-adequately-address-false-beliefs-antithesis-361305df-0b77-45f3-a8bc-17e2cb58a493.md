# Does the truth condition in naturalized epistemology adequately address false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 361305df-0b77-45f3-a8bc-17e2cb58a493
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where scientific methods justify a belief that later turns out to be false. Intuitively, such a belief was never knowledge despite its justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves a truth condition that precludes the acceptance of false justified beliefs.**, **A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true fails to consider justified beliefs that turn out to be false.**
