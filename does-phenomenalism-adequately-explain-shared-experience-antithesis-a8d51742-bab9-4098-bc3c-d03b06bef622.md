# Does phenomenalism adequately explain shared experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8d51742-bab9-4098-bc3c-d03b06bef622
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people describe seeing the same object from different angles and agree on its properties. Intuitively, we either have to say that their sensory experiences refer to the same object or that they experience separate objects.**, **It is implausible that two sets of sensory experiences independently construct the same physical object without shared reality.**
