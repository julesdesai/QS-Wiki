# Is memory-influenced perception sufficient for distinguishing between competing empirical claims?

**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Determining the truth of competing empirical claims often requires reliable evidence. This requirement, together with the reliance on memory-influenced perceptions which may differ widely among individuals, indicates potential issues in resolving empirical disputes.**, **Suppose two individuals remember a shared event differently, each influenced by their memories. Intuitively, without independent verification, it is unclear whose knowledge claim is more accurate.**
