# Does Reliabilism handle the distinction between internal and external factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ace9e904-e156-4038-86c6-f827b47f9ac2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-e69ee86a-1c0a-4d0e-840e-f25c67fd8e9a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that distinguishes between external factors that render a process reliable and internal factors of which an agent is aware. For knowledge, agents may need awareness of reliability, beyond mere external reliability.**, **Suppose an agent's cognitive process is externally reliable due to environmental factors unknown to the agent. Intuitively, we may not grant this as knowledge due to the lack of awareness or internal factors contributing to the formation of belief.**
