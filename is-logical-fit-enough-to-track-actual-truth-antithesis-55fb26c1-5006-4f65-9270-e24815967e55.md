# Is logical fit enough to track actual truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55fb26c1-5006-4f65-9270-e24815967e55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a group of conspiracy theorists logically fit new pieces of evidence into their existing belief system without changing any core tenets. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the belief system's coherence does not make it more likely to be true.**, **It is implausible that the logical fit of new evidence without re-evaluation of core beliefs enhances truth probability, as conspiracy theories often maintain coherence at the cost of ignoring contradictory evidence.**
