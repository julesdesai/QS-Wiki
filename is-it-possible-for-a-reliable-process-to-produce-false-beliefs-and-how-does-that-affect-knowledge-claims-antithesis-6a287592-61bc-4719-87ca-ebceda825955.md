# Is it possible for a reliable process to produce false beliefs, and how does that affect knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a287592-61bc-4719-87ca-ebceda825955
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable Process](knowledge-as-reliable-process-synthesis-ff5c39ac-284c-4b30-bc5c-9d0e7edf807e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where a person P relies on a method that is generally reliable, like expert testimony, for forming beliefs about medical conditions. Sometimes experts err, producing instances of reliable yet false belief.**, **A reliable process that systematically leads to false beliefs at times challenges the sufficiency for knowledge. The presence of false output from a reliable process questions its adequacy for ensuring true beliefs.**
