# Can a person know a proposition if it becomes important only after forming the belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b36b3172-3bc2-4f76-8d06-6e5e0930f49d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-1a7f6ba4-b667-492e-9754-ad3a76a8b666.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief about a proposition when the stakes are low, later those stakes increase significantly, but the truth of the proposition and the person's justification remain unchanged. Intuitively, we would say that the prior belief should not now constitute knowledge if sensitive to rising stakes, challenging the consistency of standards.**, **It is implausible that a belief can shift from knowledge to non-knowledge solely because stakes were initially low and later raised, if the proposition truth and justification stay constant.**
