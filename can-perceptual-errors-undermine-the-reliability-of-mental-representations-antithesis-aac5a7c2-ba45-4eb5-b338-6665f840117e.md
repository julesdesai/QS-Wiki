# Can perceptual errors undermine the reliability of mental representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aac5a7c2-ba45-4eb5-b338-6665f840117e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-e49b01dd-7eac-432b-990b-a606f4616c7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person in a fog mistakenly sees a man as a tree.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the mental representation is unreliable as an indicator of the external truth.**, **This presents a challenge to the idea that perceptual experiences reliably indicate external truths when the representations are accurate.**
