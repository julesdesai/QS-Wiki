# Is it plausible that knowledge can arise from non-virtuous habits?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 33bc7e4d-54fd-45ce-bfab-216d8e329806
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that beliefs arising from non-virtuous habits could be considered knowledge. Non-virtuous habits do not reliably connect beliefs to truth in a way that intellectual virtues do.**, **A belief is justified if it is produced by cognitive faculties or habits that constitute intellectual virtues. If the belief arises from non-virtuous habits, it seems incorrectly labeled as justified, challenging the notion it constitutes knowledge.**
