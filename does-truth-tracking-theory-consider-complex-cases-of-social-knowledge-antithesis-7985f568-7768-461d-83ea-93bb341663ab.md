# Does truth-tracking theory consider complex cases of social knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7985f568-7768-461d-83ea-93bb341663ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's belief is primarily justified through reliable testimony from experts rather than direct truth-tracking. In such a scenario, intuitively, the knowledge does not stem from personal tracking of truth but from recognizing others as trustworthy truth-trackers.**, **Suppose we have a scenario involving knowledge via expert testimony that bypasses direct truth-tracking. Intuitively, we either value indirect social truth-tracking or require direct individual truth-tracking. If we value indirect tracking, then truth-tracking conditions may misguide the direct individual interpretation.**
