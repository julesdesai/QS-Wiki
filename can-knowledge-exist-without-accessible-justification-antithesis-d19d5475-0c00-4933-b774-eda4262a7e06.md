# Can knowledge exist without accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d19d5475-0c00-4933-b774-eda4262a7e06
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Internalism](hybrid-internalism-synthesis-3d9ce0d9-6ba2-4665-a054-ed06708d6ee0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition and the proposition is true, yet the person cannot access the justification for their belief. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires accessibility to the justification for a belief. This principle together with the inability to access the justification entails that the person lacks knowledge.**
