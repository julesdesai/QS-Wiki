# Is our knowledge of other minds dependent solely on sensory experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e4ef72e-d15b-419a-a70d-80542543f98f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person A interacts with person B and attributes mental states like happiness or sadness to them. Intuitively, we either acknowledge that A's knowledge of B's mental state comes from sensory experiences of B's behavior or from an inference about B's mental states.**, **It is implausible that knowledge about other people's mental states can solely be reduced to sensory experiences since it often involves understanding intentions or emotions that are not directly observable.**
