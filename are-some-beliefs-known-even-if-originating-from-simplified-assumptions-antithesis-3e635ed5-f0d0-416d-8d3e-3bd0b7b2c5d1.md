# Are some beliefs known even if originating from simplified assumptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e635ed5-f0d0-416d-8d3e-3bd0b7b2c5d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-9325fd0f-53e7-40e1-8065-c0d6f77ce447.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientific model assumes several simplified premises, including false ones, but accurately predicts phenomena. Intuitively, we still consider the outcome as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that scientific knowledge can arise from effective models, even when some assumptions are technically false.**
