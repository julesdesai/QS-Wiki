# Can group knowledge exist if only individuals possess intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2697116-99cf-44d4-ab62-6125d904c04e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can extend beyond individual minds to collectives or groups. This principle together with the idea that only individual intellectual virtues can foster knowledge implies groups cannot know, which counters intuitive cases of collective epistemic success.**, **Consider a scenario in which a team collaboratively arrives at a true belief through pooled intellectual resources, despite no single member possessing the capacity individually. Intuitively, we would say the team as a whole knows, despite an absence of singular intellectual virtue.**
