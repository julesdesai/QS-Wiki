# Does the context-dependence of justification imply an infinite regress of standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 97e129c7-2b3d-4ce1-82fd-5d2b4b461164
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-55297f9c-50a2-4f24-b1b0-6029f7bef7b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose that each context has its own standard of justification which itself needs justification. Intuitively, we must either stop somewhere arbitrarily or we create an infinite regress of higher-order contexts needing justification.**, **If the justification required for knowledge varies with situations, and situations call for justification themselves, there could be no ultimate foundation for knowledge.**
