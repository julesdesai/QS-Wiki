# Can the Safety Condition Theory accommodate instances of cognitive biases leading to true belief without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc1f5201-5456-452f-9ce6-b41372d32ae1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a stereotype about a group and observes a coincidental incident reinforcing that stereotype, leading to a true belief lacking in rational justification. Intuitively, such a belief does not constitute knowledge due to its foundation in bias rather than evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs formed through cognitive bias without proper justification cannot be knowledge. This principle suggests that the truth of the belief, in this case, is accidental rather than known.**
