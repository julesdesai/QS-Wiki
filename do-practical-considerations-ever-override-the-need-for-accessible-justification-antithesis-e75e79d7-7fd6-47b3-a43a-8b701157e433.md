# Do practical considerations ever override the need for accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e75e79d7-7fd6-47b3-a43a-8b701157e433
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalism](internalism-thesis-697c012e-ac77-4c5b-935c-0bf2d4d5ce83.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a professional pilot makes an urgent decision based on years of experience and immediate situational awareness. Intuitively, we must say that they have knowledge of the best course of action despite not accessing explicit justification at that moment.**, **A plausible principle is that in high-stakes practical scenarios, knowledge can be present when accessible justification is lacking, suggesting a limit to the necessity of accessible justification.**
