# Does perception-based knowledge challenge the causal theory?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person correctly perceives a cat on a mat in ordinary conditions with good lighting. Intuitively, the person knows there is a cat on the mat.**, **A plausible principle is that not all cases of seeing require an additional causal account to establish knowledge. This principle questions whether the causal theory is necessary for all perceptual knowledge.**
