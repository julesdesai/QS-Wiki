# Can knowledge arise without a direct causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d81f5243-5d01-4f71-b69f-be36e01a32e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory of Knowledge](goldmans-causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d8bdc24-364e-4571-9b16-831c07368037.md)

## Content

**A person finds a gold ring on a street and correctly believes it was left there by a passerby, but her belief is based on the mere assumption without any direct causal connection to its truth.**, **This scenario suggests that it seems possible for someone to have knowledge even without a direct causal relationship between the truth of a proposition and the belief in that proposition.**
