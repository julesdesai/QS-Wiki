# Is knowledge context arbitrarily variable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7f7cee09-7408-43db-bd24-c77ad62e2cc5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology](contextualist-epistemology-synthesis-771a8ab1-0860-48e1-822c-9b3b2cda1c63.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone alternately knows or does not know a proposition based on slight contextual shifts undermines the stability of what it means to 'know'.**, **It is implausible that a significant aspect of human cognition like knowledge should be so sensitive to minor contextual elements.**, **Consider a scenario where minor social cues shift the standards by which a person is judged to know, leading to arbitrary judgments about knowledge. Intuitively, such variability appears unwarranted.**
