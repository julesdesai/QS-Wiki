# How does one avoid arbitrariness in an infinite regression of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b409f71-b188-4852-8352-d5df92647bb2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-c0072d0f-5658-4115-88b4-8385b90f0067.md)

## Content

**An infinite series of reasons may include reasons that become progressively more tenuous, which intuitively reduces the soundness of the initial belief it aims to justify.**, **Suppose an individual claims they have infinite reasons for a belief, where some reasons appear detached from reality. It is plausible that a finite set of better reasons would avoid arbitrary connections.**
