# Is perception immune to optical illusions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85fe1d98-c65d-4bf3-aaee-4fd1f2371552
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual views an optical illusion like the Müller-Lyer illusion and perceives lines to be of different lengths. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is not immune to being misled by illusions.**, **There is a plausible principle that perception should reflect reality accurately. This principle together with susceptibility to optical illusions entails that perception is fallible.**
