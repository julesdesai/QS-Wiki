# Do contextual shifts undermine the reliability of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a73dd685-5734-4aab-93f6-6cba53afd364
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**When the standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, the reliability of knowledge claims may be compromised as these standards shift.**, **If a person makes a knowledge claim in one context, that claim might not hold up if the context shifts.**, **The shifting nature of contexts makes it difficult to maintain a stable claim to knowledge over time.**
