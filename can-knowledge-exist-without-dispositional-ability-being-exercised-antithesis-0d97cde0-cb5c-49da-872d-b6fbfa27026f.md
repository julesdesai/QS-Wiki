# Can knowledge exist without dispositional ability being exercised?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d97cde0-cb5c-49da-872d-b6fbfa27026f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowing a proposition requires the ability to form a true belief. This principle together with the observation that some individuals know propositions without actively exercising this ability entails that dispositional abilities may not be essential to knowledge**, **Consider a scenario in which someone knows a mathematical fact without engaging in any related reasoning process. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person knows the fact despite not exercising any dispositional ability to form that belief**, **It is implausible that every single instance of knowledge requires the active exercise of a dispositional ability**
