# Are intellectual virtues entirely domain-specific?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 486f3757-1b5e-4fd4-bdb3-925706384ca5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual virtues are entirely domain-specific given the fluidity and interconnected nature of intellectual endeavors. Without cross-domain adaptability, virtues are constrained and may not adequately lead to knowledge outside specified realms.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an expert mathematician applies logical reasoning principles to solve a complex puzzle in anthropology. Intuitively, the transfer of intellectual virtues suggests flexibility rather than strict domain-specificity, questioning whether virtues can be domain-restricted.**
