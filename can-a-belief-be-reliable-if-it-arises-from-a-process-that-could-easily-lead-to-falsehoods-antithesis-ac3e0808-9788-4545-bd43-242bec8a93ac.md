# Can a belief be reliable if it arises from a process that could easily lead to falsehoods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac3e0808-9788-4545-bd43-242bec8a93ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Externalism Summary](moderate-externalism-summary-synthesis-b4668bae-162e-4191-89bc-89b702818630.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition based on a process that, in slightly different circumstances, would easily lead to falsehood. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not truly know the proposition.**, **A case in which a belief arises from a highly unreliable process, which just happens to lead to a true belief in this instance, challenges the idea that reliability is enough for knowledge.**
