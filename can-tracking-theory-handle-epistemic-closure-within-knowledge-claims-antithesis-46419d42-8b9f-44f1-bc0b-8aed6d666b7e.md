# Can tracking theory handle epistemic closure within knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46419d42-8b9f-44f1-bc0b-8aed6d666b7e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50e12e5-c945-480c-9bf6-a71471f277b2.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that if a person knows one proposition, and this proposition entails a second, they should know the second if they competently deduce it.**, **According to tracking theory, different propositions may vary in truth across different scenarios. A belief in an inferred proposition may not track its truth across all scenarios if the original knowledge does not necessarily entail the truth-tracking of the inferred claim. This challenges the integrity of epistemic closure under tracking theory.**
