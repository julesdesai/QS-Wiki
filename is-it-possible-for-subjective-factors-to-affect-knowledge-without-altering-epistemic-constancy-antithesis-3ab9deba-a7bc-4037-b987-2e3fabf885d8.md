# Is it possible for subjective factors to affect knowledge without altering epistemic constancy?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ab9deba-a7bc-4037-b987-2e3fabf885d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-fa9170ce-e58d-4311-9c0f-f4410b4d69a4.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which Olivia and Paul both know the track conditions, but Paul’s fear of injury affects whether his belief counts as knowledge. Intuitively, this suggests subjective factors might alter knowledge, conflicting with the notion of epistemic constancy.**, **A plausible principle is that constancy in epistemic standards prohibits variation resultant from personal differences.**
