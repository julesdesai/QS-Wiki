# Can knowledge occur without active belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8d6d195c-3e28-4dca-9155-3a1e5f036ba1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone acquires knowledge through passive absorption, like osmosis, rather than actively forming a belief, challenges the concept that knowledge always requires active belief formation**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be acquired through passive means, such as witnessing an event and retaining the information unconsciously. This principle together with instances of passive knowledge acquisition entails that active belief formation may not be necessary for all knowledge**
