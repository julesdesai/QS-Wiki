# Does mistaken belief in a nearby world undermine knowledge in the actual world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5e53c7e-f556-4c70-a518-9383ed13f908
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition based on accurate perception and the belief is true, but in a nearby possible world, lighting conditions cause the person to form a false belief. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows in the actual world or that they don't know. If we say the person doesn't know about this case, then we deny knowledge where it seems intuitively present. If we say the person knows despite the misleading nearby world, then the safety condition appears unnecessary for knowledge.**
