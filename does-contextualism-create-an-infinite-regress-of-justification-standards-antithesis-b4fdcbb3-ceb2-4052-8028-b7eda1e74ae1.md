# Does Contextualism create an infinite regress of justification standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4fdcbb3-ceb2-4052-8028-b7eda1e74ae1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a context that demands higher justification than another context.**, **There is a plausible principle that higher standards can always be demanded further, leading to an infinite regress.**, **Intuitively, if contextualism allows shifting to higher and higher standards, this implies an endless demand for more rigorous justification without ever achieving knowledge.**
