# Does sensitivity to truth fail with necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df402888-281c-4197-b96d-addb37a13126
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario with a mathematician who believes that 2 + 2 = 4.**, **It is implausible to require that if the proposition "2 + 2 = 4" were false, the mathematician would not believe it, since necessary truths cannot be false.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires handling counterfactuals coherently, yet sensitivity to truth seems inapplicable for propositions that are necessarily true.**
