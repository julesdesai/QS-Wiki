# How can we consider a belief justified if no reason is accessible to the believer?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8f99d6f-15cd-49a9-83c3-65ec9129b1d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true fact because of a reliable but inaccessible mechanism, like a brain implant providing correct information. Intuitively, we would either have to say that the belief is justified or not justified. If we say that the belief is justified, it implies that justification can exist without awareness, which is counterintuitive. If we say the belief is not justified, it challenges the view that justification need not be accessible.**
