# Can reliability handle cases of environmental luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 12fd669a-c609-4989-8646-8fef2cf7c90d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-c54e9ff1-39e1-4224-b3be-b0979177f411.md)

## Content

**Consider someone using a normally reliable method, like reading a clock, but unbeknownst to them, the clock is broken and just happens to show the correct time. Intuitively, we would not grant this person knowledge because their belief is true merely by luck in this situation.**, **Reliability seems insufficient to distinguish between knowledge and a true belief accidentally arising from environmental luck.**
