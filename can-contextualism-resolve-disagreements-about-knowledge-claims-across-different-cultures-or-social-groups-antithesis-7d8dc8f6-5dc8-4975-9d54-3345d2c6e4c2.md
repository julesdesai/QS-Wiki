# Can contextualism resolve disagreements about knowledge claims across different cultures or social groups?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d8dc8f6-5dc8-4975-9d54-3345d2c6e4c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Different cultures or social groups may have varying standards for what counts as sufficient justification for knowledge.**, **A scenario in which one group's contextual standards for knowledge drastically differ from another's could lead to conflicting claims about what individuals know.**, **This variability might result in cross-cultural misunderstandings and disputes about whether certain knowledge claims are valid, reflecting deeper epistemic relativism rather than true knowledge.**
