# Does varying standards of knowledge contradict truth's necessity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d79bdd3f-8062-4842-859b-91688060e883
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**Knowledge requires that the proposition known must be true. If standards for knowing a proposition vary with context, there might be scenarios where a true belief doesn't count as knowledge due to heightened standards, which intuitively conflicts with the necessity of truth for knowledge.**, **Consider a case where a person correctly believes a true proposition but fails to know it due to a contextually high standard, suggesting a tension between truth and knowledge standards.**
