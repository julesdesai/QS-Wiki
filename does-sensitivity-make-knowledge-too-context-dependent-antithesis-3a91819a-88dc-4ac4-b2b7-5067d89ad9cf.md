# Does sensitivity make knowledge too context-dependent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a91819a-88dc-4ac4-b2b7-5067d89ad9cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Sensitivity](process-reliabilism-with-sensitivity-synthesis-3b7ba929-b462-4672-9048-c64e1338414e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who knows their friend moved to Paris because they have been told by a reliable mutual acquaintance and saw the travel arrangements. Intuitively, this is knowledge.**, **If the friend had not moved and the acquaintance misinformed them, they would still have believed it, given this context, thus making sensitivity an unreliable measure of knowledge.**
