## Content

**There is a plausible principle that certain kinds of knowledge, such as self-knowledge or introspective knowledge, do not rely on external perceptual structures**, **Suppose a person introspects and knows they are in pain without perceiving any external pattern or relation. Intuitively, this person has knowledge of their pain despite the lack of structural organization in their perception**
