# Do psychological insights challenge the internalist defeasibility view of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4607243-7244-4bac-a9e2-871cad3949cb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Consider cases where cognitive biases prevent a person from recognizing evidence as a defeater. Intuitively, such biases highlight limitations in the internalist requirement that one must be aware of and address all defeaters to possess knowledge.**, **If psychological factors can inhibit recognition of defeaters, this suggests the internalist requirement for knowledge might be in conflict with established understanding of human cognition.**, **This indicates that some potential defeaters may remain unnoticed and could unjustifiably restrict what might otherwise be considered knowledge.**
