# Knowledge as Perceptual Reliability

**Parent:** [Does availability of alternative evidence affect knowledge?](does-availability-of-alternative-evidence-affect-knowledge-antithesis-336914e3-d1c0-4568-9dd5-c3b1bb9f17a4.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a belief to count as knowledge, it is necessary that the belief is reliably formed through perception and is not undermined by ignored relevant evidence**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification, the proposition is true, and the belief is perceptually reliable**
