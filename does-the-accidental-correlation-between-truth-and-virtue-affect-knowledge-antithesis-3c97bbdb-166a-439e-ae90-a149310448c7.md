# Does the accidental correlation between truth and virtue affect knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c97bbdb-166a-439e-ae90-a149310448c7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where someone arrives at a true belief due to accidental correlation between their virtues and the truth. Intuitively, this seems to lack the reliability and intentionality needed for knowledge.**, **Knowledge is true belief arising from intellectual virtues. A belief true by sheer accident despite virtuous habits questions whether it is truly knowledge.**
