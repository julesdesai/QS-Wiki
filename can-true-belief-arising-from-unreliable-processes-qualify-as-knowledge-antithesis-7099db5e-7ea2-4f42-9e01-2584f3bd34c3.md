# Can true belief arising from unreliable processes qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7099db5e-7ea2-4f42-9e01-2584f3bd34c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Enhanced Virtue Reliabilism with Error Avoidance](enhanced-virtue-reliabilism-with-error-avoidance-synthesis-bae843f7-47b1-4791-af4f-a08b903c892d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person coincidentally holds a true belief based on unreliable or arbitrary processes, like horoscope readings. Intuitively, we would have to say that the belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **The requirement for intellectual virtue that reliably avoids error suggests that unreliable sources, despite leading to true belief, cannot provide knowledge.**
