# Is the connection between intellectual virtue and truth necessary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c7dcb037-dad3-4b42-a22a-2f1de1974fcd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a student makes a well-reasoned argument based on intellectual virtues such as logical reasoning and thorough analysis, but the conclusion happens to be false. Intuitively, we would judge that the student does not have knowledge, because the conclusion is false.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtues need to reliably lead to true beliefs for knowledge to occur. If intellectual virtue does not reliably connect to truth, knowledge cannot be solely based on intellectual virtues.**
