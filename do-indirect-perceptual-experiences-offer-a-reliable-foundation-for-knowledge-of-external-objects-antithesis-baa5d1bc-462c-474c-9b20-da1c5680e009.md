# Do indirect perceptual experiences offer a reliable foundation for knowledge of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** baa5d1bc-462c-474c-9b20-da1c5680e009
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person looks at a tree through a distorted glass window. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have a reliable foundation for knowledge of the tree's attributes.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge based on unreliable or distorted input is not genuine knowledge. This principle together with relying on perceptual experiences as indirect evidence entails that knowledge can be fallible.**
