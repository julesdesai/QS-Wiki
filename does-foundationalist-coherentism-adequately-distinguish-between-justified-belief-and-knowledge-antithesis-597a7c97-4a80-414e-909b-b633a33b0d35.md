# Does Foundationalist Coherentism adequately distinguish between justified belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 597a7c97-4a80-414e-909b-b633a33b0d35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**Knowledge traditionally is defined as justified true belief.**, **A belief might be justified through coherence or basic status without being true.**, **In cases where justified beliefs are false, distinguishing knowledge (true justified belief) becomes problematic, especially under a coherentist model that permits internal coherence as a sufficient ground for justification.**
