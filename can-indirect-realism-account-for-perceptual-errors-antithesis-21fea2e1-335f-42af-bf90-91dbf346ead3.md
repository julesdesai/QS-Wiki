# Can indirect realism account for perceptual errors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21fea2e1-335f-42af-bf90-91dbf346ead3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Maintained Through Inference](indirect-realism-maintained-through-inference-synthesis-ffd82d7b-be0f-4614-a603-c896b6b1b7df.md)

## Content

**There are cases where perceptual experiences do not accurately represent external objects, such as illusions or hallucinations.**, **If knowledge involves inferring the existence of external objects from perceptual experiences, then erroneous perceptions should lead to erroneous inferences.**, **It is unclear how systematic inference can reliably distinguish between accurate and erroneous perceptual experiences without assuming direct access to external reality.**
