# Is it plausible that coherence fails as a standalone criterion for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55997eb9-3366-4ee8-91e7-2f7f9b4a7407
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that coherence alone can be insufficient for justification because coherence has traditionally been considered a robust criterion.**, **Consider a scenario where a coherent set of beliefs is rejected as unjustified purely because of a change in context.**, **In such a case, intuitively, we would judge that coherence should count significantly towards justification regardless of the context.**
