# Are bodily skills alone sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da747390-6bda-41fb-afc0-5ce0dc800f81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Embodied Cognition with Reliance on Bodily Skills](embodied-cognition-with-reliance-on-bodily-skills-synthesis-e9d28d24-22c1-4ccc-a9a1-a716398534d0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a skilled dancer moves fluidly in response to a song without consciously processing lyrics. Intuitively, either the dancer has knowledge of the song’s meaning or at least some conscious belief is also necessary. If only bodily skill is sufficient, then knowledge could be attributed to pure bodily reaction, which seems counterintuitive.**
