# How does one distinguish between luck and justified belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1dc0500e-7097-4bd3-886c-8e3b7c640ecc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person predicts the stock market will rise and it does, but their belief was based on unreliable advice rather than sound analysis. Intuitively, we would judge that luck, rather than justified belief, played a role in the success, challenging the sufficiency of practical success for justification.**, **It is implausible to equate knowledge with scenarios where the outcome is due to luck, as this undermines the requirement for a truth-conducive justification.**
