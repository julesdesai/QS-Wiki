# Does pragmatic importance affect whether we attribute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4b7a9b4-3038-429b-8127-721000396864
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Knowledge Summary](invariantist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-7e971fef-e35c-4ec9-93e0-84ae5864f440.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes they know where their car is parked, which is true and justified in a low-stakes situation like idle conversation. Intuitively, if the stakes are raised, say finding the car under emergency circumstances, we might require more evidence to say they know where it is.**, **There is a plausible principle that the importance of being correct about a belief impacts our evaluation of whether someone truly knows the proposition.**
