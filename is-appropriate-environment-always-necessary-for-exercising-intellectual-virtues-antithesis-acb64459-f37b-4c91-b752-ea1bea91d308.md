# Is appropriate environment always necessary for exercising intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** acb64459-f37b-4c91-b752-ea1bea91d308
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person, using well-developed intellectual virtues, forms a true belief while in an unpredictable and chaotic environment. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge the person might still possess knowledge due to their skill and motivation.**, **The necessity of an appropriate environment for virtue epistemology is questionable if individuals can adapt their virtues to hostile or unpredictable conditions.**, **It is implausible that intellectual virtues require ideal conditions to yield knowledge in every instance, as adaptability is an aspect of expertise.**
