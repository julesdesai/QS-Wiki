# Can external stakes influence internal cognitive states in defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c12de59-e7bc-490f-bd63-53e43cf6d298
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Theory](pragmatic-encroachment-theory-synthesis-48f209b4-b71c-4b9b-814c-8c2a5af0c840.md)

## Content

**Suppose person X knows a fact under normal stakes, such as the distance to the nearest town based on GPS. Intuitively, X knows the distance.**, **In a changed scenario where getting lost has catastrophic consequences, person Y relies on the same GPS to estimate the distance. Intuitively, Y still knows the distance.**, **It seems implausible that external stakes should alter the internal cognitive states that define knowledge.**
