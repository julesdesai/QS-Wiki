# Can knowledge be retained if the causal connection is later severed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1221283d-fca1-4545-8efd-801f5b64b6a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person P learns a truth based on direct evidence, but later the evidence is destroyed or becomes inaccessible. Intuitively, we are posed to question if P retains knowledge without the persistent causal link.**, **A plausible principle is that severing causal connections removes the grounding for holding true belief as knowledge. This principle suggests that persistent causal links are essential to maintain knowledge over time.**, **It is implausible to hold knowledge if the original causative facts no longer support the belief, reflecting on the necessity and permanence of causality for sustained knowledge.**
