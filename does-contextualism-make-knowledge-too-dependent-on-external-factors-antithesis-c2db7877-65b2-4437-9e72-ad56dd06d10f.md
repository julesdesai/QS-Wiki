# Does Contextualism make knowledge too dependent on external factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2db7877-65b2-4437-9e72-ad56dd06d10f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person's knowledge shifts simply due to an external change in context, not due to any change in their understanding or evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should primarily depend on the individual's cognitive state and available evidence.**, **Intuitively, making knowledge heavily dependent on context seems to remove it from personal cognitive control, which challenges our view of knowledge as something personal and internal.**
