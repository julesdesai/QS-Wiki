# Does reliance on external factors undermine the autonomous nature of reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7dba6bb1-b6f2-4f03-9d16-c4f3c4f8886b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should stem from an individual's reasoning rather than from external factors.**, **Consider a scenario where external factors entirely influence a person's belief justification, suggesting that the individual has no autonomy over their reasoning process.**, **In such cases, intuitively, we would judge that the autonomy and internal coherence of reasoning are undermined.**
