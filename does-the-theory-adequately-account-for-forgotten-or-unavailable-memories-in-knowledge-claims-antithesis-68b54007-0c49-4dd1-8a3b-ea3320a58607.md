# Does the theory adequately account for forgotten or unavailable memories in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68b54007-0c49-4dd1-8a3b-ea3320a58607
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Phenomenalism Allowing Memory Role](revised-phenomenalism-allowing-memory-role-synthesis-5fb5abbe-6adb-4855-a476-25c8a91d11a9.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is often contingent on available evidence at a given time. This point together with the fact that memory can be unreliable or erased over time suggests that the absence of memories might undermine certain knowledge claims that depend on them.**, **Consider a case where a witness forgets key details of an event due to time passing. Intuitively, their knowledge claim about the event becomes less credible without the supporting memories.**
