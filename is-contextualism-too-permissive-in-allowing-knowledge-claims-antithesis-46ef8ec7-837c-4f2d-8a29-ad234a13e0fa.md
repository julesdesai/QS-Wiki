# Is contextualism too permissive in allowing knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46ef8ec7-837c-4f2d-8a29-ad234a13e0fa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**By allowing justification to be context-dependent, contextualism might admit too many beliefs as knowledge.**, **This permissiveness could dilute the concept of knowledge by including unreliable or poorly justified beliefs.**, **Such inclusion raises questions about the rigor of what qualifies as knowledge.**
