# Can knowledge be insensitive to false lemmas?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebd55ce8-44a4-4ef4-9b7f-4489c2aee48d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective believes a suspect committed a crime based on a fingerprint, which turns out to be the suspect’s identical twin's. The belief that the suspect is guilty is true, but due to incorrect reasoning, it seems intuitively unjustified as knowledge.**, **There is an intuition that knowledge should be based on correct reasoning rather than lucky guesses. This suggests that justification must exclude reliance on false information.**
