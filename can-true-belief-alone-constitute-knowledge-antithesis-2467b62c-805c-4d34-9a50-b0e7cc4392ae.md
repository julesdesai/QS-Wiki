# Can true belief alone constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2467b62c-805c-4d34-9a50-b0e7cc4392ae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person forms a true belief by sheer guesswork or coincidence is not a case of knowledge despite the belief being true**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just true belief, such as justification or evidence. This principle together with the notion that a true belief can sometimes occur through luck or guessing entails that not all true beliefs amount to knowledge**
