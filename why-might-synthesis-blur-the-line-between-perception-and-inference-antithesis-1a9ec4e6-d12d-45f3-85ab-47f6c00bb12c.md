# Why might synthesis blur the line between perception and inference?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a9ec4e6-d12d-45f3-85ab-47f6c00bb12c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person relies on both seeing a flashing light and hearing a siren to conclude that an emergency vehicle is nearby. Intuitively, this synthesis blurs the distinction between perception and inferential reasoning.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge derived from perception should not rely heavily on inference. This principle together with the requirement for synthesis implies that the perceptual aspect of knowledge is weakened as inferential processes dominate.**, **It is implausible to maintain a clear distinction between what is perceived and what is inferred in cases requiring extensive synthesis.**
