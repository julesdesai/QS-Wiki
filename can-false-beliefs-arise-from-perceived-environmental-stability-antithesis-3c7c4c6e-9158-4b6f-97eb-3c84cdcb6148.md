# Can false beliefs arise from perceived environmental stability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c7c4c6e-9158-4b6f-97eb-3c84cdcb6148
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Environment-Sensitive Reliability](process-reliabilism-with-environment-sensitive-reliability-synthesis-cdd770c1-1646-4221-a17f-d7d94aac715e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person forms a belief because an environment appears stable, but the stability is merely an illusion due to rare, extreme events that the process does not account for.**, **Intuitively, we might think this allows for a false belief that masquerades as knowledge because it aligns with the typical environmental expectations.**, **A belief-forming process relying on perceived rather than actual environmental stability might lead to knowledge claims vulnerable to exception cases.**
