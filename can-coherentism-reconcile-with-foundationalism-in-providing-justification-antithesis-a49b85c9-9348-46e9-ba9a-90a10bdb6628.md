# Can coherentism reconcile with foundationalism in providing justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a49b85c9-9348-46e9-ba9a-90a10bdb6628
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-1575945f-cea5-4275-8c31-c7cabab6c71a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that coherence requires mutual support among beliefs rather than support from a foundational belief. This principle together with the notion that knowledge requires a foundational belief contradicts the idea of coherentism.**, **In a scenario where all beliefs support each other equally without a self-evident foundational belief, intuitively, we might still judge those beliefs to be justified.**
