# Does contextualist epistemology respect the normative aspect of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d12b9ff-43a8-4d8b-9ed0-dda7bec2f976
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**Suppose knowledge has normative elements like justified belief that require stable criteria beyond context fluctuations. Intuitively, these elements should be consistent across contexts.**, **It is a plausible principle that normative constraints on knowledge require uniform application regardless of contextual changes.**, **If contextualism alters normative components arbitrarily, it may fail to respect the philosophical importance of knowledge as a norm.**
