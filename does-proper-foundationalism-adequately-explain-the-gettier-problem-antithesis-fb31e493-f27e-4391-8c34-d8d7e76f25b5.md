# Does proper foundationalism adequately explain the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb31e493-f27e-4391-8c34-d8d7e76f25b5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Foundationalism](proper-foundationalism-synthesis-115cfa71-603e-48b1-bce9-39f7dced12e3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a justified true belief due to properly basic foundations but achieves the truth through coincidental factors, as in Gettier cases. Intuitively, we would not judge this belief as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must avoid luck to count as knowledge. This principle along with the claim that knowledge is true belief based on properly basic foundational beliefs involves consideration that luck can play a part in belief acquisition even if foundational beliefs are true.**, **It is implausible that properly basic foundational beliefs can completely eliminate the influence of epistemic luck, thus not fully addressing the Gettier problem.**
