# Could a reliable process still lead to false beliefs under certain conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb2cacfb-a3c7-4c56-8604-f7c769de1489
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology With Virtuous Belief Formation](virtue-epistemology-with-virtuous-belief-formation-synthesis-53b228ae-7464-400d-be01-037da588b5a0.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a cognitive process that is generally reliable under normal conditions but fails under specific external interferences, resulting in false beliefs.**, **In these conditions, the process cannot be said to reliably lead to truth.**, **Intuitively, forming a false belief under such interference, despite a generally reliable process, would not count as knowledge.**
