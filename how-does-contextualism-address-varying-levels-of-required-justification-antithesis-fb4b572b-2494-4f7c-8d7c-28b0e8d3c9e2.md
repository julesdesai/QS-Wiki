# How does contextualism address varying levels of required justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb4b572b-2494-4f7c-8d7c-28b0e8d3c9e2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle suggests that the level of justification required for knowledge should be consistent. This principle together with contextualism's allowance for variability suggests an inconsistency.**, **Suppose we have a case with different contexts requiring vastly different justificatory standards. Intuitively, this leads to confusion about the concept of knowledge.**
