# Is belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0a570352-abbb-4328-84be-6a093bf9b415
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-45579dd6-32de-43f3-ae7a-76b3bb7de09b.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is told a fact by an unreliable source and believes it regardless; it happens to be true by coincidence. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows the fact, despite their belief.**, **A plausible principle claims that justified true belief, not mere belief, is required for knowledge. Thus, merely believing a true proposition does not entail knowledge.**
