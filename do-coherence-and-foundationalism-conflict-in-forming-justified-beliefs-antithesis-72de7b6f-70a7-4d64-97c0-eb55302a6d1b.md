# Do coherence and foundationalism conflict in forming justified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72de7b6f-70a7-4d64-97c0-eb55302a6d1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism in Epistemology](foundationalism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4c27bb26-6321-4022-8e66-a85ea5d939d9.md)

## Content

**Coherence among beliefs increases their reliability and justification.**, **If coherence is necessary for justification, then a solitary basic belief cannot be self-justifying.**, **If basic beliefs require coherence with other beliefs for justification, then they cannot exclusively serve as the foundation.**
