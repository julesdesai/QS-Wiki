# Can contextually varying standards provide a consistent account of knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8ed5a848-6060-4e5b-a979-08cb4ff26767
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people acquire the same piece of information in different contexts with different standards of justification.**, **It would follow that they might each attain or fail to attain knowledge differently, which questions a consistent account of how knowledge is acquired.**, **Intuitively, acquiring knowledge should not depend on factors extrinsic to the knowledge itself, such as context.**
