# Can phenomenalism account for the existence of unperceived objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aad10122-35c5-4601-8bed-3cda69fd4184
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an object, such as a tree, exists without anyone perceiving it. Intuitively, we would judge that the tree continues to exist independently of any sensory experience of it.**, **There is a plausible principle that objects have a continuous existence independent of perception. This principle together with the scenario of an unperceived tree entails that phenomenalism cannot account for this intuition about the independent existence of objects.**
