# Does a causal connection ensure the belief is reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6dfc241-4f2c-49d1-b01a-b69d90a7b63d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only causal connection but also that the causal process is reliable and understood by the believer. This principle suggests that a causal connection alone does not ensure the belief is knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone forms a true belief based on a causal connection that is random or unexplained. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief lacks the reliability needed for knowledge.**
