# Can cross-modal conflicts challenge Direct Realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 922f914b-e6ff-4fba-8d33-346f9183dca6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a tree at a distance and hears footsteps approaching from a different direction. Intuitively, these cross-modal sensory inputs do not integrate into a unified perception of the environment.**, **Direct experience of perceiving both the visual and auditory elements does not naturally integrate, causing confusion about the position of the footsteps relative to the tree.**, **Such a case suggests that different sensory modalities may not always provide a coherent and direct perception of external objects.**
