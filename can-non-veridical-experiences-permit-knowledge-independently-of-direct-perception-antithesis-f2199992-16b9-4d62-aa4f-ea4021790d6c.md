# Can non-veridical experiences permit knowledge independently of direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2199992-16b9-4d62-aa4f-ea4021790d6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-8f6e7bad-fa5f-47b7-8d7c-d5f7ea4e2a2a.md)

## Content

**A person can dream of a stream flowing in a riverbed precisely where it exists in reality without ever visiting the site. Yet, intuitively, this does not grant the dreamer actual knowledge about the stream according to their dream experience.**, **The intuition suggests that knowledge appears disconnected from direct perception in such a non-veridical case, indicating a dependency beyond mere perception in supporting knowledge.**, **It is implausible that mere coincidence of dream events and reality supports claims about knowledge without direct interacting perception.**
