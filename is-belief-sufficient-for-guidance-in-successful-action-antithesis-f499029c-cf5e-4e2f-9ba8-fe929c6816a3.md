# Is belief sufficient for guidance in successful action?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f499029c-cf5e-4e2f-9ba8-fe929c6816a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Adequacy](pragmatism-with-practical-adequacy-synthesis-7de50134-9521-4bfe-88f0-682c548d4774.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual does not consciously hold or express a belief about a proposition but operates under assumptions that align with their subconscious beliefs to successfully achieve a goal. In such a scenario, intuitively, the mere presence of an unconscious belief does not equate to practical knowledge guiding action.**, **There is a plausible principle that practical knowledge requires a conscious and explicit guiding belief, rather than subconscious guidance, for its role in successful action. This principle coupled with unconscious beliefs leading to success suggests belief alone, especially unconscious belief, may not be sufficient.**
