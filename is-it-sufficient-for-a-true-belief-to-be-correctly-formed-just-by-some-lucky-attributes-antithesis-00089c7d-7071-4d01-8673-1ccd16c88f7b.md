# Is it sufficient for a true belief to be correctly formed just by some lucky attributes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 00089c7d-7071-4d01-8673-1ccd16c88f7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who, by mere chance, always guesses the right answers on a test without any understanding or factual basis. Intuitively, we would not say that this person has knowledge, even though they have true belief.**, **Knowledge, which differs from sheer luck, requires a reliable method or foundation for forming beliefs beyond sheer happenstance.**
