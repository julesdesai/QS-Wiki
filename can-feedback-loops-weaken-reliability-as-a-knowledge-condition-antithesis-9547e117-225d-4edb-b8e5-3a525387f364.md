## Content

**Suppose a case where a person forms beliefs based on social media feedback, where a false belief circulates widely, creating an appearance of reliability and truth. Intuitively, such beliefs do not constitute knowledge, though supported by seemingly confirming evidence.**, **Social media feedback loops can falsely enhance perceived reliability, demonstrating that popularity-induced reliability does not equate to actual reliability necessary for knowledge.**
