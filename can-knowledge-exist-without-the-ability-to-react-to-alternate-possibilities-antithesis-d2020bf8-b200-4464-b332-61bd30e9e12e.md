# Can knowledge exist without the ability to react to alternate possibilities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2020bf8-b200-4464-b332-61bd30e9e12e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory With Truth Tracking](nozicks-tracking-theory-with-truth-tracking-synthesis-9a557177-6533-4827-a4a8-a2fb94f50474.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person gains a true belief but is unable to consider alternate possibilities due to cognitive limitations, like being unable to think of any reasons the belief might be false. Intuitively, either we have to say that they somehow know despite this incapacity or that their belief doesn’t qualify as knowledge. If we say they know, this seems inconsistent with the understanding of knowledge as requiring sensitivity to truth. If we say they don’t know, then it suggests tracking hypersensitivity is fundamental to knowledge beyond mere truth adherence.**
