# Do external objects necessarily require perception for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 01fb616a-d6ce-4b33-b535-d9b5658b1b23
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which scientists predict the existence of a planet based on its gravitational effects on other celestial bodies, without direct perceptual evidence of the planet itself. Intuitively, we would say that they have knowledge of the external object, here a planet, independent of direct perceptions.**, **There is a plausible principle that scientific inferential methods provide knowledge of external objects, suggesting that perception is not the sole gateway to knowledge of such objects.**
