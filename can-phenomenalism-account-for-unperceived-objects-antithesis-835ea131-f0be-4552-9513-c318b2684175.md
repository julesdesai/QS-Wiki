# Can phenomenalism account for unperceived objects?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism suggests that physical objects are logical constructions of sensory experiences.](phenomenalism-suggests-that-physical-objects-are-logical-constructions-of-sensory-experiences-synthesis-70c8baf6-067c-48d6-b521-64b5ff284db6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a tree falls in a forest with no one around to perceive it. Intuitively, we would judge that the tree's existence is independent of being perceived.**, **The intuitive reality of objects such as stars and planets existing without human perception challenges the idea that objects are entirely dependent on sensory experiences for their existence.**
