## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that their belief is reliably produced by a truth-conducive intellectual virtue**, **An intellectual virtue is a character trait that tends to lead one to form true beliefs across diverse contexts**, **Reliability is the tendency to produce true beliefs more often than false ones**
