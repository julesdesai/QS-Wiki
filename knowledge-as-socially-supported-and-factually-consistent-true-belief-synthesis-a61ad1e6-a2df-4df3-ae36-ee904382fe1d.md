# Knowledge as Socially Supported and Factually Consistent True Belief

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** a61ad1e6-a2df-4df3-ae36-ee904382fe1d
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can social practices lead to inconsistent beliefs?](can-social-practices-lead-to-inconsistent-beliefs-antithesis-7f53fbfc-59d5-4cb8-bde1-cfc0211ed6a6.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is required that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is supported by factually consistent social knowledge practices**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is supported by factually consistent social knowledge practices**
