# How does the theory explain perceptual relativity where different observers perceive differently?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dfc380cf-e178-4461-9288-9bf3bc0671f2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Strong Correspondence](direct-realism-with-strong-correspondence-synthesis-28884d49-4ced-45da-83a0-528139a02f57.md)

## Content

**In scenarios where two people perceive the same object differently, like color perception under varying lighting conditions, their perceptual experiences differ.**, **These differences suggest that perceptual experiences do not strictly correspond to external objects.**, **If perceptual relativity leads to different experiences of the same object, strict correspondence is undermined.**
