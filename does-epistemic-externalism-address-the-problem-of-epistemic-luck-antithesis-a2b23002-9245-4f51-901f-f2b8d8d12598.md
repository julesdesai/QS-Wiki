# Does epistemic externalism address the problem of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a2b23002-9245-4f51-901f-f2b8d8d12598
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a belief is externally justified but happens to be true only by luck. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **The dependence of knowledge on external factors fails to adequately address cases where truth is attained through mere luck.**, **It is implausible that knowledge correctly defined allows beliefs arising from luck to fulfill its criteria.**
