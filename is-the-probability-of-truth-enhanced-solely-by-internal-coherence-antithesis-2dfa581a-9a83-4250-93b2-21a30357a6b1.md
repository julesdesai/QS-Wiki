# Is the probability of truth enhanced solely by internal coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2dfa581a-9a83-4250-93b2-21a30357a6b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two scientific theories are both internally coherent but rely on different assumptions about unobserved phenomena. Intuitively, we either have to say that one is more probable than the other or they are equally probable. If we say they are equally probable, then such and such raises questions about the role of empirical validation. If we say one is more probable, then such and such suggests that coherence alone does not determine probability.**
