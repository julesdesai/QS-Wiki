# Can knowledge be truly safe from error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dfcb4706-842f-460a-9b4c-2310a3aa4281
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**A belief is only safe from error if it cannot be easily mistaken for a false belief in nearby possible worlds. However, it is implausible that we can accurately determine what constitutes a nearby possible world.**, **Suppose a person believes a proposition based on misleading evidence that could have easily been false. Intuitively, it seems they do not have knowledge, challenging the idea that their belief is safe from error simply because it is true in their context.**
