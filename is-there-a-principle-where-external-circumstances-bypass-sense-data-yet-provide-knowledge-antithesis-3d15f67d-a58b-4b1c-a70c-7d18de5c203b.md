# Is there a principle where external circumstances bypass sense data yet provide knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d15f67d-a58b-4b1c-a70c-7d18de5c203b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-6f280b3f-d71e-41be-89e6-75cad68b6820.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that some kinds of direct causal interactions with the environment can yield knowledge without intermediary sense data.**, **If such interactions bypass sense data, this undermines the requirement of sense data for knowledge in the indirect realist framework.**
