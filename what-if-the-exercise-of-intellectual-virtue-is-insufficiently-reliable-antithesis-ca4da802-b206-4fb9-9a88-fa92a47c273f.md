# What if the exercise of intellectual virtue is insufficiently reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca4da802-b206-4fb9-9a88-fa92a47c273f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person relies on their intellectual virtues, like careful consideration and critical thinking, to judge a scientific theory as true, yet the theory is later debunked as false through new evidence. Intuitively, we have to say that the initial judgment was not knowledge because it relied on outdated or false information.**, **For knowledge to be present, it is implausible that the exercise of intellectual virtue alone is sufficient; the process must be reliably truth-conducive. Reliability and truth-tight linkage between belief and truth must support intellectual virtues for knowledge.**
