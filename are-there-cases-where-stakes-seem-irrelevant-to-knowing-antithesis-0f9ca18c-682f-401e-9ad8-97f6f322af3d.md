# Are there cases where stakes seem irrelevant to knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0f9ca18c-682f-401e-9ad8-97f6f322af3d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-a38d2af6-3ec4-421c-a558-c594d0cbc8b7.md)

## Content

**Imagine a child correctly knowing a simple math fact with no consideration of stakes or consequences. Intuitively, introducing stakes to this scenario doesn't change our judgment about the child's knowledge.**, **It is plausible that knowledge in certain basic domains, like math, is unaffected by stakes, suggesting that the importance of stakes is limited and context-specific rather than universally applicable.**
