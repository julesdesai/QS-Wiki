# Can testimony alone justify belief in external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 94563164-44b4-41e9-8bc5-c552bc6a82da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P has never seen a tree but consistently hears testimony about them. Intuitively, we would judge that P's belief in the existence of trees might lack the same justification as someone who has direct perceptual experience.**, **It is implausible that testimony alone can justify belief in external objects without some direct or shared perceptual experience.**
