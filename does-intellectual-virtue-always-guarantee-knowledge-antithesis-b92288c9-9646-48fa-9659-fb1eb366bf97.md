# Does intellectual virtue always guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b92288c9-9646-48fa-9659-fb1eb366bf97
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person exhibits intellectual virtues such as open-mindedness and careful reasoning but still arrives at a false belief about a proposition. Intuitively, this indicates that possessing intellectual virtue does not always result in knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires the belief to be true, and intellectual virtue alone does not guarantee the truth of a belief. This suggests that intellectual virtue cannot be the sole determinant of knowledge.**
