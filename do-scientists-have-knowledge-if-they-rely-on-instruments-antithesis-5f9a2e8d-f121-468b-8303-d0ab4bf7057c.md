# Do scientists have knowledge if they rely on instruments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f9a2e8d-f121-468b-8303-d0ab4bf7057c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**A case in which a scientist uses a sophisticated but non-intellectual instrument to determine the truth of a theory is a case in which we would typically say the scientist knows the theory.**, **A belief in the validity of a scientific theory is often justified through the use of complex instruments, which are not themselves intellectual virtues. This suggests that knowledge can be mediated by non-intellectual aids.**
