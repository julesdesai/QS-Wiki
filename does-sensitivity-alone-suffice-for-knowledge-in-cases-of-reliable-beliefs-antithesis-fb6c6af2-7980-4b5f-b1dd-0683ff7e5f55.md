# Does sensitivity alone suffice for knowledge in cases of reliable beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb6c6af2-7980-4b5f-b1dd-0683ff7e5f55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a weather app has a long track record of accurate forecasts, and a person believes it will rain tomorrow based on this app. The belief turns out to be true, and the app's forecast is sensitive because if it were false, the person might not believe it.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should involve more than just sensitivity, suggesting it should also include elements like robustness across multiple scenarios or sources.**
