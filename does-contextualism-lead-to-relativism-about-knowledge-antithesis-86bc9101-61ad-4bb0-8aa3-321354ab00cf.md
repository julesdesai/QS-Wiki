# Does contextualism lead to relativism about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86bc9101-61ad-4bb0-8aa3-321354ab00cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Variable Justification](contextualism-with-variable-justification-synthesis-53c5a1dc-1391-4651-950b-f21901e771b6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge ascriptions should not reduce to mere relativistic statements depending on context. This principle together with contextually variable standards implies a threat of relativism to knowledge ascriptions.**, **Suppose knowledge claims are true in some contexts and false in others based on justification standards. Intuitively, this scenario suggests a relativistic view of knowledge, at odds with traditional epistemic objectivity.**
