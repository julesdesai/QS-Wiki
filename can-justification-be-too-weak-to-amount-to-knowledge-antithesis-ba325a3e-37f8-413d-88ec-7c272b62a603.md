# Can justification be too weak to amount to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba325a3e-37f8-413d-88ec-7c272b62a603
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Justification Theory](epistemic-justification-theory-synthesis-d0523946-79ba-4279-979e-9ba317ba7953.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a proposition based on weak justifications, like superstitions or hearsay. Intuitively, we either have to say that they know the proposition or that they don’t. If we say they know the proposition, it seems counterintuitive because the justification is inadequate. If we say they don’t know the proposition, then strong justification is implied to be necessary for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that any form of justification, regardless of its strength or basis, could count as knowledge.**
