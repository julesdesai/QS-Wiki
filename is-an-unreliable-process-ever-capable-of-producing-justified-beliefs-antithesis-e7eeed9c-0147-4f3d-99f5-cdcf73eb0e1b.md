# Is an unreliable process ever capable of producing justified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7eeed9c-0147-4f3d-99f5-cdcf73eb0e1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Knowledge Summary](reliabilist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-4cf24049-6407-4422-bb1c-63a1d2cbefdb.md)

## Content

**A person forms a belief about the weather from a coin flip if the coin always coincidentally matches the outcomes. Intuitively, this process leads to beliefs that aren't justified, even if true.**, **The reliability of the coin flip is by chance rather than through a meaningful justification.**
