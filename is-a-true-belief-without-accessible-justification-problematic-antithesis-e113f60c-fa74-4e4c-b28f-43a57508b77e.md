# Is a true belief without accessible justification problematic?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e113f60c-fa74-4e4c-b28f-43a57508b77e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-a1d061ab-4ce5-4544-95e5-a72c751db8d0.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where a person has a true belief but cannot access justification for it, it might feel like the person merely got lucky rather than gained knowledge.**, **It is plausible to think that knowledge should involve an understanding or access to the reasoning or evidence, otherwise the person might not genuinely know in an epistemically significant sense.**
