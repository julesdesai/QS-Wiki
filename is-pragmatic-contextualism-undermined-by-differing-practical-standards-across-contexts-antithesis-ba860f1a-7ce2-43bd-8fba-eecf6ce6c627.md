# Is pragmatic contextualism undermined by differing practical standards across contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba860f1a-7ce2-43bd-8fba-eecf6ce6c627
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**Practical standards and success can vary greatly depending on societal, cultural, or personal expectations influencing contexts.**, **Varying standards make it possible for a belief to be considered knowledge in one context, but not another, raising questions about the objective nature of knowledge.**, **If knowledge depends too heavily on shifting practical success criteria, it risks becoming inconsistent and subjective rather than a stable concept.**
