# Does true belief alone justify knowledge without further conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a74b446e-703d-442f-b126-6d7fb45479e4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose an instance where Frank believes a proposition that is true, but he has no justification for this belief other than a random guess. Intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge, as knowledge seems to require more than mere true belief.**, **It is plausible to assert that knowledge demands a justification or rational basis for belief, beyond randomly or coincidentally holding a true belief. True belief without justification lacks the certainty associated with knowledge.**
