# Can coherence alone establish knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 567ee102-e2da-45e6-b865-a13cf3191fac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-189c934d-40a0-41d9-b70b-1a7a0a9cc7c3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a group of people all have coherent and mutually supportive beliefs that are false. Intuitively, we would judge that these people do not have knowledge despite the coherence of their beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence adds to the justification of belief. This principle together with the possibility of a comprehensive but false belief system entails that coherence alone may not establish knowledge.**
