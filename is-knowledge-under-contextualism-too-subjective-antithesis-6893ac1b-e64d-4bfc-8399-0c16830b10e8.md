# Is Knowledge Under Contextualism Too Subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6893ac1b-e64d-4bfc-8399-0c16830b10e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-6d1ded2e-2e81-4f10-b6c8-db699d1664cc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be objective enough to be communicable and understandable across different people's perspectives.**, **Contextualism relies heavily on subjective experiences to form knowledge claims, which can vary tremendously from person to person.**, **The high degree of subjectivity implied makes shared understanding or communication about factual truths difficult, if not impossible, under contextualism.**
