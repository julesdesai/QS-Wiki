# How does phenomenalism deal with the variability and fallibility of human perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a3923e9-1fca-4078-937c-d90c37573df6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone misperceives a stick in water as bent, when it is actually straight, challenges the claim that reality is constructed from predictable sensory experiences.**, **It is implausible that a reality constructed from sensory experiences accounts for the common and varied errors in perception, highlighting the variability and fallibility of sensory data.**
