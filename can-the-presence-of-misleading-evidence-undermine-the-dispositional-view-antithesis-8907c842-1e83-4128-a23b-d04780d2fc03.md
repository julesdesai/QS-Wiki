# Can the presence of misleading evidence undermine the dispositional view?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8907c842-1e83-4128-a23b-d04780d2fc03
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that misleading evidence can prevent knowledge. This principle together with a scenario where a person has a dispositional ability but is systematically exposed to misleading evidence that causes false beliefs entails that dispositional ability is insufficient for knowledge**, **Consider a scenario where continuous misinformation leads a person with a strong dispositional ability to form false beliefs. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not possess knowledge despite their ability**
