# Is it coherent to claim both fixed standards and subject-sensitive espistemic evaluation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72dde12e-4fbc-4550-93c3-159170aaa645
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-fa9170ce-e58d-4311-9c0f-f4410b4d69a4.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where Grace and Hector evaluate their knowledge of future events differently due to personal stress levels, it seems their knowledge claims would vary. Intuitively, this challenges the coherence of having fixed yet subject-sensitive standards.**, **There is a plausible principle that epistemic evaluation consistency implies subject insensitivity in standards.**
