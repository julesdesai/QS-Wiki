# Is indirect realism compatible with phenomenological aspects of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 38a35aa0-5f89-4def-8fe4-85817c8d79b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Contextual Perception](indirect-realism-with-contextual-perception-synthesis-77e2d877-90d5-4b41-9238-09aba0491b6a.md)

## Content

**Phenomenological accounts emphasize the qualitative, subjective aspect of perception which appears directly available to consciousness.**, **Indirect realism suggests perception is filtered, abstracting away from these firsthand qualitative experiences.**, **There is a disconnect between phenomenology's emphasis on direct experience and the indirect, mediated perception posited by indirect realism.**
