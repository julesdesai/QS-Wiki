# Does contextualism get the balance of reason and evidence right?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44488b2b-2d88-4cb7-8c7f-b9542cb82c24
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**Contextualism asserts that both reason and evidence factors vary with context.**, **The balance of reason and evidence is critical, but contextualism does not define how these aspects should interact across different contexts.**, **This leaves a gap in understanding which contexts appropriately prioritize reason over evidence or vice versa.**
