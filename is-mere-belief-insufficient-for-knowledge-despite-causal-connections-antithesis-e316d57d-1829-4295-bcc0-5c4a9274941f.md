# Is mere belief insufficient for knowledge despite causal connections?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e316d57d-1829-4295-bcc0-5c4a9274941f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if they believe the proposition is a component of the view. However, consider a case where a person has a cultishly held belief based solely on repeated assertion by an authority with no causal interaction with the truth of the belief. Intuitively, we would judge that these beliefs, irrespective of any causal connection, do not constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a belief formed through non-critical acceptance without proper causal interaction with the truth could be considered knowledge.**
