# Does context-sensitivity lead to excessive subjectivity in knowledge attribution?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb1a314f-7f2f-48b4-a1dd-c801d65ab539
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where individuals apply context-sensitive standards leading to different conclusions about what is known. Intuitively, this results in excessive subjectivity and undermines objectivity in knowledge claims.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective knowledge claims should not vary simply because of individual interpretation of context. This principle together with the subjective nature of context-driven analysis suggests a conflict.**
