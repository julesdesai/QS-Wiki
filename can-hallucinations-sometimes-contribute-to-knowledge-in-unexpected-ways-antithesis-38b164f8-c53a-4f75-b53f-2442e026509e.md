# Can hallucinations sometimes contribute to knowledge in unexpected ways?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 38b164f8-c53a-4f75-b53f-2442e026509e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone hallucinates a barking dog and moves to the window, only to see an actual barking dog outside. Intuitively, the hallucination, although initially misleading, has contributed to gaining knowledge about the dog's presence.**, **It is implausible that all hallucinations lack any epistemic grounding since they can sometimes lead to genuine discoveries or insights about reality.**
