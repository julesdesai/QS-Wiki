# Does memory allow us to grasp abstract concepts or just influence perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 92ad27ef-3be3-44f1-95eb-627b574f41a7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Phenomenalism Allowing Memory Role](revised-phenomenalism-allowing-memory-role-synthesis-5fb5abbe-6adb-4855-a476-25c8a91d11a9.md)

## Content

**Abstract concepts often require understanding beyond mere perception. This condition together with the reliance on perceptual experiences, even when influenced by memory, suggests a limitation in grasping purely abstract ideas.**, **Consider an individual's attempt to understand a complex mathematical theorem which is not directly perceptible. Intuitively, relying solely on memory-influenced perceptions seems insufficient for such abstract understanding.**
