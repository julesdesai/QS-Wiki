# Can knowledge exist in cases where all alternatives are indistinguishable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d7148b7-6d3a-43d7-84dd-2dc337310c4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-5db4d183-a5a9-469a-b4ff-d831c6a59a11.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which all relevant alternatives are indistinguishable from each other in an observable way. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge is present due to belief and truth, or that it is absent due to inability to distinguish alternates. If we say knowledge is present, then the ruling out of alternatives seems unnecessary. If we say knowledge is absent, then common knowledge claims become problematic.**
