# Can imagination as a virtue lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35e9c67d-a6ec-4ec3-ac66-dbb4cb2b29a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person arrives at a true belief through imaginative speculation, which is considered an intellectual virtue. Intuitively, we would not judge that this amounts to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that the process of forming a belief must be reliably connected to truth for it to be knowledge. This principle, when combined with imaginative speculation leading to true belief, suggests these are not reliably connected and thus do not result in knowledge.**
