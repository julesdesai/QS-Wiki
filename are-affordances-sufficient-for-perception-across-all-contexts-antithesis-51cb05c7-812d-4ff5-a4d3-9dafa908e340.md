# Are affordances sufficient for perception across all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 51cb05c7-812d-4ff5-a4d3-9dafa908e340
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Ecological Direct Perception Theory](ecological-direct-perception-theory-synthesis-73031ffe-0106-4b3d-af38-bdf2623ababa.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that affordances alone account for perception in highly abstract or novel contexts, such as understanding artworks or unfamiliar scientific phenomena.**, **Consider a scenario where individuals encounter an object with no known affordances, like an abstract sculpture. Intuitively, we would judge that perception involves interpretation and engagement beyond mere affordance detection, potentially involving internal conceptual frameworks.**
