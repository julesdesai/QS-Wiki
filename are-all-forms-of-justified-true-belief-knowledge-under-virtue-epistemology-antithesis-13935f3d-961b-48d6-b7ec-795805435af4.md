# Are all forms of justified true belief knowledge under virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 13935f3d-961b-48d6-b7ec-795805435af4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who acquires a belief based on reliable testimony, and that belief is true, but the person lacks an understanding of why the testimony is reliable. Intuitively, we might doubt that they have knowledge in the fullest sense.**, **A belief acquired without an understanding or active engagement of intellectual virtues does not seem to be justified in a truly virtuous manner.**
