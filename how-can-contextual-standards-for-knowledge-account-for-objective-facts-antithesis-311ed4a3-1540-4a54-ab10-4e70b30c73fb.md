# How can contextual standards for knowledge account for objective facts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 311ed4a3-1540-4a54-ab10-4e70b30c73fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where an objective fact about the world remains constant, yet people within different contexts make diverging knowledge claims about this fact. Intuitively, knowledge should align with objective reality rather than subjective contextual standards.**, **A standard view in epistemology holds that knowledge should track reality. The idea that knowledge is dependent on fluctuating contextual standards seems to undermine the connection between knowledge and objective facts.**
