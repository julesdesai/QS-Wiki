# Can unreliable processes sometimes lead to true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8541caae-a29c-43f5-a545-1ca18b3ac2e6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fec1f444-460d-4835-9da9-5fd5405f3ea1.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person forms a true belief through an unreliable process, such as reading a notoriously inaccurate news source. Intuitively, we would judge that the true belief obtained this way does not amount to knowledge.**, **An unreliable cognitive process can still accidentally produce true beliefs, which means true belief does not guarantee knowledge if the process is unreliable.**
