# Is it problematic that the tracking theory doesn't account for experiential knowledge persons obtain irrespective of mapping divergent scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8fe974e2-80c2-4858-b763-5b11a6822963
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Consider individuals whose beliefs about immediate perceptual experiences are accurate. Suppose under stress, these experiences might present differently, yet the consistency and veracity of typical beliefs corroborate knowledge claims notwithstanding potential divergence in alternative scenarios.**, **Experiential knowledge, while situationally bounded, is accepted through recognized accuracy, suggesting the tracking approach's constraints on experiential truth might be conversely limiting.**
