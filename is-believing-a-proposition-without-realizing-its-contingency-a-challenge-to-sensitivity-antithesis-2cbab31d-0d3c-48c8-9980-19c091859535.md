# Is believing a proposition without realizing its contingency a challenge to sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2cbab31d-0d3c-48c8-9980-19c091859535
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-68f75ad1-a4e1-40d8-ae80-33e70d924463.md)

## Content

**A person believes a proposition about a scientific phenomenon, not realizing its truth depends on contingent conditions (e.g., current laws of physics). In alternate conditions where the proposition is false, the person maintains belief due to unawareness of this dependency. Intuitively, this oversight challenges the notion of sensitivity.**
