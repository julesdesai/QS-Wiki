## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **The relevance of a subject's practical interests does not alter the truth-conditions of a knowledge claim**, **Context affects whether knowledge is ascribed, but not whether it is true that someone knows something**
