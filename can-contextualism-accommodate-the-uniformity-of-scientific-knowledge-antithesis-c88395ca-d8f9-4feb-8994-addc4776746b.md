# Can contextualism accommodate the uniformity of scientific knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c88395ca-d8f9-4feb-8994-addc4776746b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Variable Justification](contextualism-with-variable-justification-synthesis-53c5a1dc-1391-4651-950b-f21901e771b6.md)

## Content

**Scientific knowledge is generally considered objective and stable, regardless of context. It is implausible that the standards for justification in science should vary with context.**, **Suppose a scientific theory is knowledge in one context but not in another due to contextual justification standards. Intuitively, this undermines the uniformity and reliability of scientific knowledge.**
