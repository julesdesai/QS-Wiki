# Is truth creditability inherently tied to intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb689480-88c2-499c-9464-9536e0831f27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d110ebcb-cebc-4b41-b013-8e3cca593b6d.md)

## Content

**A case where someone uses a reliable but uncomprehended method, such as a predictive algorithm, to form true beliefs without understanding or employing intellectual virtues challenges the tying of truth to cognitive abilities**, **People relying solely on instinctive judgments or short-term memory may form true beliefs that aren't attributable to long-term cognitive nature, questioning credit to intellectual virtues**, **Blind trust in a reliable informant can lead to true and justified belief, even if it does not involve personal intellectual engagement, undermining attributability of truth to the knower’s abilities**
