# What if justification is mistakenly rooted in intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 185a0519-f78c-444b-8dd5-6b4182373f6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Person K develops a belief about a legal principle based solely on their own overconfidence and charisma, assuming their intuitive grasp is always correct. Despite their belief being true, intuitively their confidence doesn't equate to valid justification or knowledge.**, **The presumption that confidence or similar traits might serve as intellectual virtues suggests a flawed perception of justification, where a false sense of intellectual virtue might lead to erroneous claims of knowledge.**
