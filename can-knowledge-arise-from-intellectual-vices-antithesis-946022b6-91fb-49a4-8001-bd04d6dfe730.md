# Can knowledge arise from intellectual vices?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 946022b6-91fb-49a4-8001-bd04d6dfe730
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all true beliefs arising from sources that are not considered intellectually virtuous should not count as knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief through methods usually deemed unreliable, such as guessing. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might still judge that they know the proposition if their track record is accurate enough.**
