# Can different conversational contexts be reliably distinguished?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5b3664e-03ee-42ca-a711-ae59ee03bb18
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-70123d28-f52a-42a2-8d85-6f92ac2f5bd1.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone is engaged in a casual conversation and then a structured interview about the same knowledge claim. The boundaries and effects of the conversational shift are often unclear, leading to confusion about the justification of a belief.**, **It is implausible that most individuals accurately perceive distinct conversational contexts in which their knowledge or justification supposedly changes.**
