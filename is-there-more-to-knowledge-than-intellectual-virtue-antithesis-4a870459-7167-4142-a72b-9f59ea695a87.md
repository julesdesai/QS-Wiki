# Is there more to knowledge than intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4a870459-7167-4142-a72b-9f59ea695a87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cfcf3dee-48b2-4ff8-855e-aba5061f4a51.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that knowing requires more than just a belief formed through intellectual virtue.**, **There is a plausible principle that social and contextual elements, such as testimony or societal validation, also play crucial roles in acquiring knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which an individual forms a belief through intellectual virtue, but without corroborating evidence from others that is typical in their community. Intuitively, this lack undermines our confidence in claiming they know the proposition.**
