# Does contextual coherentism handle self-referential beliefs consistently?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 872e7722-88dd-4ce4-af78-6a3b6685e98d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Coherentism](contextualist-coherentism-synthesis-156207e7-3c9d-49a2-8bb7-7097c1d027cd.md)

## Content

**Consider a context in which a person believes "I am always wrong" based on perceived past errors. In this context, the belief coheres with their other beliefs.**, **Coherence involves alignment of beliefs with contextual information, but a belief that claims its own falsehood cannot be true if it is coherent, which is paradoxical.**
