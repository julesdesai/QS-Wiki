# Does phenomenalism accommodate the notion of objective truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 982456e8-d781-4cec-9863-dfdeb2021c72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which different observers have conflicting sensory experiences of the same event, such as a traffic accident. Intuitively, we would judge that there is an objective truth about what happened, independent of personal experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is objective and not contingent upon individual sensory experiences. This principle together with the concept of reality as constructed from subjective experiences undermines the ability of phenomenalism to accommodate objective truth.**
