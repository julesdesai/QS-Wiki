# Is knowledge subjective or objective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28a64baf-ae07-4b06-8c68-c888443d2069
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-c00af758-5073-4931-9585-2f560dddd897.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be objective and independent of subjective consensus. This principle together with requiring inter-subjective agreement on perceptual experiences suggests that knowledge becomes a subjective consensus rather than an objective truth.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a group agrees on a perceptual experience incorrectly due to a collective bias. Intuitively, either their agreement constitutes knowledge, or it does not. If it does, then knowledge becomes fragile and potentially erroneous. If it does not, requiring inter-subjective agreement undermines objective knowledge.**
