# Does justification in knowledge really require causation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f3e448e-36c8-41a6-95ac-a54b081c99a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where someone's belief is justified independently, perhaps through strong justification techniques like deduction, with no causal link to the actual fact. Intuitively, this should still qualify as knowledge.**, **A justified belief could be grounded in strong independent evidence, such as mathematical proof, and be considered knowledge even absent a direct causal link to the actual fact.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification in knowledge does not always necessitate causation, especially where alternative forms of justification are robust.**
