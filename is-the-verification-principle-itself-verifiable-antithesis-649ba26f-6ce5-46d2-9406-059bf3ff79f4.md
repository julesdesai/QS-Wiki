# Is the verification principle itself verifiable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 649ba26f-6ce5-46d2-9406-059bf3ff79f4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verificationism](verificationism-synthesis-742a7e84-42a2-4d13-8c35-af2e03d93250.md)

## Content

**The statement "A statement only has meaning if it can be empirically verified" is itself not empirically verifiable**, **If consistent application of the verification principle requires a statement to have meaning only if it is empirically verifiable, then the principle itself would be meaningless by its own criteria**, **It is implausible to adopt a principle that cannot stand by its own standards of meaningfulness**
