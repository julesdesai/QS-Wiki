# Can coherence ever account for epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6b2f89d-7f27-4db7-ae3a-a0bf0f76a792
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Temporal Coherentism](temporal-coherentism-synthesis-f4dfebfd-2f02-4b67-a5dd-1a5ab136482a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a coherent belief system due to coincidental factors rather than systematic reasoning. Intuitively, we would have to say that this person’s beliefs are unjustified despite coherence. If we say coherence is sufficient for justification, then epistemic luck would inappropriately satisfy the criteria.**
