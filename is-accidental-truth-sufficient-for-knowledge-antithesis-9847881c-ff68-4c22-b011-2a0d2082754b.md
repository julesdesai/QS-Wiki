# Is accidental truth sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9847881c-ff68-4c22-b011-2a0d2082754b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person P believes there will be a train at 5 PM due to an old schedule, but the train was rescheduled without P's knowledge and only accidentally matches 5 PM because of an unrelated delay. Intuitively, we would judge that P does not have knowledge, as the truth is accidental.**, **There is a plausible principle that accidental truths cannot constitute knowledge. This principle together with the need for a causal link suggests that true belief must not be merely accidental.**, **It is implausible that mere coincidence suffices for knowledge if the relevant facts causally leading to belief are unrelated.**
