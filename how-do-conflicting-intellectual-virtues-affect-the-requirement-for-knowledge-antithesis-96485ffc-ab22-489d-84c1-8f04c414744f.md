# How do conflicting intellectual virtues affect the requirement for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96485ffc-ab22-489d-84c1-8f04c414744f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two intellectual virtues, such as open-mindedness and critical thinking, lead a person to form sharply opposed beliefs about the same proposition. In such a scenario, it is unclear which belief, if any, results from intellectual virtue.**, **It is implausible that knowledge could require belief formation from intellectual virtue if multiple conflicting virtues can both apply to the same proposition, because it would undermine the stability and reliability required for knowledge.**
