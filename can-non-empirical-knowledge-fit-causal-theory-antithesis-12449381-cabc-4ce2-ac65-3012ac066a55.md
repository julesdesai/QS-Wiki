# Can non-empirical knowledge fit causal theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 12449381-cabc-4ce2-ac65-3012ac066a55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Consider knowledge of moral values acquired through philosophical reflection rather than causal interaction. Intuitively, such knowledge seems genuine despite the lack of a causal link to facts.**, **Moral or philosophical beliefs may be justified by non-empirical means and represent true knowledge without satisfying causal criteria. This challenges the applicability of causal theory.**, **There is a plausible principle that non-empirical knowledge does not always involve a causal connection to facts, suggesting the theory’s limitation.**
