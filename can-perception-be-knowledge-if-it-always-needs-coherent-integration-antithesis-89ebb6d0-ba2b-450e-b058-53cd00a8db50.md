# Can perception be knowledge if it always needs coherent integration?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89ebb6d0-ba2b-450e-b058-53cd00a8db50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism](coherentism-synthesis-47013a86-4e6f-4466-86d9-11bbc22ccdaf.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a lightning strike for the first time with no existing beliefs about lightning. Intuitively, their perception alone should provide knowledge, even though coherent integration with existing beliefs is unavailable.**, **It is implausible that perception should always require integration with existing coherent belief systems to contribute to knowledge.**
