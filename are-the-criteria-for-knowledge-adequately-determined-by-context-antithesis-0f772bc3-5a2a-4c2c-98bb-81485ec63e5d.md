# Are the criteria for knowledge adequately determined by context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0f772bc3-5a2a-4c2c-98bb-81485ec63e5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes they know a piece of information based on contextually low criteria for knowledge. Intuitively, one might judge that their belief is not genuine knowledge if the criteria can shift so dramatically in another context.**, **It is implausible that variable criteria for knowledge, which depend heavily on context, can guarantee that true knowledge is being identified across contexts.**
