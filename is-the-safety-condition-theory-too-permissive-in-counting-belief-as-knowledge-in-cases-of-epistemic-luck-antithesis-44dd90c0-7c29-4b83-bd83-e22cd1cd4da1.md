# Is the Safety Condition Theory too permissive in counting belief as knowledge in cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44dd90c0-7c29-4b83-bd83-e22cd1cd4da1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-89579919-7815-4da3-898a-02510d3efc48.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately predicts a coin will land heads due to a lucky streak. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person has knowledge or that they do not due to epistemic luck. If we say the person has knowledge, then we are ignoring the role of luck. If we say they do not have knowledge, then the Safety Condition Theory mischaracterizes knowledge.**
