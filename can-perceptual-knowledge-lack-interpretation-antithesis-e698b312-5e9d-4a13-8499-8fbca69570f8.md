# Can perceptual knowledge lack interpretation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e698b312-5e9d-4a13-8499-8fbca69570f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-04d59a43-df5d-4ff1-bc30-b363f66f9a03.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees rain and decides to use an umbrella. In this scenario, intuitively, the person perceives it is raining without requiring further interpretation of the sensory data.**, **There is a principle that some perceptual knowledge is straightforward and does not require interpretive mediation to be considered accurate.**
