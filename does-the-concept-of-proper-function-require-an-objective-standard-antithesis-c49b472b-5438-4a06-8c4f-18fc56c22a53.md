# Does the concept of proper function require an objective standard?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c49b472b-5438-4a06-8c4f-18fc56c22a53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**In a hypothetical culture where cognitive faculties operate based on unconventional logics or standards different from our own, true beliefs might still arise. Intuitively, we might still attribute knowledge to these beliefs despite differing standards of "proper functioning."**, **A principle of epistemology requires an objective standard for 'proper function' to determine knowledge. This principle, when combined with the variability of cultural cognitive standards, challenges the necessity of universal standards for defining proper function.**, **It is implausible to claim that an absolute objective standard must govern the notion of proper function across all cultures and contexts to account for true beliefs as knowledge.**
