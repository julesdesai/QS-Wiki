# Does truth-tracking theory account for the role of luck in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 59ee7433-714e-43a4-80a3-858109c7aa92
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person correctly believes a true proposition purely due to luck and maintains the belief regardless of its truth in nearby worlds, thus failing to track truth in a systematic way. Intuitively, the person wouldn't possess knowledge as their belief is not anchored in reliable truth-tracking but instead in chance.**, **A principle suggesting that knowledge must be insulated from the influence of luck could imply that truth-tracking theory is inadequate due to its neglect of this factor.**
