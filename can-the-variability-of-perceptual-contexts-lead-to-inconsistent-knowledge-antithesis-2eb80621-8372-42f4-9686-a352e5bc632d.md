# Can the variability of perceptual contexts lead to inconsistent knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2eb80621-8372-42f4-9686-a352e5bc632d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Contextualism Summary](perceptual-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9fb6be1-a4e1-46bf-91f2-7fc63c3abb0c.md)

## Content

**Changes in perceptual contexts, such as environmental shifts, health changes, or emotional states, cause variance in perception over time.**, **If knowledge depends on contextually influenced perception, then what is known may change or become inconsistent as contexts shift.**, **If perception and consequently knowledge are subject to such variability, this undermines the stability of knowledge claims.**
