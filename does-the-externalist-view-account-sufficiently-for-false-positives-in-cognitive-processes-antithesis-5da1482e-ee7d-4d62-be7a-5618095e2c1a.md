# Does the externalist view account sufficiently for false positives in cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5da1482e-ee7d-4d62-be7a-5618095e2c1a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliance on External Factors](externalism-with-reliance-on-external-factors-synthesis-94d45e40-e833-458e-8cba-8623e44201d6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a case where a generally reliable cognitive process produces a true belief due to chance rather than its typical function. Intuitively, the accidental truth does not align with what we consider justification.**, **Reliability allowing for occasional false positives without discernment between justified beliefs and accidental truths questions its sufficiency as justification.**
