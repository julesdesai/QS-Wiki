# Does justification depend solely on contextual factors without being arbitrary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 747ad30f-b0c0-45d2-bc56-7b546dc85ee8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that justification of beliefs is entirely context-dependent because this could potentially lead to arbitrary judgments about what is justified.**, **Suppose we have a case where two contexts with different criteria for justification are applied to the same set of beliefs, making it hard to determine which context correctly justifies the beliefs.**, **In such scenarios, intuitively, we would judge that justification should have some stable criteria, independent of context, to avoid arbitrariness.**
