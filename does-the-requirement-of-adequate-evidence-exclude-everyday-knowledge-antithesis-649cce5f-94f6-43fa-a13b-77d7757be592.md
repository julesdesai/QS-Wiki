# Does the requirement of adequate evidence exclude everyday knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 649cce5f-94f6-43fa-a13b-77d7757be592
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View](evidentialist-view-synthesis-6f63629b-c949-4605-9acc-d91a2708886a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone accurately reports the color of the sky by simply looking at it. Intuitively, we judge that this is knowledge, despite the lack of rigorous evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that everyday knowledge does not always require strong evidence but rather depends on simpler, direct observation. This principle, coupled with observational scenarios, indicates that sufficient evidence as per evidentialist standards may not align with how we typically attribute knowledge.**
