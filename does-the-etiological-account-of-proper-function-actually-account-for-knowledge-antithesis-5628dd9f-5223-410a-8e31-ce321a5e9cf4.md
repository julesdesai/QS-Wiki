# Does the etiological account of proper function actually account for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5628dd9f-5223-410a-8e31-ce321a5e9cf4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where cognitive faculties produce true beliefs, but the individual has inherited a genetic defect that alters cognitive function. We would intuitively judge the person as lacking knowledge.**, **Justification based on reliable truth-producing faculties presupposes a reliable design plan. If the design plan includes genetic defects, the concept of reliability is undermined.**, **A principle of proper functionality implies that any deviation from intended cognitive design results in a lack of knowledge. If knowledge is present despite defects, proper functionalism is inadequate.**
