# How do justified true beliefs interact with omitted false assumptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f1b3fde-4a7c-4d9a-b4ef-d3930a7d4f96
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma Theory](no-false-lemma-theory-synthesis-4a11ee42-2be9-4836-9dd6-d365881bb277.md)

## Content

**A justified true belief can occur without known reliance on false assumptions, as when a detective infers a suspect’s guilt from several pieces of evidence, none of which directly relate to hidden false assumptions. Here, the intuitiveness of knowledge is questioned when omissions of faulty assumptions are indirect.**, **If omitted indirect falsehoods play no genuine part in the justification, the clarity of distinction between influence and independent reasoning comes into play, undermining the knowledge claim.**
