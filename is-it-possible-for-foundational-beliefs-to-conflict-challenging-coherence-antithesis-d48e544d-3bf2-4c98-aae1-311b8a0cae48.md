# Is it possible for foundational beliefs to conflict, challenging coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d48e544d-3bf2-4c98-aae1-311b8a0cae48
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Foundationalism](epistemic-foundationalism-synthesis-e90eff9d-4b30-41a2-b4e3-4bbc38385542.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have foundational beliefs that logically contradict each other. Intuitively, we must say that they cannot both serve as reliable foundations for knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires coherence among beliefs. This principle together with contradictory foundational beliefs entails that coherence is necessary for knowledge.**
