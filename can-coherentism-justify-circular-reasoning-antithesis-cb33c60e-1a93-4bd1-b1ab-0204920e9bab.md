# Can coherentism justify circular reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cb33c60e-1a93-4bd1-b1ab-0204920e9bab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a belief that is justified within a coherent system of beliefs that includes circular dependencies, such as believing proposition A because of B, and B because of A. Intuitively, we recognize that circular reasoning does not provide genuine justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that logical circularity undermines genuine justification even within a coherent set of beliefs.**
