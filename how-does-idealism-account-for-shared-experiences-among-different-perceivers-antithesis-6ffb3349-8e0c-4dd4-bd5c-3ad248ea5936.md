# How does idealism account for shared experiences among different perceivers?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ffb3349-8e0c-4dd4-bd5c-3ad248ea5936
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Idealism](idealism-synthesis-6a8ea4b4-8cf6-44ff-98fb-cf08805f781d.md)

## Content

**Suppose different individuals in different locations experience the same event, such as witnessing an eclipse, without coordinating their perceptions. Intuitively, this shared experience suggests an underlying reality independent of individual mental phenomena.**, **Shared perceptual experiences among multiple individuals challenge the notion that reality is primarily mental, as it implies an external consistency not reliant on personal mental constructs.**
