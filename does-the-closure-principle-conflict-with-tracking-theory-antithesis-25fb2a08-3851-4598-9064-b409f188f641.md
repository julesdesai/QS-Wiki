# Does the closure principle conflict with tracking theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 25fb2a08-3851-4598-9064-b409f188f641
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism with Nozick's Tracking Theory](epistemic-externalism-with-nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-99a4169f-6bac-4b5a-b46b-52fe3916b903.md)

## Content

**The closure principle states that if someone knows a proposition and knows this proposition entails another, then they should know the other proposition.**, **Consider a situation where someone knows they have hands, but doesn't know they aren't a handless brain in a vat.**, **Tracking theory implies one cannot know they are not a brain in a vat, as beliefs don't track in such skeptical scenarios, conflicting with closure.**
