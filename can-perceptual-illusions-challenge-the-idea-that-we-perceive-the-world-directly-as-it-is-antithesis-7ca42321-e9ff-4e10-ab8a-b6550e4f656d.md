# Can perceptual illusions challenge the idea that we perceive the world directly as it is?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ca42321-e9ff-4e10-ab8a-b6550e4f656d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-25d0b7e3-6f66-46c3-a45d-bb41c1dbe70b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a straight stick appears bent when partially submerged in water due to refraction. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that our perception of the stick as bent is not a direct representation of its real, straight condition.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptions should accurately reflect the properties of objects to allow direct perception. This principle together with the illusion scenario entails that our perceptions are sometimes intermediated by conditions like refraction.**
