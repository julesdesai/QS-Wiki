# Can a belief be useful yet not be justified or true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b81f14fd-4872-4d8c-b239-afe81316be67
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge with Justification](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-with-justification-synthesis-570c8b94-2cc9-4f79-b923-9eacb64ddf74.md)

## Content

**A person believes that wearing a lucky charm increases their chances of success; this belief is not justified or true, yet it might boost their confidence and therefore be useful.**, **Intuitively, usefulness of a belief does not necessarily mean it is justified or true, challenging the idea that useful beliefs are knowledge.**
