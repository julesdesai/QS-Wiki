# How is an infinite chain of reasons different from circular reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider an infinite regress of reasons, where each belief is supported by another without end. Intuitively, if each belief does not truly advance understanding but merely points to another belief, the regress seems akin to circular reasoning, where beliefs ultimately just refer back to themselves.**, **A plausible principle is that valid justification must advance understanding, rather than simply cycling through beliefs without progress. This principle, together with the structure of an infinite regress, suggests that the regress does not advance understanding in a meaningful way.**
