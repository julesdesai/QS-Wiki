# Can false justification lead to true belief without constituting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 00941a0d-3ec3-47b3-87b7-67c40a635df5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who believes they will win a lottery because they've dreamt about it, and, by sheer chance, they do win. The belief is true, but it stems from unreliable and irrelevant justificatory grounds. Intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge, as the justification is unrelated to the outcome.**, **Even when the belief is coincidentally true, relying on false justification disqualifies it from being considered knowledge.**
