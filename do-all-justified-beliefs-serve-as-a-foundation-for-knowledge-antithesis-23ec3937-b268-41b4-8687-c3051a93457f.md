# Do all justified beliefs serve as a foundation for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23ec3937-b268-41b4-8687-c3051a93457f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-basic-beliefs-synthesis-db3b35bf-6f35-4ce4-82b6-698bdde0b345.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle stating not all justified beliefs necessarily contribute to a foundation. Such beliefs may lack relevance or significance.**, **Consider a scenario where someone justifies a belief based on personal anecdote while broader data contradicts it. Intuitively, this belief does not serve as a substantial foundation.**, **It is implausible that every justified belief plays a meaningful role in supporting the entirety of one's knowledge structure.**
