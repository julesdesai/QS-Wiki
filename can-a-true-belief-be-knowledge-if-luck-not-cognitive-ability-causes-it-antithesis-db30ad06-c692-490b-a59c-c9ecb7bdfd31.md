# Can a true belief be knowledge if luck, not cognitive ability, causes it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db30ad06-c692-490b-a59c-c9ecb7bdfd31
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-193f50ad-c71a-4e26-bf9b-21d5bbf78043.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person P forms a true belief that there are birds outside because they heard birds chirping, but it turns out the sound came from a hidden recording. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might judge that P's belief is not knowledge, despite being true.**, **A belief being true solely due to external factors like luck, without credit to cognitive ability, challenges the notion that knowledge requires credit for the truth.**
