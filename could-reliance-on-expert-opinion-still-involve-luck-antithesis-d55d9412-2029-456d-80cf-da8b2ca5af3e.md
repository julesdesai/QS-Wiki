# Could reliance on expert opinion still involve luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d55d9412-2029-456d-80cf-da8b2ca5af3e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scientist forms a true belief based on expert consensus, but the consensus is coincidentally corrected just after the consultation due to a newfound evidence error. Intuitively, reliance on expert opinion doesn’t eliminate luck completely from forming true beliefs.**, **While intellectual virtues advocate for informed reasoning, the unpredictable correction of expert consensus suggests that epistemic luck cannot be fully eliminated.**
