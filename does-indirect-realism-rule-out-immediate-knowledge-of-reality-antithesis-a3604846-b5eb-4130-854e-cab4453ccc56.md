# Does indirect realism rule out immediate knowledge of reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3604846-b5eb-4130-854e-cab4453ccc56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person directly intuits a fact without any intermediary cognitive processing. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person has immediate knowledge of that fact.**, **There is a plausible principle that if someone has immediate knowledge of a fact, they do not rely on intermediaries or representational models. This principle together with the indirect realism view entails that immediate knowledge of reality is not possible.**
