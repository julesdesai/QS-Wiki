# Can truth alone be enough for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eeb21b12-318b-47dc-8ff7-cd4f847c3457
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-687e35ad-b9eb-49bd-a730-d8bc9269ea3a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that true belief must also be justified to count as knowledge, not just true. This alone is insufficient for claiming that a person knows a proposition if the proposition is true.**, **Consider a scenario where a person accidentally guesses a true fact. Intuitively, we would not say they have knowledge of the fact simply because their guess was true.**
