# Does phenomenalism contradict scientific objectivity?

## Content

**A case in which scientific instruments detect particles that are not observable directly by the senses still contributes to our objective understanding of the world.**, **It is implausible that objective reality, as described by science, depends entirely on direct sensory experiences, given that much scientific understanding involves entities beyond direct perception.**
