# Can reliability account for understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** beb33744-36d7-4f8e-a62c-baef7188efb7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person reliably forms true beliefs but lacks any coherent comprehension of why those beliefs are true. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge requires some level of understanding.**, **Suppose a person can correctly predict weather patterns through a reliable algorithm they do not understand. It is implausible that this person possesses genuine knowledge of meteorology.**
