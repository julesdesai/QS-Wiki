# Does safety require implausible levels of accuracy in predicting possible worlds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6dd3c81e-af5e-4c65-b578-33e029519c1d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Intellectual Virtue Epistemology](safe-intellectual-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-f848b9b7-8a1e-463c-8e96-66e43c041eac.md)

## Content

**A belief is safe if in nearby possible situations it remains true.**, **Creating an accurate account of all possible worlds in which a belief might be tested is implausible and overly demanding.**, **If safety requires predicting numerous potential situations, the standard for safety might be unrealistically high.**
