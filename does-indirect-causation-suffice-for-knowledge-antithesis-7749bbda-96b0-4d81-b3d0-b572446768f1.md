# Does indirect causation suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7749bbda-96b0-4d81-b3d0-b572446768f1
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-thesis-0c6eaf7b-9d38-4e55-b770-1b9de7b33981.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition because of a chain of reasoning that begins with a perception of a directly related fact, yet they remain unaware of this relation. Intuitively, we would judge that indirect causation suffices for knowledge.**, **Assertions of indirect causal links highlight how deeply indirect causes challenge the necessity of direct causation in knowledge attribution.**
