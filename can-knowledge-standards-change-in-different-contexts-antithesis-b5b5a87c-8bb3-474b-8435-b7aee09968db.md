# Can knowledge standards change in different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b5b5a87c-8bb3-474b-8435-b7aee09968db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Knowledge Summary](invariantist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-7e971fef-e35c-4ec9-93e0-84ae5864f440.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person tells the truth about a scientific fact while speaking at a conference versus during a casual conversation. Intuitively, listeners may feel the person is more knowledgeable in the conference setting.**, **There is a plausible principle that the interpretation of a person's knowledge can vary depending on the context in which they express belief. This principle suggests that knowing is not invariant across different situations.**
