# Knowledge as Contextual Foundationalism

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does context allow for reliable differentiation of true belief?](does-context-allow-for-reliable-differentiation-of-true-belief-antithesis-1db61781-7dc1-4595-bda5-f4e22737a156.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is grounded in foundational beliefs that are justified given the context**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is grounded in contextually justified foundational beliefs**
