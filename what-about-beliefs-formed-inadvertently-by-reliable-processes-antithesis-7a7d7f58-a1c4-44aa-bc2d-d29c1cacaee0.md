# What about beliefs formed inadvertently by reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a7d7f58-a1c4-44aa-bc2d-d29c1cacaee0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is unaware of a reliable process influencing their belief formation, such as subliminal perception, which leads to a true belief. Intuitively, we might not attribute knowledge to the person despite the reliability of the process because the belief was formed inadvertently.**, **This questions the sufficiency of reliable cognitive processes in granting knowledge when the belief is not the result of intentional cognitive conduct.**
