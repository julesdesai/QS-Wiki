# How does mere belief ensure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a60fabf6-deea-47ed-85ae-ee35de6b6647
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which someone holds a belief due to cultural conditioning and social pressure, yet the belief is true. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that the person's mere belief does not constitute knowledge due to a lack of understanding.**, **There is a plausible principle that belief alone can be influenced by many factors unrelated to knowledge, such as peer pressure.**, **It is implausible that simply having a belief ensures that it qualifies as knowledge without further justification.**
