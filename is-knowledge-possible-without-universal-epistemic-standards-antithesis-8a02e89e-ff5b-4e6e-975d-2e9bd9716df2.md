# Is knowledge possible without universal epistemic standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a02e89e-ff5b-4e6e-975d-2e9bd9716df2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Suppose epistemic standards vary entirely from one person to another, making no standard universally applicable. Intuitively, the notion of shared or communicable knowledge becomes questionable, as there would be no common ground to assess truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that shared knowledge presupposes some level of universal standards for evaluating claims. If epistemic standards are not universal, this principle indicates a fundamental challenge to achieving mutual understanding.**, **If no universal epistemic standards exist, it becomes implausible to claim any consistent form of knowledge that can be mutually recognized or verified across individuals.**
