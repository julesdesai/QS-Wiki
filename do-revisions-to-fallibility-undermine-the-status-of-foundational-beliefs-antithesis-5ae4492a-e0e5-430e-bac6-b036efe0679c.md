# Do revisions to fallibility undermine the status of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ae4492a-e0e5-430e-bac6-b036efe0679c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Suppose foundational beliefs are continually revised based on new evidence, leading to changes in what is accepted as true. This ongoing revision raises doubts about the constancy and reliability required for such beliefs to serve as an unshakeable foundation for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a constantly shifting foundational belief could serve as a solid basis for knowledge when infallibility is unattainable.**
