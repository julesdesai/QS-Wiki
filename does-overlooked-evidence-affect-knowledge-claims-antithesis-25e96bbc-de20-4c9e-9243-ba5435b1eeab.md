# Does overlooked evidence affect knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 25e96bbc-de20-4c9e-9243-ba5435b1eeab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No defeaters theory of knowledge](no-defeaters-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-293021ef-516a-4641-9c48-087d1223e339.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person believes a proposition based on strong evidence and it is indeed true. Nonetheless, there exists additional evidence, unknown to the person, that would conflict with this evidence if found. Intuitively, possessing all relevant evidence seems essential for knowledge.**, **In such a scenario, there is an impression that knowledge requires ruling out all possibilities of conflicting information.**
