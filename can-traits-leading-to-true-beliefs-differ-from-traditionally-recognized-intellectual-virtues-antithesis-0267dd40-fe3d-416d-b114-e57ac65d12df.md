# Can traits leading to true beliefs differ from traditionally recognized intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0267dd40-fe3d-416d-b114-e57ac65d12df
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**An intellectual virtue is defined as a trait contributing to the formation of true beliefs, yet intuitively beneficial traits like luck may lead to true beliefs without typical virtues.**, **Consider a scenario where a person correctly arrives at knowledge not through intellectual virtue but through sheer luck. In such a scenario, it is intuitive to claim knowledge despite this lack of traditional virtue.**
