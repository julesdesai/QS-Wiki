# Can a proposition be known based solely on belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f88f054f-03b9-49f5-b6ef-87f203c6b741
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that mere belief does not account for the internal justification required for knowledge. This principle together with a person knowing a proposition if they believe it suggests a lack of justification necessary for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that merely holding a belief equates to knowledge without further consideration of the evidence or the reliability of the belief, as belief alone lacks the normative standards traditionally associated with knowledge.**
