# Is causal connection sufficient for knowledge when accuracy is due to chance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 967c5b98-6518-49c5-a03b-8b0570a68c35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b3086f02-fe19-4187-b3b5-dcf882df82f4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a broken clock that coincidentally shows the correct time, and they come to believe that this is the correct time. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of the correct time.**, **Even though there is a true belief connected causally to a fact, due to the person's belief being based on an unreliable source, intuitively, it does not count as knowledge.**, **This raises a question about whether mere causal connection between a belief and a fact is sufficient for knowledge, especially when the accuracy seems to be due to chance.**
