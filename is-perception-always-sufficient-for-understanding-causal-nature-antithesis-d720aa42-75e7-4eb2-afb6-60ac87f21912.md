# Is perception always sufficient for understanding causal nature?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d720aa42-75e7-4eb2-afb6-60ac87f21912
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-aa743ec0-e3e4-4aae-9408-fa20370276b8.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perception must reveal causal structures to lead to knowledge. This principle together with the possibility of misperception entails that perception might not suffice for accurate understanding.**, **If perception can be flawed or misled, then it may not always be a sufficient means to understanding the causal nature of reality.**
