# Can one know something without knowing all underlying facts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 03142ff5-ffe2-4053-b7b6-dd164928d340
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma View](no-false-lemma-view-synthesis-d417d5a7-7b9d-4d6b-8a89-c64775b0283f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true proposition because of a chain of reasoning that includes false intermediate beliefs. Intuitively, this person does not have knowledge of the final proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that for knowledge, a person's reasoning must be wholly underpinned by actual truths. This principle together with the requirement for no false beliefs challenges the condition that a proposition merely needs to be true and justified for knowledge.**
