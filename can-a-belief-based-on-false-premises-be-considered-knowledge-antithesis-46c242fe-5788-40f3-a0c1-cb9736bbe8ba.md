# Can a belief based on false premises be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46c242fe-5788-40f3-a0c1-cb9736bbe8ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true proposition because of a cognitive process that generally delivers accurate beliefs but relies on false premises in this instance. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know the proposition.**, **It is implausible to claim that knowledge can arise from false premises, as knowledge typically requires a true grounding for belief.**
