## Content

**Take the Müller-Lyer illusion where two lines of the same length appear different due to arrow-like tails. Intuitively, this example shows that mere appearance can convincingly override direct reality perception.**, **If optical illusions can consistently deceive perception, this questions the notion that perception straightforwardly presents direct reality or mere appearance.**
