# Does context-dependent knowledge lack normative force?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e3043b6-60e5-4dde-91be-5e84a03221df
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where different contexts lead to vastly different standards for knowing a proposition, resulting in disparate judgments about an individual's responsibility to know. Intuitively, this weakens the normative force of asserting knowledge.**, **Normative principles in epistemology assert that knowing carries certain epistemic responsibilities or norms. If knowledge is purely context-dependent, these responsibilities risk becoming arbitrary and lacking substantial normative force.**
