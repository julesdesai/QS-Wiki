# Can coherence provide knowledge without a reliable link to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 170d1435-9fc0-435c-a366-62cf4937eb70
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Adaptive Coherentism](adaptive-coherentism-synthesis-f17f0588-6de8-4c0d-a6e6-63e09dfee45f.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that coherence with other beliefs guarantees the truth of a belief.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a coherent set of beliefs includes false propositions. Intuitively, we either have to say that the beliefs are knowledge or not. If we say they are knowledge, then false beliefs become part of knowledge. If we say they are not knowledge, then coherence is insufficient for knowledge.**
