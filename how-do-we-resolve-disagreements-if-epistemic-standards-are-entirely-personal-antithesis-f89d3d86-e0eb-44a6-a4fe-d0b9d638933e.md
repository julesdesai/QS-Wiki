# How do we resolve disagreements if epistemic standards are entirely personal?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f89d3d86-e0eb-44a6-a4fe-d0b9d638933e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals disagree on a proposition's truth due to different epistemic standpoints. Without universal standards, resolving the disagreement seems intuitively difficult or impossible.**, **There is a plausible principle that disagreements presuppose some common epistemic standards for rational resolution. Without these, disagreements may remain unresolved.**, **If epistemic standards differ completely among individuals, it is implausible to provide a mechanism for resolving disagreement in a rational and fair manner.**
