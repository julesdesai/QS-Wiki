# Is practical reasoning affected by invariant standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef8e76ee-665a-4bb1-85eb-9913573dc1b2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**Suppose you are trying to make a high-stakes decision and you require more assurance than usual, compared to a low-stakes decision where you require less assurance. Intuitively, you would require different knowledge standards for these decisions.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should inform action, but under invariant standards, it fails to adapt to the context of decision-making, leading to impractical conclusions.**
