# Can knowledge be derived from impractical beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cfbe9a8f-6317-497c-a30a-bb2acd5de51a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**Imagine a philosopher who holds a complex metaphysical belief that offers no practical actions but is internally consistent and supported by logic. Intuitively, such beliefs can form knowledge even without direct practical verification.**, **Beliefs might qualify as knowledge through coherence and logical support, independent of facilitating practical action and resultant verification.**
