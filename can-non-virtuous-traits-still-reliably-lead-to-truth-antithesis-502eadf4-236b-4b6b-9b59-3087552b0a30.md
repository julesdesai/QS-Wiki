# Can non-virtuous traits still reliably lead to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 502eadf4-236b-4b6b-9b59-3087552b0a30
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d7a894af-29e5-42fe-8e5e-957e2009566d.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who uses coercion or manipulation to gather information and consistently arrives at the truth due to their ability to extract information from others unwillingly.**, **This suggests that coercion and manipulation, though not intellectual virtues, can be reliable methods of arriving at truth, questioning the necessity of virtue.**
