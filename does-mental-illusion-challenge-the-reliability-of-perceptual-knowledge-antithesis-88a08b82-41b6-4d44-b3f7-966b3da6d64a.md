# Does mental illusion challenge the reliability of perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 88a08b82-41b6-4d44-b3f7-966b3da6d64a
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-thesis-2daa98ba-8f8c-4200-a8b1-c2a336a65d2f.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that perceptual experiences alone can provide accurate knowledge when such experiences are prone to illusion or hallucination.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person sees a mirage of water in a desert. Intuitively, we either have to say that they know water is present or they do not. If we say they know, then knowledge is inaccurate. If we say they do not know, then perceptual experience alone is unreliable.**
