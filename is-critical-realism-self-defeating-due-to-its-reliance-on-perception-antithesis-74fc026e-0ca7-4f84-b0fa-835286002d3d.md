# Is critical realism self-defeating due to its reliance on perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74fc026e-0ca7-4f84-b0fa-835286002d3d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where critical realism relies on perception filtered through mental processes, yet such perception fails to accurately reflect reality. Intuitively, we would judge that this reliance undermines its claim to represent reality.**, **There is a plausible principle that a theory should not undermine its own foundational claims. This principle, together with a reliance on distorted perception, suggests that critical realism is self-defeating.**
