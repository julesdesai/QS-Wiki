# How does contextualism affect the standard of evidence required for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 00365fc0-0f61-423d-b883-e76acfe5d23c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism adapts justification to situations](contextualism-adapts-justification-to-situations-synthesis-6de50ec6-d692-4487-9677-ea468fdc8bbe.md)

## Content

**There is a principle in epistemology that knowledge requires a certain standard of evidence or justification.**, **If justification can vary by context, the standard of evidence required for knowledge can also vary, potentially leading to arbitrary or fluctuating standards.**, **Consider a situation where a low standard of evidence suffices in one context, but a higher standard is required in another. It seems arbitrary that the same proposition can both meet and fail the standard for knowledge based merely on context.**
