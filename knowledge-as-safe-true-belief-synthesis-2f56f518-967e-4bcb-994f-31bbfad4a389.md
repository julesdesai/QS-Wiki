# Knowledge as Safe True Belief

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 2f56f518-967e-4bcb-994f-31bbfad4a389
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can a reliable process that occasionally fails still constitute a basis for knowledge?](can-a-reliable-process-that-occasionally-fails-still-constitute-a-basis-for-knowledge-antithesis-3881fed6-d661-41b8-aeaf-7a986f9dc794.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is true in all nearby possible worlds**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief is true in all nearby possible worlds**
