# Can invariant knowledge standards coexist with context-sensitive knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 433d10f3-c43f-4b24-af4c-1ee2e203f71d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if knowledge standards are invariant, then changing circumstances should not affect whether someone knows a proposition.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person P's practical stakes change dramatically even though their evidence and belief do not. Intuitively, we would judge that P's status as knowing should not change if knowledge standards are invariant.**, **In a case where someone faces different practical consequences for being right or wrong, their knowledge attribution seems to shift unless knowledge standards are truly invariant.**
