# Do foundational beliefs necessarily ensure the justification of other beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c51771a-58c8-4773-977d-1ddb4cfb6e81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Knowledge Summary](foundationalist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-d6291bb5-6e86-4606-a555-64e99394a6d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who holds a foundational belief that is misunderstood or misapplied to derive further beliefs. Intuitively, those derived beliefs would lack proper justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that any justified belief derived from another belief relies on accurate application or understanding of that foundational belief.**
