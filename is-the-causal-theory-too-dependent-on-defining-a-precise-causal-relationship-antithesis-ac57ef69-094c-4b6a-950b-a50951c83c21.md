# Is the causal theory too dependent on defining a precise causal relationship?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac57ef69-094c-4b6a-950b-a50951c83c21
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person P discovers a fact via an unreliable means, such as a horoscope, yet the fact happens to be true due to chance. Intuitively, P does not possess knowledge in this case.**, **The causal theory struggles to distinguish between genuine knowledge and mere accidentally true beliefs in such cases, because the causal link is too loosely defined.**
