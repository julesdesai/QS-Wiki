# Can deception invalidate purportedly reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f8af2b2-2eb3-4c4c-8c89-c76f547fc766
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fec1f444-460d-4835-9da9-5fd5405f3ea1.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person has a generally reliable cognitive process, but a specific belief was formed because they unknowingly were fed false information by a deceitful friend. Despite the past reliability, intuitively, we would see this belief as lacking in knowledge.**, **Even reliable cognitive processes can yield false beliefs when subjected to intentional deception, questioning their infallibility in knowledge acquisition.**
