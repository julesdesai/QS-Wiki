# Does subjective manipulation nullify the safety of a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15f2d703-0407-47ce-a2ad-c02a110f634c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a highly trusted expert deliberately gives a false statement about a medical condition to a patient. The patient believes the expert's false statement, though in a wide range of similar and nearby situations, the expert gives correct information. Intuitively, there is no knowledge about the condition because the belief is false.**, **A belief is supposed to be safe when, in nearby possible worlds, it matches reality. In this case, minor alterations like expertise manipulation suggest the potential for falsehood, questioning the safety criterion.**
