# Can a false belief ever be justified in foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5aefbb2-1e3a-4448-aea3-fd2aac63b56a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Pragmatic Grounds](foundationalism-with-pragmatic-grounds-synthesis-35d5e861-b788-4e52-a02b-4488aba3b8d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification requires truth of the belief. This principle together with the idea that justification can rest on false basic beliefs entails situations where a person is justified in holding a false belief.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes a false proposition due to relying on basic beliefs that appear justified but are actually false. In such a scenario, intuitively, the justification of their belief seems questionable.**
