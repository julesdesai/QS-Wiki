# Knowledge as Process Reliabilist Justified True Belief

**Parent:** [Can a person know something without explicit awareness of their justification?](can-a-person-know-something-without-explicit-awareness-of-their-justification-antithesis-02ed5657-a21d-42e6-ade6-2567f6a56782.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, the belief must be the result of a generally reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have process-reliabilist justification, and the proposition is true**
