# Are there beliefs that are both true and insensitive yet not knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dba54217-9364-48ee-b777-33510face050
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person has a belief in a true proposition that would have been false in a slightly altered scenario, suggesting the belief is insensitive. Intuitively, we might say the belief does not qualify as knowledge.**, **Although insensitivity suggests knowledge, if the slight change of scenario yields a falsehood, one might reconsider if the belief was truly known under the initial circumstances.**
