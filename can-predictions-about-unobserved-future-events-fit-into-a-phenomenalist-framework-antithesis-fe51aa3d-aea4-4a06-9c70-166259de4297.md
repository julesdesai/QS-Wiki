# Can predictions about unobserved future events fit into a phenomenalist framework?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe51aa3d-aea4-4a06-9c70-166259de4297
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a meteorologist predicts a storm based on past and present data but the storm hasn't yet been experienced or observed. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their prediction constitutes a form of knowledge about something not currently reliant upon sensory experience.**, **Suppose a scientist makes a prediction about a future eclipse. We must either say their knowledge is based on sensory experience or on theoretical models. If the former, then future events aren't truly known until experienced. If the latter, knowledge transcends sensory experiences.**
