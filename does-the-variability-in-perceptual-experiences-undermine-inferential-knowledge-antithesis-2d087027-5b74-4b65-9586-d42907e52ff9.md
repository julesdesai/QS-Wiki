# Does the variability in perceptual experiences undermine inferential knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d087027-5b74-4b65-9586-d42907e52ff9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where diverse perceptual experiences lead to conflicting inferences about the same external object. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that one cannot have knowledge about the object.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be derived from perceptual experiences if those experiences are inconsistent.**, **A person whose inferences about an external object change with perceptual variations may not achieve knowledge about that object.**
