# Does a lack of detailed understanding of the reliability undermine claims of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5180e677-96d5-47f8-a0c4-ce6271afe0b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person trusts a complicated computer algorithm known to be mostly reliable to form all their beliefs about weather forecasts.**, **If the person does not understand how the algorithm works and cannot assess its reliability personally, it seems problematic to assert they possess knowledge simply because the outcome is mostly correct.**, **Without a more nuanced grasp of reliability, claiming knowledge appears oversimplified.**
