# Can a person intuitively have knowledge without defeaters being relevant?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba705e02-5a2b-471c-ba15-fcb1f910ba5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Defeaters Theory of Knowledge](no-defeaters-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-0be8e529-9437-4b4e-96a3-c585180c1210.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief based on reliable memory and there are no defeaters present, but the belief is in an environment where they could easily have been defeated unknowingly. Intuitively, we judge that the person still knows the belief.**, **The absence of defeaters should not affect the judgment of knowledge if the belief was formed reliably and remains true.**
