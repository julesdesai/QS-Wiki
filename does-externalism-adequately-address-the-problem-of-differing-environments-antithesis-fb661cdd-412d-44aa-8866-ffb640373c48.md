# Does externalism adequately address the problem of differing environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb661cdd-412d-44aa-8866-ffb640373c48
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-9fb6627e-b63e-4d81-a81a-128eb1da4172.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual is in an environment where reliable testimony is given regarding a certain fact. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that the belief formed based on this testimony constitutes knowledge.**, **Now, consider a different scenario where the same individual is in an environment where testimony is unreliable, and they form the same belief in that environment. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief does not constitute knowledge despite having the same subjective experience as in the first scenario.**
