# Does the causal theory of knowledge account for intuitions in Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9adb69b1-f4f1-4d1f-a169-01dd42d3c526
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-336c94a4-e4b0-4a1c-b1db-218c9b8f6add.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a justified true belief through misleading evidence, such as Smith believing that the person who will get the job has ten coins in their pocket because the boss told him so, but it turns out to be true because Jones also has ten coins in his pocket and gets the job. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that Smith does not know who will get the job.**, **A case in which a person has a justified true belief due to misleading evidence poses a problem for the requirement of an appropriate causal connection because the belief is true by coincidence rather than through an appropriate causal link with the fact.**
