# Can a person know something without having internal access to justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bcf78741-ce4c-45a0-88a3-a26930f852d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Externalism Summary](moderate-externalism-summary-synthesis-b4668bae-162e-4191-89bc-89b702818630.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowing requires some form of internal access to justification. This principle together with a scenario where a person believes truly and reliably without such access undermines the idea that this belief constitutes knowledge.**, **It is implausible that someone could claim to know something if they have no idea how or why they are justified in their belief.**
