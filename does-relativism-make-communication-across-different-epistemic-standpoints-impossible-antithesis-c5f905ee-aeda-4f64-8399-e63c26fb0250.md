# Does relativism make communication across different epistemic standpoints impossible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5f905ee-aeda-4f64-8399-e63c26fb0250
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where each participant in a dialogue holds knowledge based on their personal epistemic standards. Without shared standards, communication and understanding seem intuitively compromised.**, **There is a plausible principle that effective communication assumes some shared epistemic grounds for interpreting propositions and evaluating claims.**, **If epistemic standards vary entirely by individual, it becomes implausible to believe that meaningful communication and understanding can be achieved across different perspectives.**
