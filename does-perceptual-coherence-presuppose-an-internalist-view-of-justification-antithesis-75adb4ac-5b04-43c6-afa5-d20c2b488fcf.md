# Does perceptual coherence presuppose an internalist view of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 75adb4ac-5b04-43c6-afa5-d20c2b488fcf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which external conditions are unknown but perceptions are coherent internally. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that this suggests an internalist view of justification because it excludes external evidence.**, **It is implausible that internal coherence alone satisfies the need for justification in determining the truth of knowledge claims, as it excludes external verification.**
