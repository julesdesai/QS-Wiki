# Can beliefs formed in ideal conditions still refute the Safety Principle?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 897cf455-7042-440a-8f63-43ad639f7128
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge with Safety Principle](knowledge-with-safety-principle-synthesis-36cb0228-2d5a-4aa3-87ba-01c1622dfdc5.md)

## Content

**Consider an observer who sees a tree in daylight and believes it is a tree, which is true, but later learns they were in an area with realistic artificial tree replicas. Intuitively, it might be debated whether they truly knew at that moment it was a real tree.**, **The belief might track truth well due to correct visual observation, but surrounding misinformation changes this landscape.**, **In nearby possible worlds, the belief could easily have been false if they unknowingly observed replicas.**
