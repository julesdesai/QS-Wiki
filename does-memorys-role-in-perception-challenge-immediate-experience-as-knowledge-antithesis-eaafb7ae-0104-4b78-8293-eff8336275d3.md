# Does memory's role in perception challenge immediate experience as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eaafb7ae-0104-4b78-8293-eff8336275d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which recognizing a friend in a crowd relies on stored memories of their appearance. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception is a mere immediate experience or it incorporates memory. If we say perception incorporates memory, it challenges the claim that knowledge is directly acquired through perception alone.**
