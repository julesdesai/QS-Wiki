# Does practical success adequately account for environmental conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a9be5e2-2797-4b83-9bbc-497450be8f8d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a farmer believes it will rain based on unreliable folklore, and it does rain. Intuitively, attributing knowledge here seems wrong because the belief isn't based on sound meteorological evidence, despite practical success.**, **Even if contextual evidence like weather patterns could have provided a better basis for justification, relying on pragmatic success ignores or oversimplifies complex environmental conditions, which questions the robustness of such justification.**
