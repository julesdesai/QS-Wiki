# Does pragmatic encroachment dismiss the distinction between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7fec474-03c2-4d46-b68d-f9152a7b40c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Summary](pragmatic-encroachment-summary-synthesis-e897bf05-5a21-418d-ae35-b563f51919c6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone believes a weather forecast and it is indeed accurate, but due to the importance of an outdoor event they hesitate to say they know it will rain. Intuitively, the belief should suffice for knowing given its accuracy and reliability.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is a true, justified belief. This principle together with the scenario entails that practical stakes should not interfere with knowledge attribution when such prerequisites hold.**
