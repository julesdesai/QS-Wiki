# Can the causal theory handle domains of metaphysics or mathematics?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6aa6fdf6-5676-444c-a1b1-4de44ad2ecbd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Consider a mathematical or metaphysical proposition, such as a theorem, believed based on abstract reasoning. Intuitively, we would judge such knowledge is not grounded in causal links.**, **A person’s belief in a mathematical truth, verified through logical deduction, does not rely on a causal connection in the same way empirical beliefs do. Such beliefs should still be considered knowledge, suggesting the causal theory is inadequate.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be based on non-causal grounds, especially in abstract domains where truth is established independently of empirical causation.**
