## Content

**For a belief to constitute knowledge, it must be the result of a cognitive virtue**, **A cognitive virtue is a character trait that reliably leads one to true beliefs**, **The exercise of cognitive virtues is not constrained by specific environmental functions**, **A belief formed through the exercise of cognitive virtues constitutes knowledge if it is true**
