# Can knowledge be predicated on isolated instances of truth that defy regular expectations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05632291-d84e-4467-af95-e3365533e215
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes their exam results are being posted today because this is typically the scheduled day, though this year it was changed to a different day by mistake, yet the original schedule accidentally aligns again with this person's belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know their exam results are being posted due to relying on isolated luck.**, **It is implausible that isolated instances of truth resulting from expectation-defying luck suffice for the knowledge claim.**
