# Does coherence account adequately for the source of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 29793f62-62d3-4444-af20-5f71c5761d78
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Adaptive Coherentism](adaptive-coherentism-synthesis-f17f0588-6de8-4c0d-a6e6-63e09dfee45f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person acquires beliefs through unreliable means but maintains coherence among them. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know.**, **There is a plausible principle that the source of a belief affects the legitimacy of its coherence. This principle together with an emphasis on coherence suggests a need to consider the origin of beliefs in justifying them.**
