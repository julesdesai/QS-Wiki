# Does the filtering of perception through mental processes detach knowledge from reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a758252-4f77-4cba-8e35-528ec3a7a6f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person’s perception is so comprehensively filtered by their mental processes that the resultant perception bears little resemblance to the actual external event. Intuitively, we either have to say that this constitutes knowledge or not. If we say it constitutes knowledge, then knowledge is disconnected from reality. If we say it does not constitute knowledge, then knowledge requires unfiltered perception.**
