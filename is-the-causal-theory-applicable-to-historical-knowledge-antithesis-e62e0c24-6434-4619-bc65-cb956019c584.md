# Is the causal theory applicable to historical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e62e0c24-6434-4619-bc65-cb956019c584
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that historical knowledge may not rely on a direct causal relationship, as historical facts do not causally influence present beliefs.**, **Consider a historian who knows that Julius Caesar was assassinated based on historical records; this knowledge seems disconnected from any causal chain involving the fact of assassination itself.**
