# Do practical stakes affect knowledge in the same context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 901601b7-d25b-4e85-9a18-dba047755b71
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people in the same context face different practical outcomes based on their belief accuracy. Intuitively, if knowledge standards are invariant, both should know or not know regardless of practical stakes.**, **There is a plausible principle that contextual factors such as practical stakes should not change knowledge attribution if standards are truly invariant.**, **In a situation where stakes differ but the context remains constant, knowledge attribution seems incorrectly influenced by stakes.**
