# Can basic beliefs really be both self-evident and communicable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ed8c1658-6154-4841-b6bb-eccd962179cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Communicable Justification](foundationalism-with-communicable-justification-synthesis-8dbcdd2b-eb8b-4593-a7ca-0bfc89d9258b.md)

## Content

**A basic belief is one that supposedly does not rely on any other beliefs for its justification, claiming self-evident status. However, what is self-evident to one person might not be self-evident to another, raising doubts about the communicability of such beliefs.**, **If a belief is truly self-evident, its justification should not require explanation or communication to others, which may cause internal conflict with the requirement for communicable justification.**, **Consider two individuals with different cultural backgrounds who each find different sets of beliefs self-evident, creating a scenario where they are unable to communicate their justification effectively to each other.**
