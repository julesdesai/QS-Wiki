# Do varying standards for justification impact what we count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b318fa79-a6b7-458a-8b12-94b99795baf7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Knowledge Summary](invariantist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-7e971fef-e35c-4ec9-93e0-84ae5864f440.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person provides minimal evidence to support their belief in a casual discussion, versus providing comprehensive evidence in a court testimony. Intuitively, listeners might consider the person knowledgeable in the latter but not in the former.**, **It is implausible that a single fixed standard of justification suffices across all varying contexts of conversation and inquiry.**
