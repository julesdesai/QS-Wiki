# Is the need for infinite reasons self-defeating?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8754edd8-835d-469b-841f-b12cf19c9c3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**The requirement for infinite justification may invalidate its own premise since any claim of knowledge requires infinite reasoning, which itself must be justified infinitely, leading to paradox.**, **Consider if the principle demanding infinite reasons itself lacks finite justification; this undermines the principle from being seen as knowledgeable.**, **A self-defeating principle might undermine its conditions by creating demands it cannot satisfy without contradiction or circular reasoning.**
