# Can one believe and yet not know a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a93aa860-8671-437d-add8-7d66d9f5011b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-f73c0505-1eaa-4768-8143-11a2f8c9ca92.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where one believes that the earth is flat based on personal interpretation of evidence.**, **In such scenarios, the belief is there, but knowledge is typically not ascribed due to conflicting evidence.**, **The presence of belief does not automatically equate to knowledge if the proposition itself is false or poorly justified.**, **Belief alone without adherence to broader justified truths fails to fulfill the criteria for knowledge.**
