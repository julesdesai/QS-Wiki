# Can high probability be insufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 262f11f5-3d16-4e31-b12d-a748dfef6b59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Bayesian Epistemology](bayesian-epistemology-synthesis-4576807d-29a3-407d-a965-1c884989f7fe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a belief that, with a high degree of probability, is true, yet the belief is based on misleading evidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not genuinely know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing requires not just probabilistic support but also reliable and unobstructed evidence. This principle suggests that high probability alone without reliable evidence does not suffice for knowledge.**
