# View 4: No Luck Condition on Knowledge

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from a reliable process and is not subject to epistemic luck**, **Epistemic luck occurs when the true belief is due to chance rather than cognitive competence**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief arises from a reliable process, and is not subject to epistemic luck**
