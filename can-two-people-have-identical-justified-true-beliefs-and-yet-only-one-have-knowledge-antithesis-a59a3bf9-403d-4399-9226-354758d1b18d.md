# Can two people have identical justified true beliefs and yet only one have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a59a3bf9-403d-4399-9226-354758d1b18d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals, Alice and Bob, have identical justified true beliefs about a proposition but differ in complexity of thought or reasoning abilities. Intuitively, one could argue that only one possesses knowledge due to their superior cognitive processing.**, **The distinction challenges the notion that mere justified true belief, without intellectual virtue or competence differences, equates to knowledge.**
