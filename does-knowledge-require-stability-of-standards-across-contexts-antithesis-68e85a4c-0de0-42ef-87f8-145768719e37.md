# Does knowledge require stability of standards across contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68e85a4c-0de0-42ef-87f8-145768719e37
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where the standards for knowing change drastically across contexts without a change in evidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge is unstable or that the concept of knowledge becomes meaningless.**, **If we say knowledge is unstable, then the reliability of knowledge claims becomes highly questionable.**, **If we say the concept of knowledge becomes meaningless, then the utility of knowledge for communication and decision-making is undermined.**
