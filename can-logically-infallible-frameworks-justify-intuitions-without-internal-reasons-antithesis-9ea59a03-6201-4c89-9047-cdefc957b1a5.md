# Can logically infallible frameworks justify intuitions without internal reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9ea59a03-6201-4c89-9047-cdefc957b1a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Justification Theory](internalist-justification-theory-synthesis-60885d02-ba8c-49f7-a8ac-6cdf9db1e5a8.md)

## Content

**In chess, a grandmaster intuitively knows the best move without consciously processing the full line of reasoning internally.**, **This belief about the best move feels justified due to vast experience and pattern recognition.**, **The grandmaster cannot always access each reason, suggesting intuition can bypass internal access to justification.**
