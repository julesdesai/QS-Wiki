# Does externalist reliabilism adequately differentiate between human and artificial cognition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7372ca2-bba4-43b0-9e2c-9a580b409846
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an artificial intelligence forms beliefs through algorithms and data inputs in a manner deemed globally reliable. However, intuitively, we might question whether such machine-formed beliefs align with our concept of human epistemic justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that human epistemic justification involves elements of understanding and consciousness that may not be replicated by external processes alone.**
