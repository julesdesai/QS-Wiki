# Does contextualism undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8b05397-1a7d-49fb-bfda-d75c075c8f06
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Knowledge](contextualism-about-knowledge-synthesis-b64cba08-2a7b-44b8-b81d-d7241d8be6e0.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true. A true proposition must have an objective truth value, independent of context. If knowledge attributions depend on the context of the ascriber, then they undermine the objectivity required for a proposition's truth.**
