# Can intellectual virtues differ among individuals while still constituting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 58c10609-5caa-42aa-b7a2-eccd0790ff0b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual virtues are the same for everyone, as individuals can vary widely in their cognitive abilities and dispositions.**, **Suppose we have a case in which two people form the same true belief, one through rigorous analysis and the other through a superficial understanding. Intuitively, we would say the former has knowledge, while the latter might not, demonstrating that intellectual virtue can be seen as context-dependent and varied among individuals.**
