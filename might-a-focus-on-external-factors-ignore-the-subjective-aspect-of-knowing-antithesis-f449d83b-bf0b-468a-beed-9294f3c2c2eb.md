# Might a focus on external factors ignore the subjective aspect of knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f449d83b-bf0b-468a-beed-9294f3c2c2eb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliance on External Factors](externalism-with-reliance-on-external-factors-synthesis-94d45e40-e833-458e-8cba-8623e44201d6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that considers the subjective aspect of belief an essential component of knowing. This principle, alongside a focus on external factors, risks sidelining the subjective experience intrinsic to epistemic assessment.**, **Imagine a situation in which an individual possesses knowledge solely due to external factors, devoid of any subjective conviction; intuitively, this lacks completeness as a case of knowledge.**
