# Does the Safety Theory overlook the role of justification in knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d19b828a-e816-444a-8d22-704e8c7f2da8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where an individual holds a true belief due to luck without any evidence or justification. Intuitively, this person does not seem to know the proposition, even though the belief is true and happens to be safe from error.**, **A plausible principle asserts that knowledge traditionally involves a justified belief component. This principle suggests the Safety Theory might be insufficient if it does not account for the necessary justificatory structure traditionally seen as part of knowledge.**
