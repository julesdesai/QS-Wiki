# Does extreme evidence potentially negate sensitivity concerns?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85b6bb38-8d00-4a14-80cb-93105931c422
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-102d682a-8eb5-4983-bf10-f287ee1765f9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a detective has overwhelming forensic evidence a particular suspect committed a crime. Even if the evidence were tampered slightly, the detective would still believe in the suspect's guilt. Intuitively, the detective knows who committed the crime.**, **It is plausible that the weight of evidence can override the lack of sensitivity in the belief.**
