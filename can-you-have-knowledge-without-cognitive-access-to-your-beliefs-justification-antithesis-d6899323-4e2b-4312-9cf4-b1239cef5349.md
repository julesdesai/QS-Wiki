# Can you have knowledge without cognitive access to your belief's justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d6899323-4e2b-4312-9cf4-b1239cef5349
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-eb8cad20-b2ac-4ebe-8b63-c23141145814.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds a true belief but has no access to the justification due to amnesia. Intuitively, we either have to say that this person knows the proposition or that they don't. If we say they know, it suggests people need not be aware of their reasons for knowledge. If we say they don’t know, it contradicts the idea that access is unnecessary for knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that for a belief to be justified as knowledge, one must have access to the reasons or justification for it.**
