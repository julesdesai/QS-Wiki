# Can coherentism distinguish between genuine and fictitious coherent systems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9899025-b6d2-45a5-9a82-88d3917fedf5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone constructs a fictitious story with a cohesive narrative about a fictional country. In such a case, intuitively, the coherence of these details does not confer justified belief about real geography or politics.**, **The distinction between real-world coherence and fictional coherence suggests that coherentism alone cannot account for knowledge about the world. Fictional coherence challenges the validity of coherentism in distinguishing genuine truth.**
