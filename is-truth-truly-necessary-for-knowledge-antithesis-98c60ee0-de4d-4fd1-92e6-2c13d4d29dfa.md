# Is truth truly necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98c60ee0-de4d-4fd1-92e6-2c13d4d29dfa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge requires truth if practical success in action arises from a justified false belief.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person has a justified belief that aids in decision-making and success, such as a misplaced belief in a bridge's stability that ensures safe passage due to fortuitous structural support. Intuitively, either we must acknowledge knowledge in such cases despite falsehood or deny knowledge's practical value.**
