# Is the idea of an infinite chain of reasons compatible with empirical findings on human reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1bda6730-43c8-4eef-8378-c5272e2e6753
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Empirical psychology indicates that humans do reason finitely and often rely on heuristics, not endless chains of justification.**, **If infinitism describes a process humans cannot engage with, then its application to human epistemology seems mismatched.**, **An epistemic theory effective for human reasoning should align with cognitive limitations and psychological findings.**
