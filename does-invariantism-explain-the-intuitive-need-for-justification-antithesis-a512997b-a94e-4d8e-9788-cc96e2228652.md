# Does invariantism explain the intuitive need for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a512997b-a94e-4d8e-9788-cc96e2228652
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief purely by guessing. Intuitively, we ought to say that they do not know the proposition, even if it is true.**, **The view that a person knows a proposition if they believe it and it is true implies knowledge can exist without justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just true belief; it requires justification.**
