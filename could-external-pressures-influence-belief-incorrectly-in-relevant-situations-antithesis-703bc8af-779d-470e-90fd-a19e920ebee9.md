# Could external pressures influence belief incorrectly in relevant situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 703bc8af-779d-470e-90fd-a19e920ebee9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-68f75ad1-a4e1-40d8-ae80-33e70d924463.md)

## Content

**There is a case where a person’s belief in a proposition is due to social pressure, such as peer consensus. If the proposition were false, but peer pressure remained, they would still believe it. Intuitively, belief due to social factors rather than truth-tracking undermines sensitivity.**
