# Could integrating reliability and responsibility be too demanding for what counts as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef261bd2-21c3-40cf-a621-6851833499f2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Mixed Virtue Epistemology](mixed-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9b1d3c06-dd01-4be5-8e49-871c85befc1a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we imagine a scenario where someone holds a true belief by following socially accepted norms that are reliable but lack deep intellectual responsibility involvement. Intuitively, this seems closer to knowledge than not.**, **This indicates a potential over-demandingness in requiring an integration of both reliability and responsibility, as everyday knowledge might often bypass one component.**
