# Does the role of deceit in testimony undermine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8fd8fde-df9d-4226-a025-bad834f744d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person P deliberately provides false testimony to mislead others about an event they did not witness. Intuitively, we must say that this undermines trust in testimony as a source of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that testimony should be honest to be reliable. This principle together with the possibility of deceit suggests vulnerability in knowledge that relies heavily on testimonial accounts.**
