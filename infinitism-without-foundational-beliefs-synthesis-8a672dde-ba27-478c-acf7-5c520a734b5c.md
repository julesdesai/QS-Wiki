# Infinitism without Foundational Beliefs

**Parent:** [Can foundational beliefs ever be inherently justified?](can-foundational-beliefs-ever-be-inherently-justified-antithesis-d1635522-b126-4da9-a5b5-1a61db17ee89.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the justification involves an infinite chain of reasons without requiring foundational beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and there exists an infinite chain justifying the proposition**
