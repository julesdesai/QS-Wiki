# Do contextualism's implications for communication suggest a breakdown in shared knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc9c3a09-18b3-4c50-9d28-6b7be0113814
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-cec2b7f9-8a52-4ab7-9dd4-6f75547db097.md)

## Content

**Knowledge claims involve communication, which requires some level of shared understanding. If knowledge standards change with context, intuitively, communication could break down when contexts differ, threatening shared knowledge.**
