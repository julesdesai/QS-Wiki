# Does believing a proposition necessarily imply knowing it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9cf0b500-c1f7-42cb-b789-6b50c98efa61
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone firmly believes an incorrect proposition due to cognitive bias. Intuitively, we would not consider this person to know the proposition because knowledge requires both truth and justified belief.**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe it. Given belief without truth or justification results in no knowledge, it seems insufficient as a sole requirement.**
