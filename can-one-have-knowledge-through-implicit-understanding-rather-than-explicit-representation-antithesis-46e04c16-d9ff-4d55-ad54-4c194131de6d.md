# Can one have knowledge through implicit understanding rather than explicit representation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46e04c16-d9ff-4d55-ad54-4c194131de6d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a musician knows how to play a piece by heart without being able to articulate the notes explicitly. Intuitively, this implies knowledge without an explicit mental representation.**, **There is a plausible principle that implicit understanding constitutes a form of knowledge. This principle together with the absence of an articulated mental representation implies knowledge does not always require explicit representational correspondence.**
