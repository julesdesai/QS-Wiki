# Why does direct perception not necessitate a background knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18fd18b6-f51c-4da7-b494-e8b660b06c09
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone perceives a complex scientific phenomenon, like a chemical reaction. Intuitively, they must possess some background scientific knowledge to understand what they see as a chemical reaction.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceiving meaningful information involves some understanding or background knowledge. This principle contradicts the claim that a person knows a perceptual proposition just by directly perceiving the fact without just inferential support.**
