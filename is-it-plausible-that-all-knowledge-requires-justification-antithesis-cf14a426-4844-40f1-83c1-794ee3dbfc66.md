# Is it plausible that all knowledge requires justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf14a426-4844-40f1-83c1-794ee3dbfc66
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalism](internalism-thesis-697c012e-ac77-4c5b-935c-0bf2d4d5ce83.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that every piece of knowledge requires explicit justification, especially in cases of basic perceptual beliefs or memory.**, **Consider a case where a person simply knows that the sky is blue by looking at it. Intuitively, they know this without needing to access explicit justification for their perception, challenging the requirement for accessible justification.**
