# Does the concept of non-repeating reasons conflict with human reasoning patterns?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f192ee94-e51e-4014-a202-1160dae97fdf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-fd5e1271-f2df-4333-a434-50b9d95350fd.md)

## Content

**Human reasoning frequently involves cycles and repetitions, yet an infinite series of non-repeating reasons excludes such natural patterns.**, **It is implausible for humans to generate an infinite series of genuinely new and distinct reasons without repeating any, given cognitive limitations.**, **If justification requires an infinite series of non-repeating reasons, then typical human reasoning processes are insufficient for knowledge.**
