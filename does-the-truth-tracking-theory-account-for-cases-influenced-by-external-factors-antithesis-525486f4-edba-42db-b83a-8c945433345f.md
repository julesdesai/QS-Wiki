# Does the truth-tracking theory account for cases influenced by external factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 525486f4-edba-42db-b83a-8c945433345f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which external factors, such as manipulation or coercion, lead someone, Emma, to believe a true proposition. Intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge, as her belief is externally controlled rather than truly formed.**, **Knowledge implies an internal cognitive process that reliably tracks truth, uninfluenced by external manipulation. If Emma's belief results from external control, it lacks the autonomy characteristic of knowledge.**
