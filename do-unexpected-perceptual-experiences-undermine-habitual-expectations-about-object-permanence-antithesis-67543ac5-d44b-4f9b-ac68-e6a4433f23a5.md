# Do unexpected perceptual experiences undermine habitual expectations about object permanence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67543ac5-d44b-4f9b-ac68-e6a4433f23a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where an individual repeatedly witnesses a chair disappearing from a room and then reappearing after a few moments. Intuitively, this scenario challenges the expectation of the chair's permanence based on repeated perceptual patterns.**, **It seems implausible that habitual expectations can fully account for object permanence if those expectations are easily violated by unusual experiences.**
