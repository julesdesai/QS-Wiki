# Does coherence ensure the accuracy of knowledge about external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5565a886-fd8b-45ab-a07b-ac45bc6ef521
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Coherence Theory](phenomenal-coherence-theory-synthesis-37d80231-0661-49bf-9bec-8b3f779084e8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual's perceptions are all part of a coherent system but are systematically deceived or manipulated by an external device, leading to false beliefs about external objects. Intuitively, we either have to say that the individual has knowledge because of coherence or lacks knowledge due to deception. If we say the individual has knowledge, then coherence is unjustly prioritized over truth. If we say the individual lacks knowledge, then coherence is not sufficient for knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that the accuracy of knowledge about external objects cannot be guaranteed by coherence alone, as coherence might coexist with deception or error.**
