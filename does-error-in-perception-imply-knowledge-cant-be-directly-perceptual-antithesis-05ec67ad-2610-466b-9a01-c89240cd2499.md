# Does error in perception imply knowledge can't be directly perceptual?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05ec67ad-2610-466b-9a01-c89240cd2499
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Selective Direct Realism](selective-direct-realism-synthesis-744f6327-3f79-490f-be88-a0aa209da42b.md)

## Content

**Perceptual errors, such as mistaking a rope for a snake in dim light, show that perception can lead to false beliefs.**, **Since perceptual errors involve propositions that do not relate to unaltered foundational parts of reality, it challenges the notion that perception directly provides knowledge.**, **In perceptual error cases, perceptions do not remain stable and lead to inconsistencies across varying conditions.**
