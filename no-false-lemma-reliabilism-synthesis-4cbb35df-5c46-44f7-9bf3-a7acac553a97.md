# No-False-Lemma Reliabilism

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 4cbb35df-5c46-44f7-9bf3-a7acac553a97
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can a reliable process still lead to non-knowledge?](can-a-reliable-process-still-lead-to-non-knowledge-antithesis-3fdd17ee-7635-41f2-a989-62254d0668f8.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the process does not involve any false lemmas**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief arises from a reliable cognitive process, and the process does not involve any false lemmas**
