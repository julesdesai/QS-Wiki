# Could shared knowledge invalidate the individual possession of intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e255337f-9dcc-4422-808e-34c6d979c725
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-43f1bd52-38ff-4c3b-a3d7-408481a71c52.md)

## Content

**Suppose two astronomers each accurately believe that a new planet exists due to their separate research, but unknowingly rely on falsified data exchanged between their research teams. Intuitively, neither holds knowledge though their belief is true, hence their belief lacks independent intellectual virtue.**, **If a belief relies on corrupt yet coincidentally reliable data, the intellectual virtue in acquiring knowledge might be undermined.**
