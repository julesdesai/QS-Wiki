# How reliable is perception when it is manipulated or controlled?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fa8eeb47-6e94-48e8-8b95-4eedbfde3809
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Experience Consistency](phenomenal-experience-consistency-synthesis-dff6895e-5f7e-47e4-9fd7-5b1463da077a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which perception is altered by virtual reality to simulate real environments. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge from these experiences is unreliable or that simulated reality can provide genuine knowledge. If we say it is unreliable, then all technology-mediated experiences are questionable. If we say simulated reality provides genuine knowledge, then the line between perception and reality is blurred.**
