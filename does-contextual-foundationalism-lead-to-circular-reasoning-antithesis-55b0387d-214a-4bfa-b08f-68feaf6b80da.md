# Does contextual foundationalism lead to circular reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55b0387d-214a-4bfa-b08f-68feaf6b80da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**Suppose foundational beliefs are justified by context, and context is justified by foundational beliefs. Intuitively, we either have to say that one justifies the other, or both justify each other reciprocally. If one justifies the other, the justification chain is incomplete. If they justify each other, it leads to circular reasoning, which is epistemically suspect.**, **Circular reasoning can prevent a clear foundation upon which knowledge claims can be built in a stable manner.**, **Stable foundations are typically those that avoid circular dependencies and provide independent justification.**
