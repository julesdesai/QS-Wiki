# Is causal connection sufficient for knowledge in imagined distant scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 66dd8bd6-b2cf-4497-a64c-cc59909cd736
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory With Causal Relation to Truth](goldmans-causal-theory-with-causal-relation-to-truth-synthesis-95e17f8c-2d52-493c-a8f5-41488d26cf0a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes there is life on a distant planet because of a random dream, and coincidentally, there is life on that planet. In such a case, intuitively, we would not say the person has knowledge despite the true belief.**, **It is implausible that a true belief originating from an unreliable source, even if causally connected to the fact, constitutes knowledge.**, **A case in which belief about a distant event is caused by daydreaming, and the event is true, raises doubts about the sufficiency of causal links for knowledge.**
