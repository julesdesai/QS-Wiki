# Do epistemic virtues account for individual differences in cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 73ce9dee-7d86-4579-bf43-1851a4800188
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism Summary](proper-functionalism-summary-synthesis-5b044e3f-97d9-4386-9c2f-bd333212a4a8.md)

## Content

**Imagine two individuals with inherently different perceptual acuities due to genetic variability. One gets true beliefs due to sharper faculties, while the other, with dulled ones, forms them on luck. Intuitively, both shouldn't have equal claims to knowledge.**, **Epistemic virtues such as attention and judgment may highlight variability not accounted for in proper functionalism, challenging the equality of resulting true beliefs as knowledge.**
