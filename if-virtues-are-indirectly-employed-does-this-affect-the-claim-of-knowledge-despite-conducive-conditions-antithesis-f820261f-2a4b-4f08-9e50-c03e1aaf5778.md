# If virtues are indirectly employed, does this affect the claim of knowledge despite conducive conditions?

## Content

**Imagine that a person forms a belief based not directly on their own virtues but indirectly through others' expertise, in an otherwise conducive condition. We often judge intuitively that this lacks first-hand knowledge.**, **When intellectual engagement is indirect and dependent on others, yet it still aligns with conducive outcomes, there's a challenge to intuitive and direct knowledge claims.**
