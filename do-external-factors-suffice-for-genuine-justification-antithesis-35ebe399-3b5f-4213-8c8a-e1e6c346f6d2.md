# Do external factors suffice for genuine justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35ebe399-3b5f-4213-8c8a-e1e6c346f6d2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Foundationalism](externalist-foundationalism-synthesis-3e0224db-6723-4288-8a1b-8819cbac7570.md)

## Content

**A scenario in which a person forms a belief about the stock market's rise based solely on a reliable external information service, without understanding any financial concepts, shows that external factors alone might not be sufficient for genuine justification. Intuitively, without internal grasp of concepts, we might judge genuine justification as lacking.**
