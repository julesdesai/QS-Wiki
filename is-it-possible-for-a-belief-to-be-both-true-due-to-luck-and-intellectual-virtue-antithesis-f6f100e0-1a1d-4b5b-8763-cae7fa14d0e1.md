# Is it possible for a belief to be both true due to luck and intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6f100e0-1a1d-4b5b-8763-cae7fa14d0e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief based on a reliable intellectual virtue, but an unexpected and lucky event confirms the truth of the belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that the belief arises from intellectual virtue but is also true because of luck.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief can simultaneously arise from intellectual virtue and be true due to luck. This principle together with the idea that intellectual virtue should eliminate luck in a belief's truth entails a contradiction.**
