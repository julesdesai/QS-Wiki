# Does Infinitism adequately account for the immediacy of perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ae7dceb-bcce-4854-9a78-a121f67926c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-dd7098a7-016a-4f9d-9b36-944f2be4f1f4.md)

## Content

**Consider a simple perceptual case where a person sees a tree and believes there is a tree.**, **In such cases, knowledge appears immediate and directly justified without invoking infinite chains of prior reasons.**, **Intuitively, perceptual knowledge does not seem to require infinite justification to be valid or known.**
