**Consider a case where a person holds an irrational belief that is consistent within an otherwise rational, coherent belief system. Intuitively, irrational beliefs undermine the idea of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs constituting knowledge must be rational. This principle together with coherent systems containing irrational beliefs challenges coherentism.**
