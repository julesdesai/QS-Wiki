# Can a belief be true and justified but not count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4925e0b-46b4-410e-97f9-2b0c4bf72c2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual believes a proposition, the proposition is true, and the individual's belief is justified by reliable but fallible foundational beliefs. Intuitively, we would judge that the individual does not know the proposition due to potential errors in the foundational beliefs despite them being reliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only belief, truth, and justification but also an additional condition that rules out the possibility of being wrong due to reliable yet fallible beliefs.**
