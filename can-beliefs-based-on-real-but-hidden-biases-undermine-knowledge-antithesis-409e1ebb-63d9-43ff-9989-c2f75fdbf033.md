# Can beliefs based on real but hidden biases undermine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 409e1ebb-63d9-43ff-9989-c2f75fdbf033
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**A person could have a belief that is true and has justification which does not come from false information but is subconsciously influenced by a bias they are unaware of.**, **Intuitively, there is a strong inclination to think that knowledge should be immune to personal biases or hidden motives.**, **If true beliefs can be influenced by subconscious biases unknown to the believer, it raises the question of whether such a belief genuinely constitutes knowledge.**
