# Does a false belief imply no knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 31a4a3d6-adcb-4144-9a81-b05fb9af7825
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Refined Virtue Epistemology](refined-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed8e6ab8-d432-4955-9aec-982a4b148be7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person firmly believes in a proposition that is, in fact, false. In such a case, it is implausible to say that the person has knowledge, as knowledge requires truth.**, **Truth is a necessary component for knowledge, and thus any belief that is false cannot be considered knowledge.**
