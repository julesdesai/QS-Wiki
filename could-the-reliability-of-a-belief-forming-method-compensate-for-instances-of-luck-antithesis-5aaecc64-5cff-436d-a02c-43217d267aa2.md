# Could the reliability of a belief-forming method compensate for instances of luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5aaecc64-5cff-436d-a02c-43217d267aa2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Luck View](no-luck-view-synthesis-bccfbb90-e542-414d-b962-c3799a2855c9.md)

## Content

**A reliable belief-forming method can mitigate the role of epistemic luck in arriving at a true belief.**, **Suppose a person uses a highly accurate device that occasionally malfunctions due to random external factors, yet mostly provides reliable information. This person could still be said to have knowledge despite occasional luck-induced errors.**, **The consistent success of a method outweighs sporadic luck-influenced errors, suggesting the presence of knowledge even when luck occasionally affects the belief.**
