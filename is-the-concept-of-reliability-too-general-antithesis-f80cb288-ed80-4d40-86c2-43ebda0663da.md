# Is the concept of reliability too general?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f80cb288-ed80-4d40-86c2-43ebda0663da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a general description of reliability can account for the varied nature and contexts of different types of knowledge.**, **Suppose a person uses a reliable process to identify species of plants and also another distinct process to understand quantum mechanics. Intuitively, the notion of reliability in these different domains suggests nuanced differences not captured by a singular concept.**
