# Can one know something without having any belief about that thing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30d0d648-c2b4-49ac-90a3-54a3f00e0c78
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Belief Necessary View](no-belief-necessary-view-synthesis-81f9ccf2-9484-47e2-8ae4-3b9845798b1d.md)

## Content

**Knowledge seems to require at least some form of mental assent or commitment to a proposition, which belief provides.**, **It is implausible that someone can know a fact without at least implicitly assenting to that fact.**, **If a person can know something without believing it, counterintuitively, they could also simultaneously deny it while allegedly "knowing" it.**
