# Can external factors undermine knowledge even if the belief is virtuous?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d2a35bc-da5e-4103-a2a5-421808856e5c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief through intellectual virtue, but external factors beyond their control make their belief unreliable. Intuitively, the presence of undermining external factors suggests the belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **An external factor that induces unreliability in belief formation challenges the notion that knowledge arises reliably from intellectual virtue.**
