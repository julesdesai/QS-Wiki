# Does reliabilism conflict with internalist intuitions about justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 59542242-0d2f-4e3e-934f-494cf4385f49
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**Internalists argue that justification requires awareness or access to the reasons for one's beliefs. Intuitively, this seems correct because we often demand that people reflect on their beliefs to claim they are justified.**, **Reliabilism allows for justification that does not require awareness of the reliability of the process, which conflicts with the internalist intuition of needing access to reasons.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief formed by a reliable subconscious process. Intuitively, we might feel uneasy passing such a belief as justified knowledge due to lack of awareness.**
