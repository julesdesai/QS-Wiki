# Can a coherent belief system justify false beliefs under Foundationalist Coherentism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5f25fd3-3a26-44a9-965f-7dcc6fa057c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**A coherent belief system relies on the interconnections and support among beliefs to establish justification.**, **It is possible for a system of interconnected beliefs to be internally consistent yet partially or wholly false if it lacks a relationship with external reality.**, **Without an external check, coherence might justify beliefs that are false, questioning the reliability of coherence as a primary measure of justification.**
