# How does disjunctivism handle the learning and recognition processes in perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca0065d3-3d6b-4f67-a84b-d090796df2db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-6c9ba281-6911-4c85-9826-b83f47b211cf.md)

## Content

**Consider a child learning to recognize dogs through both seeing real dogs and through imagination or hallucination of dogs. The recognition process appears similar in both cases.**, **The similarity in learning and recognition suggests that the experiences have more in common than disjunctivism allows.**, **If recognition can arise equally from veridical perception and hallucination, then there is tension with the fundamental difference assertion.**
