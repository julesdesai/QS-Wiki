# Can knowledge rely on misleading true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a375d76-9b1e-4816-bfe1-abea07a33f9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma View](no-false-lemma-view-synthesis-d417d5a7-7b9d-4d6b-8a89-c64775b0283f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition on the basis of misleading true evidence that suggests a false background assumption. Intuitively, the belief might still not amount to knowledge despite being true and justified.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief should not rely on misleading true evidence to be considered knowledge. This principle together with the idea that knowledge requires no false grounds entails that misleading true evidence could also disqualify knowledge.**
