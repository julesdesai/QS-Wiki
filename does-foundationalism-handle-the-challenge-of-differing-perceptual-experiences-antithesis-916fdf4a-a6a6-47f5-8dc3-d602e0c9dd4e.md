# Does foundationalism handle the challenge of differing perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 916fdf4a-a6a6-47f5-8dc3-d602e0c9dd4e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**Perceptual experiences can vary among individuals, leading to different beliefs about the same sensory event.**, **For example, two people looking at a dress might disagree on its color because of lighting conditions affecting perception.**, **A principle of foundationalism is that basic beliefs should be evident to the senses regardless of individual differences.**, **If foundationalism requires belief to align among individuals for justification, differing perceptions pose a problem.**
