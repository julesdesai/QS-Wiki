# Can external objects be known without context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1dd75ce9-2804-4619-a8e1-0f3a65fd9c94
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person stumbles upon an unfamiliar object and ascertains its function through direct observation challenges the necessity of context in knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that certain empirical observations can yield knowledge about external objects without contextual interpretation.**, **It is implausible that context is always required for understanding external objects, as direct evidence can sometimes be sufficient.**
