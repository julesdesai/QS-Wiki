# Can perception be purely imaginative without a real-world counterpart?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 892f92b7-6435-4218-aadf-16e5814b71f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Perspective](phenomenological-perspective-synthesis-c5a75a88-a263-494b-bef9-8c1e426bd706.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person experiences the vivid sensation of seeing a landscape that does not exist. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person is not in direct perceptual contact with an actual landscape.**, **The immediate feel of perception can be indistinguishable from an imaginative or hallucinatory experience, which does not involve real-world relations.**
