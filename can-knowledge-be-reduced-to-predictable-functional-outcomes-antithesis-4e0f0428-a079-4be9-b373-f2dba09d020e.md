# Can knowledge be reduced to predictable functional outcomes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e0f0428-a079-4be9-b373-f2dba09d020e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person predicts the taste of an apple without actually experiencing its flavor. Intuitively, we would judge that actual taste experience, rather than mere prediction, constitutes knowledge of the flavor.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires engagement with direct experiences rather than solely relying on functional predictability.**
