# Does contextualism nullify the concept of absolute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67fc8325-a3e6-49cb-804b-19dd92e2b7cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a universally accepted fact is questioned in a particular context. Intuitively, we either have to say that the fact is no longer knowledge, or that context doesn't affect such knowledge. If we say it's no longer knowledge, this negates the idea of absolute knowledge. If we say context doesn't affect it, this challenges the core idea of contextualism.**, **A plausible principle is that certain facts are absolute and should remain unaffected by contextual nuances. This principle, together with contextualism, entails a contradiction in acknowledging absolute facts.**
