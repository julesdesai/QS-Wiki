# Can practical utility be necessary for knowledge in purely observational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69fa9f79-a668-445f-af00-cb07406d1819
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d7b209ba-b523-44b3-ad9a-c228c53c8771.md)

## Content

**Observational beliefs, such as noting the weather, may not always have obvious practical benefits.**, **Individuals can know observational truths without deriving any direct practical benefit.**, **Stipulating practical benefit as a criterion might exclude simple, everyday knowledge.**
