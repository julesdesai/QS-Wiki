# Can knowledge persist without continuous verification through sensory data?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17eeb2e9-867c-4ef1-b193-a7d0086c8a46
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empiricism Summary](empiricism-summary-synthesis-4e15833e-fa6c-416c-a78e-6563a82d7628.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person learns a historical fact, such as a specific event from the 18th century, through books and education. Intuitively, we either have to say that this knowledge persists without sensory verification or it doesn’t persist. If we say it persists without continuous verification, then knowledge does not rely solely on ongoing sensory data.**
