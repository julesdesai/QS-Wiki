# Is belief alone sufficient for claiming knowledge even with reliability and responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b92c524-1a05-4eb9-b6b6-cec3bf86ae66
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Mixed Virtue Epistemology](mixed-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9b1d3c06-dd01-4be5-8e49-871c85befc1a.md)

## Content

**Think of a scenario where an expert temporarily doubts their conclusion due to misinformation but still holds the true proposition under consideration through reliability and responsibility. Intuitively, we might still attribute knowledge despite doubt.**, **If belief requires unwavering certainty, then including doubt challenges the sufficiency of mere true belief for defining knowledge under conditions of reliability and responsibility.**
