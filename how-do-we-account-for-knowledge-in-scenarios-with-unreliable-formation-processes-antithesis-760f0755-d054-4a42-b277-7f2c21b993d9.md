# How do we account for knowledge in scenarios with unreliable formation processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 760f0755-d054-4a42-b277-7f2c21b993d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable True Belief](knowledge-as-reliable-true-belief-synthesis-73884ab1-e6ad-49fe-9997-a4e027a9805c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Jane, forms a belief that it is sunny based solely on reading a faulty weather app that predicts sunshine inaccurately. Intuitively, we would judge that Jane does not know that it is sunny, even if it turns out to be true.**, **A belief formed from an unreliable process, such as consulting a faulty weather app, cannot constitute knowledge, as it lacks the reliability required for knowledge.**
