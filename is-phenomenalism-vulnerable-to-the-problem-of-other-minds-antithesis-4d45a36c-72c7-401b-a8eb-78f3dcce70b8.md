# Is phenomenalism vulnerable to the problem of other minds?

**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism suggests that physical objects are logical constructions of sensory experiences.](phenomenalism-suggests-that-physical-objects-are-logical-constructions-of-sensory-experiences-synthesis-70c8baf6-067c-48d6-b521-64b5ff284db6.md)

## Content

**The belief in other minds involves accepting the existence of experiences not directly perceivable. Intuitively, this reliance on inference challenges the idea that objects, including minds, are only constructions of one's sensory experiences.**, **If phenomenalism were true, it would be difficult to justify belief in other minds, as such acceptance assumes a reality beyond one's direct experiences.**
