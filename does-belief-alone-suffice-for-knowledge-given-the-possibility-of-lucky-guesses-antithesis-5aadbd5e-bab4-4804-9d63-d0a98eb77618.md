# Does belief alone suffice for knowledge given the possibility of lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5aadbd5e-bab4-4804-9d63-d0a98eb77618
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-0c8c051d-f89d-468b-87e6-c5a1fb54f910.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes their team will win a match without adequate evidence and it turns out they win because of an unexpected event like a sudden injury to the opponent's star player. Intuitively, we would judge that the person’s belief is correct but it does not count as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge requires more than just believing something true; it requires justification. In scenarios where belief is true by accident or luck, intuitively we would judge that it does not amount to knowledge.**, **The belief that something is true does not necessarily mean it is known if the truth of the belief is not appropriately connected to the person's reasons for holding that belief.**
