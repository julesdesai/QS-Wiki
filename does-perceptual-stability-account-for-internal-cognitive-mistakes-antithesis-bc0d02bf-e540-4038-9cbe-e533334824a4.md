# Does perceptual stability account for internal cognitive mistakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc0d02bf-e540-4038-9cbe-e533334824a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone consistently perceives that it is hot, due to a fever, when it is actually cold. Intuitively, their stable internal perceptual experiences do not provide knowledge since cognitive mistakes are influencing perception.**, **There is a plausible principle that cognitive biases or errors can undermine the reliability of stable perceptions, suggesting that stability needs to be supplemented by other factors to result in knowledge.**
