# Can knowledge be merely true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0ceb052-9215-48be-9324-9deef7ffa151
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Coherentism](contextualist-coherentism-synthesis-598dc05f-03d6-4aeb-b0db-fa7173390f96.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person flip a coin predicting heads without any reasoning and gets it right. They believe truly that it landed heads. Intuitively, guessing correctly does not constitute knowing.**, **Knowledge requires more than true belief, as mere true belief through chance or guessing lacks justification.**
