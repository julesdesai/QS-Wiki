# Does contextualism fail to account for disagreement resolution?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4c30926-29aa-4699-a395-4dbf586c233c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-970b1696-c212-4dd0-be78-254d7b0cf7ce.md)

## Content

**When two parties disagree on a fact, it is expected that there exist ways to resolve the disagreement. If knowledge is context-dependent and varies with standards, it becomes implausibly difficult to find a resolution.**
