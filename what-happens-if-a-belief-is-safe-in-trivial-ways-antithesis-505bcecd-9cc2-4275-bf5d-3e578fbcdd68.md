# What happens if a belief is safe in trivial ways?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 505bcecd-9cc2-4275-bf5d-3e578fbcdd68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a true belief about today's weather because they believe a false premise combined with a lucky guess, where in most trivially similar situations the belief would hold. Intuitively, this does not count as knowledge.**, **Not easily false beliefs in trivially similar situations can still be based on incorrect reasoning, which undermines the claim that the belief constitutes knowledge.**
