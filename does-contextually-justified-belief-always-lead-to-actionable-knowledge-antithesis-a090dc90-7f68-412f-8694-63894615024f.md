# Does contextually justified belief always lead to actionable knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a090dc90-7f68-412f-8694-63894615024f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-b0409110-d832-4fdb-9fe3-2e01026d1f88.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where beliefs justified in context do not translate into effective action due to context change. Intuitively, this suggests contextual justification might not correlate with actionable knowledge.**, **It is implausible that justified belief in one context guarantees practical outcomes in another.**
