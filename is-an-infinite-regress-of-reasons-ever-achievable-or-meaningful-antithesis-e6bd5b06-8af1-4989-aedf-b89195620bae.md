# Is an infinite regress of reasons ever achievable or meaningful?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e6bd5b06-8af1-4989-aedf-b89195620bae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-db00e7c1-6a13-4ca5-8f09-6fae7cd29e0f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person attempts to justify a belief about the color of an apple. Intuitively, we would judge that an infinite series of reasons for such a mundane belief is excessive and unnecessary.**, **It is implausible that any belief, particularly simple ones, genuinely requires an infinite chain of reasons for justification, suggesting the requirement is over-demanding and not applicable to everyday knowledge.**
