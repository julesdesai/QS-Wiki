# Does the notion of true belief accommodate complexity in unknowable contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52860d6c-8600-47bd-ad10-505a4a0fccd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [True Belief as Primary in Knowledge](true-belief-as-primary-in-knowledge-synthesis-f83d0a87-6cde-4f50-a75a-0024fde50cce.md)

## Content

**Beliefs about propositions in complex, unknowable environments cannot guarantee truth or knowledge.**, **A true belief in such contexts could be superficial without understanding or relevance, questioning its status as knowledge.**, **Knowledge in contexts beyond our grasp requires more than accidently true beliefs as such occurrences shouldn't define truth's precedence.**
