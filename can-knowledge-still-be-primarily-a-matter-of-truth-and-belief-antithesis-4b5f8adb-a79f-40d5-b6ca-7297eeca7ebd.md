# Can knowledge still be primarily a matter of truth and belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b5f8adb-a79f-40d5-b6ca-7297eeca7ebd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Consider the principle that the essence of knowledge lies in its being true belief. This principle challenges any additional conditions like intellectual virtue, suggesting that even without it, truth and belief may suffice for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that true belief without virtue is never knowledge. This implausibility raises doubts about the necessity of the virtue condition in all cases of knowledge.**
