# Is knowledge independent of any external social factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c114a868-73e6-4c2b-a22c-c73db3e48dad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone claims knowledge of a complex scientific theory solely based on their internal understanding, while being completely isolated from peer review or scientific community validation. Intuitively, we would judge that such solitary understanding does not suffice as scientific knowledge.**, **It is implausible that internal factors alone determine knowledge, especially when social validation and consensus are crucial in areas like science.**
