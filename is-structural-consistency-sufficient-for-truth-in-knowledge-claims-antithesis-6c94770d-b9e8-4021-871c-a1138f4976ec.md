# Is structural consistency sufficient for truth in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c94770d-b9e8-4021-871c-a1138f4976ec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structural Realism](epistemic-structural-realism-synthesis-4cc8fed2-62df-47a3-af1a-0c0e49fc23b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person understands the structural relationships between particles in physics, yet the real-world interactions deviate from these structures due to unknown factors. Intuitively, if structural coherence does not align with all real interactions, then knowledge based solely on these structures can be deemed inaccurate.**, **It is implausible that all truthful knowledge claims can solely rely on structural consistency without detailed confirmation in worldly particulars.**
