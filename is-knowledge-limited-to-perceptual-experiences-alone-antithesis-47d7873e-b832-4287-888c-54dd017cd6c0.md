# Is knowledge limited to perceptual experiences alone?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47d7873e-b832-4287-888c-54dd017cd6c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Phenomenalism](moderate-phenomenalism-synthesis-66f89d49-d085-4cff-b6cd-a9e130c91f7b.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that our knowledge is limited solely to perceptual experiences, as we often have knowledge of abstract concepts like numbers and mathematics which do not directly arise from perceptual experience.**, **Consider a case in which a mathematician provides a proof of a theorem using abstract reasoning; intuitively, we judge that the mathematician has knowledge of the theorem even though it does not pertain to sensory perception.**
