# Does a high chance of reliability imply knowledge if it fails in a specific instance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52c0aea6-62cd-4a0a-8b8c-b16f0c156729
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a meteorologist uses a statistical model that is 90% accurate to predict the weather, but on a particular day, their prediction is incorrect. In this case, the reliable process produces a false belief.**, **The belief-forming process was reliable in general but failed in this specific instance, suggesting that reliability alone is insufficient for knowledge if the outcome is not true.**
