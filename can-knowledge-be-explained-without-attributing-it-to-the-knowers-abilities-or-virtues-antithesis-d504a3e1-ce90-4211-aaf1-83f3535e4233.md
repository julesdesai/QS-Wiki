# Can knowledge be explained without attributing it to the knower's abilities or virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d504a3e1-ce90-4211-aaf1-83f3535e4233
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ad239dac-f508-456d-984a-1344bc3c3ad8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we encounter a case in which societal influences or instincts lead accurately to knowledge without individual cognitive exertion. Intuitively, the person still possesses knowledge.**, **It is plausible to believe that environmental and social scaffolding can sometimes lead to knowledge independently of the knower's abilities or virtues.**
