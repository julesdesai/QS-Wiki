# Is causal explanation sufficient for knowledge of necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c42db456-6399-4cdc-ae93-76a026183d31
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge of necessary truths, like laws of logic or mathematics, does not rely on causal relationships since these truths are immutable and not subject to causal influence.**, **Consider a scenario where a mathematician derives a theorem from axioms; intuitively, this form of knowledge is not founded on a causal link with the theorem being true.**
