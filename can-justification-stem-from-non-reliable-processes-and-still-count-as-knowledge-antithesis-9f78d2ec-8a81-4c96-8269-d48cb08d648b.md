# Can justification stem from non-reliable processes and still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f78d2ec-8a81-4c96-8269-d48cb08d648b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliability](virtue-epistemology-with-reliability-synthesis-6944a8d9-8cde-45e2-a167-ffb34caa5f33.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person arrives at a true belief through a process that is not typically reliable, like guessing, yet happens to be correct. Intuitively, one might consider this belief as knowledge due to its truth, challenging the link between reliability and justification.**, **It is implausible to suppose that true beliefs arrived at through non-reliable processes can never count as knowledge.**
