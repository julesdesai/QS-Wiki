# How can foundational beliefs alone justify other beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e1304d1-25eb-4142-8501-b6d6b2ab910d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-1575945f-cea5-4275-8c31-c7cabab6c71a.md)

## Content

**In a case where a foundational belief is isolated without contributing to the coherence of the overall belief system, intuitively, it seems unable to provide justification to other beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification often requires systemic coherence rather than reliance on a singular self-evident belief.**
