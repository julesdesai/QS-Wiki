# What if reliable beliefs fail to account for new evidence over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a belief is formed reliably, yet the believer fails to revise the belief in light of new evidence, leading to a false belief. Intuitively, such stagnation in belief modification questions the reliability criterion for continued knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should reflect an ability to adapt beliefs to new evidence; a failure in updating challenges the status of knowledge.**
