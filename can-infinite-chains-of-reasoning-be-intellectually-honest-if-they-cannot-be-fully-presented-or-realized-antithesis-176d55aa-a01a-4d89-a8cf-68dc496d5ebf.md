# Can infinite chains of reasoning be intellectually honest if they cannot be fully presented or realized?

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that intellectual honesty in justification requires acknowledging limits to our reasoning capacity. This principle implies that claiming infinite justifications betrays intellectual honesty.**, **Truth-telling about justifications implies transparency, compromised if the chain extends beyond conceivable comprehension.**
