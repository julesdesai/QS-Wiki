# Can reliabilism handle cases of clairvoyant intuition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 58335bdd-a278-4a9d-a2d0-4b5cc2f9aa77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a reliable clairvoyant ability to predict future events, such as the outcome of a political election, even without empirical evidence or rational justification**, **In this case, reliabilism could claim the person knows the result of the election based purely on their clairvoyant intuition**, **However, intuitively, we might resist the notion that such a belief amounts to knowledge due to the lack of rational grounds or understanding**
