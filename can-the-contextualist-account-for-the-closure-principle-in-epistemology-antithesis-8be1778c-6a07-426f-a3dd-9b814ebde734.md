# Can the contextualist account for the closure principle in epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8be1778c-6a07-426f-a3dd-9b814ebde734
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**The closure principle suggests that if a person knows one proposition, and this proposition logically entails another, then the person should also know the latter proposition.**, **Consider a situation where someone knows they have hands and, by implication, knows they are not a brain in a vat. Intuitively, there is a strong case for maintaining both knowledge claims.**, **Contextualism might struggle to maintain closure if shifts in context can elevate standards for connected knowledge claims unexpectedly.**
