# Does every instance of perceptual error undermine justification in the causal theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3acb5aba-4c74-4918-bdf8-6786f561ce6b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory](goldmans-causal-theory-synthesis-55eaa724-2453-46d2-8e70-b1e77bdcb753.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person sees a bent stick in water and forms the belief that the stick is bent is a case of perceptual error.**, **A case of perceptual error is a case where there is no causal connection between belief and fact.**, **A belief formed in perceptual error does not meet the requirements of the causal theory.**
