# Can intellectual virtues account for all cases of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bbfd3b33-d803-4e47-a77d-a737b67f7700
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief due to a lucky guess. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite their belief being true.**, **A person may have a true belief without relying on an intellectual virtue, as in the case of a guess or random choice.**, **It is implausible that intellectual virtues can account for cases of knowledge where true belief arises from luck or chance**.
