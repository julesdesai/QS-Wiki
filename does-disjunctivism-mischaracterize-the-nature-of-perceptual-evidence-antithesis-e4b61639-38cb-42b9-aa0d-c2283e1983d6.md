# Does disjunctivism mischaracterize the nature of perceptual evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4b61639-38cb-42b9-aa0d-c2283e1983d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-19882849-157e-4dc6-b31d-66173a7c79c0.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person sees a tree and another where a person merely dreams of seeing a tree. In both cases, they have the same kind of perceptual access to reality, yet disjunctivism seems to claim only one has access to facts. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to categorize these differentially merely based on the veridical nature of perception.**, **The notion that perceptual experience could be evidence for knowledge, independent of its veracity, raises doubts about the sufficiency claim of perceptual experience for knowledge.**
