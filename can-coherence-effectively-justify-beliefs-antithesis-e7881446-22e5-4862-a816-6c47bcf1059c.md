# Can coherence effectively justify beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7881446-22e5-4862-a816-6c47bcf1059c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimal Foundational Coherentism Summary](minimal-foundational-coherentism-summary-synthesis-0e7aa745-4942-4fbb-a96c-6043bbe35e5d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a coherent belief system could be internally consistent but entirely disconnected from external reality. A belief system could be coherent due to internal logical consistency yet still be incorrect about the world. Intuitively, justification should involve some external alignment with reality beyond mere coherence.**
