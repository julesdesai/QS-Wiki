# Is it irrational to align knowledge thresholds with practical stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56f03fa0-3cdf-4a98-9249-bb4823f101a7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment with Influence of Practical Stakes](pragmatic-encroachment-with-influence-of-practical-stakes-synthesis-1e83d295-b88f-4064-889e-097b4fb938ca.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that rational deliberation should separate evidential justification from practical consequences. This principle implies that elevating knowledge thresholds solely based on practical stakes is irrational.**, **Consider a scenario in which a business executive must make a decision based on market forecasts. Intuitively, the level of practical stakes should not influence the executive’s knowledge of market trends determined by evidence.**
