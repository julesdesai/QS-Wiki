# Are finite and infinite chains reconcilable within the theory of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4bc15b2c-ca53-4eb7-958f-bb8a2d5d4f9c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-f73c0505-1eaa-4768-8143-11a2f8c9ca92.md)

## Content

**An infinite chain implies endless reasons, while practical reasoning ends at a finite point where one feels justified.**, **Philosophically, a finite chain cannot be stretched to infinite without inherently changing its nature.**, **The assumption that finite and infinite chains can coexist undercuts the foundational difference between finite conclusions and infinite regress.**
