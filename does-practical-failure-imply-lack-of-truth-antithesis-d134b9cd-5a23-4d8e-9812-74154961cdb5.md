# Does practical failure imply lack of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d134b9cd-5a23-4d8e-9812-74154961cdb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**A faulty scientific experiment may fail in practice due to technical errors, despite the underlying scientific theory being true and well-supported theoretically. Practical failure doesn't negate truth in all cases.**, **Truth can exist when actions grounded in a belief fail practically, as the issue may lie with the execution, not the knowledge.**
