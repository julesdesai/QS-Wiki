# Is the belief's truth in nearby possible worlds sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eea8e24f-eb90-4c29-9d1b-cce59da07e57
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Externalism Summary](safety-condition-externalism-summary-synthesis-29bd2257-ee31-4256-8506-f8628368ab8d.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person correctly believes a proposition, but their belief is based on false premises in nearby possible worlds. Intuitively, we would say this person does not have knowledge, as their reasoning is flawed.**, **It is plausible that true beliefs in nearby possible worlds should be based on sound reasoning to count as knowledge, which challenges the view that mere truth in such worlds is sufficient.**
