# Could contextual phenomenalism lead to skepticism about external reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24a52438-0b15-44b3-93de-87470e1fe2e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**Suppose knowledge relies on contextualizing perceptual experiences; intuitively, this could imply that external reality is unknowable outside of contextually-mediated perceptions.**, **It is implausible that we cannot have direct knowledge of external reality without contextual overlays.**, **A scenario where all perceptual data is hypothetical or imagined challenges the ability to claim knowledge of an independently existing external reality.**
