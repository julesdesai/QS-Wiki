# Can improper but reliable functioning cognitive faculties produce knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 272fe39c-b9a7-4882-a29e-214636811da1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism Summary](proper-functionalism-summary-synthesis-5b044e3f-97d9-4386-9c2f-bd333212a4a8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a cognitive faculty that consistently produces true beliefs but as a result of an accident rather than a well-designed plan. Intuitively, we might not consider the beliefs formed through this means as knowledge.**, **A cognitive faculty functioning reliably but not according to a design plan casts doubt on whether knowledge is present despite the reliability of true belief attainment.**
