# Can sensitivity explain judgments of knowledge in testimonial beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9bdf3ae-1290-4a88-ad11-32e056082ea2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person trusts a news report saying a political candidate has won an election, which turns out true. Intuitively, the person might be said to know the result based on reliable testimony.**, **If the report were false in a close possible world, it's plausible their belief would change, hence remaining sensitive, though the knowledge could rest more fundamentally on the credibility of the source.**
