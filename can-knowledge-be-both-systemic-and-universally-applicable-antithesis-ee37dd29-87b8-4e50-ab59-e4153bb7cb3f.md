# Can knowledge be both systemic and universally applicable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee37dd29-87b8-4e50-ab59-e4153bb7cb3f

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Pluralism](epistemic-pluralism-synthesis-81e4116f-f2b8-419c-97e5-1ca5c482c2fd.md)

## Content

**A universally applicable definition of knowledge would require a standard that transcends individual epistemic systems.**, **Epistemic pluralism implies that knowledge is contingent upon the system applied, which conflicts with universality.**, **Conceptual tension arises when attempting to integrate systemic knowledge claims with global applicability.**
