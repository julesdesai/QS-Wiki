# Does contextualism allow for manipulation of knowledge attributions based on context selection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca33e638-4337-4510-bdc7-3308c3950e72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who shifts the context of a discussion to undermine or reinforce knowledge claims selectively.**, **While such manipulation appears possible, it seems counterintuitive that knowledge could be subjectively altered just by changing the conversational or situational context.**, **There is a potential for abuse by using contextual shifts to strategically affirm or deny knowledge depending on desired outcomes, raising ethical concerns about knowledge attribution.**
