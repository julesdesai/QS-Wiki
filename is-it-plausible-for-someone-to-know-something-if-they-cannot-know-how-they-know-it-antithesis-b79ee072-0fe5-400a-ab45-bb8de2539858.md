# Is it plausible for someone to know something if they cannot know how they know it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b79ee072-0fe5-400a-ab45-bb8de2539858
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as an externalist account of knowledge](reliabilism-as-an-externalist-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-f53d2eeb-6036-46fc-bee7-531fa51577e9.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims to know a proposition because it is true and they believe it, but they have no understanding of the reliability of how they came to that belief; intuitively, it seems problematic to assert they have knowledge.**, **Knowledge seems to imply not just that a belief is true and reliably formed, but also that it is accompanied by some level of metacognitive awareness or understanding of the justification.**
