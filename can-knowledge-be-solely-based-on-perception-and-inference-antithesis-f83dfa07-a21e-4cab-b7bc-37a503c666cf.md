# Can knowledge be solely based on perception and inference?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f83dfa07-a21e-4cab-b7bc-37a503c666cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Inference](phenomenalism-with-inference-synthesis-5c9b476a-f87e-418f-98f3-b4842f3fdc21.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's perceptual experience is indistinguishable from a vivid hallucination and they infer knowledge from it. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have genuine knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that for knowledge to be genuine, it must be based on a reliable method. This principle together with the unreliability of the inferred knowledge from hallucinations entails that perceptual experiences alone cannot provide genuine knowledge.**
