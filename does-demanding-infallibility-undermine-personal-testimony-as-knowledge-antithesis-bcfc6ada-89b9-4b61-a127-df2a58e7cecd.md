# Does demanding infallibility undermine personal testimony as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bcfc6ada-89b9-4b61-a127-df2a58e7cecd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infallibilism](knowledge-as-infallibilism-synthesis-82427764-33dd-4339-b187-9cedd7ac6a36.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a friend recounts a personal experience of traveling to a foreign country. Despite the potential for forgotten details or exaggerations, intuitively, we accept this as knowledge about the friend's experience.**, **There is a plausible principle that personal testimony, though fallible, can provide genuine knowledge when reasonably believed and true, suggesting infallibilism's standard might be excessively demanding.**
