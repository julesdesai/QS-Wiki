# Does presupposing reliability and truth-tracking neglect the necessity of cognitive access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8bc6d80-6329-491a-84fd-158ee8503082
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Nozick's Condition](process-reliabilism-with-nozicks-condition-synthesis-5d350bc0-f5e6-4b33-b9c5-feeee7d84d21.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual forms beliefs that are reliable and truth-track, yet the individual has no cognitive awareness of the reliability of their belief formation mechanisms. Intuitively, we would judge that without cognitive access, it cannot be classified as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some form of cognitive access to the belief-forming process. This principle together with the scenario indicates a potential flaw in the view.**
