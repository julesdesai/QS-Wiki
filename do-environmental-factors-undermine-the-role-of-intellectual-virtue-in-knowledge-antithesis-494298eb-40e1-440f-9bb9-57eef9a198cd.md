# Do environmental factors undermine the role of intellectual virtue in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 494298eb-40e1-440f-9bb9-57eef9a198cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which an individual's intellectual virtues are undermined by unpredictable environmental factors, yet they coincidentally form a true belief. Intuitively, we might still want to count this as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that external factors impacting the success of intellectual virtues do not necessarily negate the knowledge status of a true belief. This principle implies that intellectual virtue might not have a central role in defining knowledge.**
