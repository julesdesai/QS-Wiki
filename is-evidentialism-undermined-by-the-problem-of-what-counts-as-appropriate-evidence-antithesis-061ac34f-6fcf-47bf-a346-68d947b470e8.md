# Is Evidentialism undermined by the problem of what counts as appropriate evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 061ac34f-6fcf-47bf-a346-68d947b470e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-a76d832e-447a-4168-b9be-6c867a1a7af1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that evidence needs to be clearly defined to serve as a basis for belief justification. This principle together with widespread disagreement about what constitutes appropriate evidence suggests that evidentialism lacks clarity and reliability.**, **It is implausible that evidence-based beliefs can be justified if the nature of appropriate evidence is unclear or subjective.**
