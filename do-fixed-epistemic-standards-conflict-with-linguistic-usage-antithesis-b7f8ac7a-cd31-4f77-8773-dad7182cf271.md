# Do fixed epistemic standards conflict with linguistic usage?

## Content

**Consider a case where speaker A asserts "I know X" and speaker B counters in a stricter context. Intuitively, B might be correct in saying A doesn't know.**, **Everyday language about knowing appears sensitive to context shifts, suggesting knowledge isn't entirely invariant.**, **If our ordinary linguistic practices suggest variance, fixed epistemic standards challenge common usage.**
