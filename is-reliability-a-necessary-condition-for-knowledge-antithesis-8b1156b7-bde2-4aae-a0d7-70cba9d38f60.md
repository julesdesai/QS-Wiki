# Is reliability a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b1156b7-bde2-4aae-a0d7-70cba9d38f60
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief based on a method that is generally unreliable. Intuitively, it seems possible for them to still know the proposition in this instance if their belief happens to be true.**, **It is implausible that reliability should be a necessary condition for knowledge because even unreliable methods can randomly yield true beliefs.**
