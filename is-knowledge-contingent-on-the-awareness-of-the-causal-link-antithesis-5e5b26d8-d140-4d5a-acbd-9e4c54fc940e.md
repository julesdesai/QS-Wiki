# Is knowledge contingent on the awareness of the causal link?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e5b26d8-d140-4d5a-acbd-9e4c54fc940e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who correctly predicts an occurrence based on instinct without awareness of why beliefs correlate with facts. Intuitively, such a belief feels less like knowledge due to lack of awareness of causal connections.**, **There is a plausible principle that awareness of the causal relationship is integral to deem beliefs as knowledge. This principle implies unconscious correct beliefs due to instincts fail to meet full criteria for knowledge.**, **It is implausible to consider unrecognized causal links sufficient for knowledge since awareness significantly validates the established connection between a belief and its truth.**
