# Why should mental representations require an external reality to correspond to?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30f09f0c-5c06-42eb-b7dd-3a5e7452cca7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Representationalism](epistemic-representationalism-synthesis-c86e8f4e-5440-4a21-8e04-4337bf022fd8.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that mental representations must always correspond to an external reality to constitute knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where hypothetical or imaginative thought leads to a profound understanding or insight. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person has gained some form of knowledge or understanding, even without an external reference point.**
