# Does contextualism imply that contradictory knowledge claims can both be true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2368411a-0737-4e70-ac63-630548dbb371
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**If knowledge depends on the context of the knower, then a person might claim to know a proposition while another might claim not to know it based solely on contextual changes.**, **This suggests that two conflicting claims about knowledge could be considered true depending on context, leading to potential contradictions.**, **Such a situation implies that basic logical principles of non-contradiction might be violated.**
