# Does reliability alone sufficiently guarantee knowledge if it can produce true beliefs by luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e6d1a7d8-4532-4419-b40e-b50efe57089b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-c84f45b2-357a-4b29-be66-6a09ffb029e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses a reliable method to guess answers on a multiple-choice test, getting most answers right by luck. Intuitively, this is not knowledge**, **A reliable process might still yield true beliefs by luck rather than genuine cognitive success, challenging the notion that reliability alone suffices for knowledge**
