# How does the virtue theory handle the prevalence of epistemic ambiguity?

## Content

**Imagine a philosopher using rigorous reasoning to reach a true conclusion about a complex philosophical issue, where epistemic ambiguity makes it unclear whether the belief depends on luck. Intuitively, it's problematic to claim the exclusion of luck.**, **Situations involving significant epistemic ambiguity suggest that intellectual virtues alone may be insufficient to fully eliminate luck, putting into question the foundational claim of virtue epistemology.**
