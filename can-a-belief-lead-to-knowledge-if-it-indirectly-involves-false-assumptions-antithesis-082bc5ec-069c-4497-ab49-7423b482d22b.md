# Can a belief lead to knowledge if it indirectly involves false assumptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 082bc5ec-069c-4497-ab49-7423b482d22b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma Theory](no-false-lemma-theory-synthesis-4a11ee42-2be9-4836-9dd6-d365881bb277.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes it is raining because they heard it on the radio, but the radio station is known to sometimes broadcast false weather reports. Intuitively, if the person’s belief that it is raining turns out to be correct, we may still question whether they truly know it is raining because the information source is unreliable due to false assumptions.**, **Suppose a person forms a true belief that their friend is at home because they see their friend's car in the driveway. Assume the car was left there by someone else for repair. The justification for the belief involves a false assumption, leading us to question whether this amounts to knowledge.**
