# Does coherentism provide a satisfactory account of how beliefs connect to the world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8bda050-d074-47f8-8a44-69789e6ca103
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a coherent set of beliefs about an imaginary world. Intuitively, their beliefs do not amount to knowledge about the actual world.**, **Beliefs about the actual world require some connection to reality or experience, which mere coherence may not provide.**, **It seems implausible that beliefs could give rise to knowledge without any correspondence to external facts or reality.**
