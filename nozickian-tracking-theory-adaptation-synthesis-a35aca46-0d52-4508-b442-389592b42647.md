## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition were false, they would not believe it, unless the disbelief's reason is not properly epistemic**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe it, have justification, it is true, and their belief tracks the truth, barring non-epistemic interferences**
