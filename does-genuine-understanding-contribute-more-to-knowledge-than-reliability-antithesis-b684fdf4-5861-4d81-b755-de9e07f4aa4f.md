# Does genuine understanding contribute more to knowledge than reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b684fdf4-5861-4d81-b755-de9e07f4aa4f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fec1f444-460d-4835-9da9-5fd5405f3ea1.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone solves a math problem by memorizing a method without understanding the underlying principles but the method is reliable and yields the correct solution. Intuitively, we might hesitate to say they truly "know" the solution.**, **Knowing typically implies understanding, and mere outcome reliability might lack the depth of understanding required for knowledge.**
