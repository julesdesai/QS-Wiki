# Can intellectual errors coexist with virtuous knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0eb3cb6f-aebe-4822-98c1-3277486fb182
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that virtuous epistemic activity should minimize the likelihood of intellectual error. This principle together with a scenario where errors do coexist with virtuous knowing suggests a contradiction between virtue and likelihood of error.**, **Consider a scenario in which an eminently knowledgeable scientist makes a factual error in one of their fields due to unforeseen experimental conditions. Intuitively, this seems to present a case where knowing is virtuous yet fallible, challenging whether errors can coexist with virtues.**
