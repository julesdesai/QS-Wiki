# Is knowledge reducible to practical success?

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a businessperson makes consistently successful decisions solely through luck and without understanding. Intuitively, such practical success does not seem to constitute knowledge in the absence of understanding.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires understanding and justification beyond practical success. This implies knowledge is not reducible to mere practical success.**
