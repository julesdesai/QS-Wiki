# Can knowledge exist even if not all relevant alternatives are ruled out?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d3daf68-0684-4437-b901-ab2564faa893
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person P knows that the object in front of them is a zebra at a zoo and not a cleverly painted mule. Intuitively, we would say P knows it's a zebra despite not addressing the painted mule alternative.**, **There is a plausible principle that if someone knows a fact, they do not need to rule out every relevant alternative explicitly to have knowledge.**
