# How does perception truly correspond to reality under contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 698a544f-52f3-4c20-8dd8-cdcda377bde4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two observers perceive a situation differently yet claim knowledge based on their perceptions. Intuitively, we would judge that only one perception can accurately correspond to reality if it claims truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that truthful knowledge must correspond directly to an objective reality. This principle, coupled with the reliance on personal perception within different contexts, creates tension with the objectivity of reality.**
