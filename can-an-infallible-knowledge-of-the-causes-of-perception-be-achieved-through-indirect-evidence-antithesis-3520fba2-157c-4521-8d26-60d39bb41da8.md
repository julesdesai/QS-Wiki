# Can an infallible knowledge of the causes of perception be achieved through indirect evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3520fba2-157c-4521-8d26-60d39bb41da8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that infallible knowledge of the causes of our perceptions can be achieved when experiences are mediated through indirect sources that can be misleading or erroneous.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person concludes their headache is caused by dehydration, based solely on feeling thirsty. Intuitively, we have to say that either their conclusion is based on a possibly incorrect inference, or it is not. If it is based on incorrect inference, then infallible knowledge cannot be clearly derived. If it is not, their certainty is unwarranted.**
