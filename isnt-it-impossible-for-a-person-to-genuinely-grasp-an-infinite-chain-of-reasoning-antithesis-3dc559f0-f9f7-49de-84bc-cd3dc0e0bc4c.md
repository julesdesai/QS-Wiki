# Isn't it impossible for a person to genuinely grasp an infinite chain of reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3dc559f0-f9f7-49de-84bc-cd3dc0e0bc4c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-a48a630f-d4e8-4dc2-b25a-518a64dbd1fb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims to know the mathematical fact that 2+2=4. If knowing this requires an infinite chain of reasoning, the person would need to comprehend that infinite series, which is not feasible for finite beings.**, **A justified belief requiring an infinite chain of reasoning assumes that it is possible for a human mind to grasp infinite concepts, which contradicts our limitations.**
