# How do we account for different perceptions under identical conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4a1f951e-c888-4576-b4ab-1a681b556312
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-42a15467-8636-4e99-a9b6-42a7b3d54195.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two people perceive the same object differently due to varying past experiences or expectations, one person sees a face in abstract shapes while the other sees random patterns. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception involves subjective interpretations or that there is a fundamental flaw in direct realism. If we say perception is purely direct, variations in perception under identical conditions remain unexplained.**
