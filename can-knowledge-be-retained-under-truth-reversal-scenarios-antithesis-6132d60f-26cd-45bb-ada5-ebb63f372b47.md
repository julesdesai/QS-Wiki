# Can knowledge be retained under truth-reversal scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6132d60f-26cd-45bb-ada5-ebb63f372b47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-f1663159-56f4-4f6f-b53a-d60dc9dbfcb7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a true proposition based on reliable evidence, but if the proposition were false, the person’s belief would remain due to the nature of the evidence. Intuitively, we might judge the person still knows the proposition despite not satisfying the requirement that if the proposition were false, they would not believe it.**, **There is a plausible principle that someone who justifiably holds a true belief based on compelling evidence should be said to have knowledge irrespective of hypothetical contrary beliefs. This principle would contradict the claim that knowledge requires sensitivity to the truth of the proposition under condition of falsity.**
