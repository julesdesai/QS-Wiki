# Does contextual variation affect knowledge assessment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f184885-0655-4c32-92d3-33f2d8d7e0ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person claims to know a trivial fact at a casual dinner and the same person claims to know a complex scientific fact in a scientific conference. Intuitively, we would judge that the standards for what counts as knowing are different in the two contexts.**, **It is implausible that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant regardless of context if different intuitions arise in distinct situations regarding knowledge assessment.**
