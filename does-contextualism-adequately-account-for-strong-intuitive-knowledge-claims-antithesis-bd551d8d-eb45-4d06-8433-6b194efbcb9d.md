# Does contextualism adequately account for strong intuitive knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd551d8d-eb45-4d06-8433-6b194efbcb9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**In cases where someone strongly judges that another person knows a proposition, this judgment often seems robust regardless of context.**, **If contextualism implies that the knowledge claim depends on varying standards, it may fail to account for these strong, stable intuitions about knowledge.**, **There is an intuition that some knowledge claims are universally valid, challenging the idea that they are contingent on context.**
