# Does knowledge truly depend on the context of the knower?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 072f229e-c266-475a-a4c1-bde141f537d5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people witness the same event and acquire the same evidence supporting a true proposition about it. Intuitively, it seems they both should know the proposition regardless of contextual differences.**, **It seems implausible that two people with the exact same evidence and truth conditions would have different knowledge statuses based solely on their contextual differences.**
