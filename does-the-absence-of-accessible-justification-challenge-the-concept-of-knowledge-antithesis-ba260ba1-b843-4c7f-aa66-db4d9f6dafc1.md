# Does the absence of accessible justification challenge the concept of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba260ba1-b843-4c7f-aa66-db4d9f6dafc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as an externalist account of knowledge](reliabilism-as-an-externalist-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-f53d2eeb-6036-46fc-bee7-531fa51577e9.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief is formed through a reliable process but lacks any accessible justification for the individual; intuitively, this appears to undermine the concept of genuine knowledge as it is traditionally understood.**, **It is implausible that knowledge could be present when individuals have no access to the justification or reliability required to substantiate their beliefs.**
