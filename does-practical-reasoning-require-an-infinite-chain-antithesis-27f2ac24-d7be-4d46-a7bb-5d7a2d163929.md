# Does practical reasoning require an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27f2ac24-d7be-4d46-a7bb-5d7a2d163929
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Epistemology](infinitist-epistemology-synthesis-6da6e9b6-0ddd-4346-9ef8-d27afad7386e.md)

## Content

**In practical reasoning, people often act on beliefs without needing infinite chains of justification, yet they succeed and are considered knowledgeable.**, **Consider a scenario where someone navigates using a map and compass with limited reasoning and obtains the correct direction. Intuitively, we do not require infinite justification to consider this person knowledgeable about direction.**
