# Can structural realism handle theoretical anomalies effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4231e93-69d5-4e56-b0ce-9fdec3b1c6a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structural Realism](epistemic-structural-realism-synthesis-4cc8fed2-62df-47a3-af1a-0c0e49fc23b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a well-accepted structural theory in physics encounters an anomaly that challenges its assumptions. Intuitively, addressing such anomalies requires revising the structure itself or incorporating new elements rather than relying on structural consistency alone.**, **It is implausible that addressing all theoretical anomalies can be achieved without modifying the perceived structures, suggesting a limitation in the view that knowledge is merely about structural alignment.**
