# Does contextualism risk trivializing the notion of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dabe836e-1c63-49bd-8a07-60517a104acf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism on Epistemic Justification](contextualism-on-epistemic-justification-synthesis-c8f3ec75-75e9-400a-9969-d95699e610cc.md)

## Content

**Consider contexts with minimal standards where almost any belief can be justified; if such beliefs count as knowledge, the concept may become too lenient.**, **In such cases, the risk is that knowledge becomes trivial and loses its significance as a robust epistemic status.**
