# Can differing contextual thresholds of justification destabilize our understanding of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 004d4940-7cfb-484c-93c6-2454cd8ff8bc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism highlighting context-dependent justification](contextualism-highlighting-context-dependent-justification-synthesis-c9f62fb8-5b79-43c8-99f6-6461f5a6c05a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's belief is considered knowledge in a low-stakes context because the justification requirement is minimal. Intuitively, the same belief might not be considered knowledge in a high-stakes context due to increased justification demands.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain consistent across contexts to be meaningful, suggesting that variable contextual thresholds might lead to contradictions or instability in what counts as knowledge.**
