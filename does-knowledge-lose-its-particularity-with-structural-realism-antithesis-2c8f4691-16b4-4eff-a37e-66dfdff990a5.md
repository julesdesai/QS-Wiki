# Does knowledge lose its particularity with structural realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c8f4691-16b4-4eff-a37e-66dfdff990a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structural Realism](epistemic-structural-realism-synthesis-4cc8fed2-62df-47a3-af1a-0c0e49fc23b5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should provide specific and particular details about the entity or subject matter. This principle implies that if structures only capture general relations, then such generalities might fail to afford substantive knowledge.**, **In theoretical terms, a person might truly know the structure of a social network, understanding its many connections, but miss the key detail that a misinformation campaign is occurring. Intuitively, this lack of detail suggests incomplete knowledge.**
