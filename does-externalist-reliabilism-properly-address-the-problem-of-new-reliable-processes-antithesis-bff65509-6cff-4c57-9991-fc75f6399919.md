# Does externalist reliabilism properly address the problem of new reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bff65509-6cff-4c57-9991-fc75f6399919
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a completely new technology, unknown to others and untested over time, consistently produces true beliefs. Intuitively, we might judge that additional confirmation is required before considering beliefs formed by this new process as justified.**, **There is a plausible principle that a process must not only be statistically reliable but also well-established or understood before it can confer justification.**
