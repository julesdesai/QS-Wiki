# Does testimonial knowledge challenge virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 70e38cca-0c0d-4681-9594-824d13561f69
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person acquires true belief through testimony, without exercising their own intellectual virtue, is not necessarily a case of the person having knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that testimonial knowledge requires accepting testimony to constitute an intellectual virtue. This principle and the challenge of verifying all testimonies independently suggest that knowledge through testimony may not satisfy the requirements of virtue epistemology.**
