# Does intuitive disagreement about near miss cases pose a problem for the safety condition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4cc0184-46ea-4400-ac47-7420a321b5f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's belief is true and safe, but by a narrow margin, as small changes could easily render the belief false. Intuitively, we might disagree on whether the belief counts as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be robust against intuitive disagreement in near miss cases.**
