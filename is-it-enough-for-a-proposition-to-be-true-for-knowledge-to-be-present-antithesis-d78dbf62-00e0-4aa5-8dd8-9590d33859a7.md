# Is it enough for a proposition to be true for knowledge to be present?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d78dbf62-00e0-4aa5-8dd8-9590d33859a7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-4f173a54-3943-4ddb-a2cd-a6bb34479be2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition that is indeed true, but the person’s belief is based on a misunderstanding. Intuitively, we would not consider this as knowledge, because the belief was not derived from understanding or correct inference.**, **Truth alone does not constitute knowledge if it occurs independently of justification or understanding.**
