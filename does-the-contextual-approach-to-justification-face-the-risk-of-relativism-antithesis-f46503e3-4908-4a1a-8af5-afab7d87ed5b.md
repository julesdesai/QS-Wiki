# Does the contextual approach to justification face the risk of relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f46503e3-4908-4a1a-8af5-afab7d87ed5b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that epistemic relativism is a threat because it leads to the conclusion that no belief is more justified than another merely due to differing contexts.**, **Suppose we have a case where different contexts lead to conflicting justifications for the same belief, raising the dilemma of how to resolve such conflicts without slipping into relativism.**, **In such scenarios, intuitively, we would judge that there should be a non-relative way to assess justification.**
