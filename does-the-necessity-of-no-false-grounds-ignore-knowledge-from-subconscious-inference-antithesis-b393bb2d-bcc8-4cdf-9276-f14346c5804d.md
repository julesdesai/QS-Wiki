# Does the necessity of no false grounds ignore knowledge from subconscious inference?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b393bb2d-bcc8-4cdf-9276-f14346c5804d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as No False Grounds Theory](knowledge-as-no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-4c78162e-e116-4027-9605-d681fbf46d3f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a chess grandmaster intuitively plays a brilliant move based on subconscious inferences formed from extensive experience, while consciously holding a false belief about an unrelated chess position. Intuitively, the grandmaster knows the move is correct.**, **Knowledge can arise from subconscious inference without a conscious stream of reasoning, suggesting that conscious false beliefs unrelated to the knowledge claim might not be detrimental.**
