# Can unintentionally true beliefs constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56b933c2-f8b5-4227-9ccf-33e1a559ddfd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person arrives at a true belief through a flawed reasoning process. Intuitively, we would judge that their belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires both true belief and correct reasoning. This principle together with the flawed reasoning in the scenario considered entails that mere accidental truth cannot suffice for knowledge.**
