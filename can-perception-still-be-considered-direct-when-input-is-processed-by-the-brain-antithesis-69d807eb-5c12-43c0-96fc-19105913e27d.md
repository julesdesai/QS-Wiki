# Can perception still be considered direct when input is processed by the brain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69d807eb-5c12-43c0-96fc-19105913e27d
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-thesis-65da2b06-0c60-4e66-b428-771d2431fe55.md)

## Content

**Neurological findings indicate that sensory data is interpreted by the brain before we become conscious of it.**, **If we were perceiving directly, brain processing should not alter the immediate experience.**, **Brain damage or alterations can change perception, indicating indirect involvement of neurological processes.**
