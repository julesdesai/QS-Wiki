# Is coherence a sufficient condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9631ad9f-b1d2-4f5c-8efa-c9c0878a8082
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-762c00d9-ee32-4648-8d8e-67643edf20ba.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person's consistent beliefs about astrology are based on coherent experiential patterns. Intuitively, we either have to say that these beliefs constitute knowledge or that coherence is not a sufficient condition for knowledge. If we say these beliefs constitute knowledge, then such beliefs are wrongly elevated. If we say coherence is not sufficient, then alternative conditions are required for knowledge.**
