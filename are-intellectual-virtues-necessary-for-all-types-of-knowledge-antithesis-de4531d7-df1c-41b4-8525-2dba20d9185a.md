# Are intellectual virtues necessary for all types of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** de4531d7-df1c-41b4-8525-2dba20d9185a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that every instance of knowledge requires the exercise of intellectual virtues, especially in cases of simple perceptual knowledge like recognizing a familiar face.**, **A person who recognizes a face may do so without conscious exercise of any intellectual virtues, suggesting some knowledge does not need intellectual virtue involvement.**
