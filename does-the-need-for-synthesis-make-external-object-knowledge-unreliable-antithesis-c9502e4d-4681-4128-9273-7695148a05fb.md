# Does the need for synthesis make external object knowledge unreliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9502e4d-4681-4128-9273-7695148a05fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose that knowledge of an external object requires a synthesis of perceptual and non-perceptual evidence. Intuitively, this process might lead to gaps or errors in knowledge if the synthesis is flawed.**, **There is a plausible principle that relying on multiple sources to synthesize knowledge can introduce errors as inconsistencies between sources might not be easily resolved. This principle together with the dependence on synthesis implies that knowledge of external objects could be unreliable.**, **It is implausible that such synthesized knowledge could always accurately depict external reality without falling prey to subjective interpretation errors.**
