# Can there be perception without mental principles if perception involves direct interaction with sensory input?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 533d7b95-df46-4aa1-b781-782931fb28e4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism Summary](transcendental-idealism-summary-synthesis-6a7150cf-600b-4ee3-8215-3d235222eec9.md)

## Content

**Suppose perception is solely a process of sensory organs providing data with no mental principles involved.**, **In this case, perception does not require the mind applying its own principles to sensory input for meaningful understanding.**, **The ability to recognize and respond to sensory stimuli without conscious interpretation suggests perception might not need internal structuring by the mind.**
