# Can a belief be considered true because of competence if the competence is not appropriately connected to the truth of the proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f569ab4a-fd86-47a7-b99b-d1d31e5d8595
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Apt Belief](knowledge-as-apt-belief-synthesis-6ac0cb6c-f2b3-4636-9c8a-2a793aab4e4b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where someone possesses expert skill or competence in a particular area, yet their belief happens to be true for reasons unrelated to their competence. Intuitively, the truth of their belief should not be attributed to their competence.**, **A person's belief might be true and related to some general competence, but if the truth doesn't stem from this competence, it challenges the aptness requirement.**, **It seems counterintuitive to claim the aptitude of a belief if the truth is due to factors outside the relevant competence of the believer.**
