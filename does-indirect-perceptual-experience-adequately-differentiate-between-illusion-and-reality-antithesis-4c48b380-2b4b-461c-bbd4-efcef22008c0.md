# Does indirect perceptual experience adequately differentiate between illusion and reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c48b380-2b4b-461c-bbd4-efcef22008c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person observes a bent stick partially submerged in water. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person might misinterpret the stick as being naturally bent, despite it being an illusion caused by water refraction.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires discrimination between illusion and reality, but without direct perception, distinguishing the two becomes problematic.**
