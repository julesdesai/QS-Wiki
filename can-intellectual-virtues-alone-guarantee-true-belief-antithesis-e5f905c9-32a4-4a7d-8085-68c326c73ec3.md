# Can intellectual virtues alone guarantee true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e5f905c9-32a4-4a7d-8085-68c326c73ec3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person is an expert in a field but forms a false belief due to incomplete data challenges the connection between intellectual virtue and true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that even the exercise of intellectual virtues can yield false beliefs if external information is flawed or incomplete.**, **It is implausible that intellectual virtues can ensure true belief without considering the reliability and completeness of the information.**
