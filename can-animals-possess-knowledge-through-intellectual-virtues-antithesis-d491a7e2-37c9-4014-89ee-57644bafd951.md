# Can animals possess knowledge through intellectual virtues?

## Content

**Consider a primate that consistently selects ripe fruit over unripe fruit through refined sensory detection. This case might invite the intuition that the primate's skill fulfills the criteria for intellectual virtue, yet whether this equates to knowledge is controversial.**, **The abilities of non-human entities challenge the assumption that intellectual virtues necessarily imply human-like knowledge.**
