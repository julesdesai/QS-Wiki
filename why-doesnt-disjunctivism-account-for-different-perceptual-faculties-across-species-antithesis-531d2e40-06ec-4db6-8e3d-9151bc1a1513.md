# Why doesn't disjunctivism account for different perceptual faculties across species?

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which humans perceive a flower's color differently than bees, who see ultraviolet patterns. Intuitively, perception varies between species, suggesting that immediate knowledge via perception might not capture all facets of reality without other forms of justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that perception is limited and different across species. This principle suggests that what is directly perceived might not be the complete or same truth for all perceiving beings.**
