# Is the sensitivity requirement satisfied too easily for lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee11b9b4-6d9c-471b-91e2-d5a746b01c96
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa7f9900-da54-4309-8d89-06019053aee3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person guesses a fact correctly, and no nearest world contradicts them due to luck. Despite the guess being true and sensitive by definition, intuitively it does not appear to count as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle indicates that knowledge should not arise from mere luck despite a sensitive belief.**
