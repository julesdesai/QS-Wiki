# Are practical contexts robust in distinguishing between knowledge and belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3f6531a-2e28-4455-a74b-a23c756d687b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two individuals have different practical stakes in the truth of a proposition, affecting their claims of knowledge versus mere belief. Intuitively, this suggests practical contexts might not clearly distinguish between knowing and merely believing.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing should be clearly differentiated from believing, but practical influences appear to muddy this distinction.**
