# Does externalist reliabilism account for the subjective aspect of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b0aee48-4eda-481d-a9eb-dff90fa3c36b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Justification often involves a subjective component where the individual perceives their belief-forming process as reliable based on their evidence. It is implausible to say that external reliability alone suffices if it entirely disregards the believer's perspective.**, **Consider a scenario where two individuals form similar beliefs, one through a process they understand to be reliable and the other through a misunderstood one. Intuitively, we would judge there to be a difference in justification between the two.**
