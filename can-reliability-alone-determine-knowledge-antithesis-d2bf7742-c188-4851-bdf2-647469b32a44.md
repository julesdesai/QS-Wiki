# Can reliability alone determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2bf7742-c188-4851-bdf2-647469b32a44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on a reliable process but in an unusual case, the belief is false. In such a scenario, intuitively we would not say that the person knows the belief.**, **Intuitively, knowing a proposition involves not only the reliability of the process but also the truth of the particular belief in question.**
