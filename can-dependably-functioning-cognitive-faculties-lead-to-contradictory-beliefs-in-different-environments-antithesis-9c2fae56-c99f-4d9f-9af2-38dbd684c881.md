# Can dependably functioning cognitive faculties lead to contradictory beliefs in different environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c2fae56-c99f-4d9f-9af2-38dbd684c881
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Dependability View](epistemic-dependability-view-synthesis-57cd5008-8279-46a6-a04a-50cbd8dfd618.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual with dependably functioning cognitive faculties forms a belief that is true in one environment but false in another due to misleading evidence. Intuitively, the dependability of the cognitive faculties does not consistently lead to truth across environments.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not lead to true beliefs that are dependent on misleading information. This principle, together with a case where cognitive faculties dependably function yet lead to true beliefs in misleading circumstances, suggests a contradiction.**
