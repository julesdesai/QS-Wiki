# Is it possible to have knowledge without access to justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** afb09575-d925-411e-a6b9-c09a0ba4ee59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual has a true belief formed by a reliable process, but they have no idea why the belief is justified or whether it is reliable. Intuitively, we either have to say that this case lacks knowledge or accept unknowable justifications. If we say it lacks knowledge, then truth and reliability alone are insufficient. If we accept unknowable justifications, then our intuition about the need for understandable reasons seems violated.**
