# Does utility always correlate with truth in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6e2e204a-1ef2-41d3-abea-448259f9aab4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Coherentism](pragmatism-with-coherentism-synthesis-7996efa8-d25e-4d54-acc0-6469819e4077.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where believing a false proposition is useful for a person's survival or happiness. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that while the belief is practical, it does not equate to knowledge as the proposition is false.**, **It is implausible that the usefulness of a belief necessarily implies its truthfulness, which is essential for knowledge under the traditional definition.**
