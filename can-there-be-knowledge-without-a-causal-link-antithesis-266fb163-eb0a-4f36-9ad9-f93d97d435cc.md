# Can there be knowledge without a causal link?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 266fb163-eb0a-4f36-9ad9-f93d97d435cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f882c843-046b-4ab6-afcf-19932fafd538.md)

## Content

**A person who forms a belief based on accurate but non-causally linked information, like guessing correctly, intuitively lacks knowledge despite the truth of their belief.**, **Consider a scenario where a detective reaches the true conclusion about a crime based on misleading evidence, without any causal link to the actual events. Intuitively, the detective does not have knowledge even though the conclusion is true.**
