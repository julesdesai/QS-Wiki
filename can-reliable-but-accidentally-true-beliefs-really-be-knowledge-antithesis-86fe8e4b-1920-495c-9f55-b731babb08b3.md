# Can reliable but accidentally true beliefs really be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86fe8e4b-1920-495c-9f55-b731babb08b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ed1f1377-7007-46b3-89c9-5ab38981916f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief through a generally reliable process, but the belief is true only by accident, such as guessing correctly without any evidence.**, **An individual believes a coin is under a cup based on a random guess; if they happen to be right, it challenges whether reliability alone grants knowledge.**
