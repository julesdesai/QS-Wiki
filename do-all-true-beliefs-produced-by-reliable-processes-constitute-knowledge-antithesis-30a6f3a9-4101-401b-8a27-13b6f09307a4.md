# Do all true beliefs produced by reliable processes constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30a6f3a9-4101-401b-8a27-13b6f09307a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person is in a virtual reality scenario and uses all sensory data reliably, forming true beliefs about their surroundings. Intuitively, the fact that this is a virtual world, unknown to the believer, means they do not truly know these beliefs about 'reality'.**, **A belief being true and produced by a reliable process might still not constitute knowledge if the context or environment is misleading.**
