# Is reliabilism challenged by the problem of cognitive malfunction?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7dc0899-9bdc-406d-bafe-27b1934e3ba3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Process-Oriented Justification](reliabilism-with-process-oriented-justification-synthesis-8900612b-46bb-42d6-a4c1-a983dd2da82b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms beliefs based on a process affected temporarily by a malfunction, leading to mostly false beliefs but one true belief. Intuitively, we would judge the true belief is not knowledge.**, **The person believes the proposition, and the proposition is true, but the cognitive process, due to malfunction, is not generally reliable.**
