# How do unpredictable nearby anomalies affect the applicability of the safety condition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b1bd9e0-4750-4d98-8ddf-dd74d25db4ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an astronomer believes a true fact about a star based on consistent observations. However, in a nearby possible world, unforeseen phenomena cause the star to behave erratically, leading to a false belief. Intuitively, we would judge the astronomer knows the fact in the actual world due to reliable observations.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge in the actual world should not be jeopardized by anomalies in nearby worlds if the belief is strongly supported by evidence. This principle, if accepted, challenges the necessity of the safety condition which takes such possibilities into account.**
