# Are intellectual virtues necessary for ordinary knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a0c1487b-dab1-4056-9e9d-a0908f6ee8d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-05879bdc-4686-45ec-8d5f-c387922414d1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that ordinary knowledge claims, such as remembering one's phone number, do not always stem from conscious exercises of intellectual virtues. This principle, together with the lack of necessity to consciously deploy intellectual virtues for all known beliefs, entails that knowledge can exist without active intellectual virtue involvement.**
