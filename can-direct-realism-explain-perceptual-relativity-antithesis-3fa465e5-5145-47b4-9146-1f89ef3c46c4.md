# Can Direct Realism explain perceptual relativity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3fa465e5-5145-47b4-9146-1f89ef3c46c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals perceive a mountain from different distances, with one seeing it clearly and the other seeing it as a faint outline. Intuitively, we would judge that their perceptions differ based on their perspectives, though the mountain itself remains unchanged.**, **A plausible principle is that if perception provides direct access to objects, different perspectives should not yield varying perceptual experiences. This principle together with the relativity of perception challenges the directness of perceptual access.**
