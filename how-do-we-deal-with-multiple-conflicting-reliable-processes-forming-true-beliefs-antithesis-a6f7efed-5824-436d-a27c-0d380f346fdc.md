# How do we deal with multiple conflicting reliable processes forming true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6f7efed-5824-436d-a27c-0d380f346fdc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable True Belief](knowledge-as-reliable-true-belief-synthesis-73884ab1-e6ad-49fe-9997-a4e027a9805c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we encounter two reliable scientific methods, Method A and Method B, each leading to conflicting but true beliefs about different aspects of the same phenomenon. Intuitively, we are challenged to determine which, if either, constitutes genuine knowledge for the knowers involved.**, **When two reliable processes conflict, determining which belief constitutes knowledge becomes problematic, casting doubt on reliability as a sole criterion for knowledge.**
