# Can justification be a reliable process without conscious access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 11eb6bfa-40dc-46ef-853a-c3a3d64458f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Access Internalism](access-internalism-synthesis-1ebb0a7e-8cd6-40ff-bf0f-a16161232f85.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person’s belief is justified by a subliminal cognitive process that is highly reliable, yet they have no conscious access to it. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that reliability alone without conscious access cannot fully justify the belief for knowledge.**, **A subliminal cognitive process justifying a belief, without conscious access, challenges the adequacy of relying solely on reliability for justification.**
