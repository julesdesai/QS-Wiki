# Does perception always yield useful information?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae4cac2c-6388-4cf9-9975-3f28e988deeb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-08f9dcda-197c-4176-877f-438b2d186f9d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person under the influence of a hallucinogen perceives a pink elephant in their living room. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception provides inaccurate information here, or that it is useful. If we say perception is useful about this case, then we must accept erroneous and unreliable data as informative. If we say perception provides inaccurate information about this case, then perception cannot be relied upon for successful interaction with the world.**
