# Can shifting contextual standards lead to contradictory knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c28c98b6-1098-4e30-86bc-af4ea28a18bb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where what is known in one context becomes unknown in another context due to changing standards, yet the facts themselves remain unchanged. Intuitively, we should avoid contradictions in knowledge claims since they imply that a person could simultaneously know and not know the same proposition based solely on context.**, **Contradictory knowledge claims are implausible because they defy the principle of non-contradiction, a basic logical tenet.**
