# Is it possible to have knowledge without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 180f608d-5179-4621-a6c5-465d667ea1f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism: Contexts Influence Justification, Not Knowledge](moderate-contextualism-contexts-influence-justification-not-knowledge-synthesis-be3d790f-5519-485d-8f0d-32864e4a3e9e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person correctly guesses the answer to a question and the answer is true; intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of the answer.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge requires belief that is grounded in more than a mere guess.**, **The idea that a person knows a proposition if they merely believe it is in tension with the principle that belief must be more than a guess.**
