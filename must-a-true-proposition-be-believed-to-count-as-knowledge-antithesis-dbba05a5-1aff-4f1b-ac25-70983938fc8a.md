# Must a true proposition be believed to count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dbba05a5-1aff-4f1b-ac25-70983938fc8a

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-2eef6ba9-ea95-4aea-9a96-5c60688dde0e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a proposition is true but not believed by an individual, it cannot be said that the individual knows it.**, **In cases where a person is unaware of the truth of a proposition, intuitively, we might not ascribe that knowledge to them.**
