# How can perceptual illusions be accounted for if perception is a straightforward apprehension of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c80eb99-f734-488f-bbb9-03e7df35abd8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-2d3f5cf7-4de1-4047-bfb0-105e4b8ca669.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a straight stick appears bent under water due to refraction. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the stick is bent, yet it is not.**, **A plausible principle is that perception directly reveals external objects as they are. This principle, together with the phenomenon of perceptual illusions, entails that in some cases perception is misleading.**
