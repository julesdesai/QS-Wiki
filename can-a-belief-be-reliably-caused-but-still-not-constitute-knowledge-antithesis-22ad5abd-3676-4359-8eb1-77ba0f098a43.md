# Can a belief be reliably caused but still not constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22ad5abd-3676-4359-8eb1-77ba0f098a43
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism With Causal Connection](reliabilism-with-causal-connection-synthesis-6437a935-6c42-4960-8b8c-5c5438cbdf16.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person forms a true belief that it is raining outside because they see someone carrying a wet umbrella, although it is raining, the person carrying the umbrella just came from a fountain, so the belief is not knowledge.**, **A belief that is true and caused by a reliable process can still fail to constitute knowledge if the causal connection does not track the truth of the proposition directly.**
