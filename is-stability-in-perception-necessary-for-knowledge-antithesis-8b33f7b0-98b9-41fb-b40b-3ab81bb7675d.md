# Is stability in perception necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b33f7b0-98b9-41fb-b40b-3ab81bb7675d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone immediately knows they are holding a cup of coffee based on touch alone in a dark room, without needing stable perceptual experiences over time. Intuitively, stable perception is not necessary for certain kinds of immediate knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that immediate and direct perception can result in knowledge without requiring cross-temporal stability. This principle suggests that stability may not be required if immediate perception suffices.**
