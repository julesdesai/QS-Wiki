# Does the variability of context make knowledge unreliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fc07e438-4852-4e3e-b712-4f07ac20bdd7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that reliable knowledge should not fluctuate based on the situation in which the claim is made. This principle together with variable contextual standards entails knowledge is unstable.**, **Suppose we have a case in which through travel, a person's contextual standard shifts and their knowledge claims alter accordingly. Intuitively, knowledge should not oscillate with geographical or temporal context.**
