# Does including luck in the formation of belief negate reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6c50748-6ad5-4889-89e2-2a2ce62c1c06
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliabilism](knowledge-as-reliabilism-thesis-72dce40c-4bce-46d9-be3b-311bf9457188.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person correctly believes a fact due to a lucky guess that just happens to align with a generally reliable process.**, **It is implausible that the result is considered knowledge because the element of luck undermines the reliability aspect necessary for knowing.**
