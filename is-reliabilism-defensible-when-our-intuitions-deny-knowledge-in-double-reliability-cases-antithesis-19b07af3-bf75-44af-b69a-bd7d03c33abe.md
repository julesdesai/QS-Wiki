# Is reliabilism defensible when our intuitions deny knowledge in double-reliability cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 19b07af3-bf75-44af-b69a-bd7d03c33abe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which two equally reliable processes produce opposing beliefs about the same proposition, such as one indicating rain and the other sun. Intuitively, it seems difficult to attribute knowledge to either belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must have a unique and non-opposed belief outcome. This principle suggests that reliabilism's allowance for conflicting truths could challenge its applicability in such instances.**
