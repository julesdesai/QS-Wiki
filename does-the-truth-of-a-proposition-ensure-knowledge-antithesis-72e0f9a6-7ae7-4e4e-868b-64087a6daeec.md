# Does the truth of a proposition ensure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72e0f9a6-7ae7-4e4e-868b-64087a6daeec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**A proposition being true does not automatically ensure knowledge if the truth is accidental or stumbled upon without appropriate justification. Without appropriate intellectual virtues engaging with this truth, the truth alone fails to supply the convertibility required for genuine knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person guesses the winning lottery numbers correctly. Despite the belief and the truth of the win, intuition would suggest that this does not count as genuine knowledge since the truth was achieved through luck, not cognitive virtue.**
