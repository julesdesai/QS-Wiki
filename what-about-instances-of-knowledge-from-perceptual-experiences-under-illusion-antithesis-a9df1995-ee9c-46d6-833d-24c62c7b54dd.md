# What about instances of knowledge from perceptual experiences under illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9df1995-ee9c-46d6-833d-24c62c7b54dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Triple-A Virtue Epistemology](triple-a-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5ec13be3-cfbe-4179-96b3-378e0ebd05b2.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where someone sees a bent stick in water due to refraction, but they know from past knowledge that it is straight. They form the belief that the stick appears bent but is actually straight.**, **Their belief is accurate and based on prior skillful formation, yet intuitively seems more dependent on past knowledge than direct perception, challenging direct adroitness in forming the belief.**
