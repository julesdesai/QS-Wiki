# Can physical objects truly be reduced to sensory experiences alone?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96415ceb-4a04-43be-aaea-3a2a90ca8a86
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual is blindfolded and touches a physical object, a rock, yet has never seen or heard of a rock. The scenario suggests that the concept of the rock cannot be fully reduced to the sensory experience of touch alone**, **There is a plausible principle that concepts of physical objects can’t be fully captured by isolated sensory experiences. This principle challenges the notion of reducing physical objects to sensory experiences alone**
