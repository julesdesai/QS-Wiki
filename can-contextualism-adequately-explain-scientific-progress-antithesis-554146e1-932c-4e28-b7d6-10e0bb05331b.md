# Can Contextualism Adequately Explain Scientific Progress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 554146e1-932c-4e28-b7d6-10e0bb05331b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-6d1ded2e-2e81-4f10-b6c8-db699d1664cc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that scientific progress relies on building upon previously established knowledge that is stable and reliable across different contexts.**, **Contextualism implies that knowledge could shift significantly based on varying contexts, which might undermine the consistency required for cumulative scientific progress.**, **This inconsistency could result in questioning the very foundation of scientific theories if their validity changes with context.**
