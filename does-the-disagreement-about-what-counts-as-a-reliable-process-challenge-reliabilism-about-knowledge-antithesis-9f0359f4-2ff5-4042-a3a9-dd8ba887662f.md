# Does the disagreement about what counts as a reliable process challenge reliabilism about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f0359f4-2ff5-4042-a3a9-dd8ba887662f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-e6324e74-fe2e-4167-be14-2fab090edb3a.md)

## Content

**Consider a process where a psychic claims to obtain true beliefs about future events.**, **There is controversy over whether such a psychic's supposed process is genuinely reliable, reflecting divergent intuitions on reliability.**, **If the reliability of a process is itself disputed or nebulous, it suggests that merely relying on a process's perceived reliability is insufficient to confer knowledge.**
