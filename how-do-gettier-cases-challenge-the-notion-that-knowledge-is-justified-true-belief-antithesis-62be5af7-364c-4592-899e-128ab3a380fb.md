# How do Gettier cases challenge the notion that knowledge is justified true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 62be5af7-364c-4592-899e-128ab3a380fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a clock, which stopped working exactly 24 hours ago, showing the correct time. Intuitively, we would judge that the person’s belief about the time is true but not knowledge.**, **A person can have justified true belief without having knowledge, as shown by cases where their belief is true by luck or coincidence.**
