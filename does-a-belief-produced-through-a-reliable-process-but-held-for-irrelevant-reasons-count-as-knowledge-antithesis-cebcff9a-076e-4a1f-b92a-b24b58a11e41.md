# Does a belief produced through a reliable process but held for irrelevant reasons count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cebcff9a-076e-4a1f-b92a-b24b58a11e41
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Consider someone who believes it will rain because their knee hurts, and it happens to rain. Their belief comes true, but the reason is not causally related to the rain. Intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge.**, **The notion that knowledge entails a causal link between reasoning for belief and the truth of belief appears violated in this case.**
