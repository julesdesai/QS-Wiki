# Are intuitions about epistemic closure problematically ignored by reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a0a1b06-9022-4d01-a3a6-9b9a5ede577d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be closed under known entailment, meaning if a person knows proposition A and also knows A entails B, particularly through reliable means, they should know B.**, **Reliabilism can sometimes ignore the intuition that epistemic closure is necessary, where all entailments of reliably obtained knowledge should themselves be known.**
