# How does pragmatism handle epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cb0e498a-7047-43d8-bb75-08a42e803ed7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person achieves practical success by sheer luck in their belief (e.g., gambling). Intuitively, we have to say that this kind of luck undermines genuine justification.**, **It is implausible that epistemic luck can coexist with true knowledge, as luck undermines the reliability of the belief.**
