# Can past reliability imply future knowledge despite changing conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0c5bdbb-a5bb-420f-a644-4523967cf7d2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e2d53466-acc3-45a1-95cc-71c95728df76.md)

## Content

**A ship captain relies on a compass known to work historically but now affected by interference, leading to incorrect navigation. Past reliability does not guarantee present knowledge.**, **Imagine a technology product whose past successful predictions become flawed due to a software update. Intuition suggests that earlier reliability does not equate to current knowledge.**
