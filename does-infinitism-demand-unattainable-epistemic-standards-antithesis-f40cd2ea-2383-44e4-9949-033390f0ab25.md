# Does Infinitism demand unattainable epistemic standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f40cd2ea-2383-44e4-9949-033390f0ab25
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-db00e7c1-6a13-4ca5-8f09-6fae7cd29e0f.md)

## Content

**A plausible epistemic principle is that standards of belief justification should be attainable by human cognitive agents. The infinite requirement for justification sets a standard beyond basic human capabilities.**, **It is implausible that epistemic principles which set unattainable standards are practically meaningful or useful for human knowledge acquisition.**
