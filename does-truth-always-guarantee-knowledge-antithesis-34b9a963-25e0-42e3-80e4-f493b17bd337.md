# Does truth always guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 34b9a963-25e0-42e3-80e4-f493b17bd337
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism to knowledge with reliable cognitive process](externalism-to-knowledge-with-reliable-cognitive-process-synthesis-e78416a8-ed0b-42d6-b63b-0cc8fec7b97b.md)

## Content

**A person might believe a true proposition through a wildly unreliable method, such as guessing.**, **Intuitively, if the belief method is unreliable, we would not consider it knowledge despite the truth of the proposition.**, **This indicates that truth alone does not suffice for knowledge when reliability is absent.**
