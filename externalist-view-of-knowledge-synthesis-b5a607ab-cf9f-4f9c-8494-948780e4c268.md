# Externalist View of Knowledge

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** b5a607ab-cf9f-4f9c-8494-948780e4c268
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is gathered evidence always necessary for knowledge?](is-gathered-evidence-always-necessary-for-knowledge-antithesis-d9725b61-d35c-41aa-84ca-f4240594e3c1.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief in the proposition is justified**, **Justification for knowledge can be external rather than internally accessible**, **Knowledge does not require that the knower is aware of or has access to the evidence or reasons for their belief**
