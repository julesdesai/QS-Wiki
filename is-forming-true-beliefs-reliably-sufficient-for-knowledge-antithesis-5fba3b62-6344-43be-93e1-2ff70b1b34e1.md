# Is forming true beliefs reliably sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5fba3b62-6344-43be-93e1-2ff70b1b34e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person reliably forms beliefs that are true by coincidence or luck. Intuitively, we would judge that even reliable true belief formation does not constitute knowledge if it is due to luck.**, **It is implausible that mere reliability ensures knowledge when belief accuracy results from fortuitous circumstances rather than dependable processes.**
