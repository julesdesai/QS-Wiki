# How does inferential phenomenalism handle abstract knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2df0ef6a-7a4d-4107-8189-6fc349e2a868
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a mathematician makes inferences about numbers, which are abstract entities not directly perceived. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the mathematician knows mathematical truths.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge of abstract entities can be derived solely from perceptual experiences and inferences from them.**, **Knowledge about abstract concepts requires a framework beyond inferential phenomenalism, which relies on perceptual experiences.**
