# Do reliable cognitive processes guarantee the truth of a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6e9d0c90-f6c8-427a-90cf-cec9848908ff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an agent has reliable cognitive processes but forms a false belief due to misleading evidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the reliability of the process does not guarantee the truth of the belief.**, **It is implausible that reliability alone can account for the truth of every belief, especially when external misleading factors are present.**
