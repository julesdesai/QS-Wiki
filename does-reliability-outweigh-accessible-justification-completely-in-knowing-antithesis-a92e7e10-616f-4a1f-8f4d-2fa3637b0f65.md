# Does reliability outweigh accessible justification completely in knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a92e7e10-616f-4a1f-8f4d-2fa3637b0f65
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Externalism Summary](hybrid-externalism-summary-synthesis-2250092f-2630-4da2-af9c-09ed6c4e9cfe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes something true because of a reliable process but has reasons suggesting it is false. It is plausible we would say they do not know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that to know, one must have internally accessible justification that aligns with truth. This principle entails doubts about claims known purely via external reliability.**
