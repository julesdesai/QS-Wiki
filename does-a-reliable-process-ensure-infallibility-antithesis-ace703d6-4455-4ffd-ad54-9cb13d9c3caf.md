# Does a reliable process ensure infallibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ace703d6-4455-4ffd-ad54-9cb13d9c3caf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief via a reliable process, such as scientific reasoning, but the belief is subsequently shown to be false due to newer evidence. Intuitively, the initial belief was not knowledge.**, **If reliable processes always produced true beliefs, false beliefs from these processes would never be unjustified, which seems counterintuitive.**
