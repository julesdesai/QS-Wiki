# Does context-dependence create a problem for universal truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8cd23121-2325-44d5-aea8-47080beff223
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-4f1ad7a2-76eb-4161-89f7-f4b83097a73b.md)

## Content

**Consider the case of mathematical truths, such as 2+2=4, which are considered universally true irrespective of context. In such cases, justification should not depend on context but rather on logical principles.**, **There is a plausible principle that mathematical and logical truths do not depend on contextual factors for their justification. This suggests that the justification for believing in such truths is acontextual.**, **It is implausible that the justification for belief in universal mathematical truths should vary with changing contexts, challenging the notion that all knowledge depends on context.**
