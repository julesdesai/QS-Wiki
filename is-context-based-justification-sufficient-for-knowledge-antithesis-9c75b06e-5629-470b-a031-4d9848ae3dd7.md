# Is context-based justification sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c75b06e-5629-470b-a031-4d9848ae3dd7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-b757e054-5c50-4073-a4ee-c1ee757cbdbb.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where an employee believes they are performing well based on feedback from a meeting. Intuitively, if the feedback was meant for a different employee, their belief about performing well lacks proper justification.**, **A belief justified in an incorrect context could lead to false claims of knowledge.**, **Contextual appropriateness may not adequately secure the truth necessary for knowledge, leading to errors of judgment.**
