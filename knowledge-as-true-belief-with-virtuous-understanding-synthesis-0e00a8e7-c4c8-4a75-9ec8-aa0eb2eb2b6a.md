# Knowledge as True Belief with Virtuous Understanding

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [If a belief is true and virtuous but lacks understanding, is it knowledge?](if-a-belief-is-true-and-virtuous-but-lacks-understanding-is-it-knowledge-antithesis-d6890df7-018b-4b5c-9e5e-a641f75ec8c8.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is true belief that arises from intellectual virtue which reliably avoids error**, **Knowledge requires understanding of why a proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief arises from intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their intellectual virtue involves understanding why the belief is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe it, the proposition is true, and their intellectual virtue involves understanding why this belief is true**
