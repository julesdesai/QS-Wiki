**Parent:** [Is a reliably formed belief always sufficient for knowledge?](is-a-reliably-formed-belief-always-sufficient-for-knowledge-antithesis-35f6896c-16a1-45f3-b560-2818eddca494.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is formed by a cognitive process that remains reliable across a wide range of circumstances**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief is formed by a cognitive process that remains reliable in a wide range of circumstances**
