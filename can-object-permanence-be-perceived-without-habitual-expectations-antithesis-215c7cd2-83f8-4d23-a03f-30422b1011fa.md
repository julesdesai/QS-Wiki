# Can object permanence be perceived without habitual expectations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 215c7cd2-83f8-4d23-a03f-30422b1011fa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Visualize a situation where an infant sees an object and it suddenly disappears from view. Intuitively, the child might lack the expectation that the object continues to exist out of sight due to insufficient experience.**, **A plausible principle is that object permanence requires the cognitive development of expectations based on repeated exposure. This principle implies that without habitual expectation, object permanence may not be perceived.**
