# Does coherence guarantee reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3ab98ee-2782-45bf-b457-4a3cc960bb7d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Knowledge Summary](coherentist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-1fa3c944-9677-491a-8edd-ea154ce998c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a coherent system of beliefs arises purely from unreliable testimony. Intuitively, the system appears coherent but unreliable.**, **A plausible principle is that reliability is crucial for justification to lead to knowledge. Coherence alone does not guarantee reliability, thus questioning its sufficiency for grounding beliefs.**
