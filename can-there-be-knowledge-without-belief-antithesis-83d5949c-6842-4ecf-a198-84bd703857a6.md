# Can there be knowledge without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83d5949c-6842-4ecf-a198-84bd703857a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person performs a complex calculation correctly and gives the right answer without actually believing it, perhaps due to lack of confidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person knows the correct answer despite not believing it.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a person can provide consistent and accurate answers over repeated trials, then they possess knowledge of the subject matter. This principle together with the lack of belief in individual trials suggests that belief is not necessary for knowledge.**
