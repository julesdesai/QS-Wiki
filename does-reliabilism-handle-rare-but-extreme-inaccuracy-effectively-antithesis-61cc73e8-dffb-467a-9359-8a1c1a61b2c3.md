# Does reliabilism handle rare but extreme inaccuracy effectively?

## Content

**Picture a situation where a person uses a highly reliable GPS that, once in a hundred uses, leads one far astray. Getting lost on the day you rely on it initially suggests a breakdown in knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle advocating against knowledge stemming from systems susceptible to disastrous inaccuracies. This principle indicates that knowledge's connection to reliability needs further security against severe unexpected failures.**
