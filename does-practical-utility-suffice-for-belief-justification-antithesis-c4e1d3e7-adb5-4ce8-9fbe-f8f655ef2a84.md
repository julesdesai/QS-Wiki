# Does practical utility suffice for belief justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c4e1d3e7-adb5-4ce8-9fbe-f8f655ef2a84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Consequences](pragmatism-with-practical-consequences-synthesis-9fee2762-7d04-464a-9533-5a40ce1aa186.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a flawed mathematical theorem because it led to a successful engineering outcome by coincidence. Intuitively, their belief is not justified purely due to the coincidental success.**, **Justification in traditional epistemology involves evidence or reasons supporting the truth of a belief, not merely its practical success.**
