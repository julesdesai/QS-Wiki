# Does merely conditional belief meet the criteria for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fea22497-e320-4544-98d4-582f42165cb7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds the belief that their flight will be on time, but qualifies their belief with "unless the airline sends a delay notice." Intuitively, we either have to say that their belief as conditional can count as knowledge, or it cannot. If we say it can, then knowledge can rest on conditional beliefs, which seems insecure. If we say it cannot, then conditionality undermines the importance of unstable beliefs in the understanding of knowledge.**, **There is a principle that knowledge does not rest on conditions that destabilize belief security because security requires belief robustness in all relevant regards.**
