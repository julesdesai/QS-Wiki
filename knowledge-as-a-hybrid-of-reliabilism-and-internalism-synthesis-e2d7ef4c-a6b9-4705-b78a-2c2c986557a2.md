# Knowledge as a Hybrid of Reliabilism and Internalism

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they are aware of no strong internal evidence against the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is produced by a reliable process, and they are aware of no strong internal evidence against it**
