# Is true belief necessary if someone has intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7c9edb96-5da9-4b16-8f3e-a5986f6e6839
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Expanded Virtue Reliabilism](expanded-virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-be1f6636-aeca-4898-8b76-a37fb9c31f3b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an intelligent scientist forms a belief that is false based on their reasoning in a field with little known guidance, using their intellectual virtues diligently. Intuitively, we would have to acknowledge they possess intellectual virtues despite no true belief resulting.**, **It is implausible that forming a false belief in a genuine pursuit of truth indicates a lack of intellectual virtues.**
