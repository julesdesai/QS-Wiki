# Are cognitive faculties in and of themselves sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3d4ab58-1143-49a2-8c15-8f182e5cf370
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-c68e0504-3343-450c-a9ae-cebbb34ec2ad.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's cognitive faculties function perfectly and appropriately, and they form a belief based on strong, coherent evidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that despite all efforts, they might not have knowledge if their belief was manipulated covertly by misinformation. This suggests that beyond intellectual virtue and properly functioning faculties, additional criteria are necessary to ensure knowledge is truly attained**, **There is a plausible principle that functioning cognitive faculties should lead to true beliefs, but this principle does not guarantee that properly working faculties always yield knowledge if the environment is misleading**
