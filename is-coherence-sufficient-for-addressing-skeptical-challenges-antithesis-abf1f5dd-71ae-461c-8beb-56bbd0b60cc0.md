# Is coherence sufficient for addressing skeptical challenges?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abf1f5dd-71ae-461c-8beb-56bbd0b60cc0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Knowledge Summary](coherentist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-1fa3c944-9677-491a-8edd-ea154ce998c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario in which skeptical hypotheses are consistent with all a person’s beliefs. Intuitively, coherence may fail to refute such skeptical challenges decisively.**, **A plausible principle is that coherent systems must directly address skepticism to provide robust grounds. Without overcoming skepticism, coherence might not suffice for knowledge.**
