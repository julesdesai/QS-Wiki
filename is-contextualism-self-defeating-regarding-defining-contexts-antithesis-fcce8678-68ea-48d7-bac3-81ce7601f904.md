# Is Contextualism self-defeating regarding defining contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fcce8678-68ea-48d7-bac3-81ce7601f904
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Suppose each context must itself be justified for setting its standards for knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that the justification for setting such standards requires further justification, creating a higher meta-context.**, **Intuitively, this meta-context requirement results in never-ending layers of contexts, making it difficult to define any one context definitively.**
