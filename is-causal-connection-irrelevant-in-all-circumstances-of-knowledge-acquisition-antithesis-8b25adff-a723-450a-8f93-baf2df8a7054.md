# Is causal connection irrelevant in all circumstances of knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b25adff-a723-450a-8f93-baf2df8a7054
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes it will rain because they have arthritis pain, which correlates with humidity changes. Intuitively, the lack of a direct causal connection between pain and rain undermines the knowledge claim despite a belief tracking the truth.**, **While a belief can track truth through correlation, intuitively, knowledge seems to require a causal explanation that links the belief with the state of affairs beyond mere correlation.**
