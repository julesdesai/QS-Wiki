# Is causal theory sufficient for addressing Gettier problems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24e09fc8-48b6-45f6-9af3-53808b901577
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person has a justified true belief that a distant event occurred because of a reliable source, while actually a rare coincidence caused their belief to be true. Intuitively, this should not count as knowledge.**, **A causal connection to a fact making a proposition true can still lead to Gettier-type problems where additional coincidences prevent the belief from being true knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that the causal condition alone cannot resolve all Gettier cases, as it does not account for coincidental truth.**
