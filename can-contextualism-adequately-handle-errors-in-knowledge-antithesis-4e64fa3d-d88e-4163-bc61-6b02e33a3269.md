# Can contextualism adequately handle errors in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e64fa3d-d88e-4163-bc61-6b02e33a3269
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person asserts they know their flight is on time based on an announcement. Later, it is delayed but the context was never altered. Intuitively, this case suggests knowledge was lacking from the outset despite contextual support.**, **If a person’s knowledge claim is subject to only contextual factors, then there is no assurance that these claims genuinely reflect reality, leading to potential errors that contextualism may miss.**
