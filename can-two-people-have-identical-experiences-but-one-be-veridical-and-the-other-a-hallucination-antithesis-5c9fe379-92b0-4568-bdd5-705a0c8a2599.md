# Can two people have identical experiences but one be veridical and the other a hallucination?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5c9fe379-92b0-4568-bdd5-705a0c8a2599
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-e62747dd-0f26-4379-9406-3d7f6fb883b9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people see an apple. One perceives it veridically, another hallucinates the same image. Intuitively, both can have the same experience.**, **It is plausible that two experiences are indistinguishable to the perceiver if one is a veridical perception and the other a hallucination. This challenges the claim of fundamentally different kinds of experiences.**
