# Can naturalized epistemology account for a priori knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0cdf1bc-a524-4694-a310-30efae9f6c93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person knows a mathematical truth without empirical investigation. Intuitively, this is a case of knowledge without empirical justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that some knowledge is obtained independently of sensory experience or empirical methods.**, **If justification consists in processes studied by natural sciences, then it excludes forms of a priori knowledge.**
