# Does context always affect perception of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8d1613d3-0c77-4b77-bf2b-6ee258f2a3dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Perception](contextualism-in-perception-synthesis-8503929a-e1fd-4ac2-b5bb-0ff2ee925a19.md)

## Content

**A person can know a proposition when their perception is not influenced by other contextual factors, as shown when a book perceived on a brightly lit table is correctly identified regardless of surrounding conversations**, **It is possible to judge correctly that a proposition is known without integrating contextual factors when a system, like a scientific measurement, operates independently of context**, **People often recognize propositions correctly even if they perceive reality in static contexts, as when a shared sense of smell identifies fresh bread regardless of being indoors or outdoors**
