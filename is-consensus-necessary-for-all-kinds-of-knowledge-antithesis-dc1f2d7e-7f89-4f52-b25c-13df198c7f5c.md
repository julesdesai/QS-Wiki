# Is consensus necessary for all kinds of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc1f2d7e-7f89-4f52-b25c-13df198c7f5c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-c00af758-5073-4931-9585-2f560dddd897.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that some forms of knowledge are inherently subjective and do not require external validation. This principle challenges the notion that all knowledge must emerge from inter-subjective agreement.**, **Consider a case where a person knows their own emotional state. Intuitively, this knowledge does not require inter-subjective agreement or verification from others.**
