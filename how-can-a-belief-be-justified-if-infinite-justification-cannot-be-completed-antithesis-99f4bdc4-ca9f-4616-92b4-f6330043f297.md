# How can a belief be justified if infinite justification cannot be completed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99f4bdc4-ca9f-4616-92b4-f6330043f297
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Conceptual Infinite Regress](infinitism-with-conceptual-infinite-regress-synthesis-14e25e58-6ba7-452c-ac18-aba278058c50.md)

## Content

**A justified belief is supposed to be supported by reasons which provide epistemic grounding.**, **An infinitely regressive chain of reasons would never truly be completed or reachable.**, **This implies that no belief can ever be fully justified if justification is inherently infinite in nature.**
