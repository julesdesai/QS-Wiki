# Is it possible to gain knowledge with intellectual virtue but without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c843b05a-6d7c-4858-b5f2-88f13d2c656b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person exercises intellectual virtues in investigating a claim but ultimately remains undecided and without belief.**, **Intellectual virtues, by definition, should lead to belief formation or revision, yet belief is absent in this scenario.**, **Knowledge attribution requires belief, even in scenarios demonstrating intellectual virtue.**, **Without belief, no true proposition can be known, undermining the sufficiency of virtue alone for knowledge.**
