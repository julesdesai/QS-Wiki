## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that there is a non-deviant causal connection between the belief and the fact it represents**, **A non-deviant causal connection lacks intervening causes that disrupt the direct linkage between belief and fact**
