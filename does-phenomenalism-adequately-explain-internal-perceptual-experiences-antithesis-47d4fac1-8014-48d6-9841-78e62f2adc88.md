# Does phenomenalism adequately explain internal perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47d4fac1-8014-48d6-9841-78e62f2adc88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that internal perceptual experiences, such as feeling pain or hunger, align consistently with external cross-modal perceptions.**, **A case in which a person feels sharp, localized pain but no corresponding visual or tactile sensation from the external world challenges the idea of coherence across sensory modalities.**
