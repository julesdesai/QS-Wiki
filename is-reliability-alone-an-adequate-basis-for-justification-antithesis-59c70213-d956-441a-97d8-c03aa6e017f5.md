# Is reliability alone an adequate basis for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 59c70213-d956-441a-97d8-c03aa6e017f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses a highly reliable machine that unexpectedly malfunctions in a specific instance, leading the person to form a true belief by sheer coincidence. Intuitively, we would not consider the person justified in holding the belief because the reliability was compromised in this instance, despite the truth of the belief.**, **This indicates that reliability in general is insufficient for justification when specific instances deviate from expected reliability.**
