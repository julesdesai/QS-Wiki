# Can knowledge arise from unreliable cognitive traits?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff9f6fa3-79af-4ede-aa7f-2b3ee647b543
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's belief is true and the result of an unreliable process that occasionally delivers true beliefs.**, **An unreliable cognitive trait cannot be considered an intellectual virtue, as it does not dependably produce true beliefs.**, **In academic culture, a pattern of unreliability generally undermines the credibility of knowledge attribution.**, **A true belief that results from an unreliable process is intuitively considered as not arising from intellectual virtue.**
