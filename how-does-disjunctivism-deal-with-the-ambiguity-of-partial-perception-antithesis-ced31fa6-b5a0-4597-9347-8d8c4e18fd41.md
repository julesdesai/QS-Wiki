# How does disjunctivism deal with the ambiguity of partial perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ced31fa6-b5a0-4597-9347-8d8c4e18fd41
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person reads a sign partially obscured by fog, leading to a misinterpretation of the sign’s message while believing it accurately. Intuitively, without clear perception, trust in the knowledge claim wavers.**, **If knowledge requires direct perception or true belief and perceptual experiences justify beliefs if veridically connected, partial perception shouldn't justify a clear belief.**, **It is implausible for people to rely on incomplete perception without allowing for knowledge clarification.**
