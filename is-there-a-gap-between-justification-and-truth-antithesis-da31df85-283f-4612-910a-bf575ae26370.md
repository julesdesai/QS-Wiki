# Is there a gap between justification and truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da31df85-283f-4612-910a-bf575ae26370
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-foundationalism-synthesis-a32c22e3-fd99-46f1-bd06-3346824cbc70.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues can lead to justified beliefs that are not true. In cases where a belief is justified by intellectual virtues but turns out false, intuitively, there is a gap between justification and truth that must be addressed to fulfill the conditions for knowledge. It is implausible to claim that intellectual virtue alone bridges this gap without ensuring truth, suggesting an insufficiency in the definition of knowledge as simply true belief based on intellectual virtues.**
