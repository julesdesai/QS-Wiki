# Does this view account for the role of luck in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 82d23c0d-c871-4771-a599-27c3aaad0419
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology Summary](naturalized-epistemology-summary-synthesis-ad83a102-e1e6-4596-90b6-8b3b07d31230.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge is compatible with epistemic luck because knowledge implies a level of control over the truth of the proposition**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person guesses the answer to a complex problem and gets it correct by sheer chance. Intuitively, we either have to say that it was lucky or that it was not knowledge. If we say it was lucky, then it was not knowledge**
