# Is belief a necessary component of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b76fadb-bfd1-48ea-bcf6-b9e802b51c85
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology Summary](naturalized-epistemology-summary-synthesis-ad83a102-e1e6-4596-90b6-8b3b07d31230.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge does not require belief because a person can know something without explicitly believing it due to absent-mindedness or distraction**, **Consider a scenario in which someone knows a fact, like "the earth is round," but is momentarily distracted and does not reflectively assent to it. Intuitively, they still know the fact**
