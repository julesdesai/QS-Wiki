# Does Reliabilism Handle Subjective Justification Issues Adequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30d2f522-87f4-418f-8574-e121dc436e91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**Imagine two individuals form the same belief by the same reliable process, but one has misleading evidence questioning the belief's validity. Intuitively, the individual with misleading evidence might be seen as lacking knowledge.**, **Subjective justification might influence whether a reliably formed belief counts as knowledge, which reliabilism might underestimate.**
