# Is belief necessary if action is based on guesswork?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5aa4084a-186a-4a46-8653-6b2ff15c0f35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual acts on a guess that turns out to be true and practically beneficial, but they did not genuinely believe it. Intuitively, we would say that this is not knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that genuine belief is crucial for knowledge, and mere guesswork cannot meet this criterion.**
