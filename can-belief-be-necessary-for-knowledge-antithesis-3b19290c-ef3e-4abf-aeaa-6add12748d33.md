# Can belief be necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3b19290c-ef3e-4abf-aeaa-6add12748d33
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-dea02623-290c-44c3-896b-2f967081eb1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation wherein someone acts rightly out of habit, without consciously holding a belief about their actions, yet their actions align perfectly with known truths. Intuitively, we would judge they possess practical knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle stating that not all forms of knowledge require a conscious belief; for instance, skills or know-how may not involve explicit beliefs.**
