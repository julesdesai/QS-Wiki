# How does a lucky guess align with the evidentialist theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27009391-d039-4707-8a47-dbb1053704cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person makes a lucky guess about a factual proposition that turns out true is not a case in which we typically attribute knowledge.**, **This case challenges the principle that a true belief accompanied by sufficient confidence counts as knowledge if it lacks proper evidential support.**, **Intuitively, we would not say that guessing correctly without evidence constitutes knowledge.**
