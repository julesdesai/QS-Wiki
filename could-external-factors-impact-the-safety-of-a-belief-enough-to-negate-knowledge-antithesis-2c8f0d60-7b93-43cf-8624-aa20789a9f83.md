# Could external factors impact the safety of a belief enough to negate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c8f0d60-7b93-43cf-8624-aa20789a9f83
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person narrowly avoids taking an unreliable news source's false report due to an incidental but true report from a usually reliable source. In this scenario, intuitively, the person's belief seems precariously close to being wrong, suggesting a lack of safety.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be considered safe, it should not depend heavily on such fragile circumstances. This principle indicates that external factors impacting belief safety might render the knowledge claims as insufficiently robust.**
