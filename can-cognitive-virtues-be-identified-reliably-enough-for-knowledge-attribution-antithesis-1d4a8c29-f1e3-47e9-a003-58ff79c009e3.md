# Can cognitive virtues be identified reliably enough for knowledge attribution?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d4a8c29-f1e3-47e9-a003-58ff79c009e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ad239dac-f508-456d-984a-1344bc3c3ad8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where it is unclear which cognitive abilities or virtues are being exercised. Intuitively, this ambiguity makes it difficult to determine if the person knows something based on those virtues.**, **It is implausible that for every instance of knowledge, one can clearly attribute it to specific cognitive virtues. This uncertainty challenges the principle that all knowledge results from cognitive abilities or virtues.**
