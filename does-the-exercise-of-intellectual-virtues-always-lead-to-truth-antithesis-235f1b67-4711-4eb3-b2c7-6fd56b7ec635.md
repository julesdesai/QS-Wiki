# Does the exercise of intellectual virtues always lead to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 235f1b67-4711-4eb3-b2c7-6fd56b7ec635
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology combines knowledge with intellectual virtues](virtue-epistemology-combines-knowledge-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-5b32e3fe-f322-4af8-b790-236786e70562.md)

## Content

**The exercise of intellectual virtues such as open-mindedness and diligence does not guarantee arriving at a true belief, as biases or external constraints sometimes override virtues.**, **Consider an investigative journalist who pursues a story with every known method of verification, yet concludes with an incorrect narrative due to fabricated sources.**, **If justification necessarily involves success in exercising intellectual virtues but does not always lead to truth, then knowledge might exist even where true belief is missing, conflicting with the initial claims of virtue epistemology.**
