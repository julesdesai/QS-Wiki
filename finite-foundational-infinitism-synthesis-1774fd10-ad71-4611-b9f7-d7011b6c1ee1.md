# Finite Foundational Infinitism

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 1774fd10-ad71-4611-b9f7-d7011b6c1ee1
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is belief undermined by infinite regress?](is-belief-undermined-by-infinite-regress-antithesis-205dd28b-a0bc-453b-991d-e2f007020d13.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the justification involves either a finite chain of reasons terminating in a foundational belief or an infinite, non-repeating chain of reasons**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and there exists either a finite chain or an infinite chain of reasons justifying the belief**
