# Why might sensitivity be insufficient for tracking conditional beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a2c87396-5421-4243-b66f-5505cdb630ae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes that if it is snowing, the roads will be icy, and this conditional belief holds. Intuitively, such a person might be said to know about the road conditions given the weather.**, **In a close possible world where it is snowing but the roads are not icy due to salt trucks, the person would still believe the roads are icy, demonstrating that the belief lacks sensitivity because it would not vary with the truth of the condition.**
