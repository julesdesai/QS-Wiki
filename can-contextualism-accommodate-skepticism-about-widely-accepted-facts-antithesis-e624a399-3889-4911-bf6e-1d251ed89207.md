# Can contextualism accommodate skepticism about widely accepted facts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e624a399-3889-4911-bf6e-1d251ed89207
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Knowledge](contextualism-about-knowledge-synthesis-3f771a9e-26d6-43c4-b604-a31f8bd9947c.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a context where reliable sources are generally distrusted despite their accuracy.**, **In such a context, contextualism suggests that the truth of widely accepted facts is questionable.**, **It is counterintuitive to suggest that the truth of widely accepted facts is dependent on the context's trust level.**
