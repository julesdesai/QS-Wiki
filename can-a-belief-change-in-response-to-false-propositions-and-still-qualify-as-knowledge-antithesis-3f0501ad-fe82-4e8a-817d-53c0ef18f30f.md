# Can a belief change in response to false propositions and still qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f0501ad-fe82-4e8a-817d-53c0ef18f30f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1814de97-222c-437f-bd26-a9ce4edff4f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's beliefs respond to changes in the truth of propositions, but the person is also prone to false belief changes when confronted with misleading evidence. Intuitively, we would judge such a person's knowledge status to be questionable.**, **It is plausible to suggest that susceptibility to false belief changes may undermine the reliability required for knowledge.**
