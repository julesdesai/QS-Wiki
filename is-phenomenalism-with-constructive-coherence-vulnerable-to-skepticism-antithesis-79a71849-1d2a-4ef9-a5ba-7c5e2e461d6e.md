# Is phenomenalism with constructive coherence vulnerable to skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 79a71849-1d2a-4ef9-a5ba-7c5e2e461d6e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is manipulated by an evil demon to have perfectly coherent but systematically false perceptions. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of external objects.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be immune to such radical scenarios. This principle together with the reliance on coherent perceptions suggests that phenomenalism might be vulnerable to skeptical challenges.**
