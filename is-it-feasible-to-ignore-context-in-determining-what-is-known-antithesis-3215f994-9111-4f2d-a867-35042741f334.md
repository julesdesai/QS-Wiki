# Is it feasible to ignore context in determining what is known?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3215f994-9111-4f2d-a867-35042741f334
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Interest-Relative Invariantism Summary](interest-relative-invariantism-summary-synthesis-34d6e50b-4fc4-4003-bede-59691f379a3b.md)

## Content

**In cases where practical interests shift the perceived certainty of a proposition, ignoring contextual variations seems counterintuitive**, **Consider a case where low stakes make skepticism about a proposition less pronounced, which changes in higher stakes without altering the truth, challenging the non-contextual claim of knowledge**, **A plausible principle suggests that context, and not just invariant truths, plays a significant role in what agents claim to know**
