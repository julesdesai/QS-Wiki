# Can virtue epistemology account for cases of lucky true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d496260c-b505-42bb-afd8-92cd182e7186
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a truth due to a lucky guess. Intuitively, we do not consider this case as an instance of knowledge, even if the belief is true.**, **A reliable cognitive character trait should consistently lead to true beliefs, but luck does not involve such consistency.**, **Knowledge, typically, requires more than a lucky conjunction of events, even if it results in true beliefs.**, **If a true belief arises from luck rather than a reliable trait, it challenges the requirement for intellectual virtue in obtaining knowledge.**
