# Does context-dependent justification undermine universal knowledge principles?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 397ee1f8-0f1c-4f21-9d5e-6db35c5e0b6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism With Context-Dependent Justification Standards](contextualism-with-context-dependent-justification-standards-synthesis-a8126985-f485-4c51-b545-16d8a583ea1e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that universal principles govern what counts as knowledge. This principle together with the claim that justification standards vary by context raises doubt about the existence of universal knowledge principles.**, **In adopting context-dependent justification, one risks disclaiming the possibility of formulating general epistemic rules or principles that apply over all contexts.**
