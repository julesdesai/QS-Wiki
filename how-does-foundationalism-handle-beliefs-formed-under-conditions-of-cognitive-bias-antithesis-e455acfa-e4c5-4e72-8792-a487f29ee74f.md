# How does foundationalism handle beliefs formed under conditions of cognitive bias?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e455acfa-e4c5-4e72-8792-a487f29ee74f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**Cognitive bias can influence belief formation, leading individuals to unjustified or incorrect beliefs.**, **For instance, confirmation bias might cause a person to believe information that supports their existing beliefs without sufficient evidence.**, **Foundationalism suggests that basic beliefs are justified independently of such biases.**, **If foundationalism allows biased beliefs to appear self-evident, it might misidentify truly justified beliefs.**
