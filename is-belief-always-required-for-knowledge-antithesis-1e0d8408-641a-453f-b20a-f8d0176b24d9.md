# Is belief always required for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e0d8408-641a-453f-b20a-f8d0176b24d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person acts on a true proposition without consciously believing it, such as reacting implicitly based on subconscious reasoning. Intuitively, this may sometimes be seen as having knowledge without explicit belief**, **If explicit belief is necessary for knowledge, subconscious insights that lead to correct outcomes without active belief may be unfairly excluded from being knowledge**
