# Can accidental truths be considered knowledge through virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c21f9e61-1f03-4f16-9405-fae3f8787782
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue epistemology view on knowledge](virtue-epistemology-view-on-knowledge-synthesis-e49b38ea-0e37-4416-ac67-ff35516a157e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where an accidental truth is believed as a result of exercising intellectual virtues. Intuitively, it seems wrong to call such beliefs knowledge merely due to chance.**, **There is a plausible notion that genuine knowledge cannot result from accidental truths, even if virtues are exercised.**
