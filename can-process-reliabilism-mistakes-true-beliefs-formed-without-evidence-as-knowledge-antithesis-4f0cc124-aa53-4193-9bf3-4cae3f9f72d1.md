# Can Process Reliabilism mistakes true beliefs formed without evidence as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f0cc124-aa53-4193-9bf3-4cae3f9f72d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Evidence Sensitivity](process-reliabilism-with-evidence-sensitivity-synthesis-e3e15d29-f0bc-4d87-b0aa-fbeab510c604.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a true belief is formed by chance without any evidence present, but the process is generally reliable. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief is not knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires evidence sensitivity. This principle, together with lack of evidence in belief formation, entails the belief is not knowledge.**
