# Can knowledge be based purely on perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f7341af-398d-4144-8220-75aaaf5573e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Lemmas View](no-false-lemmas-view-synthesis-031e6f6f-2b49-47e4-821c-d2421562acef.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees someone who looks exactly like their friend in a crowd, but it is actually a stranger. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their belief that their friend is in the crowd is not knowledge even if it turns out to be true.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceiving something accurately does not always guarantee knowledge if the perception is mistaken about an essential feature of the situation. This principle together with the notion that knowledge requires no false lemmas entails that having accurate perception is insufficient if a crucial detail is mistaken.**
