# Can someone believe a proposition without knowing it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f7c566a-03f0-48fb-b161-eb81ed3b548b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-54895291-30c8-42cf-8322-baea34575bd1.md)

## Content

**A person can strongly believe a proposition based on misleading evidence, but if the proposition turns out to be false, intuitively, we would judge that they do not know it.**, **Suppose someone reads inaccurate information in a reputable source and believes it as true. Intuitively, this seems to be a case of belief without knowledge.**
