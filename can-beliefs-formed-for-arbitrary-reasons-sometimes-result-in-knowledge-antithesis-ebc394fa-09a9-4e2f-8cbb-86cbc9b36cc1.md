# Can beliefs formed for arbitrary reasons sometimes result in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebc394fa-09a9-4e2f-8cbb-86cbc9b36cc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Justification Theory](internalist-justification-theory-synthesis-60885d02-ba8c-49f7-a8ac-6cdf9db1e5a8.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone believes it will rain because they randomly selected to do so using a coin toss.**, **It coincidentally turns out to rain, yet this belief lacks solid internal justification, as the reason was arbitrary.**, **Despite lacking internal access to reasons, the belief was true and corresponded with reality.**
