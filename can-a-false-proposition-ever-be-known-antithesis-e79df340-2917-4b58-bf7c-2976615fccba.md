# Can a false proposition ever be known?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e79df340-2917-4b58-bf7c-2976615fccba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Take a case in which a person believes a proposition that fits all the criteria of evidence and context but later turns out to be false due to conditions unknowable to the person. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to consider them as having known the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot encompass propositions that are false, despite contextual and evidential appearances.**
