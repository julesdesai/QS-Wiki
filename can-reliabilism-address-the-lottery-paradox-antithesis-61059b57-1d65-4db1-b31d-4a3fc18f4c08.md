# Can reliabilism address the lottery paradox?

## Content

**Suppose we have a lottery with a vast number of tickets, and a person forms a belief about each ticket that it is a loser, supported by the high probability given the process used.**, **Intuitively, despite the high probability, our judgment is to withhold certainty and thus question whether probability-based reliability provides sufficient justification for knowledge.**
