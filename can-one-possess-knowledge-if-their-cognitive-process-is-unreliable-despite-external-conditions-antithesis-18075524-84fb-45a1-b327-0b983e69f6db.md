# Can one possess knowledge if their cognitive process is unreliable despite external conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18075524-84fb-45a1-b327-0b983e69f6db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has an unreliable cognitive process but arrives at a true belief due to external aids or cues. Intuitively, we would not consider this as knowledge.**, **It is implausible that truly knowing something could stem from an unreliable cognitive process, even if environmental factors help.**
