# How do we handle misleading reliable belief-forming processes?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a belief-forming process is usually reliable, but in a specific situation, due to unusual interference, it leads to a false belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the proposition despite the usual reliability of the process.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a belief to be true; if a reliable process produces a false belief, the requirement for knowledge is not met.**
