# Are there boundary cases where reliability is not clear-cut?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7fc16ff6-c791-4f61-b43f-e648c7c96662
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-383ba069-e0f1-4291-bdc7-eacefc701040.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person deduces a fact from mixed accurate and inaccurate data, leading them to a true belief. Intuitively, we would feel ambiguity in whether the process was uniformly reliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a process's reliability is not uniformly clear, its outcome's knowledge status is contestable. This principle casts doubt on whether reliability uniformly applies across all contexts.**
