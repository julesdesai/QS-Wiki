# Interest-relative invariantism accommodates shifting standards by interest

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 61712d5d-7be8-4453-930e-d7aa6ce3ca01
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Do different domains require differing standards of justification?](do-different-domains-require-differing-standards-of-justification-antithesis-02bf57ab-a9a3-438b-97ec-0058dbd31ca4.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the standard of justification is dependent on the subject's interests**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and meet the justification standard determined by their interests**
