# How does virtue epistemology handle Gettier cases?

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9deda8b7-9bee-4f9f-90d0-2d2b46259731.md)

## Content

**A Gettier case involves a scenario where someone has a justified true belief but still fails to have knowledge. For instance, imagine a situation where a person believes a colleague owns a specific make of car because they saw them driving it, but it turns out the colleague borrowed the car from someone else. Unbeknownst to the first person, the colleague actually owns an identical car. Intuitively, we judge that despite having a true and justified belief, the person does not have knowledge in this case.**, **It is implausible that simply possessing a true belief through any reliable cognitive ability guarantees knowledge, as demonstrated by Gettier cases where reliable faculties still lead to apparent knowledge failure. This suggests the need for a condition beyond true belief and reliability for knowledge.**
