# Do omniscient beings undermine the need for belief in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca40cd73-9590-454a-8201-8b4c290d524c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider the hypothetical scenario of an omniscient being who has all true propositions at their disposal without the need for belief because they do not require cognitive processes as humans do. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might question whether belief is necessary for knowledge as this being possesses truth without belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge in its highest form does not require belief but direct access to truth, challenging the requirement that knowledge necessitates belief in the proposition.**
