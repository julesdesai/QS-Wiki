# Can a belief formed by an unreliable process still be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 661816b5-ee08-417f-b037-f0506dee4382
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology With Virtuous Belief Formation](virtue-epistemology-with-virtuous-belief-formation-synthesis-53b228ae-7464-400d-be01-037da588b5a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person uses an unreliable method to form a belief that happens to be true on one occasion.**, **The method does not generally lead to truth and fails most of the time.**, **A belief formed in this way appears to lack the reliability associated with intellectual virtues.**, **Intuitively, such beliefs do not appear to constitute knowledge, even if they happen to be true.**
