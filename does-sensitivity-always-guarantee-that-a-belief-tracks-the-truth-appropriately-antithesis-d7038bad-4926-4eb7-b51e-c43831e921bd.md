# Does sensitivity always guarantee that a belief tracks the truth appropriately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d7038bad-4926-4eb7-b51e-c43831e921bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes that a bridge is safe to cross based on its appearance. Intuitively, if the bridge's appearance remains unchanged despite unsafe modifications, the belief is not sensitive to the actual condition of the bridge.**, **A belief that seems to track the truth may not always be sensitive to changes if reliability hinges on superficial or misleading indicators rather than substantial changes in the truth.**
