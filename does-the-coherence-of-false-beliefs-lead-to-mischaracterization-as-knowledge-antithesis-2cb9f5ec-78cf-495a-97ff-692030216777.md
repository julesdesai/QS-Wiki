# Does the coherence of false beliefs lead to mischaracterization as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2cb9f5ec-78cf-495a-97ff-692030216777
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Coherentism](fallibilist-coherentism-synthesis-f085a715-bc4b-46dd-bc93-6874997728cd.md)

## Content

**A belief system containing coherent but false beliefs about a fictitious entity might be considered knowledge under coherentism.**, **The counterintuitive possibility that a well-organized set of false beliefs could be considered knowledge suggests that coherence alone may mischaracterize what constitutes genuine knowledge.**
