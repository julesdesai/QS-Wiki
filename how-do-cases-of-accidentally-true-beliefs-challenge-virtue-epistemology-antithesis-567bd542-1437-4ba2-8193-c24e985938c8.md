# How do cases of accidentally true beliefs challenge virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 567bd542-1437-4ba2-8193-c24e985938c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-72984f03-1ce3-46fd-9fd6-13203e340ace.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief by accident rather than through the exercise of intellectual virtue. Intuitively, we would not consider this true belief as knowledge.**, **A person can be said to have knowledge according to virtue epistemology only if their true belief arises from an intellectual virtue. A true belief formed accidentally does not arise from an intellectual virtue.**
