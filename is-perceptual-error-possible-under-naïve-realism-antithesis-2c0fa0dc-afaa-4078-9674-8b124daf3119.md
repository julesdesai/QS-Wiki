# Is perceptual error possible under Naïve Realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c0fa0dc-afaa-4078-9674-8b124daf3119
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-960c5650-a545-46a7-9f9f-61ad44274751.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a stick appears bent when partially submerged in water. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that our perception is not directly presenting the stick as it truly is.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception operates as a straightforward presentation of external objects, then errors in perception should not occur. This principle together with frequent perceptual errors entails that perception does not always straightforwardly present the external world.**
