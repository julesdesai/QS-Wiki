# Is intellectual virtue necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d04953f5-faf3-49ca-8863-835de21b53ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**A person may achieve true belief through luck rather than due to their intellectual virtues. Intuitively, we would suggest this is not a case of real knowledge**, **It is plausible that knowledge should require more than mere cognitive success, such as a relation to the individual's intellectual character**, **Cognitive success due to luck or external factors diminishes the role of intellectual virtue, challenging its necessity for knowledge**
