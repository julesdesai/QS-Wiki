# Can virtue epistemology adequately address disagreements among epistemic peers?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd90a344-55a0-4d2e-9659-156aab7855dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-db8e4632-83fc-4e14-a2b9-f64cf1d5bb50.md)

## Content

**In cases where two equally virtuous individuals with identical evidence still disagree on a proposition, the reliable path to truth through virtues is questioned.**, **This challenges the idea that cognitive virtues alone always lead to truth, as they cannot always resolve epistemic disputes.**
