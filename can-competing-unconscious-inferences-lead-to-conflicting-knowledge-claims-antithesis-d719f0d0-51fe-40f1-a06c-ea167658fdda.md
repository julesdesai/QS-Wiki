# Can competing unconscious inferences lead to conflicting knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d719f0d0-51fe-40f1-a06c-ea167658fdda
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals unconsciously infer different explanations for the same observed phenomenon based on their unique past experiences. Intuitively, we either have to say that both have knowledge or that neither does. If we say both have knowledge, then knowledge becomes contradictory. If we say neither has knowledge, then unconscious inferences undermine the very concept of knowledge.**
