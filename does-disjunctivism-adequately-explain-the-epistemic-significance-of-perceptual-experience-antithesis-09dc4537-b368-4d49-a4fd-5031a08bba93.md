# Does disjunctivism adequately explain the epistemic significance of perceptual experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 09dc4537-b368-4d49-a4fd-5031a08bba93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-6c9ba281-6911-4c85-9826-b83f47b211cf.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person perceives a tree and gains knowledge of the tree's existence through this perception.**, **If a similar perceptual experience could occur without the tree's existence, it raises questions about how knowledge is directly gained through perception alone.**, **Perceptual experiences that could occur in illusion or hallucination might undermine their role as direct knowledge sources.**
