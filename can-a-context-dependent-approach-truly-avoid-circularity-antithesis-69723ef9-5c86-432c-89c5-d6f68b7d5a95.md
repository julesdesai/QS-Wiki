# Can a context-dependent approach truly avoid circularity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69723ef9-5c86-432c-89c5-d6f68b7d5a95
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Coherence](contextualist-approach-to-coherence-synthesis-974832c4-5139-4ec9-9891-da5bb6a159f3.md)

## Content

**Circularity is often criticized in epistemology because it fails to adequately justify beliefs without presupposing what it aims to establish.**, **Suppose we have a case where context provides external factors to avoid circularity, yet those external factors are themselves justified by the same context.**, **In such a case, intuitively, we would judge that circularity has not been effectively avoided.**
