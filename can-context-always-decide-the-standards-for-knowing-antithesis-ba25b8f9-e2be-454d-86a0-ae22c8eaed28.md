# Can context always decide the standards for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba25b8f9-e2be-454d-86a0-ae22c8eaed28
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**The variability of standards for knowledge can lead to contradictions across contexts, questioning whether context is an appropriate determinant of knowledge standards.**, **Suppose there are two equally reliable but different contexts (e.g., scientific versus layman) that result in different knowledge attributions for the same proposition. Intuitively, this raises doubts about the robustness of context as a determinant of standards.**
