# Is undefeated justification necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52adceda-fc99-4850-a6b2-938adf62d2e5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a true proposition based on strong evidence without knowing of contrary evidence. Intuitively, this person seems to have knowledge even without addressing the unknown contrary evidence as a defeater.**, **A belief that is based on strong evidence and is true should count as knowledge even if there are unknown contrary facts that the believer is not aware of.**, **If we require knowledge to be free of all possible unknown defeaters, it may become practically impossible to achieve knowledge.**
