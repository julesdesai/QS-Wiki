# Is knowledge truly a direct acquaintance with reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8d6a386d-a326-40c5-a347-8090c4dafcef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where atmospheric conditions cause a mountain to appear closer than it is. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that a person viewing the mountain does not have direct acquaintance with its true distance.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires accurate understanding of all relevant aspects of an object. This principle together with observations about perceptual errors entails that knowing something cannot rely only on direct perception.**
