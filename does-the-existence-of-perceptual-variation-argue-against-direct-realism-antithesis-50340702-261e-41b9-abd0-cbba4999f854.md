# Does the existence of perceptual variation argue against direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50340702-261e-41b9-abd0-cbba4999f854
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two observers view the same object under different lighting, resulting in different color perceptions. Intuitively, we would judge that their perceptions cannot both be direct access to knowledge of the object's color.**, **There is a plausible principle that direct access should yield consistent and invariant knowledge about external objects. Perceptual variation challenges this by showing inconsistent access.**
