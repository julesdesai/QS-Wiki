# Can coherence alone account for new or novel perceptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2021371-bb54-490d-bff0-b678d12f8c86
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person encounters a completely novel object, resulting in a sudden coherence of perceptions. Intuitively, we would judge that previous perceptions did not imply nor explain the novelty.**, **It is implausible that mere coherence of past perceptions can account for the introduction of genuinely novel entities into the experiential network.**
