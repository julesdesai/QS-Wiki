# Can a reliable process still produce a false belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89d3afa3-1532-48e9-9e95-a9c425af077a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as a Knowledge Theory](reliabilism-as-a-knowledge-theory-synthesis-b5bdc6be-6410-4aae-a2f1-e20391888e99.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a generally reliable cognitive process produces a single false belief due to deception in that instance. Intuitively, we would judge that the resultant belief is not knowledge.**, **It is plausible that reliability must entail correct belief formation in every instance for it to suffice for knowledge.**
