# Can you justify the reliability of a rare but true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b165a8da-5f6e-47d6-a1ad-f916c9a5056c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person predicts an earthquake with accuracy due to an unusual but unrecognized signal. Intuitively, while the prediction is true, knowing its reliability remains insufficient due to the rarity and obscurity of evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliability as a standard for knowledge struggles with rare, true beliefs that resist clear justification.**
