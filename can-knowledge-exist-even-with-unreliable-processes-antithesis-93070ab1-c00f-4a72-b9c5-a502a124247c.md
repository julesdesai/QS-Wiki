# Can knowledge exist even with unreliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93070ab1-c00f-4a72-b9c5-a502a124247c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief about the time by glancing at a stopped clock and happens to see the correct time. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if someone does not have knowledge, then the process by which they formed their belief cannot be considered genuinely reliable. This principle together with the fact that the person looked at a stopped clock, which is not reliable, challenges the notion that reliability is necessary for knowledge.**
