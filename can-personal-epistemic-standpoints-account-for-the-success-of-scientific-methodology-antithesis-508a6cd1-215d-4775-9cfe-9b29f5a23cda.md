# Can personal epistemic standpoints account for the success of scientific methodology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 508a6cd1-215d-4775-9cfe-9b29f5a23cda
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Consider the scientific method, which relies on repeatable and verifiable experiments to establish knowledge. Intuitively, this method depends on objective standards rather than individual epistemic standpoints.**, **If epistemic standards are not universal, it becomes difficult to account for the success and reliability of scientific knowledge that transcends individual perspectives.**, **It is implausible to claim that the success of scientific endeavors, which require consistency and universality, can be explained by standards that are inherently personal and variable.**
