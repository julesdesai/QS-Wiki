# Is disjunctivism too restrictive about what constitutes knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 865332d1-55d9-46a5-8ecf-2d18e6b0b4f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-19882849-157e-4dc6-b31d-66173a7c79c0.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a scientist makes a well-supported inference from indirect observational data without direct perceptual contact with the subject. Intuitively, this still seems to constitute knowledge despite lacking direct perceptual contact.**, **It is plausible that knowledge can sometimes involve indirect awareness, questioning if direct perception is necessary for all knowledge.**
