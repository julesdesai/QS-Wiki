# Is infinite justification capable of providing certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0301dfcf-c2e4-4788-b911-8de4d98cb512
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**Infinite justification by its nature may never reach certainty, as new reasons can always modify or contradict previous ones, preventing final verification.**, **Suppose a person attempts to provide endless reasons for their belief; the lack of a final reason leads to uncertainty as any reason could, in principle, be challenged by a new reason.**, **If ultimate certainty requires finality in justification, then infinite justification, lacking this endpoint, cannot be entirely certain.**
