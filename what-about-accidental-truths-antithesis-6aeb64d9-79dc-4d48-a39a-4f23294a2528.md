# What about accidental truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6aeb64d9-79dc-4d48-a39a-4f23294a2528
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f882c843-046b-4ab6-afcf-19932fafd538.md)

## Content

**Envision a scenario where someone believes it is raining due to a leaky roof's random dripping sound, and it coincidentally happens to be raining—a correct belief without a causal link or perception. Intuitively, this should not be considered knowledge.**, **The intuitive judgment in this case is that despite the true belief, the absence of causation or perception diminishes the claim to knowledge.**
