# Can one know false beliefs according to Nozick's theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c40039eb-21be-4220-b056-eea155dfef08
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Reliabilism](nozicks-conditional-reliabilism-synthesis-29b3c3e0-b3ff-4b45-a994-893f15be46b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a false belief that they are drinking water when it is, in fact, clear poison. Intuitively, they do not know they are drinking water.**, **On Nozick's view, if their belief were true (i.e., if it were water), then they would know it.**, **The principle implies that one could have knowledge of false beliefs if counterfactual circumstances change it to a true belief, which contradicts the intuitive concept of knowledge.**
