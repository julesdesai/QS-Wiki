**Parent:** [Could someone be said to know a proposition if their reliable process is unknown to them?](could-someone-be-said-to-know-a-proposition-if-their-reliable-process-is-unknown-to-them-antithesis-a82d40e2-ac96-4ca0-8786-b68c3713dbf9.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they have a justified true belief formed through a reliable process**, **A belief-forming process is reliable if it generally leads to true beliefs in similar situations**, **Justification requires some awareness of the reliability of the belief-forming process**
