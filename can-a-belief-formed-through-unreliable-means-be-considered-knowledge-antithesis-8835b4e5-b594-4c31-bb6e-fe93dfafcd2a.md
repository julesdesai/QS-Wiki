# Can a belief formed through unreliable means be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8835b4e5-b594-4c31-bb6e-fe93dfafcd2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person arrives at a true belief using an unreliable process, like flipping a coin. Intuitively, we would not say that the person knows the proposition, even if their belief is true.**, **Intellectual virtue, defined as reliably leading to true beliefs, seems incompatible with a process that is not consistently reliable in generating true beliefs, challenging the view that knowledge requires intellectual virtue.**
