# Can reliable cognitive processes lead to false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee107cf5-fbd0-40fe-bc08-5000924cc999
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-4db07ebc-4cb3-402b-b335-2b01121c3e1a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief using a cognitive process that is generally reliable but occasionally leads to false beliefs. Intuitively, one might argue that this person does not know the proposition despite the overall reliability**, **It is implausible that the mere likelihood of a process leading to truth ensures knowledge if it sometimes errs in particular instances**
