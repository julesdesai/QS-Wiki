# How can we handle the new evil demon problem within Process Reliabilism?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a tree is outside their window based on reliable perception. In an alternative scenario, an evil demon deceives them into maintaining this belief, even though there is no tree. Intuitively, we would judge that the person is justified in their belief in both cases, as they cannot distinguish between the two scenarios.**, **A plausible principle is that people should have the same level of justification for their beliefs if they cannot differentiate between being deceived and not being deceived, even if the process is unreliable. This principle and the thought experiment suggest justification isn't solely dependent on reliability.**
