# Is perception alone sufficient for the formation of knowledge as basic belief suggests?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f60182a0-22ad-45da-a8d7-838fb9842fb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person perceives an object in dim lighting, forming a justified belief about its color that later turns out to be incorrect due to the inadequate conditions. Intuitively, it seems that perception alone, under certain conditions, fails to serve as a reliable foundation for knowledge.**, **This scenario implies that perception must meet certain conditions to be a trustworthy source of foundational beliefs for knowledge.**
