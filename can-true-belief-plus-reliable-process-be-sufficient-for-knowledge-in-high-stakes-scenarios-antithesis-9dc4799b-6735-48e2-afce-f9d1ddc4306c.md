# Can true belief plus reliable process be sufficient for knowledge in high-stakes scenarios?

**Node ID:** 9dc4799b-6735-48e2-afce-f9d1ddc4306c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Internalism](contextualist-internalism-synthesis-130e5ac9-b512-486c-b0ba-4156d7ff0e7d.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes they have reached their flight gate due to trusting the airport signs, which are generally reliable.**, **If in a high-stakes situation, such as a sudden gate change not reflected in signs, the belief is true, yet the person may lack knowledge because reliable processes fail due to specific conditions.**
