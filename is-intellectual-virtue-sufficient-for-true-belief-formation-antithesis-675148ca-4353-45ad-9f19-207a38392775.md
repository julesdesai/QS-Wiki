# Is intellectual virtue sufficient for true belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 675148ca-4353-45ad-9f19-207a38392775
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-35af9163-7530-4447-9474-ccb688222939.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person exhibits intellectual virtue, such as careful reasoning, but forms a false belief as their sources of information are corrupted. Intuitively, we might judge that intellectual virtue does not always lead to true beliefs.**, **This suggests that the presence of intellectual virtue alone is not sufficient for the formation of true beliefs, questioning its role in knowledge.**
