# Does functional phenomenalism explain illusion or error in perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f211734-74a7-4664-9f20-36d2b4145241
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a stick in water appears bent, though it is straight. Intuitively, we either have to say that the perceptual knowledge is mistaken or that functional roles are insufficient for accuracy. If we say perception is mistaken, then functional roles must account for illusions. If we say roles are inadequate, then functional phenomenalism may not fully capture perceptual errors.**
