# What about testimonial knowledge derived from others' virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebb795ea-4622-4125-bec1-9ce635c2e3c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person gains true belief through testimony, relying on the intellectual virtue of the testifier, not their own. Intuitively, we often regard testimonial knowledge as valid.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can be derived from another's intellectual virtue, but might not require the knower's own exercise of such virtues. This principle suggests that the requirement for personal intellectual virtue in knowledge formation can be overstated.**
