# Can the context of unknown false beliefs negate apparent knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ec3497b-b66f-44d5-a39c-7ccfcc4e577e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Lemma Theory](no-false-lemma-theory-synthesis-4a11ee42-2be9-4836-9dd6-d365881bb277.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a scientist believes a certain drug cures a disease based on a series of experiments, but unbeknownst to them, some experiments are flawed. Intuitively, while the belief in the drug's efficacy may be justified, the unknown falsehoods in experiments negate the certainty of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if reliance on flawed data forms part of the reasoning, the resulting belief is suspect in terms of knowledge. This principle, combined with the fact of unknowable flawed data, challenges knowledge even with formationally correct aspects.**
