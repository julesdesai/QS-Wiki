# Can one have justified foundational beliefs without context emphasis?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d13e9e0e-dfa7-4fba-934b-bb1e0159e844
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-b0409110-d832-4fdb-9fe3-2e01026d1f88.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief based on foundational beliefs not tailored to the specific context in which the belief is considered. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief lacks justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification requires beliefs to be specific to the context in which they arise. This principle together with the scenario suggests that justification may be absent without context emphasis.**
