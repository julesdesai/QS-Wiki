# Are beneficial practical consequences necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 31f685fe-0073-4371-869d-6b5ec9c92c14
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Consequences](pragmatism-with-practical-consequences-synthesis-9fee2762-7d04-464a-9533-5a40ce1aa186.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows a proposition with no immediate practical consequence, such as knowing the solution to a complex puzzle. Intuitively, they have knowledge even if it does not lead to any practical outcome.**, **Knowledge can be valuable for understanding or intrinsic reasons, without needing immediate practical relevance.**
