# How do hallucinations challenge the role of stability in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91e50b8e-8efa-498c-9982-4c79a5a7a4ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has stable, recurring hallucinations of an intruder in their home across different contexts and times. Intuitively, this recurring perception does not secure knowledge of an actual intruder.**, **It is implausible that stability in hallucinations, which are known to be false, can constitute knowledge, suggesting that stability must be properly integrated with other truth-indicating processes.**
