# Can false beliefs not stemming from malfunction be explained?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7aa2c735-2800-4b8e-a504-3d3eb119b755
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Proper Functionalism](knowledge-as-proper-functionalism-synthesis-95adcceb-ce7f-44ef-aea6-bebac418915b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person holds a false belief despite their faculties functioning properly, such as when they are misled by someone they trust. Intuitively, this seems to challenge the idea that proper functioning always leads to true beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that proper functioning should account for the reliability of information sources to avoid false beliefs.**
