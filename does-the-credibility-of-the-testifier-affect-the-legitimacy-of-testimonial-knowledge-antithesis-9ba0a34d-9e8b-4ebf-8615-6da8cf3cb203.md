# Does the credibility of the testifier affect the legitimacy of testimonial knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9ba0a34d-9e8b-4ebf-8615-6da8cf3cb203
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Justification Theory](testimonial-justification-theory-synthesis-a0572f9e-ea0a-45f6-9bf1-a16e71038692.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the credibility of an informant affects the justification of beliefs acquired from their testimony. This principle together with the idea that testimony can serve as independent knowledge entails a tension when informants vary in reliability.**, **Consider a scenario where a known habitual liar provides testimony. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge obtained from such a source lacks justification due to credibility issues.**
