# Does overemphasis on reliability ignore the subjective aspect of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a024ae2c-f32b-4dfc-bb1f-b128695f4cf0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Consider that a person might justifiably believe something based on their evidence, yet their belief is not produced by the typically reliable process. Intuitively, their belief might still seem justified based on subjective reasoning.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification should take into account the subjective aspect of belief formation. Exclusively emphasizing external reliability can overlook subjective justification.**
