# Knowledge as Justified True Belief with Contextual Truth Condition

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** e33f6615-b04d-49d5-b1e9-20e394bb8bed
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can perceptual knowledge exist without absolute certainty?](can-perceptual-knowledge-exist-without-absolute-certainty-antithesis-989a43c9-0be5-4c63-a82b-c8d2b00501ab.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is justified**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the truth condition is determined contextually and not by absolute certainty**
