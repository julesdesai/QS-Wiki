# Can Reliabilism Explain Why Certain Beliefs Are Not Knowledge in Demon Worlds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 46ac263f-a552-49a9-8516-5502504261e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we imagine a "demon world" where an evil demon interferes with reliable cognitive processes to occasionally produce false beliefs. Intuitively, beliefs might fall short of knowledge despite the reliability of the process when uncontaminated.**, **In such scenarios, the possibility of systematic deception might undermine the adequacy of reliance on reliable processes alone for knowledge attribution.**
