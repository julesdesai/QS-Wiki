# Does cross-modal perception require integration that is not direct?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d50a947e-9fc1-43bf-9d4a-b39ce9d0c603
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism with Direct Cross-Modal Sensing](naive-realism-with-direct-cross-modal-sensing-synthesis-96f2c374-253a-4f49-a9c2-1ee8405679ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person perceives an object as a whole through touch and sight simultaneously. Intuitively, we would judge that the integration of the sensory inputs from both modalities requires some form of processing that is not direct.**, **There is a plausible principle that integration of sensory inputs from different modalities necessitates a mediator to combine them without contradiction. This principle together with the claim that perception is direct implies a conflict in how cross-modal perception combines inputs.**
