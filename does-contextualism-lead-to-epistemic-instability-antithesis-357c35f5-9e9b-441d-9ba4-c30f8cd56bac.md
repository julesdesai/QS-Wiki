# Does contextualism lead to epistemic instability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 357c35f5-9e9b-441d-9ba4-c30f8cd56bac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism on Epistemic Justification](contextualism-on-epistemic-justification-synthesis-c8f3ec75-75e9-400a-9969-d95699e610cc.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person's context changes frequently, leading to shifts in what counts as justification; this suggests that knowledge lacks stability.**, **If context is continually shifting, then the idea of a stable and coherent concept of knowledge becomes implausible.**
