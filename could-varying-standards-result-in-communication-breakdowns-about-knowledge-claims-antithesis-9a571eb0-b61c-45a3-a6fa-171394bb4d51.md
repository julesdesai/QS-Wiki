# Could varying standards result in communication breakdowns about knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a571eb0-b61c-45a3-a6fa-171394bb4d51
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c0d03a39-c775-403c-87e2-d078dab04731.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where two individuals are discussing a knowledge claim, different contextual standards might lead each individual to different conclusions about the claim's validity.**, **If the two individuals do not share the same context, they might disagree about whether a claim constitutes knowledge, leading to miscommunication.**, **Such disagreements about knowledge claims due to contextual differences could challenge the practicality of communicating knowledge.**
