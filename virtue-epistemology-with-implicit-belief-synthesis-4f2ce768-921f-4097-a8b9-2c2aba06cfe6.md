# Virtue Epistemology with Implicit Belief

**Depth:** 5

**Parent:** [Is believing a proposition necessary for knowledge?](is-believing-a-proposition-necessary-for-knowledge-antithesis-55db3127-d284-4421-aca9-b858ed57b495.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they implicitly believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the implicit belief is a result of intellectual virtue**, **An intellectual virtue is a trait that systematically leads to acquiring true beliefs**, **Implicit belief involves a tendency to act as if the proposition is true**
