# Can consciousness be entirely bypassed in the acquisition of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c4d26be1-c61a-4ced-a9c4-47f00666a97b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Approach](virtue-epistemology-approach-synthesis-b14f243d-0108-46b0-abbb-b26bd31de791.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual forms a belief unconsciously, influenced solely by intellectual virtue, without any conscious belief in the truth of the matter. Intuitively, it is questionable whether the individual truly possesses knowledge in this case.**, **Knowledge typically involves some degree of awareness or consciousness on the part of the knower.**
