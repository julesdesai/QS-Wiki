# Can justified beliefs lead to knowledge if standards differ greatly?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8301b807-a12a-4ad5-85a7-f6ac43f00849
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person A knows a proposition under low standards of justification, but person B does not know the same proposition under higher standards. Intuitively, if both are in similar epistemic situations, it seems odd that one knows and one does not**, **If justification standards vary with context too much, it could allow for situations where individuals with similar evidence reach different conclusions about knowledge, which challenges the consistency of knowledge**
