# Can there be knowledge without the possibility of explanation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e629468f-02f4-4d0e-917d-b15382d217a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone holds a true belief, but they cannot explain or articulate any reason or cause for this belief. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not possess knowledge since explanations are typically required for knowledge.**, **It is implausible for an unknowable justification to suffice in classifying a belief as knowledge.**
