# Are hallucinations a reliable measure of undermining perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91316693-f7a2-48ca-825f-4427e0ca4318
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**Hallucinations occur when there is a false perception of reality that is not matched by external stimuli. In such cases, intuitively we would judge that hallucinations are an unreliable measure of general perceptual reliability.**, **Suppose we examine cases where hallucinations are rare or unprecedented within a context. In these cases, the assumption that their presence universally undermines reliability is implausible.**
