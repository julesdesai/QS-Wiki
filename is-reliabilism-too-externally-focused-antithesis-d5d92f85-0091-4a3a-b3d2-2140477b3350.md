# Is reliabilism too externally focused?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d5d92f85-0091-4a3a-b3d2-2140477b3350
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires internal justification or understanding, involving a clear cognitive grasp of why a belief is true. This principle raises concerns about whether focusing solely on external reliability might overlook the importance of internal reasoning processes.**, **Suppose we have a scenario in which a person consistently uses a reliable shortcut (e.g., a calculator for math problems) without understanding the math involved. Intuitively, reliance on this external tool without internal comprehension seems insufficient for genuine knowledge.**
