# Can false but useful beliefs qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24d00ae5-380d-4d0b-984d-57fd14b3531c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes something false that leads to practical success, such as a placebo effect. Intuitively, this is not knowledge.**, **Practical success without truth fails to satisfy the criteria for knowledge given that knowledge involves an accurate representation of reality.**
