# Can someone believe a true proposition based on external indicators without genuine justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0bdf91d7-559a-42c9-901d-161d46cd7cd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes it is noon because they hear a clock chime, but the clock is malfunctioning and stopped, even though it happens to be noon. Intuitively, the person does not seem to truly know that it is noon.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief requires more than just external coincidence to be considered justified knowledge. This principle suggests that mere true belief due to external indicators without reliable sources does not constitute justified knowledge.**
