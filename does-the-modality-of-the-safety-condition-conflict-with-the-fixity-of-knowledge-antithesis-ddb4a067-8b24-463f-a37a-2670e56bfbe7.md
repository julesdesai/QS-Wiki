# Does the modality of the safety condition conflict with the fixity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ddb4a067-8b24-463f-a37a-2670e56bfbe7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-b5a72759-42a3-4d9b-b74c-df40c84bdc9d.md)

## Content

**The notion of safety involves evaluating most nearby possible worlds, but knowledge is typically considered a more stable or certain state.**, **Consider the situation where slight changes in conditions across possible worlds make a true belief safe or unsafe. Intuitively, we expect knowledge to remain fixed irrespective of such changes.**
