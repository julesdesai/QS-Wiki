# Is the truth condition always sufficient by itself for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 63e8fffa-43a5-41ec-881a-2a03c82a7404
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a true belief arises from random guessing rather than intellectual virtue or reliable processes. Intuitively, we would judge that despite the truth condition being met, knowledge is not present without virtuous or reliable cognition.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth must be accompanied by other components like justification or reliability to constitute genuine knowledge. This principle implies that truth on its own is insufficient.**
