# Can conflicting perspectives both have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f94b7f86-c887-483c-8266-7be7fdc88830
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism about Knowledge](relativism-about-knowledge-synthesis-db569ddc-fb91-431d-962c-b8c6e0bc3b22.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two evaluators have conflicting perspectives on the same proposition, each believing they know it. Intuitively, they cannot both truly know the proposition if one denies what the other affirms.**, **If knowledge is perspective-dependent, then conflicting perspectives would both claim knowledge, leading to contradictions.**
