# Can an infinite chain of reasons really provide justification without a foundation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4354878-3276-4fcc-846f-8810b82f60fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person attempts to justify their belief in a proposition by citing an endless series of beliefs, none of which have a foundational starting point. Intuitively, it seems implausible that such a series provides genuine justification, as each belief depends on another without a clear initial anchor.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine justification requires at least one fundamental belief or point of certainty. This principle together with the absence of a foundational belief in an infinite chain entails that the chain cannot provide genuine justification.**
