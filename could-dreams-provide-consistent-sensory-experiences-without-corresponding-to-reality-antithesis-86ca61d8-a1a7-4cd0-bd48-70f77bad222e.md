# Could dreams provide consistent sensory experiences without corresponding to reality?

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person consistently dreams of flying, resulting in rich sensory experiences; intuitively, these do not provide knowledge of actual flying ability.**, **A scenario where dream experiences consistently contradict waking reality suggests that only relying on sensory consistency is insufficient for knowledge.**
