## Content

**Knowledge is a true belief arising from intellectual virtue**, **Intellectual virtues are reliable cognitive faculties, skills, or traits**, **The exercise of these intellectual virtues leads to the formation of true beliefs**, **The belief must be true because of the agent’s intellectual virtue**, **For a belief to count as knowledge, it must be sensitive, meaning if the belief were false, the agent would not hold it**
