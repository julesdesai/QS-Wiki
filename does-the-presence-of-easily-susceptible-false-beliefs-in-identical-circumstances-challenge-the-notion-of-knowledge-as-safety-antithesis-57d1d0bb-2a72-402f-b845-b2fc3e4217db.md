# Does the presence of easily susceptible false beliefs in identical circumstances challenge the notion of knowledge as safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57d1d0bb-2a72-402f-b845-b2fc3e4217db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person believes their car is parked in front of their house based on visual confirmation, but identical looking cars often frequent the area. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know their car is there due to possible false beliefs occurring in similar setups.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is threatened by the availability of false beliefs in practically identical conditions, which undermines the 'could not easily have been false' criterion of knowledge.**
