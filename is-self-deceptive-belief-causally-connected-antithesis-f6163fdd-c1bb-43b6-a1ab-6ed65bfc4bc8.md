# Is self-deceptive belief causally connected?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6163fdd-c1bb-43b6-a1ab-6ed65bfc4bc8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who intentionally ignores news of a stock collapse because of personal bias yet correctly believes the stock value will rise through sheer optimism. We intuitively struggle to call such biased belief knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that self-deceptive beliefs may not be causally connected appropriately for true knowledge. This principle together with the necessity of truthful causation puts self-deception at odds with acknowledged knowledge.**, **It is implausible that self-deception can lead to knowledge, as it distorts the causal conditions required for maintaining truth in beliefs.**
