# Are reliable processes sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8880fa0-5b99-4e37-8cd3-01c8054b0f2c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliabilism](externalism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c10e5c74-1dbc-479d-ba5e-469176c5d537.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a belief formed by a generally reliable cognitive process but fails in this specific instance. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition or not. If we say they know, then the reliability criterion is violated. If we say they do not know, then reliabilism is insufficient for knowledge.**
