# Is knowledge possible with conflicting evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3da52a0-17e5-4b8f-bb04-5cda5a2018ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a historian holds a belief about a historical event, supported by extensive documentation, but newly discovered conflicting evidence from a credible source challenges the original belief. Intuitively, we might judge that the historian’s knowledge is compromised by this new evidence despite the existing justification.**, **Conflicting evidence presents a challenge to the claim that an individual can know a proposition solely based on existing supporting evidence.**
