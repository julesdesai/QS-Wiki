# Can cognitive malfunction occasionally lead to genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 63fd0865-f0cd-4c98-9594-3fc6bc8fcf9b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-ea617099-7596-484d-aca6-1b318b0033ac.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual with a cognitive dysfunction that randomly provides true beliefs without a clear design plan. Intuitively, these beliefs can sometimes constitute knowledge despite the lack of proper function.**, **It is implausible that only cognitive faculties functioning according to a design plan can yield knowledge, given that disorders or anomalies can sometimes produce true beliefs.**
