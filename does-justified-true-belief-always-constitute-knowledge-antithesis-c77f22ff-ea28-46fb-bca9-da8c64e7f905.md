# Does justified true belief always constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c77f22ff-ea28-46fb-bca9-da8c64e7f905
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person justifiably believes a true proposition based on faulty reasoning. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **A justified true belief obtained through unreliable methods is insufficient for knowledge.**
