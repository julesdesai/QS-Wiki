# How can varying standards affect the consistency of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 013064d3-d4d2-4da8-aa89-760762e3f63a
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-thesis-de4edc71-c75a-4fcd-afbe-4333fb44b1b5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be consistent across different contexts.**, **Consider two contexts with different standards where a person is said to know the same proposition in one context but not in another. Intuitively, this inconsistency challenges the nature of knowledge.**, **Varying standards threaten the consistency of attributing knowledge.**
