# Does practical success alone ensure knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a2a6e0e-8d46-4dff-8a59-4a43237d0a51
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a belief that a treatment will cure their illness because it has done so in the past for many individuals. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge if the treatment's success was due to hidden factors unrelated to the belief.**, **Even if the belief leads to practical success, like recovery, it is implausible to say it constitutes knowledge if the belief was based on misleading or incomplete evidence.**
