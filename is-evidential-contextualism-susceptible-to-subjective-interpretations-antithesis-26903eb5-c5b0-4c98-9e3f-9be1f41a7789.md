# Is evidential contextualism susceptible to subjective interpretations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 26903eb5-c5b0-4c98-9e3f-9be1f41a7789
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**The idea that the amount of evidence needed for justification depends on the context opens room for subjective interpretation of what constitutes a context.**, **Since context might be interpreted differently by different individuals, this can lead to subjective judgments about what counts as evidence or knowledge.**, **Inconsistencies in subjective context interpretation challenge objective assessment in evaluating knowledge.**
