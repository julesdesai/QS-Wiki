# Is it sensible to claim knowledge when justification is inaccessible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6261bb0c-aa28-4eb6-aec3-f14c081119a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism highlighting context-dependent justification](contextualism-highlighting-context-dependent-justification-synthesis-c9f62fb8-5b79-43c8-99f6-6461f5a6c05a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition but cannot articulate why they are justified in that belief due to contextual inaccessibility. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief is less robust than one where justification is accessible.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be transparent or understandable, which entails that inaccessible justification might fail to meet the reliability required for true knowledge.**
