# Is all belief arising from virtue knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d23e852a-9932-4180-aec0-171e7222ab7f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person exercises intellectual virtues and forms a true belief about a future event, which coincidentally aligns with the truth not due to their intellectual endeavor. Intuitively, we might doubt whether this should truly count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must involve more than merely exercising intellectual virtue and arriving at a true belief. This principle implies that belief formed from virtue is insufficient for establishing knowledge.**
