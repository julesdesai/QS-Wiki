# Can reliance on foundational beliefs alone be sufficient for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae87686e-f061-46ba-8888-e87ecbf732fd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-c35a9476-dc46-42db-bda2-17c735809ae7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that any justified belief must be open to verification or challenge. This principle entails that relying solely on foundational beliefs may not be sufficient.**, **Empirically, beliefs that are considered self-evident or empirically certain can sometimes be proven false, suggesting they should not be the sole basis for justification.**
