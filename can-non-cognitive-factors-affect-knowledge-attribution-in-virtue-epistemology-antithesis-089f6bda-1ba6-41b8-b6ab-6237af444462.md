# Can non-cognitive factors affect knowledge attribution in virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 089f6bda-1ba6-41b8-b6ab-6237af444462
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person's belief is influenced by strong emotional biases rather than intellectual virtues. Intuitively, beliefs heavily influenced by emotions are not typically seen as knowledge.**, **A cognitive character trait should not primarily depend on emotions if it is to be considered an intellectual virtue.**, **Emotions can affect belief formation in ways unrelated to the reliability of cognitive traits.**, **If emotional processes undermine reliable cognitive traits, this challenges the notion that knowledge comes from intellectual virtue.**
