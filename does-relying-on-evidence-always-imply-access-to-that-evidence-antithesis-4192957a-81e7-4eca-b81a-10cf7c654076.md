# Does relying on evidence always imply access to that evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4192957a-81e7-4eca-b81a-10cf7c654076
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that evidence supporting a belief can exist outside the awareness of the believer, yet still contribute to the formation of knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where scientific data supports a person's belief, but the person has no direct access to this data. Intuitively, we might say the person's belief arises from genuine knowledge.**
