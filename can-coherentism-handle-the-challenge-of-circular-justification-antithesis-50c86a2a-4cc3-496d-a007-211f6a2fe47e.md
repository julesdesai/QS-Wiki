# Can coherentism handle the challenge of circular justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50c86a2a-4cc3-496d-a007-211f6a2fe47e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism View](coherentism-view-synthesis-4dcf0483-85cc-4ed6-8a5e-86aaec2b7626.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief system where individual beliefs justify each other in a circular manner, such as 'belief A is true because of belief B, and belief B is true because of belief A'. Intuitively, we would judge that circular justification fails to provide adequate support for any belief.**, **Circularity within a belief system contradicts a plausible principle that justification needs an independent basis or reason.**
