# Is the existence of an objective reality meaningful if our perceptions are always imperfect representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b7a9a95-59e2-4eb3-adc8-04ad1dd6a5a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-de08aecd-5e9b-4f9b-874a-d2dc97cb1697.md)

## Content

**Suppose a color-blind individual perceives a red apple as gray. Intuitively, this perception is an imperfect representation due to the person's sensory limitation.**, **The fact that the individual's perception cannot accurately represent the true color suggests that objective reality's existence might be conceptually irrelevant if inaccessible.**, **If all perceptions are flawed, the notion of objective reality becomes problematic since it cannot be known with certainty.**
