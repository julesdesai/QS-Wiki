# Do hallucinations undermine direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e6928d1-1e24-4777-ae65-0efed30ba643
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person experiences a vivid hallucination of a pink elephant in the room due to a medical condition. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person perceives an actual pink elephant or they do not. If we say they do perceive a pink elephant, then hallucinations provide knowledge of non-existent objects. If we say they do not, then hallucinations show that perception can mislead without access to reality.**, **It is implausible that hallucinations reveal real external objects, instead they point to the mind's role in perception.**
