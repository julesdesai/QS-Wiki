# Can knowledge be attributed to someone who accidentally forms a true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ca78c35-9bf8-41cd-a892-4aa0ebf60ad0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone forms a true belief accidentally, without using any intellectual virtue or cognitive ability. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite the truth of their belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a true belief is formed accidentally, without the use of intellectual virtue, it cannot qualify as knowledge. This principle challenges the idea that knowledge necessarily involves intellectual virtue.**
