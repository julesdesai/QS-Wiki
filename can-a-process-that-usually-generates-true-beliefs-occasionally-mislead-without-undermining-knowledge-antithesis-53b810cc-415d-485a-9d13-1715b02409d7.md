# Can a process that usually generates true beliefs occasionally mislead without undermining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 53b810cc-415d-485a-9d13-1715b02409d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a process like a normally accurate clock that is occasionally faulty, indicating an incorrect time. A person believing the incorrect time does not have knowledge.**, **Despite being generally reliable, the clock's occasional faults lead to false beliefs, which argues against the attribution of knowledge.**
