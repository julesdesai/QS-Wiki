# Could knowledge be independent of contextual variation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c4f5463-6ae2-47f1-bc03-56675c936d9a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**The standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, implying that knowledge is context-dependent. Contrast this with the plausible idea that knowledge might possess universal standards that remain irrespective of contextual changes.**, **Consider a scenario where two scientists observe the same phenomenon, and only one is credited with knowledge because of context-specific standards. Intuitively, this suggests an inconsistency with the concept of universal knowledge.**
