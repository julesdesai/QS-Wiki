# Can a belief track the truth and yet fail to be knowledge due to preemption?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6ac9b7a-5b62-4dd5-affe-ea4476ef0a03
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-34b45a3c-9960-48d7-bb61-82e72864432f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a proposition and that belief is true and would have been held if true and not held if false. If, however, the causal chain supporting the belief is preempted by another factor that would have led to the belief even if false, intuitively we may judge that the belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **In this scenario, intuitively we may feel discomfort in claiming knowledge even though the formal conditions of truth-tracking are satisfied because the belief’s basis was interfered with by misleading evidence that wasn't acted upon.**
