# Can there be true beliefs that do not require an interconnected web?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d9a39f7-1ad9-458f-b045-611f23d1a6ea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Foundationalism](coherentist-foundationalism-synthesis-815fa62f-5e40-4962-8349-ea400fc3f5ce.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a mathematical truth such as "2+2=4" based solely on direct intuition without relying on other beliefs. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows this mathematical truth.**, **A mathematical intuition does not require an interconnected web of other beliefs, as it seems to stand alone as self-evident.**
