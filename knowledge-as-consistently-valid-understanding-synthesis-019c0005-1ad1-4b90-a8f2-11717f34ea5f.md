# Knowledge as Consistently Valid Understanding

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is it theoretically intuitive that understanding a proposition with false practical consequences can constitute knowledge?](is-it-theoretically-intuitive-that-understanding-a-proposition-with-false-practical-consequences-can-constitute-knowledge-antithesis-08dd7106-5621-49b6-b6da-dcdece78e6cc.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they understand the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the understanding consistently yields valid practical outcomes**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they understand the proposition, the proposition is true, and the understanding consistently yields valid practical outcomes**
