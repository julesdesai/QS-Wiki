# Can intellectual virtues lead to false beliefs yet still constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e545ae97-73aa-4684-a1ff-a9667e26a312
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-05879bdc-4686-45ec-8d5f-c387922414d1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses their intellectual virtues to arrive at a belief that is later overturned due to new evidence. Intuitively, it seems that before the evidence is revealed, the person might still be said to have known the proposition temporarily, even if it becomes false.**
