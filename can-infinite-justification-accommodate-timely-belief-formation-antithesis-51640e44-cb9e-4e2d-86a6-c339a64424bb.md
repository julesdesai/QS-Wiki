# Can infinite justification accommodate timely belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 51640e44-cb9e-4e2d-86a6-c339a64424bb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Consider a practical scenario where a person needs to form a justified belief rapidly, such as a firefighter deciding whether a building is safe. Intuitively, relying on an infinite chain for justification seems impractical as it does not allow for quick decision-making.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification should be able to inform belief formation in a timely manner. This principle, together with the potential impracticality of an infinite chain, implies that such a chain may not be suitable for real-world decision-making scenarios.**
