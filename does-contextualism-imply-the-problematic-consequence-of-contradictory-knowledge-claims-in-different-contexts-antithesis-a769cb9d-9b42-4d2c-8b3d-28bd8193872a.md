# Does contextualism imply the problematic consequence of contradictory knowledge claims in different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a769cb9d-9b42-4d2c-8b3d-28bd8193872a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person makes a knowledge claim in a casual context and denies it in a more rigorous scientific context. Intuitively, it appears contradictory to both know and not know the same thing.**, **If context can freely change knowledge claims, it might lead to conceptual difficulties in maintaining what people actually know.**, **The flexibility of contextual standards may result in knowledge attribution that violates principles of logical consistency across contexts.**
