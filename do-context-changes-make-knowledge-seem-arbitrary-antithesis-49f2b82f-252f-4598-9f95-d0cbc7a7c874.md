# Do context changes make knowledge seem arbitrary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 49f2b82f-252f-4598-9f95-d0cbc7a7c874
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism With Context-Dependent Justification Standards](contextualism-with-context-dependent-justification-standards-synthesis-a8126985-f485-4c51-b545-16d8a583ea1e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person meets the justification standards in a context but due to a slight, seemingly irrelevant contextual shift, they no longer meet the justification standards. Intuitively, either we wrongly assess knowledge in one of the contexts or the assignment of knowledge seems arbitrary.**, **If we say the person knows in the initial context, then a small change should not make them lose knowledge without any change in evidence.**, **If we say the person does not know in the shifted context, then we might consider how minor changes in context can drastically affect knowledge claims, which appears arbitrary.**
