# Is intersubjective agreement necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 901104d3-bb3f-485f-b5a8-31543a76221c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-eea6cb23-77a9-47a4-87da-736833e1953e.md)

## Content

**Two people with different sensory experiences about the same phenomenon, like one person seeing a red object and another seeing it as green, face challenges in claiming objective knowledge of the phenomenon.**, **The theoretical intuition is that for a proposition to be known, it should be stable across different subjective experiences.**, **Intersubjective variability poses a problem for the claim that knowledge is merely about individual sensory appearances.**
