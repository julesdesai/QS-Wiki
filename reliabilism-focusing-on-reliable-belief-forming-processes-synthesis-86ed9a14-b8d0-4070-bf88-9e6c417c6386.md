# Reliabilism focusing on reliable belief-forming processes

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 86ed9a14-b8d0-4070-bf88-9e6c417c6386
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can foundationalism accommodate moral knowledge?](can-foundationalism-accommodate-moral-knowledge-antithesis-becee479-b7d2-4eb2-955f-54d00c3d0445.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the justification involves processes that reliably lead to true beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief results from reliable processes**
