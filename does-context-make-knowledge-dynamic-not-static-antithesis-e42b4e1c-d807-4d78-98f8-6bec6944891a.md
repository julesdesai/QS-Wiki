# Does context make knowledge dynamic, not static?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e42b4e1c-d807-4d78-98f8-6bec6944891a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider the claim that knowledge, traditionally seen as static once obtained, becomes dynamic with contexts that can change over time and place. Intuitively, this feels contrary to the classical understanding of knowledge as something stable and unchanging once acquired.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain consistent across different circumstances once it is affirmed. This principle together with contextualism implies a conflict, as knowledge could change with shifting contexts.**
