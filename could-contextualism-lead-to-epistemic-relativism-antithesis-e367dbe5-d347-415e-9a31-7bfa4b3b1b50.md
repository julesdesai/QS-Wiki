# Could contextualism lead to epistemic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e367dbe5-d347-415e-9a31-7bfa4b3b1b50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that knowledge should maintain some level of uniformity across differing perspectives to resist relativism. This principle together with the context-sensitive nature of knowledge standards suggests that epistemic relativism might arise, where knowledge is not universally agreed upon.**, **Suppose we have a case in which what counts as knowledge radically shifts between cultural or social contexts. Intuitively, this could lead to a form of relativism that challenges universal epistemic standards.**
