# Is knowledge possible through indirect perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f2931dc-37a7-407d-835a-a2ed11e22976
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person understands the structure of a molecule through a scientific model rather than direct perception of the molecule itself. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person does not know the structure or that they do. If we say the person does not know, then many scientific understandings would be denied knowledge status. If we say the person does know, then knowledge through indirect perception is possible.**, **There is a plausible principle that scientific models offer a form of indirect perception that can lead to knowledge. This principle suggests that indirect ways of understanding might offer valid knowledge.**
