# Does proper functionalism ignore the role of reasoning in knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 700e4677-794e-4fe3-9f23-ea94680cc412
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-56ac2890-cc65-4132-90ba-c28a7f97b59c.md)

## Content

**A person may acquire true beliefs through sound reasoning even if some cognitive faculties are not functioning perfectly.**, **Proper functionalism suggests that knowledge depends heavily on properly functioning faculties rather than reasoning processes.**, **The emphasis on faculties may deemphasize the value of coherent reasoning paths in arriving at true beliefs.**
