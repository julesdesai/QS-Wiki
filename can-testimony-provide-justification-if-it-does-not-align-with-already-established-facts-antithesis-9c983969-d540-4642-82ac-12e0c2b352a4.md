# Can testimony provide justification if it does not align with already established facts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c983969-d540-4642-82ac-12e0c2b352a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Justification Theory](testimonial-justification-theory-synthesis-a0572f9e-ea0a-45f6-9bf1-a16e71038692.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual receives testimony that contradicts well-established facts. Intuitively, we either have to say that the testimony alone is sufficient for knowledge or that established facts hold greater weight. If we say the testimony alone is sufficient, then established facts can be disregarded, leading to conflicts with prior knowledge. If we say established facts hold greater weight, then testimonial knowledge is secondary.**, **It is implausible that testimony can override established facts to justify knowledge without additional support or reasoning.**
