# Are abstract concepts knowable under phenomenalist instrumentalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e7d107d3-790e-4ef4-a047-0638c2d844b0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalist Instrumentalism](phenomenalist-instrumentalism-synthesis-4537874c-c8f8-46da-9468-0a115148189b.md)

## Content

**Mathematical or abstract knowledge, such as the Pythagorean theorem, does not typically involve predictions about future perceptual experiences. It is implausible that mathematical knowledge should be understood solely in terms of perceptual predictions.**, **Abstract concepts like justice might be known by people without apparent direct connection to perceptual predictions. This poses a difficulty for the idea that only future predictive claims are knowable.**
