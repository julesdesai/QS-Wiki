# Are there forms of perceptual experience that disjunctivism cannot account for?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ad4f89d-e686-47a7-8b62-dcf44937dd98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-ef0ab88b-769a-4e6f-afc6-1fb31c926a76.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case of afterimage perception where an observer sees colors projected on a white wall after looking at a bright light. Intuitively, it is unclear if this should be categorized as direct awareness or as an illusion.**, **A plausible principle is that comprehensive frameworks should fully address all kinds of perceptual experiences. The failure to clearly categorize afterimages within disjunctivism challenges this principle.**, **There is a logical fact that definitional clarity in categories such as direct awareness and illusion requires that all instances of perception have a defined category, which is problematic here.**
