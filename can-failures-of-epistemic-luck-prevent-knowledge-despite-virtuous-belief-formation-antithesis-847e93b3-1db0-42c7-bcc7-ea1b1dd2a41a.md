# Can failures of epistemic luck prevent knowledge despite virtuous belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 847e93b3-1db0-42c7-bcc7-ea1b1dd2a41a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology With Virtuous Belief Formation](virtue-epistemology-with-virtuous-belief-formation-synthesis-53b228ae-7464-400d-be01-037da588b5a0.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where someone correctly believes it will rain tomorrow based on well-honed meteorological skills, but the prediction coincidentally aligns with an anomaly in weather patterns.**, **Although the belief resulted from intellectual virtue, the truth aligns coincidentally given the unexpected anomaly.**, **Intuitively, since the true belief holds due to luck rather than because of the virtue, it seems questionable to consider it knowledge.**
