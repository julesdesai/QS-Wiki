# Are chance events improperly accommodated within causal theory for knowledge?

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a random event leads to a true belief, like a coin flip that coincidentally predicts a weather outcome.**, **Chance events can cause true beliefs without constituting reliable bases for knowledge.**, **If random events undermine justification, the requirement of a reliable causal process may be inappropriate for simple causation.**
