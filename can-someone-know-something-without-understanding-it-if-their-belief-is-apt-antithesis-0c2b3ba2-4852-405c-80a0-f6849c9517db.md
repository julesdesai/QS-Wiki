# Can someone know something without understanding it if their belief is apt?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c2b3ba2-4852-405c-80a0-f6849c9517db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Apt Belief](knowledge-as-apt-belief-synthesis-6ac0cb6c-f2b3-4636-9c8a-2a793aab4e4b.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that someone can know a proposition without understanding it, even if their belief is apt.**, **Suppose an individual has an apt belief about a complex scientific theory without any comprehension of the underlying mechanisms; intuitively, it seems peculiar to attribute them with knowledge.**, **Apt belief lacking understanding does not necessarily equate to possessing knowledge, as comprehension appears essential.**
