# Can knowledge exist without perceptual experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c45af83-7427-4d4e-b80b-026561f43cdc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Intersubjective Phenomenalism](intersubjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-0a89d160-2bee-479f-b8d3-046de6da5ef6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can exist in forms not requiring direct perceptual experience, such as mathematical truths. This principle together with the idea that knowledge is based solely on perceptual intersubjective agreement suggests that perceptual experience is not the only path to knowledge.**
