# Does memory provide sufficient evidence for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f75b3cd-7f20-4c47-9b99-24397fbfefa9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person relies on memory to believe a proposition about a past event, even though they no longer have direct access to the original evidence.**, **Intuitively, we would judge that they have knowledge of the past event, yet the initial evidence may no longer be accessible or sufficient.**, **This suggests that knowledge can persist even without current sufficient evidence.**
