# Can sensory experiences provide a complete account of objective reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 923f9174-9e3b-4350-b805-2526092ff068
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-11423d6b-b29a-46b2-967d-bb4b03435a75.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has a consistent set of sensory experiences of a pink elephant in their room, yet no physical pink elephant is present. Intuitively, we would judge that there is no objective reality corresponding to this sensory experience.**, **It is implausible that all consistent sensory experiences correspond to actual external objects.**, **Objective reality is often presumed to include objects that do not directly correspond to any particular sensory experience. For instance, distant galaxies that we have not observed still exist objectively.**
