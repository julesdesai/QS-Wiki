# Are mistaken beliefs due to cognitive errors still impactful on the theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e1eefb6-628b-4486-ad85-c7d804954354
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-193f50ad-c71a-4e26-bf9b-21d5bbf78043.md)

## Content

**Suppose an expert in a given field forms a belief based on sound cognitive ability, but due to an error in judgment, this belief is false. Intuitively, believing based on good cognitive abilities but erring suggests such beliefs might still be significant despite not being knowledge.**, **Errors in reasoning challenge the idea that successful cognitive functioning alone should earn intellectual credit and hence knowledge.**
