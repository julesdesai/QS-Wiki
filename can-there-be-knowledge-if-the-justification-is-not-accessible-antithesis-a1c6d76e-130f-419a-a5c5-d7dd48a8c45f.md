# Can there be knowledge if the justification is not accessible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a1c6d76e-130f-419a-a5c5-d7dd48a8c45f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Internalism with External Reliability](modified-internalism-with-external-reliability-synthesis-5d3adb72-ce8d-4976-b1d3-5d6a6f159cf7.md)

## Content

**Consider someone who unconsciously forms true beliefs through reliable intuition but cannot articulate why they believe it.**, **These beliefs are based on reliable processes, and they turn out to be true, therefore enhancing the justification.**, **Yet, the person lacks accessible justification for their belief, challenging the claim that knowledge requires accessible justification.**
