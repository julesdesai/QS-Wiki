# Is contextualist epistemology potentially self-refuting through its application?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ecf160fb-ef5c-4e66-ab59-f236fb13d5c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**If contextualism is correct, then the truth of contextualism itself should be context-dependent, raising questions about its stability as a philosophical view.**, **A plausible principle is that fundamental philosophical theories should not undermine themselves by being arbitrarily true or false.**, **If contextualism applies to itself, then the claim 'contextualism is true' becomes context-sensitive, potentially leading to its self-refutation.**
