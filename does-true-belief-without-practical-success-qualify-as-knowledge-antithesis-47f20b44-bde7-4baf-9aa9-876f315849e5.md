# Does true belief without practical success qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47f20b44-bde7-4baf-9aa9-876f315849e5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1312135d-680e-4dd5-9377-79ee82721c0c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a true belief that the traffic light is green; however, they do not successfully act on this belief by crossing the road due to unexpected barriers. Intuitively, we might judge that the person still has knowledge of the traffic light's color despite their lack of successful action.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge can exist independently of its practical application. This principle suggests that a true belief about some fact should be considered knowledge even if it does not lead to successful action.**
