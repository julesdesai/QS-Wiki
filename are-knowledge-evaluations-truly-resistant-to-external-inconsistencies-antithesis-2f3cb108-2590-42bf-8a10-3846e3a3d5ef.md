# Are knowledge evaluations truly resistant to external inconsistencies?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2f3cb108-2590-42bf-8a10-3846e3a3d5ef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone claims to know something within one context but must retract or modify that claim due to conflicting information from another context. Intuitively, this reveals an inconsistency between what should be clear and certain.**, **It is implausible that knowledge evaluations can be entirely internally consistent when there is an unavoidable influence from competing or conflicting contextual knowledge claims.**
