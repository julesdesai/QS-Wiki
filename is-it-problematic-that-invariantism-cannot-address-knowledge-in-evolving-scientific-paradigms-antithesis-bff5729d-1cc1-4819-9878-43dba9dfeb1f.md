# Is it problematic that invariantism cannot address knowledge in evolving scientific paradigms?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bff5729d-1cc1-4819-9878-43dba9dfeb1f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Epistemic Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-epistemic-standards-synthesis-0f962817-db98-42be-9977-f6a5e2bd4d1d.md)

## Content

**In science, paradigms shift with new evidence and theories. Beliefs once known may be overturned. Intuitively, scientific progress suggests dynamic standards.**, **The evolution of scientific knowledge suggests varying standards rather than fixed ones.**, **Invariant standards imply scientific knowledge should remain unchanged across paradigms, which is implausible.**
