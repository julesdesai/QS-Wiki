# Does connecting reasons infinitely risk circularity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e719baf9-69ac-44d3-b72f-defb2a2a8ce1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-d655bb23-a2c0-4c5f-b0d3-18cd986ab587.md)

## Content

**An infinite chain of reasons risks becoming circular because it lacks a foundational starting point.**, **Suppose each reason depends on a prior one ad infinitum, intuitively this tendency undermines the independence necessary to build justified beliefs.**, **Without a terminal or first reason, justification risks becoming a circuitous endeavor rather than a linear acquisition of knowledge.**
