# Does the requirement of minimal reflective access lead to infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3b2e551d-68a1-4420-88ce-7bc094f17a26
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimalist Internalism](minimalist-internalism-synthesis-c1aff540-8ee4-4dca-921e-260ac4a2c333.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where each justified belief requires reflective access to another justified belief. Intuitively, this would lead to an infinite regress of justifications.**, **There is no plausible stopping point in the chain of justification if each belief requires further reflective access.**, **Infinite regress in justification problems suggests that justification must have a non-reflective element.**
