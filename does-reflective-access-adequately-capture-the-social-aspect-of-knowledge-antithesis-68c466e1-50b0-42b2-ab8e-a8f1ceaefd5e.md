# Does reflective access adequately capture the social aspect of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68c466e1-50b0-42b2-ab8e-a8f1ceaefd5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimalist Internalism](minimalist-internalism-synthesis-c1aff540-8ee4-4dca-921e-260ac4a2c333.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge is often socially acquired and dependent on others’ expertise.**, **Consider a situation where knowledge results from trust in expert testimony rather than personal reflection. Intuitively, reflective access might undermine the role of social processes.**, **The requirement for minimal reflective access may inadequately capture how social structures contribute to acquiring knowledge.**
