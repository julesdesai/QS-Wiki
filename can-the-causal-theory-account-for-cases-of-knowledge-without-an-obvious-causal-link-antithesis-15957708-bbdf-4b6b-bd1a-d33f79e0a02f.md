# Can the causal theory account for cases of knowledge without an obvious causal link?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15957708-bbdf-4b6b-bd1a-d33f79e0a02f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dc61c008-27ed-4921-863e-badc9b961db6.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all knowledge must have a clear causal link, as illustrated in cases of mathematical knowledge where no direct causal connection to physical events is evident.**, **In scenarios where someone derives knowledge through logical deduction, we would intuitively consider that they have knowledge despite lacking a causal connection to the external world.**
