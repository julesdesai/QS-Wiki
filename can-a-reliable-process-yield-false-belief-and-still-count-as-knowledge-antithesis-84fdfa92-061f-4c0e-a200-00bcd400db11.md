# Can a reliable process yield false belief and still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84fdfa92-061f-4c0e-a200-00bcd400db11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliability](virtue-epistemology-with-reliability-synthesis-6944a8d9-8cde-45e2-a167-ffb34caa5f33.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a reliable process that under normal conditions yields true beliefs, but due to unusual environmental factors, it produces a false belief. Intuitively, one might still consider beliefs from such processes as knowledge, challenging the necessity of truth for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that every single instance of a reliable process must produce truth for it to be classified as knowledge.**
