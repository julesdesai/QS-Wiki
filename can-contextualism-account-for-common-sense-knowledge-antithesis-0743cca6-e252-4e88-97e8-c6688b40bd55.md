# Can contextualism account for common-sense knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0743cca6-e252-4e88-97e8-c6688b40bd55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge Summary](contextualist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-c38eaf7b-9feb-49d3-8375-2d19895c61fa.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that common-sense knowledge should not require complex contextual analysis. This principle together with a context-dependent framework could make common-sense knowledge impractically complex.**, **Consider a scenario where an individual claims to know the sun will rise tomorrow, a common-sense belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that applying various contexts to such a belief complicates what is straightforward knowledge.**
