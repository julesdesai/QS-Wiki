# Does belief alone satisfy conditions for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 966786a2-4422-46cb-a9f2-f4cd35834dc9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Pragmatic Grounds](foundationalism-with-pragmatic-grounds-synthesis-35d5e861-b788-4e52-a02b-4488aba3b8d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that mere belief is insufficient for knowledge without additional criteria such as reliability. This principle together with believing a proposition as a condition for knowledge entails cases where individuals might lack knowledge despite belief.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an individual believes a proposition while being systematically misled. Intuitively, we might say that this individual lacks knowledge despite their belief.**
