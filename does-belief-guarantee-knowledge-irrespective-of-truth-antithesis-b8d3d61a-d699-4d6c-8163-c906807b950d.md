# Does belief guarantee knowledge irrespective of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8d3d61a-d699-4d6c-8163-c906807b950d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-9835edc1-dd3b-4e9d-81fa-8a065de25b95.md)

## Content

**A person can hold countless beliefs, many of which may only coincidentally align with the truth. Intuitively, belief alone without considering the truth conditions of those beliefs does not suffice for knowledge.**, **Just because a person believes a proposition does not mean it has any claim to truth, yet belief is posited as sufficient for knowledge.**
