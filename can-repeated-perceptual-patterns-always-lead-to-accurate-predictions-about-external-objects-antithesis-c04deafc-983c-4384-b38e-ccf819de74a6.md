# Can repeated perceptual patterns always lead to accurate predictions about external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c04deafc-983c-4384-b38e-ccf819de74a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person observes a ball bouncing and predicts it will continue to bounce in a consistent pattern, but suddenly, the ball defies this pattern due to an unnoticed factor like wind. Intuitively, the person’s habitual expectation of the pattern fails to predict the ball's behavior accurately.**, **It is implausible that all external objects are predictable based on repeated perceptual patterns if external or unseen factors can lead to unexpected results.**
