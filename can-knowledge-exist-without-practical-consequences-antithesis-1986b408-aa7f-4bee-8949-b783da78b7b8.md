# Can knowledge exist without practical consequences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1986b408-aa7f-4bee-8949-b783da78b7b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable Pragmatism](knowledge-as-reliable-pragmatism-synthesis-601e2ec8-4358-4137-9d1e-243ee7e2906f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge is a mental state independent of its consequences. This principle together with evidence from cases of theoretical pursuits, like a mathematician proving abstract theorems for intellectual satisfaction, suggests knowledge does not need to have practical consequences or usefulness.**
