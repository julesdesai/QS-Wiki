# Does the argument from predictive coding suggest perception is not direct access to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b60b0b9c-1253-4f1d-bf82-63b0664a1d54
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perception involves direct, unmediated access to external objects. In predictive coding, perception involves top-down predictions based on prior knowledge, suggesting mediation.**, **The involvement of predictive mechanisms implies that perception might not be as direct as previously thought.**
