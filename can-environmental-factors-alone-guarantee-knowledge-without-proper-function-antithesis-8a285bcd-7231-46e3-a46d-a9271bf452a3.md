# Can environmental factors alone guarantee knowledge without proper function?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a285bcd-7231-46e3-a46d-a9271bf452a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where environmental factors are conducive to truth, but an individual's cognitive faculties are systematically unreliable. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that the individual does not possess knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a conducive environment without properly functioning faculties does not guarantee knowledge. This principle together with the possibility of unreliable faculties entails that environmental conduciveness is insufficient for knowledge.**
