# Does believing a proposition necessarily lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 448fad56-bfc5-4a65-a90d-0b0421128b35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition based on unfounded assumptions or wishful thinking.**, **It is implausible that merely believing a proposition, without proper justification, constitutes knowledge.**, **If mere belief is not enough for knowledge, then the assertion that a person knows a proposition if they believe it is problematic.**
