# Virtue Epistemology Theory of Knowledge

**Parent:** [Is sensitivity adequate for distinguishing between actual knowledge and accidental truth?](is-sensitivity-adequate-for-distinguishing-between-actual-knowledge-and-accidental-truth-antithesis-868aa292-859a-4557-b332-5688950b8f83.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person's belief is the result of the exercise of epistemic virtues like intelligence and carefulness**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief arises from epistemic virtues**
