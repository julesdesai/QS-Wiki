# Must internal defeats be evident for belief to lack knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1acf8e1f-b4a6-41b3-994d-42c39fb0778d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a juror is convinced of a defendant's guilt based on rigorous evidence, all personally verified. Suppose later evidence emerges suggesting an internal oversight was made. Intuitively, the belief remains knowledge if not immediately evident.**, **A plausible principle is that justified true belief, even with silent internal defeats unknown, remains knowledge if the belief formation was thorough and reliable. Hidden defeats without evident impact should not negate knowledge.**
