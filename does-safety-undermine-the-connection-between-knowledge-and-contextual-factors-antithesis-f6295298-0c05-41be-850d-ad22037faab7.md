# Does safety undermine the connection between knowledge and contextual factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6295298-0c05-41be-850d-ad22037faab7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person believes they are in a safe neighborhood based on extensive, accurate crime statistics over time. However, in nearby possible worlds, random crimes drastically alter these statistics. Intuitively, they still know about their neighborhood's safety.**, **Knowledge influenced by contextual and empirical factors suggests that a strict adherence to safety may overlook critical aspects of how knowledge functions in real-world contexts.**
