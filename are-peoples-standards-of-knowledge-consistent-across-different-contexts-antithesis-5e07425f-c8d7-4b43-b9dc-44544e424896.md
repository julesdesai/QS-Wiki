# Are people's standards of knowledge consistent across different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e07425f-c8d7-4b43-b9dc-44544e424896
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who confidently asserts a fact in a casual setting but hesitates to make the same assertion in a high-stakes environment, such as a court. Intuitively, the stakes affect their assertion but not necessarily their actual knowledge.**, **The varying assertiveness based on context suggests that the pragmatic standards of asserting knowledge differ from the cognitive standards of having knowledge.**
