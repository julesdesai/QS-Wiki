# How can variations in perceptual conditions affect knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cfce98af-f401-48b5-b3b3-b53a279c8647
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Experience Consistency](phenomenal-experience-consistency-synthesis-dff6895e-5f7e-47e4-9fd7-5b1463da077a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person observes a red apple under different lighting conditions, and it appears orange. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person's knowledge of the apple's color is inconsistent or that lighting does not affect perceptual knowledge. If we say the knowledge is inconsistent, then knowledge based on perception is unstable. If we say lighting does not affect knowledge, then we ignore the influence of conditions on perception.**
