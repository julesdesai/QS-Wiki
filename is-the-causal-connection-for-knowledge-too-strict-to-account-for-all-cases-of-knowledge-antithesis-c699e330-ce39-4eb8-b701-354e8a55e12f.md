# Is the causal connection for knowledge too strict to account for all cases of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c699e330-ce39-4eb8-b701-354e8a55e12f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P forms a true belief about the weather tomorrow based on a reliable weather report. Intuitively, we would judge that P knows the weather report is true.**, **In this scenario, the causal theory would deny that P knows because the belief about tomorrow's weather is based on the report's reliability rather than the actual fact of tomorrow's weather, which has not occurred yet.**
