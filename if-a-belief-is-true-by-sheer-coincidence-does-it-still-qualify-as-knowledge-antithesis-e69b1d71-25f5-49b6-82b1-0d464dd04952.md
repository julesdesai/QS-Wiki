# If a belief is true by sheer coincidence, does it still qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e69b1d71-25f5-49b6-82b1-0d464dd04952
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**A person might believe they will win the lottery and, by sheer coincidence, their ticket wins. Intuitively, we do not attribute knowledge to the person even though the belief is true.**, **The process of forming a belief about winning the lottery based on no evidence is unreliable, as it does not generally produce true beliefs.**
