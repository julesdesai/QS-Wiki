# Can intellectual virtues guarantee the truth of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0958d105-63f3-403b-9d9c-c074aafb0239
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-eb390c86-5ce7-412f-bf22-00e631bfa2b2.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues are qualities like open-mindedness and intellectual courage that guide individuals in forming true beliefs. It is implausible to assert that these virtues alone ensure the truth of beliefs.**, **A situation where a person uses all appropriate intellectual virtues yet still arrives at a false belief challenges the sufficiency of intellectual virtues for truth.**, **Consider a detective who rigorously and virtuously examines all evidence available using intellectual virtues but is misled by a forgery, resulting in a false belief. Despite the exercise of intellectual virtues, the belief is false and thereby challenging the idea that virtue alone guarantees truth in beliefs.**
