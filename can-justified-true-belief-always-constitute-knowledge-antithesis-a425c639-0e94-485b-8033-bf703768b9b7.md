# Can justified true belief always constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a425c639-0e94-485b-8033-bf703768b9b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Context-Sensitive Invariant Endorsement](context-sensitive-invariant-endorsement-synthesis-de3f576d-97e3-4b7e-b7cd-f089d4d156aa.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a student, Ari, forms a belief that the next coin flip will land heads based on prior observation of much more heads than tails. The coin indeed lands heads, and Ari holds a justified true belief. Intuitively, we either have to say that Ari knows the outcome or that he doesn't, but calling it knowledge seems problematic because the justification wasn't linked to the truth.**
