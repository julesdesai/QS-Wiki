# Can external factors influence the safety of a belief without affecting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec324890-6de4-4694-94c1-c41f30c190ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an external factor, such as an earthquake, could unpredictably alter the outcome of an originally safe belief, but the believer remains unaware of such potential events. Intuitively, we would judge that the unexpected external factor should not affect the believer's knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that unforeseen external events should not undermine a belief's status as knowledge unless perceived or likely. This principle, together with the unpredictability of external factors, entails that safety might be irrelevant to knowledge.**
