# Is truth sufficient for knowledge if belief is absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96e68998-1daf-422f-9660-401c71a7b569
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-9835edc1-dd3b-4e9d-81fa-8a065de25b95.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that truth is a necessary, but not sufficient, condition for knowledge. This principle entails that additional conditions beyond truth must be present for knowledge.**, **A situation where a proposition is true, but no one believes it, suggests that truth without belief does not constitute knowledge.**
