# Can phenomenalism explain the phenomenon of synesthesia?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84cfa02b-5341-4c88-9835-afc07b84ee9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person experiences synesthesia, wherein they hear colors or taste shapes. Intuitively, we either have to say that these experiences align with other sensory modalities or that they do not. If we say they align, then this contradicts normal cross-modal perception coherence. If we say they do not align, then it challenges the explanation of consistent phenomenal experiences.**, **A synesthete's cross-modal perceptual experiences, such as tasting numbers, are not accounted for by consistent phenomenal experiences aligning across senses.**
