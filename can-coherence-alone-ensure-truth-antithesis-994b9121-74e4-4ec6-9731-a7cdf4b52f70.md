# Can coherence alone ensure truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 994b9121-74e4-4ec6-9731-a7cdf4b52f70
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes they are being followed by aliens and constructs an elaborate and internally consistent belief system around this idea. In such a scenario, the belief system is coherent, yet intuitively we would judge that the belief is likely false.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence does not necessarily indicate truth, as coherence can exist in imaginative or fictional contexts such as novels or theories about non-existent entities. This principle together with the belief that coherence alone ensures truth entails a contradiction.**
