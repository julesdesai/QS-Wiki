# Does the presence of intellectual virtue guarantee truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a81f2e6-8f5e-4fa4-af5e-d9f52eac1b0f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliability](virtue-epistemology-with-reliability-synthesis-6944a8d9-8cde-45e2-a167-ffb34caa5f33.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief through an intellectual virtue but the belief turns out to be false. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the presence of intellectual virtue does not necessarily guarantee truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that possessing intellectual virtue should lead to true beliefs. This principle, together with scenarios where this does not hold, suggests doubts about the sufficiency of virtue for truth.**
