# Do contextual cues vary too much to provide consistent justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7147783c-fce4-42a4-b5a7-c545888bfdd2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**Suppose two knowers have contradictory beliefs based on their distinct contexts and both are justified locally.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification should not vary in such a way that opposing beliefs are equally justified, as it would lead to the conclusion that contradictory beliefs are both true when both cannot be.**
