# Can certain bizarre or improbable scenarios undermine the link between intellectual virtue and knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce8895ab-46c8-4f7d-a176-1c7910949004
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-6b17452f-daf9-4e36-a5b5-c7d2a1d34c57.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person predicts a lottery number through a complex sequence of cognitive processes perceived as virtuous. Despite the correct prediction, intuitively, either the person knows the outcome or does not. If we say they know because of intellectual virtue, it seems implausible they actually know. If we say they do not know, then intellectual virtue alone is not sufficient for knowledge.**
