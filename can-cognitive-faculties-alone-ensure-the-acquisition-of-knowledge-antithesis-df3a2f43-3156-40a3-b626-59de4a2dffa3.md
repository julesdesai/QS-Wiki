# Can cognitive faculties alone ensure the acquisition of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df3a2f43-3156-40a3-b626-59de4a2dffa3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-eb390c86-5ce7-412f-bf22-00e631bfa2b2.md)

## Content

**Cognitive faculties are mental abilities like perception and memory, which aid belief formation. It is implausible that cognitive faculties by themselves can always guarantee acquiring knowledge.**, **Imagine a case where a person forms a belief based on a memory that is factually incorrect, even if they exercised normally reliable cognitive faculties, the belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **In a scenario where someone perceives a mirage but perceives it as a real oasis, it demonstrates how cognitive faculties, working normally, can lead to false beliefs, suggesting they aren't sufficient alone for knowledge.**
