# Is a direct causal link necessary for all knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2b9bac36-154d-4967-852b-c971d337e322
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge about mathematical truths does not require a causal connection. This principle suggests that beliefs about mathematical truths, such as 2+2=4, can be known without a direct causal link to the external world.**, **Consider a scenario where a mathematician believes that the Riemann Hypothesis is true. Intuitively, it seems possible for the mathematician to know the hypothesis without having any direct causal interaction with an external fact.**
