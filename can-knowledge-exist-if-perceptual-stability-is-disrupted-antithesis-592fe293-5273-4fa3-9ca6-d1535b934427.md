# Can knowledge exist if perceptual stability is disrupted?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 592fe293-5273-4fa3-9ca6-d1535b934427
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Experience Consistency](phenomenal-experience-consistency-synthesis-dff6895e-5f7e-47e4-9fd7-5b1463da077a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person perceives a straight stick in water as bent due to refraction. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their knowledge of the stick's shape is disrupted.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires consistent perceptual experiences. This principle together with the scenario suggests that perceptual consistency is not always maintained.**
