# Does intellectual character sufficiently account for known false data?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9baec620-4fe1-4d2a-ae47-a6e181ed9756
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a scientist correctly believes a theory due to their intellectual virtue, but the data supporting their theory is later found to be false.**, **Supposedly reliable cognitive character traits must lead to truth, yet false data challenges this reliability.**, **Revised beliefs based on false data remind us that knowledge requires robustness beyond immediate epistemic virtue.**, **Attributing knowledge despite false data suggests intellectual virtue is insufficient alone for true belief.**
