# Can direct perception ensure knowledge despite indistinguishability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f5a5f811-d1aa-478e-991b-88e74fe20b08
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-72a215c5-4d26-4e6e-86ab-383aabdf6290.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where one has a real perception of an object and another where there is an indistinguishable hallucination without the object being present. In such scenarios, intuitively, claiming certain knowledge of the object in the case of perception becomes problematic if the hallucination prevents distinguishing the two experiences.**
