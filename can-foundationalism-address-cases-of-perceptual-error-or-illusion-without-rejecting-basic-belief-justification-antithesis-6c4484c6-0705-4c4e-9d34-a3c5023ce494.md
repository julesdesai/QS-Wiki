# Can foundationalism address cases of perceptual error or illusion without rejecting basic belief justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c4484c6-0705-4c4e-9d34-a3c5023ce494
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone looks at a mirage and believes "There is water," and this belief feels basic. Intuitively, this seems to initially halt further question of justification.**, **In situations of perceptual error, the basic belief seems incorrectly justified because further evidence shows otherwise. Hence, perceptual errors challenge the reliability of basic belief justification.**
