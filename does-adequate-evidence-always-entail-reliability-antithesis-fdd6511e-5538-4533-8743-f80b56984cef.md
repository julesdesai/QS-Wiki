# Does adequate evidence always entail reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fdd6511e-5538-4533-8743-f80b56984cef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief appears justified by historical evidence that was later revealed as falsified or manipulated. Intuitively, the reliability of the basis for belief is compromised, raising doubt about possessing true knowledge.**, **It is implausible to equate adequate evidence with reliability if the evidence itself is eventually proven unreliable.**
