# Does moral knowledge challenge stable criteria?

**Node ID:** e73767bc-334e-4e3c-b281-b47fce4fe3ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**A case in which a culture believes it is morally justified in practicing a specific tradition, while another culture believes it is wrong, raises the issue whether knowledge of moral facts has stable criteria across different contexts.**, **If moral knowledge can vary between cultures or time periods, it seems implausible that the standards for knowing moral propositions remain constant regardless of context.**
