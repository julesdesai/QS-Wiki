# Does dependence on the perceiving mind introduce subjectivity into all knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02a9625d-4c5d-43d8-b153-50d38ada9e5f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism Summary](transcendental-idealism-summary-synthesis-0df97e88-4364-4bc5-aaf8-288ab627f355.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if knowledge is dependent on individual minds, then subjective variance should follow for all knowledge claims**, **Empirical cases where independent verification leads to objective consensus challenge the idea that all knowledge is inherently subjective**, **Consider a scenario where two people perceive the same event and, through objective methods, agree on the knowledge claim, suggesting that not all knowledge is subjective**
