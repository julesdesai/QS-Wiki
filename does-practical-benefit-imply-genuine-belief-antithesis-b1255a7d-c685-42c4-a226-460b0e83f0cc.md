# Does practical benefit imply genuine belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1255a7d-c685-42c4-a226-460b0e83f0cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Instrumentalism with Externalist Justification](instrumentalism-with-externalist-justification-synthesis-69903d30-4d37-4781-884e-3e9ef74484e3.md)

## Content

**Practical benefits from a belief may result even if the believer does not genuinely hold the belief, such as acting 'as if' it were true in certain scenarios**, **A genuine belief implies a psychological state where the person accepts the proposition as true**, **For a belief to constitute knowledge, it should be genuinely held by the individual rather than merely playing a role for practical outcomes**
