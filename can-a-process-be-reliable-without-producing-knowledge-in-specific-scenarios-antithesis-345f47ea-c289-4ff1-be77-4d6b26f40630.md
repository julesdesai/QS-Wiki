# Can a process be reliable without producing knowledge in specific scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 345f47ea-c289-4ff1-be77-4d6b26f40630
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Nozick's Condition](process-reliabilism-with-nozicks-condition-synthesis-5d350bc0-f5e6-4b33-b9c5-feeee7d84d21.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person is in a simulated environment with all indicators of truth naturalized. Intuitively, we either have to say that this simulated process is unreliable or conclude the person doesn’t know. If we say it is unreliable, our definition of reliability is challenged. If we conclude the person doesn’t know, reliability in a controlled environment is insufficient for knowledge.**
