# Does phenomenalism account for the external world's persistence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6e1474b4-745d-4639-928c-73a2fca7eec9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-eea6cb23-77a9-47a4-87da-736833e1953e.md)

## Content

**A person leaves a room, and another person enters and perceives the room the same way as the first person did, suggesting the room persists independently of individual perception.**, **Intuitively, the persistence of the room suggests an external reality that is independent of any one person’s sensory experiences.**, **If the room did not have an independent reality, the consistency in perception by different individuals would be inexplicable.**
