# Does the Safety Theory fail in lottery scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 779f989b-7472-44e0-adc5-bf31c9ae3cc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Consider the lottery scenario where a person believes their ticket will not win based on the high number of total tickets, and it does not win. Intuitively, despite this true belief, it does not seem like knowledge.**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe it and their belief is safe. The individual's belief in the lottery scenario is formed safely—most nearby worlds would also reflect non-winning—yet it lacks the element of knowledge.**
