# Can perceptual experiences form the basis of knowledge without prior beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 597775b4-a8f7-48de-872f-48d61bd82840
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Memory-Integrated Phenomenalism](memory-integrated-phenomenalism-synthesis-20b6ad46-c9b1-41b8-87a0-7619b03b72c5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a perceptual experience is the very first cognitive event for a person who has no prior beliefs. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks any knowledge despite having a perceptual experience.**, **It is implausible that perceptual experiences alone, without any prior cognitive framework or belief, can constitute knowledge.**
