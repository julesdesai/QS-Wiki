**Parent:** [Is reliability always enough for the justification of beliefs?](is-reliability-always-enough-for-the-justification-of-beliefs-antithesis-6d3acdae-f477-4a1c-91b6-c6d1ac372307.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **A belief formed by a reliable cognitive process has a high probability of resulting in a true belief**, **The reliability of a cognitive process is evaluated in relation to the context in which it operates**
