# Can there be knowledge without an infinite regress of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c7cd6778-8270-4f72-b1b3-0cc1cfbe4eed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Epistemology](infinitist-epistemology-synthesis-6da6e9b6-0ddd-4346-9ef8-d27afad7386e.md)

## Content

**In many cases of knowledge, people do not seem to consciously engage in an infinite regress of reasons. Intuitively, this suggests that knowledge might not require infinite chains of reasons.**, **The practical constraints of human reasoning make it unlikely that infinite regress is a necessary condition for knowing something.**
