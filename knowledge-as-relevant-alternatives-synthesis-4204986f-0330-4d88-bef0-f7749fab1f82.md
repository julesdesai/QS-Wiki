# Knowledge as Relevant Alternatives

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can non-virtuous motives result in knowledge?](can-non-virtuous-motives-result-in-knowledge-antithesis-ba9de8a6-dd52-4418-800a-639023705519.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person can rule out relevant alternatives to the proposition being true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and they can rule out relevant alternatives to the proposition being true**
