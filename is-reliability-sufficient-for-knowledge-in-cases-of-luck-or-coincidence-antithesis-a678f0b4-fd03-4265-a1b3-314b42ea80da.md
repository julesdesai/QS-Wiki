# Is reliability sufficient for knowledge in cases of luck or coincidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a678f0b4-fd03-4265-a1b3-314b42ea80da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable True Belief](knowledge-as-reliable-true-belief-synthesis-73884ab1-e6ad-49fe-9997-a4e027a9805c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Sarah, witnesses a lottery draw and forms the belief that she has won due to a printer error showing her numbers as the winning ones. Intuitively, we would judge that Sarah does not know she won, even though her belief could be true by luck.**, **A belief formed through an accidental or coincidental process lacks the robust connection to truth required for knowledge, even if the process is usually reliable.**
