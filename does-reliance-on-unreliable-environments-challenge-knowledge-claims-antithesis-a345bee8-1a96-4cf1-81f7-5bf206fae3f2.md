# Does reliance on unreliable environments challenge knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a345bee8-1a96-4cf1-81f7-5bf206fae3f2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief is formed by a usually reliable process but in an environment that the believer does not know is unreliable, thus leading to a false belief. Intuitively, this does not seem like a case of knowledge.**, **Reliable processes are context-dependent, and it is implausible to claim knowledge without considering environmental factors that might undermine reliability.**
