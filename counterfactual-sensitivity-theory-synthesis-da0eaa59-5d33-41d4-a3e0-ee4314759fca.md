## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, their belief must be sensitive to whether the proposition would be true in close possible worlds**, **A person knows a proposition if their belief would not hold in a nearby world where the proposition is false**, **A person can know a necessary truth if their methods for belief formation align with understanding necessary truths**, **Counterfactual sensitivity accounts for both contingent and necessary truths**
