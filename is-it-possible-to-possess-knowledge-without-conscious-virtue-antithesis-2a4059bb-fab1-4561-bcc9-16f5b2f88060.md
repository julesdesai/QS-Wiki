# Is it possible to possess knowledge without conscious virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2a4059bb-fab1-4561-bcc9-16f5b2f88060
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a child's belief is both true and justified, though they lack the cognitive capacity to embody intellectual virtues. Intuitively, despite the lack of virtue, we recognize the child as possessing knowledge.**, **It is implausible that the absence of intellectual virtues in young or cognitively limited individuals always excludes them from having knowledge. This implausibility challenges virtue as a strict necessity.**
