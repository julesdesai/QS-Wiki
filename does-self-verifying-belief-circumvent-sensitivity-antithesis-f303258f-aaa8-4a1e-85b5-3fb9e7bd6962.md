# Does self-verifying belief circumvent sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f303258f-aaa8-4a1e-85b5-3fb9e7bd6962
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-f1663159-56f4-4f6f-b53a-d60dc9dbfcb7.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person believes a proposition ("I am thinking") that verifies itself by the very act of believing. Intuitively, we might judge that such self-verifying beliefs do constitute knowledge regardless of tracking external truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that internally-certified truths secure knowledge without sensitivity to alternate conditions contrary to the deeply personal aspects, such as existence queries.**
