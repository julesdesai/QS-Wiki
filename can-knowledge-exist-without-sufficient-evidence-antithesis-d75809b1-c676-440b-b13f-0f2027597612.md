# Can knowledge exist without sufficient evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d75809b1-c676-440b-b13f-0f2027597612
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-dea02623-290c-44c3-896b-2f967081eb1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person acquires knowledge through immediate perception or intuition, seemingly without relying on previously gathered evidence. Intuitively, we might still judge that the person knows the proposition.**, **A direct appeal to a theoretical intuition suggests that there can be instances of immediate knowledge that do not rely on an accumulation of prior evidence, such as perceptual or intuitive knowledge.**
