# Does the variability of sensory conditions challenge Naïve Realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ccffa489-b378-4d26-8e22-c7e50cf22dde
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-960c5650-a545-46a7-9f9f-61ad44274751.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that changes in lighting, distance, or angle should not alter the identity of external objects. This principle together with perception's variability under different conditions entails that perception does not reliably present objects as they are.**, **In a case where a white surface appears yellow under a dim light, intuitive judgment challenges the view that perception straightforwardly presents the external object's true color.**
