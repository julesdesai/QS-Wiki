# Does the concept of knowledge as justified true belief conflict with practical knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3b33e52-fcab-48aa-882c-5055e1ad5013
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-f73c0505-1eaa-4768-8143-11a2f8c9ca92.md)

## Content

**Many everyday instances where we claim to know do not involve explicit justification.**, **Consider a scenario where someone claims to know it will rain due to looking at the sky, without justification through belief or truth evaluations.**, **In such scenarios, knowledge seems attributed without a conscious appeal to justified true belief.**, **Practical instances of knowing often bypass the rigorous demands of justified true belief.**
