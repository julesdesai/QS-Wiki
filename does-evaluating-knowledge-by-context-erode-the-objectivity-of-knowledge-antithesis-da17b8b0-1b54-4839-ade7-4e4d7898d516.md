# Does evaluating knowledge by context erode the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da17b8b0-1b54-4839-ade7-4e4d7898d516
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Virtue Contextualism](externalist-virtue-contextualism-synthesis-69e2f760-46ad-465f-a31e-67e150de071d.md)

## Content

**If knowledge is evaluated by contextual standards, then knowledge claims depend on the variable standards of specific situations.**, **Variable standards could imply that knowledge claims are less objective, as they change with different contexts.**, **Knowledge that is less objective might be doubted for its reliability or universal applicability.**
