# Can Direct Realism account for the existence of non-perceived objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4cfba8db-7209-4f59-b7d5-3c0a817eee89
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a tree falls in a forest with no one around to hear it. Intuitively, we would judge that the tree exists independently of perception, yet its sound is not directly perceived.**, **A plausible principle is that direct realism should account for all objects existing whether or not they are perceived. This principle together with the reality of non-perceived events implies reliance on something beyond direct perception, such as inference.**
