# Can unreliable lucky guesses count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 088cb6a3-84ed-42a5-a686-323e8db0ac1f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-b7a77c41-f711-4239-97c6-124b43e5d847.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief that accurately predicts a future event by using a method that usually fails. Intuitively, such a belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **If a reliable cognitive process is one that generally produces true beliefs, then using a method that usually fails does not qualify as a reliable process.**
