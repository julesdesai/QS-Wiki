# Can a reliable process produce a false belief and still count as knowledge when it accidently turns true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe26a4f8-da14-4a71-b75b-0a343d921aff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on a normally reliable eye-witness testimony that is wrong due to a rare failure. Intuitively, we would say that at the moment of forming the belief, the person does not know the truth.**, **A cognitive process is reliable if it generally leads to true beliefs. But if the belief was initially false and became true due to an external change, it challenges the validation of knowledge by reliable processes.**
