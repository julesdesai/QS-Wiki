# Does an endless chain of justifications provide any more security than a finite one?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5bc66bd4-758e-46b3-a001-3e48533983e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Suppose each segment of an infinite chain is itself subject to error; extending the chain does not inherently reduce error.**, **The complexity added by potentially infinitely many justifications seems to complicate rather than resolve the issue of belief security.**, **If nothing in an infinite chain guarantees error-free reasons, it seems no different from a finite chain in terms of epistemic security.**
