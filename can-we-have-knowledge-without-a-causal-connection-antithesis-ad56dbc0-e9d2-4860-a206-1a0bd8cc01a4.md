# Can we have knowledge without a causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad56dbc0-e9d2-4860-a206-1a0bd8cc01a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cee9090d-2e34-47f2-b4ee-a59054387264.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes that the stock market will rise based on a reliable economic forecast. It turns out the market rises due to unforeseen political events instead. Intuitively, we would judge that the person could still be said to know the market would rise despite the lack of a direct causal connection to their belief.**, **A person who reads a reliable historical document and forms a belief about past events may have knowledge even though their belief is not causally connected to those past events.**
