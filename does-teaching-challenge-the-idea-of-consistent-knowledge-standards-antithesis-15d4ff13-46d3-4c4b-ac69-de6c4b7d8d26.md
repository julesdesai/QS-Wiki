# Does teaching challenge the idea of consistent knowledge standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15d4ff13-46d3-4c4b-ac69-de6c4b7d8d26
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Epistemic Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-epistemic-standards-synthesis-0f962817-db98-42be-9977-f6a5e2bd4d1d.md)

## Content

**In a university setting, a professor adjusts how they assess students' knowledge depending on the course level (undergraduate vs. graduate). Intuitively, the epistemic standards used are adapted rather than fixed.**, **Effective pedagogical practices suggest contextual adjustment according to students' level of understanding.**, **If teaching uniformly applies invariant standards, this adjustment would be incorrect, yet it seems necessary.**
