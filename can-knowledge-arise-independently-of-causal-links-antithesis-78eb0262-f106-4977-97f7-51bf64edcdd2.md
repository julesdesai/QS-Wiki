# Can knowledge arise independently of causal links?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 78eb0262-f106-4977-97f7-51bf64edcdd2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person correctly infers a fact about the world based on statistical data without observing the event itself. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that they possess knowledge even though there is no direct causal link to the fact.**, **It is implausible that a lack of direct causal connection nullifies the possibility of holding true knowledge, as shown by knowledge arising from inferred data.**
