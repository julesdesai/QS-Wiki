# Can true beliefs held for practical reasons lack epistemic value?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0473056f-3fa2-47a5-92e8-11b0cfc05fa7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a detective forms a belief about a suspect's guilt based purely on the suspect's confession under duress, which leads to a successful conviction, suggesting true belief obtained pragmatically might lack epistemic value.**, **Consider a case where a student uses belief in completed study plans successfully passing exams without understanding the material, highlighting that pragmatic reasons might not confer genuine epistemic merit.**
