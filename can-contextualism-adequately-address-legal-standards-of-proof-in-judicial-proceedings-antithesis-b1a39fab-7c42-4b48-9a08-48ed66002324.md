# Can contextualism adequately address legal standards of proof in judicial proceedings?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1a39fab-7c42-4b48-9a08-48ed66002324
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c8c66eb7-99be-427a-b717-b1ecb74e1290.md)

## Content

**In legal contexts, the standard of proof must be clear and stable to ensure fairness and justice.**, **Contextualism implies that the standard for what is "known" could vary with different legal discussions or contexts.**, **Such variability could lead to inconsistencies in legal judgments and undermine the legal system's reliability.**
