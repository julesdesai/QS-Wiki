# Is contextualism undermined by linguistic intuitions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18655c8a-265f-4598-851e-2a81b9f74978
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-bf708b38-e467-425c-8744-5d490abcb982.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that linguistic intuitions underlie our understanding of knowledge claims.**, **Consider two speakers in the same context, one affirming "I know," and the other using "I don't know," without any apparent miscommunication.**, **Such linguistic intuitions suggest that the truth conditions of knowledge cannot meaningfully vary with context if they lead to clear inconsistencies.**
