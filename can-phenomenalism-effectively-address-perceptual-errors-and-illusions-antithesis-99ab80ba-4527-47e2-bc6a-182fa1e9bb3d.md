# Can phenomenalism effectively address perceptual errors and illusions?

## Content

**Suppose someone sees a straight stick half-submerged in water and perceives it as bent. Intuitively, the sensory experience diverges from objective reality, suggesting a problem in reducing objects to sensory experiences alone**, **It is implausible that sensory experiences are sufficient for understanding or correcting perceptual errors without an objective standard**
