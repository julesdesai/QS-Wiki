# Do perceptual errors reveal a flaw in knowledge mediated through perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1789eb5-d14a-4c5e-ad6f-27f9fce21f65
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-3b2a41b4-2333-4928-b6a1-340c94708483.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person mistakenly perceives a mirage as water in the desert, challenging the concept of mediated perception as an accurate conduit for knowledge about reality**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception can be systematically deceptive, it calls into question the reliability of perception in aligning knowledge with reality**, **It is implausible to assume that all perceptual experiences accurately represent reality, as demonstrated by optical illusions and hallucinations**
