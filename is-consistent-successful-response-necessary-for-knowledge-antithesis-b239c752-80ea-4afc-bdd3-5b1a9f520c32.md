# Is consistent successful response necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b239c752-80ea-4afc-bdd3-5b1a9f520c32
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Direct Realism](pragmatic-direct-realism-synthesis-4173acef-5348-4f34-a95b-b6ba164e9e84.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where person P perceives a situation directly and responds correctly intermittently due to contingent factors. Intuitively, it seems plausible that P knows important aspects of the situation despite lack of consistent successful responses.**, **A person may correctly understand a complex system even if they occasionally fail to predict its behavior, questioning if consistent outcomes are required.**
