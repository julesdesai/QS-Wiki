# Can knowledge exist without causing successful action?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9df7090d-78bb-45ce-adbe-520f07566df2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1312135d-680e-4dd5-9377-79ee82721c0c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual knows a particular fact, such as the capital of France is Paris. Intuitively, whether or not this knowledge leads them to successfully book a trip to Paris does not affect the status of their knowledge.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that practical action is not required for knowledge or redefine what knowledge entails. If we say practical action is not required for knowledge, then knowledge should be viewed separately from the ability to act. If we redefine what knowledge entails, then knowledge could lose its integrity as an intellectual achievement.**
