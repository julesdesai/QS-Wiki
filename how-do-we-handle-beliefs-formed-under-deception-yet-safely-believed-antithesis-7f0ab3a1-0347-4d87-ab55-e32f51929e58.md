# How do we handle beliefs formed under deception yet safely believed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7f0ab3a1-0347-4d87-ab55-e32f51929e58
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which a person is thoroughly deceived by convincing fake evidence but forms a belief that coincidentally turns out to be true and safe in similar scenarios. Intuitively, we could argue that this should not count as knowledge.**, **Even safety in similar situations cannot override the deceptive basis on which the belief was formed, which intuitively undermines its status as knowledge.**
