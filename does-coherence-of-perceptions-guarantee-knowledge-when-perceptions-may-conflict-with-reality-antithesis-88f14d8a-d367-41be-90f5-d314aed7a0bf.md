# Does coherence of perceptions guarantee knowledge when perceptions may conflict with reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 88f14d8a-d367-41be-90f5-d314aed7a0bf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Unconscious Processes](phenomenalism-with-unconscious-processes-synthesis-53c9374d-552a-49b3-b121-1513a04b6dab.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person’s perceptions form a coherent set but are systematically incorrect about the external world is a case in which the person cannot be said to know facts about the external world.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just internal coherence but also a correspondence with external reality.**
