# Can knowledge be contingent on elements outside one’s epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 04e435a0-ab8c-4de8-9334-5557699f3013
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective has compelling evidence leading to a conclusion that is false due to unforeseen tampering by a third party. Intuitively, the detective appears justified in their belief.**, **Elements beyond one's epistemic field, which affect the truth of a belief, seem to undermine the rigidity of the truth requirement in defining knowledge.**
