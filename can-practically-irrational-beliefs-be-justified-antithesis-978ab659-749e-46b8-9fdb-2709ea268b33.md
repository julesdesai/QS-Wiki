# Can practically irrational beliefs be justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 978ab659-749e-46b8-9fdb-2709ea268b33
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-c54e9ff1-39e1-4224-b3be-b0979177f411.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person holds a true belief formed by a typically unreliable process, like randomly guessing correctly, driven by irrational impulses. Despite truth, intuitively, their belief seems unjustified due to its irrational formation.**, **Even if a process occasionally yields true beliefs, the absence of rationality undermines the justification that reliability seeks to afford.**
