# How can we account for knowledge in cases where the same reliable process yields different results based on context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f65dfc7-fb91-4043-ab2f-a7ef9ff75026
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-c84f45b2-357a-4b29-be66-6a09ffb029e2.md)

## Content

**Consider two people using the same weather prediction app. The app is reliable but gives differing results depending on location. Intuitively, one's belief about tomorrow's weather might be knowledge, whereas the other's might not**, **It is implausible that a reliable process that produces diverse results in varying contexts equally constitutes knowledge for each result**
