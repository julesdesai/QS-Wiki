# Do practical considerations allow irrelevant factors to justify belief?

**Node ID:** 6f284d8e-39b3-4fc0-98e4-234c3ae93e9d

**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a fortunate event will occur because they wore a lucky charm, and the event does occur, the reliance on such irrelevant factors challenges the integrity of pragmatic justification.**, **Imagine a patient who believes a medication will work because their doctor smiled when prescribing it, and this justification coincides with a positive outcome, suggesting practical justification can incorporate extraneous elements.**
