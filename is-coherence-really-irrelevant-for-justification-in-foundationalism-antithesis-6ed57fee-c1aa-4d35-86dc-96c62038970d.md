# Is coherence really irrelevant for justification in foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ed57fee-c1aa-4d35-86dc-96c62038970d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the coherence of a set of beliefs contributes to the justification of individual beliefs. This principle, together with the observation that inconsistent beliefs are typically seen as unjustified, entails that coherence cannot be entirely irrelevant for justification.**, **Consider a scenario in which an individual's basic belief conflicts with all other beliefs they hold. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the lack of coherence undermines the justification of the basic belief.**
