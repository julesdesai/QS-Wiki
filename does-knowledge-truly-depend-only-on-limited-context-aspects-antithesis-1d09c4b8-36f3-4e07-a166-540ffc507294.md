# Does knowledge truly depend only on limited context aspects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d09c4b8-36f3-4e07-a166-540ffc507294
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-550a9ee8-c8d3-41b5-bd1e-c079e9d505d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is in a noisy environment and cannot hear clearly. In this scenario, intuition may suggest that the person's context affects their ability to know a proposition.**, **It is plausible to think that knowledge may depend on a broad range of contextual factors beyond limited aspects. This is because contextual elements such as social interactions and environmental conditions might influence knowing a proposition.**
