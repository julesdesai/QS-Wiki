# Can empirical evidence ever conflict with perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7f02418f-32f4-40ca-82cf-912ff964f79c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person perceives a mirage in the desert, seeing water where there is none. In such a scenario, empirical tests reveal the absence of water. Intuitively, we would judge that empirical evidence conflicts with direct perception, questioning the reliability of perception in empirical verification.**, **There is a plausible principle that empirical evidence is sometimes required to correct misperceptions. This principle together with the scenario entails that perception is not always in harmony with empirical evidence.**
