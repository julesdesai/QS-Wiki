# Is it possible for a belief to be reliable yet still influenced by luck and count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e331e64e-3182-458a-a1b8-99b7187b56c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Luck View](no-luck-view-synthesis-bccfbb90-e542-414d-b962-c3799a2855c9.md)

## Content

**A belief that is reliable on the whole can still be partially influenced by luck and considered knowledge.**, **Imagine an experienced athlete making a last-minute winning shot affected partly by unpredictable wind conditions despite their skill. The athlete's success is mostly due to skill, but partially luck, and intuitively, we would consider the athlete's action knowledgeable.**, **Even if part of a belief's truth emerges from luck, as long as the reliable method is the primary influence, the belief can still be classified as knowledge.**
