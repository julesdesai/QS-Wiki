# How can conflicting standards for knowledge coexist without inconsistency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3786125-3a28-47dc-80f8-089d844387fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Pluralism](epistemic-pluralism-synthesis-81e4116f-f2b8-419c-97e5-1ca5c482c2fd.md)

## Content

**If two epistemic systems have different standards for knowledge, they may lead to contradictory conclusions about what is known.**, **A person may be considered to know a proposition in one epistemic system but not in another due to differing standards.**, **Inconsistent knowledge claims across systems could undermine the notion of a single, coherent epistemic status of any given belief.**
