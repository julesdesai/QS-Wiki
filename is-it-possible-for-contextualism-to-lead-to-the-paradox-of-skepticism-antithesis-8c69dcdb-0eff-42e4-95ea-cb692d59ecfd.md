# Is it possible for contextualism to lead to the paradox of skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c69dcdb-0eff-42e4-95ea-cb692d59ecfd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person is in a rigorous epistemic context that leads them to adopt skeptical attitudes about even common knowledge claims. Intuitively, this could lead to a paradox where nothing can be known if skepticism is always applicable.**, **Skepticism can be understood as a position that nothing can be definitely known. If contextual standards can trigger skepticism, it challenges the possibility of achieving any secure knowledge under varying contexts.**
