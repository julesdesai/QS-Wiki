# How does the theory cope with the variability of sensory modalities across species?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab86e649-68e3-4df7-8b19-b3389347dcce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Strong Correspondence](direct-realism-with-strong-correspondence-synthesis-28884d49-4ced-45da-83a0-528139a02f57.md)

## Content

**Different species perceive the world in varied ways, such as bats using echolocation which humans cannot directly perceive.**, **These variations suggest that perceptual experiences differ greatly even when there is a shared external reality.**, **If perception varies across species, then perceptual experiences do not uniformly correspond to external objects for a universal reality.**
