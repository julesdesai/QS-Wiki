# Can epistemic virtues adequately define knowledge if they lack objectivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57988286-7478-467c-b51c-592af5cd571a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people exemplify the same intellectual virtues but arrive at different conclusions due to personal biases. Intuitively, we would judge that subjective factors can undermine the uniformity of knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge solely defined by intellectual virtues remains objective and consistent across different individuals, considering the variance introduced by personal biases or contexts.**
