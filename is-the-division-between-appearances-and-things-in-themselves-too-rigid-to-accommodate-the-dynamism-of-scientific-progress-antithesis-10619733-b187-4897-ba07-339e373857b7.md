# Is the division between appearances and things-in-themselves too rigid to accommodate the dynamism of scientific progress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 10619733-b187-4897-ba07-339e373857b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**Consider a plausible idea that scientific theories progressively refine our understanding of reality, inching closer to truths about the very nature of things. If scientific progress implies closer contact with reality itself, the strict division between appearances and things-in-themselves seems unnecessarily rigid and undermined by empirical success.**
