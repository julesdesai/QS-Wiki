# Do contextual cues adequately secure the truth requirement for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 655c5246-3fb1-4fee-b80d-b2b587396d4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**Consider a belief justified by contextual cues that imply conditions known to be unreliable or incomplete.**, **The principle that truth is necessary for knowledge would require justification independent of potentially faulty contextual cues, otherwise, any belief could be questioned if the contextual basis is misinformed.**
