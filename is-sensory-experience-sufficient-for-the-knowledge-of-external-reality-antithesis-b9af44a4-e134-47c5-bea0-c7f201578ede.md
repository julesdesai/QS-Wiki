# Is sensory experience sufficient for the knowledge of external reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b9af44a4-e134-47c5-bea0-c7f201578ede
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-6256cdee-fde6-4d81-b9e8-d1897bfcfd04.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person consistently experiences a vivid world in a virtual reality simulation. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that this person lacks actual knowledge of the external world despite having coherent sensory experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a connection to an external reality independent of the sensory experiences themselves. This principle together with the lack of such a connection in virtual simulations suggests that sensory experiences are insufficient for knowledge of the external world.**
