# Is it plausible that all knowledge must be analyzed by scientific standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02642493-58bf-40a8-84e3-9411f7bf148b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology Summary](naturalized-epistemology-summary-synthesis-ad83a102-e1e6-4596-90b6-8b3b07d31230.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all forms of knowledge are subject to scientific analysis because certain domains, such as ethics, aesthetics, or metaphysics, involve subjective judgments or insights that elude empirical testing**, **Suppose a philosopher has profound insights about moral truths that are widely regarded as knowledge but do not align with scientific methodologies. Intuitively, these insights still count as knowledge**
