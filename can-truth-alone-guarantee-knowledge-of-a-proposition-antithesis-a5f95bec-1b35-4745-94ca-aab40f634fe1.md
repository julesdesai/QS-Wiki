# Can truth alone guarantee knowledge of a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5f95bec-1b35-4745-94ca-aab40f634fe1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Contextual Stops](infinitism-with-contextual-stops-synthesis-554d1150-ce74-450b-ad43-18770e414e5e.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true, but mere truth doesn't account for the justification or belief aspects of knowledge.**, **Instances might occur where a proposition is accidentally true, but without justification or belief, it shouldn't count as knowledge.**, **Truth by luck or coincidence seems inadequate for asserting genuine knowledge of a proposition.**
