# Does belief grounded in false contextual assumptions qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c04f645e-c134-438b-935c-d6b43def5d04
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a meteorologist predicts rain based on a number of reliable indicators, but unbeknownst to her, all the instruments are faulty, yet the true reason for the coming rain is a rare weather event she did not consider.**, **In such a case, it seems intuitive that the meteorologist does not actually have knowledge about the weather forecast, even though her prediction is correct.**, **The assumption that knowledge cannot rest upon any accidental truths challenges our understanding of how justification validates belief as knowledge.**
