# Does Constructivism adequately account for scientific knowledge generated through experimentation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17fef2cd-89c3-4226-a7ad-ca7412407db9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism Summary](constructivism-summary-synthesis-38220637-7d58-4a33-978f-6de4b5b7771f.md)

## Content

**Scientific experimentation often produces knowledge that transcends individual perceptions or cognitive biases, such as the reproducibility of experimental results across varied contexts and observers. This challenges the idea that knowledge is constructed solely from individual perceptual experiences and cognitive processes.**
