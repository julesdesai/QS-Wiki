# Do the implications of consensus provide a counterexample to the tracking theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 54364ad6-df5a-4017-9d45-673063e974cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Suppose an overwhelming consensus among experts in a field supports a proposition that holds true across every examined case. If the proposition were false, peers would identify flaws and withdraw collective belief. Intuitively, the initial belief still reflects knowledge, pointing toward expert validation within knowledge matrices despite failing counterfactual tracking provisos.**, **Consensus-based knowledge arises through cumulative affirmative validation and not strictly through tracking conditionalities within possible world permutations.**
