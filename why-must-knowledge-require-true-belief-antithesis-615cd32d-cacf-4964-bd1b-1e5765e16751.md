# Why must knowledge require true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 615cd32d-cacf-4964-bd1b-1e5765e16751
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition for entirely virtuous reasons but the proposition is false. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is knowledgeable or not. If we say the belief is knowledgeable, then truth seems unnecessary. If we say the belief is not knowledgeable, then virtuous reasoning without truth is insufficient.**, **A person can exercise intellectual virtues while being mistaken due to false information, demonstrating that virtues do not guarantee truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that virtuous inquiry should ideally lead to truth, but it does not always do so in practice.**
