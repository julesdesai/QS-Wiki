# Is finite accessibility a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e525984b-f6a3-4f2b-acac-f04d30b2e31f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-4b9cf48f-532c-40a3-a960-718d00ef564e.md)

## Content

**A case in which an individual has reasons that form an infinite chain yet cannot access those reasons seems to intuitively lack knowledge.**, **Suppose our understanding of knowledge entails practical accessibility of reasons. It is implausible that knowledge can exist without such accessibility.**, **Consider a scenario where reasons backing a belief are infinite and dispersed, making them inaccessible. Intuitively, knowledge without accessible reasons seems incoherent.**
