# How does the problem of epistemic regress impact foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0722b26-4e42-479c-83f9-5c52c09f47c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Foundationalism](evidentialist-foundationalism-synthesis-26c84477-fc91-4190-8a0e-24d90c56393c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where each belief in a chain requires support from a previous belief, resulting in an infinite regress. In such a scenario, intuitively, foundationalism seeks to avoid infinite regress by positing foundational beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that an infinite regress of justification would either terminate in arbitrary belief or require some non-inferential knowledge. Without foundational beliefs that are self-justified or non-inferentially justified, the justificatory chain becomes incoherent.**, **A belief justified by another belief without a foundational ending point results in no secure knowledge, challenging foundations that do not provide self-justifying beliefs at some level.**
