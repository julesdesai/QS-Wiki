# Does luck play an inappropriate role in granting knowledge in tracking theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c3b550e-130c-4616-ae11-c9a680f6c381
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-34b45a3c-9960-48d7-bb61-82e72864432f.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person believes a true proposition, and their belief coincidentally tracks the truth due to factors the believer is unaware of or cannot control. The involvement of luck makes it intuitive to deny knowledge.**, **This intuition persists because the tractability of luck suggests an element of epistemic luck rather than genuine epistemic achievement, clashing with tracking theory's conditions.**
