# What if justification isn't accessible to the knower?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 805163a0-be68-49df-b246-95d938c179dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who correctly identifies the correct solution to a problem but cannot verbally justify or explain their reasoning. It seems counterintuitive to claim they truly possess knowledge**, **There is an intuitive expectation that knowledge should involve some ability to explain or justify one's belief, at least to oneself**, **When justification is entirely inaccessible, it challenges our traditional account of knowledge, which includes an element of accessible reasoning or explanation**
