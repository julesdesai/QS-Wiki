# How do we account for cognitive faculties in non-biological entities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4cf44fc1-0577-47fb-a9da-8dcd4cf1a073
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism Summary](proper-functionalism-summary-synthesis-5b044e3f-97d9-4386-9c2f-bd333212a4a8.md)

## Content

**Consider an artificially intelligent system that systematically produces true beliefs, but without any biological design or naturally occurring cognitive faculties. Intuitively, there is a question of whether this system possesses knowledge.**, **A theoretical view stipulating knowledge requires naturally occurring cognitive faculties may not sufficiently address cases of artificial systems that meet reliability and true belief criteria.**
