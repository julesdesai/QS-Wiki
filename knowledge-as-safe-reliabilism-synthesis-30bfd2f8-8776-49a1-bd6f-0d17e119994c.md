# Knowledge as Safe Reliabilism

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 30bfd2f8-8776-49a1-bd6f-0d17e119994c
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can a true belief formed by a reliable process still fall short of knowledge?](can-a-true-belief-formed-by-a-reliable-process-still-fall-short-of-knowledge-antithesis-f8a2e63d-ab0d-4bba-a74c-077056179bc9.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that in nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, the proposition is not false**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process, and in nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, the proposition is not false**
