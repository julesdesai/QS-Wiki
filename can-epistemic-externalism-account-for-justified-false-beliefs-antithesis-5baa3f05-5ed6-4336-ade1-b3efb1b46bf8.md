# Can epistemic externalism account for justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5baa3f05-5ed6-4336-ade1-b3efb1b46bf8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which external factors justify a belief, but the proposition is false. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is justified but not knowledge or revise what external justification means.**, **A person can have justified beliefs without the proposition being true, challenging the notion that external factors alone suffice for knowledge.**, **It is unlikely that a theory allowing for justified false beliefs accurately captures the nature of knowledge.**
