# Can justified true belief be independently sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8ec6820-bac4-4602-99e2-4e4fbb8612ec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-ddc2fd27-140a-48fe-b697-40a3c8c11bfc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that practical benefits alone constitute justification because one can have practically beneficial beliefs that are not true.**, **A case in which someone believes a true proposition and achieves practical success, yet lacks other epistemic virtues like evidence, suggests that practical success is insufficient for knowledge.**
