# Can constructivism explain knowledge that cannot be actively constructed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7d4b7c8-327f-4aae-89df-b44207eb6a18
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivism](constructivism-synthesis-c5cc0372-1259-4a9f-927f-8a96c3c904c0.md)

## Content

**There are cases where people have knowledge of abstract truths, such as mathematical propositions, without sensory interaction with the world.**, **In these cases, individuals have knowledge without the process of integrating sensory experiences.**, **This challenges the notion that all knowledge must result from active construction through world interaction.**
