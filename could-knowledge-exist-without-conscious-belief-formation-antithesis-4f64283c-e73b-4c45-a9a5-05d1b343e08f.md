# Could knowledge exist without conscious belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f64283c-e73b-4c45-a9a5-05d1b343e08f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Proper Functionalism](knowledge-as-proper-functionalism-synthesis-95adcceb-ce7f-44ef-aea6-bebac418915b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual forms a true belief subconsciously, such as when recognizing a friend's face among a crowd without consciously reasoning. Intuitively, this might still be considered knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that subconscious true belief formation doesn't necessarily require conscious belief to be knowledge.**
