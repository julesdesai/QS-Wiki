# Can too much credit undermine human agency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0b526b1a-cee2-4d06-9f18-5f5b5ec862d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Virtue and Credit Epistemology](hybrid-virtue-and-credit-epistemology-synthesis-7563744d-4993-49c2-9fb5-fa60254b49ad.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where the belief is entirely credited to external factors such as environment or teaching, leaving no room for the believer's intellectual virtue. Intuitively, we might be reluctant to attribute knowledge to the believer if their agency is minimized.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some degree of human agency or contribution, not solely external credit. This principle, alongside the view of credit epistemology, suggests that excessive attribution of credit can conflict with our sense of agency.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be properly credited if this attribution removes the agency or significant contribution by the individual possessing the belief.**
