# Is reliability and truth-tracking insufficient when considering the influence of chance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d051a18-8bd6-49bb-9adf-ce1f1769f192
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Nozick's Condition](process-reliabilism-with-nozicks-condition-synthesis-5d350bc0-f5e6-4b33-b9c5-feeee7d84d21.md)

## Content

**A belief is formed reliably and tracks truth, yet there is a high possibility of chance affecting the outcome randomly. Intuitively, such a scenario suggests skepticism about whether the belief qualifies as knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge is retained under conditions where chance undermines the reliability of beliefs.**
