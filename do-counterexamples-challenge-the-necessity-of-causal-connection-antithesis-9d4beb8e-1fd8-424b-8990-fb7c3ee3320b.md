# Do counterexamples challenge the necessity of causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d4beb8e-1fd8-424b-8990-fb7c3ee3320b
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-thesis-0c6eaf7b-9d38-4e55-b770-1b9de7b33981.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true proposition based on a false premise that happens to lead to the truth. Intuitively, either we say that this is not knowledge, or accept that causal connection is not necessary.**, **If we say this is not knowledge, then we concede exceptions to the causal theory. If we say this is knowledge, then the necessity of causation is challenged.**
