# Are accidental truths justified if produced by reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac9a8f94-2ffa-42d9-b878-cd41c0389308
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Coherentism](reliabilist-coherentism-synthesis-d6c1c642-66ac-40b0-82ca-187cf7250017.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a belief happens to be true due to coincidence, even though it is produced by a reliable process. Intuitively, a belief that is true by accident seems unjustified, despite the process’s general reliability.**, **It is implausible that accidental truth confers justification, even if the cognitive process is usually reliable.**
