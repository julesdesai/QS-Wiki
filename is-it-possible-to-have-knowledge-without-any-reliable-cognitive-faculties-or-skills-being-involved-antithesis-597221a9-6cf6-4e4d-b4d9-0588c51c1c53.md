# Is it possible to have knowledge without any reliable cognitive faculties or skills being involved?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 597221a9-6cf6-4e4d-b4d9-0588c51c1c53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9deda8b7-9bee-4f9f-90d0-2d2b46259731.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone acquires a true belief through sheer luck or coincidence, such as guessing the outcome of a coin toss without any prior experience or skill with predictions. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not truly know the outcome despite the belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must involve some reliable method or skill in forming true beliefs. This principle together with the idea that sheer luck or random guessing can sometimes result in true beliefs entails that such cases challenge the notion of intellectual virtues as necessary for knowledge.**
