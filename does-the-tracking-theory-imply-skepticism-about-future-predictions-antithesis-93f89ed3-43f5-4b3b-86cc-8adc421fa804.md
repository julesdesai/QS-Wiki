# Does the tracking theory imply skepticism about future predictions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93f89ed3-43f5-4b3b-86cc-8adc421fa804
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dda35d00-5b10-4342-b443-f197350806e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scientist predicting an eclipse based on robust astronomical calculations but the prediction remains conditional on various assumptions. Intuitively, we would still attribute knowledge to the scientist about the eclipse despite possible conditions changing.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of the future can be held based on present facts and well-substantiated predictions. This principle suggests skepticism about future predictions undercuts legitimate scientific knowledge.**
