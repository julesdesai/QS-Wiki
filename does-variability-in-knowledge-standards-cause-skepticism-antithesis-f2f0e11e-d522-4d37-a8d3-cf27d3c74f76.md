# Does variability in knowledge standards cause skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2f0e11e-d522-4d37-a8d3-cf27d3c74f76
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Defeat Condition](contextualism-with-defeat-condition-synthesis-644e1e82-6b43-472b-8e60-41eaf47756ee.md)

## Content

**If the standards for knowing a proposition vary significantly with different contexts, it might lead to global skepticism where knowledge is never securely attainable.**, **Intuitively, variability in standards across contexts could imply that knowledge is less robust than traditionally conceived, leading to doubt about any claim to know.**
