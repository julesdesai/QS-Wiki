# Can knowledge be attained through non-virtuous means?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce43c11a-a82a-4d43-973a-ef98a546fd2f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Virtuous True Belief](knowledge-as-virtuous-true-belief-synthesis-0cc7c39c-3c22-4b7d-8311-bdde6f1e8698.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person uses unreliable methods to come to a true belief, perhaps by guessing or flipping a coin. Intuitively, we may be inclined to say that they know the proposition if the belief turns out to be true for reasons unrelated to their method.**, **A belief formed through unreliable or non-virtuous methods can still result in knowledge if the proposition believed is true independently of those methods.**
