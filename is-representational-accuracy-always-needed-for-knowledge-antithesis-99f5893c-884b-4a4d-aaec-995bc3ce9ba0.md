# Is representational accuracy always needed for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99f5893c-884b-4a4d-aaec-995bc3ce9ba0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses a simplified map that is not entirely accurate to navigate a city but still successfully reaches their destination. Intuitively, we would say the person has knowledge of the city’s layout sufficient for navigation.**, **A plausible principle is that successful action on knowledge does not always require complete accuracy in representational models. This principle challenges the idea that knowledge must align perfectly with a representational model.**
