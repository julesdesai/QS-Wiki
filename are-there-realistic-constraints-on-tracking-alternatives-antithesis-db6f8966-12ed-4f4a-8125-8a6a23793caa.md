# Are there realistic constraints on tracking alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db6f8966-12ed-4f4a-8125-8a6a23793caa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1a215329-c53d-4dbe-9509-6b517d1bb8ad.md)

## Content

**Imagine an agent believes a proposition based solely on a trusted authority, who is correct in actual circumstances but incorrect in many possible alternatives. Intuitively, this should count as knowledge given the agent has good justification in actual circumstances.**, **It is implausible that agents should be aware of or track all possible alternatives to qualify their belief as knowledge.**
