# Knowledge as Social Contextual Justification

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is justified within a social context but retains an individual evaluative component**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the belief is justified in a way that is socially contextual while ensuring evaluative rigor, the proposition is true, and the belief is held**
