# Is knowledge possible without the truth of a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 64ac067f-ebfa-4359-92a1-8b6f44311f84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**A person claims to know a proposition, yet the proposition is false. Intuitively, one cannot know a false proposition because knowledge, by definition, requires truth.**, **A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true. If the proposition is not true, then knowledge of it is not possible.**
