# Can propositions known without aiding decision-making still be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 667eb37d-0093-4210-bf7c-4b8dfbf10797
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who knows detailed historical facts about a long-dead civilization which have no impact on any modern decision-making. Intuitively, this knowledge is still valid even though it is not tied to practical reasoning.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge must have a practical decision-making aspect. Some knowledge appears to solely contribute to understanding without leading to practical decisions.**
