# Must beliefs always be supported by evidence to be justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1c20282d-10e4-493e-bf4e-308f1206f8e6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-dea02623-290c-44c3-896b-2f967081eb1b.md)

## Content

**There are cases where beliefs are justified through coherentism, a theory that allows for justification through a network of beliefs rather than direct evidence, suggesting that beliefs can be justified even in the absence of specific evidence.**, **It is implausible to argue that foundational beliefs must always be underpinned by evidence, as such beliefs may serve as starting points for knowledge without requiring prior justification.**
