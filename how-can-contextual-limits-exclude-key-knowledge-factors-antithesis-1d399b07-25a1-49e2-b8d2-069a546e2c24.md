# How can contextual limits exclude key knowledge factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d399b07-25a1-49e2-b8d2-069a546e2c24
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-550a9ee8-c8d3-41b5-bd1e-c079e9d505d4.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowing a proposition depends solely on limited contextual aspects, as many factors, such as cognitive capacity or expertise, are not limited but are essential to knowledge.**, **A case where two individuals receive the same information in different mental states challenges knowledge dependency on limited contextual aspects because their cognitive contexts vary.**
