# Is practical utility the only metric for evaluating belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 88adf696-1d32-4764-b9b4-714135f1c323
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-08f9dcda-197c-4176-877f-438b2d186f9d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that beliefs should be evaluated based on both truth and evidence, not just practicality. This principle together with a belief evaluated solely on usefulness entails that we ignore whether the belief is supported by factual evidence.**, **Consider a belief in astrology that provides comfort and practical benefits to an individual. Intuitively, we would judge that this belief lacks genuine knowledge as it is not supported by scientific evidence despite its practical benefits.**
