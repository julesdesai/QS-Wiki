# Can knowledge be merely practical or useful?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cca64b14-9e9c-4bc1-907f-b9240dcb92cb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief about how to perform a certain task, and the belief is true but has no practical application in their current situation. In such a scenario, it is intuitive to think that the belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just practical or useful value, such as an understanding of why the belief is true. This principle implies that true belief with mere practical usefulness is insufficient for knowledge.**
