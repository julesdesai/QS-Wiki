# Can knowledge be reduced to practical success?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 838fc075-5091-49c8-bbed-236cf7925e4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1312135d-680e-4dd5-9377-79ee82721c0c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief about a natural disaster that helps them survive. Intuitively, the belief is valuable for survival, but the reduction of knowledge to mere practical success may overlook other epistemic values such as justification and accuracy.**, **A plausible principle is that epistemic value includes more than just practical success, encompassing aspects like truth-tracking and coherence, which suggest that practical success alone is insufficient for defining knowledge.**
