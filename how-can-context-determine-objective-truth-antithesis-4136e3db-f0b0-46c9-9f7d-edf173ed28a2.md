# How can context determine objective truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4136e3db-f0b0-46c9-9f7d-edf173ed28a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Contextualism](phenomenal-contextualism-synthesis-37be371b-8eaa-49b9-96b8-60f2ca6919b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two different societies have entirely different contextual frameworks for understanding the same natural phenomenon, such as the Earth revolving around the Sun. Intuitively, we either have to say that one society knows the truth while the other does not, or that both societies have knowledge which contradicts the notion of an objective reality.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective truths exist independent of perceptual frameworks. This principle together with differing contextual understandings entails that knowledge tied solely to contextual experiences fails to capture objective reality.**
