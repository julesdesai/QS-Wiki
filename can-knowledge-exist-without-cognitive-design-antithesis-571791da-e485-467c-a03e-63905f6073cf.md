# Can knowledge exist without cognitive design?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 571791da-e485-467c-a03e-63905f6073cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who evolves naturally in a simulated environment where their faculties are not "designed" but rather developed randomly. If they form true beliefs due to their surroundings without a specific design for truth, intuitively we might still consider that they have knowledge.**, **Cognitive design implies that faculties are constructed to produce true beliefs. However, environments that naturally select for effective belief-forming processes—without the notion of 'design'—could still result in knowledge.**, **It is implausible to require that cognitive faculties must be intentionally designed for truth for them to yield knowledge, as evolutionary processes often operate without intentional design yet lead to functional outcomes.**
