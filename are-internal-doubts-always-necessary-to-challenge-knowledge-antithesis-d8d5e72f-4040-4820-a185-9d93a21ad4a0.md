# Are internal doubts always necessary to challenge knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8d5e72f-4040-4820-a185-9d93a21ad4a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Consider an accomplished chef tasting pasta and confidently identifying spices used without hesitation. Intuitively, the chef knows what spices are used. However, suppose the chef experiences momentary internal doubt due to recent article questioning traditional spice identification but sticks to initial belief by expertise.**, **It is implausible that minor internal doubts challenge solid knowledge, especially when initial beliefs are correct and backed by expertise. Doubts based on tentative skepticism are not sufficient to negate knowledge.**
