# How do dreams and hallucinations fit into knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 215cb92f-d9be-4245-a7d0-892104ba8633
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is dreaming or hallucinating vividly and experiences sensations that seem real. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that these experiences are not consistent with reality in the same way sensory experiences of an awake, fully conscious person are.**, **There is a plausible principle that says knowledge based solely on sensory experiences can be deceptive if those experiences are disordered or indistinguishable from non-reality-inducing experiences. This principle suggests that not all knowledge derived from sensory experiences is reliable.**
