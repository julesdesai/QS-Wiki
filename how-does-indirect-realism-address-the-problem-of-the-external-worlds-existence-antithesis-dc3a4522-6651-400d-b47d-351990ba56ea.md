# How does indirect realism address the problem of the external world's existence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc3a4522-6651-400d-b47d-351990ba56ea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Maintained Through Inference](indirect-realism-maintained-through-inference-synthesis-ffd82d7b-be0f-4614-a603-c896b6b1b7df.md)

## Content

**Indirect realism proposes that perceptual experiences as representations do not directly present external objects, merely infer them.**, **The existence of the external world becomes contingent on inferential structures rather than experiential evidence.**, **The dependence on inference may fail to adequately address the ontological question of the existence of an external world beyond perception.**
