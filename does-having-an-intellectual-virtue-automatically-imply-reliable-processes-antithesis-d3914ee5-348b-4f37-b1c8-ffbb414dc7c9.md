# Does having an intellectual virtue automatically imply reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3914ee5-348b-4f37-b1c8-ffbb414dc7c9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where an individual is known for their intellectual virtues yet relies on a process that is generally unreliable. Intuitively, we either have to say that intellectual virtue and reliable processes are distinct or that the presence of one ensures the other. If we say they are distinct, then the presence of intellectual virtue does not ensure knowledge. If we say they are the same, this contradicts observed cases of unreliable processes linked with intellectual virtues.**
