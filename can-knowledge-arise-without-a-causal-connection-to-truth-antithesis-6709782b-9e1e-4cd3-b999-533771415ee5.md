# Can knowledge arise without a causal connection to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6709782b-9e1e-4cd3-b999-533771415ee5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-db8e4632-83fc-4e14-a2b9-f64cf1d5bb50.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition is true because they believe it to be justified, but there is no causal connection to the truth itself.**, **Intuitively, we find it difficult to attribute knowledge to the person, which suggests that a causal connection might be necessary for knowledge.**
