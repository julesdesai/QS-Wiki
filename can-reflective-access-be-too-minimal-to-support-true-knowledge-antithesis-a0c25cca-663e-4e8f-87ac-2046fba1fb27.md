# Can reflective access be too minimal to support true knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a0c25cca-663e-4e8f-87ac-2046fba1fb27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimalist Internalism](minimalist-internalism-synthesis-c1aff540-8ee4-4dca-921e-260ac4a2c333.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that reflective access must provide sufficient depth to support a claim to knowledge.**, **Suppose reflective access is so minimal that it cannot adequately engage with evidence or reasons. Intuitively, this might seem inadequate for genuine knowledge claims.**, **Minimal reflective access may not guarantee the depth required for distinguishing true knowledge.**
