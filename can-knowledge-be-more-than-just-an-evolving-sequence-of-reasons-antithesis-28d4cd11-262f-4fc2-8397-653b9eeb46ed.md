# Can knowledge be more than just an evolving sequence of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28d4cd11-262f-4fc2-8397-653b9eeb46ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**There are instances where knowledge derives from immediate perception or intuition, which doesn't involve a complex sequence of reasons.**, **Cases where direct experiences inform beliefs suggest that knowledge can be more immediate than unfolding reasons.**, **Evolving sequences imply a temporal element that some forms of immediate or perceptual knowledge lack, suggesting another dimension of knowledge outside the sequence.**
