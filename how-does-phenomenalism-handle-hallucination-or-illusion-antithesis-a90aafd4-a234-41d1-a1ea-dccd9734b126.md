# How does phenomenalism handle hallucination or illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a90aafd4-a234-41d1-a1ea-dccd9734b126
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**A person under the influence of hallucinogens believes they see a dragon in their living room. Intuitively, we judge that the dragon does not exist as a physical object.**, **If knowledge of objects is based on patterns of sensory experiences, there is no distinguishing basis for judging the hallucinated dragon as not real.**
