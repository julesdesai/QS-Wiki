# Can belief coherence alone determine the status of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d50b46d-0064-4b92-ad4b-d304d332e907
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Coherence Theory](phenomenal-coherence-theory-synthesis-37d80231-0661-49bf-9bec-8b3f779084e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual holds a coherent belief system about perceptual experiences, but this belief system is entirely based on false information or manipulation by an external source. Intuitively, we would judge that the individual does not possess genuine knowledge despite the coherence of their beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle stating that genuine knowledge requires not only internal coherence among beliefs but also correspondence with external reality. This principle entails that knowledge cannot be solely determined by the coherence of belief systems without consideration of their truth.**
