# Can the inability to explain one's belief defeat the claim to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** badd020a-8ac5-4273-ba7d-3ef9e99634fa

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true, safe belief about a proposition but cannot articulate any reasons for this belief. Intuitively, the inability to explain undermines their claim to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a person must provide reasons or explanations for their beliefs to qualify as knowledge.**
