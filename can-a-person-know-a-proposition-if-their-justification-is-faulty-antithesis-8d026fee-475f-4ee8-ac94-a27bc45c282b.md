# Can a person know a proposition if their justification is faulty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8d026fee-475f-4ee8-ac94-a27bc45c282b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a true proposition but their justification is based on faulty reasoning. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition, despite the faulty justification, or that they do not.**, **If we say they know, this challenges the requirement that justification must be sound to support knowledge.**, **If we say they do not know, this suggests that the mere truth and belief are not enough without proper justification.**
