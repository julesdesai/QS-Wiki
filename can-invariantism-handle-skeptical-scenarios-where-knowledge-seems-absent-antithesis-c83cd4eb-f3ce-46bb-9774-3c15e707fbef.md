# Can invariantism handle skeptical scenarios where knowledge seems absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c83cd4eb-f3ce-46bb-9774-3c15e707fbef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is deceived by an evil demon into believing something false. In this scenario, intuitively, the person does not know the proposition they believe.**, **The claim that knowledge does not depend on the context of the knower suggests that even in skeptical scenarios, if a proposition is true, a person knows it.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should require justification that excludes deception.**
