# Does the necessity of justification conflict with intuitive cases of knowledge without explicit justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 924b6d35-cc5f-4518-8eb2-9d9aa4976c8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person knows the location of a well-used pen without contemplating justification, simply due to habitual actions or memory traces. Intuitively, it seems this person has knowledge without propositional justification.**, **This scenario suggests that justification as classically conceived may not be necessary for knowledge.**
