# Is justification necessary at all for knowledge if it need not be accessible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 535e59bd-c2ce-4f52-9eee-2f2ac855e23b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9deda8b7-9bee-4f9f-90d0-2d2b46259731.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual has a true belief formed entirely by chance without any accessible justification, such as someone randomly picking lottery numbers that turn out correct. Intuitively, we either have to say that this individual knows due to luck or does not know because of the lack of justification. If we say they know due to luck, then we discard justification entirely as necessary for knowledge. If we say they do not know, we indirectly uphold justification as a necessary element, thereby implying its significance contradicts its lack of necessity in the theoretical view.**
