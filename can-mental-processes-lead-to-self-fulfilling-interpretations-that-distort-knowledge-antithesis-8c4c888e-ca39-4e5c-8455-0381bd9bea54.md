# Can mental processes lead to self-fulfilling interpretations that distort knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c4c888e-ca39-4e5c-8455-0381bd9bea54
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's mental processes lead them to anticipate certain outcomes, which in turn influences their perception in a way that fulfills their expectations. Intuitively, we would judge that such knowledge is distorted by self-fulfilling interpretations.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be distorted by self-fulfilling perceptions. This principle, together with the influence of mental processes on perception, suggests that such perception can distort knowledge.**
