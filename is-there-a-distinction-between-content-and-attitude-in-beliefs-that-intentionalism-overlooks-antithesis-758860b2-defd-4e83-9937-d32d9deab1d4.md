# Is there a distinction between content and attitude in beliefs that intentionalism overlooks?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that distinguishes content (the proposition itself) from the attitude one has towards that content (e.g., belief, doubt). If perception has intentional content that represents external reality, it may conflate these elements of cognition, overlooking the complexity of mental states.**, **Consider a case in which a person perceives an ambiguous sound and is unsure whether it represents a car or plane. Intuitively, we have to say that perception's content is distinct from the attitude towards that content.**
