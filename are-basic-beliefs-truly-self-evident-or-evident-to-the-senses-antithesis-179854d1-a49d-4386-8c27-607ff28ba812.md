# Are basic beliefs truly self-evident or evident to the senses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 179854d1-a49d-4386-8c27-607ff28ba812
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**A belief is self-evident if it requires no external validation or reasoning to be justified.**, **A belief is evident to the senses if it is directly experienced through sensory perception, such as seeing a tree to believe it is there.**, **Doubt arises when some beliefs considered self-evident, such as mathematical intuitions, are not universally self-evident.**, **Sensory perceptions can be misleading, as illusions or hallucinations can produce false beliefs that seem evident to the senses.**
