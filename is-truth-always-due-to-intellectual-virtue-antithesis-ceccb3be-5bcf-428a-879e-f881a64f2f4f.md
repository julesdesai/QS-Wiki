# Is truth always due to intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ceccb3be-5bcf-428a-879e-f881a64f2f4f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-40b3d691-a5bb-479c-ada3-1931521a86e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a true proposition because of a coincidental fact unrelated to any intellectual virtue, such as their environment reinforcing a specific worldview. Intuitively, this truth seems external to the person's intellectual virtue.**, **Intellectual virtue implies a control or mastery over how truths are believed. When truths are believed due to external coincidences, this mastery is absent.**, **If the truth of a belief can arise independently of intellectual virtue, this could challenge the idea that knowledge is due to intellectual virtue.**
