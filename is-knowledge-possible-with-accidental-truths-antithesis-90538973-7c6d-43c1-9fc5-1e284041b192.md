# Is knowledge possible with accidental truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 90538973-7c6d-43c1-9fc5-1e284041b192
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief that it will rain tomorrow based on reading tea leaves, and it happens to rain. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows it will rain just because they got it right by chance.**, **A reliable process is generally understood as one that yields true beliefs more often than not, and reading tea leaves does not qualify as such a process.**
