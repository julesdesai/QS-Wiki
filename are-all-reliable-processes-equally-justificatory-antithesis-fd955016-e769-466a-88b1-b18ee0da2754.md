# Are all reliable processes equally justificatory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd955016-e769-466a-88b1-b18ee0da2754
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-c54e9ff1-39e1-4224-b3be-b0979177f411.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider two cognitive processes: one being an expert's informed intuition in a familiar setting and the other a superstition that unpredictably yields true beliefs. Both may be equally reliable in terms of hitting true beliefs. Intuitively, the first process seems more justificatory than the latter.**, **Different reliable processes possess varying degrees of credibility, yet reliabilism treats all as equally justificatory without considering qualitative differences.**
