# Do optical illusions undermine direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7de7f0b4-c3d9-4c81-8790-3de99e461263
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-960c5650-a545-46a7-9f9f-61ad44274751.md)

## Content

**Consider the Müller-Lyer illusion in which lines of equal length appear different due to arrow-like endings. Intuitively, the expert knowledge of the lines' actual equality challenges the directness of perceptual experience.**, **Optical illusions provide cases that serve as counterexamples to the principle that perception straightforwardly presents external object properties.**
