# Does the theory account for shared knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e8390ad6-f57b-4697-a81d-10ee21abb330
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is often distributed across members of a community, not necessarily attributable to individual virtues.**, **Consider a research team where the group as a whole has a true belief due to the interaction of various different skills and insights, none of which individually leads to knowledge.**, **If knowledge involves the successful application of cognitive virtues, it may struggle to account for cases of knowledge that emerge from collaborative efforts without clear individual virtuous contribution.**
