# Is an intellectual virtue always tied to truth-conducive qualities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e93e309-36b9-439b-a0fb-73024018765f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology With Virtuous Belief Formation](virtue-epistemology-with-virtuous-belief-formation-synthesis-53b228ae-7464-400d-be01-037da588b5a0.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person consistently forms beliefs influenced by rigid, dogmatic adherence to authority, which happens to align with truth in their societal context.**, **The qualities leading to these true beliefs are not reliably truth-conducive and rely haphazardly on external factors.**, **Intuitive assessment may suggest that these beliefs formed by such qualities lack genuine intellectual virtue and fail to constitute knowledge.**
