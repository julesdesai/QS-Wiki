# Can reality exist independently of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7512653-ce08-49d2-8f88-b6b30622d50c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-ab29b70f-4a42-463b-b303-b38517d8aa22.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a world can exist without the need for any perceivers. This principle together with the idea that perceptual experience is necessary for reality entails that reality would cease to exist if perceivers cease to exist.**, **An uninhabited distant galaxy may exist without being perceived. Intuitively, it seems wrong to assert that such a galaxy doesn't exist because it's unperceived.**
