# Is adequate evidence always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06b332d1-6b2a-48eb-992b-6bd07b7fef6d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person accurately predicts the outcome of a random coin toss multiple times. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that the person knows the outcomes despite lacking adequate evidence.**, **In some lucky cases, individuals might arrive at true beliefs without comprehensive evidence, questioning if evidence is always necessary for knowledge.**
