# Does Reliabilism account for Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 473b69cc-d246-452a-9b76-0166aef63beb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-e69ee86a-1c0a-4d0e-840e-f25c67fd8e9a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it is noon because their clock shows noon, but the clock is broken and it is a coincidence that it is actually noon. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, despite the belief being true and produced by a reliable method of checking the time.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be based on lucky coincidence. This principle together with the scenario suggests that a reliable cognitive process alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
