# Can Evidentialism accommodate perceptual beliefs formed without evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e35ce446-bcac-472a-9ba8-310da57394dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-a76d832e-447a-4168-b9be-6c867a1a7af1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person sees a tree and forms the belief that there is a tree, based solely on perception. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is justified or that it is not. If we say the belief is justified, then perception alone can provide evidence, which challenges the view that additional evidence is needed.**
