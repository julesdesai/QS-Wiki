# Does Direct Realism adequately explain the variability of perception under different conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99b654ee-75e7-4359-a854-77949b876a5b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person perceives the color of a red apple differently under varied lighting conditions, such as daylight and artificial light. Intuitively, we may say that the person's perception is affected by the conditions rather than solely by the apple's properties.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge of the true color should not vary if perception provides direct access to external objects. This principle together with variable color perception entails that knowledge is influenced by more than direct access alone.**
