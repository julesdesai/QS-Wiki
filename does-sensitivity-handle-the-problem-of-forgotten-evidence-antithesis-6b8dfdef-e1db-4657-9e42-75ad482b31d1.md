# Does sensitivity handle the problem of forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b8dfdef-e1db-4657-9e42-75ad482b31d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a scientist knows a specific fact based on their research but later forgets the evidence, yet still believes the fact accurately. Intuitively, we might still attribute knowledge to the scientist despite the absent evidence.**, **This belief is sensitive because if the fact were false, the scientist might not believe it, despite lacking immediate access to the original evidence supporting their belief.**
