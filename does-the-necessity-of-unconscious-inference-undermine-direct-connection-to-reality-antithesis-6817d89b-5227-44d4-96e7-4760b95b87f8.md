# Does the necessity of unconscious inference undermine direct connection to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6817d89b-5227-44d4-96e7-4760b95b87f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with a Role for Unconscious Inference](indirect-realism-with-a-role-for-unconscious-inference-synthesis-78a86651-c747-4945-9c1d-0df1bc77c499.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a direct and unmediated connection to reality. This principle together with the requirement for unconscious inference, which introduces a mediated step, entails that the connection to reality is not direct.**, **Indirectly knowing a proposition through unconscious inference implies that perception does not straightforwardly reflect external reality.**
