# Can self-justifying beliefs actually avoid circularity in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee32aa43-3bcf-40e9-9bb9-543da318b033
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalist Coherentism](foundationalist-coherentism-synthesis-b8a8c906-b541-4af2-8e95-f0f08da1d9c7.md)

## Content

**A circular justification occurs when a belief is justified by a chain of reasoning that ultimately refers back to the initial belief.**, **Self-justifying beliefs might be accused of circular reasoning if their justification includes referring to qualities within themselves as a reason for their own belief.**, **To avoid circularity, self-justifying beliefs must establish unique, incontrovertible grounds for justification inherently present in the beliefs themselves.**
