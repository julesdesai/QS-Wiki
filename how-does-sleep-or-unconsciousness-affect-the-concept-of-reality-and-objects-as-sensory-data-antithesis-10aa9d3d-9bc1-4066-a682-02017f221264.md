# How does sleep or unconsciousness affect the concept of reality and objects as sensory data?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 10aa9d3d-9bc1-4066-a682-02017f221264
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-6256cdee-fde6-4d81-b9e8-d1897bfcfd04.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for something to consist entirely of sensory data, it must be dependent on being perceived. This principle together with the fact that reality persists during sleep or unconsciousness suggests a challenge to the view that objects are solely logical constructions out of sensory experiences.**, **Consider a person asleep in a room. Intuitively, we would judge that the objects in the room continue to exist even when the person is not perceiving them.**
