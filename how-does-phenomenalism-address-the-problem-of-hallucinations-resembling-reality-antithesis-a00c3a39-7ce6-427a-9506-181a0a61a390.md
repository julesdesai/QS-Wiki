# How does phenomenalism address the problem of hallucinations resembling reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a00c3a39-7ce6-427a-9506-181a0a61a390
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates a complex, realistic scene indistinguishable from reality. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the hallucination is not the same as perceiving reality despite both being sensory experiences.**, **It is implausible that hallucinations, merely by virtue of matching sensory experience patterns, could constitute genuine aspects of reality. This challenges the view that reality is just consistent patterns of experiences.**
