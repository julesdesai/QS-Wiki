# Can justified beliefs be coherent without being externally verified as true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0da7947-ec81-4149-b34f-550f88e61d59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Temporal Coherentism](temporal-coherentism-synthesis-f4dfebfd-2f02-4b67-a5dd-1a5ab136482a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a coherent set of propositions about the world, but these propositions are all false due to external misinformation. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, despite having justified beliefs.**, **It is implausible that belief justification solely based on coherence can lead to knowledge in cases where external truths contradict the belief system.**
