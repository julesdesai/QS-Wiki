# Knowledge without Explicit Belief

**Node Type:** synthesis

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is belief always necessary for knowledge?](is-belief-always-necessary-for-knowledge-antithesis-9acf10fd-b00d-4a57-a3a8-e8fa501c2785.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For implicit knowledge, it is sufficient that the individual has a reliable process or method leading to a true proposition**, **Implicit knowledge does not require holding an explicit belief in propositional form**
