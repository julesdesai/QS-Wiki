# How does phenomenalism deal with the problem of error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ded6f3e-b75f-4c2f-bbfc-8fb01695e418
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-eea6cb23-77a9-47a4-87da-736833e1953e.md)

## Content

**Sensory experiences are sometimes misleading, such as when a straight stick looks bent in water.**, **A person who sees the stick in the water and believes it to be bent does not have knowledge, even though it corresponds to their sensory experiences.**, **The occurrence of sensory illusions challenges the idea that knowledge is solely about phenomena as they appear.**
