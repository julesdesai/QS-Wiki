# Can phenomenalism account for mathematical objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 942e3f9a-20f7-4057-9786-dd47aba87d0b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Consider mathematical entities such as numbers or geometric shapes that are not physically perceivable. Intuitively, we recognize their existence independent of sensory experience.**, **If knowledge of objects is based on patterns of sensory experiences, then mathematical objects would seemingly not exist.**
