# Can knowledge exist in the absence of awareness of external factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c94592b-2ac2-451c-bdbd-ddf112d74ab1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for a person to have knowledge, they must be aware of the conditions that justify their belief.**, **Consider a situation where a person has true beliefs formed under reliable conditions but is unaware of these external reliability factors. Intuitively, it seems like the person should not be said to have knowledge if they are unaware.**
