# How does luck play into knowledge attribution?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93e382f5-b9b9-430b-8b20-f88c26cd5526
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Virtue and Credit Epistemology](hybrid-virtue-and-credit-epistemology-synthesis-7563744d-4993-49c2-9fb5-fa60254b49ad.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that if a belief is true due to luck, it cannot constitute knowledge. This principle, combined with the view of knowledge as true belief involving intellectual virtue or credit, suggests that credit or virtue needs to exclude luck.**, **Suppose a person has a true belief that involves an intellectual virtue; however, the truth of the belief was entirely due to lucky circumstances rather than the person's intellectual virtue. Intuitively, this belief does not constitute knowledge because the truth element resulted from luck.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be attributed to a belief whose truth is only a result of luck, even if the belief displays intellectual virtues or credit.**
