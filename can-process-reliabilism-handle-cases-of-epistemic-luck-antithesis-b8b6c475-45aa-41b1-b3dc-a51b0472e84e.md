# Can process reliabilism handle cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8b6c475-45aa-41b1-b3dc-a51b0472e84e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person, Jane, forms a belief that the time is 2 PM by looking at a clock that stopped exactly 24 hours ago, but by sheer luck, it correctly displays the current time. Intuitively, it seems Jane does not know that it is 2 PM, even though her belief is true.**, **The belief was formed through a normally reliable process, as relying on a clock generally provides accurate time.**, **The external conditions (a stopped clock) indicate that despite the reliability of the process in general, in this specific instance it leads to knowledge that seems problematic due to luck.**
