# Does the role of luck undermine the reliabilist theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0650ed6-0003-4e02-bac7-0480ac785e40
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-498bb613-4448-44e8-9d2e-1c93717bc9f2.md)

## Content

**A scenario where a true belief is formed through luck, such as finding a lost watch and correctly guessing the time, challenges the idea that a belief formed by a reliable process is knowledge.**, **A true belief acquired purely by luck lacks the necessary reliability to count as knowledge.**
