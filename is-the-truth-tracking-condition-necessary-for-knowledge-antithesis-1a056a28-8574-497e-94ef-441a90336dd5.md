# Is the truth-tracking condition necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a056a28-8574-497e-94ef-441a90336dd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1a215329-c53d-4dbe-9509-6b517d1bb8ad.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a belief that matches the truth in all nearby possible worlds, due to their reliable methods, but happens to fail the truth-tracking condition in a single, far-fetched scenario. Intuitively, it seems this should still count as knowledge despite this condition failing.**, **There must be a plausible principle that reliable methods should outweigh occasional failure in extreme scenarios.**
