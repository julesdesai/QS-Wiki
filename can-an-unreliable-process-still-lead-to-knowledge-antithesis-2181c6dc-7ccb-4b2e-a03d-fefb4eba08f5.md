# Can an unreliable process still lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2181c6dc-7ccb-4b2e-a03d-fefb4eba08f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person predicts tomorrow's weather by flipping a coin. A correct prediction about the weather made through a coin flip, although coincidentally accurate, would not intuitively be considered knowledge.**, **It is implausible that an unreliable process such as coin flipping can produce knowledge, even if it accidentally results in a true belief.**
