# Can environmental deception create a gap in knowledge safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c6e0c1a1-5eb5-4ae4-9149-c82d0c48b469
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which a person enters a room full of imitation flowers and believes, just as they have frequently observed, that all flowers are real. Typically, these beliefs are true except for this carefully crafted deceptive environment. Intuitively, one might suggest that such careful deception could deceive anyone, yet knowledge appears absent here.**, **A belief is not safe if in nearby possible worlds a minor change, like the presence of imitation flowers, leads to a false belief. However, careful deception raises concerns about the belief's safety.**
