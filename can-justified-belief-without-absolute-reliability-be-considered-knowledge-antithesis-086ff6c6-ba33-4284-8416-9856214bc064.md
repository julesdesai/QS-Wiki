# Can justified belief without absolute reliability be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 086ff6c6-ba33-4284-8416-9856214bc064
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person has a belief that is justified through a generally reliable source, but the source occasionally produces false outputs. Even if the belief is true and justified, intuitively, the presence of doubt about the source's reliability suggests the person lacks knowledge.**, **A belief is justified if it rests on foundational beliefs that are reliable but not infallible; yet, for it to qualify as knowledge, it must overcome the infallibility challenge.**
