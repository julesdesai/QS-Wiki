# Do cases of perceptual adaptation challenge the directness of perception about external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6fbb652d-90f2-45bf-935a-a0c9978cf6d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone wears inverting lenses that make the world appear upside down, and after some time, they adapt and perceive the world correctly. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is adaptable and not directly tied to the external state of objects.**, **It is implausible that if perception provides direct access, such significant adaptation would be necessary, as direct perception should be stable.**
