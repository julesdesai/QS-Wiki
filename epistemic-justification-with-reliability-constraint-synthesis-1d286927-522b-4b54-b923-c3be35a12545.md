# Epistemic Justification with Reliability Constraint

**Parent:** [Is justification truly sufficient for knowledge if beliefs can be formed via unreliable processes?](is-justification-truly-sufficient-for-knowledge-if-beliefs-can-be-formed-via-unreliable-processes-antithesis-511cfee2-78c1-4de1-956a-a07ee6c83417.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is justified**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is not arrived at through epistemic luck**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed through a reliable process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is justified, the belief is not a matter of luck, and the belief is formed through a reliable process**
