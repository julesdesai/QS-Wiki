# When do coincidences undermine evidential supports for beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3fe3ecd-f3f1-47d0-9d4e-8289b3235767
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a belief about a natural phenomenon is true and supported by coincidentally correct historical data. Intuitively, one might still attribute knowledge to this belief despite the coincidental factor.**, **It is implausible that every coincidentally correct evidential support inherently revokes the status of knowledge, given the belief's truth.**
