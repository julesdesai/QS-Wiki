# Do nearby possible worlds adequately safeguard knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c530b3a3-b301-494c-a486-a8867d97276e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that nearby possible worlds can fully account for the safety of a belief. Determining what counts as nearby is often subjective and unclear.**, **Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief based on a lucky guess. Intuitively, their belief does not constitute knowledge even if there are no nearby worlds where it is false.**
