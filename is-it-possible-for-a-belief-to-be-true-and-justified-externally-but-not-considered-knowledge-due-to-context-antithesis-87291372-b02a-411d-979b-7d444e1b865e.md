# Is it possible for a belief to be true and justified externally but not considered knowledge due to context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87291372-b02a-411d-979b-7d444e1b865e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone believes a politician will win an election based on a single favorable, yet unreliable, poll. When the politician wins, intuitively, the belief was true, but it lacks the rigor we associate with true knowledge because the context was misrepresentative.**, **There is a plausible principle that context and the broader evidential situation play a significant role in determining whether a belief is knowledge. Without contextual reliability, external justification remains insufficient.**
