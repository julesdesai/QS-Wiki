# Does perceptual consensus guarantee knowledge of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5456c599-3ff8-4bf5-8cbe-d83fea12840a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Baseline Phenomenalism](perceptual-baseline-phenomenalism-synthesis-c5e3ac41-9c0b-44e1-9f51-d5e2e022393a.md)

## Content

**Consensus among observers about what is perceived does not necessarily correlate with the truth of an external object's existence.**, **A thought experiment where a group sees a mirage and agrees on its presence questions consensus as a marker of truth.**, **It is implausible to equate consensus with knowledge without verifying the external object's independent existence.**
