**Consider a situation where a person sees a mirage of water in a desert, and believes that there is water ahead. This belief does not constitute knowledge as there is no water present.**, **Perception alone can sometimes lead to false beliefs when faced with hallucinations or illusions, challenging the reliability of direct perception.**
