# Does Direct Realism address cases where perception differs across species?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b85f7f8c-6c07-417e-be7e-2a6870a4c54b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a dog senses a higher pitch unperceivable by humans. Intuitively, we would judge both perceivers are accessing reality differently due to their sensory apparatuses.**, **It is implausible to maintain that perception provides the same direct access to the external world for all species if sensory capabilities differ significantly.**
