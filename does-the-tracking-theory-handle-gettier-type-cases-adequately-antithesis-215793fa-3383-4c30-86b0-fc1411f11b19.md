# Does the tracking theory handle Gettier-type cases adequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 215793fa-3383-4c30-86b0-fc1411f11b19
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory With Truth Tracking](nozicks-tracking-theory-with-truth-tracking-synthesis-9a557177-6533-4827-a4a8-a2fb94f50474.md)

## Content

**Consider a Gettier-type case where a person comes to a true belief through justified, but ultimately coincidental, reasoning, such as deducing someone's location based on a faulty map that luckily corresponds to reality. Intuitively, we judge that the person does not truly know the location, even though their belief is true.**, **Gettier cases challenge the notion that tracking truth through sensitivity and adherence is sufficient for knowledge, suggesting that an additional criterion might be necessary to differentiate knowledge from lucky guesses.**
