# Can a reliable process lead to true beliefs that are not justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1251bfbe-72af-4ee3-ada9-7fbb5dde6c72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge](reliable-process-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8a084bde-8e94-4b49-86d3-6339aef5c9e6.md)

## Content

**There may be cases where a belief is true and formed through a reliable process but lacks justification for the believer**, **It is possible for a person to accidentally arrive at a true belief even if the process leading there is generally reliable**, **Justification requires a rational connection between the belief and the process, which might not exist in some reliable processes**
