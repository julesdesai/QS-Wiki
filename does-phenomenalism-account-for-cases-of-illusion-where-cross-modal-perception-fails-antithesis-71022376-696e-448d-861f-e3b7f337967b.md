# Does phenomenalism account for cases of illusion where cross-modal perception fails?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 71022376-696e-448d-861f-e3b7f337967b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a straight stick in water that looks bent but feels straight when touched. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perceptual experiences are not coherent across different sensory modalities.**, **A situation in which visual perception indicates a bent stick while tactile perception indicates a straight stick is a case in which cross-modal perception is not explained by consistent phenomenal experiences that align across senses.**
