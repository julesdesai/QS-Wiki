# Can knowledge be sensitive without being reliably acquired?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd662b0d-48ed-4c6d-ac8e-6af5a8232437
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a stock will rise based on the last stockbroker tip received. The belief changes according to tips, making it sensitive. Intuitively, we judge such tips to be unreliable for genuine knowledge.**, **A sensitivity condition fulfilled through unreliable methods challenges the intuition that knowledge must be reliably obtained, not just responsive to truth conditions.**
