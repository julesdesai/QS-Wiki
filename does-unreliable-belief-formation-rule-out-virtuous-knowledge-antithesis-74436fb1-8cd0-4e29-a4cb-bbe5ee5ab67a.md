# Does unreliable belief formation rule out virtuous knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74436fb1-8cd0-4e29-a4cb-bbe5ee5ab67a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone with intellectual character virtues acquires a belief through an unreliable process, such as guessing, yet the belief is true and aligns with responsible conduct. We might question whether the unreliability of the process disqualifies this from being knowledge.**, **It is plausible to suggest that belief formation processes, even if unreliable, might still result in knowledge if intellectual character virtues are sufficiently displayed.**
