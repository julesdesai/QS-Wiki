# Knowledge as Virtue-Enhanced Reliability

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed by a reliable cognitive process grounded in intellectual virtue**, **Intellectual virtue involves cognitive character traits like open-mindedness or intellectual courage, which support reliable belief formation**
