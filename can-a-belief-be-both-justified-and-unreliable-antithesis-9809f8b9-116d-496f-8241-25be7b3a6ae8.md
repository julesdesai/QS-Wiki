# Can a belief be both justified and unreliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9809f8b9-116d-496f-8241-25be7b3a6ae8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which someone forms a belief based on a hunch or intuition that is occasionally correct, such as guessing the outcome of a coin flip. Intuitively, we would not regard this as reliable, yet it could still be considered justified by some subjective criteria.**, **A cognitive process that is subjectively felt as reliable but objectively regarded as unreliable complicates the link between justification and reliability.**
