# Are knowledge standards truly context-independent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43978ec8-06e8-4b43-98d4-5a092730bc47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that if knowledge standards are truly invariant, they should apply consistently across all contextual variations.**, **Consider a scenario in which two identical beliefs are held in contexts that differ solely in practical stakes. Intuitively, knowledge judgments should be identical if standards do not change.**
