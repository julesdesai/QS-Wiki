# Is error-avoidance sufficient for knowledge when beliefs are not formed through intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fa2d432d-1071-4063-bda4-ba779afb692a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Enhanced Virtue Reliabilism with Error Avoidance](enhanced-virtue-reliabilism-with-error-avoidance-synthesis-bae843f7-47b1-4791-af4f-a08b903c892d.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a belief is constantly true because of environmental factors, not because of the person's intellectual efforts. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that knowledge is not present.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge depends on the individual's intellectual reliability rather than accidental truth, suggesting environmental luck undermines knowledge.**
