# Can justified true belief fail to be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dab4abac-3577-481c-bc2e-ddde63b0e23f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-foundationalism-synthesis-a32c22e3-fd99-46f1-bd06-3346824cbc70.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on solid evidence and proper reasoning, and the belief is true. Intuitively, there are situations, such as Gettier cases, where this does not amount to knowledge because of the presence of luck. If we say that this is not knowledge, then the role of intellectual virtues needs to account for this discrepancy, which is not captured by mere justified true belief.**
