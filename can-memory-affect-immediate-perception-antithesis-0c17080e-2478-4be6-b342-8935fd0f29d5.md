# Can memory affect immediate perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c17080e-2478-4be6-b342-8935fd0f29d5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Perspective](phenomenological-perspective-synthesis-c5a75a88-a263-494b-bef9-8c1e426bd706.md)

## Content

**Suppose a case in which a person immediately perceives a familiar room and notices its changes due to previously stored memory. Intuitively, knowledge of changes relies not only on immediate perception but also on memory comparison.**, **This suggests that perception cannot solely account for understanding without the aid of memory.**
