# How does Proper Functionalism account for environmental variations affecting belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 522b9e74-a72c-472d-a37b-bd54d76dd5b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-17c9fccb-5f3e-4011-91c3-5213c66550ac.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is in an environment unlike the one for which their cognitive faculties were designed. In such a scenario, it is plausible that their faculties would not function optimally and thus would not produce true beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that cognitive faculties must function in environments conducive to their design. This principle together with the variability of environments entails that deviations from designed environments could result in false beliefs.**
