# Can hallucinations provide cases where Naïve Realism fails?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87da49a5-692b-4da7-bc3a-5591c6f1d4f3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-960c5650-a545-46a7-9f9f-61ad44274751.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person hallucinates a pink elephant in the room. Intuitively, we have to say that the pink elephant does not exist in the external world or that it does. If we say the pink elephant does not exist, then hallucinations challenge the notion that perception always presents external objects directly. If we say the pink elephant does exist, then we deny empirical facts about the world.**, **A hallucination, by definition, does not correspond to any external object existing in the world.**
