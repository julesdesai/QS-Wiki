# Can reliabilism accommodate the intuition that testimony should be considered reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a2bcc6c8-f346-45c9-a4cb-1f77e25273c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a405882b-2e95-4de6-9817-4516092396fb.md)

## Content

**In many cases, people form beliefs based on testimony, which we intuitively consider to be a form of knowledge**, **However, it is plausible that many testimonies involve mechanisms that aren't always reliable, particularly if we consider variables like the knowledge of the testifier or the chain of transmission**, **Reliabilism struggles to account for how testimony can lead to justified beliefs when the process isn't reliably truth-conducive**
