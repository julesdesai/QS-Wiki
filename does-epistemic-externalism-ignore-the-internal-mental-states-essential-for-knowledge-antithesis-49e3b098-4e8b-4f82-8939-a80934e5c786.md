# Does epistemic externalism ignore the internal mental states essential for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 49e3b098-4e8b-4f82-8939-a80934e5c786
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people are mentally identical, but one has external justification and the other does not. Intuitively, we would judge that their knowledge should be similar, if not identical.**, **A person's internal mental states seem crucial for determining knowledge, which externalism does not adequately capture.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be fully explained without reference to a person's mental states.**
