# Is there a point where too much evidence becomes a hindrance to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 974a073f-f2f5-48ed-8dbf-009de694f2ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an overload of comprehensive evidence leads a person to doubt their true instincts about a situation. Intuitively, we might judge that excessive evidence has impaired their genuine understanding.**, **Excessive evidence potentially obscuring true belief suggests a limit on evidence quantity being beneficial for knowledge.**
