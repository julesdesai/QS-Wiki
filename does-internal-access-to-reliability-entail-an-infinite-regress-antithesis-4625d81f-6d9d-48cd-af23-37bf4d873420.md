# Does internal access to reliability entail an infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4625d81f-6d9d-48cd-af23-37bf4d873420
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Reliabilism Summary](internalist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-afd99030-664a-4a62-9431-6f8bc1e36717.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims they have knowledge because they have internal access to their cognitive process's reliability.**, **To check this reliability, they need access to the reliability of the method used to assess the first process.**, **This need for further assessment leads to an infinite regress, which makes internal access impractical for achieving knowledge.**
