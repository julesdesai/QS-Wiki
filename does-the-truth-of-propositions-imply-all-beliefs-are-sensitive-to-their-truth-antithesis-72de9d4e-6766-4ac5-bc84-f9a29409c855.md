# Does the truth of propositions imply all beliefs are sensitive to their truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72de9d4e-6766-4ac5-bc84-f9a29409c855
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity Condition](contextualism-with-sensitivity-condition-synthesis-dd26ba8e-d20d-4379-a4ae-bc06eaa28734.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where a person P believes a false proposition and later the proposition becomes true, P's belief was not initially sensitive to the truth of the proposition**, **For P to have known the proposition at the time of belief, it would have needed to be true at that time**, **If the standard for knowing requires truth and sensitivity, P cannot be said to have known the proposition until it became true**
