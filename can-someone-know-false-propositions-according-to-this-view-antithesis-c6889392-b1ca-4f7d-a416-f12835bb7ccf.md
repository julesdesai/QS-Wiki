# Can someone know false propositions according to this view?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c6889392-b1ca-4f7d-a416-f12835bb7ccf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a person knows a proposition, then the proposition must be true. This principle together with the claim that knowledge is true belief implies that one cannot know false propositions.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person firmly believes in a false proposition due to intellectual virtue, but the proposition is actually false. Intuitively, they cannot be said to know the false proposition.**
