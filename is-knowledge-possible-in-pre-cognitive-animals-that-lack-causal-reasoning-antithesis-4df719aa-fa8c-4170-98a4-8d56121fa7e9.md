# Is knowledge possible in pre-cognitive animals that lack causal reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4df719aa-fa8c-4170-98a4-8d56121fa7e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cee9090d-2e34-47f2-b4ee-a59054387264.md)

## Content

**Suppose some intelligent non-human animals form beliefs based on instinctual patterns, such as anticipating a change in seasons. These beliefs might be true but are not causally derived from understanding or reasoning. Intuitively, we might still regard these beliefs as some form of knowledge.**, **Animals or very young children often rely on altered environments that promote survival-related beliefs without a causally reasoned path.**
