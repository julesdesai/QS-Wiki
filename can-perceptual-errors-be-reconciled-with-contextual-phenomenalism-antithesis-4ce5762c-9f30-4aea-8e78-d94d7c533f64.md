# Can perceptual errors be reconciled with contextual phenomenalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4ce5762c-9f30-4aea-8e78-d94d7c533f64
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where someone perceives a tilted object's shape incorrectly due to perspective; intuitively, this undermines their knowledge about the object's true form.**, **A plausible principle is that perceptual errors introduce doubt about the reliability of knowledge that heavily depends on perception.**, **There is a problematic tension in reconciling perceptual errors with the view that perceptual experiences are foundational to knowledge.**
