# Is it possible for sensory experiences to provide a complete explanation of causality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 043a2c7c-ae77-4c96-8d1a-145d7c86c87f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone rolls a ball across a table, and it stops due to friction. The experience of the ball coming to rest seems to imply a causal relationship not fully explained by the sensory experience of seeing the ball stop**, **It is implausible that sensory experiences alone explain the cause and effect without recourse to further understanding of underlying laws or principles**
