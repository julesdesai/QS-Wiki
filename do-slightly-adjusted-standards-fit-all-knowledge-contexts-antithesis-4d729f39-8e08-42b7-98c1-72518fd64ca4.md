# Do slightly adjusted standards fit all knowledge contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4d729f39-8e08-42b7-98c1-72518fd64ca4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-550a9ee8-c8d3-41b5-bd1e-c079e9d505d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a physician diagnoses a patient using the same standards in an emergency and a routine checkup. Intuitively, the standards for knowing a medical proposition should differ drastically rather than adjust slightly.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge standards should significantly vary across different contexts to accommodate the diverse demands of each situation. This principle implies that slightly adjusted standards are insufficient.**
