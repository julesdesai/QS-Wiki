# Can process reliabilism explain epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 015cdf9b-e366-4a8f-b2c6-b245c30d51b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**Consider a principle that, for a belief to qualify as knowledge, the believer must be epistemically responsible in forming that belief. This clashes with a version of reliabilism that might exonerate all reliable processes without evaluating the agent's conduct or diligence.**, **Think of a careless scientist who frequently guesses experiment outcomes but due to sheer luck or environmental setup always guesses correctly. Intuitively, we wouldn’t commend this as knowledge.**, **The absence of a requirement for epistemic responsibility within purely process-oriented views complicates the identification of knowledge.**
