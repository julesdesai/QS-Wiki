# Can false intermediate causation undermine causal theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce0ebcb6-b876-4a0c-b119-1ebb7dbd1089
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c3b94507-0858-4bfa-a251-208b7829ae1f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person sees a raccoon and a fox but incorrectly assumes the fox alone causes footprints in the snow. Their belief about raccoon footprints in the snow is true but based on a false intermediate cause.**, **Intuitively, if the causation involved a false intermediary, it undermines the claim of causal knowledge even with a true belief.**
