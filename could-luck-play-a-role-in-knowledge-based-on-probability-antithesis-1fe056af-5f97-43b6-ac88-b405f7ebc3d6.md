# Could luck play a role in knowledge based on probability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fe056af-5f97-43b6-ac88-b405f7ebc3d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-4f173a54-3943-4ddb-a2cd-a6bb34479be2.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a true belief results from lucky chance rather than reliable methods. Intuitively, this undermines the claim that the belief constitutes knowledge, even if foundational beliefs were probabilistically sound.**, **Luck and accidents pose challenges if they contribute to the outcomes labeled as knowledge.**
