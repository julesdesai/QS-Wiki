# Is sole perception insufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3cba737e-1de3-4982-8059-dd5db73b02a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-c00af758-5073-4931-9585-2f560dddd897.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person sees a rare comet in the sky while alone. In such a case, it seems intuitive that the person knows about the comet from their perceptual experience, even though no one else can immediately verify it.**, **A person knows a perceptual experience directly, even if others cannot immediately verify it, suggesting a divergence from the need for inter-subjective agreement.**
