# Can belief alone suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f7b95d6-e800-4380-8cf5-039ac9e20155
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone sincerely believes the proposition that tomorrow will rain because they consulted a very unreliable weather service. Intuitively, we either have to say that their belief constitutes knowledge because they believe it, or that it does not because their source is unreliable. If we say it constitutes knowledge, then every belief, no matter how unsupported, counts as knowledge. If we say it does not, then belief alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
