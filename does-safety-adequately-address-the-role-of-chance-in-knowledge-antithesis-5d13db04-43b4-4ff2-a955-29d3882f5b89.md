# Does safety adequately address the role of chance in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d13db04-43b4-4ff2-a955-29d3882f5b89
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone makes a correct prediction about a natural event purely by chance, yet in all nearby worlds, chance favors the same prediction occurring correctly. Intuitively, chance involvement diminishes the claim of knowledge despite the safety of belief.**, **Safety in belief may not account sufficiently for external interference through chance, challenging whether all safe beliefs are knowledge.**
