# Can practical considerations alone reliably guide perception for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e731086c-5d94-4ead-9d2d-5180046b9df8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person perceives a mirage in the desert and plans to drink from it, judging it useful and actionable. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of water being present.**, **There is a plausible principle that usefulness does not guarantee truth. This principle together with the belief that a proposition is useful and actionable can lead to incorrect judgments about knowledge.**
