# Can inadequate evidence sometimes support genuine belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** caf93c8c-8ac9-4b78-9cdb-1cebc3b752f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that individuals can know based on seemingly minimal evidence if the evidence guarantees truth through reliable processes.**, **Suppose someone predicts an earthquake with little evidence other than animal behavior changes, which are often unreliable. If the earthquake occurs, intuitively, it seems they had knowledge.**
