# Does justification always strengthen knowledge independently?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a02da9a4-72db-4d55-a804-37456d5493f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should uniformly contribute to the stability of knowledge. This principle together with the idea that the context can overrule justification suggests that context might sometimes undermine rather than support knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has strong justification for a belief in a casual context but this justification seems insufficient in a high-stakes context. Intuitively, justification should not alter its contribution to knowledge based merely on stakes.**
