# Is contextualism overly complex for practical use?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d608ae8-e2a4-4ace-962d-5dd543f8f587
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-bce49363-1810-4900-8ccf-1e421dbc62ca.md)

## Content

**Practical theories should ideally be simple enough for wide understanding and application.**, **The complexity involved in constantly assessing different contexts and varying standards makes contextualism difficult to practically apply without confusion.**, **It is implausible for a theory requiring constant assessment of varying standards to be feasible for practical use in everyday knowledge claims.**
