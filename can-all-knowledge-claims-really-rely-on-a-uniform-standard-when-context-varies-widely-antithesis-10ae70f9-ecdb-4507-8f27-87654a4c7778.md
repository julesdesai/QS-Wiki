# Can all knowledge claims really rely on a uniform standard when context varies widely?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 10ae70f9-ecdb-4507-8f27-87654a4c7778
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Contextual Invariantism](hybrid-contextual-invariantism-synthesis-adfbf27a-f780-498c-b828-265cba5bed4f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if knowledge claims are invariant, they should not differ with context. This principle together with varying contexts of evaluation entails that invariant knowledge claims are implausible.**, **Consider a scenario where a person in different contexts makes contradictory knowledge claims about the same proposition; intuitively, this suggests knowledge cannot be context-independent.**
