# Can indirect realism accommodate perceptual errors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47033365-7232-44d4-bff9-8eb1946e0fdd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a stick in water, which appears bent due to refraction. Intuitively, we would judge that their perception is flawed or erroneous.**, **There is a plausible principle that suggests successful perception, thus knowledge, corresponds directly to reality. This principle challenges indirect realism, as the intermediaries that may cause perceptual errors could undermine true knowledge.**
