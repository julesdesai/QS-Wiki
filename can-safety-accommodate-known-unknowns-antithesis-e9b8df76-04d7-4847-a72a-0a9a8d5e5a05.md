# Can safety accommodate known unknowns?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9b8df76-04d7-4847-a72a-0a9a8d5e5a05
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is informed by a reliable source about an unknown fact, such as the number of stars in a distant galaxy. Their belief is therefore true and safely formed, yet intuitively remains uncertain and not fully knowing.**, **A belief is safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. The concept of unknown unknowns complicates how safety accounts for partially informed beliefs.**
