# Can knowledge be independent of individual memory characteristics?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b68a7dd-92ba-4b43-b159-78fec72c5059
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Phenomenalism Allowing Memory Role](revised-phenomenalism-allowing-memory-role-synthesis-5fb5abbe-6adb-4855-a476-25c8a91d11a9.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is typically sought to be objective and universal. This goal together with the personalized nature of memory that may vary significantly from person to person implies a challenge to ensuring that knowledge claims are universally valid when based on memory-influenced perceptions.**, **Imagine two people recalling the same lecture, each focusing on different aspects and thus claiming different lessons. Intuitively, such variance makes it difficult to establish a universal knowledge claim from memory-influenced perceptions.**
