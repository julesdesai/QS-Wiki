# Is it contradictory to have variable justification within a fixed knowledge framework?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d8820c7-7651-47eb-b908-350d48b5d1dc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a teacher asserts they know a student will pass a test based on preparation observed in class, a context allowing looser justification standards.**, **Later, during a crucial scholarship decision, the same assertion is dissected under strict justification criteria to determine eligibility.**, **Intuitively, it seems contradictory for the teacher’s claim to represent knowledge in one situation but not the other, undermining the stability of holding fixed criteria for what counts as knowledge.**
