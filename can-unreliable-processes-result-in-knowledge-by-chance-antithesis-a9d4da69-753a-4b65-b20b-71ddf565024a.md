# Can unreliable processes result in knowledge by chance?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9d4da69-753a-4b65-b20b-71ddf565024a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief through a process that is generally unreliable, but in this particular instance, it results in a true belief by chance. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge because the reliable process was absent.**, **It is implausible to claim that the mere occurrence of a true belief by chance can amount to knowledge without reliability.**
