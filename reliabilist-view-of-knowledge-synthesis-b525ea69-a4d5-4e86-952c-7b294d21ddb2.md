# Reliabilist View of Knowledge

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** b525ea69-a4d5-4e86-952c-7b294d21ddb2
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is gathered evidence always necessary for knowledge?](is-gathered-evidence-always-necessary-for-knowledge-antithesis-d9725b61-d35c-41aa-84ca-f4240594e3c1.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief in the proposition is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **A reliable cognitive process is one that produces a high percentage of true beliefs**, **Knowledge does not require access to external evidence**
