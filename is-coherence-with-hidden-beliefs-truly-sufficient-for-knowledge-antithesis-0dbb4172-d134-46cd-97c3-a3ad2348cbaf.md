# Is coherence with hidden beliefs truly sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0dbb4172-d134-46cd-97c3-a3ad2348cbaf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Non-Conscious Elements](coherentism-with-non-conscious-elements-synthesis-025108b6-66b5-4784-9945-6373af308ffa.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's belief coheres with a complex set of non-conscious beliefs that the person cannot consciously access or describe. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows purely because of coherence, or that coherent non-accessible beliefs do not confer knowledge. If we say coherence alone is enough, then it seems implausible that unaccessed beliefs give knowledge. If we deny knowledge, coherence is not sufficient on its own.**
