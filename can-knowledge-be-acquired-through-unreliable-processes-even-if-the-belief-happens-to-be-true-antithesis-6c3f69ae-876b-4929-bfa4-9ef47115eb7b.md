# Can knowledge be acquired through unreliable processes even if the belief happens to be true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c3f69ae-876b-4929-bfa4-9ef47115eb7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it is raining outside by looking out a window, but the window is actually displaying a recorded video of rain. Intuitively, we would judge that the person's belief does not constitute knowledge despite being true.**, **The belief-forming process here was not reliable because the medium through which the belief was formed, the recorded video, does not generally produce true beliefs about current weather conditions.**
