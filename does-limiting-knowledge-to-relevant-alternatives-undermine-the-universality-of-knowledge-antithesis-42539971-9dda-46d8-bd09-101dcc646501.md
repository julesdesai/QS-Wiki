# Does limiting knowledge to relevant alternatives undermine the universality of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42539971-9dda-46d8-bd09-101dcc646501
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**A person can have knowledge that extends beyond merely relevant alternatives, as some forms of knowledge apply universally or objectively, regardless of the context-specific alternatives.**, **There are scenarios where knowledge is held to be objective, such as mathematical truths, where no relevant alternatives seem applicable, suggesting that knowledge may not depend on context.**
