# Can beliefs formed under epistemic luck count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b3c0ac4-7961-45c6-8bc3-28628d03768d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Expanded Virtue Reliabilism](expanded-virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-be1f6636-aeca-4898-8b76-a37fb9c31f3b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief through sheer luck even though their cognitive faculties are functioning as intellectual virtues. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that there is no knowledge due to the luck involved.**, **A reliable method influenced by intellectual virtue that leads to belief is thought to preclude luck, yet this scenario shows that seeming reliable influence does not always rule out luck.**
