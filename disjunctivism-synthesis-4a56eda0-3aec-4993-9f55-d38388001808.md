## Content

**In a veridical perception, what one perceives is the external world directly**, **In an illusion, one does not perceive the external world directly**, **Veridical perceptions and illusions are fundamentally different epistemic states**, **For a person to know a proposition from perception, it is necessary that it is a veridical perception**, **For a person to know a proposition from perception, it is necessary that the proposition is believed and true**
