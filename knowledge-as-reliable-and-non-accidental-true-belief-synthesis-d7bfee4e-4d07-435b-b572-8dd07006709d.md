# Knowledge as Reliable and Non-accidental True Belief

**Node Type:** synthesis

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can justification ensure knowledge in the presence of misleading evidence?](can-justification-ensure-knowledge-in-the-presence-of-misleading-evidence-antithesis-a0abfa92-3b7a-4ec8-b8e1-31252522b143.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their true belief is not accidentally true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process, and their belief is not accidentally true**
