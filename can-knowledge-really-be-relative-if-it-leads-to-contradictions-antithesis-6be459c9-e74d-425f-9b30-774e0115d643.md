# Can knowledge really be relative if it leads to contradictions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6be459c9-e74d-425f-9b30-774e0115d643
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Relativism](epistemic-relativism-synthesis-8a36f430-fbe7-4356-93bb-efe42975c670.md)

## Content

**Suppose person A belongs to an epistemic community whose framework states that proposition P is true, while person B belongs to a different community whose framework states that P is false.**, **In this scenario, both A can be said to know P, and B can be said to know not-P.**, **If both A and B truly have knowledge of P and not-P respectively, this leads to a contradiction, as both P and not-P cannot be true simultaneously.**
