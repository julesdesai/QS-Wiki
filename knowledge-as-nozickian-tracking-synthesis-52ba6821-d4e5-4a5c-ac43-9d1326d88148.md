**Parent:** [Can reliabilism address beliefs derived from inherently unreliable yet locally accurate processes?](can-reliabilism-address-beliefs-derived-from-inherently-unreliable-yet-locally-accurate-processes-antithesis-eedc7bca-ef28-4773-bb5c-cf91e6f1de1b.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition were false, the person would not believe it**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition were true, the person would believe it**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief tracks the truth across possible scenarios**
