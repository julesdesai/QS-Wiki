# Can knowledge be equated with merely beneficial belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c01327c2-c762-4a4a-b40c-d16a2e7ed63c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c88c0bac-fed4-4271-b530-0511020766f8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief that significantly benefits them but is unrelated to the truth of the proposition. Intuitively, this does not seem to qualify as genuine knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge must relate closely to the truth of a proposition, irrespective of any practical benefits.**
