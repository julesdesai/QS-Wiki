# Does foundationalism permit circular reasoning if all chains end in basic beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0dbdb45a-88d7-4b4d-9284-6df0d4a8812c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that any form of reasoning that allows circular validation of beliefs is epistemically unsatisfactory. This principle suggests that foundationalism risks circularity if chains of justification always end at basic beliefs without further grounding.**, **Consider a scenario where a person justifies a belief by tracing it back to basic beliefs, which themselves were initially justified by the interconnected belief network. In such a scenario, intuitively, foundationalism seems to permit circularity.**
