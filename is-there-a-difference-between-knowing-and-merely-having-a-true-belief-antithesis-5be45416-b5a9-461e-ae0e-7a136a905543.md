# Is there a difference between knowing and merely having a true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5be45416-b5a9-461e-ae0e-7a136a905543
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-0c8c051d-f89d-468b-87e6-c5a1fb54f910.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where someone believes a fact as a result of unconscious bias yet lacks supporting evidence, like assuming a coin will land heads without understanding probability. If the coin lands heads, intuitively, we would say the belief was true but not knowledge.**, **The scenario highlights that having a true belief owing to bias or unsupported hunches isn't sufficient for knowledge. Intuitively, justification plays a critical role in distinguishing knowledge from mere true belief.**, **An acceptable condition is that knowledge should consist of more than true belief by ensuring the presence of an appropriate explanation or justification tying belief to truth.**
