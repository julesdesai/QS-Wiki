# Do different stakes imply different standards for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 368d5cfa-bef8-4bd2-85d3-bef21354a8ae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two scenarios: one where a person needs to know a fact to make a trivial decision, and another where the same person needs to know the same fact to make a life-altering decision. Intuitively, the standards for knowing should be consistent across both scenarios.**, **There is a theoretical intuition that knowing something should not involve varying standards influenced by what is practically at stake.**, **It is implausible that identical true beliefs should require different standards of justification simply because the stakes involved are different.**
