# Can circular reasoning provide justification in an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f182175-81e7-4324-a74a-5840f03d2adc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-fd5e1271-f2df-4333-a434-50b9d95350fd.md)

## Content

**A plausible reasoning pattern involves returning to earlier beliefs, which clashes with the requirement for non-repeating reasons.**, **If an infinite chain of reasons cannot repeat, then the legitimate use of circular reasoning for justification is disallowed.**, **Suppose knowledge requires non-repeating reasons; then, conventional reasoning that employs circular methods becomes invalidated.**
