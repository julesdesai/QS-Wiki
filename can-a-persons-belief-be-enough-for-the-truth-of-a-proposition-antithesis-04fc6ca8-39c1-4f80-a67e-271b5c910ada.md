# Can a person's belief be enough for the truth of a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 04fc6ca8-39c1-4f80-a67e-271b5c910ada
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Infinitism](coherent-infinitism-synthesis-f2c99927-f862-4ee8-8b5b-2fefd2717ee2.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes they can fly unaided. Intuitively, this belief cannot make the proposition true. A belief does not alter the external world to ensure the truth of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs do not determine physical truths. This principle together with the nature of external factual constraints entails that mere belief cannot account for the truth of propositions requiring factual correspondence.**
