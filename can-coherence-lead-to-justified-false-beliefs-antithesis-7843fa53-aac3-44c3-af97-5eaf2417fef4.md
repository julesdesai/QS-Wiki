# Can coherence lead to justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7843fa53-aac3-44c3-af97-5eaf2417fef4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Coherentist-Internalist View](hybrid-coherentist-internalist-view-synthesis-ffa44d27-7377-4f6d-8451-515975b92108.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that coherence of beliefs is supposed to lead to justified belief. This principle together with the possibility of all coherent beliefs being false entails that one can have justified false beliefs.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person’s beliefs are coherent but factually incorrect. Intuitively, we would judge that such coherence does not prevent the beliefs from being false.**
