# Is contextual reliability sufficient for foundational beliefs to ground knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84a74bde-e2c7-400e-a62b-0a716fe74762
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a foundational belief is deemed reliable in one context but unreliable in another due to varying evidentiary standards. Intuitively, we would question whether such variability can genuinely underpin knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that foundational beliefs must be universally reliable to serve as a basis for knowledge. This principle suggests a problem for contextual foundationalism relying solely on contextual reliability.**
