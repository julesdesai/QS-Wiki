# Is causality alone enough for normative knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 734b5045-2418-4928-8d85-3edcd1afe14f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone knows they ought to keep a promise based on moral reasoning. Intuitively, this knowledge does not depend on a causal relationship but on normative principles.**, **It is implausible that normative knowledge, such as moral or ethical knowledge, can be satisfactorily captured by causal connections with truths alone.**
