# Are justified false beliefs an issue for foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5077d27e-0b38-402d-a456-493b6dc5d764
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Pragmatic Grounds](foundationalism-with-pragmatic-grounds-synthesis-35d5e861-b788-4e52-a02b-4488aba3b8d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a justified belief that turns out to be false. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that their belief, while justified, cannot constitute knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that justification should entail accuracy. This principle together with having justified false beliefs appears to question the viability of foundationalism to ensure true belief.**
