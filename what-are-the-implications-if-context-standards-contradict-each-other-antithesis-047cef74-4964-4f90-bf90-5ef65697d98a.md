# What are the implications if context standards contradict each other?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 047cef74-4964-4f90-bf90-5ef65697d98a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**Consider a context where the standards of knowledge conflict, such that satisfying one set of standards results in failing another. Intuitively, we cannot say that knowledge is present if standards can contradict.**, **It is implausible to hold that varying context standards that lead to contradictions still support a coherent concept of knowledge.**
