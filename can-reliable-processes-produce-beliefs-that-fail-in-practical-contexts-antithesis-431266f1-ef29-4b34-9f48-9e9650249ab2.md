# Can reliable processes produce beliefs that fail in practical contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 431266f1-ef29-4b34-9f48-9e9650249ab2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a reliable cognitive process leads to a true belief that fails when tested in different practical contexts. Intuitively, such a belief may not be considered knowledge.**, **It is plausible that knowledge should confer practical efficacy or applicability, which reliability does not inherently provide.**
