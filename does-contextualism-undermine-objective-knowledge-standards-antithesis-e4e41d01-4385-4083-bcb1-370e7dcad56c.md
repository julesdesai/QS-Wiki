# Does contextualism undermine objective knowledge standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4e41d01-4385-4083-bcb1-370e7dcad56c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism highlighting context-dependent justification](contextualism-highlighting-context-dependent-justification-synthesis-c9f62fb8-5b79-43c8-99f6-6461f5a6c05a.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where justification standards change significantly with the context, such that what is known in one scenario is not known in another. Intuitively, we might question whether such fluctuation fits with the idea of objective knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective knowledge should be independent of personal or situational variations, suggesting that highly variable contextual thresholds might undermine the objectivity of knowledge claims.**
