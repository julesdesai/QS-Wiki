# Does contextualism lead to relativism concerning truth and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8bb93e0-2c7c-4d3a-a96f-2442759dff1a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-8d92b68a-d96e-49d3-9b29-39a25430afac.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that suggests standards for justification could vary so much from one context to another that what is known in one context is not known in another. This principle together with the notion of varying justification leads to the conclusion that truth could become relative, undermining objective knowledge.**, **Suppose a situation where a person's belief is justified in a lenient context but not in a strict one. Intuitively, we might say this challenges the objectivity of knowledge claims.**
