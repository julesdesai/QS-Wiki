# Are there situations where evidence is unnecessary but knowledge still exists?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9449747f-c010-493c-8054-f339a0c4526b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone experiences a vivid memory of an event that they know happened despite a lack of additional evidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that this recollection constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that certain psychological states such as memory can provide knowledge without needing additional evidence.**
