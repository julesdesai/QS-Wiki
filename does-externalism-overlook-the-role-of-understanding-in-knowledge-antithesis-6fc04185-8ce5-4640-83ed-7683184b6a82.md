# Does externalism overlook the role of understanding in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6fc04185-8ce5-4640-83ed-7683184b6a82
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-thesis-767dbb09-481f-47c3-bbe5-cc6951f6aa86.md)

## Content

**Understanding involves a coherent grasp of the relations and context of a proposition. Even if someone reliably believes something true, without understanding, attributing knowledge seems problematic.**, **In a case where a person remembers a true fact from a coherent but deeply misunderstood text, they lack understanding even if the belief formation process was reliable. This raises doubts about whether their reliable belief constitutes true knowledge.**
