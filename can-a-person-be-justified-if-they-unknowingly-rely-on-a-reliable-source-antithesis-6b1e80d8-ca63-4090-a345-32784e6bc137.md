# Can a person be justified if they unknowingly rely on a reliable source?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b1e80d8-ca63-4090-a345-32784e6bc137
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person discovers a clock that is stopped, but they don't realize that the clock usually is reliable. If once a day the clock shows the correct time, and the person forms a belief about the time based on this, intuitively, they lack knowledge because they lack awareness of the reliability.**, **This raises questions about whether justification requires awareness of the reliability of the source.**
