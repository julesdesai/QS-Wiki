# Does pragmatic contextualism wrongly dismiss luck in the acquisition of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72c61a64-8d2b-47bb-9ec6-7025127444f3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**A person might achieve practical success based on a belief simply due to luck rather than justified belief.**, **Knowledge is traditionally considered to involve justified true belief, not just practical success, which may be attributed to chance.**, **Attributing knowledge to mere practical success overlooks cases where success is achieved through luck without a solid grounding in truth.**
