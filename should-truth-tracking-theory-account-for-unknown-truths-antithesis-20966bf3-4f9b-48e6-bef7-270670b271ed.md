# Should truth-tracking theory account for unknown truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20966bf3-4f9b-48e6-bef7-270670b271ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person unknowingly possesses a true belief because it happens to be true by chance, with the truth of the belief largely disconnected from the person's capacity to track that truth across nearby possible worlds. In such a case, intuitively, the person lacks knowledge because their belief is not truth-tracking in any meaningful sense.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be more reliable than mere coincidental truths, suggesting truth-tracking must be substantiated beyond just incidental truth.**
