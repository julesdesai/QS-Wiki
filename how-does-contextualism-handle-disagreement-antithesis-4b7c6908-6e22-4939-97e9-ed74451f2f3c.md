# How does contextualism handle disagreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b7c6908-6e22-4939-97e9-ed74451f2f3c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals disagree about a proposition due to differing contexts they find themselves in. Intuitively, for both to be correct in their knowledge claims seems implausible.**, **There is a plausible principle that if two individuals have the same evidence, they should have the same knowledge claim rather than contextually varied ones.**
