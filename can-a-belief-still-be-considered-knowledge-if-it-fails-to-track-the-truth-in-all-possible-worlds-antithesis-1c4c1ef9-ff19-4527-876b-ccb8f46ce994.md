# Can a belief still be considered knowledge if it fails to track the truth in all possible worlds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1c4c1ef9-ff19-4527-876b-ccb8f46ce994
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-tracking theory of knowledge](truth-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-bf2c0c4d-aad3-4dd0-ab9e-ac7c51e27bd7.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes they are eating real sugar but actually consume a sugar substitute while under ordinary conditions they accurately identify sugar. Intuitively, this belief fails to track the truth if considered across all possible worlds involving various substances.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires consistency in tracking truth, which this scenario violates.**
