# Is it plausible to maintain that basic beliefs can serve as the foundation for knowledge when they are unjustified in isolation from other beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c1b47c74-e72e-4d45-95de-784beaf32549
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario where the belief "This is a red apple" is considered basic. Intuitively, it seems to form a foundation for additional knowledge about the apple.**, **It is implausible that such a belief remains justified in isolation, as factors like lighting condition might reinterpret the belief without other supporting beliefs.**
