# Does this view adequately address cases of clairvoyant beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd417779-3eba-492d-92f9-282d2b9835f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person with reliably clairvoyant abilities forms the belief that a political leader has died without evidence. Intuitively, we would hesitate to claim the person knows this fact, despite the reliability of the process.**, **A principle within reliabilism is that a belief is justified if it results from a reliable cognitive process, leading to true beliefs.**, **The intuition in the clairvoyance case suggests that additional factors such as evidence and awareness of the reliability of the process are relevant for knowledge claims.**
