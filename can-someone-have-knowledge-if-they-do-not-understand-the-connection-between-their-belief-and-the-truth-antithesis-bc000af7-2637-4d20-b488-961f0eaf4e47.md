# Can someone have knowledge if they do not understand the connection between their belief and the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc000af7-2637-4d20-b488-961f0eaf4e47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable True Belief](knowledge-as-reliable-true-belief-synthesis-73884ab1-e6ad-49fe-9997-a4e027a9805c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a child believes that Paris is the capital of France simply because their parent told them so, with no understanding of geography or capitals. Intuitively, we either have to say that the child knows Paris is the capital or just believes it without knowing. If we say the child knows, then knowledge seems too easily acquired without comprehension. If we say the child just believes, then understanding plays a crucial role in constituting knowledge.**
