# Does phenomenalism adequately describe intersubjective agreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02f3376a-c98f-4080-90e3-14d2b87ab0af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Suppose people from different cultures identify the same physical phenomenon, such as the concept of gravity, through different experiences. Intuitively, there is agreement on a physical reality that transcends individual sensory experiences.**, **The notion that physical objects are logical constructions out of sensory experiences challenges the basis for intersubjective agreement.**
