# Does language extend beyond perceptual experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0392ffb-9652-462d-925c-ee299adcc812
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Conceptual Empiricism](conceptual-empiricism-synthesis-7b58485d-3ea5-4513-ba7e-4d4d19b630bf.md)

## Content

**Linguistic knowledge, such as understanding the meaning of words or sentences, seems attainable without perceptual experiences.**, **A person knows the proposition "Snow is white" through linguistic comprehension, which is not always tied to perceptual concepts.**, **External linguistic facts can be understood without solely relying on concepts generated by perceptual experiences.**
