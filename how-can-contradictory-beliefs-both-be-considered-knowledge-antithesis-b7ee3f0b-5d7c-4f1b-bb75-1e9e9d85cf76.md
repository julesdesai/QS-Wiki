# How can contradictory beliefs both be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7ee3f0b-5d7c-4f1b-bb75-1e9e9d85cf76
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals hold contradictory beliefs about a proposition, each claiming true knowledge based on their personal epistemic standpoints. Intuitively, both cannot be correct, as contradictions cannot both be true.**, **A plausible principle is that contradictory beliefs cannot simultaneously be true; hence, contradictory knowledge claims present a logical inconsistency.**, **If knowledge is true relative to the individual's standpoint and contradictions arise, it becomes implausible to sustain that both parties possess true knowledge.**
