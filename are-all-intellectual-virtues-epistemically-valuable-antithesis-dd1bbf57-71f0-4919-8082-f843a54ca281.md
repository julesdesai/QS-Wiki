# Are all intellectual virtues epistemically valuable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd1bbf57-71f0-4919-8082-f843a54ca281
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues are assumed to promote epistemic goods, but consider an example of open-mindedness. Intuitively, if excessively applied, open-mindedness can lead to the endorsement of untenable beliefs, suggesting not all virtues are epistemically valuable in every context.**, **A plausible principle is that intellectual virtues need contextual application to contribute positively, hence they are not inherently good.**
