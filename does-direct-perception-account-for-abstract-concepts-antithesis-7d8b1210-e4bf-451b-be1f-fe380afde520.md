## Content

**Abstract concepts, such as justice or mathematics, are not directly perceptible in the way physical objects are.**, **Knowledge of abstract concepts often involves inferential reasoning, which is not accounted for by direct perception.**, **Critical direct realism struggles to explain how abstract knowledge is acquired without direct perception.**
