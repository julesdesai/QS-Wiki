# Does coherence with a web ensure epistemic justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f75c15cd-2ea1-42b7-9e41-816e372633fa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Justified Belief](coherentism-in-justified-belief-synthesis-5c04c2a6-2653-47f8-81ab-a9d276d37a24.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a coherent set of beliefs that are systematically false, as their information is entirely mistaken due to a deceptive source. Intuitively, we might judge that these beliefs are not justified even though coherent.**, **A plausible principle is that epistemic justification requires truth-tracking. This suggests that coherence alone, absent of a connection to reality, is insufficient for justification.**
