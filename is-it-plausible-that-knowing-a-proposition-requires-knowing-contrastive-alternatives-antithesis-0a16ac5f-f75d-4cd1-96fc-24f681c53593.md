# Is it plausible that knowing a proposition requires knowing contrastive alternatives?

**Node ID:** 0a16ac5f-f75d-4cd1-96fc-24f681c53593
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition even when they cannot specify or are unaware of specific alternatives to contrast it with, because knowledge often precedes awareness of contrasts.**, **In everyday practical situations, a person claims knowledge without considering or being able to articulate contrasting alternatives, suggesting that knowing does not always involve contrastive reasoning.**
