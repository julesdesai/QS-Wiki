# Does changing the circumstances around a belief affect its safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6610a9d4-06c1-4308-a70f-b4389435fc84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person drives down a road with counterfeit barn facades, mistaking them for real barns based on reliable eyesight. The person stops at a real barn and says, “There is a barn.” In this scenario, intuitively, the belief seems unsafe because in nearby possible circumstances, the observer could have easily been wrong.**, **A plausible principle holds that knowledge cannot be sensitive to changes in circumstance that introduce error. This principle together with the barn façade scenario implies that if a belief could easily be false in similar contexts, the safety condition might not suffice for true knowledge.**
