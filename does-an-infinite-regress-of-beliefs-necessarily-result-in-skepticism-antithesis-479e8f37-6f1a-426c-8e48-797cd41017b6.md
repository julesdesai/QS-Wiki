# Does an infinite regress of beliefs necessarily result in skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 479e8f37-6f1a-426c-8e48-797cd41017b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism as a Solution to Regress](infinitism-as-a-solution-to-regress-synthesis-946c3e4e-7f7d-432b-920f-de29dcdcb154.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which every belief requires support from another belief, resulting in an infinite regress. Intuitively, we might think that without a terminating belief, there is no solid basis for knowledge, leading to skepticism.**, **It is a plausible principle that if knowledge has no solid basis, then skepticism follows logically. Thus, an infinite regress without a terminating belief logically entails skepticism.**
