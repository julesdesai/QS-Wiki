# Can intellectual virtue account for knowledge in all cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9553dd6-aa9d-4a8b-9bbc-629b55ea3c56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**A case might be considered where an individual reaches a true belief about the outcome of an experiment through rigorous scientific method yet the result was uncontrolled and coincidentally accurate. Intuitively, we either have to say that the method ensures knowledge despite the coincidence or not. If we say it ensures knowledge, then intellectual virtue might mask the role of accidental truths. If not, then virtue is insufficient alone for knowledge.**, **It is plausible that accidental truth, regardless of the exercise of intellectual virtue, challenges notions of knowledge purely based on intellectual virtues.**
