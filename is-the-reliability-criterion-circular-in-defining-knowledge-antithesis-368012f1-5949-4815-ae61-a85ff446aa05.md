# Is the reliability criterion circular in defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 368012f1-5949-4815-ae61-a85ff446aa05
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that a reliable process is defined by its tendency to produce true beliefs. This principle, however, requires knowledge of what is true to assess reliability, resulting in a circular dependency.**, **Reliability as a measure of knowledge presupposes prior knowledge of truth, making it potentially circular.**
