# Can epistemic luck lead to knowledge in cases of true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89707bd6-c299-4e95-aeb8-726769c519ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it will rain because they randomly heard it on a reliable news outlet, and it indeed rains. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know it will rain despite the belief being true.**, **It is plausible that knowledge cannot depend solely on true belief that is merely contingent and not grounded in the individual's rational deliberation or reasoning because it does not eliminate epistemic luck.**
