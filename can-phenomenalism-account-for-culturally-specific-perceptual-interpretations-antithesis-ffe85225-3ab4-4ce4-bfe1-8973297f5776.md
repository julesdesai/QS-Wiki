# Can phenomenalism account for culturally specific perceptual interpretations?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**A case in which two individuals from different cultures perceive colors differently due to linguistic distinctions is not a case in which perceptual experiences are coherent across different sensory modalities.**, **Suppose we have a situation where colors are described in unique terms within specific linguistic contexts, thereby influencing the way they are perceived. Intuitively, this suggests that perceptual experiences cannot be fully coherent across different sensory modalities due to cultural influence.**
