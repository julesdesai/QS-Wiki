# Does contextualism obscure the demarcation of knowledge from belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a568e60-e083-4212-8247-8d1e5944f33f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-56747199-fb08-45b9-8206-960c0717486b.md)

## Content

**Distinguishing knowledge from belief is crucial for clear epistemic evaluation.**, **If contexts can blur this distinction, it becomes difficult to differentiate knowledge from mere belief.**, **It is problematic if contextualism complicates the clear demarcation between knowledge and belief.**
