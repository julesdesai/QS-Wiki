# What about cases of "Gettier problems" where true beliefs arise from faulty reasoning?

## Content

**A person might have a true belief that is coincidentally true, such as finding a friend's house because they followed a wrong address but arrived there due to an unexpected event.**, **In such cases, the causal chain is flawed or coincidental, challenging whether true belief amounts to knowledge.**
