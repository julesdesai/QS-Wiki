# Is belief a sufficient condition for knowledge if lacking justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 07f213a7-35ac-4cbd-a726-d8e78dd777b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatically Useful True Belief](pragmatically-useful-true-belief-synthesis-e72da4db-a488-4cc6-b424-04d735c0cd87.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes they will fail an exam despite having studied thoroughly and having no evidence to predict failure.**, **While the belief itself could be subjected to practical implications, intuitively, without justification, it doesn't seem to qualify as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that belief needs to be justified to be considered knowledge, not merely true or practical.**
