# Can one know something without genuinely believing it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 149940bd-1a36-4ad9-b6d5-b278c258e27b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Knowledge without Belief](externalist-knowledge-without-belief-synthesis-c20b0285-a7bd-4197-bc3d-7153e215190d.md)

## Content

**Common linguistic practices suggest that knowing something implies believing it to be true.**, **Consider a scenario where a student accurately answers a question due to memory recall without seemingly believing the answer. Intuitively, we might question if this counts as genuine knowledge if the student does not sincerely believe it.**
