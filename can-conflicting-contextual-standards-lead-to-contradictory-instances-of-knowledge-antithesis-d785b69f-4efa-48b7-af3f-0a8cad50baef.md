# Can conflicting contextual standards lead to contradictory instances of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d785b69f-4efa-48b7-af3f-0a8cad50baef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Multi-Standard Contextualism](multi-standard-contextualism-synthesis-9865cf66-a535-4334-b5b4-390c78d28080.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals are in different contexts with conflicting standards for justification. Intuitively, one may know while the other does not, leading to a contradiction if one neither has stronger evidence nor reasons than the other.**, **If a person's context allows weaker justification than another context, then one person's knowledge claim might contradict another's according to their standards.**
