# Do humans always have access to the reliability of their beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d21b3d48-c539-4130-ad51-4f7ddb34a9cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires awareness of the reliability of the process by which it is formed. This principle together with cases where individuals are unaware of the statistical reliability of their cognitive processes implies a challenge to the notion that all justified beliefs are formed by reliable means.**, **Consider a person who is unaware that their memory is becoming unreliable with age, yet they believe all their memories are true. Intuitively, their confidence conflicts with the actual reliability of their memory.**
