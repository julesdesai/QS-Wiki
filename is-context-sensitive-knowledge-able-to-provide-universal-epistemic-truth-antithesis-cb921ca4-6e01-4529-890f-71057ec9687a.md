# Is context-sensitive knowledge able to provide universal epistemic truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cb921ca4-6e01-4529-890f-71057ec9687a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**The variability in the standards for knowing a proposition suggests that what is known can differ substantially across contexts.**, **If context dictates what is known, then knowledge lacks universal truth, instead shifting with situational standards.**, **In such systems, seeking universal epistemic truth becomes problematic as context-specific standards may not align.**
