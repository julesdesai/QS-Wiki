# Does contextualism undermine the idea of universal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b98a12c2-ad6c-4eb2-b65e-e8555cec4fc8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**Universal knowledge implies that some truths are constant regardless of context.**, **Contextualism suggests knowledge is contingent on specific situations, challenging universality.**, **If justification depends heavily on variable standards, it weakens claims of universally acknowledged truths.**
