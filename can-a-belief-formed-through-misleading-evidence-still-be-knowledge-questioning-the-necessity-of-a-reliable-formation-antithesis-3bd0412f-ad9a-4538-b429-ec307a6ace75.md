# Can a belief formed through misleading evidence still be knowledge, questioning the necessity of a reliable formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3bd0412f-ad9a-4538-b429-ec307a6ace75
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Justified True Belief with Safety](knowledge-as-justified-true-belief-with-safety-synthesis-dc9dc0c7-86d5-41bb-b0b1-297f9aa9f7b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective has strong but misleading evidence about a suspect but uses their instincts to correctly identify the real suspect. Intuitively, the detective knows who the perpetrator is.**, **For a belief to be formed reliably, it must not be based on misleading evidence, according to reliability as a necessary condition.**, **It is implausible to deny the detective knowledge when their instincts are correct, despite initial misleading evidence, challenging the necessity of reliable formation.**
