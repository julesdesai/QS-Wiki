# Can virtue epistemology distinguish between different sources of true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b613d531-fff7-48ed-8f81-8c56c2708b4c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**A true belief derived from testimony might lack intellectual virtue in the recipient, even if the testimony itself was produced virtuously.**, **A belief based on intellectual virtue might be overridden by competing motivations, suggesting varying degrees of epistemic reliability.**, **Discerning whether a belief results from virtue, chance, or other factors requires consideration beyond the mere presence of virtue.**
