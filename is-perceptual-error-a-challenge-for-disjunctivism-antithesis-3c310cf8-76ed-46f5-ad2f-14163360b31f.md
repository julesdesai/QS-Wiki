# Is perceptual error a challenge for disjunctivism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c310cf8-76ed-46f5-ad2f-14163360b31f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-19882849-157e-4dc6-b31d-66173a7c79c0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals observe a mirage in the desert; both have identical visual experiences yet only one perceives actual water. Intuitively, it seems implausible that the one perceiving the mirage is directly aware of reality.**, **It is plausible that identical perceptual experiences can exist even in cases of illusion. This challenges the idea that a direct relation to facts is involved in each perceptual experience.**
