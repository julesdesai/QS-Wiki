# Can acquiring a skill without intellectual virtues truly count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2d43b8d4-8f66-4935-82b2-4b0505890029
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone learns to perform a skill purely by imitation without understanding. Intuitively, we either have to say that skill performance alone constitutes knowledge, or that understanding is required. If we say skill alone constitutes knowledge, this undermines the involvement of intellectual virtues. If we claim understanding is required, then intellectual virtues must be exercised.**, **It is implausible that all skills necessarily engage intellectual virtues, especially when they can be learned reflexively or unconsciously.**
