# Can beliefs that lead to morally wrong actions still be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5aa059ba-cb56-4351-841e-5de6e2194330
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-c3735471-f546-42ec-8e21-f2451431b4e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition that leads to successful action but involves morally wrong consequences. Intuitively, we might question whether this still counts as knowledge**, **There is a plausible principle that moral permissibility should be a consideration in the evaluation of a belief as knowledge. This principle together with the successful practical outcome alone would not suffice for the definition of knowledge**
