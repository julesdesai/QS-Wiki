# Does the need for perception and memory automatically invalidate knowledge of future events?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1766a53f-b763-4462-932f-b281af16ffc4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Memory-Aided Justification](phenomenalism-with-memory-aided-justification-synthesis-6e19c0e0-afed-4e89-9fe3-1388eabe860e.md)

## Content

**Knowledge claims about future events often rely on predictions based on patterns rather than immediate perception or memory**, **Consider a case where a meteorologist knows it will rain tomorrow due to current climate models, but has no direct perception or memory of the weather event**, **The reliance on perception and memory does not naturally extend to future-oriented knowledge claims**
