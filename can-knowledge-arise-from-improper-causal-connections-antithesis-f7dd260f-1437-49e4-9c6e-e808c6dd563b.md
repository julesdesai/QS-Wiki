# Can knowledge arise from improper causal connections?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7dd260f-1437-49e4-9c6e-e808c6dd563b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a5ebdb75-c32b-47a3-8c6b-cdebc8ceca67.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a hiker believes there is water nearby due to a mirage, but there is actually water hidden behind a rock.**, **It is implausible that the hiker's belief qualifies as knowledge due to the misleading causal relation the mirage presents.**
