# Is a reliable cognitive process always sufficient for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2960cc7e-f16e-4465-8497-ece3fd1e39ae
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism without Belief](reliabilism-without-belief-synthesis-5ad6402e-0bf8-4ba4-9e47-6461ce29b289.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person correctly predicts the outcome of an event because of a cognitive bias that coincidentally leads to the right answer. Intuitively, we would either have to say that the person is justified in their belief, which seems false, or admit that reliability in itself is not sufficient for justification.**
