# Can knowledge be undermined if belief results from luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b92ac76f-483b-4e90-9951-1e4a243ee30a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1814de97-222c-437f-bd26-a9ce4edff4f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a true belief that tracks truth across situations, but the belief is acquired through a stroke of luck, such as guessing correctly. Intuitively, we might judge that the role of luck undermines the status of the belief as knowledge.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge cannot depend on mere luck for its acquisition.**
