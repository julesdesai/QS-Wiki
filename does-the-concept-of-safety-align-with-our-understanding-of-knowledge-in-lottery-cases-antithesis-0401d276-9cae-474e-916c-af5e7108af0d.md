# Does the concept of safety align with our understanding of knowledge in lottery cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0401d276-9cae-474e-916c-af5e7108af0d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-b5a72759-42a3-4d9b-b74c-df40c84bdc9d.md)

## Content

**Consider a lottery situation where a person believes the ticket they hold will lose. This belief is true, but not safe because there is a small chance of winning. Intuitively, we judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **A case involving a high-probability belief does not necessarily ensure that the belief is safe from error in most nearby possible worlds. Therefore, a probability doesn't always align with the safety requirement for knowledge.**
