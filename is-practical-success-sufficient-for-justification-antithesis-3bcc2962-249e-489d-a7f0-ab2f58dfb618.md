# Is practical success sufficient for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3bcc2962-249e-489d-a7f0-ab2f58dfb618
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person navigates a maze successfully by randomly choosing paths. Intuitively, it seems they do not have justified belief in the correct path, even though it led to practical success.**, **It is implausible that a belief formed randomly or without reason can be considered justified, despite being successful in practice.**
