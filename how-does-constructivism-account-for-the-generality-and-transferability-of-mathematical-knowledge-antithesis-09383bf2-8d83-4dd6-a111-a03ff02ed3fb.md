# How does Constructivism account for the generality and transferability of mathematical knowledge?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

## Content

**It is implausible that mathematical truths such as 2 + 2 = 4 depend on individual perceptual experiences since these truths are universally accepted regardless of personal knowledge or experiences.**, **A scenario where individuals from vastly different cultures, education levels, and cognitive processes independently arrive at the same mathematical conclusion supports the intuition that mathematical knowledge exists outside of any active organization or integration of sensory data with existing knowledge.**
