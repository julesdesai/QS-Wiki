# How does the indistinguishability of reliable and unreliable processes affect externalist claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99a333a6-d1d7-4f61-b4ce-9d56a1cf5886
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person uses what they perceive as a reliable process but cannot distinguish it from an unreliable alternative. Intuitively, the indistinguishability affects the basis for claiming knowledge.**, **A principle of knowledge suggests discriminability between reliable and unreliable methods should exist. If a person cannot make this distinction, the reliability claim becomes problematic.**
