# Can foundational beliefs truly vary based on context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d9d7dab-5f40-4374-810d-e7ea483b7f3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that foundational beliefs should remain consistent across different contexts to maintain epistemic stability. This principle together with the idea that foundational beliefs vary based on context entails that epistemic stability is compromised.**, **Epistemic stability is crucial for coherent and reliable knowledge acquisition and retention.**, **If foundational beliefs can change with context, then knowledge becomes less reliable and open to conflicting interpretations.**
