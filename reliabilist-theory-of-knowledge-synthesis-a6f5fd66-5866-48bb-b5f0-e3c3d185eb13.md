# Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge

**Parent:** [Can inadequate evidence sometimes support genuine belief?](can-inadequate-evidence-sometimes-support-genuine-belief-antithesis-caf93c8c-8ac9-4b78-9cdb-1cebc3b752f5.md)

## Content

**A belief constitutes knowledge if it is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **Reliability of a process is defined by its tendency to produce true beliefs over false ones**, **External factors, such as evidence, are less important than the reliability of the process in determining knowledge**, **Even if evidence is minimal, the reliability of the process can still ensure knowledge**
