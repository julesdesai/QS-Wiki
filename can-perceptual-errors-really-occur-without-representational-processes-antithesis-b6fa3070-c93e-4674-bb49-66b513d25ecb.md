# Can perceptual errors really occur without representational processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6fa3070-c93e-4674-bb49-66b513d25ecb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-42a15467-8636-4e99-a9b6-42a7b3d54195.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a mirage appears as a body of water in a desert. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is producing an error not directly related to the actual environment, hinting at the role of mental interpretations.**, **It is implausible that perception, as a direct connection without mental representation, could account for discrepancies between appearance and reality, such as those seen in mirages or illusions.**
