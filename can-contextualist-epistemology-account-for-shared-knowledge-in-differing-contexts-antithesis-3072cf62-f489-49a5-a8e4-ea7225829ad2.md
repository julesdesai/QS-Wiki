# Can contextualist epistemology account for shared knowledge in differing contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3072cf62-f489-49a5-a8e4-ea7225829ad2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people are in different contexts, one high-stakes and one low-stakes, but sharing the same evidence for a belief. Intuitively, it seems contradictory for one to know and the other not to know the same fact.**, **In shared conversational contexts, individuals should be able to align their knowledge claims without these claims diverging merely due to context.**, **If contextualist epistemology allows for such disparities, it may imply an inconsistency in our understanding of collective knowledge.**
