# Is the concept of invariant standards undermined by linguistic intuitions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 144a2678-9a01-41dc-9972-3d782d1403d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-0c8c051d-f89d-468b-87e6-c5a1fb54f910.md)

## Content

**There is an intuitive sense that language can affect our understanding of knowledge attribution, such as saying "She knew it was raining," which might reflect varying assertability conditions rather than steadfast knowledge standards.**, **A plausible principle is that the meanings of everyday assertions about knowledge may shift based on how the speaker and listener understand the context of the conversation. This implies speakers could adapt standards based on conversational implicatures.**, **Intuitively, when analyzing sentences that attribute knowledge, we oftentimes find our judgments swayed by contextual factors, suggesting that contextatsensitivity in ascriptions may indicate variable standards of knowledge rather than fixed ones.**
