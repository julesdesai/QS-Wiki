# Is it possible for knowledge to arise merely from cognitive faculties without being character-based?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 650b46b1-a85b-49d0-a490-9e84db155cc3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual possesses highly accurate cognitive faculties but lacks any particular character traits that guide them. Intuitively, we must say that they have knowledge due to their accuracy or deny them knowledge due to lack of virtue. If we say they have knowledge, then intellectual virtue as character is irrelevant; if we deny, it suggests an implausible restriction on knowledge.**
