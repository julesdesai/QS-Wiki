# Reliability Theory of Knowledge

**Depth:** 5

**Parent:** [Can an inaccurate causal link invalidate knowledge?](can-an-inaccurate-causal-link-invalidate-knowledge-antithesis-2aecbbea-9500-4fd1-9ec6-ed3f79c9fa36.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a justified belief to constitute knowledge, it is necessary that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **A cognitive process is reliable if it produces a high proportion of true beliefs over false beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**
