# Does reliabilism struggle with explaining the Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 434f8a13-1515-49f7-acbe-e3e5686b70cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Process-Oriented Justification](reliabilism-with-process-oriented-justification-synthesis-8900612b-46bb-42d6-a4c1-a983dd2da82b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees what looks like a sheep in a field and believes there is a sheep, but it is actually a dog with a sheep disguise; however, behind a hill, there is indeed a sheep. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks knowledge, despite having a justified true belief.**, **The belief-forming process, visual perception, is generally reliable, but in this scenario, it leads to a belief that is luckily true but not knowledge.**
