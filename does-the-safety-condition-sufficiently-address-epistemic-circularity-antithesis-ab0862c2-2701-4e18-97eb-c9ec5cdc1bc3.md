# Does the safety condition sufficiently address epistemic circularity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab0862c2-2701-4e18-97eb-c9ec5cdc1bc3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a belief formed on the basis of another belief that itself relies on the original belief, creating a circular dependency, yet it is true and safe from error**, **Intuitively, such circularity seems to preclude genuine knowledge because the reasoning does not provide an independent grounding**, **A belief's safety from error does not eliminate the circular nature of justification that undermines knowledge claims**
