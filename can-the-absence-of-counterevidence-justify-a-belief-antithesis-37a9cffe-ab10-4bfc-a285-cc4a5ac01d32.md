# Can the absence of counterevidence justify a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37a9cffe-ab10-4bfc-a285-cc4a5ac01d32
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d871be87-85c4-4634-87bb-b00a8ec22277.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person holds a belief because there is no known evidence against it. In this scenario, intuitively, we might judge the belief justified despite lacking positive evidence.**, **This notion challenges the requirement for positive evidence for justified belief, suggesting that absence of disconfirming evidence might suffice.**
