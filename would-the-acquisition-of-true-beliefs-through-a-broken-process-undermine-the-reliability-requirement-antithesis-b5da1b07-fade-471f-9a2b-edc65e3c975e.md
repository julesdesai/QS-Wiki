# Would the acquisition of true beliefs through a broken process undermine the reliability requirement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b5da1b07-fade-471f-9a2b-edc65e3c975e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person accidentally arrives at a true belief using an unreliable method, like guessing, that coincidentally hits upon the truth. Intuitively, we would not say that this person knows the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that distinguishes between mere true belief and knowledge, which is generally considered to involve more than chance. This principle, together with the reliance on an unreliable process, entails that the acquisition of knowledge cannot rely solely on the incidental truth of beliefs.**
