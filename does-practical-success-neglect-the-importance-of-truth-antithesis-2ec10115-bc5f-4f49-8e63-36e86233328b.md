# Does practical success neglect the importance of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ec10115-bc5f-4f49-8e63-36e86233328b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves having beliefs that are not only justified but also true. This principle conflicts with the idea that practical success alone could justify belief in cases where the belief isn't truth-conducive.**, **Suppose we have a case where an entrepreneur's belief in a business's future success is based on enthusiasm rather than market analysis. Intuitively, if the business succeeds, the belief's practical success does not imply justified knowledge due to the absence of a truth-conducive basis.**
