# Can belief based on inferential evidence lead to knowledge if the premises are false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93453773-d571-4a2b-8c12-2eade07b28dc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Evidence Theory](inferential-evidence-theory-synthesis-888e1fca-9103-4ede-a19f-61db12f550f6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a detective believes a suspect is guilty based on witness testimony. The testimony is false, but the suspect is guilty due to other reasons. Intuitively, despite relying on false information, knowledge seems absent because the belief isn't correctly sourced.**, **Inferential evidence involves reasoning from premises that are assumed to be true. If these assumptions are unwarranted or false, the resultant belief lacks a reliable basis and fails to constitute knowledge.**
