# Can someone have knowledge if their faculties work properly for an incorrect theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23c80e0f-5329-4818-bd84-d043cf7a5f48
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-56ac2890-cc65-4132-90ba-c28a7f97b59c.md)

## Content

**An individual can have cognitive faculties functioning properly yet hold a majority of false beliefs because they adhere to a fundamentally incorrect theory.**, **Even if cognitive faculties function properly according to some internal standard, external theories may lead to false beliefs posing challenges to knowledge classification.**, **This raises the question of whether proper functioning requires more than adherence to an internally coherent methodology.**
