# Is relying on practical success too permissive for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bfe63b3e-42df-44b1-9132-c99b39a02c0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should exclude beliefs formed on coincidental or accidental factors. If practical success is enough for justification, then we must consider coincidental factors sufficient, which seems excessively permissive and counterintuitive.**, **Consider a scenario where an individual wins a lottery by believing in a lucky number. Intuitively, such a belief seems unjustified as it relies on coincidence, yet practical success would wrongly suggest otherwise.**
