# Can knowledge result from coincidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2eb70060-ba09-450a-b849-b92bbd12d852
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where someone forms a true belief as a result of coincidence rather than intellectual virtue. Intuitively, we would be reluctant to say that they have knowledge since their true belief was not achieved through reliable cognitive processes.**, **A coincidence leading to a true belief challenges the claim that intellectual virtue, characterized by reliable belief-forming processes, is necessary for knowledge.**
