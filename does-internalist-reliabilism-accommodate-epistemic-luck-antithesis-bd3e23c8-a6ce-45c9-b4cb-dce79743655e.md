# Does internalist reliabilism accommodate epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd3e23c8-a6ce-45c9-b4cb-dce79743655e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Reliabilism Summary](internalist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-afd99030-664a-4a62-9431-6f8bc1e36717.md)

## Content

**A person might form a belief through a cognitive process they internally deem reliable yet arrive at a true belief by chance.**, **This scenario suggests the belief is still subject to epistemic luck, despite following internally justified methods.**, **Knowledge should exclude luck, challenging internalist reliabilism's adequacy in such cases.**
