# Can contextual foundationalism adequately distinguish between justified belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 501a96b9-f5cf-4045-9f07-aca078212005
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person has a justified belief based on contextually reliable foundational beliefs, but the belief is false. Intuitively, this seems to describe justified belief without knowledge.**, **It is implausible to assert that contextually reliable foundational beliefs alone can distinguish between justified belief and knowledge if the possibility of false beliefs remains unchecked.**
