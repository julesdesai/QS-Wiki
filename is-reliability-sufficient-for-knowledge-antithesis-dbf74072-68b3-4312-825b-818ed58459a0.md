# Is reliability sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dbf74072-68b3-4312-825b-818ed58459a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as No-Belief Tracking Theory](knowledge-as-no-belief-tracking-theory-synthesis-4c331db4-d170-461b-8235-d26613794630.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that reliability alone can suffice for knowledge in cases where external conditions skew outcomes, such as a legitimately talented detective whose conclusions are consistently made unreliable by sabotage, yet still produces correct conclusions.**, **Consider a scenario where someone has a reliable cognitive process, but due to pernicious circumstances beyond their control, they often arrive at false beliefs. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that they do not have knowledge even when they occasionally arrive at a true belief through their reliable process.**
