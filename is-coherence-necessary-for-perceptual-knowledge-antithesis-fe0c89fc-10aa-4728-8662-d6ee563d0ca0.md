# Is coherence necessary for perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe0c89fc-10aa-4728-8662-d6ee563d0ca0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-2365a190-e3fc-4d37-a83b-defd0eadffa2.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a sudden revelation or insight about the world without any prior coherent web of experiences. Intuitively, they might still claim knowledge despite a lack of coherence with prior experiences.**, **It is implausible that coherence is a strict necessity for knowledge if knowledge can be obtained in the absence of a consistent perceptual prehistory.**
