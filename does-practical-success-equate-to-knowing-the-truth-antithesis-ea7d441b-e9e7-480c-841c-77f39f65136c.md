# Does practical success equate to knowing the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ea7d441b-e9e7-480c-841c-77f39f65136c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person advises against investing in a stock due to a horoscope reading. The stock crashes, and the advice leads to practical success. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to claim this person knew the stock would crash.**, **It is implausible that actions based on superstitious or non-evidence-based reasoning result in true knowledge, even if they lead to successful outcomes.**
