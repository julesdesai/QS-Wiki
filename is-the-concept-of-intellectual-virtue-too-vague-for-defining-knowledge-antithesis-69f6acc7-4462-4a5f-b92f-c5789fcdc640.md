# Is the concept of intellectual virtue too vague for defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69f6acc7-4462-4a5f-b92f-c5789fcdc640
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Consider the implausibility that a concept as vague and context-sensitive as intellectual virtue can serve as a stable condition for knowledge across varied contexts. This implausibility suggests that vagueness undermines its reliability.**, **A principle exists that clear and precise conditions are preferable for defining knowledge. This principle implies that virtue, due to its vagueness, may not serve well alongside clearly identifiable conditions like truth and belief.**
