# Does shared belief entrench justified falsehoods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2fa0f061-d3f3-464f-ad55-228083ea6478
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a widely shared belief within a society turns out to be false, such as historical misconceptions. Intuitively, we either have to say that widespread belief justifies it or that it does not. If we say it justifies, then mistaken beliefs become entrenched as knowledge. If we say it does not, then mutual belief support cannot ensure justification.**, **It is implausible that mutual support among beliefs in a community guarantees true justification when the beliefs are grounded in errors.**
