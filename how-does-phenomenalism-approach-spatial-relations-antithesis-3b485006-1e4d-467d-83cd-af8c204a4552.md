# How does phenomenalism approach spatial relations?

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism suggests that physical objects are logical constructions of sensory experiences.](phenomenalism-suggests-that-physical-objects-are-logical-constructions-of-sensory-experiences-synthesis-70c8baf6-067c-48d6-b521-64b5ff284db6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the spatial relations between objects are independent of any individual sensory experience. This principle together with the claim that objects are logical constructions of sensory experiences raises questions about whether such spatial relations are illusory.**, **The apparent constancy and stability of geometric relations in the absence of perceivers intuitively suggest an objective framework not easily reducible to experiences.**
