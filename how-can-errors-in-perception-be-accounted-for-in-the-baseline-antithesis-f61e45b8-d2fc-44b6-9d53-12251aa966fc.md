# How can errors in perception be accounted for in the baseline?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f61e45b8-d2fc-44b6-9d53-12251aa966fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Baseline Phenomenalism](perceptual-baseline-phenomenalism-synthesis-c5e3ac41-9c0b-44e1-9f51-d5e2e022393a.md)

## Content

**Errors in perception, such as optical illusions or hallucinations, are known to occur.**, **In a case where two observers perceive different things when looking at the same optical illusion, their baseline experiences do not align.**, **If common perceptual conditions include errors, the reliability of the baseline for knowledge is compromised.**
