# Can contextual justification coexist with stable epistemic principles?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f9641843-3d88-463d-9d98-cdafe488d899
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for epistemic principles, like those determining knowledge, to be stable, they must not fluctuate with context.**, **Suppose we have an academic conference where a researcher's claim is scrutinized with high justification standards. This contrasts with casual sharing of the same research at a family dinner where lower standards suffice.**, **It is implausible to maintain stable epistemic principles if what justifies a claim shifts with context, leading to potentially multiple truths about what constitutes knowledge at any given time.**
