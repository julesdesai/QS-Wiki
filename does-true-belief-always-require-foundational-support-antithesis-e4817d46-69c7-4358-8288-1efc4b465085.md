# Does true belief always require foundational support?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4817d46-69c7-4358-8288-1efc4b465085
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person holds a true belief about a complex scientific theory through testimony from an expert, without understanding the foundational beliefs underlying the theory. Intuitively, we might question whether the belief qualifies as knowledge if it lacks foundational support directly accessible to the person.**, **A plausible principle is that for knowledge to be more than true belief, the person must adequately grasp or be able to trace the belief back to foundational beliefs. This principle challenges the notion that true belief alone is sufficient for knowledge unless it connects to foundational beliefs.**
