# Are there exceptions to context-sensitive standards without peripheral adaptation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e974247e-7be5-49cf-9f63-854ec6805ff4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Context-Sensitive Invariant Endorsement](context-sensitive-invariant-endorsement-synthesis-de3f576d-97e3-4b7e-b7cd-f089d4d156aa.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where Sam judges he knows the weather based on a reliable weather app. In private deliberation, this seems to align with core knowledge standards. However, when giving this information to a pilot responsible for passenger safety, the need for peripheral assurance arises, suggesting knowledge endorsement isn't just context-sensitive through peripheral standards.**
