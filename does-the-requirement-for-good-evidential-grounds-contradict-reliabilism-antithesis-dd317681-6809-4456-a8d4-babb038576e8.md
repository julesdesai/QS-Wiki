# Does the requirement for good evidential grounds contradict reliabilism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd317681-6809-4456-a8d4-babb038576e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism](evidentialist-reliabilism-synthesis-b2fd7651-b8ba-4bbd-aa9b-2c3c304a468b.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that beliefs must be formed on good evidential grounds to count as knowledge. This principle, together with the requirement for reliability, may lead to conflicts, such as when a belief is reliably formed but the evidence is otherwise poor.**, **This contradiction suggests that the requirement for good evidential grounds undermines the sufficiency of mere reliability for knowledge.**
