# Is it possible for a false belief to undermine the reliability of the cognitive process without discounting all of its truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b612c4af-7a15-4fcc-bd87-5428bf8bed13
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a series of beliefs formed by a process, most of which are true but one is false. We might intuitively judge that they mostly have knowledge, but the one false belief undermines the process's reliability.**, **A cognitive process is reliable only if it generally leads to true beliefs. One false belief can cause a challenge in maintaining the perception of reliability.**
