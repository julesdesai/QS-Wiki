# Does tracking theory conflict with cases of knowledge through testimony?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e107c69-37fb-46f6-bb00-578f05747c01
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-ba2ab1ae-08da-4d9d-a4db-0c45d3904537.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person gains true belief by trusting an expert's testimony, which would not change in nearby situations.**, **Intuitively, beliefs based on reliable testimony can constitute knowledge even if the truth is not tracked by the testifier.**, **The requirement for tracking might exclude some instances of testimonial knowledge.**
