# Can a true belief be accidental and not count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0feb16cc-e04c-4280-95c1-dd2fb71daa66
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Foundationalism](epistemic-foundationalism-synthesis-e90eff9d-4b30-41a2-b4e3-4bbc38385542.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition that turns out to be true purely by luck or coincidence. Intuitively, we have to say that this is not knowledge even though the belief is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that accidental truth does not constitute knowledge. This principle together with beliefs formed by mere chance entails that not all true beliefs are knowledge.**
