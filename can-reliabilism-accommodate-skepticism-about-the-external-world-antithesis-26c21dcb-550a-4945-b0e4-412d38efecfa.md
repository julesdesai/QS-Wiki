# Can reliabilism accommodate skepticism about the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 26c21dcb-550a-4945-b0e4-412d38efecfa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a reliable connection to the external world. This principle together with skepticism about whether we can ever be sure of such a connection entails that we may not be able to claim any knowledge about the external world.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person's sensory experiences are indistinguishable from those caused by an evil demon. Intuitively, we would be skeptical that the person knows anything about the external world.**, **It is implausible that reliabilism, which hinges on the reliability of processes tied to the external world, can address radical skepticism effectively.**
