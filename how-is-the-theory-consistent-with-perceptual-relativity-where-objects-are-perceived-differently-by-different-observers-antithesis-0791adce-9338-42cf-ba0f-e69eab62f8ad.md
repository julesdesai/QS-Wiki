# How is the theory consistent with perceptual relativity, where objects are perceived differently by different observers?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0791adce-9338-42cf-ba0f-e69eab62f8ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-2d3f5cf7-4de1-4047-bfb0-105e4b8ca669.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a red apple looks brighter in sunlight and duller in shade. Intuitively, we would judge that these are differing perceptions of the same object.**, **A plausible principle is that objects are perceived directly and consistently by all observers. This principle, together with cases of perceptual relativity, entails that the theory requires explanation for variations in perception.**
