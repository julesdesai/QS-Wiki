# Do contextually varying standards contradict the fixed nature of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 337b3a2c-02ef-4d1e-8d8b-d9ca96caa702
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that the standards for justifying knowledge can reflectively alter the static truth of a proposition since truth is not context-dependent.**, **If justification standards vary, it creates a situation where knowledge fluctuates even when truth remains constant, leading to a disconnect between truth and knowledge**
