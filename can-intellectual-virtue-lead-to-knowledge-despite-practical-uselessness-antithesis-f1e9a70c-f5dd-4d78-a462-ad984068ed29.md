# Can intellectual virtue lead to knowledge despite practical uselessness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1e9a70c-f5dd-4d78-a462-ad984068ed29
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a philosopher arrives at a true belief through careful reasoning about an abstruse topic that has no practical application. Intuitively, such careful reasoning and truth amount to knowledge despite the lack of practical utility.**, **There is a plausible principle that emphasizes the role of truth and intellectual virtue over utility in determining knowledge. This suggests that intellectual virtue can lead to knowledge even if the belief holds no practical utility.**
