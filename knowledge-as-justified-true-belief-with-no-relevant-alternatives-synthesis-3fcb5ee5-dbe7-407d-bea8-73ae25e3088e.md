# Knowledge as Justified True Belief with No Relevant Alternatives

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 3fcb5ee5-dbe7-407d-bea8-73ae25e3088e
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does computational or instrumental error preclude genuine knowledge, even if all criteria are seemingly met?](does-computational-or-instrumental-error-preclude-genuine-knowledge-even-if-all-criteria-are-seemingly-met-antithesis-e2f5f318-1796-4250-bfd1-f38307916112.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is justified**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that no relevant alternatives undermine the truth of the belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is justified, and no relevant alternatives undermine the truth of the belief**
