# Can one have knowledge merely by believing a true proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93a7b23e-b2c4-4350-8c7d-ce60976f78a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c88c0bac-fed4-4271-b530-0511020766f8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true proposition entirely by accident or coincidence. Intuitively, we would say that the person does not have knowledge of the proposition.**, **It is implausible that merely believing a true proposition without justification constitutes knowledge.**
