# Can unreliable beliefs be accidentally justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f2d5eec9-a734-4b8f-b3a2-677d79e92288
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism and the Role of Justification](reliabilism-and-the-role-of-justification-synthesis-e3c23741-1c3a-47b4-8f99-17f5f12eafda.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief through a typically unreliable process like guessing whether a coin flip will land heads or tails. If the belief is true for unrelated reasons, intuitively we would judge this as unjustified even if true.**, **A principle suggests that the reliability of the belief process must meet necessary thresholds of truth-conduciveness. This principle entails that beliefs from unreliable processes cannot legitimately be considered as justified knowledge even when accidentally true.**
