# Can perceptual errors undermine claims to a genuine connection to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 737f824b-0fcd-4a13-be58-e396e7e2086f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-90a3baa8-9102-43d8-b9cc-d77493986c7c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a bent straw in a glass of water and believes it is genuinely bent when, in reality, it is straight. In such a scenario, intuitively we would not judge that this person knows the straw is bent.**, **A scenario with perceptual errors calls into question whether a genuine connection to reality ensures the truth of a belief, as the person's perception is flawed.**
