# Does reliance on coherence of perceptions ignore the role of language?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f95b4a67-19c9-4223-957f-ea5819d148dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that understanding and communicating perceptions often require a shared language. This principle together with coherence of perceptions alone minimizes the essential role of language in structuring perceptions.**, **Consider a scenario where individuals from different cultures or languages perceive a phenomenon differently due to linguistic influences. Intuitively, coherence alone does not bridge this gap created by language diversity.**
