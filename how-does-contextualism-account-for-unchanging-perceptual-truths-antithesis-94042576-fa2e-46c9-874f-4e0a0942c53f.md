# How does contextualism account for unchanging perceptual truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 94042576-fa2e-46c9-874f-4e0a0942c53f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which certain perceptual truths, like the color of an apple, remain constant across varying contexts. Intuitively, we would judge that these truths should not vary based on context.**, **If the truth conditions for knowledge claims can vary with context, then this might entail that all perceptual truths are mutable, which contradicts the existence of invariant perceptual truths.**
