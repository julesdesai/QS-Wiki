# Does moderate phenomenalism ignore the role of memory in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dbd566ad-0e61-4031-bb00-42c00a4bc808
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Phenomenalism](moderate-phenomenalism-synthesis-66f89d49-d085-4cff-b6cd-a9e130c91f7b.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person recalls an event from the past with high fidelity due to personal memory. Intuitively, we judge that this person possesses knowledge of the event, even though the experience itself is no longer perceptually present.**, **A plausible principle is that memory plays a crucial role in the continuity of knowledge, bridging gaps between past experiences and present beliefs. This principle together with a reliance on only direct or indirect perception suggests a gap in accounting for stored knowledge.**
