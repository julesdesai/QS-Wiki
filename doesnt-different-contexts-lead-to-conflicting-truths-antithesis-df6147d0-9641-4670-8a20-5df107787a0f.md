# Doesn't different contexts lead to conflicting truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df6147d0-9641-4670-8a20-5df107787a0f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Contextualism](phenomenal-contextualism-synthesis-37be371b-8eaa-49b9-96b8-60f2ca6919b6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people, P1 and P2, have different perceptual contexts for the same object, such as a dress appearing blue to P1 and white to P2. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that both cannot have correct knowledge about the color of the dress.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be consistent and non-contradictory. This principle together with contextual variability in perception entails that different knowledge claims could conflict, undermining the idea that all knowledge is contextually valid.**
