# Can pragmatic justification lead to true belief but not knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3fc8449-0b10-47ad-bf41-07f6040b31b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Justification](pragmatic-theory-of-justification-synthesis-cd0f39d2-c604-4de4-bb81-1900aa8a1a8f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where a belief about the stock market is justified pragmatically by the resultant financial gain, but the person lacks insight into the market dynamics. This suggests pragmatic justification may lead to mere true belief, not knowledge.**, **Consider a gambler whose belief that a particular number will win is justified by the number historically winning, although the belief lacks a rational basis, indicating that pragmatic success isn't equal to understanding.**
