# Is intellectual character always a clear indicator of genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f60019d7-915a-489b-a3b1-8e829c4831f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that true indicators of knowledge involve more than character, such as aligning with established, objective criteria.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a seemingly virtuous agent believes a conspiracy theory due to confident reasoning processes, meeting none of the criteria accepted by the scientific method. Intuitively, their intellectual character does not indicate genuine knowledge.**
