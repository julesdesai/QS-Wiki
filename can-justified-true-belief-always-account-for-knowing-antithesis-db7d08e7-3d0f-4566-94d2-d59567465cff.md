# Can justified true belief always account for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db7d08e7-3d0f-4566-94d2-d59567465cff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person Peter believes a proposition because of misleading evidence, and it turns out to be accidentally true, is not a case in which Peter knows the proposition.**, **A person can have justification and a true belief without having knowledge if the correctness of the belief is due to luck rather than reliable evidence.**
