# Can there be knowledge without a causal narrative?

## Content

**Consider an ordinary case where a person believes weather predictions and the weather occurs as predicted. Even if the causal path is opaque, there is a sense in which we attribute knowledge for the belief in the accurate prediction.**, **For everyday decisions, causal narratives might be unclear, and yet the outcomes are associated with knowledge attributions by individuals.**
