# Is the role of intellectual virtues in justification overstated?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 07f930c7-6985-4db0-8702-e02aba69b722
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Coherentism](virtue-epistemology-coherentism-synthesis-fa375fb7-6e26-497f-b63d-bbaec5305ff8.md)

## Content

**The requirement that a belief is formed through the exercise of intellectual virtues could narrowly exclude some justified beliefs formed by reliable, yet non-virtuous procedures.**, **Reliable cognitive faculties or processes that do not involve virtue, such as basic perception, might still yield justified beliefs.**, **There is a question whether justified beliefs depending solely on secure and reliable methods, even if non-virtuous, are considered valid under this framework.**
