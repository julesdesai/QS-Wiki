# Is coherence sufficient for justification in the presence of skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6fd19200-b642-4613-a739-4e5af371c035
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-7bdcae0a-f633-4c38-9857-77d6ba49435c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person in a well-designed virtual reality believes they are in Paris based on a coherent set of sensory experiences. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not actually know they are in Paris despite their coherent beliefs.**, **It is implausible that having a coherent set of beliefs is sufficient for knowledge if those beliefs are based on fundamentally unreliable information.**
