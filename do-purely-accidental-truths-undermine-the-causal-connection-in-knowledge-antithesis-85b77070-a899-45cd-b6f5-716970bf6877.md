# Do purely accidental truths undermine the causal connection in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85b77070-a899-45cd-b6f5-716970bf6877
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-916d423a-1723-4911-b4ab-5dfda1d6916a.md)

## Content

**Suppose Danny accurately predicts a stock will rise due to misinformation from an unreliable source, yet his prediction turns true due to independent market factors. Intuitively, either we claim Danny knows the stock will rise, which questions strict causal links, or we consider it mere good fortune, challenging the necessity of causality for knowledge.**
