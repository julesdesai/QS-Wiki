# Can we rely on a process that never reaches a final conclusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 475153b0-aa31-4cc3-b641-d62c3f1d3aa3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-17c5b8c2-c7de-43ec-bf90-6dedae4ad235.md)

## Content

**For justification to be meaningful, there must be some possibility of reaching a satisfactory conclusion.**, **If reasons extend infinitely without an endpoint, it suggests that conclusions are perpetually deferred.**, **It is implausible that a process deferring conclusions can provide the kind of immediate justification we expect in rational belief.**
