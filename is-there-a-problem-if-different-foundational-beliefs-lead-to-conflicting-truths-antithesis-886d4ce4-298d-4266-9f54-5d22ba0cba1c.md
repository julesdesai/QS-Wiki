# Is there a problem if different foundational beliefs lead to conflicting truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 886d4ce4-298d-4266-9f54-5d22ba0cba1c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Reliable Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-reliable-basic-beliefs-synthesis-62bc80a9-ef75-4d49-9578-a29890370bd2.md)

## Content

**A foundational belief is one that does not derive justification from other beliefs.**, **It is possible for different foundational beliefs to appear equally justified yet lead to conflicting propositions.**, **If two conflicting foundational beliefs are both considered valid bases for knowledge, there must be a rule to adjudicate between them, but foundationalism provides none.**
