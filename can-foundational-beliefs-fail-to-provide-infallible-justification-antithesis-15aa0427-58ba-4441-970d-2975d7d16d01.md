# Can foundational beliefs fail to provide infallible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 15aa0427-58ba-4441-970d-2975d7d16d01
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a belief is foundational, it should provide infallible justification for derived beliefs.**, **Foundational beliefs can sometimes lead to errors if they are, for instance, based on perceptual data that can be misleading.**, **If a foundational belief can be mistaken, then it does not provide infallible justification for subsequent beliefs.**
