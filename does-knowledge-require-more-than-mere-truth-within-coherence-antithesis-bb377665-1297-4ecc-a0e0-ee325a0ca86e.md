# Does knowledge require more than mere truth within coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bb377665-1297-4ecc-a0e0-ee325a0ca86e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Reliability Summary](coherentist-reliability-summary-synthesis-49034568-193c-4a88-8ae7-b062de898be5.md)

## Content

**A person believes a true proposition, and it fits into their coherent set. However, the way they arrived at this belief was unreliable or accidental. Intuitively, this does not seem to constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible to claim that accidental truths within a coherent set are genuine knowledge.**
