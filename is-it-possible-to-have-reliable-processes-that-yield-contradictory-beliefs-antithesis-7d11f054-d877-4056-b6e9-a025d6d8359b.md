# Is it possible to have reliable processes that yield contradictory beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7d11f054-d877-4056-b6e9-a025d6d8359b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Suppose two observers using different but equally reliable processes form contradictory true beliefs about the same event. Intuitively, we would be reluctant to ascribe knowledge to both.**, **A reasonable principle is that knowledge cannot accommodate simultaneous contradictory beliefs even if each results from a reliable process.**
