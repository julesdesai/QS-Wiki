# Are all perceptions reliable for constituting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e02d22b0-0216-4554-a059-900f52b89b84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that not all perceptions are reliable due to optical illusions or misleading senses. This principle together with the unreliable nature of perceptions entails that perceptions alone are insufficient for constituting knowledge.**, **Suppose a person perceives a bent stick in water where the stick actually remains straight. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the stick is bent, which is false, or that perception can be misleading.**
