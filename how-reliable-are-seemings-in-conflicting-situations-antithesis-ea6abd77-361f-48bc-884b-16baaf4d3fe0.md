# How reliable are 'seemings' in conflicting situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ea6abd77-361f-48bc-884b-16baaf4d3fe0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Conservatism supports knowledge based on the immediate 'seeming' of experiences.](phenomenal-conservatism-supports-knowledge-based-on-the-immediate-seeming-of-experiences-synthesis-043a7de6-376c-4af5-aacf-49b8c8b72c50.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two people experience the same event; one person has the intuitive seeming that it was enjoyable, while the other person, feeling negative emotions, has the opposite seeming that it was dreadful. Intuitively, both conflicting seemings cannot be equally justified in supporting the knowledge of the experience's nature.**
