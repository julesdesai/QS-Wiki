# Does knowing require understanding why the belief is reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72a4a86f-9f79-4353-bf2a-d61e0328248a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that understanding the reasons why a belief is reliable is necessary for knowledge. This principle together with a case in which a person has evidence to believe their belief is reliable but does not understand the reasoning entails that they do not fully possess knowledge.**, **Suppose a student correctly answers a math problem by rote application of a formula without understanding why the formula works. In such a case, intuitively, the student does not know the solution even though the belief about the correct answer is reliable and true.**
