# Might context lead to relativism in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 698955f0-98a0-48a2-ad8f-944dd1a55d36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Virtue Contextualism](externalist-virtue-contextualism-synthesis-69e2f760-46ad-465f-a31e-67e150de071d.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is true belief arising from intellectual virtue as evaluated within context implies that knowledge depends on contextual evaluation.**, **Contextual evaluation that varies with situation might lead to different conclusions about a knowledge claim in different contexts.**, **If different contexts lead to different conclusions about the same claim, this might suggest a form of epistemic relativism.**
