# Does the subject's awareness of stakes impact knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2809903a-b054-4be4-a14d-1c51a8303202
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-1a7f6ba4-b667-492e-9754-ad3a76a8b666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a subject remains unaware of the heightened practical stakes associated with a proposition that they believe. Intuitively, whether they know the proposition seems unaffected if they remain unaware of the stakes change. This suggests that knowledge attributions may not be sensitive to external stakes beyond the subject's awareness.**, **A plausible principle would be that a person's knowledge cannot be affected by factors of which they have no awareness, thus calling into question the claim that external stakes inherently modify knowledge.**
