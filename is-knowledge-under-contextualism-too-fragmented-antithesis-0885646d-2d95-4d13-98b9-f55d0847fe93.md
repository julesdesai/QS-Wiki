# Is knowledge under contextualism too fragmented?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0885646d-2d95-4d13-98b9-f55d0847fe93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where the context creates highly specialized and localized knowledge claims. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that knowledge becomes fragmented and potentially disconnected from a broader understanding of truth.**, **It is implausible that knowledge should be so fragmented as to limit cross-contextual understanding and communication.**
