# Do pragmatic successes always equate to true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c502838-2c79-4bc4-b340-fac527fe4f3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-c3735471-f546-42ec-8e21-f2451431b4e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person acts on a false belief that coincidentally results in a successful outcome purely by chance. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief is still false despite the success of the action**, **A belief's truth should not be determined solely by its ability to lead to successful outcomes, as success might occur despite the belief's falsehood**
