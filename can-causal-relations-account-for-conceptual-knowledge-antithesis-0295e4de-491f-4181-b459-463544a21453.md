# Can causal relations account for conceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0295e4de-491f-4181-b459-463544a21453
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be based on causal connections with truths, but conceptual knowledge, like understanding justice, typically lacks direct causal links.**, **Consider a lawyer's understanding of justice; intuitively, this knowledge arises from conceptual analysis rather than direct causal interactions with an objective truth.**
