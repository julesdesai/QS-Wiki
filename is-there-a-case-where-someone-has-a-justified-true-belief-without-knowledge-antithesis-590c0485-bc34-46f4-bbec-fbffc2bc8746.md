# Is there a case where someone has a justified true belief without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 590c0485-bc34-46f4-bbec-fbffc2bc8746
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-90a3baa8-9102-43d8-b9cc-d77493986c7c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is in a room with a broken clock that always shows 12 PM and happens to glance at it exactly at 12 PM, forming the true belief that it is 12 PM. Intuitively, we would not say this person knows the time, despite having a true and justified belief.**, **Situations where a justified true belief does not seem to amount to knowledge challenge the idea that a genuine connection to reality ensures the truth of the proposition.**
