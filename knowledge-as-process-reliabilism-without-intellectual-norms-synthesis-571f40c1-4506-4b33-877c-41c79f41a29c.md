# Knowledge as Process Reliabilism without Intellectual Norms

**Parent:** [Is intellectual virtue always necessary for explaining knowledge of self-evident truths?](is-intellectual-virtue-always-necessary-for-explaining-knowledge-of-self-evident-truths-antithesis-8c59e35d-5ba0-4f37-9aca-decea099d2ab.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from a reliable process without invoking intellectual virtues**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief arises from a reliable process without invoking intellectual virtues**
