# Is sensitivity to counterfactuals necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 106abc10-bfd9-4574-b570-b72dab85ec66
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a belief must not only be true and reliably produced, but must also be sensitive, meaning if the proposition were false, the belief would not be held. This principle clashes with process reliabilism which depends only on generic reliability.**, **Suppose Sally's belief that her friend is home is true because the friend happens to be there, yet if her friend were next door, she would still believe she was home. Intuitively, we judge she doesn't truly know her friend is home.**, **The lack of sensitivity to these counterfactual conditions challenges the adequacy of only needing a reliable process for knowledge.**
