# Does the introduction of a defeater always nullify knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6c17178a-b2a8-4e1d-b5d5-8f9a67007850
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Defeaters Theory of Knowledge](no-defeaters-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-0be8e529-9437-4b4e-96a3-c585180c1210.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a well-justified true belief which is later challenged by misleading but convincing defeaters. Intuitively, we might expect the person still knew the proposition initially.**, **Knowledge should not be contingent solely on the absence of defeaters if the justification and truth were sufficient at the outset.**
