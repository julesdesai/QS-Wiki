# Can knowledge arise from luck despite intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0ffc27a-2883-4cf2-84a5-1409838ae0fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-f60d76d9-bfb9-483c-b262-fa52975172be.md)

## Content

**Imagine a detective who has the intellectual virtue of thoroughness, leading him to solve a case through sheer coincidence rather than evidence.**, **Even if the belief is true and arises from virtue, the role of luck indicates that knowledge may require more than just intellectual virtue involvement.**
