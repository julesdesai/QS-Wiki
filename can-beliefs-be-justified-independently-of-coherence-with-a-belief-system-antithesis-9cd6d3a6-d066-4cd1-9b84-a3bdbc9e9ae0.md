# Can beliefs be justified independently of coherence with a belief system?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9cd6d3a6-d066-4cd1-9b84-a3bdbc9e9ae0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a belief can be justified through evidence independently of its coherence with a wider system of beliefs. This principle suggests that justification can be found in direct experiential evidence, rather than solely through relational coherence.**, **Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on clear and distinct perceptual experience, such as seeing a bird in front of them. Intuitively, we would judge this belief to be justified even if it stands in isolation from other beliefs.**
