# Can belief without proper evidence response constitute knowledge despite reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 117d85fe-f858-4a84-9fb8-c28e4d98f699
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Evidence Sensitivity](process-reliabilism-with-evidence-sensitivity-synthesis-e3e15d29-f0bc-4d87-b0aa-fbeab510c604.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual has a belief produced by a generally reliable process but the belief-forming process fails to properly respond to the available evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **The belief-forming process must respond properly to available evidence for the belief to be considered knowledge.**
