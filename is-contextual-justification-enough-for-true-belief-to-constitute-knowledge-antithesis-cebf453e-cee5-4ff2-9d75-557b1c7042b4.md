# Is contextual justification enough for true belief to constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cebf453e-cee5-4ff2-9d75-557b1c7042b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextual Pragmatism](knowledge-as-contextual-pragmatism-synthesis-58b0d4c4-4eeb-46a9-99af-766901c4c5df.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes a true proposition based on evidence that is contextually justified but weak would not lead us to intuitively judge that they have knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person believes that a coin will land heads because they have a justified belief that it's slightly weighted towards heads. The belief is contextually justified, but intuitively, it may not seem like the person knows it will land heads.**
