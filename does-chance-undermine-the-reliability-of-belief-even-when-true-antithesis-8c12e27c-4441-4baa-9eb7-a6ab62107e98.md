# Does chance undermine the reliability of belief even when true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c12e27c-4441-4baa-9eb7-a6ab62107e98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-73062a2b-1357-42ba-b901-d397b26dce96.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person's belief turns out to be true purely by chance, as in accurately guessing the outcome of a complicated event. We intuitively recognize that the truth was not reliably produced and thus not known.**, **Consider a scenario where a dice roll yields a true belief about the next outcome but the belief could just as easily have been false despite appearing reliable. Intuitively, this belief does not constitute knowledge.**
