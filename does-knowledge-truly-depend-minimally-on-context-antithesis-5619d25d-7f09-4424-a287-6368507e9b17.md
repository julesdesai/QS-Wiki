# Does knowledge truly depend minimally on context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5619d25d-7f09-4424-a287-6368507e9b17
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-b49d8dfc-4ae9-4058-b817-2db750a2772b.md)

## Content

**A case where a person believes a proposition it is going to rain and communicates this shortly before a weather broadcast predicting clear skies shows that such belief falls short of knowledge due to the shifting understanding of evidence.**, **If knowledge depends minimally on context, then external factors such as social cues or environmental changes would not affect the status of knowing, which is implausible.**, **Consider a meeting scenario where the assurance given by a manager significantly influences how confidently the employees assert knowledge, suggesting context heavily impacts knowledge assessments.**
