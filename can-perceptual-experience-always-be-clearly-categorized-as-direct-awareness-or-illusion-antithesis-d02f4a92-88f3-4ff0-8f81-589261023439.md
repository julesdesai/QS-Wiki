# Can perceptual experience always be clearly categorized as direct awareness or illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d02f4a92-88f3-4ff0-8f81-589261023439
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-ef0ab88b-769a-4e6f-afc6-1fb31c926a76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person experiences an object in low light conditions where the object's features are not clearly discernible. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that it is difficult to categorize the experience as either direct awareness or an illusion.**, **A plausible principle states that if categorizations are mandatory, then clear criteria must exist for such categorizations. The lack of clear criteria for differentiating between direct awareness and illusion challenges this principle.**, **It is implausible to hold that all perceptual experiences can sharply be classified as direct awareness or illusion, given the complex nature of intermediate cases.**
