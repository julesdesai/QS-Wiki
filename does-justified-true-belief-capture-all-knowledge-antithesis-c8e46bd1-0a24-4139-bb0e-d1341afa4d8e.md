# Does Justified True Belief capture all knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8e46bd1-0a24-4139-bb0e-d1341afa4d8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology combines knowledge with intellectual virtues](virtue-epistemology-combines-knowledge-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-5b32e3fe-f322-4af8-b790-236786e70562.md)

## Content

**In certain scenarios, an individual may have justified true belief without exercising intellectual virtue; consider someone who uses circular reasoning that incidentally aligns with the truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should involve more than mere coincidence of justification and truth, as when someone expects rain solely based on dark clouds and happens to be correct without understanding meteorology.**, **It is implausible that all cases of justified true belief qualify as knowledge if they disregard the role of intellectual virtues.**
