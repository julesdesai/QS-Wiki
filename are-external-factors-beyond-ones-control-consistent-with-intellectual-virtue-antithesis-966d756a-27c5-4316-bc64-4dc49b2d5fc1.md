# Are external factors beyond one's control consistent with intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 966d756a-27c5-4316-bc64-4dc49b2d5fc1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a belief formed from intellectual virtue, but its truth is contingent upon factors entirely outside one's control, such as environmental conditions. Intuitively, the belief may still count as knowledge, despite external uncontrollability contributing to its truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that external uncontrollable factors can impact a belief's truth without disqualifying it as knowledge. This principle together with the denial of luck-based truth implies a contradiction concerning external dependencies.**
