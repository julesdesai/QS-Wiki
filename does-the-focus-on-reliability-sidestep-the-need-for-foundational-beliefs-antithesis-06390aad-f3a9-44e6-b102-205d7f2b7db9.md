# Does the focus on reliability sidestep the need for foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06390aad-f3a9-44e6-b102-205d7f2b7db9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where reliability focuses on outcomes rather than the basis of beliefs. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that foundational beliefs are still necessary to justify the reliability claim.**, **There is a plausible principle that foundational beliefs provide the necessary grounding for reliability assessments. This principle together with the need for a foundation entails that reliability does not eliminate the requirement for foundational beliefs.**
