# Is causal interaction enough for knowing external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ab48c23-85ec-454d-9b1b-0557a247cdd4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structuralism](epistemic-structuralism-synthesis-7fb538c7-d6b1-4a5d-b302-4f53eea0d56a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person gains knowledge due to reliable, but unconscious, causal interactions with the environment, such as detecting weather changes based on subtle environmental cues. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows about upcoming weather without explicitly identifying perceptual patterns, challenging the view that knowledge solely depends on perceptual structures**
