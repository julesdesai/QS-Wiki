# Do differences in perceptual abilities across species undermine the universality of direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9f74f151-bd3b-4707-90c7-49abbb47ce3d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a human and a bat perceive their environment differently due to species-specific sensory adaptations, like echolocation for bats. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is tailored to species-specific needs rather than providing a direct and universal access to reality.**, **It is implausible that if perception provides direct access, it should vary so significantly across different species, suggesting that perception is rather specialized and mediated by biological factors.**
