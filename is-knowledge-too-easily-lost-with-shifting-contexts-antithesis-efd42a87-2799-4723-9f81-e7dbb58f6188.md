# Is knowledge too easily lost with shifting contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** efd42a87-2799-4723-9f81-e7dbb58f6188
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism With Context-Dependent Justification Standards](contextualism-with-context-dependent-justification-standards-synthesis-a8126985-f485-4c51-b545-16d8a583ea1e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows a fact in a casual conversation. When the conversation shifts to a more scrutinizing academic context, the person no longer knows the same fact because of stricter justification standards. Intuitively, it is troubling to think that mere conversational shifts can strip knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be gained or lost merely due to conversational context shifts without any change in the evidence available to the person.**
