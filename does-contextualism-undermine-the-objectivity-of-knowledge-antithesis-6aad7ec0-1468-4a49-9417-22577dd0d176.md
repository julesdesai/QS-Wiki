# Does contextualism undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6aad7ec0-1468-4a49-9417-22577dd0d176
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two people disagree about whether a person knows a proposition because they are operating in different contexts. Intuitively, it is troubling to say that both can be right, as this could undermine the idea that knowledge is objective.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, as an important epistemic concept, lacks objectivity across different contexts since knowledge typically involves objective truth.**
