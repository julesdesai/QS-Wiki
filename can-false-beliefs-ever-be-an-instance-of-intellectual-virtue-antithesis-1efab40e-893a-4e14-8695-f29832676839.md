# Can false beliefs ever be an instance of intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1efab40e-893a-4e14-8695-f29832676839
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief that is false through the use of significant cognitive abilities aimed at truth, such as logical reasoning and thorough investigation. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person has exhibited intellectual virtue even though the belief is false.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtue should be aligned with the demonstration of cognitive excellence, regardless of the truth of the resulting belief. This principle suggests that intellectual virtue might sometimes result in false beliefs, challenging the requirement that knowledge necessitates the truth of the proposition.**
