# How does tracking theory handle cases of coincidental correctness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1164d10-0145-4438-b52f-35181fcc2f34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-ba2ab1ae-08da-4d9d-a4db-0c45d3904537.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes it will rain because they interpret a random pattern of leaves as an omen and it does indeed rain is not a case where the belief tracks the truth.**, **Coincidental correctness in a belief does not ensure that the belief would remain true in similar situations.**, **Intuitively, such coincidental correctness feels more like luck than knowledge.**
