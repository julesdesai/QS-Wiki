# Can one have knowledge if their coherent set includes only one reliable belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1df73ace-9a89-4cb8-917f-76d4d8fd13fd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Reliability Summary](coherentist-reliability-summary-synthesis-49034568-193c-4a88-8ae7-b062de898be5.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person has many beliefs, but only one of those beliefs is based on a reliable method and the rest aren’t. Intuitively, the presence of one reliable belief does not constitute grounds for knowledge in this set.**
