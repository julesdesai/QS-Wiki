# Does Contextualism face a problem with unexpected shifts in context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 49b5ea4c-04ce-4990-8931-ad416039b626
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-ee61dbb1-ab63-492e-a056-b4bb19752825.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person switches from one context to another without being aware of the change in standards.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should not be easily disrupted by unnoticed contextual shifts.**, **Intuitively, if a person unknowingly loses knowledge due to an unrecognized context shift, it seems problematic for maintaining stable knowledge claims.**
