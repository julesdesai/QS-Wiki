# Is knowledge limited if it only pertains to perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9e092db-b8f5-4841-888d-e9e2bd1214b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a mathematician claims to know a complex theorem that has no perceptual counterpart. Intuitively, we would judge that this knowledge exists independently of sensory experiences and is valid.**, **It is implausible that knowledge is exclusively of perceptual experiences, since abstract and theoretical knowledge seem to be valid forms of understanding that do not rely on direct perception.**
