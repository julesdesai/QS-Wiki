# Is knowledge really context-dependent if two people with different standards both claim to be correct?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c236c502-ea31-496d-b586-a70e329fe350
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Consider two individuals discussing a proposition under different contextual standards for knowledge, where one claims to know the proposition based on relaxed standards and the other denies knowledge based on stricter standards. Intuitively, both claims cannot simultaneously be true under common understanding of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if two people claim contradictory knowledge status of the same proposition, at least one must be mistaken. This principle together with the idea that knowledge is context-dependent leads to tension in our understanding of truth in knowledge claims.**
