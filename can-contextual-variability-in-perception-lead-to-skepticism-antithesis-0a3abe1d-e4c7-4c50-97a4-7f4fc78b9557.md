# Can contextual variability in perception lead to skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0a3abe1d-e4c7-4c50-97a4-7f4fc78b9557
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**If truth conditions and perceptions can vary greatly depending on context, this could potentially lead to skepticism regarding the possibility of acquiring certain knowledge. Skepticism entails doubting the certainty of knowledge on the basis of perceived variability or instability.**, **Consider a case where contextual influences drastically alter the outcome of sensory perception, leading one to question the validity of their immediate sensory experience. Intuitively, we would judge this scenario as a potential source for skeptical doubt.**
