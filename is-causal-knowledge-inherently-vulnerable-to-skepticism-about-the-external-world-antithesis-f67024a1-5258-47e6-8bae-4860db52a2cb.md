# Is causal knowledge inherently vulnerable to skepticism about the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f67024a1-5258-47e6-8bae-4860db52a2cb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a person P forms a belief about the external world, such as seeing a red apple on the table. Intuitively, this seems like plausible knowledge.**, **Given that causal connections assume the existence of an external world, radical skepticism (which questions the existence of such a world) might challenge the validity of ascribing knowledge, thus making causal knowledge potentially unreliable.**
