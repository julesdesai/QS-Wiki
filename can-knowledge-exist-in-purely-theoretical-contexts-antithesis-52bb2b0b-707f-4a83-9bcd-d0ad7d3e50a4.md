# Can knowledge exist in purely theoretical contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52bb2b0b-707f-4a83-9bcd-d0ad7d3e50a4
**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Pragmatically Useful True Belief](pragmatically-useful-true-belief-synthesis-e72da4db-a488-4cc6-b424-04d735c0cd87.md)

## Content

**A mathematician proves a complex theorem with no foreseeable practical application, yet the theorem is true and believed.**, **The situation raises the notion that purely abstract knowledge, though practically irrelevant, is still knowledge and challenges practical implication necessity.**, **It is implausible that the absence of direct practical application negates the status of knowledge for theoretical propositions.**
