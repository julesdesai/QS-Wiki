# What if knowledge requires both truth and understanding of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 682c112a-82d1-41fa-a3b7-1738ecd6aee8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**Imagine a student correctly answers a difficult question on an exam based solely on an educated guess.**, **Intuitively, one might argue that true knowledge should involve understanding how or why something is the case.**, **The notion that guessing without understanding disrupts the concept of knowledge emphasizes a perceived need for comprehension in addition to mere truth.**
