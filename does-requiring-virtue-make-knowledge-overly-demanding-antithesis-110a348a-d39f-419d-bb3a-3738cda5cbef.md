# Does requiring virtue make knowledge overly demanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 110a348a-d39f-419d-bb3a-3738cda5cbef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the requirement of knowledge must be accessible to ordinary understandings, which may not involve knowing about intellectual virtues. This principle raises concerns about making knowledge too demanding with the virtue requirement.**, **Consider ordinary cases where people claim to know propositions based on straightforward observation or testimony without any notion of intellectual virtues. Intuitively, these instances seem to be genuine knowledge, even without invoking virtue.**
