# How do we address the burden of proof in an infinite chain of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d1966b1-908d-4677-98e1-28547ba68f1f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Foundational Beliefs](infinitism-with-foundational-beliefs-synthesis-4273f090-f358-492e-9e7c-904c2a039ab2.md)

## Content

**The burden of proof requires justification to provide adequate reasons supporting a belief or proposition.**, **In a scenario involving an infinite chain of reasons, the process may never complete, making justification perpetually deferred.**, **It is implausible that an infinite series, never reaching a conclusion, can meet the burden of proof for justification.**
