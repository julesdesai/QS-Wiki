# Does the necessity for justification challenge the notion of infallible foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bec43687-7c84-453f-9c1c-c6720709fee1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification cannot be infallible if based on potentially flawed sensory or introspective beliefs.**, **Justification sometimes involves inferential processes from foundational beliefs that could be mistaken.**, **If foundational beliefs require justification, then they cannot be considered infallible and self-justifying.**
