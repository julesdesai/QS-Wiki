# Is there a logical contradiction in expecting infinite justification from finite creatures?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6610c786-ac3d-44de-88c5-2e24bee836c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-fd5e1271-f2df-4333-a434-50b9d95350fd.md)

## Content

**Finite beings cannot fulfill infinite requirements, such as providing infinite justification for beliefs.**, **If justification demands an infinite chain, a logical contradiction arises because human beings, as finite entities, cannot produce or understand infinite justifications.**, **It is implausible to reconcile the finite nature of human cognition with the necessity for an infinite series of reasons.**
