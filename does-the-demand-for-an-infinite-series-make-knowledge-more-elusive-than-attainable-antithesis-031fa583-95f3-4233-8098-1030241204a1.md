# Does the demand for an infinite series make knowledge more elusive than attainable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 031fa583-95f3-4233-8098-1030241204a1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-fd5e1271-f2df-4333-a434-50b9d95350fd.md)

## Content

**The requirement for an infinite series of reasons elevates the standard for knowledge beyond practical attainability.**, **It is implausible that any human belief could satisfy such an extensive requirement, thereby making actual knowledge unreachable.**, **If knowledge necessitates an infinite chain of justification, then we lack justification for claiming knowledge of anything in practice.**
