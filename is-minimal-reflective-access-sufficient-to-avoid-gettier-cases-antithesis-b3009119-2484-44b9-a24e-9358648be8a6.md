# Is minimal reflective access sufficient to avoid Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3009119-2484-44b9-a24e-9358648be8a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Minimalist Internalism](minimalist-internalism-synthesis-c1aff540-8ee4-4dca-921e-260ac4a2c333.md)

## Content

**Consider a classic Gettier case where an individual has justified true belief due to luck rather than reliable reflective access. Intuitively, minimal reflective access alone doesn’t prevent such cases.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge must exclude luck in justification.**, **Reliance on minimal access may still subject beliefs to epistemic luck in the form of Gettier-style problems.**
