**Suppose the surrounding context in which a person has a veridical experience changes the meaning or understanding of the experience. Intuitively, we would say that without the appropriate context, the experience itself wouldn't provide knowledge.**, **Context-dependence suggests that mere matching of reality by a veridical perception does not automatically generate knowledge of a proposition.**
