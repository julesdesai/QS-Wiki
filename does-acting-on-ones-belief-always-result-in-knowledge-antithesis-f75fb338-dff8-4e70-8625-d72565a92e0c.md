# Does acting on one's belief always result in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f75fb338-dff8-4e70-8625-d72565a92e0c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c88c0bac-fed4-4271-b530-0511020766f8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person acts on a belief that coincidentally results in a beneficial outcome, although the belief itself was based on weak evidence. Intuitively, it seems inaccurate to consider this situation as one of knowledge.**, **Knowledge should involve justified true belief, and coincidental beneficial outcomes conflict with the notion of justification.**
