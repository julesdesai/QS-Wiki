# Can testimony truly extend knowledge beyond personal perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 613753c5-7fd5-41c0-a51c-c5c87b30d5f1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Phenomenalism](testimonial-phenomenalism-synthesis-3205e188-94ee-499a-b528-9152e3586bc6.md)

## Content

**There are scenarios where individuals have acted on reliable testimony that later turned out to be false. Intuitively, such cases challenge the claim that reliable testimony extends knowledge.**, **Suppose a case where everyone in a community believes in a historical event due to testimony, but later archaeological evidence shows the event never happened. Intuitively, the community's belief does not constitute knowledge.**
