# Does the tracking theory exclude some instances of knowledge where belief persists due to alternate reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8be089b1-86d3-43d4-935e-2147ba09c16c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone continues to hold a belief even in the presence of contrary evidence due to stubbornness or cognitive bias. The belief might still be true, and intuitively, we might consider this belief to sometimes be knowledge.**, **Persisting in belief due to personal traits does not necessarily invalidate the potential for knowledge in cases where the belief happens to track the truth accurately despite external evidence to the contrary.**
