# Must intellectual virtues directly produce the belief to qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5281bed3-0582-4d69-bc95-5a1e007751e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b6aa4282-ad09-4174-b191-6cfd3fcbdf83.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person whose intellectual virtues inspire another person to relay information, leading to a true belief in the first person**, **It seems plausible that the belief can be considered knowledge even if the virtue indirectly, rather than directly, led to it**
