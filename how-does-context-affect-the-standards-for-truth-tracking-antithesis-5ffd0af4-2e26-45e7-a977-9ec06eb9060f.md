# How does context affect the standards for truth-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ffd0af4-2e26-45e7-a977-9ec06eb9060f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where the truth-tracking required for knowing a complex scientific theory in an academic context is higher than for a mundane fact in everyday life, suggesting that standards for knowledge should pragmatically adapt to context. Intuitively, this variability questions the claim of consistent standards across contexts.**, **A principle acknowledging the practical differences in contextual knowledge expectations points to the limitations of truth-tracking theory in maintaining uniformity.**
