# Do character traits relate directly to epistemic reliability?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliance on Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-reliance-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-e0dc7230-c9bb-46c6-a298-46d61cd678d8.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues are defined as character traits promoting epistemic goods, yet it is implausible that character alone determines reliability. External factors might play a significant role in reliability.**, **Suppose an honest person consistently relies on flawed sources. Intuitively, despite their honest character, their epistemic reliability might be compromised which questions if character equates with reliability.**
