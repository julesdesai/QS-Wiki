# Can relativism account for cross-framework communication and understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 425ee429-ddc7-49d5-8bf3-cf18822bee44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2665e075-79d4-4adb-9371-55cb5a5794f8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people from different cultural backgrounds debate a historical event. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that mutual understanding requires some common epistemic standards.**, **A plausible principle is that successful communication requires both parties to agree on some objective criteria for truth. This principle together with relativism's denial of universal standards entails the impossibility of resolving disagreements across frameworks.**
