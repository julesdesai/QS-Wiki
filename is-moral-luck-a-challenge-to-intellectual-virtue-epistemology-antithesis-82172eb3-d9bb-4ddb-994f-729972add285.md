# Is moral luck a challenge to intellectual virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 82172eb3-d9bb-4ddb-994f-729972add285
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Approach](virtue-epistemology-approach-synthesis-b14f243d-0108-46b0-abbb-b26bd31de791.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual succeeds in forming true beliefs due to external conditions beyond their control, akin to moral luck. Intuitively, this external dependence seems to mitigate claims of knowledge.**, **It is implausible that reliance on external conditions aligns with the conception of intellectual virtue as solely leading to knowledge.**
