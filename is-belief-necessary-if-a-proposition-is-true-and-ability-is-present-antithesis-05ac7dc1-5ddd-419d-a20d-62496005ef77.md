# Is belief necessary if a proposition is true and ability is present?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05ac7dc1-5ddd-419d-a20d-62496005ef77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dispositional Account of Knowledge](dispositional-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-697a3559-6974-4820-bdde-37c51f6f9430.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has both the dispositional ability to form true beliefs and the proposition is true, but the person doesn't actually form the belief. Intuitively, we either have to say the person knows the proposition or not knowing due to absence of belief. If we say they know, it challenges the notion that belief is necessary. If we say they don't, it suggests that mere disposition and truth aren't enough**
