# How do different foundational beliefs interact when contexts overlap?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84a1ec77-3ef5-4cc2-9a55-8152f0fcb93f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where two different contexts overlap, and each relies on conflicting foundational beliefs. Intuitively, we either have to say that one context's foundational beliefs take precedence or that both should coexist. If we say one takes precedence, then the foundational belief of the other context becomes unjustified. If we say both coexist, then it leads to epistemic conflict and inconsistency.**, **The possibility of overlapping contexts with conflicting foundational beliefs creates conditions for incoherence in knowledge systems.**, **Incoherence among foundational beliefs undermines the integrity of knowledge claims.**
