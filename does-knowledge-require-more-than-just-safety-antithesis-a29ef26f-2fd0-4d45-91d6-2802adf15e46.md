# Does knowledge require more than just safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a29ef26f-2fd0-4d45-91d6-2802adf15e46
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a true proposition based on a horoscope reading, which is coincidentally accurate, and nearby possible worlds are consistent with this accuracy. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows based on safety alone or that they do not know because of the epistemic method used. If we say they know based on safety alone, then such beliefs count as knowledge, which seems counterintuitive. If we say they do not know due to the epistemic method, then knowledge requires more than safety.**
