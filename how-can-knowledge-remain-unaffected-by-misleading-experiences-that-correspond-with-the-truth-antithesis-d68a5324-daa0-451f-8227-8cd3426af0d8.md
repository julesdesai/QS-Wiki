# How can knowledge remain unaffected by misleading experiences that correspond with the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d68a5324-daa0-451f-8227-8cd3426af0d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**Envision a case where a person believes they are seeing a deer in the forest and they are indeed seeing a deer, but their experience is partly influenced by misleading visual phenomena like reflections.**, **Intuitively, even if the belief is true, the misleading aspect introduces uncertainty in knowledge that merely correct outcomes cannot resolve.**, **Misleading experiences, even if accidentally true, create tension with the notion of straightforward knowledge through reliable processes.**
