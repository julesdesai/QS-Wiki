# Can perceptual illusions undermine the dependency of reality on perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05729f52-d49e-4c09-94cb-503a8467c8da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-ab29b70f-4a42-463b-b303-b38517d8aa22.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if perceptual experiences are fallible, then they cannot entirely determine reality. This principle together with the occurrence of perceptual illusions implies that reality is not solely dependent on perceptual experience.**, **Consider the experience of a mirage, where water seems to be on the road. Intuitively, we understand the water is not really there despite the perceptual experience suggesting otherwise.**
