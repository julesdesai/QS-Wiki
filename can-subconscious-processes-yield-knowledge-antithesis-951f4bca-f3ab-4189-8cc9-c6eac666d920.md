## Content

**Consider a case where an individual intuitively navigates complex social situations successfully without conscious reasoning, leading others to affirm they have social knowledge. Intuitively, we might witness knowledge attribution despite the lack of explicit reasoning or belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can arise from subconscious or intuitive faculties as much as from deliberate cognitive processes.**
