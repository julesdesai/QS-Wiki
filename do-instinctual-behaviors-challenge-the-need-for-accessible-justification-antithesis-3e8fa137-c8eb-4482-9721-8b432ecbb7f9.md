# Do instinctual behaviors challenge the need for accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e8fa137-c8eb-4482-9721-8b432ecbb7f9
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalism](internalism-thesis-697c012e-ac77-4c5b-935c-0bf2d4d5ce83.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person successfully avoids danger through instinctual behavior in a crisis. Intuitively, this indicates knowledge of the threat even though justification is not accessible to the person.**, **A plausible principle is that instinctual knowledge or immediate responses to environmental cues do not require conscious access to justification, challenging the necessity for accessible justification.**
