# Can deeply-held but false beliefs coexist with truth-tracking in a way that affects knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 38abebc4-a47e-4619-ab4c-d0de9fb7a9b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1814de97-222c-437f-bd26-a9ce4edff4f7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person holds a true belief that tracks the truth and simultaneously maintains deeply-held false beliefs that influence their understanding of the true belief. Intuitively, the presence of such false beliefs could undermine our attribution of knowledge.**, **A plausible principle holds that coexisting false beliefs can contaminate the framework necessary for acquiring untainted knowledge.**
