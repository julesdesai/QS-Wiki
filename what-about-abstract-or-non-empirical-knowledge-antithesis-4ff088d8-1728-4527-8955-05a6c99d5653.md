# What about abstract or non-empirical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4ff088d8-1728-4527-8955-05a6c99d5653
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that some knowledge is abstract or non-empirical, such as mathematical truths. This principle together with knowledge not necessarily involving sensory experiences implies that sensory representation is not needed for all knowledge types.**, **Consider a scenario in which a mathematician knows a theorem without involving any sensory experience; intuitively, such knowledge does not involve sensory representation of reality.**
