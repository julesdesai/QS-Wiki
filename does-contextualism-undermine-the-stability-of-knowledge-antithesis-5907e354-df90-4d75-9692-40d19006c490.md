# Does contextualism undermine the stability of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5907e354-df90-4d75-9692-40d19006c490
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-b0409110-d832-4fdb-9fe3-2e01026d1f88.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a belief justified by one context is invalidated by a slight contextual change. Intuitively, this suggests a vulnerability in knowledge stability.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain stable across minor contextual variations. This principle together with the situation suggests contextualism might undermine knowledge stability.**
