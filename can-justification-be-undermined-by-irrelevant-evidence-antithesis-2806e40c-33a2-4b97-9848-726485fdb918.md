# Can justification be undermined by irrelevant evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2806e40c-33a2-4b97-9848-726485fdb918
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone reads a false review claiming a movie is bad and decides not to see it. However, other trusted reviews praised it and eventually lead to a decision to watch it. Intuitively, the initial belief based on the false review was unjustified due to the presence of contrary evidence.**, **A plausible principle is that irrelevant or contradictory evidence can undermine belief justification, which complicates the idea that justification strictly aligns with available evidence.**
