# Does the dependency on cognitive and perceptual processes make knowledge provisional?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7569b56e-db5d-429e-997a-b96d7bb9e594
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-3b2a41b4-2333-4928-b6a1-340c94708483.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that if knowledge is mediated by cognitive and perceptual processes, it is susceptible to revisions, making it provisional rather than absolute**, **Consider the evolution in scientific theories, such as the shift from Newtonian to quantum mechanics, revealing provisionality in knowledge claims about reality**, **A plausible principle is that if knowledge is mediated and subject to cognitive error, then it cannot be considered final or definitive in its correspondence to reality**
