# Is stability in perception defeating immediate sensory knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c7ad5cc4-37ec-4b60-ae76-0a81fd06b0b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Imagine an individual who knows it is raining by hearing rain on the roof but lacks a stable history of rain perceptions over time. Intuitively, the immediate auditory evidence should suffice for knowledge despite the absence of stable perceptual history.**, **There is a plausible principle that supports immediate sensory evidence as a sufficient source of knowledge, indicating that requiring stability might unnecessarily discount valuable immediate information.**
