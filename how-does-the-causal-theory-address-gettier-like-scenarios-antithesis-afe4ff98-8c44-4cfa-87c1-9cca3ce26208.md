# How does the causal theory address Gettier-like scenarios?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone has a justified belief that a person owns a Ford based on solid evidence, and that person indeed owns a Ford, but the evidence was completely coincidental. Intuitively, this scenario suggests that the person lacks knowledge despite the true belief.**, **It is implausible that coincidental connections, even when causal, secure the status of knowledge in all justified true belief cases.**
