# Does Reliabilism accommodate the presence of necessary external factors known to the subject?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** edc515e3-8adf-4410-a805-100beb53fc46
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-24b421a0-08f3-4c66-b0fb-2acc7f5da514.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms beliefs using a reliable cognitive process which depends on external environmental factors of which they are unaware. Intuitively, we might want the subject to recognize at least some of these factors for the belief to count as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that awareness of relevant external conditions should affect whether a belief produced by a process counts as knowledge. This principle suggests reliabilism might overlook the importance of these elements.**
