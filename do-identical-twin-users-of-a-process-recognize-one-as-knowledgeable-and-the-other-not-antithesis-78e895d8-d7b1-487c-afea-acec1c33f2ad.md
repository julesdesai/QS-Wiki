# Do identical twin users of a process recognize one as knowledgeable and the other not?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 78e895d8-d7b1-487c-afea-acec1c33f2ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Approach to Knowledge](reliabilism-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-96775f82-86aa-4964-9f14-0e86944bf355.md)

## Content

**Imagine that one twin forms true beliefs using a reliable process while the other twin, unbeknownst to themselves, uses an unreliable process to form the same beliefs. Intuitively, only the first twin's beliefs constitute knowledge.**, **Two individuals can hold similar true beliefs, but the difference in reliability of their processes affects whether those beliefs qualify as knowledge.**
