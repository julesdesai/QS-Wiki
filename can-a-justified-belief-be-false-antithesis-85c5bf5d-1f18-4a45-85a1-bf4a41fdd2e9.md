# Can a justified belief be false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85c5bf5d-1f18-4a45-85a1-bf4a41fdd2e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a belief is justified if it is produced by a reliable cognitive process. This principle together with a scenario in which a reliable news source reports incorrect information entails that a person can have a justified belief that is false.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a false proposition reported by a usually reliable source. Intuitively, we might have to say the belief is unjustified, challenging the notion of reliability guaranteeing justification.**
