# Could true belief arise without intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abd78196-3073-4c69-8ab8-077ade9ce200
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Meta-Virtue Epistemology](meta-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b3bd54fd-8ed8-46f5-92c6-aa5fa4222616.md)

## Content

**A case in which some person has true beliefs due to luck or chance rather than through any intellectual virtue is a case in which, intuitively, they still might be seen as knowing, thus challenging the necessity of virtue, first-order or meta-cognitive, for knowledge.**
