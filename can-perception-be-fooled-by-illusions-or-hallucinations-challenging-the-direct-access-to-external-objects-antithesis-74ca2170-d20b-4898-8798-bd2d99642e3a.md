# Can perception be fooled by illusions or hallucinations, challenging the direct access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74ca2170-d20b-4898-8798-bd2d99642e3a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a straight stick that appears bent when partially submerged in water. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception does not accurately capture the external object.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person hallucinates a pink elephant in the room. Intuitively, either we have to say that the elephant is there, or that perception can be inaccurate. If we say the elephant is there, this contradicts our understanding of reality. If we say perception can be inaccurate, this challenges the claim of direct access through perception.**
