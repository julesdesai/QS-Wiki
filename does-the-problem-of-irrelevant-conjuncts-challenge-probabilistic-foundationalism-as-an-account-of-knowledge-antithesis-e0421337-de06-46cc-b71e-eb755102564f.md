# Does the problem of irrelevant conjuncts challenge probabilistic foundationalism as an account of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e0421337-de06-46cc-b71e-eb755102564f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-16fe8fa2-69bc-4186-a85e-e3861ba3ba16.md)

## Content

**A belief that a person will win a lottery based on statistical understanding is intuitively not knowledge even when it turns out true.**, **In a scenario where a belief is probabilistically justified and true, the addition of irrelevant conjunctions, like "and today is Tuesday," doesn't detract from probability but seems to intuitively prevent knowledge.**, **The presence of irrelevant information can undermine our intuitive sense of having true, justified understanding even with high probability justification.**
