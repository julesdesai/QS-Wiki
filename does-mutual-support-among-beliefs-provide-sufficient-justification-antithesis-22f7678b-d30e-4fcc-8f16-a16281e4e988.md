# Does mutual support among beliefs provide sufficient justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22f7678b-d30e-4fcc-8f16-a16281e4e988
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which mutually supportive beliefs are based on false premises, such as a community's shared false memories. Intuitively, we either have to say that these beliefs are justified or that they are not justified. If we say they are justified, then all false beliefs are potentially justified. If we say they are not justified, then mutual support is insufficient for justification.**, **It is implausible that mutual support alone guarantees justification because beliefs could consistently reinforce each other's errors.**
