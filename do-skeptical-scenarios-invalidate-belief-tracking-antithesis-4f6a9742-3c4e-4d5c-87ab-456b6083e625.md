# Do skeptical scenarios invalidate belief-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f6a9742-3c4e-4d5c-87ab-456b6083e625
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3749df9e-e568-414c-88ec-cc6b23580530.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is systematically deceived, such as being in a simulation where they hold true beliefs. Intuitively, we would judge that their beliefs do not constitute knowledge, despite meeting the tracking condition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires beliefs that are robust against alternative possibilities. This principle, combined with skeptical scenarios, suggests that truth-tracking may not be sufficient for knowledge.**
