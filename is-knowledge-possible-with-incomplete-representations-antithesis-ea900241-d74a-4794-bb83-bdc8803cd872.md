# Is knowledge possible with incomplete representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ea900241-d74a-4794-bb83-bdc8803cd872
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a historian knows a historical fact through incomplete archaeological evidence that still points conclusively to a particular event. Intuitively, we might say the historian knows the event occurred.**, **It is implausible that only complete representations provide knowledge, as incomplete yet indicative evidence often leads to valid knowledge claims.**
