# Does causal theory handle cases of forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 508d83a0-07de-4888-8187-ece3bc8e1141
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge requires retaining evidence, as one can remember knowing a fact without recalling the specific evidence that caused the belief.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person remembers knowing their birthdate but has forgotten the source. Intuitively, this remains knowledge without an active causal link to the evidence.**
