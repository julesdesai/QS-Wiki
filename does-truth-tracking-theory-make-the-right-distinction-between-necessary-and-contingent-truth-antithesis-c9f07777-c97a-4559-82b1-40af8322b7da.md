# Does truth-tracking theory make the right distinction between necessary and contingent truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9f07777-c97a-4559-82b1-40af8322b7da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who knows a contingent truth about a specific event. In such a scenario, intuitively, truth-tracking is relevant for distinguishing knowledge of contingent truths but not for necessary truths.**, **It is implausible that a theory which lumps together necessary and contingent truths under one condition accurately sculpts the intuitive understanding of knowledge.**
