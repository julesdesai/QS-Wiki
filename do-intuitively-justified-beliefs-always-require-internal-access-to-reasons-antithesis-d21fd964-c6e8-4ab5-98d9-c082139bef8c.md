# Do intuitively justified beliefs always require internal access to reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d21fd964-c6e8-4ab5-98d9-c082139bef8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Justification Theory](internalist-justification-theory-synthesis-60885d02-ba8c-49f7-a8ac-6cdf9db1e5a8.md)

## Content

**A person has a strong intuitive belief that a mathematical solution is correct but cannot access the steps they used to derive it.**, **The belief is justified based on past patterns and experience, even though current access to the justification process is absent.**, **Internal access to reasoning seems unnecessary for the belief to be justified in this instance.**
