# Can perception be fallible in direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 858270c8-2f89-414e-8880-f27fdbf21cb9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Direct Realism](critical-direct-realism-synthesis-3a3a192c-9b76-44ad-9069-1225c99b4079.md)

## Content

**In some cases, human perception can be mistaken due to optical illusions, leading to a false belief about the proposition perceived.**, **Direct realism requires that perception is infallible for it to serve as a basis for knowledge.**, **Optical illusions demonstrate that perception can be unreliable, challenging the claim that perception, as required by direct realism, always provides a secure foundation for knowledge.**
