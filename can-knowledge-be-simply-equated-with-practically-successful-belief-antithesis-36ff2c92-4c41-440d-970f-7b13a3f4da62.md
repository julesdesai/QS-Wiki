# Can knowledge be simply equated with practically successful belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36ff2c92-4c41-440d-970f-7b13a3f4da62
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just belief that works in practice. This principle, together with the idea that practical success can be incidental or accidental, entails that knowledge must require a more robust connection to truth.**, **Consider a scenario where someone picks a winning lottery ticket based on a gut feeling. Intuitively, it seems mistaken to say the person knew they would win, even though their belief led to the successful outcome of winning.**
