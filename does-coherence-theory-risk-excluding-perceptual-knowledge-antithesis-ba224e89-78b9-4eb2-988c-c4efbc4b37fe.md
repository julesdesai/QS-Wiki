# Does coherence theory risk excluding perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ba224e89-78b9-4eb2-988c-c4efbc4b37fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person perceives an object directly, forming beliefs based on this perception. Intuitively, the immediacy of perceptual knowledge does not always involve coherence with pre-existing beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that some knowledge is direct and does not require coherence for initial justification.**, **Failure to account for the directness of perceptual knowledge suggests limitations in coherence theory's explanatory scope.**
