# Does knowledge always involve an intellectual virtue if propositional foundations seem absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a49d918a-b729-46a3-a873-6f9179e723d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires at least some form of propositional foundation. This principle together with the view that knowledge is not solely based on propositional foundations implies that there can be knowledge without any propositional grounding.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone possesses a skill due to muscle memory but cannot articulate the process or reason behind it. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not fully know what they are doing in an epistemic sense even if they can perform the skill.**
