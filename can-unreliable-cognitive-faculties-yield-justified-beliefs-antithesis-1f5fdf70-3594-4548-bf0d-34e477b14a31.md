# Can unreliable cognitive faculties yield justified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f5fdf70-3594-4548-bf0d-34e477b14a31
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be justified, it must be produced by reliable cognitive faculties or habits. This principle suggests that if cognitive faculties are unreliable, the belief cannot be considered justified, regardless of its truth.**, **Consider a scenario where a person's cognitive faculties are reliable only in one-off cases due to luck or coincidence; intuitively, beliefs arising in such cases are not justified. Thus, justified belief depends on consistent reliability of cognitive faculties.**
