# Can we have immediate knowledge without any cognitive processing following perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 759a1ba3-da58-4456-bbd0-5f0d5046ee0a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves some form of cognitive processing to interpret perceptual data. This principle together with the notion that perception alone delivers knowledge without such processes results in a contradiction with the cognitive involvement needed for knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which someone sees a series of numbers without interpreting them. Intuitively, they do not have knowledge of a meaningful sequence without cognitive interpretation or processing.**
