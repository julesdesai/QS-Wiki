# Do independently varying standards undermine the uniformity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fcd57f3-d95f-443e-99d6-e24994498e90
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Multi-Standard Contextualism](multi-standard-contextualism-synthesis-9865cf66-a535-4334-b5b4-390c78d28080.md)

## Content

**Consider a theoretical intuition that knowledge should be uniform across different standards to maintain its objective nature.**, **If standards for belief, truth, and justification vary independently, a proposition considered known under one set of contextual conditions may not hold under another, which could threaten the idea of a uniform and coherent conception of knowledge.**
