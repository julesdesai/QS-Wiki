# Can a person know something by coincidence according to the tracking theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6eb1b19f-e34b-4f1b-8ade-ab1f7c0b4858
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dda35d00-5b10-4342-b443-f197350806e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it will rain because they saw a rain-dance ceremony, and it coincidentally rains. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know it will rain despite their belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just accidental truth of belief. This principle together with a belief coincidentally being true entails that the belief falls short of knowledge.**
