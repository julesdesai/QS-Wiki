# Externalist Justification with No Causal Emphasis

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 542540e2-6a20-4c01-93cb-a1beb638fc58
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can false but systematically correlated beliefs be known?](can-false-but-systematically-correlated-beliefs-be-known-antithesis-0fe34563-ff19-4029-b42d-0e2894579847.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the reliable process need not include a causal connection to the fact**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief arises from a reliable process without causal emphasis**
