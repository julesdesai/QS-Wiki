# Does functionalism adequately address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 323e190f-8e24-47b2-9f17-aeb2290904af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functionalism](functionalism-synthesis-2d0849d8-6d8d-49c5-822f-95e70d5e82e6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person's mental states functionally align with reality due to coincidentally correct reasoning without justification. Intuitively, we either have to say that this person knows the proposition or that they do not. If we say this person knows the proposition, it contradicts the need for justification. If we say they do not, it challenges the view that mental states functional correspond to reality as defining knowledge.**
