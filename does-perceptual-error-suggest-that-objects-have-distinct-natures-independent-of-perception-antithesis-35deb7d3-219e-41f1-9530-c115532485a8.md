# Does perceptual error suggest that objects have distinct natures independent of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35deb7d3-219e-41f1-9530-c115532485a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism Summary](transcendental-idealism-summary-synthesis-6a7150cf-600b-4ee3-8215-3d235222eec9.md)

## Content

**Perceptual errors, like illusions or hallucinations, highlight the difference between perceived phenomena and the actual nature of objects.**, **When perception is demonstrably false, it suggests that objects have properties independent of their perception.**, **The persistence of misconceptions after errors are identified implies that the true nature of objects does not depend on subjective perception.**
