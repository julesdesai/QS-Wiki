# Can justified beliefs fail to constitute knowledge if they are false?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3429f620-2b4c-455d-8480-ece30b883c2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-498bb613-4448-44e8-9d2e-1c93717bc9f2.md)

## Content

**A person can have justification for believing a false proposition, such as having strong evidence that later turns out to be misleading.**, **A justified false belief cannot be considered knowledge because knowledge requires truth.**
