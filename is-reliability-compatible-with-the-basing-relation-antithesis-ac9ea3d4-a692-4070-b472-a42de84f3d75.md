# Is reliability compatible with the basing relation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac9ea3d4-a692-4070-b472-a42de84f3d75
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle suggests that for a person to have knowledge, their belief must be based on the grounds or reasons that actually justify it, known as the "basing relation". This principle challenges models of reliability that might support belief formation without appropriate justification linkage.**, **Suppose we imagine a scenario in which a person's belief in climate change is accurate, not from scientific evidence, but purely through political alignment. Intuitively, this disconnect in justification questions whether belief reliability suffices without a proper basing relation.**
