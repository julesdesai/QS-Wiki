# Can two people have justified opposing beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 535c39cb-e3e3-46c1-ae6e-bd8e08841c53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people witness an event from different angles, each with evidence leading them to mutually exclusive beliefs. Intuitively, we may judge that both can be justified in holding opposing beliefs, despite the contradiction.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification is perspectival, depending on evidence accessible to individuals. This principle with evidence variance suggests opposing justified beliefs can exist.**
