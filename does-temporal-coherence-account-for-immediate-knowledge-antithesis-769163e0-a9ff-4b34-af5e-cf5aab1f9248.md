# Does temporal coherence account for immediate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 769163e0-a9ff-4b34-af5e-cf5aab1f9248
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Temporal Coherentism](temporal-coherentism-synthesis-f4dfebfd-2f02-4b67-a5dd-1a5ab136482a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that some knowledge is immediate and does not require long-term coherence. This principle, together with the requirement for coherence over time, implies that temporal coherentism might fail to recognize immediate knowledge.**
