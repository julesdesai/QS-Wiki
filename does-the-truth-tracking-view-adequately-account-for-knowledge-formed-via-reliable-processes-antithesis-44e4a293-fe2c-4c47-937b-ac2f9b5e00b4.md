# Does the truth-tracking view adequately account for knowledge formed via reliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44e4a293-fe2c-4c47-937b-ac2f9b5e00b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking View](truth-tracking-view-synthesis-ffb314b5-26cd-4e37-96a0-e81b3c75b895.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a reliable cognitive process consistently produces true beliefs, yet these beliefs do not track truth across highly unusual possible worlds. Intuitively, one might argue that such beliefs should still count as knowledge.**, **Suppose there is a principle that knowledge primarily depends on reliable processes rather than tracking truth across all conceivable worlds. This principle contrasts with the truth-tracking view.**
