# Can reliable processes account for knowledge in all cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5442cfac-6734-40ba-8b61-c619e90d53a9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism and the Role of Justification](reliabilism-and-the-role-of-justification-synthesis-e3c23741-1c3a-47b4-8f99-17f5f12eafda.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition because of a reliable process like astrology, which historically lacks truth-conducive reliability. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not genuinely have knowledge despite the reliability of the astrological process.**, **There is a plausible principle that the reliability of a process should be truth-conducive in order for it to be a source of knowledge. This principle together with a case like astrological reliability entails an inconsistency in treating any reliable process as justificatory without regard to its truth conduciveness.**
