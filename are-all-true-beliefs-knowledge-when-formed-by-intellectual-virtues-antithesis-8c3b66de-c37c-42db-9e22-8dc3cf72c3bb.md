# Are all true beliefs knowledge when formed by intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c3b66de-c37c-42db-9e22-8dc3cf72c3bb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-05879bdc-4686-45ec-8d5f-c387922414d1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a true belief through an exercise of their intellectual virtues due to accidental conditions aligning. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition or that the knowledge was purely accidental. If we say the person knows the proposition, then it implies knowledge can be accidental. If we say the knowledge was purely accidental, then the presence of intellectual virtues is not sufficient for knowledge.**
