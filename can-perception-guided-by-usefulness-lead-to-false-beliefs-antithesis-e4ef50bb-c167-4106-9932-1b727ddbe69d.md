# Can perception guided by usefulness lead to false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4ef50bb-c167-4106-9932-1b727ddbe69d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person perceives using an outdated map as useful for navigation, but it is inaccurate. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the correct route, despite its usefulness at the time.**, **A case where perceptions guided by usefulness lead to inaccurate beliefs highlights that usefulness does not always correspond to factual accuracy.**
