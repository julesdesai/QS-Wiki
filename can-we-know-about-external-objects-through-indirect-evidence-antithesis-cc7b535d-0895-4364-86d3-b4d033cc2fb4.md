# Can we know about external objects through indirect evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cc7b535d-0895-4364-86d3-b4d033cc2fb4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person infers the presence of a cat by observing paw prints on the ground and hearing meowing sounds, without directly perceiving the cat. Intuitively, we would say that the person can know about the cat's presence through these indirect perceptual indicators.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of external objects can be inferential, based on perceptual evidence that indirectly points to their existence. This principle means that such knowledge is not limited to direct perceptual experiences.**
