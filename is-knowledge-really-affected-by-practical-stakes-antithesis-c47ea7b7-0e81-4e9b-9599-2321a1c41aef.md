# Is knowledge really affected by practical stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c47ea7b7-0e81-4e9b-9599-2321a1c41aef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition with minimal practical consequence, and another where the same belief has significant practical consequences. Intuitively, we would judge that the truth or falsity of the belief is unaffected by the stakes involved.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is solely dependent on belief and truth. This principle suggests that the stakes involved should not influence whether a person knows a proposition.**, **It is implausible that knowing something would depend significantly on the consequences of being mistaken about it, as knowing is often considered a matter of fact rather than evaluation.**
