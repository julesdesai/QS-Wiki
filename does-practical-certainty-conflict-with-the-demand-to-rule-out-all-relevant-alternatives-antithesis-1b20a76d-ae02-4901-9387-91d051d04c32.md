# Does practical certainty conflict with the demand to rule out all relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b20a76d-ae02-4901-9387-91d051d04c32
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person S knows they locked the door by recalling doing so. Intuitively, S can be practically certain without examining alternatives such as remembering an earlier day or being sabotaged**, **It is implausible to require eliminating all alternatives when practical certainty is an acceptable basis for knowledge in daily life.**
