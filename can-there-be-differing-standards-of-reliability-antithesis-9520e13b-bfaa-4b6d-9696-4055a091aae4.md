# Can there be differing standards of reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9520e13b-bfaa-4b6d-9696-4055a091aae4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider a process reliable if it generally produces true beliefs within normal contexts, but the same process fails in extreme or non-standard conditions. Intuitively, we would have to question whether knowledge can be claimed across varying contexts.**, **Differing conditions and contexts might challenge the reliability of a cognitive process, questioning the universality of the reliability criterion.**
