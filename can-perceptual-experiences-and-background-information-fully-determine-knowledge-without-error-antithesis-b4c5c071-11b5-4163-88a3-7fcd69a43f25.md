# Can perceptual experiences and background information fully determine knowledge without error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4c5c071-11b5-4163-88a3-7fcd69a43f25
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Inference](phenomenalism-with-inference-synthesis-5c9b476a-f87e-418f-98f3-b4842f3fdc21.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has perceptual experiences that are coupled with erroneous background information to infer knowledge about an object's nature. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows or is mistaken. If we say the person knows, then it seems incorrect because of the erroneous background information. If we say the person is mistaken, then it suggests that perceptions and background information cannot exempt error in knowledge inference.**
