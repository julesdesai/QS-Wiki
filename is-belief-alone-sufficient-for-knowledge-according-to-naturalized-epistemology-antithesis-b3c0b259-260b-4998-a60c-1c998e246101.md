# Is belief alone sufficient for knowledge according to naturalized epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3c0b259-260b-4998-a60c-1c998e246101
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person holds a belief due to social pressure rather than evidence. Intuitively, this belief does not amount to knowledge despite being held by the person.**, **There is a plausible principle that belief requires evidence or reason to contribute to knowledge.**, **A person knows a proposition if they believe it and it is true contradicts the notion of belief being insufficient for knowledge without supporting evidence.**
