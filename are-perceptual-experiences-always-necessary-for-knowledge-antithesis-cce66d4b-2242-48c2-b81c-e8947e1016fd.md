# Are perceptual experiences always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cce66d4b-2242-48c2-b81c-e8947e1016fd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**A case in which someone derives knowledge of a hypothetical scenario purely through thought experiments challenges the necessity of perception for knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that certain kinds of knowledge, like those derived from abstract reasoning, may not require perceptual experiences.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge is rooted in perception, given the existence of a priori knowledge, which is knowledge justified independently of experience.**
