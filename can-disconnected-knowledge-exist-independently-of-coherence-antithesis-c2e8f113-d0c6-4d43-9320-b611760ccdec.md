# Can disconnected knowledge exist independently of coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2e8f113-d0c6-4d43-9320-b611760ccdec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Foundationalism](coherentist-foundationalism-synthesis-815fa62f-5e40-4962-8349-ea400fc3f5ce.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person acquires a true belief through sensory perception that's entirely isolated from their other beliefs, such as immediately knowing "the sky is blue" by looking at it. Intuitively, we might say that this counts as knowledge even though it does not form part of an interconnected web of beliefs.**, **It is implausible that all forms of knowledge require integration into an existing web of beliefs, as some knowledge seems directly accessible through perception.**
