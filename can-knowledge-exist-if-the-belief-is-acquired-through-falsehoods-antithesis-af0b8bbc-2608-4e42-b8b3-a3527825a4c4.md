# Can knowledge exist if the belief is acquired through falsehoods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** af0b8bbc-2608-4e42-b8b3-a3527825a4c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a doctor believes patient has disease D because of incorrect test results, but unknowingly the patient actually has disease D due to a different undetected test. Intuitively, we have to say that the doctor's belief is not knowledge because it arises from a falsehood, even though the belief is true.**, **It is implausible to say that a belief based on false information constitutes knowledge, as it undermines the reliability of the causal connection.**, **A case where belief comes true due to unrelated truths challenges the requirement of causal connection to true facts for knowledge.**
