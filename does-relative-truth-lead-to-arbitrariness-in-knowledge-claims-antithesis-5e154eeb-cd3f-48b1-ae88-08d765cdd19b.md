# Does relative truth lead to arbitrariness in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5e154eeb-cd3f-48b1-ae88-08d765cdd19b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual claims knowledge of a proposition based solely on personal conviction and without any external verification. Intuitively, such knowledge seems arbitrary as it lacks supportive criteria besides personal conviction.**, **If truth is based only on individual standpoints without objective criteria, there is a plausible risk of knowledge claims becoming arbitrary, lacking any common standard for assessment.**, **It seems implausible to accept knowledge claims as valid if they are entirely arbitrary, as such claims would be devoid of any justification beyond subjective interpretation.**
