# Is reliability too contingent on specific conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e119c86-616e-44cd-b96f-34e7990251ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not depend closely on specific conditions that could easily change without affecting the reliability of the process.**, **Consider a scenario where the reliability of a belief-forming process is entirely dependent on conditions that the believer is unaware of, such as environmental factors. Intuitively, we would judge that such contingent factors undermine the claim to knowledge.**
