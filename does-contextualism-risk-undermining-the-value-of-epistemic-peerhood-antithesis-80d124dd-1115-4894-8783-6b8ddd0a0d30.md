# Does contextualism risk undermining the value of epistemic peerhood?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 80d124dd-1115-4894-8783-6b8ddd0a0d30
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Suppose two epistemic peers, with similar reasoning abilities and evidence, evaluate a proposition in different contexts. Intuitively, they should reach the same knowledge claims, but contextualism allows for differing conclusions, which challenges the notion of epistemic peerhood.**, **There is a plausible principle that epistemic peers should be able to reach consensus on propositions if their evidence and reasoning abilities are equivalent, and contextualism disrupts this expectation.**
