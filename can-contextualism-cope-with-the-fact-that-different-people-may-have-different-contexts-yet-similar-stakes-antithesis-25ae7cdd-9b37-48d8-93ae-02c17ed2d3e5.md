# Can contextualism cope with the fact that different people may have different contexts yet similar stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 25ae7cdd-9b37-48d8-93ae-02c17ed2d3e5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Suppose two people, A and B, both consider a proposition in similar high-stakes situations, but they are in different contexts due to differing past experiences. Intuitively, it seems problematic if one knows the proposition while the other does not, although their stakes are effectively similar.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be consistent among similar situations with similar stakes, and differing outcomes in knowledge here suggest a problem for contextualism.**
