# Could a lucky guess count as knowledge under virtue epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4107aa7-27b7-48bf-9a1a-efaea5c0e484
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b6d42ffa-59fb-457e-877f-3bcb8ac1af86.md)

## Content

**A situation can arise where a person makes a lucky guess that a proposition is true and it indeed turns out to be true.**, **A guess that results in a true belief is not a result of an intellectual virtue like careful reasoning or evidence gathering.**, **If the guess is true merely by luck, it does not showcase the exercise of cognitive virtue.**
