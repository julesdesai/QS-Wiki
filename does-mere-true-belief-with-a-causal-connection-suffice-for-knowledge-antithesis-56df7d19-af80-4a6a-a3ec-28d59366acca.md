# Does mere true belief with a causal connection suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56df7d19-af80-4a6a-a3ec-28d59366acca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b3086f02-fe19-4187-b3b5-dcf882df82f4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we encounter a person who blindly believes all articles in a satire news site. One particular satirical article happens to contain a true statement due to coincidence, about a piece of news that the person then believes. Intuitively, this person's belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **Even though there is a causal chain between the article's statement and the belief formed, and the belief is true, the unreliable source suggests that mere true belief with a causal connection is insufficient for knowledge.**
