# Is knowledge truly intersubectively verifiable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d21f68ea-53f7-4515-8352-9d095d88223b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Objective Phenomenalism](objective-phenomenalism-synthesis-91c0e065-6d6c-4b9a-9519-a161a622cd2d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person claims to see an object that none of their companions perceive. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person might still possess knowledge if their perception matches the facts.**, **Suppose groups of people consistently have different perceptual experiences of the same event. Intuitively, this suggests that intersubjective verification may not always align with objective knowledge.**
