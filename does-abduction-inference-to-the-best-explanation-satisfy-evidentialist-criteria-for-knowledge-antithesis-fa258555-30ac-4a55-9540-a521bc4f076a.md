# Does abduction (inference to the best explanation) satisfy evidentialist criteria for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fa258555-30ac-4a55-9540-a521bc4f076a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that abductive reasoning, or inference to the best explanation, is a common method for forming beliefs.**, **A case in which one arrives at a true conclusion via abduction rather than direct evidence may not align with strict evidentialist criteria.**, **If an abduction leads to true belief, it questions whether evidential sufficiency is necessary for knowledge.**
