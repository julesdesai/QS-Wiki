# Does perception really provide immediate access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5dfe5af3-f0d8-4a5b-ab7a-efc2b41cbba3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees a straw in a glass of water, and it appears bent. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have immediate access to the straw's real condition.**, **There is a plausible principle that perception should accurately reflect external objects to provide immediate access. This principle together with the knowledge that perception can be deceptive, as in the bent straw case, entails that perception does not always provide immediate access to external objects.**
