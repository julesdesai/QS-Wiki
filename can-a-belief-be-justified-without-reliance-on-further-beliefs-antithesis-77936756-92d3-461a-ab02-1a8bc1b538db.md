# Can a belief be justified without reliance on further beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 77936756-92d3-461a-ab02-1a8bc1b538db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-basic-beliefs-synthesis-db3b35bf-6f35-4ce4-82b6-698bdde0b345.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a mathematician derives a theorem based on axioms. Intuitively, this reliance suggests the theorem cannot be basic if it requires foundational assumptions.**, **A plausible principle states that knowledge often builds on existing justified beliefs. This undermines the notion of beliefs being basic without reliance.**, **It is implausible that a belief can be justified in isolation without any conceptual framework or prior belief.**
