# Does knowing require infallible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0673f788-d83e-48e6-a688-4bf5ab74cba6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who believes, with strong but fallible evidence, that a coin will land heads based on a fair toss. Should the coin indeed land heads, intuitively, we judge that it is problematic to call their belief knowledge due to the lack of infallibility in justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a level of justification that cannot be fallible. If true, this would require invariantism to reject many ordinary cases of knowledge.**
