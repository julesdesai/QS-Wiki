# Do beliefs formed by reliable faculties exclusively suffice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc19d7d2-6fb2-4926-a939-a383efc5c251
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-193f50ad-c71a-4e26-bf9b-21d5bbf78043.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a reliable cognitive faculty for forming true beliefs about a specific domain, like visual perception, that without fail results in true beliefs. Intuitively, saying such beliefs don't qualify as knowledge when true contradicts common sense.**, **The reliability of cognitive processes challenges the idea that explicit personal credit must be evident for knowledge.**
