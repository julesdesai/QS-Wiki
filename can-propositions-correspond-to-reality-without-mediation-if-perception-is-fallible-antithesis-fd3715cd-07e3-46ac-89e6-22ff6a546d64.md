# Can propositions correspond to reality without mediation if perception is fallible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd3715cd-07e3-46ac-89e6-22ff6a546d64
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person sees a distant animal and mistakenly believes it is a dog when it is actually a wolf. Intuitively, we have to say that the person's knowledge is flawed.**, **There is a principle that knowledge through perception is reliable only if perception is infallible. This principle together with the fallibility of perception entails that knowledge cannot be solely based on direct perception.**
