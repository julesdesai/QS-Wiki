# Do all true beliefs formed by intellectual virtues constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ddebaf1b-b123-4710-9b60-d61b5ee6c41d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief about a complex scientific fact through motivated reasoning, driven by an agenda rather than open inquiry. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge the person lacks genuine understanding or knowledge.**, **Intellectual virtues, as stable dispositions, might not lead to knowledge if they operate under personal biases or external pressures.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge requires more than just true belief and virtuous disposition; it requires understanding and coherence with other beliefs.**
