# Can beliefs still be justified if we can't access infinite reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1f12d7c9-1788-403f-b6dc-87955a66f0d8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-c7c8ebf0-3a7f-4e28-af18-b590ada6528d.md)

## Content

**If a chain of justification is genuinely infinite, an individual cannot possibly have access to all the reasons involved.**, **Access to only a finite subset of reasons seems to challenge the assertion that one genuinely has infinite justification.**
