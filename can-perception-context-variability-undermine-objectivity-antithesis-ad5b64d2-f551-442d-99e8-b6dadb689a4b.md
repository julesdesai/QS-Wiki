# Can perception context variability undermine objectivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad5b64d2-f551-442d-99e8-b6dadb689a4b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which the context alters the evaluation of perception so significantly that separate observers come to opposing conclusions about a basic perceptual fact. Intuitively, we would judge that this undermines the objectivity of perception.**, **Objectivity implies that perceptual facts should be evaluated independently of individual biases or contextual shifts. If context variably alters perception to produce conflicting outcomes, then objectivity is compromised.**
