# Can knowledge derived from hallucinations be considered valid?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95eaab9d-f5a0-44e2-984d-fdb0e14bfd08
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Contextualism](phenomenal-contextualism-synthesis-37be371b-8eaa-49b9-96b8-60f2ca6919b6.md)

## Content

**A person experiencing a vivid hallucination may have a perceptual context in which they perceive an object, such as a pink elephant, that does not exist in reality. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not possess knowledge about the existence of that object.**, **It is implausible that perceptual experiences that do not correspond to any external object can form a basis for valid knowledge.**
