# Can knowledge exclude accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67f46000-f020-40d4-8934-edefa293b954
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where someone has a justified, true belief that they cannot articulate or recall the justification for. In such a scenario, it feels inaccurate to attribute knowledge to them**, **A plausible principle suggests that part of knowing involves at least potential accessibility to one's reasons or justifications**, **When justification lacks accessibility, it challenges the notion that the belief can be qualified as knowledge in conventional terms**
