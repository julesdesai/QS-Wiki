# Does contextualism conflict with our ordinary use of "knowledge"?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe864951-7258-45c3-b38f-051b1eaafcc7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextualism](knowledge-as-contextualism-synthesis-98d1787d-f33b-4a95-b2e8-91902f0e81be.md)

## Content

**Ordinary language often treats knowledge as a relatively stable concept, not one subject to varying contextual standards.**, **The idea that what counts as knowledge changes with context could conflict with the way people typically talk and think about knowledge as a stable state.**, **The variability introduced by contextualism might make it difficult to communicate clearly about knowledge in everyday discourse.**
