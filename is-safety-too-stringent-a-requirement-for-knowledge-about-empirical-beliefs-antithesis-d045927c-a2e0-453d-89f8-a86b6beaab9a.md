# Is safety too stringent a requirement for knowledge about empirical beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d045927c-a2e0-453d-89f8-a86b6beaab9a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a doctor diagnoses a patient with a common illness based on evident symptoms and it is true. However, in nearby possible worlds, symptoms could indicate a rarer disease. Intuitively, we would still say the doctor knows the diagnosis.**, **A plausible principle posits that knowledge requires reliability but not necessarily safety in every nearby possible scenario. This suggests knowledge can exist even when beliefs are not safe across every possible world.**
