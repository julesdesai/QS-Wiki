# Do contextual cues adequately prevent knowledge of irrelevant truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fd391f9-f4d8-4633-bf82-8fa1e0c34041
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person having a justified true belief about an obscure fact that is contextually supported yet irrelevant to their daily reasoning or action.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should concern facts relevant and significant to one's context to be truly valuable, avoiding scenarios where one knows irrelevant truths based purely on contextual justification.**
