# How essential are intellectual virtues if knowledge can be had through other means?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b25e6b31-f17c-4a9f-9d1d-a7862452ac98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Virtuous True Belief](knowledge-as-virtuous-true-belief-synthesis-0cc7c39c-3c22-4b7d-8311-bdde6f1e8698.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that intellectual virtues are the only pathway to true belief or knowledge, given other reliable processes like sensory perception or memory may operate independently of virtues.**, **Knowledge often arises in everyday practices through reliable processes that do not necessitate active intellectual virtues, such as remembering factual information accurately over time.**
