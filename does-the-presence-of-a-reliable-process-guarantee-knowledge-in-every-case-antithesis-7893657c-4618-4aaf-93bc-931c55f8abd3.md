# Does the presence of a reliable process guarantee knowledge in every case?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7893657c-4618-4aaf-93bc-931c55f8abd3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e2d53466-acc3-45a1-95cc-71c95728df76.md)

## Content

**A person can hold a true belief about a complex mathematical theory by sheer guesswork without any understanding. In such a case, intuitively, knowing through reliability seems questionable.**, **Suppose someone forms a belief about the weather based on a flipped coin, and coincidentally gets it right every time for a week. Intuitively, this person does not know the future state, despite the belief being right.**
