# Does reliance on inaccessible external world factors shape knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3557c732-018f-4339-9223-4f0a6ecf8ef9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes there is a chair in a room because they saw it last week, but hidden from their view, the chair was moved back into the room yesterday after being temporarily removed. The belief is true but unsupported by the person's current evidence.**, **The principle that knowledge requires a certain connection between justification and current circumstances questions this belief's classification as knowledge.**, **The lack of immediate support for the belief challenges its standing as knowledge despite being true.**
