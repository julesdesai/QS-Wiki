# Does knowledge of external entities require perceptual structure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e9d2463-e505-4020-8992-9d7844a847f4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structuralism](epistemic-structuralism-synthesis-7fb538c7-d6b1-4a5d-b302-4f53eea0d56a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person accurately predicts a future event without any direct involvement of perceptual experiences or structures. Intuitively, either we have to say that the person has knowledge of the future event or that our understanding of knowledge must encompass more than just perceptual experiences. If we say they have knowledge, then knowledge extends beyond perceptual structures. If we say our understanding must change, then perceptual structures are inadequate for capturing all knowledge forms**
