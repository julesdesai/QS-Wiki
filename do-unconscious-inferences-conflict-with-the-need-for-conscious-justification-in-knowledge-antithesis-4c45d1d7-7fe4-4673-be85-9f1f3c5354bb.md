# Do unconscious inferences conflict with the need for conscious justification in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c45d1d7-7fe4-4673-be85-9f1f3c5354bb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that unconscious inferences, which operate beyond conscious awareness, can provide the conscious justification necessary for knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person is unable to articulate reasons for their belief due to it being unconsciously inferred. Intuitively, this lack of conscious justification undermines their claim to knowledge.**
