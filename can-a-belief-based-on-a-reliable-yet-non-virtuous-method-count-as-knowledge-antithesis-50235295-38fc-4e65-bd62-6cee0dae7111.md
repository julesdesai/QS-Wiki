# Can a belief based on a reliable yet non-virtuous method count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50235295-38fc-4e65-bd62-6cee0dae7111
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Expanded Virtue Reliabilism](expanded-virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-be1f6636-aeca-4898-8b76-a37fb9c31f3b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a belief is formed through a reliable but emotionally biased process such as wishful thinking, which nevertheless appears consistent in leading to true beliefs. Intuitively, we would have to say that an emotional bias undermines intellectual virtues.**, **It is implausible that reliability alone without intellectual virtues suffices for knowledge, as the process is non-virtuous.**
