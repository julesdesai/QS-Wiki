# Can one truly have knowledge without the ability to explain their belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39dbc951-c1a8-49df-8394-b7998fc1d344
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Knowledge Summary](invariantist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-7e971fef-e35c-4ec9-93e0-84ae5864f440.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person holds a true and justified belief through intuition or gut feeling but cannot articulate reasons for their belief. Intuitively, we are reluctant to call this knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves not just having justification, but understanding why a belief is justified. This principle challenges the idea of fixed standards of knowledge.**
