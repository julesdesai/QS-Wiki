# Does knowledge reduce simply to belief and truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ffc74545-c688-472f-92e2-2a7932a67367
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge can be reduced to merely believing a true proposition, as this neglects additional factors such as justification, evidence, or reliable methods.**, **Consider a scenario where someone believes a true proposition by chance, without adequate reasoning. Intuitively, we judge that this belief does not constitute knowledge.**
