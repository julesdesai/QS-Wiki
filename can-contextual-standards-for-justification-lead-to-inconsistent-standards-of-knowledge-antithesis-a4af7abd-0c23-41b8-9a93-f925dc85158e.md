# Can contextual standards for justification lead to inconsistent standards of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4af7abd-0c23-41b8-9a93-f925dc85158e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-605b5e27-4923-4bc2-b3cf-30db710f9ba8.md)

## Content

**Standards for knowing are determined by the context of the inquiry.**, **If standards for knowing can change across contexts, then two contradictory beliefs could be justified in different contexts.**, **It is implausible that both a belief and its negation can be justified at the same time.**
