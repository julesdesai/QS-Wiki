# Is knowledge possible when reliability itself is accidentally accurate?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22a57367-8509-4336-ab1b-8e6b96e3f2b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Beliefs](externalism-with-reliable-beliefs-synthesis-44f393ff-3a5c-4b93-8788-7ab032b1badd.md)

## Content

**Imagine a meteorologist employs a generally unreliable forecasting model but predicts tomorrow’s weather correctly through sheer coincidence.**, **The public trusts the prediction due to historical trust in the meteorologist but not the specific model used.**, **Intuitively, despite the accurate prediction and trust, one might claim the public lacks genuine knowledge due to the accidental nature of the reliability.**
