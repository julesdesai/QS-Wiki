# How does context-sensitivity avoid circularity in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac0d5622-9ab7-46dc-940f-24f1a1bbeea4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be justified, the standards of justification should not be set by the belief itself or lead to circular reasoning.**, **If justification is context-sensitive, then a person might adjust their standards based on contextual factors that the belief being justified itself affects, potentially leading to circular justification where belief is buttressed by fluctuating standards tailored to that belief.**
