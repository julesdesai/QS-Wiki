# Is there a case where someone's belief is true by coincidence, challenging the idea of a genuine connection to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 01bcf2fd-658e-4afb-877e-8899c9555541
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-90a3baa8-9102-43d8-b9cc-d77493986c7c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where Person A believes that their friend will arrive at 3 PM, and by sheer coincidence, the friend indeed arrives at 3 PM. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that Person A does not know that their friend will arrive at 3 PM, despite their belief being true.**, **In a situation where a person's belief results from mere coincidence, it challenges the idea that a genuine connection to reality ensures the truth of the proposition.**
