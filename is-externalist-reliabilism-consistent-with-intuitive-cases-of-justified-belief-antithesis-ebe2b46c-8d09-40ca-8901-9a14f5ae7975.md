# Is externalist reliabilism consistent with intuitive cases of justified belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebe2b46c-8d09-40ca-8901-9a14f5ae7975
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms the belief that it will rain tomorrow by reading tea leaves. This belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we would judge that this belief is not justified.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs formed by methods that are generally regarded as unreliable, such as reading tea leaves, are not justified even if they occasionally lead to true beliefs.**
