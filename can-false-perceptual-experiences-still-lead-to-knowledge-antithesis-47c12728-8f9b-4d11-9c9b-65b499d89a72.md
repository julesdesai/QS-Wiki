# Can false perceptual experiences still lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47c12728-8f9b-4d11-9c9b-65b499d89a72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Realism](phenomenological-realism-synthesis-f972efdb-cd3c-4df6-b5d4-a15ff27a6c64.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person sees a mirage and concludes that there is an oasis, but there happens to be an actual oasis at the same location. Intuitively, we would say they do not truly know there is an oasis despite the correctness of their conclusion.**, **Knowledge seems to require not just a correct conclusion but also an accurate perceptual experience if we rely on perception.**
