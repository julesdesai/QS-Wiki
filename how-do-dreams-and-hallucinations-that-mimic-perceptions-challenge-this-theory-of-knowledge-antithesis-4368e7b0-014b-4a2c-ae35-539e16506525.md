# How do dreams and hallucinations that mimic perceptions challenge this theory of knowledge?

**Terminal:** No

## Content

**Dreams and hallucinations can present perceptual experiences that feel real but do not correspond to external reality. This phenomenon, together with the role of memory in shaping perceptions, raises doubts about the reliability of knowledge claims derived from such experiences since they might be indistinguishable from genuine perceptions.**, **Suppose someone vividly remembers a conversation that occurred in a dream. Intuitively, their knowledge claim about having had this conversation is suspect, as memory-influenced perception does not distinguish the dream from reality.**
