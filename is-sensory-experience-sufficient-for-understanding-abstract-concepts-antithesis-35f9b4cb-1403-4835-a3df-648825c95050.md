# Is sensory experience sufficient for understanding abstract concepts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35f9b4cb-1403-4835-a3df-648825c95050
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empiricism Summary](empiricism-summary-synthesis-4e15833e-fa6c-416c-a78e-6563a82d7628.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that understanding abstract concepts such as justice or freedom requires more than just sensory experience. This principle together with the absence of direct sensory experience related to such abstractions entails that sensory experience is not sufficient for understanding them.**, **Abstract concepts do not have direct sensory correspondences yet are well understood in human knowledge.**
