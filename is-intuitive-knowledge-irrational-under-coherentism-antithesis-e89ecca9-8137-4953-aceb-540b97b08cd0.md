# Is intuitive knowledge irrational under coherentism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e89ecca9-8137-4953-aceb-540b97b08cd0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an artist intuitively knows which color combinations will work well in a painting without any verbalizable justification. Intuitively, we would judge that this constitutes a different kind of knowledge than justified true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that not all knowledge is amenable to rational analysis or propositional justification, suggesting that coherentism might overlook non-discursive forms of knowledge.**
