# Does phenomenalism preclude scientific knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e20ec34-b565-48e2-b486-49e2eb5ff608
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-thesis-2daa98ba-8f8c-4200-a8b1-c2a336a65d2f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that scientific knowledge often hypothesizes entities and forces not directly perceived. This principle together with phenomenalism entails that phenomenalism cannot account for the reality of theoretical entities like atoms or black holes known through indirect evidence.**
