# What if foundational beliefs change over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e2c02df-986d-4a20-a1c3-720a149c4d79
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a basic belief due to self-evidence, but later new information leads them to revise this belief. Intuitively, the initial claim of infallibility or self-evidence is undermined by the change.**, **There is empirical evidence that beliefs once considered self-evident have been revised over time, suggesting basic beliefs may not be as stable or infallible as foundationalism requires.**
