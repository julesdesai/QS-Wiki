# Can a person know something by accident even if a reliable process is used?

**Node ID:** 47ae7d5f-4c44-4a6c-8be4-68c1dea1c6fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Externalism Summary](reliabilist-externalism-summary-synthesis-3207924c-3d5f-4b8a-8088-8161a66f3954.md)

## Content

**A person can have a belief that is true by accident even if it was formed through a reliable process.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief that it will rain tomorrow because they dreamt it, and the dream was correct by sheer luck; intuitively, this person does not know it will rain despite the dream being a reliable process by coincidence.**
