# Knowledge as Anti-Luck Virtue Reliabilism

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is formed by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the reliability of the cognitive process is not undermined by the presence of relevant false beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is not formed merely by luck**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the true belief is the result of exercising cognitive virtues**
