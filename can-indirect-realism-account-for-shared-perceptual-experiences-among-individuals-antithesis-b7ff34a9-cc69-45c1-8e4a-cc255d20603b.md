# Can indirect realism account for shared perceptual experiences among individuals?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7ff34a9-cc69-45c1-8e4a-cc255d20603b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism with Contextual Perception](indirect-realism-with-contextual-perception-synthesis-77e2d877-90d5-4b41-9238-09aba0491b6a.md)

## Content

**Different individuals may report experiencing the same event or object similarly, suggesting a shared perception.**, **If perception is heavily mediated by individual interpretative processes and mental representations, differences should be more pronounced.**, **The need for mental representations to correspond to a shared reality challenges the idea that perception is heavily context-dependent for every individual.**
