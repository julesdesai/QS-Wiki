# Is there a risk of infinite regress in determining the relevant context for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc620d4d-fcb2-4b18-a4fb-10921772f5da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Suppose for any context, meta-contexts can be introduced to examine the appropriateness of the original context. Intuitively, this could lead to an infinite regress where no knowledge claim can be definitively resolved.**, **There is a plausible principle that any theory of knowledge should avoid an infinite regress, but the layering of contexts introduces this possibility, which undermines the theory’s practicality.**
