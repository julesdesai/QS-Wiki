# Does justified belief always lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c37ea095-949b-482e-a4b4-170890f36683
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has justification for a belief that is false in the actual world but coincidentally true in a possible world. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition or that they do not know it. If we say they know it, it seems inconsistent with the requirement that knowledge tracks truth. If we say they do not know it, justified belief alone does not suffice for knowledge.**
