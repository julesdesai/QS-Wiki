# What about the problem of radical relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge Summary](contextualist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-c38eaf7b-9feb-49d3-8375-2d19895c61fa.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should possess some degree of objectivity. This principle together with varying justification standards could lead to radical relativism where any belief could be seen as knowledge within some contexts.**, **Consider a scenario in which different contexts have drastically differing justification standards. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the epistemic value of knowledge is undermined.**
