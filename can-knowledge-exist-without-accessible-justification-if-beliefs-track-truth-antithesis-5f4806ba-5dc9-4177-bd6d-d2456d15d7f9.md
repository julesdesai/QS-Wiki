# Can knowledge exist without accessible justification if beliefs track truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f4806ba-5dc9-4177-bd6d-d2456d15d7f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozickian Tracking Theory Summary](nozickian-tracking-theory-summary-synthesis-44760ad8-dc58-4f04-9662-a1508a0ab6d4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual possesses a belief that is true but lacks accessible justification for that belief. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires accessible justification. Without accessible justification, the concept of knowledge becomes empty and indistinguishable from mere true belief.**
