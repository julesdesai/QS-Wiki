# Does contextualism potentially limit knowledge to immediate environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6295831e-6bda-40b2-a208-fb34c46f2033
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**Knowledge depends on the context of the knower, implying that what is known might be closely tied to the immediate environment.**, **Such dependency might limit the transferability of knowledge claims across different contexts or broader environments.**, **Restricting knowledge to immediate environments prevents it from being generalized or applied universally.**
