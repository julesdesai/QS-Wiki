# Do beliefs necessarily need an endless, non-repeating justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef627a70-e0f5-4174-a3b1-799b6624f74b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism View](infinitism-view-synthesis-bddda30f-213c-4b95-bb22-45afdb1e886a.md)

## Content

**A belief is generally accepted to require justification to be considered knowledge.**, **The requirement of an endless, non-repeating justification for every belief could result in the view that no belief is ever actually justified, given the human inability to sustain or comprehend infinite justifications.**, **This could lead to skepticism about the possibility of obtaining any knowledge under such stringent conditions.**
