# How can we account for cases where individuals have beliefs but lack introspective access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b94ce03-0607-4277-abed-08e22c8c7de2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Justification Theory](internalist-justification-theory-synthesis-60885d02-ba8c-49f7-a8ac-6cdf9db1e5a8.md)

## Content

**A person is locked in a room and has complete confidence in the belief that the room has no windows based on true visual evidence seen before they were locked in.**, **The person cannot access the justification for the belief due to the room being dark, yet they continue to believe truly and confidently that the room has no windows.**, **The person's belief is true and formed based on previous reasons but presently lacks accessible internal justification.**
