# Do reliable processes guarantee true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b48e62bb-32ae-406e-8746-ae485f5bf90f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliabilism](externalism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-979d7449-6923-4b84-9ee7-21896810f66d.md)

## Content

**A reliable cognitive process is one that generally produces true beliefs**, **There is a plausible principle that even reliable processes can occasionally lead to false beliefs. This principle together with the requirement for knowledge to involve true beliefs suggests that reliability alone is insufficient for justification**
