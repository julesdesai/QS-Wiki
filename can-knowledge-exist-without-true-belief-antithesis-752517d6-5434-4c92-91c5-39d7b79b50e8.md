# Can knowledge exist without true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 752517d6-5434-4c92-91c5-39d7b79b50e8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires true belief. This principle together with the idea that knowledge arises from intellectual virtue entails that one cannot have knowledge if they lack true belief.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has a false belief but believes it with intellectual virtue. Intuitively, they do not possess knowledge.**
