# Can the time lag in perception undermine the immediacy of knowledge about external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e57fff42-4b38-4481-bf95-7dbc78426f7f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person observes a distant star, but due to the time light takes to travel, they are actually seeing the star as it was many years ago. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that there is a temporal gap in what is perceived.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception provides direct access, it should provide access to the current state of external objects. This principle, together with the time lag in perception, entails that perception is not immediate.**
