# What if reliable processes lead to false beliefs in certain cases, can they still provide justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ac4f1194-c77b-40b6-b50c-b5a573082704
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliance on External Factors](externalism-with-reliance-on-external-factors-synthesis-94d45e40-e833-458e-8cba-8623e44201d6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person relies on generally reliable cognitive processes but, due to unusual circumstances, these processes result in a false belief. Intuitively, we would judge that such a person does not have justification for their belief.**, **If the reliability of cognitive processes can count as justification, then any false belief resulting from these processes challenges this justification.**
