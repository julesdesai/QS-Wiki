# Can a true belief from a reliable process fail to account for unknown variables?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9f014f1-7c44-450f-a4ac-fb46fa3f38c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Beliefs](externalism-with-reliable-beliefs-synthesis-44f393ff-3a5c-4b93-8788-7ab032b1badd.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a case where a person arrives at a belief that a bridge is safe to cross because the bridge inspector certified it.**, **The inspector did not notice a significant crack in the foundation due to an oversight, yet the bridge is indeed safe for unrelated reasons.**, **In this scenario, intuitively, the person lacks knowledge about whether the bridge is safe to cross despite the belief being true and formed through seemingly reliable means.**
