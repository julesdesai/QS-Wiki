# Does belief in consistent but coincidental truths challenge sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c10716be-8cdb-4d34-b370-f84f251d7662
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitive belief theory of knowledge](sensitive-belief-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a04ea135-b51c-4804-8a79-c6b4af4a4910.md)

## Content

**Consider a researcher who predicts an experiment’s outcome purely by chance, unrelated behaviors or variables coincidentally aligning with their prediction. Intuitively, we recognize that the researcher did not possess genuine knowledge even though their prediction was correct.**, **Coincidental alignment of expectations and outcomes without evidence or methodology undermines the sensitivity condition of true belief.**
