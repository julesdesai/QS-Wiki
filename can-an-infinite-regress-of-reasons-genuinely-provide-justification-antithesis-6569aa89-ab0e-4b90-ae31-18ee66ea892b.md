# Can an infinite regress of reasons genuinely provide justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6569aa89-ab0e-4b90-ae31-18ee66ea892b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-c0072d0f-5658-4115-88b4-8385b90f0067.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a chain of justification should eventually terminate in a foundational belief or self-evident truth. This principle together with the need for a terminating point implies that an infinite series undermines clarity of justification.**, **In everyday reasoning, people reach justified conclusions without consciously identifying an infinite chain of reasons, suggesting that finite chains can suffice.**
