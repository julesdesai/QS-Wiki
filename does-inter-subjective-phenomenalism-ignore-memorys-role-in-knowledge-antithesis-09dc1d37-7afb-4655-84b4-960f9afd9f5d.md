# Does inter-subjective phenomenalism ignore memory's role in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 09dc1d37-7afb-4655-84b4-960f9afd9f5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person reminisces about a past event not currently perceivable by others. In such a case, if knowledge requires shared perceptions, the person's recollection cannot constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible that memory, often independent of current external consensus, is insufficient for knowledge. Memory challenges the view that consensus on shared perceptual experiences is necessary for knowing past facts.**
