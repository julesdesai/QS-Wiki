# Can someone know a proposition even if their belief doesn't vary with its truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0dcd9e71-f225-4945-be48-636f1d8698fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person, Alice, forms a belief based on inaccurate information that coincidentally happens to lead her to a true conclusion. Intuitively, we might judge that Alice does not have knowledge, even though her belief is true.**, **It is plausible to hold that knowledge cannot result from luck or coincidence. If Alice arrives at a true belief through a lucky guess, it seems incorrect to say she knows the proposition.**
