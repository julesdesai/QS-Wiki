# Can an infinite regress of reasons ever truly justify a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b5df862-5482-4924-8b61-68e65e88b10f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-a48a630f-d4e8-4dc2-b25a-518a64dbd1fb.md)

## Content

**A justified belief built on an infinite regress of reasons implies that justification is derived from an endless sequence that can never be grasped or completed.**, **For any belief to be reliably justified, there must be a finite point of reference, which an infinite regress does not allow, raising doubts about genuine justification.**
