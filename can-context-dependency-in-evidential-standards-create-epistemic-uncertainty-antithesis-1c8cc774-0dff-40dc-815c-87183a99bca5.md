# Can context-dependency in evidential standards create epistemic uncertainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1c8cc774-0dff-40dc-815c-87183a99bca5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**When the amount of evidence needed for justification depends on the context, it can create uncertainty about how much evidence is enough in any given situation.**, **Without clear criteria defining context, knowing what counts as sufficient evidence becomes difficult and ambiguous.**, **Epistemic uncertainty arises when individuals cannot be sure about the standards of evidence required in various contexts.**
