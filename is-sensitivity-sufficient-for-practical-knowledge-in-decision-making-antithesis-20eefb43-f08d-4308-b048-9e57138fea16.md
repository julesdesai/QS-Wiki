# Is sensitivity sufficient for practical knowledge in decision-making?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20eefb43-f08d-4308-b048-9e57138fea16
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes they can make it to a meeting on time based on the usual traffic and they do arrive as planned. This belief is sensitive because if their timing were off, they would typically not hold that belief.**, **It is implausible that sensitivity alone accounts for practical knowledge, which may involve effective agency and strategic reasoning, beyond the truth and variability of belief.**
