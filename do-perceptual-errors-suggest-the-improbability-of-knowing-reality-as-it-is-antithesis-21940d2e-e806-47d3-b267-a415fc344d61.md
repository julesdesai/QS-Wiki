# Do perceptual errors suggest the improbability of knowing reality as it is?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21940d2e-e806-47d3-b267-a415fc344d61
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-de08aecd-5e9b-4f9b-874a-d2dc97cb1697.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario in which a mirage is perceived as water, suggesting water where there is none. Intuitively, errors like these highlight the fallibility of perception.**, **Such perceptual errors cast doubt on the likelihood of perceiving reality as it truly is.**, **If perception routinely leads to errors, then the certainty of knowing an independent reality becomes doubtful.**
