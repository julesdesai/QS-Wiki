# Does the relativity of reliability across contexts challenge its universal applicability to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3e7e7f73-592a-4532-bba5-1ccab9cbe25d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

## Content

**The reliability of a cognitive process can vary significantly depending on contextual factors, such as environment or culture.**, **A process might be reliable in one situation but not in another, raising questions about its suitability as a universal criterion for knowledge.**, **Consider a process deemed reliable in a scientific context but not in an everyday context, leading to inconsistent conclusions about what constitutes knowledge.**
