# Can contextualism account for knowledge despite misleading evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d6fbcd5d-074d-4ab7-a5c5-079cf6ce4891
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b7b03c88-6aa4-4b47-9f59-bc5548cc994c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief that a proposition is true based on some evidence. In a different scenario, additional misleading evidence that suggests the contrary is presented, yet this evidence is not recognized as misleading within that context. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the proposition despite the belief and the original evidence.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only truth and belief but also an absence of lingering doubt induced by misleading evidence. This principle together with the presence of misleading evidence entails that it's implausible to have knowledge.**
