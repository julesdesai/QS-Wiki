# Does reliability sufficiently guarantee the absence of Gettier-type problems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14745cc5-d11b-47e5-8989-afa29d853793
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-14b8b19c-565a-493c-b5a5-1d512c9085eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person forms a belief based on a usually reliable process, but the belief is true due to a coincidental and unrelated factor. Intuitively, the person doesn't know the proposition, despite meeting the conditions of reliable process, belief, and truth.**, **This suggests that reliability doesn't always screen off Gettier-type cases where true beliefs arise through accidental means.**
