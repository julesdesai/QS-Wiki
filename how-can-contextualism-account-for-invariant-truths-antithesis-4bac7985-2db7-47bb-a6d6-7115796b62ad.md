# How can contextualism account for invariant truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4bac7985-2db7-47bb-a6d6-7115796b62ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextualism](knowledge-as-contextualism-synthesis-b56b56df-6d59-4c6a-8de4-4cbefdc8c784.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the truth of a statement does not vary across contexts if the facts about the world remain unchanged. This principle together with contextualism entails that a knowledge claim could be true in one context but false in another, which contradicts the principle of invariant truths.**
