# Can context override factual errors in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4595cb3e-0b4f-4fb9-bba8-6369b613b4c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims to know their friend will attend a gathering based purely on context-driven prior promises. The friend, however, is unable to come due to unforeseen circumstances, illustrating a factual error.**, **Context alone should not validate knowledge claims riddled with factual inaccuracies, suggesting contextualism could mistakenly endorse erroneous knowledge claims.**
