# Is belief always sufficient for knowledge?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition solely because of peer pressure and without personal conviction; intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that genuine belief must involve personal conviction and understanding, indicating that mere belief might not suffice for true knowledge.**
