# Can a true proposition known through a reliable process be insufficient for certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e73a5e58-252b-427a-945c-4571224c7089
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge](reliable-process-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8a084bde-8e94-4b49-86d3-6339aef5c9e6.md)

## Content

**Reliable processes may produce true beliefs that agents cannot be certain of due to the fallibility of the process**, **Certainty implies no doubt, yet a process that is generally reliable can leave room for doubt in some instances**, **If certainty is a component of knowledge, then the reliability of the process must consistently eliminate doubt, which may not always happen**
