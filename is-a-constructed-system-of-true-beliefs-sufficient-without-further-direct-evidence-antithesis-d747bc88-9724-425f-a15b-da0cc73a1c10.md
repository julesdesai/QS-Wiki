# Is a constructed system of true beliefs sufficient without further direct evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d747bc88-9724-425f-a15b-da0cc73a1c10
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivist Coherentism](constructivist-coherentism-synthesis-df66a247-3331-4fc3-9810-0103a6210363.md)

## Content

**A belief system may be internally coherent and reflect true beliefs conceptually constructed, yet without external evidence directly supporting these truths, the system as a whole remains speculative.**, **Suppose we have a case in which coherent beliefs about unobservable metaphysical constructs are proposed. Intuitively, one might object that without direct or indirect evidence, even coherent and constructed truths do not assure actual truth.**
