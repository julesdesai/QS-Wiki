# How do shared delusions challenge the coherence of perceptions among different observers?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 399eb963-b70d-4d54-9f5b-fec78ce63d98
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-762c00d9-ee32-4648-8d8e-67643edf20ba.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which several people witness a mirage on a desert. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that although their perceptions are coherent among themselves, they do not correspond to external reality.**, **External objects should not be known merely by the coherence of perceptions among different observers if those perceptions are collectively mistaken.**
