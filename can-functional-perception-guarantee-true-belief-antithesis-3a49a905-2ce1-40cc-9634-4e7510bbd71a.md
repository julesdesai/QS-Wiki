# Can functional perception guarantee true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a49a905-2ce1-40cc-9634-4e7510bbd71a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person perceives a superstition as beneficial and it leads to successful outcomes by chance. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person's belief is true or that the success is coincidental. If we say the belief is true, then we overlook the role of chance. If we say it is coincidental, the perception fails to be genuinely knowledge.**, **It is implausible that usefulness and guidance by practical benefits alone can establish truth.**
