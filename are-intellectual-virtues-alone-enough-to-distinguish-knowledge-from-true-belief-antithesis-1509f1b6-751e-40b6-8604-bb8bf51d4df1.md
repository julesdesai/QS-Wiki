# Are intellectual virtues alone enough to distinguish knowledge from true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1509f1b6-751e-40b6-8604-bb8bf51d4df1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b21afb01-928a-4a07-bd62-53cb05c919ec.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that intellectual virtues contribute beyond mere production of true beliefs. A person's belief based solely on intellectual virtues risks equating true belief and knowledge without justifying a distinction.**, **Consider a case where a person's belief is true due to coincidence and guided by intellectual virtues. Intuitively, knowledge demands more, suggesting a gap in sufficiency concerning intellectual virtues.**
