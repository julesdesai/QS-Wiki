# Is knowledge truly internally consistent within a context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0812dc6c-9230-4c06-8c33-1b15140914ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows a proposition in the context of a casual conversation but fails to know the same proposition in the context of a rigorous scientific debate. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge evaluations are not entirely internally consistent within a given context.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain stable when switching between sub-contexts of the same broader context. This principle together with shifts in knowledge evaluations due to minor contextual changes entails that knowledge evaluations might not be consistent even within a single context.**
