# Can a true belief lack knowledge despite strong evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a2816ce-6eb1-455c-a605-b622722b28c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a person has a true belief and strong evidence supporting it, they should be considered to have knowledge of that belief.**, **Consider a scenario in which a detective has a large amount of evidence all pointing to the guilt of a suspect, and this evidence, though coincidentally correct, leads to a true belief about the suspect's guilt. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the detective knows the suspect is guilty.**
