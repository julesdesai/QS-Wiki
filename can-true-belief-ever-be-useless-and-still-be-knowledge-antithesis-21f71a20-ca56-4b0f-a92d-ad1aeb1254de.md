# Can true belief ever be useless and still be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21f71a20-ca56-4b0f-a92d-ad1aeb1254de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d7b209ba-b523-44b3-ad9a-c228c53c8771.md)

## Content

**A belief that is true but does not provide any practical benefit is conceivable.**, **Such a belief could be about an abstract mathematical truth with no practical application.**, **If knowledge requires practicality, this would mean these true beliefs aren't knowledge, which seems counterintuitive.**
