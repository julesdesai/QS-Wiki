# Does knowledge require an intellectual virtue in all instances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 900a4224-3d5c-472a-b9a7-f6cb16426787
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where one's belief is true and justified by empirical evidence but not rooted in an intellectual character trait. Intuitively, even without the virtue, we might still judge that one possesses knowledge.**, **It is implausible that all such justified true beliefs must also arise from an intellectual virtue to be considered knowledge. This implausibility questions the virtue necessity.**
