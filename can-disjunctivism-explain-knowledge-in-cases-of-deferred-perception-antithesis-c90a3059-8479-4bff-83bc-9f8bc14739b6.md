# Can disjunctivism explain knowledge in cases of deferred perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c90a3059-8479-4bff-83bc-9f8bc14739b6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Suppose we observe a scenario where someone sees a warning sign from a distance, interpreted correctly only upon closer examination much later. Intuitively, knowledge accuracy might come after the initial observation.**, **If knowledge involves direct perception, then the understanding after deferred perception should be knowledgeless initially.**, **It seems implausible that further examination disqualifies knowledge initially possessed through deferred perception.**
