# Is proper functionalism compatible with epistemic internalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 10cf7500-253b-400a-80fd-1f55c6ef0b48
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has access to the internal states of their cognitive faculties and consistently monitors and adjusts them. If proper function is strictly external, these internal factors might not influence knowledge.**, **A principle suggesting that knowledge requires proper function implies reliance on external processes. Internalist accounts prioritize internal states and access, which may not align.**, **If knowledge is determined by faculties functioning externally proper, then internal awareness and adjustment are insufficient for justifying beliefs.**
