# Does fulfilling cognitive tasks necessarily imply knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d224858-bbd0-482c-bd56-bd77f221cc72
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Functionalism with Cognitive Task Fulfillment](epistemic-functionalism-with-cognitive-task-fulfillment-synthesis-3edd3165-e9fd-4e05-81df-6b63dbbb89eb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person accurately identifies a bird species based on a quick glance. The person has no further evidence or justification beyond this intuition. Intuitively, we might question whether this is knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that knowing requires accessible justification. This principle suggests that task fulfillment without accessible evidence may not suffice for knowledge.**
