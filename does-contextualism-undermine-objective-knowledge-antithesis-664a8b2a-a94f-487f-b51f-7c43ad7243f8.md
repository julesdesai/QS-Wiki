# Does contextualism undermine objective knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 664a8b2a-a94f-487f-b51f-7c43ad7243f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology](contextualist-epistemology-synthesis-771a8ab1-0860-48e1-822c-9b3b2cda1c63.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people with the same evidence claim knowledge under different contexts. Intuitively, it is problematic if one lacks knowledge while the other does not solely due to differing contextual standards.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can vary between individuals with identical evidence without it impacting objective standards of truth.**, **A case in which a person knows a proposition in a relaxed context but not in a more rigorous one challenges the objectivity of knowledge.**
