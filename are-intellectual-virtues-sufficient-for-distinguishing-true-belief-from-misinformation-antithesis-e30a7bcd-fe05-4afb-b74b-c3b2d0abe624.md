# Are intellectual virtues sufficient for distinguishing true belief from misinformation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e30a7bcd-fe05-4afb-b74b-c3b2d0abe624
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person with virtuous reasoning skills forms a true belief based on misleading but ultimately correct data presents a challenge.**, **There is a plausible principle that distinguishes belief primarily formed through intellectual virtues from those influenced by chance or misleading sources.**, **It is implausible that intellectual virtues alone can differentiate between belief formed through skill and one resulting from coincidental truth in misinformation.**
