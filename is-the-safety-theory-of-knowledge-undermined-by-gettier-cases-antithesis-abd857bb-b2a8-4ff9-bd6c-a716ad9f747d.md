# Is the Safety Theory of Knowledge undermined by Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abd857bb-b2a8-4ff9-bd6c-a716ad9f747d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person sees a broken clock that coincidentally shows the correct time. In this scenario, it is true that the person believes it is that time, and it is true as well, but the source of the belief is flawed. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know the time even though the belief is correct.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just correct belief but also a reliable connection between the belief and truth. This principle together with the Gettier scenario suggests that merely having a safe true belief is not sufficient for knowledge if the process leading to belief is unreliable.**
