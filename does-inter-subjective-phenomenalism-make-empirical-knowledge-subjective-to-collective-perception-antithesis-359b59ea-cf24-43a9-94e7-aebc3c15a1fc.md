# Does inter-subjective phenomenalism make empirical knowledge subjective to collective perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 359b59ea-cf24-43a9-94e7-aebc3c15a1fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a new scientific discovery contradicts prevailing perceptions, yet is verified through empirical methods. It is implausible to dismiss it merely due to a lack of current collective acknowledgment.**, **There is a plausible principle that empirical evidence can constitute knowledge independently of consensus. If knowledge is tied strictly to current shared perceptions, it may inhibit scientific advancement and understanding.**
