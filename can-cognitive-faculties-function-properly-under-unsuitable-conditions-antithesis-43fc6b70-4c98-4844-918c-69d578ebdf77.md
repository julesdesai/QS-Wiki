# Can cognitive faculties function properly under unsuitable conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43fc6b70-4c98-4844-918c-69d578ebdf77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**Suppose cognitive faculties are influenced by external factors like stress or fatigue that typically prevent proper functioning. A belief formed under such conditions is true, and intuitively, we might still accept it as knowledge.**, **The principle that knowledge requires faculties to function properly under suitable conditions assumes that unsuitable conditions necessarily disrupt knowledge. Yet certain beliefs formed under duress might align with true beliefs accepted as knowledge.**, **It is implausible to assume that only ideal conditions permit knowledge formation, as real-world examples often involve true beliefs formed under diverse and challenging circumstances.**
