# How can coherent perceptual experiences be fallible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 249a7f88-1709-4f52-8563-ebe6a8fbc296
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-762c00d9-ee32-4648-8d8e-67643edf20ba.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if knowledge is based on coherent perceptual experiences, such experiences should be infallible. This principle, together with the fallibility of human perception demonstrated by optical illusions, entails that coherent perceptual experiences are not reliable for establishing knowledge.**
