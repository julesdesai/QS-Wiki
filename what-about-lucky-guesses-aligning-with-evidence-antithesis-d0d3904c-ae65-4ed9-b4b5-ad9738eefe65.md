# What about lucky guesses aligning with evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0d3904c-ae65-4ed9-b4b5-ad9738eefe65
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-10c49977-d180-4d95-bcbb-8124324d7ccb.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone makes a lucky guess about a future event, and then coincidentally finds evidence that supports their guess before the event occurs. If we say their guess supported by evidence counts as knowledge, it seems to allow luck a role in the formation of knowledge. If we say it does not count as knowledge, it challenges the sufficiency of evidence in producing knowledge.**, **It is implausible that luck should play any role in the process of gaining knowledge, as knowledge should be reliably tied to truth and evidence.**
