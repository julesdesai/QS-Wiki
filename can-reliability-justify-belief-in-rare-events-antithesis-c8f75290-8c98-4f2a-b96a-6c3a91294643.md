# Can reliability justify belief in rare events?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8f75290-8c98-4f2a-b96a-6c3a91294643
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a reliable process indicates the occurrence of a statistically rare event. Intuitively, we may judge that this does not establish knowledge without additional evidence or corroboration.**, **Suppose someone forms a belief regarding an imminent but unlikely natural disaster solely based on a historically reliable prediction model. It is implausible that this alone constitutes knowledge.**
