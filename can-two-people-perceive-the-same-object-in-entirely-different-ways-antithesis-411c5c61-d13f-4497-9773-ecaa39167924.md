# Can two people perceive the same object in entirely different ways?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 411c5c61-d13f-4497-9773-ecaa39167924
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenological Perspective](phenomenological-perspective-synthesis-c5a75a88-a263-494b-bef9-8c1e426bd706.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals perceive the same painting, but one sees a unicorn while the other sees a horse. Intuitively, both cannot be directly grasping the essence of the object if they perceive contradictory features.**, **Given that perception is supposed to grasp the essence directly, discrepancies such as these would suggest a problem with the theory.**
