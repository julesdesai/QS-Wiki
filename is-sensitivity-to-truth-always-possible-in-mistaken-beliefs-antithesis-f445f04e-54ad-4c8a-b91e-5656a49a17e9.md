# Is sensitivity to truth always possible in mistaken beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f445f04e-54ad-4c8a-b91e-5656a49a17e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity Condition](contextualism-with-sensitivity-condition-synthesis-dd26ba8e-d20d-4379-a4ae-bc06eaa28734.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person P believes a commonly held false proposition due to widespread error**, **P's belief cannot be sensitive to the truth because the proposition is false**, **If sensitivity is required for knowledge, common mistaken beliefs would never be considered known, which challenges our intuitive judgments on collective error**
