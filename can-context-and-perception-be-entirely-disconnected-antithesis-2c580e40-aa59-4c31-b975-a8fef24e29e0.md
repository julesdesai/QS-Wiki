# Can context and perception be entirely disconnected?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c580e40-aa59-4c31-b975-a8fef24e29e0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Perception](contextualism-in-perception-synthesis-8503929a-e1fd-4ac2-b5bb-0ff2ee925a19.md)

## Content

**There can be scenarios where perceptual inputs are entirely independent of context, such as recognizing a mathematical truth like 2+2=4 without any context influencing understanding**, **Certain abstract propositions, such as 'all bachelors are unmarried,' can be known without reliance on perceptual contexts**, **Even when contexts vary, there can still be a universal perception of reality through shared sensory modalities, like seeing a blue sky**
