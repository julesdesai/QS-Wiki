# How does the Gettier problem challenge the connection between true belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 97026198-9113-470a-b08e-f326f71c6515
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-43f1bd52-38ff-4c3b-a3d7-408481a71c52.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person has a justified true belief but does not have knowledge is possible, as illustrated by a scenario where someone believes a proposition due to a justified, though accidentally true, belief.**, **In this case, the person believes that Bus 45 has arrived on time because they see its reflection and believe the clock's time, but the bus in front of them is actually Bus 46. Therefore, though the belief is true and justified, it is not knowledge.**
