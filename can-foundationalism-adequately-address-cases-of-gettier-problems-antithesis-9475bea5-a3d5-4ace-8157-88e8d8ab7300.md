# Can foundationalism adequately address cases of Gettier problems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9475bea5-a3d5-4ace-8157-88e8d8ab7300
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**In a Gettier problem, a person holds a justified true belief through luck rather than genuine knowledge.**, **A classic case involves a person believing 'the person who will get the job has ten coins in their pocket' based on justified belief, but another candidate, unknowingly having ten coins, gets the job.**, **The principle that knowledge is justified true belief implies that Gettier cases should constitute knowledge.**, **If Gettier cases show justified true belief is not sufficient for knowledge, then foundationalism's definition is inadequate.**
