# Does memory verification challenge the requirement for intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f82f0212-5271-4a88-b8f0-fadb3867ce58
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person remembers a true proposition but cannot verify the original source of their memory, relying solely on trust in their cognitive abilities. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might question whether intellectual virtue can be claimed without verification of the belief's origin.**, **It is implausible that knowledge requires intellectual virtue if memory-based beliefs cannot be independently verified as virtuous due to the lack of immediate evidence.**
