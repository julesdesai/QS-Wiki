# Does a reliable process guarantee the absence of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d5f8285b-9981-4bf4-87fb-19898872de19
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-f6f2f982-fa0f-404e-a509-24417604aa1e.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person forms a true belief about an event by chance, even though their process is generally reliable, is a case where epistemic luck plays a role.**, **The presence of epistemic luck indicates that knowledge is not guaranteed merely by a reliable process.**
