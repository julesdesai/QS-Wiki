# Are testimonial beliefs compatible with the causal theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 744c0b25-ea95-4ef3-860c-65918abd8126
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causal Theory](knowledge-as-causal-theory-synthesis-0464f163-f3b8-4866-a3a6-cfa9cb96e27a.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone tells you a fact, and you believe it to be true based solely on their testimony. There is no direct causal interaction between you and the fact, but you are considered to possess knowledge.**, **Testimonial knowledge typically lacks a direct causal connection between the believer and the fact, questioning the necessity of a direct causal link for knowledge.**
