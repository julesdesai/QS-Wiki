# Can invariantism account for the fluidity of knowledge in social discourse?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a78749a6-140d-49a6-aef7-8c5f15b3f986
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Suppose in a discussion one person retracts a claim to know something when presented with new evidence. Intuitively, this reflects an adjustment in knowledge based on context.**, **The view's claim that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant implies their claim should not change with additional evidence.**, **It is implausible that the acquisition of new evidence should not impact what is known.**
