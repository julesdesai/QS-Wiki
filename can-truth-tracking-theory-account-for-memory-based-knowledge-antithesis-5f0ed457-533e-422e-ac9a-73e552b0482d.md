# Can truth-tracking theory account for memory-based knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f0ed457-533e-422e-ac9a-73e552b0482d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person remembers a past event accurately. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person's knowledge does not rely on present truth-tracking because memory links present belief to past truth.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately remembers a past belief that was once truth-tracking. Intuitively, we either have to say that past truth-tracking is enough or that current truth-tracking is required. If we say past truth-tracking is enough, then current conditions are unnecessary. If we say current truth-tracking is required, then truth-tracking does not fit with normal memory-based knowledge.**
