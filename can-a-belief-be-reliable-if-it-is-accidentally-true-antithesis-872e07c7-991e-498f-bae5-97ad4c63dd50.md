# Can a belief be reliable if it is accidentally true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 872e07c7-991e-498f-bae5-97ad4c63dd50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-b7a77c41-f711-4239-97c6-124b43e5d847.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person forms a belief based on faulty reasoning or a false premise, but the belief is incidentally true. Intuitively, this belief does not seem to be knowledge.**, **A belief that is true due to luck rather than a reliable process challenges the notion that the belief is produced by a method that generally produces true beliefs.**
