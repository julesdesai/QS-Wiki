# Does truth-tracking theory account for degrees of belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6401473f-bc08-49e7-b80d-04c10c908d65
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge must account for varying degrees of belief and certainty. This principle, together with the complex nature of truth-tracking, suggests that belief requiring strict binary understanding might not apply easily.**, **Consider a scenario where a person holds a belief with strong, but not complete certainty. In such a scenario, intuitively, the success of belief is better explained by degrees rather than binary truth-tracking adequacy.**
