# Can the causal connection requirement be fulfilled without knowledge being present?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e693a5f0-8812-4041-a203-b351f414f6c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person sees a tree in the distance and believes it is an oak. However, they cannot distinguish an oak from a similar tree without further evidence, like leaves or bark. Intuitively, the belief arises from seeing an oak tree, but the lack of distinguishing information questions whether the belief qualifies as knowledge.**, **In some cases, a causal connection between belief and fact might exist, but it is inadequate for generating knowledge if distinguishing information isn't accessible to the believer.**
