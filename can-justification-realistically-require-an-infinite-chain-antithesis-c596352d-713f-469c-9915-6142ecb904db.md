# Can justification realistically require an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c596352d-713f-469c-9915-6142ecb904db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Infinitism](pragmatic-infinitism-synthesis-f73c0505-1eaa-4768-8143-11a2f8c9ca92.md)

## Content

**The notion of an infinite chain of reasons is conceptually impractical for human cognition.**, **Human cognitive capacities are finite, preventing any real engagement with an infinite chain of reasons.**, **If justification required infinite chains of reasons, it would be impossible for anyone to truly be justified in practice.**, **Finite beings can only ever construct and comprehend finite chains of reasoning.**
