# Can practical stakes really change knowledge standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9523a803-8470-4031-9f2f-1e67c53b6210
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-98e3a79c-8fed-46a8-a97f-357c8246f324.md)

## Content

**A case where someone needs to be absolutely certain about the location of their keys before leaving for an international flight exemplifies high practical stakes. They inquire about the location of their keys and are told they are on the kitchen table. Intuitively, the high stakes require more evidence, potentially changing the knowledge standards required.**, **Contrast this with a scenario where the same individual is asking about their keys but is only stepping outside briefly. Here, intuitively, the stakes are lower and less evidence might be needed to claim knowledge.**, **It is implausible to suggest that the amount of evidence required for knowledge should differ merely based on the practical importance of having accurate information.**
