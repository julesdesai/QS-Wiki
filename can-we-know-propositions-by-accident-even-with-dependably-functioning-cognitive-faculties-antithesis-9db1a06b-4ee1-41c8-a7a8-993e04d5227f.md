# Can we know propositions by accident even with dependably functioning cognitive faculties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9db1a06b-4ee1-41c8-a7a8-993e04d5227f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Dependability View](epistemic-dependability-view-synthesis-57cd5008-8279-46a6-a04a-50cbd8dfd618.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a true proposition by sheer luck or an accident, but their cognitive faculties are generally dependable. Intuitively, this does not seem to be a case of knowledge.**, **Dependably functioning cognitive faculties might incidentally lead to true beliefs without the person actually understanding why the belief is true. Intuitively, this is problematic for considering it as knowledge.**
