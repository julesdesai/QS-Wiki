# What prevents finite closure of justification chains?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9b603ab6-bfb4-4ad9-9cef-073cdf4f4d8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Recursive Infinitism Summary](recursive-infinitism-summary-synthesis-e60eeb81-9314-4a67-a7c8-de308e762c47.md)

## Content

**If justification involves repeating finite sets of reasons, this suggests that justification could terminate in a finite cycle.**, **A repeating finite cycle of reasons does not provide more justification than a finite chain, contradicting the requirement for infinite justification.**, **If infinite justification is supposed to differentiate knowledge from mere belief, finite cycles would undermine this distinction, making the view problematic.**
