# How does the causal theory address hallucinations that result in true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5c2917d1-897d-4e01-848d-3253a6952332
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-9ece6694-b5cf-4143-897d-321cf0165994.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where person P hallucinates a dog and believes a dog is present due to no actual dog, yet by coincidence a dog is indeed there, there is a true belief but without a causal connection to the proposition's truth.**, **True beliefs stemming from hallucinations point to a lack of required causal connection to actual events.**
