# Is reliable belief formation always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 094f54b7-724a-47af-b672-1f3b9aafe92d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a true proposition through an unreliable method but has a long track record of truth in similar circumstances. Intuitively, this belief does not appear to arise reliably from intellectual virtue.**, **A belief that arises from an unreliable method that has often been correct in the past may still lack intellectual virtue.**
