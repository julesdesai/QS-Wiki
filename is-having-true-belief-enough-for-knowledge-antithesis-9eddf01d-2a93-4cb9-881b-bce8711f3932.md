# Is having true belief enough for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9eddf01d-2a93-4cb9-881b-bce8711f3932
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Reliabilism Summary](evidentialist-reliabilism-summary-synthesis-f38be777-625f-41ad-8d84-ebe55a9ee5fa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone randomly guesses the outcome of a coin flip, and their prediction is true, though based on no evidence. In such a situation, intuitively, we do not consider this person to have knowledge of the coin flip's outcome.**, **A true belief without a reliable method or sufficient evidence for forming the belief is typically not regarded as knowledge.**
