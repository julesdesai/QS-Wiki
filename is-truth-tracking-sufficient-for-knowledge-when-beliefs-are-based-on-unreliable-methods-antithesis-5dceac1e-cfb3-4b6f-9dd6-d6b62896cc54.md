# Is truth-tracking sufficient for knowledge when beliefs are based on unreliable methods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5dceac1e-cfb3-4b6f-9dd6-d6b62896cc54
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking View](truth-tracking-view-synthesis-ffb314b5-26cd-4e37-96a0-e81b3c75b895.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person uses an unreliable method, like flipping a coin, to form a belief that accidentally tracks the truth in actual and possible worlds. Intuitively, this may not seem like genuine knowledge despite the truth-tracking.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief derived from unreliable methods should not count as knowledge, even if it tracks truth.**
