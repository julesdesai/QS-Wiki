# How does Reliable Process Theory deal with epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fdf2a4b1-852f-4a88-bb56-cce671e6ec2e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory](reliable-process-theory-synthesis-2b23f056-a980-462c-bb6c-659be3049cf1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds a belief formed by a generally reliable process despite ample evidence suggesting the contrary. Intuitively, we either have to say that they know or are epistemically irresponsible.**, **If we say they know, we deny a fundamental role for epistemic responsibility. If we say they are irresponsible, we challenge the sufficiency of process reliability for knowledge.**
