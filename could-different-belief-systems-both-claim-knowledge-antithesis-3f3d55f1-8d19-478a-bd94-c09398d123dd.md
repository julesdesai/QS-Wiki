# Could different belief systems both claim knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3f3d55f1-8d19-478a-bd94-c09398d123dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Consider two communities with entirely different yet internally coherent belief systems about the origin of the universe. Intuitively, we would judge that not both can possess knowledge if their beliefs contradict each other.**, **There is a plausible principle that contradictory beliefs cannot both constitute knowledge, challenging the sufficiency of internal coherence for true knowledge.**
