# Can belief equate to knowledge without justification beyond being true and believed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28dc1b49-0224-41ae-aed8-4f66af7fb1a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Epistemology](infinitist-epistemology-synthesis-6da6e9b6-0ddd-4346-9ef8-d27afad7386e.md)

## Content

**A person could believe something true by pure luck, yet intuitively we would not consider this knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification beyond mere belief and truth. This principle together with the view that belief and truth alone are sufficient to constitute knowledge appears incompatible.**
