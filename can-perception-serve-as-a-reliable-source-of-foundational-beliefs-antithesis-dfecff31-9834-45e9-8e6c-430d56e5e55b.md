# Can perception serve as a reliable source of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dfecff31-9834-45e9-8e6c-430d56e5e55b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Foundationalism](proper-foundationalism-synthesis-115cfa71-603e-48b1-bce9-39f7dced12e3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person’s sensory perception is distorted due to environmental factors, such as a mirage or visual illusion. Intuitively, we would not consider beliefs formed under such conditions properly foundational.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptions must accurately reflect reality to serve as properly basic foundational beliefs. This principle together with the belief that all perceptions should be accepted as foundational entails accepting potentially false or misleading perceptions as foundational.**, **It is implausible that all perceptions can reliably serve as the foundation for knowledge without assessing their accuracy or context.**
