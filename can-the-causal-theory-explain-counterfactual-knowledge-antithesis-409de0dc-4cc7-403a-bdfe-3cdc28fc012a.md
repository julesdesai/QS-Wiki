# Can the causal theory explain counterfactual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 409de0dc-4cc7-403a-bdfe-3cdc28fc012a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge of counterfactuals — statements about what could be the case but is not — involves a causal connection between belief and any truth, as such truths are non-actual.**, **Consider a scenario where a person knows that if it had rained, the ground would be wet; intuitively, this knowledge is not based on causal interaction with what is the case.**
