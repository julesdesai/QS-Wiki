# Is knowledge possible with unknowable defeaters?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 050832a3-a417-4c13-a03d-f1caac179ca4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Imagine a historian investigates a controversial event using all available sources and discerns the truth. Unknown to them, a highly classified document contradicts some details but remains permanently inaccessible. Intuitively, the historian's established knowledge appears justified.**, **It is implausible that permanently inaccessible, unknown defeaters can overturn carefully justified conclusions. Knowledge relies on evidence and defeaters accessible within reasonable discovery limits.**
