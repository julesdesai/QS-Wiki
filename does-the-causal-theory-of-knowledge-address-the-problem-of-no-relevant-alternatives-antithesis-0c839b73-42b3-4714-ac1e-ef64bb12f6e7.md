# Does the causal theory of knowledge address the problem of no relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c839b73-42b3-4714-ac1e-ef64bb12f6e7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-336c94a4-e4b0-4a1c-b1db-218c9b8f6add.md)

## Content

**Suppose there are indistinguishable twins and a person believes they see Twin A based on a casual glance. Although the belief is true, there is no causal discrimination between the twins.**, **The lack of discriminating causal evidence between relevant alternatives undermines the claim that there is an appropriate causal connection appropriate for knowledge.**
