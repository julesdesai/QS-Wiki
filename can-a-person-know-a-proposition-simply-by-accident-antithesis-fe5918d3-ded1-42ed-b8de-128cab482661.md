# Can a person know a proposition simply by accident?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe5918d3-ded1-42ed-b8de-128cab482661
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cfcf3dee-48b2-4ff8-855e-aba5061f4a51.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on an intellectual virtue, but the specific instance of belief formation involves sheer luck. Intuitively, we would judge that the reliance on luck precludes this from being a case of knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot depend crucially on luck. This principle together with the instance involving luck entails that virtuous believers might not possess knowledge.**
