# How do epistemic norms interface with practical reasoning in an infinite justification model?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3037ef7-07f1-4c77-b443-cde66c63de77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**Practical reasoning often requires timely action, yet an infinite process of justification would never allow for timely decision-making.**, **If a belief or action cannot be justified before acting due to infinite regress, it suggests a disconnect between the necessity of knowledge and practical living.**, **Practical life demands actionable knowledge, which is unsupported if justification is eternally deferred.**
