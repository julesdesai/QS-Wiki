# Does reliance on testimony diminish the independence of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9dce28a8-585a-4d61-865b-56810b4eb4ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Phenomenalism](testimonial-phenomenalism-synthesis-3205e188-94ee-499a-b528-9152e3586bc6.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario where an individual knows a scientific theory solely through hearsay from a renowned scientist. Intuitively, their knowledge seems dependent and less robust compared to direct engagement with the theory.**, **Suppose a case in which historians rely solely on other historians' accounts without empirical evidence. Intuitively, this seems to limit the independence of their knowledge about the past.**
