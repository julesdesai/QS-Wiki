# How can external justification deal with cases of environmental luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ffc666a4-dd88-49e3-a43c-b06b0884c46d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes a proposition about a bird species based on observing similar birds, though the actual species in question is rare and easily confused. Despite the person’s belief being true, it seems that their justification was a result of environmental luck rather than knowledge.**, **It is implausible that external factors provide proper justification when the truth of the belief is dependent on fortuitous external circumstances beyond the individual's control.**
