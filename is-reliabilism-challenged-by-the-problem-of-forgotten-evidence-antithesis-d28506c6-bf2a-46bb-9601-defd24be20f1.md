# Is reliabilism challenged by the problem of forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d28506c6-bf2a-46bb-9601-defd24be20f1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person initially forms a belief based on strong evidence but later forgets the evidence while retaining the belief as true. Intuitively, it is unclear if we would still regard the individual as knowing.**, **A principle in reliabilism holds that a belief resulting from a reliable process is knowledge if true.**, **The problem of forgotten evidence questions if reliability is sufficient for knowledge when the original justification cannot be recalled.**
