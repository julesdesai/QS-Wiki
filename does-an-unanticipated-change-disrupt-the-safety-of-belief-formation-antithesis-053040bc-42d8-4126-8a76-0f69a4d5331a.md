# Does an unanticipated change disrupt the safety of belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 053040bc-42d8-4126-8a76-0f69a4d5331a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual continues to rely on a trustworthy friend for financial advice and forms a belief about the soundness of an investment based on it, unaware that the friend has just developed dementia and offers erroneous advice. Intuitively, the person's belief about the investment is not considered to be knowledge.**, **A belief may fail to be safe if, in nearby possible worlds, an unanticipated change like a friend's mental state modifies the outcome to be false. Such unanticipated changes question the assurance of the belief's safety.**
