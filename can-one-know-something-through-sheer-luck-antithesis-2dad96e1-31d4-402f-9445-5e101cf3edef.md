# Can one know something through sheer luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2dad96e1-31d4-402f-9445-5e101cf3edef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-742bbee6-7c7c-43f6-ab65-b3296ed3e338.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case in which a person stumbles upon a truth purely by coincidence, such as guessing correctly without any prior knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not count as knowledge.**, **Suppose a person's belief happens to be true and caused by the fact, but their belief formation involves a significant element of luck. Given the role of luck, intuitively, this outcome does not constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires robustness to luck. If a true belief arises due to luck, it should not qualify as knowledge.**
