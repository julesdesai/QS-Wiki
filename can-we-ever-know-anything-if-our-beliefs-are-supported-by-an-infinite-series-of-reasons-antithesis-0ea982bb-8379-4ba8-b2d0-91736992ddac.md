# Can we ever know anything if our beliefs are supported by an infinite series of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ea982bb-8379-4ba8-b2d0-91736992ddac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-a48a630f-d4e8-4dc2-b25a-518a64dbd1fb.md)

## Content

**If knowledge requires beliefs to be supported by an infinite chain of reasons, no belief can ever reach the end of justification and thus, is never truly known.**, **To claim knowledge while requiring an infinite regress of reasons results in a paradox where nothing is ever fully known due to endless justification demands.**
