# Is the perception of reality always a direct connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8682e43a-bc58-4b25-8d2a-7b4160c228ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-e62747dd-0f26-4379-9406-3d7f6fb883b9.md)

## Content

**Consider cases where misperception occurs, such as mirages or optical illusions, where perception doesn't directly connect to reality, yet it isn't a hallucination. Intuitively, these are part of ordinary perception.**, **A plausible principle states that valid perception should not lead to consistent, widespread errors, such as optical illusions. This challenges the idea that a valid perception always directly connects to reality.**
