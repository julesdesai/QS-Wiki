# Process Reliabilism with Epistemic Justification

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 6ae0cc58-f9dd-40a4-ae81-3814ce8b3955
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can false but systematically correlated beliefs be known?](can-false-but-systematically-correlated-beliefs-be-known-antithesis-0fe34563-ff19-4029-b42d-0e2894579847.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is epistemically justified**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief arises from a reliable process, and the belief is epistemically justified**
