# Can non-perceptual knowledge challenge the necessity of representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8fd9f9fd-4ba4-47e9-9c85-ee6691aafeff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-e49b01dd-7eac-432b-990b-a606f4616c7d.md)

## Content

**A case where mathematical truths are known without any referential representation of the external physical world.**, **If knowledge involves mental representations of the external world, mathematical knowledge as an abstract form challenges the necessity of such representations.**, **This suggests that knowledge may not always depend on representations linked to external perception.**
