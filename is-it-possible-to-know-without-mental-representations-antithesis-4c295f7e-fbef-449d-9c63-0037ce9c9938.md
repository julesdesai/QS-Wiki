# Is it possible to know without mental representations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c295f7e-fbef-449d-9c63-0037ce9c9938
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person immediately and directly perceives an object without forming any mental representation of it. Intuitively, we might say the person knows the object is present.**, **It is implausible that mental representations are always necessary for knowledge, as there are situations where direct perception appears sufficient.**
