# Does phenomenalism compromise our understanding of causation in the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a4ebc4c-b278-424e-a49a-e9fc4826f80b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-a405dc69-be81-4e08-bb71-e181ff4d4e8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which one billiard ball strikes another, causing the second to move. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that there is a causal relationship between the two events independent of sensory perception.**, **There is a plausible principle that causation exists in an objective manner out in the world. This principle together with the idea that objects are just collections of sensory experiences raises concerns about how phenomenalism accounts for objective causation.**
