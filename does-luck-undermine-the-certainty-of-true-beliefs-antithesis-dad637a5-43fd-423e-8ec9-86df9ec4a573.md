# Does luck undermine the certainty of true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dad637a5-43fd-423e-8ec9-86df9ec4a573
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Lemmas View](no-false-lemmas-view-synthesis-031e6f6f-2b49-47e4-821c-d2421562acef.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a student guesses the answer to an exam question and gets it right. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their belief is correct but does not count as knowledge because it is based on luck.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must be more than simply a fortunate accident. This principle together with the reliance on luck in forming a belief entails that true beliefs obtained through luck do not constitute knowledge.**
