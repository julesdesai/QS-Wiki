# Can misleading or manipulated testimony be considered a source of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50c020cc-65a0-4629-82b0-450abe5a46dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Phenomenalism](testimonial-phenomenalism-synthesis-3205e188-94ee-499a-b528-9152e3586bc6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where testimony is deliberately altered by an individual with an agenda, thus providing misleading information. Intuitively, it is difficult to regard such testimony as a basis for genuine knowledge.**, **Suppose someone testifies about a contentious event that they witnessed but presents a biased account to favor a particular side. Intuitively, reliance on such testimony for knowing the event is problematic.**
