# Does the requirement of coherence exclude justified true beliefs in isolation as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5090fd32-b854-4f31-8e97-c167fb3a96bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherence Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a3c73806-e84d-41ba-8091-ad822ea4b799.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a belief can be justified and true when considered in isolation, independent of other beliefs.**, **If coherence is necessary for knowledge, then justified true beliefs that do not align with existing beliefs would be excluded from knowledge, which seems counterintuitive.**
