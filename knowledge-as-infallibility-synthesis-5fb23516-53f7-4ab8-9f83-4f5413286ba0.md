# Knowledge as Infallibility

**Node Type:** synthesis

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can true belief without adequate reasons ever be considered knowledge?](can-true-belief-without-adequate-reasons-ever-be-considered-knowledge-antithesis-f3ff3408-3697-4deb-a4cf-8121ecfd35d2.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that it be impossible for the person to be wrong about the belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and it is impossible for the person to be wrong about the belief**
