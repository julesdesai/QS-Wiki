# Can context-based knowledge complicate communication?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b11201c7-ad29-44c0-8538-9f3cfc338a2e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-54895291-30c8-42cf-8322-baea34575bd1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientist communicates a discovery with varied standards of evidence between professional circles and public media. Intuitively, this could confuse what is accepted as knowledge, complicating consistent understanding.**, **A plausible principle is that effective communication necessitates uniform knowledge standards to prevent misunderstandings, showing context-based variability may be problematic.**
