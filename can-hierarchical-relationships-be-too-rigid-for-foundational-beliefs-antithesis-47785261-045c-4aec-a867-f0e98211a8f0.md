# Can hierarchical relationships be too rigid for foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47785261-045c-4aec-a867-f0e98211a8f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Structural Foundationalism](structural-foundationalism-synthesis-037c32e2-5bf4-4b6c-88a6-0e0659b44b9e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on a rigid hierarchy of beliefs that allows no reinterpretation based on feedback. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks knowledge due to inflexibility.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires adaptability to new information. This principle, together with the inflexibility of rigid belief hierarchies, entails that rigid structures inhibit knowledge.**
