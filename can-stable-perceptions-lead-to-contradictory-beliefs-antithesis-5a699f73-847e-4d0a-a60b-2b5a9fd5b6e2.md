# Can stable perceptions lead to contradictory beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a699f73-847e-4d0a-a60b-2b5a9fd5b6e2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people have stable perceptions of a spinning dancer illusion, with one perceiving clockwise rotation and the other perceiving counterclockwise rotation. Intuitively, both stable perceptions cannot lead to knowledge if they result in contradictory beliefs about the dancer's rotation.**, **It is implausible that stable perceptions that contribute to contradictory beliefs can simultaneously provide knowledge, suggesting that stability alone is insufficient for determining truth.**
