# Can true belief alone constitute knowledge without justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b29aa78-363b-4468-8f01-8e678717f84e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person E believes a historical fact because it was presented as trivia, without reason to doubt it. The fact is true, but intuitively it seems E lacks knowledge due to lacking justification.**, **In such cases, the absence of justification, even with truthfulness of the belief, intuitively precludes the belief from constituting knowledge. It seems that truth alone is not sufficient without a proper basis for belief.**
