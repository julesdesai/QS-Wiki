# Can the coherence of belief systems truly reflect external reality without independent corroboration?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39435aea-83ef-4501-9a88-984273560b85
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivist Coherentism](constructivist-coherentism-synthesis-df66a247-3331-4fc3-9810-0103a6210363.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two internally coherent belief systems draw upon the same external reality but lead to incompatible conclusions. In such a scenario, intuitively the coherence of beliefs alone seems insufficient for knowledge unless independent corroboration provides an external check.**, **There is a plausible principle that verification from independent means strengthens the credibility of knowledge claims beyond what coherence alone can achieve. Without such verification, the credibility of reflecting reality accurately remains questionable.**
