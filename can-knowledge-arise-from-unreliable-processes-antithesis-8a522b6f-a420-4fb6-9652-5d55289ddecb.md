# Can knowledge arise from unreliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a522b6f-a420-4fb6-9652-5d55289ddecb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism: Reliable Processes](reliabilism-reliable-processes-synthesis-f70f1f60-dbef-46bd-b415-215191e514dd.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief that results from a generally unreliable process, such as flipping a coin. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible to claim that beliefs formed from unreliable processes can constitute knowledge.**
