# Knowledge as Contextualist Justification

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 52285e2b-c119-4c56-aaa1-f50f3aaab3bb
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can beliefs unsupported by reasons or understanding still be knowledge?](can-beliefs-unsupported-by-reasons-or-understanding-still-be-knowledge-antithesis-dae3fdd6-b41e-461a-8166-5f5951e8e0f3.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is justified relative to the context**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is justified relative to the context**
