# How do misunderstandings affect justified true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe2b697c-1c71-48fc-8c8e-6cae47bb4554
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No False Lemmas View](no-false-lemmas-view-synthesis-031e6f6f-2b49-47e4-821c-d2421562acef.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a student misunderstands a mathematical theorem but accidentally uses the correct formula to solve a problem. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the student's solution does not constitute knowledge even if the answer is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding is a necessary part of justified belief. This principle together with the student's reliance on a fundamentally misunderstood theorem entails that misunderstanding can undermine knowledge.**
