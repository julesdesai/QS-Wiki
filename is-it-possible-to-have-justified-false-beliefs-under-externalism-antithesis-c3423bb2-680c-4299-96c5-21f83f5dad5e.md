# Is it possible to have justified false beliefs under externalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c3423bb2-680c-4299-96c5-21f83f5dad5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief through reliable methods, but due to unusual external factors, the belief is false. Intuitively, it seems they should still be considered justified even if the belief is not true.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification should be enough for a belief to be considered knowledge, even if unusual circumstances render the belief false.**
