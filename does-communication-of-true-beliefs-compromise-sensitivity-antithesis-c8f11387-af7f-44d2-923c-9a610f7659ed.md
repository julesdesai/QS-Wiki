# Does communication of true beliefs compromise sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c8f11387-af7f-44d2-923c-9a610f7659ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitive belief theory of knowledge](sensitive-belief-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a04ea135-b51c-4804-8a79-c6b4af4a4910.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where person A accurately tells person B some truth, but person B believes it solely due to trust in A, not because they understand or would resist falsehood if presented differently. Intuitively, we sense that B does not genuinely know the proposition communicated.**, **True beliefs formed solely on authority or trust without verifying mechanisms fail the sensitivity test, casting doubt on the knowledge they supposedly gain.**
