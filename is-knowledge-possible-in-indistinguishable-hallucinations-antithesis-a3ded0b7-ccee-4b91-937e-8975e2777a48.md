# Is knowledge possible in indistinguishable hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3ded0b7-ccee-4b91-937e-8975e2777a48
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-72a215c5-4d26-4e6e-86ab-383aabdf6290.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person experiences a hallucination that is indistinguishable from a genuine perception of an external object. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would struggle to say the person has knowledge if they cannot differentiate between hallucination and reality.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires an ability to distinguish between genuine and non-genuine experiences. This principle together with the difficulty in distinguishing indistinguishable hallucinations entails a challenge to claiming certain knowledge.**
