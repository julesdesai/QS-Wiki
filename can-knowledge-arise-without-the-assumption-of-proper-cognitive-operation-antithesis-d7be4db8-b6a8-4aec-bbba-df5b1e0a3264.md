# Can knowledge arise without the assumption of proper cognitive operation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d7be4db8-b6a8-4aec-bbba-df5b1e0a3264
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person forms a true belief through an unreliable cognitive mechanism such as superstition or guesswork. Intuitively, we might still ascribe knowledge because the belief successfully aligns with reality.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge can arise from reliably true beliefs regardless of the underlying cognitive process's operation. This principle suggests knowledge can exist independently of always assuming proper cognitive operation.**, **It is implausible to strictly link knowledge to an assumption of proper cognitive operation, as various true beliefs arise from processes typically deemed unreliable yet are accepted as knowledge.**
