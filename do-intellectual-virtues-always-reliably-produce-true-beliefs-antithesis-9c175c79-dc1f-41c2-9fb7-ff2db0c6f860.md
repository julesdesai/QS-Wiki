# Do intellectual virtues always reliably produce true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c175c79-dc1f-41c2-9fb7-ff2db0c6f860
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-72984f03-1ce3-46fd-9fd6-13203e340ace.md)

## Content

**Intellectual virtues are defined as personal qualities that reliably produce true beliefs. However, it is plausible that even the exercise of intellectual virtues could sometimes lead to false beliefs.**, **If intellectual virtues do not always produce true beliefs, then the premise that they necessarily lead to knowledge is questionable.**
