# How can ambiguous figures challenge direct perception claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c49deffb-d6f2-4ebf-8dd8-4fcea2413821
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism with Direct Cross-Modal Sensing](naive-realism-with-direct-cross-modal-sensing-synthesis-96f2c374-253a-4f49-a9c2-1ee8405679ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person views the duck-rabbit figure and perceives it first as a duck, then as a rabbit. Intuitively, we either have to say that the figure's perception changes directly or that intermediary representations are influencing how it is perceived.**, **If we say the perception changes directly, then the notion of a stable, direct awareness is challenged by its variability.**, **If we say representations influence perception, then direct access to objects is bypassed by some form of cognitive mediation.**
