# Can knowledge encompass beliefs formed through unreliable processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e8d2712-7803-4fff-b394-f3a3e1dbc9fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b31ebf9d-44f1-4ec8-963f-04afc0c6d4e4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person continuously forms beliefs from a generally inaccurate method but coincidentally, a specific belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we might feel this coincidence does not suffice for saying the person knows since the typical process is unreliable.**, **It is implausible to assert that knowledge includes beliefs arising from generally unreliable methods. If knowledge were to include such cases, the mere accidental truth of a belief might keep it within what we consider 'safe,' challenging the reliability demand of such a theory.**
