# Could both perception and hallucination share more epistemically than disjunctivism allows?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a686596a-89fa-454e-8c62-ba003c103960
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals both claim to see a ghost, one as a result of a hallucination, and one believes it due to an actual sighting. Intuitively, both are motivated by similar visual perceptions with potentially the same psychological impact.**, **There is a plausible principle that similar phenomenological experiences could equally impact someone's epistemic stance, challenging the strict divide disjunctivism proposes.**
