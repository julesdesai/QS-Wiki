# Can environmental factors impact knowledge judgments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** edd90911-cb12-4b8b-b866-ef547ca34135
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person claims to know it is raining outside while standing inside a soundproof room with no windows, relying solely on a weather app. A change in environment, such as opening a window, might alter the justification and our knowledge attribution. Intuitively, environmental changes seem to affect certainty and confidence in beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that environmental factors shift the strength of belief justification, impacting what is considered knowledge.**
