# Do socially constructed facts impact invariant knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad1990e5-48f0-44ae-90b2-50de04b6d29c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism for Knowledge Assessment](invariantism-for-knowledge-assessment-synthesis-0fb1590d-9ce0-4644-a80f-68ae37bc3da5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which social conventions determine what counts as legal knowledge, like property rights, which can vary across jurisdictions. Intuitively, different contexts rightly impact what is recognized as knowledge.**, **It is implausible that the standards for knowing socially constructed facts remain constant, as such facts inherently depend on context-specific conventions.**
