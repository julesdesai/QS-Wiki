## Content

**A person knows a proposition if they have a true belief of the proposition**, **For contingent propositions, a person knows if the belief would not be held if it were false**, **For contingent propositions, a person knows if the belief would still be held if it were true in slightly different situations**, **For necessary propositions, knowledge depends on the context-independent certainty derived from logical principles**, **Necessary knowledge is unaffected by tracking conditions**
