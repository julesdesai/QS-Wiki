# Can a belief be justified without conscious awareness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ff0500f-d0fd-4d6c-b6d7-3e66aa918906
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on reliable cognitive processes but is completely unaware of having such a belief or the processes behind it. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief lacks justification due to the person's lack of awareness.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be justified, the believer must have some conscious access to the reasons or processes that justify it. This principle together with the scenario of unconscious belief formation suggests that justification requires awareness.**
