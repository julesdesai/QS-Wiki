# Can coherence address isolated, true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d8681e4-8ade-49a8-8401-e071602be2c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-4ac849ed-bd0f-4658-91df-2567162ccdab.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person holds an isolated belief that happens to be true but stands outside their main web of beliefs. In such a case, intuitively we might still consider them to have some knowledge**, **A plausible principle holds that knowledge does not require coherence within an entire system, especially for beliefs that are independently verifiable**
