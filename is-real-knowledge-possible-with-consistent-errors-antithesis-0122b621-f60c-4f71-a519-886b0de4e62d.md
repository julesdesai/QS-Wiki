# Is real knowledge possible with consistent errors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0122b621-f60c-4f71-a519-886b0de4e62d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Realism with Consistency Conditions](phenomenal-realism-with-consistency-conditions-synthesis-05f9226f-1221-4391-b2ae-790270c7a451.md)

## Content

**Consider a person whose consistent experiences confirm a false proposition due to systematic error. Intuitively, continuous errors should not qualify as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that repeated confirmation of a fallacy cannot constitute true knowledge. This principle together with consistency in experiences of a false claim suggests that knowledge can be present without consistency.**
