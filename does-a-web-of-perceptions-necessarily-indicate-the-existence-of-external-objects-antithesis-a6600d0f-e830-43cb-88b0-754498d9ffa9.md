# Does a web of perceptions necessarily indicate the existence of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6600d0f-e830-43cb-88b0-754498d9ffa9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which multiple individuals share consistent and coherent reports of a mirage. Intuitively, we either have to say that the mirage exists as an external object or that it does not. If we say the mirage exists, then we accept a falsehood. If we say it does not exist, then consistency does not guarantee existence.**, **There is a plausible principle that existence is objective and independent of subjective experiences. This principle together with the claim that external objects exist when consistent with a web of perceptions suggests that perceptions might be misleading about reality.**
