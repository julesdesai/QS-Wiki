# Does reliabilism adequately capture knowledge when intuitions differ from truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b02e27e2-bffb-49f0-8c9b-d15ce5b3867e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-db8e4632-83fc-4e14-a2b9-f64cf1d5bb50.md)

## Content

**A scenario in which a person has a reliable belief-forming process that, by chance, leads to a false belief challenges the idea that cognitive virtues reliably lead to truth.**, **Intuitively, we would not attribute knowledge to this person despite the reliability of their belief-forming process, suggesting a disconnect between reliability and truth.**
