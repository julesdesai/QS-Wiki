# Does contextualism make knowledge too subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 578db829-246c-4af2-90cc-4f3521a31daa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**Under contextualism, justification standards depend heavily on individual context, making them subjective.**, **Subjective criteria mean two individuals could have opposing beliefs both considered knowledge.**, **This subjectivity challenges the notion that knowledge should be an objective state of affairs.**
