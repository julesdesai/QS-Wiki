# Does coherentism problematically allow for multiple, equally coherent sets of beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b3cc2e48-fdeb-48ee-9bcf-8189a8617606
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-d4794edf-a9d9-4687-b207-9409b552ac03.md)

## Content

**There can be more than one set of beliefs that are coherent but mutually incompatible.**, **If multiple, incompatible belief systems can all be justified, coherence alone is not a sufficient criterion for justification.**, **Justification should ideally lead to a convergence on truth, not multiple, conflicting sets of beliefs.**
