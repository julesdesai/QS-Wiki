# Does practical success justify a belief in an unreliable process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2095fe87-9c20-4710-ba67-0881e7c10e0d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes they will win a game of chance based on a lucky charm and does win. Intuitively, it seems that belief is not justified, despite its practical success.**, **There is a plausible principle that belief justification should not depend on unreliable or superstitious reasoning, which is not addressed by mere practical success.**
