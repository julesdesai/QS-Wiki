# Does foundationalism address the problem of circular justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd777305-9bfc-4e5f-ad6c-e88113d153aa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**Circular justification occurs when a belief is justified by another belief, which in turn is justified by the initial belief.**, **For example, believing 'I am trustworthy' is used to justify 'My memory is reliable,' and vice versa.**, **Foundationalism posits that basic beliefs do not require further justification and can stop circular reasoning.**, **If circular justification challenges the structure of foundationalism, it suggests that basic beliefs themselves might draw on other justifications.**
