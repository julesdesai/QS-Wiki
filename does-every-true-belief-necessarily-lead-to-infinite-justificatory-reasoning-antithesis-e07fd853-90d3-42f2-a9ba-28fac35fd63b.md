# Does every true belief necessarily lead to infinite justificatory reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e07fd853-90d3-42f2-a9ba-28fac35fd63b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Epistemology](infinitist-epistemology-synthesis-6da6e9b6-0ddd-4346-9ef8-d27afad7386e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone holds a true belief about a mundane fact, such as "the sky is blue." Intuitively, we do not require an infinite chain of reasons to consider this knowledge.**, **It is implausible that all instances of knowing require an infinite chain of reasoning, which seems excessive and impractical in simple cases.**
