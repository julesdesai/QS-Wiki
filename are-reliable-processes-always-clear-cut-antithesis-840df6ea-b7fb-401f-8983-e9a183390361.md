# Are reliable processes always clear-cut?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 840df6ea-b7fb-401f-8983-e9a183390361
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-b7a77c41-f711-4239-97c6-124b43e5d847.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a cognitive process is ambiguous in its reliability, such as intuition or hunches that sometimes yield true beliefs. Intuitively, it seems problematic to classify such processes as definitively reliable or unreliable.**, **A definition of a reliable cognitive process as one producing a majority of true beliefs struggles to account for ambiguous or inconsistent processes.**
