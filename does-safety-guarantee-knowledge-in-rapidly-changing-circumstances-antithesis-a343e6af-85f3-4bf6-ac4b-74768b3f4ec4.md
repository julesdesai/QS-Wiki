# Does safety guarantee knowledge in rapidly changing circumstances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a343e6af-85f3-4bf6-ac4b-74768b3f4ec4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where facts change rapidly, and a person believes something true only because they are unaware of changes that make their belief false in the immediate future**, **In such cases, intuitively, we judge that their knowledge would be compromised despite initial safety from error**, **Knowledge requires stability or constancy in the truth of a belief, which is lacking in rapidly shifting contexts even if the belief is true in nearby worlds**
