# Can knowledge occur without any accessible justification at all?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a69ceab6-4e06-450b-b742-b940a1cf17ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b3086f02-fe19-4187-b3b5-dcf882df82f4.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person somehow arrives at a true belief that an unknown asteroid will hit Earth tomorrow, yet they possess no reasoning or evidence justifying this belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the person merely guessed correctly, rather than truly knowing that the asteroid will hit.**, **Knowledge is often thought to require some form of justification that the knower can access or appeal to, suggesting that belief without any accessible justification seems more akin to a lucky guess than knowledge.**
