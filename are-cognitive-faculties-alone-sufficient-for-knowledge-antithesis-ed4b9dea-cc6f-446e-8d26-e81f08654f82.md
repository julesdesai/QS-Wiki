# Are cognitive faculties alone sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ed4b9dea-cc6f-446e-8d26-e81f08654f82
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has cognitive faculties that produce true beliefs reliably, such as an intuitive knack for solving complex puzzles. In such a scenario, we might question if such cognitive faculties alone, without reference to character virtues, are sufficient grounds for attributing knowledge.**, **It is plausible to think a person can have knowledge simply due to the excellent operation of cognitive faculties, without needing further support from virtues of intellectual character.**
