# Does invariantism account for the variability in people's assertions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 403d2a5e-9169-422c-924b-5817343cd7de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where in ordinary contexts, a person confidently asserts a proposition as knowledge, but in a high-stakes context, they refrain from asserting the same.**, **The principle that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant implies that the person's assertion shouldn't vary with context.**, **It is implausible that people would change their behavior about what they claim to know if the standards were truly invariant.**
