# Does context-dependence make knowledge too unstable or vague?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e6f0650-4fda-4d7b-8c61-1ec06266c53b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**A case where a person gradually shifts from discussing in a low-standard context to a high-standard one could prompt a change in their stated knowledge status without any change in the facts. Intuitively, this fluid shift creates an instability or vagueness in the knowledge claim.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be stable and clearly defined to be useful in communication. If knowledge is context-dependent and subject to frequent change, it threatens its utility and coherence in practical reasoning.**
