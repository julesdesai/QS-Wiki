# Is belief sufficient for knowing without true understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e5cdf59f-6f8a-41fa-add2-0050d5e6939e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a student believes a mathematical theorem is true because a teacher told them, but they cannot explain or understand the theorem. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to claim the student knows the theorem, despite their true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves understanding and the ability to explain or justify the belief, which is not captured by belief alone.**
