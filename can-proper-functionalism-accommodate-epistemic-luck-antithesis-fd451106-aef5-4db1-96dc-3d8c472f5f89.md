# Can proper functionalism accommodate epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd451106-aef5-4db1-96dc-3d8c472f5f89
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-56ac2890-cc65-4132-90ba-c28a7f97b59c.md)

## Content

**A person may form a true belief by sheer chance, which is an example of epistemic luck.**, **Properly functioning cognitive faculties in a truth-conducive environment should lead to knowledge, but epistemic luck suggests knowledge can arise absent these conditions.**, **Epistemic luck cases suggest there may be knowledge without the proper functioning of cognitive faculties.**
