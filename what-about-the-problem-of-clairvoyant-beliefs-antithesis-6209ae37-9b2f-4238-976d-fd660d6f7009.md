# What about the problem of clairvoyant beliefs?

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who forms a belief that correctly predicts future events through a clairvoyant method, which is statistically reliable despite being irrational. Intuitively, we would doubt whether this constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not just reliability, but also some connection to rational justification. Clairvoyant beliefs lack conventional rational justification.**
