# How do high-stakes situations affect knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a08b2511-5982-4ff5-90f5-b039614dfb6b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's knowledge is questioned in high-stakes situations, requiring stricter standards of evidence. Intuitively, it feels problematic if our acceptance of someone's knowledge is determined largely by the stakes involved rather than the belief's content and justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that the quality and type of evidence shouldn't fundamentally change based on varying stakes, preserving consistency in what is accepted as knowledge.**
