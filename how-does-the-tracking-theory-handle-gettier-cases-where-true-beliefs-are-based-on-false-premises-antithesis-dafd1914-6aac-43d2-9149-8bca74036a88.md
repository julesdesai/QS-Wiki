# How does the tracking theory handle Gettier cases where true beliefs are based on false premises?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dafd1914-6aac-43d2-9149-8bca74036a88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50e12e5-c945-480c-9bf6-a71471f277b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person has a true belief derived from a false premise but obtained through strong evidence, like the classic example where someone believes a stopped clock reading is correct by coincidence. Intuitively, this person lacks knowledge despite high evidential support.**, **There is an intuition that for a belief to count as knowledge, its truth should not merely occur by accident. Tracking theory, demanding belief vary with truth, seems inadequate to rule out such coincidental truths as knowledge.**
