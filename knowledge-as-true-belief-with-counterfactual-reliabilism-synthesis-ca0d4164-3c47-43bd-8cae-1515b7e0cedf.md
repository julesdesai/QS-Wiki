# Knowledge as True Belief with Counterfactual Reliabilism

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that if the proposition were not true, the person would not believe it**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the belief is true, the person believes it, the belief is counterfactually reliable, and intellectual virtue contributes**, **An intellectual virtue is a character trait that supports counterfactual reliability**
