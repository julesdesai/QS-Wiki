# Do hallucinations or illusions affect causal theories?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6cb7d554-6e73-4765-b6e2-99b323d9702c
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-thesis-0c6eaf7b-9d38-4e55-b770-1b9de7b33981.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates an event and correctly believes it occurred due to coincidental truth. Intuitively, we would judge that this is not knowledge.**, **A hallucination reaching a true conclusion by coincidence fails to establish a causal link required for knowledge.**
