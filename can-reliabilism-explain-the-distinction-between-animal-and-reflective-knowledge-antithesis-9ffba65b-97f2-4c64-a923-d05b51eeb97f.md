# Can reliabilism explain the distinction between animal and reflective knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9ffba65b-97f2-4c64-a923-d05b51eeb97f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-5d1929f3-45fe-4f02-bb7b-941a1c5017f0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where both an animal and a human form a true belief through reliable processes, like perception. Intuitively, reflective knowledge requires more than mere true belief attained reliably, such as awareness or understanding.**, **It is implausible to equate knowledge formed by mere reliable processes in animals with that formed by reflective understanding in humans.**
