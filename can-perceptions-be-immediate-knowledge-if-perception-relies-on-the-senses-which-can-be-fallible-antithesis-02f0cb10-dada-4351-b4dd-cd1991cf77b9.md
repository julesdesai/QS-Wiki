# Can perceptions be immediate knowledge if perception relies on the senses, which can be fallible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02f0cb10-dada-4351-b4dd-cd1991cf77b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person mishears a word in a noisy environment, believing it to be something it is not. Intuitively, sensory perception can be fallible, and thus knowledge can't rely just on perception without inferential justification.**, **There is a principle that states that perceptual information is subject to error due to sensory limitations. This principle, combined with the claim that perception provides immediate knowledge, conflicts with the idea that knowledge must account for sensory fallibility.**
