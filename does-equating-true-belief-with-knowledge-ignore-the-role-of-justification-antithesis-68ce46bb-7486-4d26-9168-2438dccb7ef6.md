# Does equating true belief with knowledge ignore the role of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68ce46bb-7486-4d26-9168-2438dccb7ef6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Foundationalism](fallibilist-foundationalism-synthesis-ed3484e7-a0b3-47a4-939c-ec83c0f7bb7c.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if they believe it.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes that it will rain tomorrow based purely on a horoscope prediction, and it happens to rain, making the belief true. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not know it will rain tomorrow despite their belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that true belief alone does not suffice for knowledge, as knowledge also requires proper justification. This principle suggests that equating true belief with knowledge overlooks the necessity of having sound justification.**
