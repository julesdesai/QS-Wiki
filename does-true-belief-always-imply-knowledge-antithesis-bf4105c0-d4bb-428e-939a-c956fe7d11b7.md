# Does true belief always imply knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf4105c0-d4bb-428e-939a-c956fe7d11b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism Summary](pragmatism-summary-synthesis-d0b80c94-4161-403a-9bb9-e4c173237700.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone has a true belief due to a lucky guess. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to say they have knowledge even though their belief is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that connects knowledge with a reliable method of belief formation, rather than mere truth or luck. This principle implies that knowledge requires more than just the truth of the proposition believed.**
