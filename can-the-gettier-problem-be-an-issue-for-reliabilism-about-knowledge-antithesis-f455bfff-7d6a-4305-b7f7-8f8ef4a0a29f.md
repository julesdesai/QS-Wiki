# Can the Gettier problem be an issue for reliabilism about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f455bfff-7d6a-4305-b7f7-8f8ef4a0a29f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-e6324e74-fe2e-4167-be14-2fab090edb3a.md)

## Content

**A person believes a friend drives a red car because they saw a red car in the friend's driveway, and the friend indeed drives a red car, but the car in the driveway was actually borrowed from a neighbor.**, **In the red car scenario, the person's belief is a product of an unreliable process susceptible to misfortune but results in a truth.**, **The fact that the person's belief is accidentally true undermines the claim that the belief resulted from a reliable process, suggesting it does not constitute knowledge.**
