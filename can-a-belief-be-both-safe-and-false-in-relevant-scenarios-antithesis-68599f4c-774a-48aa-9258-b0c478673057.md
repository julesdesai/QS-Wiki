# Can a belief be both safe and false in relevant scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 68599f4c-774a-48aa-9258-b0c478673057
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on misleading evidence that in most nearby possible worlds leads to a true belief, but in the actual world is false. Intuitively, we would judge that the belief was safe but false.**, **There is a plausible principle that safety alone should not result in a true belief being false. This principle together with the nature of misleading evidence entails that safety may not guarantee truth in all relevant circumstances.**
