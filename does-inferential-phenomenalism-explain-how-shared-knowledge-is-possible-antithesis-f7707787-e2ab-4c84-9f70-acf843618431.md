# Does inferential phenomenalism explain how shared knowledge is possible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7707787-e2ab-4c84-9f70-acf843618431
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where multiple people perceive the same event differently and make different inferences about it. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that they cannot all have knowledge if their inferences lead to contradictory propositions.**, **A plausible principle is that shared knowledge requires consistency in the inferences drawn from shared perceptions.**, **If inferential phenomenalism permits differing inferences from the same perceptions, it faces a challenge in explaining shared knowledge.**
