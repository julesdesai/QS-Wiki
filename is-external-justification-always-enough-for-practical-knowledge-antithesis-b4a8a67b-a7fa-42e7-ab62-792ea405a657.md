# Is external justification always enough for practical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4a8a67b-a7fa-42e7-ab62-792ea405a657
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Internalism](hybrid-internalism-synthesis-3d9ce0d9-6ba2-4665-a054-ed06708d6ee0.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person believes that the source of their information is reliable for mundane tasks, but unbeknownst to them, the source is actually guiding them correctly by accident. Intuitively, we would judge that the person has practical knowledge for mundane tasks. However, this suggests that external justification isn't based on reliability alone but rather a matter of actual effectiveness, which can diverge from presumed reliability.**
