# Does true belief require pragmatic benefit to be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91965a42-8044-47bf-8ada-dd127805ca2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientist discovers a distant galaxy's existence. The belief is true but offers no immediate pragmatic benefit. Intuitively, it seems that this is knowledge due to its truth and discovery process, regardless of practical utility.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth and epistemic justification, not necessarily pragmatic benefit. This implies pragmatic benefit is not a required component for knowledge to exist.**
