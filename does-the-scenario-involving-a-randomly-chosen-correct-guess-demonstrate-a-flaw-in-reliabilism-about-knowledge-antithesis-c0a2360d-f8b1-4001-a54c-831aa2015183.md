# Does the scenario involving a randomly chosen, correct guess demonstrate a flaw in reliabilism about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0a2360d-f8b1-4001-a54c-831aa2015183
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-e6324e74-fe2e-4167-be14-2fab090edb3a.md)

## Content

**A person places a blindfold on before throwing a dart at a board and randomly hits the bullseye, thereby correctly guessing the target.**, **In the dart-throwing scenario, the belief that the person hit the bullseye is not based on a reliable process, yet the belief is true.**, **A person having a true belief that was formed via an unreliable process does not qualify as knowledge.**
