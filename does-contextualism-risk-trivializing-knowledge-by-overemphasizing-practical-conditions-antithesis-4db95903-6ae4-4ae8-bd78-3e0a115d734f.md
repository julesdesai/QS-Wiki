# Does contextualism risk trivializing knowledge by overemphasizing practical conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4db95903-6ae4-4ae8-bd78-3e0a115d734f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which practical conditions shift frequently, such as a stock trader making decisions throughout the day. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge fluctuates continuously with each condition or that knowledge should remain stable over minor shifts. If we say knowledge fluctuates, then the value of stable knowledge is undermined. If we say knowledge remains stable, then practical conditions may not be as influential as proposed.**
