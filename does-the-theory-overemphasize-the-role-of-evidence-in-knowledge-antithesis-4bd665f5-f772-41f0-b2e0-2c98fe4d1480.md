# Does the theory overemphasize the role of evidence in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4bd665f5-f772-41f0-b2e0-2c98fe4d1480
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a lucky guess about a future event, which happens to align with scant experimental data they possess. Intuitively, such luck challenges the sufficiency of evidence as required by the theory.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can sometimes be attained without complete reliance on thorough evidential support if other reliable cognitive processes are at play.**
