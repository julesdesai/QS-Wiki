# Can knowledge arise without any accessible justification at all?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d281c113-67b2-43f9-8d69-7ee2af8fbb5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-eb390c86-5ce7-412f-bf22-00e631bfa2b2.md)

## Content

**Accessible justification involves reasons that are easily available to the belief holder upon reflection. It is implausible to argue that a belief can be considered knowledge without being accompanied by any accessible justification.**, **Suppose someone believes a proposition to be true based solely on a gut feeling or intuition without any accessible justification. Intuitively, this belief does not seem to qualify as knowledge even if it turns out to be true.**, **In a case where a person believes a true proposition simply because they overheard someone else saying it, without engaging with any reasons justifying the truth of the proposition, it is not intuitive to claim they have knowledge.**
