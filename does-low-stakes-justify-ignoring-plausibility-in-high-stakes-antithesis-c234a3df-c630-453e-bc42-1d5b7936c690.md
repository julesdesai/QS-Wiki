# Does low-stakes justify ignoring plausibility in high-stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c234a3df-c630-453e-bc42-1d5b7936c690
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Internalism](contextualist-internalism-synthesis-130e5ac9-b512-486c-b0ba-4156d7ff0e7d.md)

## Content

**Taking an umbrella because of a low chance of rain justified by a weather app is rational in low-stakes contexts.**, **In high-stakes contexts, such as needing to keep equipment dry, this justification may no longer suffice if the app fails to predict sudden weather changes.**
