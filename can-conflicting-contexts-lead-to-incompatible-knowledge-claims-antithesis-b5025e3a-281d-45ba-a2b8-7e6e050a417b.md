# Can conflicting contexts lead to incompatible knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b5025e3a-281d-45ba-a2b8-7e6e050a417b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge Summary](contextualist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-c38eaf7b-9feb-49d3-8375-2d19895c61fa.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two individuals in the same situation but with differing perceptions of the context. Intuitively, we either have to say that both have knowledge or only one does. If we say both have knowledge, it leads to contradictory knowledge claims. If we say only one has knowledge, it challenges the consistency of contextualism.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not lead to contradictions. This principle together with conflicting contextual claims entails an inconsistency within the framework.**
