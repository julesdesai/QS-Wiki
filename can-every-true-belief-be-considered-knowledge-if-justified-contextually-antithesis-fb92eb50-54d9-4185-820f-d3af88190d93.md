# Can every true belief be considered knowledge if justified contextually?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb92eb50-54d9-4185-820f-d3af88190d93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism on Epistemic Justification](contextualism-on-epistemic-justification-synthesis-c8f3ec75-75e9-400a-9969-d95699e610cc.md)

## Content

**A person who forms a belief based on a true proposition but without understanding the underlying reasons may still hold the belief for accidental or incorrect reasons.**, **In a scenario where a person believes a true proposition but is unaware of relevant evidence or justification, it is counterintuitive to claim they have knowledge.**
