# Does phenomenal realism account for nonexistent phenomena?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1cd5a65b-a57b-4a7b-95ac-a266d198f350
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Realism with Consistency Conditions](phenomenal-realism-with-consistency-conditions-synthesis-05f9226f-1221-4391-b2ae-790270c7a451.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person knows a proposition about fictional or abstract entities that do not correspond to any consistent real phenomena. Intuitively, this seems compatible with possessing knowledge despite a lack of phenomenal consistency.**, **It is implausible that knowledge of abstract or fictional propositions requires any consistency in perception of phenomena at all, questioning the necessity of phenomenal realism for such knowledge.**
