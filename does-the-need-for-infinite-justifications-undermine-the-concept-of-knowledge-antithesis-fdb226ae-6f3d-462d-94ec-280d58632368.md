# Does the need for infinite justifications undermine the concept of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fdb226ae-6f3d-462d-94ec-280d58632368
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Finite Justifications](infinitism-with-finite-justifications-synthesis-69ad7e03-7f20-4a4f-85ef-ebc999d85fe8.md)

## Content

**Consider the principle that knowledge is meant to be attainable and applicable within one's life. If knowledge requires an infinite series of justifications, it appears to be unreachable in any practical sense, making actual knowledge attainment implausible.**, **It is implausible that a concept like knowledge, which should be practically applicable, could depend on an infinite chain of justifications.**
