# Is infallibility too strict for pragmatic decision-making contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 578ac2ac-5ff5-4d3f-989f-de78537eb1c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infallibility](knowledge-as-infallibility-synthesis-8f0bcd35-bb4c-4192-816d-76a63a41903c.md)

## Content

**Pragmatic decision-making often involves acting on the best available information with some risk of error.**, **Requiring infallibility would paralyze decision-making by disallowing knowledge claims under conditions of uncertainty.**, **It is implausible to suggest that knowledge cannot inform action simply due to the possibility of error.**
