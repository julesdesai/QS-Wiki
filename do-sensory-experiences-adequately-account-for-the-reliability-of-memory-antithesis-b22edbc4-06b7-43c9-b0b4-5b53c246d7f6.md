# Do sensory experiences adequately account for the reliability of memory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b22edbc4-06b7-43c9-b0b4-5b53c246d7f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the reliability of memory is crucial for retaining consistent knowledge of past experiences. This principle requires that past sensory experiences inform present knowledge without the need for re-experience.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person reliably remembers an event from their childhood without having any current sensory experience of it. Intuitively, we would judge that they know of this event through memory rather than direct sensory experience.**
