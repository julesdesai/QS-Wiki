# Can a person’s belief alone justify their knowledge claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 262f1c9d-226f-483b-8863-07c1a6d34086
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a proposition without any supporting evidence or reason. Intuitively, mere belief should not suffice to justify a claim to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification beyond mere belief. This principle, coupled with the notion that belief alone constitutes knowledge, creates a tension between belief and justification.**, **It is implausible to regard mere belief without any supporting justification as sufficient for knowledge, contrary to the idea that belief alone constitutes knowing a proposition.**
