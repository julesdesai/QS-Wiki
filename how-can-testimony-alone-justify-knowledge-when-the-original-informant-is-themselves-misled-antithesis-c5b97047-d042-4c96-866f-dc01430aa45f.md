# How can testimony alone justify knowledge when the original informant is themselves misled?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5b97047-d042-4c96-866f-dc01430aa45f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Justification Theory](testimonial-justification-theory-synthesis-a0572f9e-ea0a-45f6-9bf1-a16e71038692.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which person A tells person B a fact that A believes to be true but is actually false. Intuitively, B does not gain knowledge because their belief is based on incorrect information.**, **It is implausible that a person can acquire knowledge from testimony if the testimony itself is based on a false premise or misinformation.**
