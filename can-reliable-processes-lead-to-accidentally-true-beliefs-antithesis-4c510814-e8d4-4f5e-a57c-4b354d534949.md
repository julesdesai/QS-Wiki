# Can reliable processes lead to accidentally true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c510814-e8d4-4f5e-a57c-4b354d534949
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-971ec002-e8db-455e-806a-7b81feaf9154.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition because of a reliable cognitive process, but the belief turns out to be true by pure chance. Intuitively, this does not seem to qualify as knowledge.**, **A person’s belief being true merely by coincidence challenges the notion of it being reliably produced and thus being knowledge.**
