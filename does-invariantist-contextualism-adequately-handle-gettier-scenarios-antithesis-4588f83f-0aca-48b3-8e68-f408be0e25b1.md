# Does invariantist contextualism adequately handle 'Gettier' scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4588f83f-0aca-48b3-8e68-f408be0e25b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Contextualism](invariantist-contextualism-synthesis-72e0f6e6-e984-47a7-9872-225586edec54.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where a person forms a belief based on a justified true belief that is accidentally true, intuitively, the person does not have knowledge.**, **Invariantist contextualism suggests that if a person believes a proposition and the proposition is true, then the person knows it.**, **This view struggles to accommodate cases where justified true beliefs fail to constitute knowledge due to luck.**
