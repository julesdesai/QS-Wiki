# Can luck and accidental truth create a failing belief safety?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e67308cc-290b-4234-98b3-37f288e79f2e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who discovers a lost keychain under a couch while looking for a TV remote, forming a belief that the keychain was there because they accidentally found it. Intuitively, the belief's truth seems coincidental rather than knowledgeable.**, **A belief is not safe if it involves luck or coincidental truth since similar searches in nearby possible worlds do not necessarily lead to the same outcome. Such accidental discoveries undermine the safety criterion for belief.**
