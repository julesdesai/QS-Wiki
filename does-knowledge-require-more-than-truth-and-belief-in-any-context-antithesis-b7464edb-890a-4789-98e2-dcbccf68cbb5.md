# Does knowledge require more than truth and belief in any context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7464edb-890a-4789-98e2-dcbccf68cbb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-d2c0abd4-6924-4f8e-8c3f-b341856f4998.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition about a future event that turns out to be true by sheer chance. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks knowledge because mere truth and belief are insufficient without justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is more than just true belief, owing to its reliance on justificatory standards. This principle contradicts the claim that knowledge is granted solely by true belief.**
