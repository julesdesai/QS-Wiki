# Is contextualism compatible with the intuitive appeal of common knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2b7474d6-5334-4d32-b73a-4bf0540352d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Common knowledge refers to information everyone in a community is aware of and accepts. Intuitively, knowledge should not vary so much that something known to all simultaneously is lost due to contextual shifts.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, especially common knowledge, can vary between contexts to such an extent that it is lost, as this would undermine social coordination and communication.**
