# How does infinitism handle immediate and foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d25c7362-e074-4f51-b79b-a0ad4caa7cc9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-fd5e1271-f2df-4333-a434-50b9d95350fd.md)

## Content

**Immediate and foundational beliefs serve as starting points for reasoning, which conflicts with the idea of an infinite chain that requires no foundation.**, **It is implausible that a person can have knowledge if immediate or foundational beliefs, which do not have infinite regress, cannot be included in a chain of reasons.**, **If knowledge requires an infinite chain of reasons, then the role of foundational beliefs becomes problematic and seemingly undermines any starting point for knowledge.**
