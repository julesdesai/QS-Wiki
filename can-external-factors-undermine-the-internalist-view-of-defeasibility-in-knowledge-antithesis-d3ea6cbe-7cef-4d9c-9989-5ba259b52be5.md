# Can external factors undermine the internalist view of defeasibility in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3ea6cbe-7cef-4d9c-9989-5ba259b52be5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person arrives at a true belief through a reliable belief-forming process but is unaware of potential defeaters. Intuitively, this belief-forming process seems to grant knowledge despite the person's lack of awareness of defeaters.**, **A belief formed by a reliable method should qualify as knowledge even if the individual is unaware of information that might act as a defeater.**, **If a person cannot be expected to be aware of all potential defeaters, internalist requirements for knowledge may be excessively demanding.**
