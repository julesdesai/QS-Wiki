# Can sensitivity to truth in knowledge be undermined by the corollary of luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5a9476a-c7e3-4363-b90e-eb1a22456d91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition due to their overall situation being fortunate and the proposition happens to be true because of this luck. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge even though the belief turned out to be true by chance.**, **It is implausible that sensitivity to truth, as defined, accounts for the absence of knowledge when true belief arises from lucky circumstances.**
