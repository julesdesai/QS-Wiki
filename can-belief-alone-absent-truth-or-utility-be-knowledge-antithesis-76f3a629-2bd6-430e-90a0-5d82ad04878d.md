# Can belief alone, absent truth or utility, be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76f3a629-2bd6-430e-90a0-5d82ad04878d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person strongly believes a proposition based on personal conviction without regard to its truth or usefulness. Intuitively, such a belief does not amount to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that requires belief to be true and justified or useful to be classified as knowledge. This suggests that belief alone, absent truth or utility, cannot be knowledge.**
