# Do all epistemic frameworks allow for equally valid justifications?

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Relativism with Justification Relative to Frameworks](epistemic-relativism-with-justification-relative-to-frameworks-synthesis-ad42e55b-b997-4355-ba09-4958e4df6c61.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which two individuals, Bob and Carol, have conflicting beliefs about the natural world due to their distinct epistemic frameworks. Intuitively, we either have to say that Bob's belief is equally justified as Carol's, or that one holds more epistemic weight. If we say Bob's belief is equally justified, then rational discourse on empirical matters becomes impossible. If we say Carol's belief has more weight, then not all frameworks are equally valid.**
