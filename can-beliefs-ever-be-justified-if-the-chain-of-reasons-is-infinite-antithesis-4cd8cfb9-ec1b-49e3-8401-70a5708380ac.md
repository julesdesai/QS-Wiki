# Can beliefs ever be justified if the chain of reasons is infinite?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4cd8cfb9-ec1b-49e3-8401-70a5708380ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Infinitism](contextualist-infinitism-synthesis-74ebcf03-3de8-4e28-950c-dc2eefc9dc13.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification ought to be complete in order to establish knowledge.**, **If justification requires an infinite chain of reasons, then it is impossible to ever complete the justification process.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can be established by an incomplete justification process.**
