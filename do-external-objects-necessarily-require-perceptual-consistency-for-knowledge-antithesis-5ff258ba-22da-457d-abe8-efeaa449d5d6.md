# Do external objects necessarily require perceptual consistency for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5ff258ba-22da-457d-abe8-efeaa449d5d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Experience Consistency](phenomenal-experience-consistency-synthesis-dff6895e-5f7e-47e4-9fd7-5b1463da077a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which external objects like distant stars are known through instruments and calculations instead of direct perception. Intuitively, we either have to say that knowledge of such objects is impossible without perception or that knowledge can be instrumentally mediated. If we say knowledge is impossible without perception, then we dismiss mediated knowledge. If we say knowledge can be instrumentally mediated, we allow for knowledge beyond direct perception.**
