# Can coherentism accommodate perceptual knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b5f48de6-242d-41f5-b2fe-566d839233c7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Epistemology](coherentism-in-epistemology-synthesis-03124e37-be18-4868-b2cf-e370b5186105.md)

## Content

**Perceptual knowledge seems to arise directly from sensory experience, rather than from mutual support within a web of beliefs.**, **A person might see a tree and form the belief that there is a tree due to this direct perception, not due to coherence with other beliefs.**, **Intuitively, perceptual knowledge appears to be a primary source of justification that does not depend on coherence.**
