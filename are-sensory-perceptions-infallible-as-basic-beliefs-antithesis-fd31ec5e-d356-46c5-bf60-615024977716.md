# Are sensory perceptions infallible as basic beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd31ec5e-d356-46c5-bf60-615024977716
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone, due to an optical illusion, believes they see water on the road when it is actually a mirage. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the belief based on this sensory perception is mistaken.**, **There is a plausible principle that sensory perceptions can be fallible, leading to incorrect basic beliefs. This principle together with the variability of human perception entails that basic beliefs evident to the senses may not always be reliable.**
