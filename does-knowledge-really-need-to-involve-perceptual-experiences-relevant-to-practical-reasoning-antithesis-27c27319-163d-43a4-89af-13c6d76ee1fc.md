# Does knowledge really need to involve perceptual experiences relevant to practical reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27c27319-163d-43a4-89af-13c6d76ee1fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Phenomenalism](pragmatic-phenomenalism-synthesis-6f843995-ce4f-432d-b326-41e0e18386c6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a mathematician believes a complex mathematical theorem based purely on rigorous logical proof with no connection to perceptual experience. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the mathematician has knowledge of the theorem.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can sometimes be purely abstract and not involve perceptual experiences. This principle suggests that knowledge does not require perceptual experiences relevant to practical reasoning.**
