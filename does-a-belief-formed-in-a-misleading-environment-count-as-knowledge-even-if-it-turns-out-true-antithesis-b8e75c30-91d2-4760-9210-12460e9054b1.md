# Does a belief formed in a misleading environment count as knowledge even if it turns out true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8e75c30-91d2-4760-9210-12460e9054b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety](knowledge-as-safety-synthesis-32d99a7a-9598-4d4c-84b2-a8ab3d13c5e9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief that a wire is live because they see it sparking, but unknown to them, the sparking is an isolated and rare defect, yet the wire is indeed live. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the wire is live because the circumstances deceive even though the belief is true.**, **It is implausible that a person can have knowledge if their true belief is formed in a misleading environment unrelated to the truth of the belief itself.**
