# Is all perceptual experience reliable for forming knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56cc892f-8560-477c-b885-652d53117f5f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Contextualism](phenomenal-contextualism-synthesis-37be371b-8eaa-49b9-96b8-60f2ca6919b6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is under the influence of a drug that distorts their perceptual experiences, such as LSD. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person's altered perceptions cannot be relied upon for accurate knowledge formation.**, **There is a plausible principle that not all perceptual experiences are equally reliable for forming knowledge. This principle together with the assumption that all contextual experiences form valid knowledge calls into question the reliability aspect.**
