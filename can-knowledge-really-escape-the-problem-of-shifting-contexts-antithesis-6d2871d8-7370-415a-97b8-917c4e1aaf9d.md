# Can knowledge really escape the problem of shifting contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d2871d8-7370-415a-97b8-917c4e1aaf9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**Suppose one must frequently shift contexts in their everyday environments, leading to constant reevaluation of knowledge claims. Intuitively, this persistent shifting complicates and perhaps undermines the utility of claiming to 'know' anything at all.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, necessary for decision-making and action, should be so easily destabilized by contextual shifts.**
