# Does coherentism allow for accidental coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e8a8961-8741-46b6-9079-288bb3325f10
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone holds a coherent set of beliefs about their daily routine, but this coherence is due entirely to cognitive biases resulting in accidental accuracy. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the beliefs are not truly justified despite their coherence.**, **There is a plausible principle that mere coherence without dependable processes should not render beliefs justified. Coherence could be an accidental byproduct of flawed reasoning or biased information gathering.**
