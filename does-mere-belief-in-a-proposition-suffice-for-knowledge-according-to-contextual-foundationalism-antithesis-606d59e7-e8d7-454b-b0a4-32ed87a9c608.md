# Does mere belief in a proposition suffice for knowledge according to Contextual Foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 606d59e7-e8d7-454b-b0a4-32ed87a9c608
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-3cd555af-8ec3-406f-b70a-64f89852bc49.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on wishful thinking without any evidence. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that they do not have knowledge.**, **A plausible requirement of knowledge is that it should have proper justification beyond mere belief. This implies that belief alone does not suffice for knowledge.**
