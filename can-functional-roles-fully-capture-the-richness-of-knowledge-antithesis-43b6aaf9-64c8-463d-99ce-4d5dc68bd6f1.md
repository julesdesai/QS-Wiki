# Can functional roles fully capture the richness of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43b6aaf9-64c8-463d-99ce-4d5dc68bd6f1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functional Phenomenalism](functional-phenomenalism-synthesis-2d380001-bba7-40c1-89a8-df1e50353485.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge encompasses qualitative aspects beyond functional roles. This principle suggests that knowledge is richer than just predictable outcomes or functional impacts.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person knows the experience of feeling cold on a winter day that cannot be fully captured by functional roles. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that knowledge of experience includes qualitative richness.**
