# Can one know propositions about unperceived phenomena?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** befab1fc-2f6b-49a9-9f7c-a509a3183eea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-eea6cb23-77a9-47a4-87da-736833e1953e.md)

## Content

**There are many propositions about unperceived phenomena, such as historical events or distant galaxies, that people claim to know.**, **Knowledge of unperceived phenomena cannot rely on direct sensory experiences, because these phenomena are not presently observed.**, **People frequently claim to have knowledge of phenomena, like the existence of dinosaurs or the structure of the Milky Way galaxy, based on indirect evidence rather than direct perception.**
