# How does the causal theory address cases with epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 01f32044-45fe-4298-a7ac-83bc637eb34c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-336c94a4-e4b0-4a1c-b1db-218c9b8f6add.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes their keys are in the kitchen because they left them there, but unbeknownst to them, someone else moved the keys elsewhere and then back. Here, the belief is true and causally connected, but subject to epistemic luck.**, **Epistemic luck can result in true beliefs that are not knowledge, since the truth of the belief appears to be coincidental rather than via an appropriate causal link.**
