# Knowledge Through Contextual Coercion

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** af7a033b-9d4c-4e61-a9d4-8bc0931dfa89
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can beliefs formed under duress be knowledge?](can-beliefs-formed-under-duress-be-knowledge-antithesis-fe972929-e5e4-4829-b660-58e13f0c6ac0.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief aligns with objective reality**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is not necessary that the belief is formed free from contextual coercion**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief aligns with objective reality**
