# Are all intellectual virtues epistemically relevant?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0fea36fb-6ac2-4318-8346-84a7e8eb7bf6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has intellectual virtues unrelated to belief formation, such as aesthetic judgment. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that these virtues do not contribute directly to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that only certain intellectual virtues are relevant for the formation of knowledge, such as truth-seeking.**, **It is implausible that every intellectual virtue contributes equally to the epistemic quality of beliefs.**
