# Could knowledge derive from a blend of virtue and external aids?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0dfac4c-e375-4cf7-88fc-9530a7a93880
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge depends solely on internal intellectual virtues if external tools or aids can consistently result in true beliefs.**, **Consider a scenario where a person uses a computer program that they don't understand, yet it consistently guides them to true beliefs. In such a scenario, it seems intuitive to credit them with knowledge despite absence of an intellectual virtue concerning the program's mechanics.**
