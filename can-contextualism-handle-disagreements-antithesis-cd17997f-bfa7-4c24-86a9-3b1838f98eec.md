# Can Contextualism Handle Disagreements?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd17997f-bfa7-4c24-86a9-3b1838f98eec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-6d1ded2e-2e81-4f10-b6c8-db699d1664cc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people observe the same event but interpret it differently due to their subjective experiences. Intuitively, we would judge that at least one person must be more correct than the other.**, **If knowledge is defined by context, then disagreements might just be a reflection of differing contexts, and thus both individuals could be considered correct, which seems counterintuitive.**, **The requirement that opposing interpretations can both be true undermines the notion of a singular reality.**
