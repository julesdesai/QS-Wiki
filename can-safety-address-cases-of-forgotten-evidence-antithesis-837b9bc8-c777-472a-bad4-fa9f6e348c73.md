# Can safety address cases of forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 837b9bc8-c777-472a-bad4-fa9f6e348c73
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person once had strong evidence for a belief but has since forgotten this evidence. They retain the belief and it remains true, though the current basis for the belief seems weak. Intuitively, this does not constitute knowledge.**, **A belief is safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. Forgotten evidence complicates the assessment of whether a belief safely qualifies as knowledge.**
