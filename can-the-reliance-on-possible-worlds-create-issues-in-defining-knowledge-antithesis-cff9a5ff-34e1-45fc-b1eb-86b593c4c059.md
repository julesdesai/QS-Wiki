# Can the reliance on possible worlds create issues in defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cff9a5ff-34e1-45fc-b1eb-86b593c4c059
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-34b45a3c-9960-48d7-bb61-82e72864432f.md)

## Content

**Tracking theory depends on hypothetical scenarios across possible worlds to establish truth-tracking, which can lead to problems because our intuitions about knowledge focus on actual evidence and justifications available to the believer in the actual world.**, **Reliance on counterfactual situations may intuitively seem to disconnect from our practical understanding of knowledge acquisition as grounded in actual reasoning processes.**
