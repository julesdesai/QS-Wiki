# How can we differentiate between true perception and hallucination without justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76edbf99-a3c0-4c5b-b08d-071a046c7319
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates seeing a green tree and believes it is real. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the subject needs justification to differentiate between true perception and hallucination.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a way to rule out misleading information, like hallucinations. This principle together with the view that perception provides immediate knowledge with no justification results in a contradiction with the need for distinguishing true perceptions from false ones.**
