# Can reliance on outdated information constitute a reliable process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9104f2ee-4ced-49b9-9507-51f690fa8664
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliabilism](knowledge-as-reliabilism-synthesis-e43dfc1f-e9e5-4ef5-90d3-e8588e1acc09.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who believes that Paris is the capital of France based entirely on a geography book from the 19th century, which is true by current standards. Intuitively, we would judge this is not knowledge due to reliance on outdated information.**, **Even if a cognitive process fits within a historically reliable framework, reliance on obsolete methods prevents it from being genuinely reliable for forming current true beliefs.**
