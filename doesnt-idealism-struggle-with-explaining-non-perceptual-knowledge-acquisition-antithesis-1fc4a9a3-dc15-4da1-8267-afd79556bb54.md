# Doesn't idealism struggle with explaining non-perceptual knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fc4a9a3-dc15-4da1-8267-afd79556bb54
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Idealism](idealism-synthesis-6a8ea4b4-8cf6-44ff-98fb-cf08805f781d.md)

## Content

**Mathematical knowledge, such as the truth of 2+2=4, does not rely on sensory perception or mental constructs tied to external reality. Intuitively, mathematical truths exist independent of perceptual experiences.**, **The existence of abstract knowledge not derived from perceptual experiences challenges the view that knowledge is constituted solely by mental phenomena.**
