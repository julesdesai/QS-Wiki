# Does changing context challenge the communicability of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27843836-b41a-4379-a0e2-75ac8b8a2209
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which an individual attempts to share knowledge, but due to differing contextual understandings, the recipient fails to grasp or adequately evaluate the claim. Intuitively, this challenges the view that knowledge can be effectively communicated and understood across different contexts.**, **There is a plausible principle that for knowledge to be of practical use, it must be communicable between individuals, yet different contexts appear to obstruct this process.**
