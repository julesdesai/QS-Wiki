# Can someone mistakenly have knowledge due to varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a5ff15f-ce77-4b37-b39a-72f941521a9f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism adapts justification to situations](contextualism-adapts-justification-to-situations-synthesis-6de50ec6-d692-4487-9677-ea468fdc8bbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person believes it is raining because they see a wet street. Intuitively, in this context, we might say the person knows it is raining.**, **Consider another situation where the same person believes it is raining because of the wet street, but later it is revealed a street sweeper had just passed by. Intuitively, it seems incorrect to say the person knew it was raining.**, **If justification for belief varies with context, then a person can be seen as knowing in one context but not another despite having the same belief and truth conditions in both scenarios.**
