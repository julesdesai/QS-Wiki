# Can reliability be undermined by environmental factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec22e035-8a40-414b-a27d-680ff8529205
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a generally reliable cognitive process leads to a true belief in an environment full of misleading evidence. Intuitively, we might argue the person does not know given the misleading context.**, **There is a reasonable principle that knowledge should be sensitive to the surrounding environment and misleading evidence may undermine the reliability of the process.**
