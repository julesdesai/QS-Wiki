# Does reliance on intellectual virtue exclude cases of lucky knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cdabd4c4-381d-4d99-a1b6-ad9f9de65944
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-35af9163-7530-4447-9474-ccb688222939.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief by chance or luck, such as by guessing correctly. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the belief does not result from the exercise of intellectual virtue.**, **It is implausible that an account of knowledge would exclude all forms of knowledge acquired through chance or luck, as some forms of lucky knowledge are still considered knowledge.**
