# Could beliefs that serve as useful deceptions count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4975940-9298-476b-aca2-7509f919c078
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Coherentism](pragmatism-with-coherentism-synthesis-7996efa8-d25e-4d54-acc0-6469819e4077.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person benefits from holding a false belief due to social deception. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that being deceived, even beneficially, does not equate to knowing.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth. This principle together with the acceptability of deceptions as useful beliefs contradicts the notion of deception as knowledge.**
