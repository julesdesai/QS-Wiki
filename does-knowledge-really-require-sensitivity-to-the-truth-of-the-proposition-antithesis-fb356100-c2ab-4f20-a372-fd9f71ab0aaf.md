# Does knowledge really require sensitivity to the truth of the proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb356100-c2ab-4f20-a372-fd9f71ab0aaf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Sensitivity Theory](nozicks-sensitivity-theory-synthesis-094644b1-cf24-4462-a6c6-f40dc1c2de1a.md)

## Content

**A person can have a justified true belief in a proposition without that belief being sensitive to the truth of the proposition.**, **A person on a fairground with a set of signs reading "The fair opens at 9 AM" may believe the fair opens at 9 AM because they've seen the signs, and it is true that the fair opens at 9 AM, although they would still believe it if the signs were misleadingly left out from yesterday.**
