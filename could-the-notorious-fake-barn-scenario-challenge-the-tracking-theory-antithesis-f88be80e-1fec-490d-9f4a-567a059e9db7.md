# Could the notorious "Fake Barn" scenario challenge the Tracking Theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f88be80e-1fec-490d-9f4a-567a059e9db7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-afb98a5d-d8c8-4475-9a7e-863f117c2239.md)

## Content

**In a region filled with fake barns, a person sees a real barn and believes it is a barn. Intuitively, this person does not have knowledge.**, **If the person were looking at a fake barn, they might not pretend to have a belief that they are looking at something that is not a barn.**, **In a similar situation with fake barns, if the person is looking at a real barn, they would still believe it is a barn.**, **The conditions in the fake barn scenario can be slightly modified such that the person’s belief tracks the truth but still does not amount to knowledge.**
