# Does contextualism make knowledge claims subjective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2374319e-82d1-4c6f-a90f-d81ead569d68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism adapts justification to situations](contextualism-adapts-justification-to-situations-synthesis-6de50ec6-d692-4487-9677-ea468fdc8bbe.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be objective rather than subjective.**, **If justification for a belief varies depending on the context, then knowledge can become dependent on subjective criteria, such as who is making the judgment and under what conditions.**, **If knowledge is subject to personal or situational biases, then it could lead to contradictory claims where a person both knows and does not know the same proposition in different contexts.**
