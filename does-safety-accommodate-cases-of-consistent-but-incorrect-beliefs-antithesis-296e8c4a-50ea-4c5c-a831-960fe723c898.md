# Does safety accommodate cases of consistent but incorrect beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 296e8c4a-50ea-4c5c-a831-960fe723c898
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-526116dd-9931-4287-b7a5-0d90efcd6c5e.md)

## Content

**People can hold beliefs that consistently guide them incorrectly due to systematic errors in their belief system.**, **If such beliefs persist across nearby possible worlds, they could satisfy the safety condition but still be false, undermining the reliability of safety as a knowledge criterion.**
