# How does contextualism address shifting standards within a single conversation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a074226-d8c7-48c1-bd82-bb1e0f11dc18
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a conversation shifts from discussing everyday matters to high-stakes matters that demand stricter standards for knowledge. In such a situation, intuitively, it seems problematic for someone to know a proposition at one moment and then not know it at another simply because the stakes have changed within the same conversation.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be subject to frequent and arbitrary changes. This principle suggests that contextual shifts within the same discourse challenge the coherence of knowledge.**
