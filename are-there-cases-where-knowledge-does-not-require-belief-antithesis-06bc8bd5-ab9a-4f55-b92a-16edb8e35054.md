# Are there cases where knowledge does not require belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06bc8bd5-ab9a-4f55-b92a-16edb8e35054
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalism](internalism-thesis-697c012e-ac77-4c5b-935c-0bf2d4d5ce83.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person solves a complex mathematical problem correctly but underestimates their abilities and does not believe they have solved it correctly. Intuitively, we might still say that they know the solution due to their competence and correct understanding.**, **There is a plausible principle suggesting that in some cases, possessing accurate competence and understanding might suffice for knowledge, even if belief is absent.**
