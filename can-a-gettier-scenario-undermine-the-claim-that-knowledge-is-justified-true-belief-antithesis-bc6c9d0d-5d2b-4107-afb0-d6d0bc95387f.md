# Can a Gettier scenario undermine the claim that knowledge is justified true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc6c9d0d-5d2b-4107-afb0-d6d0bc95387f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person, Smith, believes that a colleague owns a Ford because he sees him driving one every day. The colleague indeed owns a Ford. However, unknown to Smith, the evidence is misleading because the colleague also frequently rents a similar Ford. Intuitively, we would judge that Smith does not have knowledge of the colleague owning a Ford despite having justified true belief.**, **A case in which a person has a justified true belief but lacks knowledge suggests that justified true belief is insufficient for knowledge.**
