# Can contextual factors provide inconsistent justifications?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4182bcae-6661-403c-b240-5f3dcec51789
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**A scenario involves a person believing a proposition while the contextual cues support the belief, yet those cues depend on misleading information.**, **A plausible principle is that a belief should be justified without relying on misleading information. If justified beliefs depend on misleading contextual cues, the reliability of these justifications becomes questionable.**
