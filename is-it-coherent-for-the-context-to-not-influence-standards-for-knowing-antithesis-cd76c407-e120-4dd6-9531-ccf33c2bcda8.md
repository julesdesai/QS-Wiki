# Is it coherent for the context to not influence standards for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd76c407-e120-4dd6-9531-ccf33c2bcda8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**Different contexts imply different stakes or implications of being right or wrong, suggesting that the standards for whether someone knows something could reasonably vary along with the context.**, **In cases of differential stakes, such as a quiz versus a courtroom, our intuitions are that the evidential standards for knowing should vary with context, which suggests standards for knowing are context-sensitive.**
