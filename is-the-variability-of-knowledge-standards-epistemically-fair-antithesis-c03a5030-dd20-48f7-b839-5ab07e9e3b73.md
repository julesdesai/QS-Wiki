# Is the variability of knowledge standards epistemically fair?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c03a5030-dd20-48f7-b839-5ab07e9e3b73
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**The standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, suggesting potential unfairness in knowledge attributions.**, **Consider two people with equal evidence for a belief: one knows due to being in a low-stakes context, the other doesn't in a high-stakes context. Intuitively, this seems unfair and suggests a conceptual issue with variable standards.**
