# Can beliefs that are not foundational still provide justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 330577d7-41a6-44ba-99a7-4988607a2bc3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-c35a9476-dc46-42db-bda2-17c735809ae7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a justified belief that is based on beliefs that are not self-evident or empirically certain. Intuitively, this belief still seems justified.**, **It is plausible to think that some beliefs can derive their justification from coherence with other beliefs, rather than solely from foundational beliefs.**
