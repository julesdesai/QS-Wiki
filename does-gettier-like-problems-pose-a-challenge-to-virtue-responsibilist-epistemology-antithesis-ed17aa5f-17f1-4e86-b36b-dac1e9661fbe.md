# Does Gettier-like problems pose a challenge to virtue-responsibilist epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ed17aa5f-17f1-4e86-b36b-dac1e9661fbe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on intellectual virtue, and the belief turns out to be true by coincidence; intuitively, we would judge that the belief is not knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be coincidentally true. This principle together with the view that a belief arising from intellectual virtue is sufficient for knowledge suggests a contradiction if the belief is true by coincidence.**
