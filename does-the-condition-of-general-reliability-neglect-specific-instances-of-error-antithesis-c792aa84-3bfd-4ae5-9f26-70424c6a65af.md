# Does the condition of general reliability neglect specific instances of error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c792aa84-3bfd-4ae5-9f26-70424c6a65af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which a generally reliable cognitive process accidentally results in a false true belief due to random chance. In such a case, intuitively we might say the person does not know.**, **A plausible principle is that true belief resulting from reliable processes should not depend on chance occurrences for it to be considered knowledge.**
