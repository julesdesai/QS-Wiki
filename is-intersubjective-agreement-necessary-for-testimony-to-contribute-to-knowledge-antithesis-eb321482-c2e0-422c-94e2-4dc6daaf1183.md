# Is intersubjective agreement necessary for testimony to contribute to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eb321482-c2e0-422c-94e2-4dc6daaf1183
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which persons A and B witness a theft. A reports seeing a tall suspect, while B reports a short suspect. Intuitively, we would judge that without intersubjective agreement, the testimony cannot reliably contribute to perception-based knowledge.**, **It is implausible that testimony contributes to knowledge without sufficient intersubjective agreement to confirm a shared perceptual account.**
