# Can a person lack knowledge despite a reliable belief-forming process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f11066e0-e9b5-44bf-ad9a-563b486cfeee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-73062a2b-1357-42ba-b901-d397b26dce96.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief through a generally reliable process but in a particular instance, an unusual and undisclosed factor makes the process unreliable. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks knowledge of the proposition.**, **Consider a case where a person is unaware that a generally reliable process was corrupted in a specific instance yet they retain their belief. Intuitively, we would judge this to be a lack of knowledge.**
