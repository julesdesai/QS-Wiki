# Can context lead to perceptual errors that undermine knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 223fdad1-dbf5-4307-a599-86f507573ebf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Contextualism Summary](perceptual-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9fb6be1-a4e1-46bf-91f2-7fc63c3abb0c.md)

## Content

**In certain situations, context may lead individuals to misinterpret sensory information, such as seeing a rope as a snake in dim light.**, **Such perceptual errors can lead to knowledge claims that do not correspond to reality, despite being contextually justified.**, **If context can lead to perceptual errors, it challenges whether contextually modified perception can reliably be said to lead to knowledge.**
