# Can coherence justify widely divergent interpretations of evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30d879e3-c3bb-4e52-88a0-62e3926a3566
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism: System of Beliefs](coherentism-system-of-beliefs-synthesis-446f5d76-49b9-4fbc-a49a-1aaf884652f2.md)

## Content

**Different individuals can interpret the same evidence differently yet coherently within separate frameworks.**, **Coherence allows for multiple, potentially incompatible interpretations of the same evidence.**, **Knowledge should yield consistent truths, not divergent interpretations.**, **Therefore, coherence alone may not adequately justify any particular interpretation as knowledge.**
