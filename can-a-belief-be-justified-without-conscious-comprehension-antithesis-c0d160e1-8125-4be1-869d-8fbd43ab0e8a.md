# Can a belief be justified without conscious comprehension?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0d160e1-8125-4be1-869d-8fbd43ab0e8a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Non-Conscious Elements](coherentism-with-non-conscious-elements-synthesis-025108b6-66b5-4784-9945-6373af308ffa.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person holds a belief that fits within a coherent network they are not consciously aware of. It seems counterintuitive to claim that the belief is justified without any conscious comprehension of its relationship to the network.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification for belief requires at least some degree of conscious comprehension of how the belief fits within a coherent system.**
