# Are contextual factors reliable indicators of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2a31742b-b63a-43fc-afd4-e994a4d4a86f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where two people in different conversational contexts reach conflicting conclusions about whether they know the same proposition. Intuitively, we either have to say that one of them lacks knowledge, or redefine knowledge as context-dependent.**, **It is implausible that the mere context of a conversation can alter the truth of a knowledge claim, as this would imply that knowledge depends on external rather than intrinsic criteria.**
