# Does invariantism address the impact of expertise on knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fde4ae96-a0cb-4e6b-b72f-b6ad8e95bfea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an expert and a novice both believe a true proposition, but the expert has extensive evidence that the novice lacks. In this scenario, intuitively, the expert, not the novice, is more justified in claiming knowledge.**, **The principle that the standards for knowing a proposition remain constant across different contexts suggests both should equally know the proposition.**, **It is implausible that expertise and additional evidence do not affect whether a belief qualifies as knowledge.**
