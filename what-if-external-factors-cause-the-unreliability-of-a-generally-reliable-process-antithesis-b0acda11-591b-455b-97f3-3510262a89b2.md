# What if external factors cause the unreliability of a generally reliable process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0acda11-591b-455b-97f3-3510262a89b2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**Suppose a generally reliable weather forecast leads someone to believe it will rain, but due to an unexpected meteorological anomaly, it doesn't. Intuitively, we consider the belief as unjustified.**, **A process that generally leads to true beliefs can be undermined by external factors, which suggests that occasional failings challenge its reliability.**, **The knowledge claim falters since the anomaly affects the process's reliability, calling into question the connection between reliability and knowledge.**
