# Can justified true belief require an infinite chain of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 610d50ae-effe-4185-8317-5f75081a2f77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Justified Belief](coherentism-in-justified-belief-synthesis-5c04c2a6-2653-47f8-81ab-a9d276d37a24.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief system contains no foundational beliefs that terminate justification. Intuitively, this suggests an infinite regress problem where each belief requires another to justify it.**, **In an infinite regress of justifications, it is implausible that any single belief can ever be fully justified.**, **If no belief is fully justified due to an infinite regress, then it seems doubtful that the coherence of a web of beliefs can establish justification.**
