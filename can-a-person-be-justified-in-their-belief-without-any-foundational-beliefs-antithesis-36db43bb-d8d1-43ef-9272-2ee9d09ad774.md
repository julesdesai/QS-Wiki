# Can a person be justified in their belief without any foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36db43bb-d8d1-43ef-9272-2ee9d09ad774
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief solely from eyewitness testimony. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person needs some foundational belief about the reliability of testimony to be justified.**, **There is a plausible principle that justified beliefs must be supported by more fundamental beliefs or evidence. This principle together with the necessity of foundational beliefs entails that justification requires some form of foundational belief.**
