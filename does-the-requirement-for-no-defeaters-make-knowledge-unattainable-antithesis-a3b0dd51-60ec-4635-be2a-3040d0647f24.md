# Does the requirement for no defeaters make knowledge unattainable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3b0dd51-60ec-4635-be2a-3040d0647f24
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone’s justified belief is later undermined by new evidence they couldn’t possibly have known at the time. Intuitively, this person had knowledge before the new evidence came to light.**, **If a belief must be free from all possible defeaters to count as knowledge, then knowledge might become too stringent to achieve.**, **Demonstrating a belief is free from all potential defeaters, known or unknown, may set an unattainable standard for knowledge.**
