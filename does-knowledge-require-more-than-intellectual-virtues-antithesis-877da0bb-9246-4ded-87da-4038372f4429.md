# Does knowledge require more than intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 877da0bb-9246-4ded-87da-4038372f4429
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a trusted and intelligent detective forms a belief about a suspect's guilt based on solid evidence and reasoning, which are intellectual virtues, but the belief is actually false due to hidden evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the detective does not know the suspect is guilty.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a belief is false, regardless of the exercise of intellectual virtues, it cannot constitute knowledge. This principle together with the necessity of truth for knowledge implies that intellectual virtue is not sufficient for knowledge if the belief is false.**
