# Can a belief be considered safe if minor alterations to circumstances lead to falsehood?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24181741-2d17-449f-97e8-8484d28f27fb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8bfe37cc-0831-4bcb-9f14-728b24c81cc0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person P believes a proposition R, and R is true.**, **Imagine minor alterations to the circumstances result in R becoming false, even though P's belief remains the same in those nearby situations.**, **An intuition might be that P knows R given the current circumstances and substantial evidence, despite the belief's instability in slightly altered scenarios.**, **If a belief's safety requires truth in most nearby possible worlds, then P's belief in R wouldn't be safe, contradicting our intuition about P's knowledge.**
