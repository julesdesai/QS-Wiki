# Is ruling out relevant alternatives always possible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aeaa7823-c3d9-4c98-98c1-3f311f72cd46
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-d7f52b7c-07b8-44a2-8804-2022a32ed27e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if ruling out relevant alternatives is required for knowledge, then this process must be feasible in practice. This principle together with real-life cognitive limitations entails that ruling out all relevant alternatives could be unachievable.**
