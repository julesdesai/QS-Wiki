# Does contextualism handle cross-contextual communication effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ab9d99d-8145-48f7-920d-28446fa35c6b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scientist conveying a knowledge claim to the general public whose contextual understanding is less stringent. Intuitively, there might be a failure in accurately communicating knowledge due to differing standards.**, **If the standards for knowing change based on conversational context, effective cross-contextual communication may be impaired as knowledge is interpreted differently by parties in different contexts.**
