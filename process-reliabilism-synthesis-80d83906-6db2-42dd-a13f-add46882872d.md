## Content

**A person knows a proposition if the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **The reliability of a cognitive process determines the justification of a belief**, **The standards for knowing a proposition depend on the reliability of the belief-forming process, not the context**
