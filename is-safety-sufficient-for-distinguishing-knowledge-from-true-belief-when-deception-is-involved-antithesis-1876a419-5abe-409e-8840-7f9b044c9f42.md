# Is safety sufficient for distinguishing knowledge from true belief when deception is involved?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1876a419-5abe-409e-8840-7f9b044c9f42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition View](safety-condition-view-synthesis-739cc2e2-5111-4640-9af6-f9324f258620.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person confidently believes they recognize their friend in a crowded place when it is actually an actor hired to dress identically to their friend. Intuitively, the belief is false in the actual world.**, **In some nearby possible worlds, this belief might still be true if the actual friend happened to be at the same place, making the belief safe yet not knowledge.**
