# Does perceptual constancy challenge immediate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72492514-7dfd-4e43-8424-705c9b167da0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-63f38132-cda9-42dc-9c71-38670cc01e77.md)

## Content

**Perceptual constancy is the tendency to see familiar objects as having standard shape, size, and color regardless of changes in angle, distance, or lighting.**, **In a scenario where a coin is viewed from different angles but is perceived as round, perceptual constancy suggests built-in cognitive adjustments.**, **If perception is influenced by cognitive adjustments, then perception may not be purely direct and immediate knowledge of external objects.**
