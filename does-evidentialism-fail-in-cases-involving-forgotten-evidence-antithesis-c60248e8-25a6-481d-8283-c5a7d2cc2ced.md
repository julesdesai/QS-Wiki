# Does Evidentialism fail in cases involving forgotten evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c60248e8-25a6-481d-8283-c5a7d2cc2ced
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-a76d832e-447a-4168-b9be-6c867a1a7af1.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person holds a belief for which they once had evidence, but they can no longer recall it. Intuitively, we either have to say that the belief is still justified or that it is not. If we say it is justified, then current evidence is not always necessary for justification, posing a challenge to evidentialism.**
