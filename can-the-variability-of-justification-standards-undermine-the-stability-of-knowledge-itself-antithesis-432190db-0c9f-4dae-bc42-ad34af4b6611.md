# Can the variability of justification standards undermine the stability of knowledge itself?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 432190db-0c9f-4dae-bc42-ad34af4b6611
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-55297f9c-50a2-4f24-b1b0-6029f7bef7b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people, in the same situation, hold beliefs about the same proposition but are subject to different contextual standards of justification. Intuitively, if both beliefs are true and they are similarly justified, they both should know the proposition, but this leads to contradictory attributions of knowledge.**, **Suppose knowledge requires a true belief justified by stable standards. If justification is context-dependent, the conditions for having knowledge might change unpredictably, undermining the validity of knowledge claims.**
