# Can a belief track the truth and yet be considered counterintuitive or unreliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cb1d1cc8-009a-4550-a96c-d8f8f872e937
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it is raining because their neighbor always mentions the weather accurately. If the belief changes with weather, it tracks the truth. However, intuitively we would judge that relying on a neighbor's mention is not an ideal tracking method for knowledge.**, **It is plausible that knowledge requires a belief to be obtained in a methodologically sound way rather than through coincidental or unreliable means. A belief that tracks the truth but is methodologically unsound does not intuitively amount to knowledge.**
