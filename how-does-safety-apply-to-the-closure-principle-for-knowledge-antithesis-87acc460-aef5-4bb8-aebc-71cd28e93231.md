# How does safety apply to the closure principle for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87acc460-aef5-4bb8-aebc-71cd28e93231
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-b5a72759-42a3-4d9b-b74c-df40c84bdc9d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if you know one proposition, and know it entails another, you should know the second proposition as well. But the safety condition can imply exceptions to this principle.**, **Suppose a person knows a proposition safely but its entailment isn't safe. According to the closure principle, they should know the entailment, but the safety condition implies they don’t.**
