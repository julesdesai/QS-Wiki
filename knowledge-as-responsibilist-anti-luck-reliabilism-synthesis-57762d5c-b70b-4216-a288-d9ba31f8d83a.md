# Knowledge as Responsibilist Anti-Luck Reliabilism

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does reliability ignore luck affecting knowledge?](does-reliability-ignore-luck-affecting-knowledge-antithesis-f5d70159-4fac-4e3c-9412-1462fd59b9fd.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is formed by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the formation of the belief reflects the agent's cognitive responsibility**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief be free from significant dependence on luck**
