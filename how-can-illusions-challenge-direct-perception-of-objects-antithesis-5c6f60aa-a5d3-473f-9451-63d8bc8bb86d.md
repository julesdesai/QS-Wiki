# How can illusions challenge direct perception of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism with Direct Cross-Modal Sensing](naive-realism-with-direct-cross-modal-sensing-synthesis-96f2c374-253a-4f49-a9c2-1ee8405679ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a straight stick appears bent when placed in water. Intuitively, we either have to say that the perception of the stick is erroneous or that it is mediated by some representation that distorts its appearance.**, **If we say the perception is erroneous, then we recognize that our direct access to objects is fallible.**, **If we say the perception is mediated, then this suggests that intermediary representations play a role in how objects are perceived.**
