# Do practical successes always correlate with truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2af67d87-bbf1-47cb-895f-83042783bd91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**Consider a person throwing a dice, predicting a six, and betting on it. The dice lands on six due to sheer chance, not some underlying truth about their belief. Practical success in prediction does not necessarily equate with truth.**, **Random or coincidental practical success does not confirm the truth of a belief unless there's a causal connection—knowledge requires more than mere successful outcomes.**
