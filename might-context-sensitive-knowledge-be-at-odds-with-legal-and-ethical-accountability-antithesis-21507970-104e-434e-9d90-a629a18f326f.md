# Might context-sensitive knowledge be at odds with legal and ethical accountability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21507970-104e-434e-9d90-a629a18f326f
**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-cec2b7f9-8a52-4ab7-9dd4-6f75547db097.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that legal and ethical systems rely on stable concepts of knowledge for accountability. Contextual shifting of knowledge standards seems to conflict with this principle, as it could lead to inconsistencies in accountability.**
