# Does every justified belief equate to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe753761-c975-4a75-9501-37668686ccef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a reliable news source's weather prediction but it turns out to be incorrect due to unforeseen circumstances. Intuitively, we either have to say the belief was justified but did not constitute knowledge, or that justification must result in true belief. If we say that not all justified beliefs are true, then justified belief does not necessarily equate to knowledge.**
