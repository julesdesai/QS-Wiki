# Does contextualism allow for too much subjectivity in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd42ab5d-d06e-4426-9790-1fecc62841e9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-7d8a3858-3738-47ab-85b6-1a71b73ecaf5.md)

## Content

**A situation where two observers perceive the same event differently due to varying contexts leads to contradictory knowledge claims.**, **It is implausible that both observers can be said to know the truth if their contextual interpretations lead to opposing conclusions.**, **Suppose knowledge requires contextual interpretation; intuitively, this may lead to rampant relativism, where no objective knowledge is possible.**
