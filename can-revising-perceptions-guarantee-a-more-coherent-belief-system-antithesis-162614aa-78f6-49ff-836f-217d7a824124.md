# Can revising perceptions guarantee a more coherent belief system?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 162614aa-78f6-49ff-836f-217d7a824124
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism](coherentism-synthesis-fe89b418-bb8b-4164-8874-fa4a88946376.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where every new piece of perceptual information disrupts rather than reinforces coherence.**, **Intuitively, it is possible that constantly revising perceptions to fit a belief system may lead to greater incoherence and inconsistency.**, **If perceptions are always revised to increase coherence, it implies they are malleable and unreliable foundations for knowledge.**
