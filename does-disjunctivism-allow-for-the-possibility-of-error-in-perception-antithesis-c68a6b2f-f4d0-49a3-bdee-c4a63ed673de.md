# Does disjunctivism allow for the possibility of error in perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c68a6b2f-f4d0-49a3-bdee-c4a63ed673de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an optically deceptive situation makes a gray object appear blue. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception might be subject to error without inferential justification.**, **It is implausible that perceptions are infallible; thus, relying purely on direct perception without further justification could lead to incorrect knowledge claims.**
