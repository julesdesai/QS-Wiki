# Is reliabilism undermined by cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b052cf14-db06-4138-8252-a894db0e161a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Process-Oriented Justification](reliabilism-with-process-oriented-justification-synthesis-8900612b-46bb-42d6-a4c1-a983dd2da82b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes that it is noon based on a clock that has stopped but happens to be correct. Intuitively, we would judge that the person lacks knowledge.**, **A person in the scenario believes the proposition and it is true that it is noon.**, **The cognitive process leading to the belief, relying on the stopped clock, is not reliable.**
