# Are epistemic virtues sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aa9935fe-f710-4438-ae9a-d2a88d17a817
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**A person may possess epistemic virtues and apply them, yet fail to have knowledge due to other factors like deception or misinformation.**, **Consider a case where an epistemically virtuous person follows reliable methods but is fed false information from a typically trustworthy source. Intuitively, this does not result in knowledge despite the application of virtue.**, **Claiming that the possession and application of epistemic virtues suffice for knowledge overlooks potential failures in acquiring true beliefs due to external sources.**
