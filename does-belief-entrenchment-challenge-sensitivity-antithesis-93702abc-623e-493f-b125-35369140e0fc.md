# Does belief entrenchment challenge sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93702abc-623e-493f-b125-35369140e0fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-f1663159-56f4-4f6f-b53a-d60dc9dbfcb7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person irrationally clings to their belief regardless of new evidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that such irrational beliefs can never qualify as knowledge or that they might under some conditions. If we say irrational beliefs can qualify as knowledge, then knowledge should not require belief to be sensitive to the proposition's truth. If we say irrational beliefs can never be knowledge, then widely accepted cases of justified beliefs under error might also be disqualified.**
