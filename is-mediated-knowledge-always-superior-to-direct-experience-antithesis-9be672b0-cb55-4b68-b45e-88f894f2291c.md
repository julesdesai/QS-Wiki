# Is mediated knowledge always superior to direct experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9be672b0-cb55-4b68-b45e-88f894f2291c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism with Instrumental Enhancement](representationalism-with-instrumental-enhancement-synthesis-27f89a71-7167-40fa-bf3b-4c0e55b55fea.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has direct experience of tasting an apple and a mediated representation via a nutrition analyzer that states its taste profile. Intuitively, we either have to say that mediated knowledge is superior or that direct experience provides a more authentic understanding. If we say mediated is superior, then it undermines the credibility of lived experience. If we say direct experience is more authentic, then it challenges the necessity of mediation for knowledge.**
