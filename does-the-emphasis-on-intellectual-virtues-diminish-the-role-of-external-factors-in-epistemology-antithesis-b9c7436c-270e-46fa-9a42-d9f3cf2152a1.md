# Does the emphasis on intellectual virtues diminish the role of external factors in epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b9c7436c-270e-46fa-9a42-d9f3cf2152a1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's belief is true primarily due to external factors, like an accurate instrument, rather than their intellectual virtues. Intuitively, it seems that the reliable external source plays a critical role in justifying the belief.**, **A belief that is externally justified but not primarily due to personal intellectual virtues challenges the sufficiency of intellectual virtues alone in constituting knowledge.**
