# Is utility alone sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57d91d51-a5e8-4bfc-af3c-097c879fbd5a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief about the weather leads to making practical plans, but the belief is unfounded and coincidentally true. Intuitively, this would not be considered knowledge despite its practical usefulness.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires both truth and justification, and practical usefulness alone does not fulfill these criteria. This implies that utility alone is insufficient to constitute knowledge.**
