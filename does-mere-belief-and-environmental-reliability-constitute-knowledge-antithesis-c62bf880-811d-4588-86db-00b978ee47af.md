# Does mere belief and environmental reliability constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c62bf880-811d-4588-86db-00b978ee47af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-45579dd6-32de-43f3-ae7a-76b3bb7de09b.md)

## Content

**A person is in a reliably designed environment producing true beliefs, yet lacks understanding of the mechanisms or reasons. Intuitively, calling this knowledge seems unsatisfactory.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some awareness or comprehension of the belief's grounding, which raises a challenge if reliability is assumed sufficient.**
