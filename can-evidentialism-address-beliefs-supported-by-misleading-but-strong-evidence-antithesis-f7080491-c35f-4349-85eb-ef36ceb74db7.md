# Can Evidentialism address beliefs supported by misleading but strong evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7080491-c35f-4349-85eb-ef36ceb74db7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-a76d832e-447a-4168-b9be-6c867a1a7af1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has strong evidence that a public figure committed a crime, which later turns out to be fabricated. Intuitively, we would judge that while the belief was supported by strong evidence, the eventual truth shows it was not justified. This raises questions about the sufficiency of strong evidence for justification.**
