# Can knowledge exist without perceivable structures?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 94fccf76-c340-4f40-83c6-b4638f90a3ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structuralism](epistemic-structuralism-synthesis-7fb538c7-d6b1-4a5d-b302-4f53eea0d56a.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all knowledge must be derived from perceptual experiences, since abstract concepts like mathematical truths can be known without direct sensory perception**, **Consider a scenario where a person knows a mathematical truth solely through logical deduction without perceptual experience. Intuitively, we would judge that this person has knowledge despite the absence of perceptual structures**
