# Do hallucinatory experiences challenge the direct perception claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e4197e6-3e33-47b4-b976-41a9d50c2423
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism](naïve-realism-synthesis-25d0b7e3-6f66-46c3-a45d-bb41c1dbe70b.md)

## Content

**Hallucinations can produce experiences indistinguishable from genuine perception, even in the absence of the corresponding external object. In such cases, intuitively we struggle to claim any direct connection to an external reality as there is none present.**, **Knowledge is typically based on genuine perceptions that align with reality. This principle suggests a gap between hallucinations and the perception of real-world objects.**
