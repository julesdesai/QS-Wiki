# Can a single anomalous experience undermine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3aa94b02-845c-4a4c-9278-7d04891f2f45
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Realism with Consistency Conditions](phenomenal-realism-with-consistency-conditions-synthesis-05f9226f-1221-4391-b2ae-790270c7a451.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person holds a belief that remains consistent across numerous experiences but encounters a single conflicting perception due to an unusual state, such as illness. Intuitively, this single anomaly should not negate their knowledge of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that multiple corroborative experiences should outweigh a singular conflicting experience. This principle together with the reality that perceptions can sometimes be anomalous entails that consistency is not a necessary condition for knowledge.**
