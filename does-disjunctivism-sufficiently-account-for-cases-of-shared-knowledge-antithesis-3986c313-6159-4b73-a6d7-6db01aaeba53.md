# Does disjunctivism sufficiently account for cases of shared knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3986c313-6159-4b73-a6d7-6db01aaeba53
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Consider knowledge that is commonly known, such as "the sun sets in the west," and accessed through direct perception or true belief. Intuitively, this knowledge should remain regardless of direct perception on a day without observation.**, **If knowledge requires direct perception or true belief, then shared knowledge without perception at each instance becomes questionable.**, **It is implausible that consequence day-to-day beliefs lose their justification because they are not veridically connected at all times.**
