# Does knowing simply require a belief to be merely useful and not true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 45f434c2-1a56-4933-ac97-59aad4b28df0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge with Justification](revised-pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-with-justification-synthesis-570c8b94-2cc9-4f79-b923-9eacb64ddf74.md)

## Content

**A person might believe that their favorite sports team will win, finding this belief useful for maintaining enthusiasm; however, if the team loses, the belief is clearly not true.**, **Intuitively, knowing something implies it must also be true, not merely useful.**
