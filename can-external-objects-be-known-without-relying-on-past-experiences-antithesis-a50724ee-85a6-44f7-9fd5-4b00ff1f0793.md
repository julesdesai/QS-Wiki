# Can external objects be known without relying on past experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a50724ee-85a6-44f7-9fd5-4b00ff1f0793
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Appeal to Memory](phenomenalism-with-appeal-to-memory-synthesis-2b7704c1-89ef-4cc3-a1d9-427b8175dc3f.md)

## Content

**A scenario in which an individual experiences an object for the first time and obtains accurate knowledge without referencing any past experiences could suggest that external objects can be understood directly.**, **Intuitively, direct realist theories that suggest external objects are immediately perceived contradict the need to infer their existence from memory.**
