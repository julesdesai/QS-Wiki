# Can reliabilism explain knowledge when accidental reliability occurs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0592d65b-0cf9-41e0-8646-1a45c83d62ec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a man always correctly predicts his son's behavior based on random hunches with no basis, yet the outcomes are always correct. Intuitively, one might doubt the presence of true knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle emphasizing genuine knowledge should not rest on systematically coincidental accuracies. This principle suggests that accidental reliability may not equate to reliable knowledge formation.**
