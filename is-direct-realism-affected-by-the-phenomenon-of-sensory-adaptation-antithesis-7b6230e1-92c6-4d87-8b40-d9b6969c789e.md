# Is Direct Realism affected by the phenomenon of sensory adaptation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7b6230e1-92c6-4d87-8b40-d9b6969c789e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**Consider that over time, a person can become unaware of the feel of their clothes on their skin through sensory adaptation. Intuitively, this lack of sensation does not mean the external object (clothing) ceases to exist.**, **Sensory adaptation shows that continued direct sensory contact with an object does not guarantee constant perception of that object.**, **This phenomenon challenges the claim that perception provides constant, direct knowledge of external objects.**
