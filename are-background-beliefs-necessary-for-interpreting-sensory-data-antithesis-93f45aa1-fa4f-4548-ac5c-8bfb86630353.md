# Are background beliefs necessary for interpreting sensory data?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93f45aa1-fa4f-4548-ac5c-8bfb86630353
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-11423d6b-b29a-46b2-967d-bb4b03435a75.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where someone experiences a loud noise and interprets it as thunder. Intuitively, we either have to say that this interpretation is informed by background knowledge or that it is purely based on the sensation. If we say it is informed by background knowledge, then sensory experience alone is insufficient for knowledge. If we say it is purely based on sensation, then it seems we cannot reliably determine the source of the sound.**, **It is plausible that understanding sensory data often requires a framework of pre-existing beliefs or knowledge beyond the sensory experience itself.**
