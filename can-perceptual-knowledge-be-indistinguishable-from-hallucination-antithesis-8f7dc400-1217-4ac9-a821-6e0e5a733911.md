# Can perceptual knowledge be indistinguishable from hallucination?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f7dc400-1217-4ac9-a821-6e0e5a733911
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4b61ef48-0e05-458a-ab35-9c24d16d77b9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where Alice and Bob both perceive a red apple. Alice sees a real apple, while Bob hallucinates due to a virtual reality setup, but both have the same perceptual experience and confidence in believing there is a red apple. Intuitively, their states of knowledge should not differ.**, **In situations where perceptual knowledge is indistinguishable from hallucination to the perceiver, the distinction between knowledge and mere belief is threatened.**
