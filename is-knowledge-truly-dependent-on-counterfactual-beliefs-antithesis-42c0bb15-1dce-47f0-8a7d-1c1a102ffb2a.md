# Is knowledge truly dependent on counterfactual beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42c0bb15-1dce-47f0-8a7d-1c1a102ffb2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Reliabilism](nozicks-conditional-reliabilism-synthesis-29b3c3e0-b3ff-4b45-a994-893f15be46b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a highly convincing yet perfectly misleading guide asserts the falsehood that a bridge is safe. If one were lucky enough to avoid the bridge collapsing, intuitively one would not know that the bridge is safe.**, **According to the principle that knowing is contingent on reliable tracking, if the guide had spoken the truth, one would have known the bridge was safe.**, **It is implausible to claim knowledge where the belief's accuracy hinges on fortune or random chance.**
