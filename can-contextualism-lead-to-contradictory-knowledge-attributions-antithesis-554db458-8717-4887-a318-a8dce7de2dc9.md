# Can contextualism lead to contradictory knowledge attributions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 554db458-8717-4887-a318-a8dce7de2dc9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Knowledge](contextualism-about-knowledge-synthesis-b64cba08-2a7b-44b8-b81d-d7241d8be6e0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a single proposition cannot both be true and false. This principle together with the claim that knowledge attributions depend on the context of the ascriber entails that the same proposition could be known to be true in one context and false in another.**
