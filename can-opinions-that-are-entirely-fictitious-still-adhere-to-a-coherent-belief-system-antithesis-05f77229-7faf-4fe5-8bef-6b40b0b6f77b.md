# Can opinions that are entirely fictitious still adhere to a coherent belief system?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05f77229-7faf-4fe5-8bef-6b40b0b6f77b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Partial Correspondence Coherentism Summary](partial-correspondence-coherentism-summary-synthesis-73282c9a-d730-43f4-a933-7c2ffa7c9bab.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has a belief system that is coherent but based entirely on a fictional narrative like a novel.**, **They might have coherent beliefs about the narrative, but the beliefs do not correspond to actual states of affairs.**, **If knowledge required merely coherence, fictional narratives could be deemed as knowledge, challenging the necessity of correspondence to reality.**
