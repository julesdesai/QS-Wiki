# Can a true belief formed from a generally unreliable process count as knowledge simply due to usefulness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a4f11db-7321-41a3-9af4-932265f35b34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Imagine a belief about an election outcome formed by reading a biased source, but it turns out true and empowers strategic decision-making. Intuitively, this reliance on an unreliable source should not be considered knowledge.**, **The notion of knowledge presupposes reliability in the alignment of process and truth, seemingly at odds if termed knowledge here.**
