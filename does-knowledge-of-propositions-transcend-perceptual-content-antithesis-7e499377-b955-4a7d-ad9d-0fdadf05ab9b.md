# Does knowledge of propositions transcend perceptual content?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e499377-b955-4a7d-ad9d-0fdadf05ab9b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Phenomenalism](causal-phenomenalism-synthesis-d4ddc536-27de-43a0-8a38-7c671da830bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person has knowledge of moral truths, such as "stealing is wrong," that does not rely on sensory experience for validation. Intuitively, we would acknowledge these moral propositions as knowable apart from perceptual experiences.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge of some propositions—like moral or mathematical truths—transcend purely perceptual content.**
