# Does pragmatic contextualism adequately address non-practical forms of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7f10d0d-2e30-4170-8d46-7ccf26d04c50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**Some propositions pertain to theoretical or abstract knowledge, which may not directly correspond to practical success.**, **Knowledge of abstract truths should not inherently depend on their practical applicability or contextual situations.**, **If pragmatic contextualism limits knowledge to practical contexts, it may neglect significant domains of understanding that are knowledge without practical application.**
