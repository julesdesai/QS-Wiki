# How do we differentiate genuine knowledge from mere perceptual belief in varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 227ebac8-e791-42a9-a5cb-fe31ef1cb129
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-8ade45c1-54b8-462e-aef7-204b002692b3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals have the same perceptual experience of a red apple. In this scenario, one individual is in bright daylight and the other in colored lighting that shifts the apple's appearance. Intuitively, only one has genuine knowledge of the apple's color.**, **If context affects perceptual validation such that one person possesses knowledge and another does not, then the distinction between genuine knowledge and perceptual belief becomes problematic within context-dependent frameworks.**, **A case where a person perceives an object to be blue in normal lighting while another, under blue-tinted lighting, perceives the same object as blue poses a challenge to establishing what constitutes knowledge versus belief depending on context.**
