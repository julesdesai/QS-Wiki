# Can causal theory capture knowledge derived from testimony?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f03010bd-a339-4ade-ab9f-53076b99a622
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person knows something based on reliable testimony from another. Intuitively, this knowledge arises from trust in the authority, not solely from a causal link between belief and truth.**, **Consider the knowledge that Paris is the capital of France gained through textbooks; this does not seem to involve a direct causal interaction with the truth of the world.**
