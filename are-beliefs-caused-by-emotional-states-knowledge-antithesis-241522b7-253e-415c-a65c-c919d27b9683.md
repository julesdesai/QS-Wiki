# Are beliefs caused by emotional states knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 241522b7-253e-415c-a65c-c919d27b9683
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's belief is influenced by their emotional state, such as anxiety predicting a storm due to fear rather than evidence. Intuitively, we might question if this belief truly qualifies as knowledge despite being correct.**, **There is a plausible principle that emotional states influencing beliefs challenge their causal connection to truth. This principle suggests emotions could obscure the factual basis needed for valid knowledge.**, **It is implausible for emotionally motivated beliefs to be declared knowledge if they mislead the perceiver from correctly identifying facts through mere emotional channels.**
