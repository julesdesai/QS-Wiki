# Does the theory allow for infinite regress of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d62c8b34-5c90-4430-8614-58db6bcf0eaa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Pragmatic Grounds](foundationalism-with-pragmatic-grounds-synthesis-35d5e861-b788-4e52-a02b-4488aba3b8d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where each belief needs a further belief for justification, leading to infinite regress. In such a scenario, intuitively, achieving ultimate justification seems impossible.**, **A plausible principle is that stopping infinite regress requires some form of ultimate justification. This principle together with questioning whether basic beliefs truly halt justificatory regress raises concerns about the theory's foundation.**
