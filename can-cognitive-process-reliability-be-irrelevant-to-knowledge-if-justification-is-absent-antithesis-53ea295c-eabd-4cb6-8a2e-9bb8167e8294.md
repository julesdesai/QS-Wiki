# Can cognitive process reliability be irrelevant to knowledge if justification is absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 53ea295c-eabd-4cb6-8a2e-9bb8167e8294
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as an externalist account of knowledge](reliabilism-as-an-externalist-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-f53d2eeb-6036-46fc-bee7-531fa51577e9.md)

## Content

**Imagine a world where cognitive processes are generally reliable, but all individuals lack any justification or awareness of their reliability; intuitively, one might question whether knowledge could truly be said to exist under these circumstances.**, **Reliable cognitive processes yielding knowledge without any justification or awareness challenges our intuitions about what it means to "know" something in a meaningful way.**
