# Does tracking fail to account for knowledge in skeptical scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9b5c1e77-8f50-48f0-a717-67399a386d11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-34b45a3c-9960-48d7-bb61-82e72864432f.md)

## Content

**In skeptical scenarios, such as the brain-in-a-vat thought experiment, a person's experiences are indistinguishable from reality, suggesting their beliefs cannot track the truth because they would hold their beliefs in both the real and skeptical scenarios.**, **The intuition arises that knowledge is absent in such scenarios due to the failure to track truth since the person cannot discriminate between being in reality or a deception-based scenario.**
