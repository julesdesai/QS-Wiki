# Does introspective knowledge require a causal link?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1412145-e9e0-4b5d-829e-d43e994d6053
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that a person can have introspective knowledge of their own mental states without any external causal connection. For example, a person can know they are in pain simply by introspective awareness.**, **Suppose a person believes they are thirsty and this belief is true because they are indeed thirsty. Intuitively, the person knows they are thirsty without needing a causal link to an external fact.**
