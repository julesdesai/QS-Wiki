# Can the causal theory handle knowledge of historical facts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cdf595cc-61c8-4dbf-9120-2bce155c3522
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Suppose a historian determines that a specific ancient civilization engaged in trade with another civilization based on archaeological evidence. Intuitively, we believe the historian knows this fact.**, **Historical events cannot causally impact beliefs across time if they are isolated in time. The causal theory suggests the lack of a direct causal link undermines the claim of knowledge, conflicting with our intuition.**
