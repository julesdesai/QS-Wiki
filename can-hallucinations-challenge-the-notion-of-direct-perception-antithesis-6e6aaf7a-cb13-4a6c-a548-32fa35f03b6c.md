# Can hallucinations challenge the notion of direct perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6e6aaf7a-cb13-4a6c-a548-32fa35f03b6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-15b36dc9-362d-489d-8156-bddc0dd6b522.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where an individual experiences a vivid hallucination of an object that does not exist. Intuitively, this hallucination appears just as real as a genuinely perceived object.**, **If hallucinations can appear as convincing as direct perceptions, the idea that knowledge through perception is direct becomes questionable since hallucinations can lack a connection to reality.**
