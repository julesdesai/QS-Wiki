# Can moral virtues impact the attribution of intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93681334-5ed5-495b-8468-c8d5f3f549b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person acts in morally virtuous ways which influence their beliefs, aligning them with true propositions. Intuition suggests that such moral alignment is atypical for knowledge.**, **Moral actions and virtues are generally separate from the realm of epistemic virtues which concern cognitive traits.**, **The integration of moral into intellectual virtues can bias belief formation away from independent truth-seeking.**, **Knowledge focuses on reliably formed true beliefs, not on moral virtue, challenging the sufficiency of intellectual virtue without independence.**
