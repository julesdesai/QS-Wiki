# Knowledge as Reliably Virtuous Belief

**Parent:** [Does truth derived from reliable processes lead to accidental knowledge?](does-truth-derived-from-reliable-processes-lead-to-accidental-knowledge-antithesis-dc014bcf-91f6-490d-af8e-cc51a09d1513.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process guided by intellectual virtue**, **An intellectual virtue involves cognitive faculties reliably aiming at truth in a way tempered by reflective judgment**
