# Can a person know something without recognizing all relevant alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84ad14da-3f6e-44cc-9e7b-56c6276adc5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-5db4d183-a5a9-469a-b4ff-d831c6a59a11.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person correctly identifies the facts of a situation without consciously considering all possible alternatives. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person might still possess knowledge.**, **A person can have justified beliefs and true beliefs without being aware of every conceivable alternative scenario.**
