# Can extraneous virtues invalidate knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57afcea8-983f-4934-90ce-3806d5d494c0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition because they possess an intellectual virtue unrelated to the truth of that proposition, such as eloquence. In such a scenario, intuitively we would not judge that the person knows the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires intellectual virtues directly related to obtaining the truth. This principle together with the irrelevance of certain intellectual virtues to truth entails that not all intellectual virtues suffice for knowledge.**
