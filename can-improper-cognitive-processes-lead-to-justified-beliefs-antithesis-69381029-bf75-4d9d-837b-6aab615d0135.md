# Can improper cognitive processes lead to justified beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69381029-bf75-4d9d-837b-6aab615d0135
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-aeaab3c5-c7cb-4be4-bb9f-cb5fcc744f10.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual relies on intuition, which deviates from traditional cognitive processing, and consistently produces true beliefs. Intuitively, recognition as knowledge is possible despite improper faculties.**, **Justification from traditional reliable processes challenges the trust in non-standard processes yielding truth.**, **A principle that justification originates only from traditional faculties is insufficient if atypical processes effectively lead to true beliefs.**
