# Do causal chains capture the complexity of indirect knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 752fc8ab-87a7-4bb4-9ac4-a851778ef25c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who has knowledge based on complex scientific findings that involve multiple layers of research and interpretation. Intuitively, we would judge that the knowledge such a person has cannot be easily reduced to a simple causal link to each proposition known.**, **There is a plausible principle that for complex knowledge, especially in scientific fields, the causal chain cannot be linear or direct, challenging simple causal connections as accounts of knowledge.**
