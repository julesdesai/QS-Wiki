# Does phenomenalism adequately explain hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 38dc298a-58a1-4f61-9473-091d057799db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that hallucinatory experiences, which provide no coherent cross-modal perceptual experiences, align with any consistent phenomenal experience.**, **Suppose we have a case in which someone hallucinates seeing and hearing events that are not real. Intuitively, these experiences provide no coherent or reinforcing alignment across other senses, thus challenging the coherent perception claim.**
