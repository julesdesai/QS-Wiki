# Is inferring the existence of external objects from perception alone flawed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 57eb71bc-e960-431b-949f-63622a8b1d8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Inference](phenomenalism-with-inference-synthesis-5c9b476a-f87e-418f-98f3-b4842f3fdc21.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person sees an object under distorted conditions and infers its existence based on perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the inference may not be trustworthy.**, **It is implausible that inferences from distorted or misleading perceptions can reliably establish the existence of external objects.**
