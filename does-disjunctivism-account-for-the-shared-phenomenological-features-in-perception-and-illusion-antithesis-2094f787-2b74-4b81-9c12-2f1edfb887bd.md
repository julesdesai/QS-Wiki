# Does disjunctivism account for the shared phenomenological features in perception and illusion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2094f787-2b74-4b81-9c12-2f1edfb887bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-6c9ba281-6911-4c85-9826-b83f47b211cf.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone visually perceives a red apple and another person hallucinates a red apple. The redness is a shared experiential feature.**, **The phenomenological similarity between veridical perception and hallucination challenges the idea that they are fundamentally different.**, **If veridical and hallucinatory experiences share phenomenological features, then the fundamental difference claim is questioned.**
