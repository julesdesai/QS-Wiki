# Does invariantism adequately account for intuition about stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c383b6b9-f7f0-4a39-a696-b0684f975dd1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-519a57d4-27a1-46ac-ac38-c130e30ad088.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where high stakes make us intuitively deny someone knows, but low stakes allow us to affirm it. Intuitively, invariantism should not permit such shifts if knowledge standards remain unchanged.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge attribution should align with invariant standards rather than shifting intuitively with stakes.**
