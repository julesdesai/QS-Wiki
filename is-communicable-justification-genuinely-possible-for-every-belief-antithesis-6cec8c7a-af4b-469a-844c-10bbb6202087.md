# Is communicable justification genuinely possible for every belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6cec8c7a-af4b-469a-844c-10bbb6202087
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Communicable Justification](foundationalism-with-communicable-justification-synthesis-8dbcdd2b-eb8b-4593-a7ca-0bfc89d9258b.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a mathematician understands a proof that another cannot grasp despite clear communication efforts; the proof remains self-evident to the mathematician but not communicable to the other. This scenario calls into question the universality of communicable justification for all beliefs.**, **The demand for justification to be communicable assumes equal epistemic access or capacity, which may not be achievable across different individuals or contexts.**
