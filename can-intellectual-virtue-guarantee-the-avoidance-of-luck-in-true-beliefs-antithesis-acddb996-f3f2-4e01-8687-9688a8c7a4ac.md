# Can intellectual virtue guarantee the avoidance of luck in true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** acddb996-f3f2-4e01-8687-9688a8c7a4ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**A person believes a proposition P based on intellectual virtue, but an entirely separate factor ensures the truth of P; intuitively, the belief seems reliant on luck, not virtue.**, **Suppose the presence of intellectual virtue in forming a belief cannot exclude the influence of coincidental truth; this implies that virtue alone cannot ensure knowledge.**
