# Can reliable belief alone genuinely suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1399208-e73e-448c-980c-b4a39f347167
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Externalism Summary](disjunctivist-externalism-summary-synthesis-838ba4d9-493f-4109-9c69-34614a236108.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be based solely on belief that happens to be correct by chance. This principle suggests that relying solely on the reliability of belief might lead to accepting accidental truths as knowledge.**, **Consider a case where a person holds a belief that the weather will be sunny tomorrow because they randomly guess it, and it turns out to be true. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the weather will be sunny despite their belief being correct.**, **Accepting reliable belief as sufficient for knowledge might permit cases where the believer merely guesses or is otherwise lucky, which does not align with common intuitions about knowledge.**
