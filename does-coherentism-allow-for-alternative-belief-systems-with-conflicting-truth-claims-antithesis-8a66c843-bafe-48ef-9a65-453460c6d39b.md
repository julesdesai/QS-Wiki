# Does coherentism allow for alternative belief systems with conflicting truth claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a66c843-bafe-48ef-9a65-453460c6d39b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism View](coherentism-view-synthesis-4dcf0483-85cc-4ed6-8a5e-86aaec2b7626.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two distinct and internally consistent belief systems, each coherent within itself but containing mutually exclusive truth claims. Intuitively, we have to say that one must be false since coherent systems cannot both align with all truths of the world.**, **Therefore, if justified belief is solely based on coherence, then both systems could be wrongly considered justified, which is implausible.**
