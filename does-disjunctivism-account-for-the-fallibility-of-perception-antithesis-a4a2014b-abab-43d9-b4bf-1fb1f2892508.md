# Does disjunctivism account for the fallibility of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4a2014b-abab-43d9-b4bf-1fb1f2892508
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-19882849-157e-4dc6-b31d-66173a7c79c0.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual sees a bent stick in water which appears straight. It is implausible that this perceptual experience provides direct awareness of a fact about the stick's shape in reality.**, **A principle that knowledge involves infallible direct awareness may be questionable in light of common perceptual mistakes, suggesting that perceptual experience alone cannot be sufficient for knowledge.**
