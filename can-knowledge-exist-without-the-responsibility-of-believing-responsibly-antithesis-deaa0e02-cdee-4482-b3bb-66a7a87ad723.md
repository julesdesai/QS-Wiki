# Can knowledge exist without the responsibility of believing responsibly?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** deaa0e02-cdee-4482-b3bb-66a7a87ad723
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who, through mere chance, inherits a belief from a reliable source without scrutinizing it and yet holds the belief as true. In such a scenario, we might still acknowledge that the person knows the truth despite lacking responsible epistemic conduct.**, **There are plausible intuitions suggesting that knowledge can simply be a true belief sustained by reliable testimony rather than the believer's responsible conduct.**
