# Doesn't coincidental correctness still constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 81f1827f-e879-4faa-a61e-05f9cdb0732b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-5b3e9103-4910-4e24-af7b-eaff612fb6ae.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a meteorologist predicts rain based on an inaccurate weather model, yet it actually rains due to unrelated atmospheric conditions. Intuitively, one may still claim the meteorologist "knew" it was going to rain.**, **There is a plausible principle that having a true belief with evidence, even if coincidentally correct, still counts as knowledge if the belief aligns with reality.**
