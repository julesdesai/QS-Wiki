# Does knowledge depend on nomologically abnormal situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b5d400b7-4649-48a5-8ea8-e76ee980b8bc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Reliabilism](nozicks-conditional-reliabilism-synthesis-29b3c3e0-b3ff-4b45-a994-893f15be46b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone watches a news broadcast that usually reports accurate weather data, indicating it will rain. Even if a rare fluke causes the broadcast to falter and relay incorrect information, intuitively, the person's belief in impending rain was knowledge prior to the error.**, **Nozick's requirement for truth-tracking implies that one's belief did not track the future weather because, in this unexpected case, it would not rain.**, **The theory suggests that the reliability of ordinary knowledge would have to stand the test of extraordinary conditions, which seems an impractical criterion.**
