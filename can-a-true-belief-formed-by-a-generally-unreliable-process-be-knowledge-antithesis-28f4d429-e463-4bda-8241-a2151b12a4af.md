# Can a true belief formed by a generally unreliable process be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28f4d429-e463-4bda-8241-a2151b12a4af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-31a3f075-3bd7-4d17-afc1-467206784345.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes something true based on a guess. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **A reliable cognitive process is one that generally leads to true beliefs.**, **Even though the belief is true, because it is formed by guessing, the cognitive process is generally unreliable, challenging the idea that this is knowledge.**
