# Does safety adequately capture what makes knowledge distinct from true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 605a736c-10eb-44e9-a6fd-ade2538cb471
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Safety Theory](knowledge-as-safety-theory-synthesis-80153c33-42b8-4596-bd42-fa1a51acc2f7.md)

## Content

**A person, Alex, believes a true proposition about the weather because of a reliable forecast. If the forecast had been incorrect, Alex would not have believed the proposition. Intuitively, this is a case of knowledge.**, **In some hypothetical scenarios, Alex still forms the true belief via the forecast without the knowledge being safe as other forecasts were generally inaccurate, showing safety is not sufficient.**
