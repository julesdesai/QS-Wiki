# Can ignored context affect the credibility of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1abc3d39-abd2-469a-902d-ac04bcb45ee1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-54895291-30c8-42cf-8322-baea34575bd1.md)

## Content

**Suppose a well-known factual claim is made in an informal setting like a social gathering, and the same claim is presented in a formal academic conference. Intuitively, the credibility of the claim as knowledge should not waver based on setting alone.**, **There is a plausible principle that for knowledge to be stable, the foundational justification should withstand scrutiny across diverse contexts, disregarding peripheral influences like setting.**
