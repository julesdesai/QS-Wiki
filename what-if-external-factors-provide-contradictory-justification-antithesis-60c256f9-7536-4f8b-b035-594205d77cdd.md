# What if external factors provide contradictory justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60c256f9-7536-4f8b-b035-594205d77cdd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Suppose a student receives different information from two equally credible expert sources about a historical event, making them justifiably uncertain. Intuitively, the external factors alone cannot reconcile this contradiction to form knowledge.**, **It is implausible for external factors alone to always produce a coherent justification in cases where sources or evidence are contradictory or ambiguous.**
