# Do different types of perception necessarily lead to differentiated epistemic statuses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7f67493-e206-47d4-ae5c-9dbd4fcd093c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Imagine two separate individuals hearing the same song, one live and one through a digital stream, both with indistinguishable audio qualities. It is implausible that the means of perception necessarily assigns a different epistemic status to each hearing when the output is phenomenologically the same.**
