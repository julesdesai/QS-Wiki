# Does competence always fulfill the requirements of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 472f30ed-715f-4f13-9f54-b90b5b6eedbd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that mere competence without proper alignment with external facts can yield unjustified beliefs.**, **Consider a scenario where a highly competent individual consistently forms beliefs about topics they usually know well, but due to an unexpected change in those topics' underlying facts, their beliefs become false. Intuitively, their competence alone does not justify the beliefs.**
