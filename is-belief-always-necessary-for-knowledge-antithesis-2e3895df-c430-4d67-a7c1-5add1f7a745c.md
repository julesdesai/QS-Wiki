# Is belief always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e3895df-c430-4d67-a7c1-5add1f7a745c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology combines knowledge with intellectual virtues](virtue-epistemology-combines-knowledge-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-5b32e3fe-f322-4af8-b790-236786e70562.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately identifies the solution to a math problem without confidently believing in their answer due to self-doubt.**, **In scenarios where individuals doubt their correct answers, they possess knowledge despite lacking firm belief in their own proposition.**, **It is implausible that someone needs an actual belief in the proposition to hold knowledge if they consistently produce true outcomes while lacking self-belief.**
