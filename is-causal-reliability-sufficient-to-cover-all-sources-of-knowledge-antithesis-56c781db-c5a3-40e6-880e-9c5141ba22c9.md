# Is causal reliability sufficient to cover all sources of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56c781db-c5a3-40e6-880e-9c5141ba22c9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**A principle suggests that a belief must be reliably caused by the truth to count as knowledge. However, this does not account for cases where reliable methods fail unexpectedly, yet knowledge remains.**, **Consider a reliable thermometer that temporarily malfunctions, but someone still accurately predicts the weather through intuition; intuitively, this remains knowledge despite the external causal failure.**
