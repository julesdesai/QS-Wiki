# How do we decide when a belief is justified within an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** feb9bd4b-2174-44f1-b38b-21677b18ab3a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-21450005-9ac1-4d45-9121-350dbebcb2cf.md)

## Content

**For a belief to be justified, there must be a clear point at which justification is recognized.**, **An infinite chain of reasons lacks a finite stopping point, thus making it unclear when a belief is justified.**, **If it is unclear when a belief is justified, it challenges our ability to claim that we have knowledge.**
