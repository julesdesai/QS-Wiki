# Is it plausible to claim knowledge when context misunderstood?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a85c7a80-fbd3-47f8-ad00-9dd15c9d5bd3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone misinterprets the context of a situation and asserts knowledge based on this misinterpretation. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not, in fact, know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires an accurate understanding of context. This principle, together with the possibility of misinterpreting context, entails that knowledge claims might often be incorrect if context is misjudged.**
