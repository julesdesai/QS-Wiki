# Can knowledge arise from reliable processes that are not intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67a68125-e471-488f-a997-5debdbce2d6f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Anti-Luck Virtue Epistemology](anti-luck-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-fe5301ab-5101-4f93-ba1f-e195247489de.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge can arise from reliable cognitive processes. This principle together with the claim that only intellectual virtues can lead to knowledge entails that reliable but non-virtuous processes cannot yield knowledge, which seems counterintuitive.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has a belief that is true due to a non-intellectual cognitive mechanism that is highly reliable. Intuitively, we would be inclined to say that the person knows the proposition, even though the belief does not arise from intellectual virtue.**
