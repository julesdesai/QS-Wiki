# Can knowledge be attained given the infinite requirement for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1cf44750-9198-4bbe-a957-7e2722679bf7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Finite Infinitism](finite-infinitism-synthesis-ec56b28f-9687-4f6e-b141-48dc2e075636.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be attainable. If justification requires potentially infinite reasons, then it may never be practically attainable.**, **If a person needs potentially infinite reasons to be justified in their belief, most ordinary beliefs lack this justification.**, **If infinite justification is required, for most propositions, it might not be practical or possible to achieve sufficient reasoning to satisfy the requirement for knowledge.**
