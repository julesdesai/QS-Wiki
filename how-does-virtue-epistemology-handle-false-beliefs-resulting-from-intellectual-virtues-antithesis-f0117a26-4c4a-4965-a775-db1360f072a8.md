# How does virtue epistemology handle false beliefs resulting from intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0117a26-4c4a-4965-a775-db1360f072a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Suppose an intellectually virtuous person consistently uses sound reasoning yet occasionally arrives at false beliefs due to misleading information. Intuitively, we would not undermine their intellectual virtues based on isolated cases of error.**, **Intellectual virtues might lead to false beliefs in certain contexts, suggesting that true belief alone is not sufficient for knowledge even if it typically arises from intellectual virtue.**
