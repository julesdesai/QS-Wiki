# Does the inability to refute skepticism imply a lack of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2de4b2d5-2931-475d-bc74-374e7b80cc95
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Neo-Mooreanism](neo-mooreanism-synthesis-aeb63ebc-6515-4a29-8322-7a2c352d1501.md)

## Content

**People often know their own names, even if they cannot formally refute skeptical scenarios challenging this knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that they possess this knowledge regardless of their philosophical ability.**, **Knowledge seems accessible even if an individual cannot actively refute skeptical hypotheticals, indicating that knowledge does not entirely depend on refuting all skepticism.**
