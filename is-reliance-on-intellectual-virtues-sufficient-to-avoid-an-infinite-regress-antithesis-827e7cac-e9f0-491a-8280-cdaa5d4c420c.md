# Is reliance on intellectual virtues sufficient to avoid an infinite regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 827e7cac-e9f0-491a-8280-cdaa5d4c420c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which reliance on intellectual virtues still requires justification of the virtues themselves. Intuitively, we either have to say that the virtues are self-justifying or they require external justification. If we say the virtues are self-justifying about this case, then they might be arbitrary. If we say they require external justification about this case, then this introduces the need for further justification, leading to regress.**
