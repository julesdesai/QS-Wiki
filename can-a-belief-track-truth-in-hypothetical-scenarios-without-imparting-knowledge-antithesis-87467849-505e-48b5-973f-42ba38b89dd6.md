# Can a belief track truth in hypothetical scenarios without imparting knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87467849-505e-48b5-973f-42ba38b89dd6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person believes their car won't start because they left the lights on. Their belief tracks the truth because truth changes if the battery's condition changes. Intuitively, if the car starts after being fixed, and the person was unaware of a mechanic fixing it, it seems the belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **The fulfillment of a tracking condition does not automatically entail knowledge if the person is unaware of corrective actions affecting the truth conditions of their belief.**
