# If basic beliefs require no justification, are they epistemically arbitrary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e61e26c6-28af-4fd4-823b-9a6b0d4ace0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that any belief lacking justification criteria appears epistemically arbitrary. This principle together with the foundationalist view that basic beliefs require no further justification entails the possibility that basic beliefs are arbitrary.**, **Consider a scenario where a person embraces a set of basic beliefs based purely on cultural upbringing. In such a scenario, intuitively, the beliefs might seem consistent but arbitrary without independent justification.**
