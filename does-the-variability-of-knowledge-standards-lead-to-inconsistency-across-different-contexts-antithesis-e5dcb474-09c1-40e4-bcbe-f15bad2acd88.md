# Does the variability of knowledge standards lead to inconsistency across different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e5dcb474-09c1-40e4-bcbe-f15bad2acd88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**When the standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, it might lead to inconsistencies in claims like "I know" or "they know" across those contexts.**, **If a person knows a proposition in one context due to certain evidence, they might be considered not to know it in another context due to differing evidence standards.**, **These shifts in standards can create conflicting judgments about whether a person knows the proposition.**
