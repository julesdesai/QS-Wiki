# Can belief cohesion alone suffice for knowledge if coherence with false beliefs is possible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6ef40117-7d26-4653-aa57-06f708922e2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Coherentism](pragmatism-with-coherentism-synthesis-7996efa8-d25e-4d54-acc0-6469819e4077.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a network of beliefs that are all coherent, but many of these beliefs are false. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the coherence of these beliefs alone does not make them knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must connect to reality in a substantial way. This principle together with the possibility of coherent, yet mostly false, belief systems entails that coherence alone does not suffice for knowledge.**
