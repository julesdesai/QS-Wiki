# What if different contexts demand conflicting standards of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 544ab226-eaba-4a06-9b47-f84673bd7744
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Reliabilism](contextualism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c7c3d2a5-9a25-4e3f-9e1f-65010441e024.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two different contexts with conflicting standards for knowing the same proposition. Intuitively, in one context, the proposition is known, and in another, it is not.**, **If conflicting standards can both apply to a single proposition depending on the context, this leads to inconsistencies in the concept of knowledge.**
