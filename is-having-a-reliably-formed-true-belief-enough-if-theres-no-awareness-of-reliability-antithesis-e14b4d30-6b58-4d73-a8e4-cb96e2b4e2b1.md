# Is having a reliably formed true belief enough if there's no awareness of reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e14b4d30-6b58-4d73-a8e4-cb96e2b4e2b1
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliabilism](knowledge-as-reliabilism-thesis-72dce40c-4bce-46d9-be3b-311bf9457188.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowing requires some level of awareness or understanding of the reliability of one's belief-forming processes.**, **A belief formed by merely reliable processes lacks this awareness aspect, leading to the intuition that it cannot qualify as knowledge.**
