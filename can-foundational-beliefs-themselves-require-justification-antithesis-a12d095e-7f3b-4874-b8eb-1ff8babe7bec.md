# Can foundational beliefs themselves require justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a12d095e-7f3b-4874-b8eb-1ff8babe7bec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-3cd555af-8ec3-406f-b70a-64f89852bc49.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where foundational beliefs are in turn questioned and need justification themselves. In this scenario, the supposed foundational nature seems undermined.**, **A plausible principle is that any belief, to be secure, must have some form of justification to avoid infinite regress of justification. This principle suggests foundational beliefs might need justification themselves.**
