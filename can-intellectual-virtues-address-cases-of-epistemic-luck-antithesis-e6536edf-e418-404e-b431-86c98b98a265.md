# Can intellectual virtues address cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e6536edf-e418-404e-b431-86c98b98a265
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**Individuals can acquire true beliefs through virtuous methods yet be accused of merely being lucky if certain conditions change.**, **A belief achieved through intellectual virtue can coincide with epistemically lucky situations, casting doubt on the consistent reliability of virtue.**, **There may be instances where competing explanations for a true belief are more plausible due to luck rather than virtue alone.**
