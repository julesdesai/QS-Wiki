# Can beliefs be justified without an infinite chain of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b14e0138-efc4-4ed3-b8b9-00fc479633c6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism and Non-Foundational Justification](infinitism-and-non-foundational-justification-synthesis-6eeef37c-3f39-4c5c-91c9-b90fbd483157.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a belief is directly supported by overwhelming evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief is justified without requiring an infinite chain of reasons.**, **A principle of epistemic efficiency suggests that the sufficiency of evidence can justify belief without infinite regress. This principle together with the requirement for endless supporting beliefs entails a conflict.**
