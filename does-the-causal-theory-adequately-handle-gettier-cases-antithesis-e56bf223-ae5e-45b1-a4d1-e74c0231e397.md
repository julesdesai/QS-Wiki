# Does the causal theory adequately handle Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e56bf223-ae5e-45b1-a4d1-e74c0231e397
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50c982e-2605-4111-a4b2-875786679360.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person P believes they see their friend at the park because they visually perceive someone who looks like their friend. In reality, their friend is indeed at the park but concealed behind a tree, while the person they perceive is a stranger. Intuitively, we would not say that P truly knows their friend is there.**, **According to the causal theory, P's belief is true and caused by the friend's presence, satisfying the theory, yet intuitively this does not constitute genuine knowledge.**
