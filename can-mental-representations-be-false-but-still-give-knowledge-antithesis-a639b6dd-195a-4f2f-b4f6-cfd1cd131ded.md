# Can mental representations be false but still give knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a639b6dd-195a-4f2f-b4f6-cfd1cd131ded
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a mental representation that inaccurately depicts the external world but leads to successful actions. Intuitively, we might still want to say the person knows something relevant to those actions.**, **There is a plausible principle that success in action can be indicative of knowledge. This principle together with the incorrect representation implies knowledge is possible even with inaccuracies in mental representations.**
