# How do we differentiate between genuine perception and illusion in forming knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4fbc869-3c7d-48e1-89e1-b1e7f12d0a87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4b61ef48-0e05-458a-ab35-9c24d16d77b9.md)

## Content

**Suppose a magician creates an elaborate illusion of an oasis in a desert that convinces a survivor of its reality, yet neither is there an actual oasis nor a direct environmental connection causing the perception. This false belief derived from perception cannot be knowledge.**, **There must be criteria to differentiate genuine perception (leading to knowledge) from illusions that contribute only to false beliefs.**
