# Does the coherence of perceptions necessarily imply their truthfulness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf60d0d1-ebf1-40cd-b9f9-8c9d0d3fad7c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Phenomenalism](coherent-phenomenalism-synthesis-762c00d9-ee32-4648-8d8e-67643edf20ba.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that coherence of perceptions across different individuals implies truthfulness. This principle, together with the possibility of mass hysteria leading to coherent but false perceptions, entails that coherence does not guarantee truthfulness of perceived objects.**
