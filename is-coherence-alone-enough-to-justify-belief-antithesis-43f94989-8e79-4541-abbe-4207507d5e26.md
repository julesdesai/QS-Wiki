# Is coherence alone enough to justify belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 43f94989-8e79-4541-abbe-4207507d5e26
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Adaptive Coherentism](adaptive-coherentism-synthesis-f17f0588-6de8-4c0d-a6e6-63e09dfee45f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification requires more than coherence. This principle together with the idea that coherence alone can justify a belief entails that unjustified beliefs could appear justified.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person has a set of coherent but false beliefs. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that their beliefs are not justified.**
