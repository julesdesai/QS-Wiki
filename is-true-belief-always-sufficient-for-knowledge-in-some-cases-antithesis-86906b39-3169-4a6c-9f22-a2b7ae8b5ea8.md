# Is true belief always sufficient for knowledge in some cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 86906b39-3169-4a6c-9f22-a2b7ae8b5ea8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Epistemology](responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-69752ad8-5d3c-409a-957c-e718f4ec264b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone forms a belief randomly that happens to be true but results from no active engagement with evidence or critical evaluation of sources. Intuitively, we might judge that this person knows the proposition despite not fulfilling intellectual responsibilities.**
