# Does subject sensitivity result in inconsistent knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bbd9ad69-6d23-4238-8e48-2029b2e56fb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people, Person A and Person B, are assessing the same proposition with different practical concerns. Person A faces high stakes, while Person B faces low stakes. Intuitively, we might judge that Person A does not know the proposition due to the stakes, while Person B does know it.**, **There is a plausible concern that allowing practical concerns to affect knowledge leads to contradictions where the same proposition can be known and not known simultaneously by different subjects.**
