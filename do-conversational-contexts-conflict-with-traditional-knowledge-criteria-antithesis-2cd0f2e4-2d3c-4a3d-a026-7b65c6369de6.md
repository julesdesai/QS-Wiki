# Do conversational contexts conflict with traditional knowledge criteria?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2cd0f2e4-2d3c-4a3d-a026-7b65c6369de6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Traditional criteria of knowledge include truth, belief, and justification. If contextual elements can influence knowledge claims, these criteria might be overlooked or altered by the conversational factors.**, **A person’s knowledge claim is traditionally validated by these criteria independently of context, suggesting that relying on conversational context conflicts with established epistemic standards.**
