# Can a person have knowledge if their belief stems from an unreliable process but accidentally turns out true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a0297c68-240f-4c2d-95ca-0ee8c80c5d5b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person uses a horoscope page to forecast their career moves and coincidentally achieves success aligning with those predictions.**, **Intuitively, the belief, though true by chance, arises from a non-robustly reliable method and should not qualify as knowledge.**, **Accuracy resulting from unreliable processes challenges the link between reliability and knowledge.**
