# Can safety ensure knowledge in cases of accidental truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce1727e2-9134-4187-a85a-da87680e9082
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes it is noon based solely on a randomly functioning watch. The watch does read noon and it actually is noon. In this scenario, intuitively we would not say the person truly knows it is noon.**, **A belief is considered safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. In scenarios where incidental accuracy, like the randomly correct watch, occur, the belief may still falsely conclude knowledge.**
