# Can contextual dependence lead to inconsistent standards for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1c3aa00-ff98-40f4-81f3-927975e21e44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Virtue Contextualism](externalist-virtue-contextualism-synthesis-69e2f760-46ad-465f-a31e-67e150de071d.md)

## Content

**If intellectual virtue is context-dependent, then the standards for what counts as an intellectual virtue can vary across different contexts.**, **Different standards for intellectual virtue in different contexts could lead to cases where a belief counts as knowledge in one context but not in another.**, **Inconsistent standards for knowledge in different contexts might undermine the reliability of knowledge claims.**
