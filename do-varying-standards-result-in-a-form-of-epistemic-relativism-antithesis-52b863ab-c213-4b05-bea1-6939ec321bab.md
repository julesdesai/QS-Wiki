# Do varying standards result in a form of epistemic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52b863ab-c213-4b05-bea1-6939ec321bab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism shifts the standard for knowledge based on context to avoid skepticism.](contextualism-shifts-the-standard-for-knowledge-based-on-context-to-avoid-skepticism-synthesis-7d6433be-792c-4311-80b2-1c167a3e3980.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not vary subjectively across contexts. This principle together with context-dependent truth conditions for knowledge entails potential epistemic relativism.**, **Consider a scenario in which two people in different contexts disagree about whether something is known; intuitively, this disagreement signals a relativistic view of knowledge.**, **It is implausible that knowledge claims should be true for one person while false for another simply due to variations in context.**
