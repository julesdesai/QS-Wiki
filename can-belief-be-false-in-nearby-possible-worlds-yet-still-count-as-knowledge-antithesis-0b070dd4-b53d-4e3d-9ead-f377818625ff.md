# Can belief be false in nearby possible worlds yet still count as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0b070dd4-b53d-4e3d-9ead-f377818625ff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on strong evidence, but in a nearby possible world, the evidence is misleading, making the proposition false. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might still judge that the person knows the proposition in the actual world despite the nearby world falsity.**, **There is a plausible principle that if a person is justified in believing a proposition in the actual world, and it is true, then they can be said to know the proposition, even if in nearby possible worlds the proposition is false. This principle together with the safety condition in the view challenges the necessity of the safety condition for knowledge.**
