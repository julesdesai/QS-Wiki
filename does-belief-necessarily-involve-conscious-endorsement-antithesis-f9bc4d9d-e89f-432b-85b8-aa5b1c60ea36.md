# Does belief necessarily involve conscious endorsement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f9bc4d9d-e89f-432b-85b8-aa5b1c60ea36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Recursive Infinitism Summary](recursive-infinitism-summary-synthesis-e60eeb81-9314-4a67-a7c8-de308e762c47.md)

## Content

**To say a person knows a proposition because they believe it suggests belief is a conscious endorsement of that proposition.**, **There are cases, for example, implicit biases or subconscious beliefs, where individuals do not consciously endorse propositions they implicitly accept.**, **If belief does not necessarily entail conscious endorsement, then simply having a belief is insufficient for constituting knowledge.**
