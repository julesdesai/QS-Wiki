# Can reliability alone suffice for knowledge without understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76467ca9-fc43-4cf6-9e03-3d1925552552
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-3f203114-9a4a-4d29-9451-dfb5538c3c76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person uses a calculator to obtain the result of a complex calculation but does not understand the underlying mathematical principles. Intuitively, we would judge that this is not a case of knowledge, even if the result is reliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding is a necessary condition for knowledge. This principle together with the reliance on external devices without comprehension entails that reliability alone fails to provide justification.**
