# How can varying practical stakes affect knowledge if standards are invariant?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d48ae1b4-1d7d-4aeb-8164-4b581e235a64
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-1a7f6ba4-b667-492e-9754-ad3a76a8b666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person faces two different practical situations regarding the truth of the same proposition. Intuitively, it seems that the practical stakes do affect whether the person knows the proposition in each scenario. However, if the standards for knowledge are invariant, the same proposition should be known or not known regardless of practical stakes.**, **It is plausible that knowledge should not vary with changing stakes if the standards are truly invariant. This plausibility challenges the idea that practical stakes can affect knowledge without altering invariant standards.**
