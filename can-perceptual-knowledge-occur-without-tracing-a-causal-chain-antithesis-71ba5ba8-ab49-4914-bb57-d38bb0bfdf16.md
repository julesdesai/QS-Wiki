# Can perceptual knowledge occur without tracing a causal chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 71ba5ba8-ab49-4914-bb57-d38bb0bfdf16
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a principle that perceptual knowledge arises from a reliable mechanism rather than a specific causal connection. This suggests that sensory systems provide knowledge through their functionality.**, **Consider a case where a person sees a tree and believes there is a tree present. This perceptual knowledge may not depend on tracing a specific causal chain from the tree to the perception.**
