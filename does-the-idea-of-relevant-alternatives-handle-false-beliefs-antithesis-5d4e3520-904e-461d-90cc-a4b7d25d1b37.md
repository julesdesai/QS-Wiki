# Does the idea of relevant alternatives handle false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d4e3520-904e-461d-90cc-a4b7d25d1b37
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contrastivism](contrastivism-synthesis-b472000e-958d-4f0b-baf2-3d82da5c8cf3.md)

## Content

**A person might hold a false belief and still be considered knowledgeable due to the irrelevance of available alternatives, suggesting contrastivism may fail to address how false beliefs can disrupt knowledge.**, **Intuitively, a claim to knowledge demands truth which should not depend on the relevance of alternatives, as falsely believing there are no relevant alternatives doesn't create justified knowledge.**
