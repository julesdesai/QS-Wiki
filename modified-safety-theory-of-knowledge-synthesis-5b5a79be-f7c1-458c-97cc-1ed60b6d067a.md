## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, the belief must not be easily false**, **A belief is safe if, in all nearby possible worlds where the person forms the belief in the same manner, the belief is true**, **A person knows a proposition if their belief is true in almost all similar and nearby situations**, **Knowledge is immune to being easily false under varied but minor changes in circumstances**, **Luck or coincidental truth does not count as knowledge even if the belief is true**
