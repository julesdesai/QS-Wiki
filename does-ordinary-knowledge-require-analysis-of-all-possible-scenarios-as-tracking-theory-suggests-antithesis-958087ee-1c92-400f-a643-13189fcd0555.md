# Does ordinary knowledge require analysis of all possible scenarios as tracking theory suggests?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 958087ee-1c92-400f-a643-13189fcd0555
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d50e12e5-c945-480c-9bf6-a71471f277b2.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that ordinary knowledge claims require us to consider all possible scenarios and verify if beliefs hold across them. Ordinary cognition is limited in its capacity to evaluate extensive counterfactual scenarios.**, **Tracking knowledge across extensive possible scenarios suggests a requirement that is overly demanding for ordinary knowledge practices, which do not generally engage in such analyses.**
