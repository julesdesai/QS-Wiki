# Is self-referential justification coherent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39d78596-e7ef-45e3-a2e9-3d14e6d87d56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Recursive Infinitism Summary](recursive-infinitism-summary-synthesis-e60eeb81-9314-4a67-a7c8-de308e762c47.md)

## Content

**Justification with a self-referential structure implies using a reason that refers back to itself to provide support.**, **A reason that refers back to itself without any external support is circular, and circular justifications are typically not considered strong support.**, **If circular justifications are not considered valid, then a self-referential structure cannot provide adequate justification for knowledge.**
