# Can shifting standards challenge shared communal knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9d23fe32-5483-4b1d-8039-5e37ba67e1af
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Imagine a community that relies on shared knowledge for decision-making but faces differing individual contexts with different knowledge standards. Intuitively, this suggests a lack of a stable foundation for communal knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a robust communal knowledge framework can persist if individual knowledge claims are subject to varying standards across different contexts.**
