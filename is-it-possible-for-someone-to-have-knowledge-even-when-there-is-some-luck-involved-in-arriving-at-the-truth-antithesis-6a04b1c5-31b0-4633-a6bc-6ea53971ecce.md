# Is it possible for someone to have knowledge even when there is some luck involved in arriving at the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a04b1c5-31b0-4633-a6bc-6ea53971ecce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-Luck View](no-luck-view-synthesis-bccfbb90-e542-414d-b962-c3799a2855c9.md)

## Content

**A person can still have knowledge even if part of how they arrived at a true belief involved some degree of luck.**, **Consider a person guessing the answer to a very complex mathematical problem and getting it right. They may not know how to solve it, but their belief is true. Intuitively, knowledge attribution in such cases seems plausible despite the involvement of luck.**, **Epistemic states undergo involvements where luck plays a necessary but minority role, yet intuitively do not sabotage the whole knowledge claim.**
