# How do we address the challenge of differing foundational beliefs among individuals?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0285eae1-f013-44f3-9f3e-eecd5e3ba7a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Foundationalism](proper-foundationalism-synthesis-115cfa71-603e-48b1-bce9-39f7dced12e3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two individuals have mutually exclusive properly basic foundational beliefs due to cultural indoctrination. Intuitively, we would not judge both sets of beliefs as justified knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that foundational beliefs must be universally compelling to qualify as the basis for knowledge. This principle together with the idea that different foundational beliefs can exist for different people implies an inconsistency in what can be considered knowledge.**, **It is implausible that foundational beliefs can allow for significant diversity without calling into question their validity as universally applicable justifications.**
