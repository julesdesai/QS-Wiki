# Does sensitivity handle cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eb22c8c2-a3d2-447d-813c-f619d12cad61
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-c636dae2-8d6f-478b-827f-65895cd50362.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a true proposition based on evidence, but the evidence is accidentally misleading, and the belief would still be true.**, **Intuitively, we might judge that the person knows the proposition despite the misleading evidence due to epistemic luck.**, **In such cases, the belief does not track the truth across all nearby possible worlds, challenging the sensitivity requirement for knowledge.**
