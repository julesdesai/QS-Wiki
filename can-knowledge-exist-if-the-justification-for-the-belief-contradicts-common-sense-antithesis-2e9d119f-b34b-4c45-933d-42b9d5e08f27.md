# Can knowledge exist if the justification for the belief contradicts common sense?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e9d119f-b34b-4c45-933d-42b9d5e08f27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Reliable Processes](externalism-with-reliable-processes-synthesis-89e590de-768c-4370-a7df-27a5b4f5eaa5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes they will win the lottery because a statistically reliable astrologer predicted it.**, **Despite the prediction process being reliable in some odd track record, common sense tells us that lottery predictions are highly unlikely to grant knowledge.**, **When the justification contradicts common sense, the notion that reliable processes confer knowledge becomes suspect.**
