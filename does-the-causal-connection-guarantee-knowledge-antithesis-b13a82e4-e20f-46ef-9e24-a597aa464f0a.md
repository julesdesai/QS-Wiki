# Does the causal connection guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b13a82e4-e20f-46ef-9e24-a597aa464f0a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person sees a broken clock showing the correct time by chance. Intuitively, we would say that the belief is not knowledge despite the accurate causation from the clock face to the observer’s belief.**, **A reliable causal connection in a coincidental accurate display does not intuitively produce knowledge. This suggests the insufficiency of mere causation for knowledge claims.**
