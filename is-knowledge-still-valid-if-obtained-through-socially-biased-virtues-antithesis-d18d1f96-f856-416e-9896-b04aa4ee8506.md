# Is knowledge still valid if obtained through socially biased virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d18d1f96-f856-416e-9896-b04aa4ee8506
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario in which a person forms true beliefs through virtues that are biased by their social or cultural context. Intuitively, we might question whether knowledge obtained in this way is genuinely justified.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, which should be objective, can rest on socially biased virtues without challenge. This calls into question whether socially influenced virtues can reliably lead to true beliefs.**
