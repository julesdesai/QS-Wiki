# Is intellectual virtue truly a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0c96e787-8b96-4a6b-ae50-f2a142b071d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge does not have to arise from intellectual virtue. This principle, together with counterexamples where knowledge seems present without clear intellectual virtue, suggests intellectual virtue may not be necessary for knowledge.**, **Suppose a case where an untrained person stumbles upon a true scientific hypothesis through unmethodical exploration. Intuitively, it seems this person could still be said to know the hypothesis despite the lack of intellectual virtue.**
