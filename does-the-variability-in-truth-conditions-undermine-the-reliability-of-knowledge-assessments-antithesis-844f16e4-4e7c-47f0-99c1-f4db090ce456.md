# Does the variability in truth conditions undermine the reliability of knowledge assessments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 844f16e4-4e7c-47f0-99c1-f4db090ce456
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Suppose two experts evaluate the same data under different contexts and arrive at opposing conclusions about whether knowledge is present. Intuitively, such discrepancies reduce the reliability of knowledge assessments since they depend on variable truth conditions.**, **A principle of reliable epistemic evaluation holds that knowledge assessments should converge among competent judges. If truth conditions vary with contexts, this reliability is called into question, undermining the utility of contextualist evaluations.**
