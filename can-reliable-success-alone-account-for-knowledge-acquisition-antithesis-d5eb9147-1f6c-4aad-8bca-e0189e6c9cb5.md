# Can reliable success alone account for knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d5eb9147-1f6c-4aad-8bca-e0189e6c9cb5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Approach](virtue-epistemology-approach-synthesis-b14f243d-0108-46b0-abbb-b26bd31de791.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider a case in which a machine consistently produces true beliefs due to reliable mechanisms. Intuitively, calling this knowledge appears to mischaracterize what we mean by knowledge in human epistemic agents.**, **It seems implausible that mere reliable success in forming true beliefs suffices for genuine knowledge.**
