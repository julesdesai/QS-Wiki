# Can the variability of unconscious influences undermine the consistency required for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e886350-172d-4723-8076-f793415e960f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person's unconscious influences change over time, leading to inconsistent interpretations of similar experiences. Intuitively, we either have to say that such variability does not impact knowledge or that it does. If we say it does not impact knowledge, then knowledge tolerates inconsistency. If we say it does impact knowledge, then unconscious influences render knowledge unstable.**
