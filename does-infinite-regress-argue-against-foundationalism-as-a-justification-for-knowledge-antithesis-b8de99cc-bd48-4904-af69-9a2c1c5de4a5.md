# Does infinite regress argue against foundationalism as a justification for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8de99cc-bd48-4904-af69-9a2c1c5de4a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Suppose that each belief requires justification by another belief, leading to an infinite chain of justifications. It is implausible that we have an infinite series of justified beliefs due to cognitive limitations.**, **Basic beliefs, which do not require further justification, are posed as a solution to avoid infinite regress, but without justification, it is unclear how these basic beliefs can confer knowledge to derived beliefs.**
