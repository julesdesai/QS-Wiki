# Does the theory imply an infinite regress problem when ruling out alternatives?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23ec4a7c-9057-4779-b343-5b22634039b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-5db4d183-a5a9-469a-b4ff-d831c6a59a11.md)

## Content

**If ruling out relevant alternatives is required for knowledge, it might entail that one must also rule out alternatives to those alternatives, leading to a potential infinite regress.**, **An infinite regress of ruling out alternatives would make attaining knowledge impractically cumbersome or outright impossible.**
