# Does Direct Realism accommodate the influence of prior mental states on perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 45b6ea1d-1a70-47a5-9125-5dcc03ff1dc6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where two people view an abstract painting, with one seeing it as chaotic and the other as harmonious due to their different artistic preferences. Intuitively, we would judge that their prior mental states influence their perceptual experience.**, **It is implausible to claim that such experiences provide direct access to external objects if perception is significantly shaped by subjective factors.**
