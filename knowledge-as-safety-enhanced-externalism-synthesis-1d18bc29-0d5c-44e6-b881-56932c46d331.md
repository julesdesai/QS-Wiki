# Knowledge as Safety-Enhanced Externalism

**Depth:** 5

**Parent:** [Does reliability ignore luck affecting knowledge?](does-reliability-ignore-luck-affecting-knowledge-antithesis-f5d70159-4fac-4e3c-9412-1462fd59b9fd.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the reliability of the cognitive process is not undermined by the presence of relevant false beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that in most nearby possible worlds where the person forms the belief in a similar way, the belief is true**
