# Can Process Reliabilism account for false but intuitive perception-based beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6692bd90-653a-45be-ad02-00746d2b978a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-54c291ff-d1f5-45c3-8197-657cbffa4347.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone looks out their window and mistakenly believes there's snow when it's actually white sand, and they have no reliable way to distinguish between the two appearances. The belief is formed using normal visual perception, which is generally reliable, yet the belief is false. Intuitively, the person seems justified in their belief as they acted upon standard perception cues.**, **A plausible principle suggests that intuitive perceptual beliefs should be treated as justified if they are formed via reliable processes, even in isolated cases of honest error. This shows potential tension when Process Reliabilism bases justification solely on outcome truth.**
