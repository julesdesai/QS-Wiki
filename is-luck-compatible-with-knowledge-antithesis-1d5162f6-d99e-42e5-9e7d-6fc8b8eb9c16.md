# Is luck compatible with knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d5162f6-d99e-42e5-9e7d-6fc8b8eb9c16
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person C finds a winning lottery ticket. C believes they will win today based on this ticket, and indeed, they win, but it is based on luck. it is intuitive that C does not truly know they will win.**, **Even if C had virtuous character traits, like being prudent or attentive to details, the winning outcome relies on luck rather than those virtues. This suggests justified true belief is not sufficient for knowledge in this case.**
