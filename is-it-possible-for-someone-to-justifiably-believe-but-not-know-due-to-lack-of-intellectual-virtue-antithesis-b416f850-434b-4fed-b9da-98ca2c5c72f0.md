# Is it possible for someone to justifiably believe but not know due to lack of intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b416f850-434b-4fed-b9da-98ca2c5c72f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person justifiably believes a true proposition due to trustworthy testimony, yet has not engaged their own intellectual virtue. Intuitively, this person does not know because their belief lacks the necessary intellectual virtue.**, **Justified belief arising from others without employing one's intellectual virtue raises questions about the authenticity of knowledge.**
