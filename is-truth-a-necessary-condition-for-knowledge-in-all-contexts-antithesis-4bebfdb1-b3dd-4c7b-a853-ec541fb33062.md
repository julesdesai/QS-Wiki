# Is truth a necessary condition for knowledge in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4bebfdb1-b3dd-4c7b-a853-ec541fb33062
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliability](virtue-epistemology-with-reliability-synthesis-6944a8d9-8cde-45e2-a167-ffb34caa5f33.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is in a virtual reality simulation where all sensory input and cognitive processing are deceived, yet their beliefs coincide with truths about reality. Intuitively, one might question whether their "truths" count as knowledge in the traditional sense, challenging the necessity of truth as contextually defined.**, **There is a plausible principle that the notion of truth in epistemology is context-sensitive and not absolute, which might allow for knowledge without truth in some cases.**
