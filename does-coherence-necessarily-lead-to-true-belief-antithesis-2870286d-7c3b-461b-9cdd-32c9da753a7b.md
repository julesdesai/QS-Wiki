# Does coherence necessarily lead to true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2870286d-7c3b-461b-9cdd-32c9da753a7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Instrumental Coherence Theory](instrumental-coherence-theory-synthesis-188d4652-e220-47fa-9e2f-da98cf583190.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which direct perception and instrumentally aided perception cohere, but both are mistaken due to external distortion, as in a mirage observed both with naked eye and through binoculars. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the coherence does not necessarily lead to true belief.**, **A strong intuition exists that coherence might be achieved even when all perceptions are systematically distorted, suggesting that coherence is not sufficient for knowledge.**
