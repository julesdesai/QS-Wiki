# Does the requirement of seeming truth imply all true beliefs are justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17cd1049-b3f5-4789-bb3c-5ec8cab5a780
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Conservatism: Seeming Justification](phenomenal-conservatism-seeming-justification-synthesis-de77a591-8397-48c7-98a8-42a761bdf13a.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person who believes their horoscope, which by chance, accurately predicts their day. The belief seems true to them temporarily but is based on unreliable sources. Intuitively, this does not appear as justified knowledge, suggesting seeming is not sufficient for justification.**
