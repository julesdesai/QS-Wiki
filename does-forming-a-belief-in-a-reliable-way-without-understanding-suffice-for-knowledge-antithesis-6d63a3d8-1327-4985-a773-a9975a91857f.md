# Does forming a belief in a reliable way without understanding suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6d63a3d8-1327-4985-a773-a9975a91857f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person reliably predicts stock prices using a method they do not understand at all. Intuitively, we may hesitate to call this knowledge.**, **A knowledge principle necessitates some level of understanding of the belief-forming process for it to constitute knowledge.**
