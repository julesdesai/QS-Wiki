# Are intellectual virtues truly necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91b90e47-c962-4bca-bbe4-74929c436738
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person knows their own name without actively exercising intellectual virtues like open-mindedness or intellectual courage. In such a case, intuitively we would judge that the person still knows their name.**, **There is a plausible principle that some knowledge is basic and does not require the active exercise of intellectual virtues.**, **It is implausible that all knowledge acquisition requires virtues, as some knowledge is obvious or innate.**
