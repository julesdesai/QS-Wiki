## Content

**The standards for knowledge are determined in part by the context in which the belief is evaluated**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **In some contexts, it is not necessary for a person to have conscious access to evidence to know a proposition**,**For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the person believes the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief satisfies the contextual standards of reliability or evidence**
