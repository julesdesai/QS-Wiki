# Is reliability sufficient if counterexamples exist?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d826cf42-df41-426c-8a4f-ef62ff35357f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-9b4935ff-e1a0-4d23-aeca-7dfa716ad7fb.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where someone forms a belief using a usually reliable process, but the process fails in atypical situations where it unluckily yields false beliefs. Intuitively, this person's belief should not count as knowledge.**, **A belief formed by relying on past reliable performance cannot guarantee true beliefs in unexpected or unusual contexts, undermining the necessity of reliability alone.**
