# Can knowledge be reliably produced even with lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37c2358f-3d01-4c90-b120-1fe3947ed1f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fec1f444-460d-4835-9da9-5fd5405f3ea1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person guesses the answer to a difficult question and gets it right. In such a scenario, even though the guess was correct, intuitively we would judge that this is not an instance of knowledge.**, **A process that sometimes produces true beliefs due to lucky guesses does not seem reliably truth-conducive.**
