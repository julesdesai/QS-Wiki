# Do intellectual virtues cover all aspects of belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47856869-1d2b-449a-bad3-37cd2b39a1ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief is formed with intellectual virtues, but crucial aspects of the belief rely on external factors not controlled by virtues. Intuitively, we might judge that intellectual virtue alone does not suffice for knowledge if external factors play a significant role.**, **There is a plausible theory that belief formation is influenced by both internal and external factors. This theory suggests that solely relying on intellectual virtue might overlook important external contributions to knowledge.**
