# Does conviction necessarily align with sufficient evidence for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0188fa32-7373-45e1-b730-cf996c26a996
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that conviction does not always correlate with sufficient evidence.**, **Suppose we encounter a case where a person has a belief supported strongly by emotional conviction but little supporting evidence. Intuitively, we might not regard this conviction as knowledge, indicating a disparity between conviction and evidence.**
