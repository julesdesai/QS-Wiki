# Does contextualism unnecessarily complicate the structure of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91c8c8a2-d7ca-4230-a606-0ea05fed8f36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Simpler theories of knowledge often involve straightforward criteria for knowledge attribution, like truth, belief, and justification.**, **Contextualism introduces variability in knowledge standards based on the situation, potentially adding complexity without clear epistemic benefit.**, **This complexity might make contextualism less appealing or practical for everyday epistemic evaluation, as it requires constant reassessment of contextual factors.**
