# Can knowledge truly remain context-independent given practical interests?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e333d266-aba7-42b1-9b64-1d5f9d5aae56
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Interest-Relative Invariantism Summary](interest-relative-invariantism-summary-synthesis-34d6e50b-4fc4-4003-bede-59691f379a3b.md)

## Content

**A case in which an agent believes they cannot afford to be wrong about a proposition, yet the standards for knowledge do not change to reflect their heightened interest, challenges the independence of knowledge from context**, **In situations where practical interests are significantly high, intuitively, one might believe that the criteria for what constitutes knowledge should shift accordingly**, **A plausible principle suggests that if a person's stakes are high, their inquiry becomes more stringent, which conflicts with a fixed standard for knowledge**
