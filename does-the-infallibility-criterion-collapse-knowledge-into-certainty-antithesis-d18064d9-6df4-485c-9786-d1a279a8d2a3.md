# Does the infallibility criterion collapse knowledge into certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d18064d9-6df4-485c-9786-d1a279a8d2a3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infallibility](knowledge-as-infallibility-synthesis-8f0bcd35-bb4c-4192-816d-76a63a41903c.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has absolute certainty in their belief about a mathematical proof.**, **Certainty implies no doubt, aligning with the infallibility condition for knowledge.**, **If knowledge requires infallibility, then knowledge and certainty are indistinguishable, which conflicts with cases where knowledge is attributed without certainty.**
