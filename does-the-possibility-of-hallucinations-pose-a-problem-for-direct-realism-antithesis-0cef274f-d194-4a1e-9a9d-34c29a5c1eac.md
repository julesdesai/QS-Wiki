# Does the possibility of hallucinations pose a problem for direct realism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0cef274f-d194-4a1e-9a9d-34c29a5c1eac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-42a15467-8636-4e99-a9b6-42a7b3d54195.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person experiences a hallucination during a fever that appears as vividly as any real perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception cannot be a direct access to reality, as hallucinations present no external objects.**, **It is implausible that perception truly involves an external object when hallucinations occur, suggesting that mental processes are influencing what we perceive as real.**
