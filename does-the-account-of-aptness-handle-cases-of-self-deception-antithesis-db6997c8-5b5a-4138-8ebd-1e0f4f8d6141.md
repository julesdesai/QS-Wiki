# Does the account of aptness handle cases of self-deception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db6997c8-5b5a-4138-8ebd-1e0f4f8d6141
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Triple-A Virtue Epistemology](triple-a-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5ec13be3-cfbe-4179-96b3-378e0ebd05b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes they are healthy because they ignore symptoms and only focus on good health results. The belief turned out to be true because the symptoms were unrelated to a serious issue.**, **Intuitively, their belief is not because of a skillful understanding of their health, demonstrating the difficulty in ensuring beliefs are both apt and skill-based when self-deception is involved.**
