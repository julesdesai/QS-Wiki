# Can externalism explain lucky true beliefs as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cc50e5a1-2fc6-4356-b26c-da33b2ba2775
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-5b44df35-6663-4278-91dc-65b3fcbf870c.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where someone believes a coin will land on heads while flipping it, purely by guesswork, and it does land on heads. Intuitively, we do not consider this to be knowledge irrespective of the belief being true.**, **A true belief resulting merely from chance, even in a reliable process, should not be satisfied as knowledge as it undermines our notion of justified true belief.**
