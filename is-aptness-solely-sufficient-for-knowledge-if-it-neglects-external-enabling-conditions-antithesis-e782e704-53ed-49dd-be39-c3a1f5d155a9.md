# Is aptness solely sufficient for knowledge if it neglects external enabling conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e782e704-53ed-49dd-be39-c3a1f5d155a9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Apt Belief](knowledge-as-apt-belief-synthesis-6ac0cb6c-f2b3-4636-9c8a-2a793aab4e4b.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that aptness in belief requires not only internal competence but also appropriate external conditions for the exercise of that competence.**, **A belief's truth being due to competence might still fail to suffice for knowledge if the external conditions obstruct or interfere with the competence's expression, making mere aptness insufficient.**, **Consider a situation where an individual's belief is apt due to a background condition negligently ignored, intuitively we would judge this does not constitute knowledge.**
