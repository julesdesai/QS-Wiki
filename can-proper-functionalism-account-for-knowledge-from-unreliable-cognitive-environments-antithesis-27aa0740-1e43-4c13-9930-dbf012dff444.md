# Can proper functionalism account for knowledge from unreliable cognitive environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27aa0740-1e43-4c13-9930-dbf012dff444
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-56ac2890-cc65-4132-90ba-c28a7f97b59c.md)

## Content

**There are environments in which cognitive faculties are generally unreliable but still produce some true beliefs.**, **If cognitive faculties produce true beliefs even in unreliable environments, then those beliefs could be considered as knowledge.**, **A belief produced in an unreliable environment might still not be considered knowledge if proper functionalism holds.**
