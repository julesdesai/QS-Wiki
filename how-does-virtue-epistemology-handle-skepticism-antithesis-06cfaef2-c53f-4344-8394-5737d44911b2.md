# How does virtue epistemology handle skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06cfaef2-c53f-4344-8394-5737d44911b2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider the skeptical challenge that questions whether we can ever have true beliefs given the possibility of universal deception, such as being in a computer simulation. Intuitively, we might doubt that intellectual virtues can straightforwardly provide a response to such radical skepticism.**, **There is a plausible principle that skepticism represents a fundamental challenge to claims of knowledge. This principle, together with doubts about the conclusive power of intellectual virtues, suggests inadequacies in addressing skepticism.**
