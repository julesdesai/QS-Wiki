# Can reliability account for situations where a process becomes unreliable without the subject's knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4dd6177c-a6b9-4d9e-a8e7-dbfca581062a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-c84f45b2-357a-4b29-be66-6a09ffb029e2.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a typically reliable process becomes flawed, unknown to the user. The individual continues to form beliefs based on this process. Intuitively, this change calls into question whether their subsequent beliefs can be considered knowledge**, **A process ceasing to be reliable, unbeknownst to the subject, suggests that mere historical reliability does not ensure ongoing knowledge**
