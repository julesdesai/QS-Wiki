# Is belief with no practical consequences really knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7fcd735c-bd6d-41d0-8541-00aa647d1c51
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextual Pragmatism](knowledge-as-contextual-pragmatism-synthesis-58b0d4c4-4eeb-46a9-99af-766901c4c5df.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes the sun will rise tomorrow, which is true, and they are justified based on past observations, yet they derive no practical consequences from this belief. Intuitively, most would say this is still knowledge, challenging the necessity of practical consequences.**, **It is implausible that a person must derive practical consequences from a belief to claim knowledge; many scientific and mathematical truths are known without direct practical implication.**
