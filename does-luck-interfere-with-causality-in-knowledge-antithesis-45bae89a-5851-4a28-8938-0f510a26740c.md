# Does luck interfere with causality in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 45bae89a-5851-4a28-8938-0f510a26740c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Goldman's Causal Theory of Knowledge](goldmans-causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d8bdc24-364e-4571-9b16-831c07368037.md)

## Content

**Imagine an archer who shoots an arrow at a target, and a wind gust redirects the arrow, but another subsequent wind gus surprisingly redirects it back on course, hitting the target.**, **In this case, the success seems to depend on luck rather than a reliable causal chain, suggesting that causal connections in knowledge might not ensure reliability.**
