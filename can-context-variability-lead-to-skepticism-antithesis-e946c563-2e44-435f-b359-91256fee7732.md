# Can context variability lead to skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e946c563-2e44-435f-b359-91256fee7732
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if knowledge attribution can vary with context, then it opens the door to radical skepticism, where no one can truly know anything because contexts could become infinitely demanding. This principle together with contextualism implies that knowledge could be seen as elusive or unachievable in some contexts.**, **Consider a situation where context makes the standards for knowing so high that even the best evidence is insufficient. Intuitively, this illustrates how contextualism could push people toward skepticism about their own knowledge.**
