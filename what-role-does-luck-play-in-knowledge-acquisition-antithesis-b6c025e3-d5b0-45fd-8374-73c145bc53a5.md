# What role does luck play in knowledge acquisition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6c025e3-d5b0-45fd-8374-73c145bc53a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person accurately predicts the outcome of an event by luck rather than skill or evidence. Intuitively, this does not seem to constitute genuine knowledge**, **Knowledge is traditionally thought to require more than true belief; it requires a non-accidental, justified connection to the truth**, **Cognitive success achieved through luck undermines the essential role of intellectual virtue and reasoned justification, which are central to defining knowledge**
