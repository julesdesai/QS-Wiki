# Does the claim that perception provides immediate knowledge eliminate the role of memory in knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aa9079fe-7f47-420a-a177-22ef78c5a542
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone sees a complex event and claims to know all its details solely through perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that memory is necessary to retain and make sense of these details.**, **It is implausible that perception alone without the aid of memory can provide complete knowledge of complex, temporally extended events. This conflicts with the idea that perception gives immediate, full knowledge.**
