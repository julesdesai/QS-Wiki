# Knowledge as Infallibilism

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is a belief's safety from error in nearby possible worlds truly achievable or definable?](is-a-beliefs-safety-from-error-in-nearby-possible-worlds-truly-achievable-or-definable-antithesis-f926aa67-285e-4de7-85b0-14d72a02c711.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that there be no possibility of error in the belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and there is no possibility of error in the belief**
