# Is cognitive ability necessary for believing fundamental truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44d26eae-e69c-439e-8453-40239f319846
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-193f50ad-c71a-4e26-bf9b-21d5bbf78043.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that some fundamental truths are accepted based on societal or cultural transmission rather than cognitive inquiry, like believing in mathematical axioms. Intuitively, one might consider such beliefs as knowledge due to their truth and foundational acceptance.**, **If fundamental truths are accepted as knowledge without specific individual cognitive verification, then cognitive ability may not always be necessary for knowledge.**
