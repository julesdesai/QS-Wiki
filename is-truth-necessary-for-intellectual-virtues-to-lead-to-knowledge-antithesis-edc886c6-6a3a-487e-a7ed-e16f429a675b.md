# Is truth necessary for intellectual virtues to lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** edc886c6-6a3a-487e-a7ed-e16f429a675b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d110ebcb-cebc-4b41-b013-8e3cca593b6d.md)

## Content

**A person can engage effectively in scientific method through intellectual virtues like reasoning and observation, leading to a false yet justified belief about a phenomenon**, **Consider historical cases where prominent scientists used intellectual virtues to arrive at conclusions we now know to be false, challenging the alignment of truth necessity with intellectual virtues**, **Inferences drawn well from incomplete data through cognitive skills may reach wrong conclusions, suggesting that intellectual virtues can still function even if not leading to truth**
