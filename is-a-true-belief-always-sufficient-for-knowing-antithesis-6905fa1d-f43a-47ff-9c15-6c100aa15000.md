# Is a true belief always sufficient for knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6905fa1d-f43a-47ff-9c15-6c100aa15000
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-ea617099-7596-484d-aca6-1b318b0033ac.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which someone guesses the outcome of a coin toss and it turns out to be correct. Intuitively, this does not seem like knowledge, even though the belief is true.**, **The principle that true belief constitutes knowledge ignores the need for justification or reliable method.**
