# Can differing contexts lead to contradictions in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 050c4acb-dd74-4f6e-b29b-96da435257e4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person claims to know a fact in a relaxed conversational context, but the claim is challenged in a more stringent intellectual debate. Intuitively, this scenario suggests potential for contradictory interpretations regarding whether the person truly knows the fact.**, **If the standards for knowing change based on context, it implies that a single knowledge claim can be simultaneously true and false depending on the context. This seems inconsistent with our general understanding of truth.**
