# Does Nozick's view exclude logical truths as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 019aa9d5-ab2f-45cb-bb40-16b73b84cdab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Reliabilism](nozicks-conditional-reliabilism-synthesis-29b3c3e0-b3ff-4b45-a994-893f15be46b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a mathematician believes a logical truth such as "2+2=4." Intuitively, she knows this truth.**, **Logical truths are true in all possible worlds. Thus, the belief in a logical truth cannot track differing truth values across possible worlds, as the truth never changes.**, **If belief in logical truths cannot be contingent upon counterfactual conditions, then the requirement for reliable truth-tracking might exclude mathematical or logical knowledge.**
