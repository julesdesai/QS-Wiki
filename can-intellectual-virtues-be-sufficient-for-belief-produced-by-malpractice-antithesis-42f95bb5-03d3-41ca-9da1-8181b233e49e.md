# Can intellectual virtues be sufficient for belief produced by malpractice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42f95bb5-03d3-41ca-9da1-8181b233e49e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology With Virtuous Belief Formation](virtue-epistemology-with-virtuous-belief-formation-synthesis-53b228ae-7464-400d-be01-037da588b5a0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a competent scientist manipulates results to confirm a hypothesis they already believe to be true, but the hypothesis is indeed true.**, **The belief is true and coincides with cognitive achievement due to the scientist's expertise, albeit reached through intellectual misconduct.**, **Despite the alignment of truth and cognitive achievement, intuitively, this belief does not constitute knowledge due to the lack of epistemic integrity in the conduct.**
