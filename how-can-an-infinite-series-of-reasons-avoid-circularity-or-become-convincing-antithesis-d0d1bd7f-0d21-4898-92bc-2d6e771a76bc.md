# How can an infinite series of reasons avoid circularity or become convincing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0d1bd7f-0d21-4898-92bc-2d6e771a76bc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-c0072d0f-5658-4115-88b4-8385b90f0067.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person provides a reason R1 for a belief, then provides R2 for R1, continuing infinitely. Intuitively, it seems implausible that each subsequent reason does not eventually circle back and rely, even indirectly, on a prior reason.**, **If a series of reasons is non-repeating, and each supports the next, intuitively it must be the case that some reasons will be so abstract that they lack practical relevance to the original belief.**
