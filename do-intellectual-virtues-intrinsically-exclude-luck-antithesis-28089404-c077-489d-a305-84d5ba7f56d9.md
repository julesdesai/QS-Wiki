# Do intellectual virtues intrinsically exclude luck?

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an experienced birdwatcher identifies a rare bird correctly due to its distinctive call, but unbeknownst to them, a prankster is mimicking the bird call perfectly with a recording. The correct identification seems due to luck.**, **This suggests that knowledge attributed to intellectual virtues can still involve epistemic luck, undermining the claim that virtues alone can exclude luck.**
