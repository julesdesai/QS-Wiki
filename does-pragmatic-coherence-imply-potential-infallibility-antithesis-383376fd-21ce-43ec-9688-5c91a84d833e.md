# Does pragmatic coherence imply potential infallibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 383376fd-21ce-43ec-9688-5c91a84d833e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherence Theory](pragmatic-coherence-theory-synthesis-0a69366a-ab49-4b15-a3e1-b772859c47c7.md)

## Content

**If pragmatic coherence were definitive of knowledge, coherent beliefs would be infallible.**, **In reality, even well-coherent systems can contain errors or be shown false, implying coherence does not equal infallibility.**, **Potential fallibility indicates pragmatic coherence as insufficient for true knowledge claims.**
