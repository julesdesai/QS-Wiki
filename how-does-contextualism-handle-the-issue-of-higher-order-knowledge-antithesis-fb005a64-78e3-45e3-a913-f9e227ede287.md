# How does contextualism handle the issue of higher-order knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fb005a64-78e3-45e3-a913-f9e227ede287
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Many philosophical theories of knowledge involve knowing that one knows something, known as higher-order knowledge. Consider a person claims to know a fact, but cannot explain or elaborate on how they know this fact.**, **In contexts with elevated standards, this lack of higher-order knowledge could be critical, yet in everyday contexts, it might be ignored.**, **Contextualism might be challenged to account for how different layers of knowledge and justification interact when assessing knowledge claims.**
