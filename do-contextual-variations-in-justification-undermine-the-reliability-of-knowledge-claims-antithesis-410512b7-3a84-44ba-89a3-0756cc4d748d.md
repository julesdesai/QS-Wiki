# Do contextual variations in justification undermine the reliability of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 410512b7-3a84-44ba-89a3-0756cc4d748d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Multi-Standard Contextualism](multi-standard-contextualism-synthesis-9865cf66-a535-4334-b5b4-390c78d28080.md)

## Content

**Suppose contextual standards for justification vary significantly, such that a person needing different amounts of evidence in different contexts can claim to know the same proposition.**, **If one can claim knowledge in some contexts and not in others without a change in evidence, the reliability of knowledge claims is undermined.**
