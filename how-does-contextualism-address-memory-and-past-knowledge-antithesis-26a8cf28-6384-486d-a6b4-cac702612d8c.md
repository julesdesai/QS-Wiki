# How Does Contextualism Address Memory and Past Knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 26a8cf28-6384-486d-a6b4-cac702612d8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-6d1ded2e-2e81-4f10-b6c8-db699d1664cc.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone remembers an event differently as years go by. Intuitively, we either have to say that their new memory is more accurate now or that the original memory was more accurate.**, **Contextualism suggests that knowledge changes with context, but this implication threatens the stability of knowledge over time, as memories could be seen as losing validity based on context changes.**, **It is implausible to claim that our recollections of past knowledge should adapt with evolving contexts without potentially distorting the truth of past events.**
