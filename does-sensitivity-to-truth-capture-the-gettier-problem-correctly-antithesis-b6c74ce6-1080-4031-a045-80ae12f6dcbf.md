# Does sensitivity to truth capture the Gettier problem correctly?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6c74ce6-1080-4031-a045-80ae12f6dcbf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a Gettier scenario where a person forms a belief based on defective evidence but coincidentally happens to be true.**, **It is intuitively judged that the belief does not constitute knowledge, even if it is sensitive to truth, due to the accidental correctness.**, **There is a plausible principle that antidotes to Gettier problems require more than sensitivity, suggesting a different mechanism for aligning truth and belief.**
