# Does context undermine the universality of justification in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fab1250c-55f9-472f-8865-88724dd92477
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if they believe the proposition and have justification for it, which implies a universal relation between justification and knowledge. If standards for knowing vary with context, then justification may lose its universality, which is intuitively problematic.**, **In a case where a person's strong justification for a belief leads to knowledge in a lenient context but not in a stringent context, intuitively, there seems to be an inconsistency in the role justification plays.**
