# Is knowledge possible without conscious belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ffe49cac-7cdd-4786-977f-0b02a6720a82
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Modified Foundationalism with Non-Conscious Justification](modified-foundationalism-with-non-conscious-justification-synthesis-e080555b-8494-4f36-b1f9-a93cbae9e68f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a disposition to act in ways that are consistent with a particular belief but never consciously endorses or feels this belief. Intuitively, we would judge the person does not truly know the proposition because their belief is not a conscious part of their mental life.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing involves consciously held beliefs. This principle suggests that dispositions to believe, without conscious endorsement, are inadequate for knowledge.**
