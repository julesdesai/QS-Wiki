# Do intuitions suffice as evidence for basic self-evident beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** abac5479-1abf-4569-9f6d-31e5af20d46f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Communicable Justification](foundationalism-with-communicable-justification-synthesis-8dbcdd2b-eb8b-4593-a7ca-0bfc89d9258b.md)

## Content

**Suppose person A and person B look at a color swatch under different lighting conditions. Person A believes the swatch is blue, while person B believes it is green, with each claiming their belief is self-evident. These differing intuitions cast doubt on the reliability of intuitions as evidence for self-evident beliefs.**, **Intuitions, which foundationalism relies upon for basic self-evident beliefs, may be misleading or vary across individuals, challenging their role as reliable justifications.**
