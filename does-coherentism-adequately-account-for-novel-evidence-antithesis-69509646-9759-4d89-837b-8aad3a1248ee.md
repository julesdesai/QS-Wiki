# Does coherentism adequately account for novel evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69509646-9759-4d89-837b-8aad3a1248ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Knowledge Summary](coherentist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-1fa3c944-9677-491a-8edd-ea154ce998c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a coherent system of beliefs does not update with new evidence. Intuitively, the system seems unjustified in neglecting new, relevant information.**, **There is a plausible principle that robust grounds for beliefs require responsiveness to new evidence. Coherence alone might not mandate updating systems with novel evidence.**
