# Can one's belief track falsehoods yet still be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b6125ea-4593-4e90-b906-647d79fe1547
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dda35d00-5b10-4342-b443-f197350806e5.md)

## Content

**Imagine someone who holds a true belief based partly on false premises that are coincidentally inconsequential to the belief's truth. Intuitively, they might still be seen as having knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that false components in reasoning do not necessarily undermine the truth of an overall conclusion. This principle together with beliefs based on some falsehoods implies such beliefs can still be knowledge.**
