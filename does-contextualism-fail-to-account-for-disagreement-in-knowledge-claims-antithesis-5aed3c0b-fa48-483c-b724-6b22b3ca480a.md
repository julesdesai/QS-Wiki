# Does contextualism fail to account for disagreement in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5aed3c0b-fa48-483c-b724-6b22b3ca480a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-bce49363-1810-4900-8ccf-1e421dbc62ca.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people with differing contextual standards disagree about whether a belief counts as knowledge.**, **Intuitively, resolving such disagreements should involve appealing to common standards of justification.**, **If contextualism allows for differing standards, it fails to provide a common ground for resolving disagreements in knowledge claims.**
