# Do differing perceptions across observers indicate direct realism's failure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f451c5ff-8d80-4d66-aea8-482ca770224b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Selective Direct Realism](selective-direct-realism-synthesis-744f6327-3f79-490f-be88-a0aa209da42b.md)

## Content

**Different observers in the same environment often have varying perceptions, such as differences in color perception due to color blindness.**, **If different observers do not perceive the same unaltered foundational parts of reality, then knowledge does not involve direct perception of reality.**, **Variations in perception among different individuals indicate that perceptions are not stable across varying conditions.**
