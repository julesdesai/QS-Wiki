# Is contextualism compatible with the closure principle of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7dd461c-2843-41b3-bf93-99c95273b89b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person knows that they have hands and also knows that having hands entails they are not a brain in a vat. The closure principle, which is that if one knows a proposition and knows that this proposition entails another, then one should know the second proposition. Intuitively, the person should know they are not a brain in a vat.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be closed under known entailment. Contextualism suggests standards can change, which appears to violate this closure principle by allowing someone to know the first proposition but not the second.**
