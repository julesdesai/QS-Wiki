# Externalist Contextualism as Knowledge Adjustment

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does coherence necessarily imply justification in all cases?](does-coherence-necessarily-imply-justification-in-all-cases-antithesis-46bab098-54b4-4321-8937-fe5870470654.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is justified according to the external contextual factors**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is justified according to the external contextual factors**
