# Can phenomenalism account for unperceived objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02873720-09e1-4c8a-9e9c-e54772f629de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a tree falls in a remote forest, and no one is present to perceive it. Intuitively, we would judge that the tree still exists and makes a sound.**, **Physical objects as logical constructions out of sensory experiences would imply the tree does not exist or make a sound without perception.**
