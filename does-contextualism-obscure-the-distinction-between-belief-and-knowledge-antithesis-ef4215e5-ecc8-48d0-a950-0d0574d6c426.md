# Does contextualism obscure the distinction between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ef4215e5-ecc8-48d0-a950-0d0574d6c426
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge](contextualist-knowledge-synthesis-93bee04f-150e-4686-ac45-c85acedfb5db.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that belief and knowledge are distinct mental states, distinguished by factors such as justification.**, **Contextualism may allow for knowledge standards so low in certain contexts that they blur the line between justified belief and genuine knowledge.**, **In such a situation, it becomes implausible to maintain the distinction between belief and knowledge, undermining the clarity of their differentiation.**
