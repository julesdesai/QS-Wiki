# Can knowledge exist without the capacity for reflection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ddbe56e5-e757-41cd-8f07-33333480fad5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge](reliable-process-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8a084bde-8e94-4b49-86d3-6339aef5c9e6.md)

## Content

**It seems plausible that someone could have a true belief formed through a reliable process but lack the capacity to reflect on their belief or the process**, **Reflection is often associated with how knowledge is assessed and validated internally**, **Without reflection, it's unclear whether a person can truly be said to 'know' something rather than merely possess accurate information**
