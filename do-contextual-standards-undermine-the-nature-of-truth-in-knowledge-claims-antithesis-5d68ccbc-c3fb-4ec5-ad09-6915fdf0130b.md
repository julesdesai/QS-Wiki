# Do contextual standards undermine the nature of truth in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d68ccbc-c3fb-4ec5-ad09-6915fdf0130b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an employee claims to know about their company’s financial health based on a report. During a meeting with investors, the truth of this knowledge must be supported by rigorous evidence.**, **In an internal meeting among lower-level employees, the same claim might be regarded as knowledge based on assumptions not supported by hard data.**, **Intuitively, if truth in knowledge claims is contextual, then truth becomes negotiable, challenging the classic idea that knowledge is justified true belief.**
