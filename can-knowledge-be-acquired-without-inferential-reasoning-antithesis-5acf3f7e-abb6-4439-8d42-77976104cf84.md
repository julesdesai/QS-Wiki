# Can knowledge be acquired without inferential reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5acf3f7e-abb6-4439-8d42-77976104cf84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that inferential reasoning contributes to knowledge acquisition. This principle together with knowledge is directly acquired from external objects without mediation entails that knowledge acquisition is non-inferential.**, **Consider a scenario where a complex pattern is recognized and understood through inference rather than direct perception. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that inferential reasoning contributes to knowledge acquisition.**
