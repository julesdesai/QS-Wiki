# Does contextualism risk arbitrary epistemic standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec385fa8-fe7f-4c04-b03f-74d7a98a80d4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that epistemic standards should be based on objective criteria.**, **Suppose the context determines standards in an arbitrary fashion leading to differing knowledge claims without a clear basis. Intuitively, it seems implausible and potentially unfair that such significant standards could rest on arbitrary contextually dependent factors.**
