# Can a belief be justified if the evidence is misleading?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dbecd3fc-6748-4948-8a18-588ecb237043
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-d871be87-85c4-4634-87bb-b00a8ec22277.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that suggests if all available evidence leads to a false conclusion, then the belief formed on that basis might still be seen as justified despite being incorrect.**, **This principle highlights a potential disconnect between evidence being adequate and the truth of the belief, questioning if misleading evidence can suffice for justified belief.**
