# Is being caused by the truth sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone forms a true belief based on a lucky guess rather than evidence or reliable methods. Intuitively, this seems insufficient for us to say they possess knowledge.**, **A principle suggests that if a belief is caused in the right way by the truth of the proposition, it should be considered knowledge. However, in cases where the cause is a mere coincidence, this principle does not hold as intuitively providing knowledge.**
