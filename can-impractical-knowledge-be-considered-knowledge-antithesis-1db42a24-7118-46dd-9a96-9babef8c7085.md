# Can impractical knowledge be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1db42a24-7118-46dd-9a96-9babef8c7085
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-af5a1410-77c2-4ceb-b390-2ced8876b488.md)

## Content

**Consider the case of someone who spends their life studying an ancient language that is no longer spoken or used. Intuitively, we would judge that this person possesses knowledge of the language, even if it has no current practical use.**, **It is implausible that knowledge requires practical usefulness, as many academic pursuits are valuable despite having little immediate practical application.**
