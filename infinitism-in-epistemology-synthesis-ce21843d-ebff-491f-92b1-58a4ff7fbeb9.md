## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the belief can be justified by an infinite, non-circular chain of reasons**, **An infinite chain of reasons does not contain any foundational beliefs**
