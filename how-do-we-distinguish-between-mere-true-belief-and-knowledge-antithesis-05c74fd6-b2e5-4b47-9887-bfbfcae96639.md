# How do we distinguish between mere true belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 05c74fd6-b2e5-4b47-9887-bfbfcae96639
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person guesses the outcome of a coin flip, and it turns out to be true. Intuitively, guessing does not equate to knowing, even if the guess is correct.**, **A plausible principle is that knowing requires more than just a lucky guess. This principle together with the guessing scenario suggests that the presence of luck undermines the qualification for knowledge.**
