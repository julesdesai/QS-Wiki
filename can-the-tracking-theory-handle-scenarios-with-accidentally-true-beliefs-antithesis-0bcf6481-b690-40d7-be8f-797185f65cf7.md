# Can the Tracking Theory handle scenarios with accidentally true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0bcf6481-b690-40d7-be8f-797185f65cf7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3fc68b12-ca29-40d7-8341-51e40d71c5e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes that the time is noon, their watch reads noon, and it is indeed noon due to a random power outage causing the watch to stop earlier that day. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows it is noon despite having a true belief.**, **In such a scenario, if the proposition about the time were false, the person would believe it because their watch still reads noon. Hence, the belief persists even if false.**, **Even if the time were slightly different, say one minute past noon due to the power outage, the belief via the stopped watch would persist. Hence, under slight changes, the belief remains unchanged.**
