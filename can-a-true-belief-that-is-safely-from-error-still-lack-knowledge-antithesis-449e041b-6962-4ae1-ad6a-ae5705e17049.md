# Can a true belief that is safely from error still lack knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 449e041b-6962-4ae1-ad6a-ae5705e17049
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**A person may have a belief that is true and formed in a safe manner but still lack knowledge because they do not understand the reasons why it is true**, **In a scenario where someone guesses the correct number in a lottery without any understanding of the lottery process, intuitively, we would not say they know the outcome of the lottery**, **While a belief can be safely protected from error across nearby possible worlds, this does not guarantee the individual has reflective access to why the belief is true**
