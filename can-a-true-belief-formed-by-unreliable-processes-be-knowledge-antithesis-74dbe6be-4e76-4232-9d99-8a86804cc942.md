# Can a true belief formed by unreliable processes be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74dbe6be-4e76-4232-9d99-8a86804cc942
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief by consulting an unreliable source, such as a consistently incorrect individual, yet the belief is accidentally true. Intuitively, we would not consider this as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be produced by a reliable process which consistently leads to truth. This principle combined with accidentally true beliefs formed by unreliable means entails that reliability is necessary for knowledge.**
