# Does the truth condition adequately distinguish knowledge from belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 64224401-8bb8-4dc3-bef6-f00a9c3bb827
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-basic-beliefs-synthesis-db3b35bf-6f35-4ce4-82b6-698bdde0b345.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which two people correctly guess the outcome of a random event. Intuitively, their correct guesses do not equal knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that discerning knowledge requires more than true belief, such as reliability or causation. This complicates the direct association of truth with knowledge.**, **Suppose a context in which truth is coincidental rather than derived from understanding. This indicates the truth condition may not be sufficient to distinguish knowledge.**
