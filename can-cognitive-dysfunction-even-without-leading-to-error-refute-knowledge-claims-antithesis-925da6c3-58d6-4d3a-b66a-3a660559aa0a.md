# Can cognitive dysfunction, even without leading to error, refute knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 925da6c3-58d6-4d3a-b66a-3a660559aa0a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where an individual's cognitive faculties are dysfunctional, yet they do not lead to factual errors due to chance. In such a situation, intuitively we would judge that knowledge claims are refuted by the dysfunction, not by the outcome.**, **It is implausible that a true belief resulting from cognitive dysfunction counts as knowledge.**
