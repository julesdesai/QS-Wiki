# Does knowledge always require intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7feacd8d-afc9-4e0e-b3e1-73bbdb4bfaaf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-40b3d691-a5bb-479c-ada3-1931521a86e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person guesses the correct lottery numbers purely by chance without any intellectual virtue involved. In such a case, intuitively, the person does not know the lottery numbers despite holding a true belief.**, **The concept of intellectual virtue implies that knowledge requires reliable cognitive character traits, which are absent in the lottery example.**, **Knowledge is generally understood to require more than the mere possession of a true belief, suggesting that intellectual virtue alone may not be sufficient for knowledge.**
