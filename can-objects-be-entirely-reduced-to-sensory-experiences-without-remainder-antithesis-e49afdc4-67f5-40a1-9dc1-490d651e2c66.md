# Can objects be entirely reduced to sensory experiences without remainder?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e49afdc4-67f5-40a1-9dc1-490d651e2c66
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-6256cdee-fde6-4d81-b9e8-d1897bfcfd04.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is experiencing a red apple through sight, smell, and touch. Intuitively, we would judge that the apple's existence is more than just these sensory experiences; it has properties independent of our perception.**, **It is plausible that objects have an existence and nature that transcends the particular sensory experiences they evoke. This suggests that objects cannot be fully reduced to logical constructions out of sensory experiences.**
