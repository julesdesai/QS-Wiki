# Can knowledge be accidental?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db10c8d8-765b-497e-ab8c-f5a2b8a444f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person's cognitive faculties accidentally produce a true belief due to a random, fortunate influence, such as a lucky guess. Intuitively, we might think that the person still possesses knowledge because the belief is true and justified through luck.**, **A principle in epistemology suggests that knowledge excludes merely lucky guesses. This principle together with the idea that sometimes accidental processes yield justified true beliefs challenges the notion that proper function is necessary for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that a strict causal link from proper functioning to true belief is required for the concept of knowledge, as situations involving epistemic luck suggest alternate sources of justified belief.**
