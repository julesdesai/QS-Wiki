# Can contextualism account for fixed standards of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ec0b57aa-2a4d-4a33-8742-fc1a6b856006
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification Requirement](contextualism-with-justification-requirement-synthesis-53681182-6c43-4114-bdf6-ad3c1e338d57.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a consistent standard of evaluation across different contexts. This principle together with varying standards of knowledge across contexts entails an inconsistency in how we evaluate knowledge claims.**, **Suppose there is a case where an individual is deemed to know a proposition in one context but not in another solely due to differing standards. Intuitively, this seems implausibly arbitrary for a concept as stable as knowledge.**
