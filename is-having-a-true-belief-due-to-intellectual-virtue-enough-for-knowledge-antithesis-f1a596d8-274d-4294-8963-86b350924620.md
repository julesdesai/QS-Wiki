# Is having a true belief due to intellectual virtue enough for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1a596d8-274d-4294-8963-86b350924620
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-f60d76d9-bfb9-483c-b262-fa52975172be.md)

## Content

**Consider a student who guesses the answer to an exam question correctly due to a hunch developed from diligent study habits, an intellectual virtue.**, **In this case, although the belief is true and stems from an intellectual virtue, it seems correct to say the student does not truly "know" the answer, indicating more than true belief from virtue is necessary for knowledge.**
