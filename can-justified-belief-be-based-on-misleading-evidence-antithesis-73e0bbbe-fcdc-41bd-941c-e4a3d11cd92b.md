# Can justified belief be based on misleading evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 73e0bbbe-fcdc-41bd-941c-e4a3d11cd92b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person reads a falsified science article claiming that eating carrots significantly improves eyesight. The person subsequently believes this claim based solely on the article. Intuitively, we might consider the belief justified even though it is based on misleading evidence because the person has no reason to doubt the article's credibility., **There is a plausible principle suggesting that justified beliefs should be based on true or reliable evidence, which conflicts with posit that mere alignment with available evidence suffices.**
