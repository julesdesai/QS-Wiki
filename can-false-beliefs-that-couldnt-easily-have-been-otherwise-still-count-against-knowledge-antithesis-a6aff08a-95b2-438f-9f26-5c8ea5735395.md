# Can false beliefs that couldn't easily have been otherwise still count against knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6aff08a-95b2-438f-9f26-5c8ea5735395
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-92f8baec-50d4-4810-a26f-7675d59c578a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a true and safe belief about a proposition, but they also hold many false beliefs about related propositions that couldn't easily have been false. Intuitively, these false beliefs affect our judgment of their knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that systemic false beliefs can undermine the claim that a safe true belief constitutes knowledge.**
