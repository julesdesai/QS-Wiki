**Parent:** [Does the reliabilist account overlook the social nature of knowledge?](does-the-reliabilist-account-overlook-the-social-nature-of-knowledge-antithesis-21e2916f-fbe3-4f58-84fa-25866c6d6f91.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief results from a reliable social-cognitive process that collectively yields true beliefs more often than not**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief results from a reliable social-cognitive process**
