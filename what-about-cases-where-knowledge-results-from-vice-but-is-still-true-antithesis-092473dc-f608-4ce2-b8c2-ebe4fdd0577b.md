# What about cases where knowledge results from vice but is still true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 092473dc-f608-4ce2-b8c2-ebe4fdd0577b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person forms a true belief through epistemic vice, such as arrogance or closed-mindedness. Intuitively, we would find it problematic that such a belief might be considered knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that beliefs formed through significant epistemic vices should not be classified as knowledge, regardless of their truth.**, **The scenario in which epistemic vices lead to true belief challenges the notion that only intellectual virtues can produce knowledge.**
