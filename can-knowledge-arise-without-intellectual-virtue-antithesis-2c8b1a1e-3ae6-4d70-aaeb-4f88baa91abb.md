# Can knowledge arise without intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2c8b1a1e-3ae6-4d70-aaeb-4f88baa91abb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d110ebcb-cebc-4b41-b013-8e3cca593b6d.md)

## Content

**A belief can be true and justified through luck rather than through the exercise of intellectual virtues, such as in the case of someone randomly guessing the correct answer on a test**, **A person may arrive at a true belief by accident, such as blindly guessing the outcome of a coin flip, which challenges the claim that knowledge must be creditable to a knower's cognitive abilities**, **In the case of a skilled archer who misses the target but the arrow bounces back and hits, the result can be correct without expressing cognitive skills, suggesting true belief does not always stem from cognitive competence**
