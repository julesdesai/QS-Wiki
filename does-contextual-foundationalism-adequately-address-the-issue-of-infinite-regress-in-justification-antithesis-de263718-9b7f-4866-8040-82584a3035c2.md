# Does contextual foundationalism adequately address the issue of infinite regress in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** de263718-9b7f-4866-8040-82584a3035c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief is said to be justified by contextually reliable foundational beliefs, but those foundational beliefs themselves require further justification. Intuitively, this would suggest a need for further foundational justification.**, **A plausible principle is that foundational beliefs should not themselves require justification to avoid an infinite regress. This principle entails that contextual foundationalism might fail if foundational beliefs end up requiring further justification.**
