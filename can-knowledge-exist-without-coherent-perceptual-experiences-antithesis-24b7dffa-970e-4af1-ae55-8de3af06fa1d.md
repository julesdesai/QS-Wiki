# Can knowledge exist without coherent perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24b7dffa-970e-4af1-ae55-8de3af06fa1d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Coherence Theory](phenomenal-coherence-theory-synthesis-37d80231-0661-49bf-9bec-8b3f779084e8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person accurately predicts a future event solely based on a random guess, resulting in knowledge without coherent perceptual experiences. Intuitively, we would judge that the person possesses knowledge, even though it lacks a coherent system of perceptual experiences.**, **It is implausible that all instances of knowledge must be based on coherent systems of perceptual experiences, as demonstrated by situations where accurate knowledge arises from mere coincidence or luck.**
