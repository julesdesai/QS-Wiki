# Do Gettier cases show that virtue epistemology fails to define knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 18e46c18-9689-4916-81a6-dfc9f11dcc80
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-6b17452f-daf9-4e36-a5b5-c7d2a1d34c57.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person P has a justified true belief because they inferred it from a false proposition, but their conclusion happens to be true due to unrelated facts. Intuitively, we would judge that P does not have knowledge.**, **The idea that knowledge is justified true belief arising from intellectual virtue is challenged by scenarios where the belief's truth is coincidental, revealing a gap between intellectual virtue and knowledge.**
