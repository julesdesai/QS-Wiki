# Does inaccessible justification invalidate knowing a proposition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ad05a578-b7eb-4b4f-a907-1754086f99f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition true without being able to explain why. Intuitively, it seems they lack knowledge because they cannot provide reasons for their belief**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires some level of accessibility to the justification behind one's belief**, **If justification is entirely inaccessible, then it is implausible that the person can genuinely claim to know the proposition, since knowing typically includes being able to explain or defend one's belief**
