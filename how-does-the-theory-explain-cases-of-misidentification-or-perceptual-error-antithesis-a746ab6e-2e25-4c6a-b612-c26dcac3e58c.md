# How does the theory explain cases of misidentification or perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a746ab6e-2e25-4c6a-b612-c26dcac3e58c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naïve Realism Summary](naïve-realism-summary-synthesis-2d3f5cf7-4de1-4047-bfb0-105e4b8ca669.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where someone mistakes a cardboard cutout of a dog for a real dog. Intuitively, we either have to say that the perception is directly of a real dog or a cardboard cutout. If we say the perception is of a real dog, then it is wrong. If we say the perception is of a cardboard cutout, then the person is mistaken about their perception.**, **A plausible principle is that accurate perception requires recognizing objects for what they are. This principle, together with misidentification, entails that perception involves more than direct apprehension alone.**
