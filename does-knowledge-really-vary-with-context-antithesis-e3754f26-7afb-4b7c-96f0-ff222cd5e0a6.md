# Does knowledge really vary with context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3754f26-7afb-4b7c-96f0-ff222cd5e0a6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Safety Condition](contextualism-with-safety-condition-synthesis-de372d20-3394-4687-9741-2b1cb43580bc.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should remain consistent across different contexts. This principle suggests that varying standards for knowledge in different situations create instability in what we consider to be knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case where a person is considered to know a proposition in a low-stakes context but not in a high-stakes context. Intuitively, it seems problematic if the truth of knowledge can change simply due to alterations in the stakes.**
