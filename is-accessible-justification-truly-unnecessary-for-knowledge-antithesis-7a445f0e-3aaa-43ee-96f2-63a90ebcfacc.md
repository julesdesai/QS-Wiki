# Is accessible justification truly unnecessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a445f0e-3aaa-43ee-96f2-63a90ebcfacc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Externalism Summary](disjunctivist-externalism-summary-synthesis-838ba4d9-493f-4109-9c69-34614a236108.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification that the knower can access. This principle together with the view that knowledge does not require accessible justification seems inconsistent because they contradict each other.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person believes a true proposition but cannot provide any justification or explanation when asked about their belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know the proposition because they lack accessible justification.**, **If knowledge does not require accessible justification as the view states, then a person with no understanding or justification could still know, which is implausible.**
