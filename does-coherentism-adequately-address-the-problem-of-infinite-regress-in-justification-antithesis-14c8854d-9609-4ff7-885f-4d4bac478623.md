# Does coherentism adequately address the problem of infinite regress in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 14c8854d-9609-4ff7-885f-4d4bac478623
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism View](coherentism-view-synthesis-4dcf0483-85cc-4ed6-8a5e-86aaec2b7626.md)

## Content

**If a belief is justified by coherence within a web of beliefs, consider that each belief may require further coherence checks leading to an infinite regress. Intuitively, we would judge such an infinite chain to be problematic for the notion of definitive justification.**, **A plausible principle suggests that justification requires a firm foundation or stopping point, which may not be afforded by coherence alone.**
