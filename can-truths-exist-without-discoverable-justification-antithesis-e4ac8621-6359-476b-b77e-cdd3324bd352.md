# Can truths exist without discoverable justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4ac8621-6359-476b-b77e-cdd3324bd352
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Recursive Infinitism Summary](recursive-infinitism-summary-synthesis-e60eeb81-9314-4a67-a7c8-de308e762c47.md)

## Content

**There are propositions that could be true independently of whether they can be known or justified by human beings.**, **If a proposition is true but there exists no finite or infinite means for humans to justify this proposition, knowledge of it seems inaccessible.**, **If knowledge requires justification as a component, undiscoverably true propositions would remain unknowable, posing a problem for the universal applicability of the theoretical view.**
