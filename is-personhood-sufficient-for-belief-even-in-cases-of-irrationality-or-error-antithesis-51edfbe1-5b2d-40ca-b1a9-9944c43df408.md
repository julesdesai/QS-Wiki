# Is personhood sufficient for belief, even in cases of irrationality or error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 51edfbe1-5b2d-40ca-b1a9-9944c43df408
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Reliable Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-with-reliable-basic-beliefs-synthesis-62bc80a9-ef75-4d49-9578-a29890370bd2.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if they believe it.**, **It is implausible that simply being a person entails reliable belief formation, especially in cases of cognitive impairments or biases.**, **If a person can irrationally hold a belief, and this suffices for knowledge, then instances of irrational beliefs would incorrectly qualify as knowledge.**
