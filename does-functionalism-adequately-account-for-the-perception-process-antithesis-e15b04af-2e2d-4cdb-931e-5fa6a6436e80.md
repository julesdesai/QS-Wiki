# Does functionalism adequately account for the perception process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e15b04af-2e2d-4cdb-931e-5fa6a6436e80
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Functionalism](functionalism-synthesis-2d0849d8-6d8d-49c5-822f-95e70d5e82e6.md)

## Content

**Suppose perception involves direct acquaintance with reality, providing a basis for knowledge that is beyond mere functional processing. Intuitively, if perception is only a functional process, it excludes this direct acquaintance as a component of knowledge, conflicting with how we understand perceptual knowledge.**
