# Is the requirement of justified true belief sufficient to account for knowledge in all contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30e1a1bb-b627-4f5b-85e0-76a4355dbb91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person holds a justified true belief due to reliable testimony, but does not understand the underlying facts.**, **Intuitively, lacking comprehension of the facts implies lacking knowledge, even if one holds a justified true belief.**, **If understanding is absent, justified true belief alone seems insufficient for knowledge.**
