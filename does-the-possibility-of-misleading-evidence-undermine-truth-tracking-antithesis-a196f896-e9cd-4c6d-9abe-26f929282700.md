# Does the possibility of misleading evidence undermine truth-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a196f896-e9cd-4c6d-9abe-26f929282700
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Imaginatively, consider a case where a person believes a proposition based on misleading but ultimately incorrect evidence, and the person never wavers in their belief even in possible worlds where the proposition is false. This would intuitively mean that the person lacks knowledge because their belief, though true in the actual world, does not track truth in light of available evidence.**, **It is implausible for knowledge to disregard misleading evidence that affects belief formation and persistence, suggesting truth-tracking theory insufficiently addresses the role of evidence.**
