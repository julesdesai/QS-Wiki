# Can context-independent knowledge exist in highly contextual environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 34a25840-b2ce-4749-929f-8b2b7b02d7f7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism: Contexts Influence Justification, Not Knowledge](moderate-contextualism-contexts-influence-justification-not-knowledge-synthesis-be3d790f-5519-485d-8f0d-32864e4a3e9e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a knowledge claim that arises from novel or highly specific contexts, such as the use of scientific equipment or language-specific nuances; intuitively, we find that context significantly influences knowledge validity.**, **A plausible principle holds that context deeply influences what is recognizable as knowledge from a given epistemic standpoint.**, **Positing that knowledge standards are unaffected by context undermines the role of context in defining what is known.**
