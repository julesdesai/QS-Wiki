# Can perception mislead us about causal structures?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f7eac9f-abd6-4d00-bf2a-15070483acce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-aa743ec0-e3e4-4aae-9408-fa20370276b8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an object appears blue in normal lighting conditions but red under a specific light filter. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that the perception under the light filter does not accurately reflect the causal structure of the object.**, **A perception that misrepresents an object's color under certain conditions suggests that perception does not always lead to accurate knowledge about causal structures.**
