# Does reliability adequately address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bd03c9e8-47ef-49e8-a95a-f246c1937186
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b0906775-f1f5-4edb-9c31-9ab274e2992b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person arrives at a true belief through a reliable process, yet has additional justified but false beliefs affecting the reasoning. Intuitively, we would judge that this is not genuine knowledge.**, **Consider the case where a person forms a belief that a clock shows the correct time because it is working, though unknown to them, the clock had stopped just before their glance, leading them to the right time by chance. It is implausible that this person has knowledge.**
