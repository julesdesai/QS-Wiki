# Does virtue epistemology adequately handle cases of epistemic injustice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eadfc968-df72-4631-9d85-3201c904b0fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**A person's exercise of intellectual virtues can be unfairly discredited due to biases, affecting whether their belief is considered knowledge.**, **In a biased epistemic community, virtuous contributions might be overlooked or undervalued, leading to misunderstandings about what counts as knowledge.**, **Knowledge depends not only on the presence of intellectual virtues but also on fair acknowledgment and assessment of those virtues.**
