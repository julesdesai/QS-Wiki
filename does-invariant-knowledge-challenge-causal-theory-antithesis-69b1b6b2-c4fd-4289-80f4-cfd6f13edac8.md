# Does invariant knowledge challenge causal theory?

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**It is plausible to consider that some knowledge remains invariant across different possible worlds, where causal chains may differ. Such invariants suggest a form of knowledge independent of specific causal facts.**, **Consider mathematical truths like geometric axioms. These remain true across possible worlds, and knowledge of them does not rely on causal versions of reality.**
