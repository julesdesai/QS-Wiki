# How does invariantism explain disagreement about knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 80b39695-d0c1-455b-9125-4303492b109d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where two individuals with similar evidence reach different conclusions about what they know. Intuitively, their disagreement suggests varying standards for knowledge.**, **The view's claim that knowledge does not depend on the context of the knower implies both should reach the same conclusion.**, **It is implausible that different individuals with the same evidence would disagree if standards were truly invariant.**
