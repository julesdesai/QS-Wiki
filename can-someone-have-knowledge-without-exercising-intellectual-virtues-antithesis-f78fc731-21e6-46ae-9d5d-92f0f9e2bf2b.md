# Can someone have knowledge without exercising intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f78fc731-21e6-46ae-9d5d-92f0f9e2bf2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue epistemology focusing on intellectual virtues](virtue-epistemology-focusing-on-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-f3a48039-3cf5-452a-9c79-9ac1b05ad9d2.md)

## Content

**A person can come to know a true proposition by luck without exercising any intellectual virtue, such as getting the right answer to a difficult question purely by guessing.**, **In such a case where knowledge is acquired by luck, it does not seem that intellectual virtues played any role, challenging the necessity of intellectual virtues for knowledge.**
