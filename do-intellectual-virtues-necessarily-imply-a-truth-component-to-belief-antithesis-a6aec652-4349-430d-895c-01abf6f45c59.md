# Do intellectual virtues necessarily imply a truth component to belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6aec652-4349-430d-895c-01abf6f45c59
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-0020c014-7b91-4782-b27a-7c265359a5c7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has intellectual virtues leading them to hold a belief regarded as false.**, **Cognitive character traits traditionally linked to epistemic virtue should lead to true, not false, beliefs.**, **If intellectual virtues consistently result in false beliefs, their adequacy for ensuring knowledge correctness is questioned.**, **Truth is an essential feature of knowledge; virtuous conduct without truth fails to satisfy knowledge criteria.**
