# Can knowledge exist without the belief tracking the truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 36ca41b7-c81f-4c9c-b5d0-a5f4010e2beb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d302593-f2de-4e10-8195-85a314e81400.md)

## Content

**Some beliefs can be true accidentally rather than because they accurately track the truth across situations.**, **Consider a scenario where someone believes something true based on a reliable method but would still hold the same belief if the method were unreliable.**, **This suggests that tracking the truth is neither necessary nor sufficient for knowledge, as accidental truths may not constitute knowledge.**
