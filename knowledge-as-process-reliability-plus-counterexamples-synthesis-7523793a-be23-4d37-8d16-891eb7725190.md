# Knowledge as Process Reliability Plus Counterexamples

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Could an unreliable process occasionally yield knowledge?](could-an-unreliable-process-occasionally-yield-knowledge-antithesis-720c261a-b210-4ed9-8796-9ac7a9329594.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is the result of a reliable cognitive process**, **A cognitive process is reliable if it yields a high ratio of true to false beliefs in the long run**, **Occasional deviations from reliable processes can yield knowledge under abnormal conditions**
