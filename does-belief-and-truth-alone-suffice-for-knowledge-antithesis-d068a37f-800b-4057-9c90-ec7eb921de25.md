# Does belief and truth alone suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d068a37f-800b-4057-9c90-ec7eb921de25
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-4b9cf48f-532c-40a3-a960-718d00ef564e.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone sincerely believes a proposition and the proposition is true purely by chance. Intuitively, we would not say that this person knows the proposition despite belief and truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that true belief alone is insufficient for knowledge without appropriate justificatory connection.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes something based solely on a guess, and it turns out true. Intuitively, we would judge this does not count as knowledge, highlighting belief and truth are not sufficient.**
