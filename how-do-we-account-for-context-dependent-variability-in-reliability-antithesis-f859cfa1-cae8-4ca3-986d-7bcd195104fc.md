# How do we account for context-dependent variability in reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f859cfa1-cae8-4ca3-986d-7bcd195104fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-9b4935ff-e1a0-4d23-aeca-7dfa716ad7fb.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's belief is formed through news found online, which is generally reliable, yet the reliability varies greatly depending on the source. Intuitively, beliefs from less reliable sources do not constitute knowledge even if generally true.**, **The variability in reliability of online information sources indicates that general reliability isn't a universal metric for all beliefs, challenging the consistency of reliability as a criterion.**
