# Does the externalist theory adequately address Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 358e5b7b-2a6d-41a1-99df-38e433d2243b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a Gettier case where a person has a belief that is true and formed in a seemingly reliable way because of deception but becomes true by accident. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that simply having a true belief, even if formed in a generally reliable way, is insufficient to avoid Gettier problems.**
