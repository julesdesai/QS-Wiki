# How do shifting contexts undermine the consistency of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76247068-4335-48d8-9f7f-6932852e7736
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Variable Justification](contextualism-with-variable-justification-synthesis-53c5a1dc-1391-4651-950b-f21901e771b6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person claims to know a fact in one context but not in another context with stricter standards. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge should not change merely due to shifting standards.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be a stable property of a belief. This principle together with the varying context standards in contextualism entails that knowledge cannot be stable across different contexts.**
