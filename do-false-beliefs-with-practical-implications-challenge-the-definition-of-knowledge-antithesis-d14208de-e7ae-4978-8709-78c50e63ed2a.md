# Do false beliefs with practical implications challenge the definition of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d14208de-e7ae-4978-8709-78c50e63ed2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatically Useful True Belief](pragmatically-useful-true-belief-synthesis-e72da4db-a488-4cc6-b424-04d735c0cd87.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes vaccines cause autism, this belief is false but led to altering public health policy by reducing vaccination rates.**, **This belief may have practical implications, yet intuitively it does not constitute knowledge due to its falsehood.**, **It is plausible that knowledge cannot encompass false beliefs, regardless of practical outcomes.**
