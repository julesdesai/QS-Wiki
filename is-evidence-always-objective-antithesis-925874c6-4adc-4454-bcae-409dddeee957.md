# Is evidence always objective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 925874c6-4adc-4454-bcae-409dddeee957
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-10c49977-d180-4d95-bcbb-8124324d7ccb.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where different individuals interpret the same set of evidence in opposing ways due to their subjective biases or perspectives. Intuitively, each individual believes they have the justified true belief based on the evidence they have. If evidence is subjective, it raises doubts regarding whether it can universally justify true beliefs.**, **It is implausible that subjective interpretations of evidence alone can always form a justified true belief. Evidence should be objective to serve as a foundation for knowledge.**
