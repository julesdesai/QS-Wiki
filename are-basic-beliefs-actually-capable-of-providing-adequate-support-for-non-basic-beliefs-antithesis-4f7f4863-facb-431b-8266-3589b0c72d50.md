# Are basic beliefs actually capable of providing adequate support for non-basic beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f7f4863-facb-431b-8266-3589b0c72d50
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism Summary](foundationalism-summary-synthesis-254febae-17e3-437c-93f6-f73426d75660.md)

## Content

**Consider a belief based on a supposed basic belief which presumes certain sensory experiences but later is found to be inconsistent with other sensory data. It is implausible that knowledge built on such shaky foundations is truly justified.**, **The inability of basic beliefs to stand scrutiny suggests inadequacies in their role in supporting justified non-basic beliefs.**
