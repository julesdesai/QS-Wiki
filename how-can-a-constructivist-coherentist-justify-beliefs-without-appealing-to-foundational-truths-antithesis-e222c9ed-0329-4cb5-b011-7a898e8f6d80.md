# How can a constructivist coherentist justify beliefs without appealing to foundational truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e222c9ed-0329-4cb5-b011-7a898e8f6d80
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivist Coherentism](constructivist-coherentism-synthesis-df66a247-3331-4fc3-9810-0103a6210363.md)

## Content

**A belief system that relies solely on coherence lacks foundational axioms, and thus, any belief may be consistently denied or affirmed, making grounding stability of knowledge problematic.**, **Suppose we have a case in which traditional foundational truths are dismissed, leaving all beliefs only linked by coherence. Intuitively, one might object to this system as unstable because foundational truths often serve to anchor belief systems in ways coherence alone does not.**
