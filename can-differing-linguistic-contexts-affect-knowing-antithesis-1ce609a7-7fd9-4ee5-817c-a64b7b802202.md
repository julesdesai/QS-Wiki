# Can differing linguistic contexts affect knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ce609a7-7fd9-4ee5-817c-a64b7b802202
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-3cc4c7ad-204f-4c1b-ac17-f362044e72b5.md)

## Content

**Suppose two individuals in different language communities use words that express the same belief. Due to subtle linguistic differences, the standards for justification might shift, influencing whether we regard these as cases of knowledge. Intuitively, language influences how justification and truth are perceived.**, **It is plausible that language nuances might affect epistemic standards, suggesting variability in knowledge based on linguistic context.**
