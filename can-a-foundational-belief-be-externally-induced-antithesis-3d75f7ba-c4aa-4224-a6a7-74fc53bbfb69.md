# Can a foundational belief be externally induced?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d75f7ba-c4aa-4224-a6a7-74fc53bbfb69
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person’s belief that is self-evident to them is actually the result of external brain stimulation or implant. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the belief lacks epistemic justification despite its self-evidence to the person.**, **There is a plausible principle that externally induced beliefs, even if self-evident to the subject, cannot be justified as basic beliefs.**
