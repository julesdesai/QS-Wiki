# Can knowledge be context-independent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42130118-e787-4d6b-b53f-ae5b24fe6f87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativistic Contextualism with Stable Core](relativistic-contextualism-with-stable-core-synthesis-3b7ed9ae-6709-4913-87ed-5986155c06e2.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is commonly thought of as entirely context-independent, making it implausible that any component of knowledge can depend on the context of the knower.**, **If the standards for core propositions are constant, then they should not be subject to contextual variation, contrary to claims of context dependence.**
