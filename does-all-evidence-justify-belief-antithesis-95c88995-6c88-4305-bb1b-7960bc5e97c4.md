# Does all evidence justify belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95c88995-6c88-4305-bb1b-7960bc5e97c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-bfc3ee3a-740c-4ee5-bb7b-12c5efc1c03c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a large amount of irrelevant evidence related to their belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the sheer amount of evidence alone does not justify the belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that not all evidence is relevant to the truth of a belief. This principle together with the notion that evidence must support the truth of a belief entails that irrelevant evidence does not justify a belief.**
