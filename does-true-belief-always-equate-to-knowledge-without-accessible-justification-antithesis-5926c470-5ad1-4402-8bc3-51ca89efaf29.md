# Does true belief always equate to knowledge without accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5926c470-5ad1-4402-8bc3-51ca89efaf29
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Externalism Summary](reliabilist-externalism-summary-synthesis-3207924c-3d5f-4b8a-8088-8161a66f3954.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief about a mathematical theorem based solely on a reliable calculator, yet cannot access the justification of the theorem's proof; intuitively, they know the result but lack full understanding.**, **Accessible justification seems necessary for full epistemic access to knowledge rather than true belief alone.**
