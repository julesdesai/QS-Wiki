# Do deceptive experiences have knowledge potential?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20782ff7-6516-4e66-aaba-a7c70951b253
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-e9744a1e-a377-4989-9c30-b08459f11a98.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person has a deceptive experience but, due to external information or cross-checking, accurately knows a proposition about reality. Intuitively, we might judge that they have knowledge despite the deceptive nature of the initial experience.**, **The potential for knowledge exists even with deceptive experiences if supplemented by further information, which questions the requirement for knowledge being tied to only veridical perceptions.**
