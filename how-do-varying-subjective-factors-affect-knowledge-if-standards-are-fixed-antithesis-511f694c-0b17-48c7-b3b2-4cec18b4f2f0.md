# How do varying subjective factors affect knowledge if standards are fixed?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 511f694c-0b17-48c7-b3b2-4cec18b4f2f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-fa9170ce-e58d-4311-9c0f-f4410b4d69a4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which Alice is nervous about an upcoming test and believes she has prepared thoroughly. Intuitively, her nervousness seems to affect whether we consider her belief to be knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that the same standards should lead to the same conclusion, regardless of subjective factors. This principle together with the idea that subjective factors can alter epistemic standards entails a contradiction.**
