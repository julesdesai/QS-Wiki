# Does the influence of context on perception lead to a regress problem for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 98278399-3211-4e8c-b16c-0dd436c40058
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Contextualism Summary](perceptual-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9fb6be1-a4e1-46bf-91f2-7fc63c3abb0c.md)

## Content

**The context itself needs to be perceived, which requires another context to interpret it, leading to a potential infinite regress.**, **If knowing requires perceiving context, and context itself needs perception, this could lead to a never-ending series of contexts that need to be understood.**, **A regress problem suggests that context-dependent perception might not be a viable foundation for knowledge.**
