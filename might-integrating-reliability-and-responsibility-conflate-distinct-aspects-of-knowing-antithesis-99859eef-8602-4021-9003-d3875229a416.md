# Might integrating reliability and responsibility conflate distinct aspects of knowing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99859eef-8602-4021-9003-d3875229a416
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Mixed Virtue Epistemology](mixed-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-9b1d3c06-dd01-4be5-8e49-871c85befc1a.md)

## Content

**There's a plausible principle that reliability concerns the accuracy of processes and responsibility concerns the ethical engagement of agents. Integrating them may conflate these distinct dimensions.**, **An example can be seen where someone's reliable yet thoughtless calculation still leads to true belief. The separation of these elements suggests intellectual virtue may encompass more distinct aspects.**
