# Does the presence of intellectual virtue always necessarily lead to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 08b4002f-37fc-4b5c-9ae4-bf3dbcdfaea4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person uses intellectual virtue to form a belief but some aspect of the situation still results in a false belief; it seems that virtue does not guarantee truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtue may increase likelihoods of truth but does not necessitate truth in all scenarios.**
