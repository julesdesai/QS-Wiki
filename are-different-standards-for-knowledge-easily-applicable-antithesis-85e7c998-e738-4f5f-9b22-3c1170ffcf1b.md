# Are different standards for knowledge easily applicable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85e7c998-e738-4f5f-9b22-3c1170ffcf1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-70123d28-f52a-42a2-8d85-6f92ac2f5bd1.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that laypeople consistently adjust their standards of knowledge intuitively across different contexts without explicit awareness of doing so.**, **Imagine a scenario where someone claims to know a trivial fact while chatting with friends and then doubts it in a formal debate setting. Intuitively, the shift should not affect their actual knowledge of the fact.**
