# Does contextualism obscure the distinction between belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1e38c416-d51e-4863-be4d-9f4bf79c0a41
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on contextually feasible criteria but doesn't sustain this belief in another context, which does not challenge their belief. Intuitively, unclear criteria between contexts could blur the line between belief and true knowledge.**, **It is implausible that the distinction between belief and knowledge can remain clear if what counts as knowledge is overly contingent upon contextual criteria.**
