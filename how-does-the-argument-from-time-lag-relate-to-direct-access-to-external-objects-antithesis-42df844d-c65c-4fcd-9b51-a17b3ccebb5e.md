# How does the argument from time-lag relate to direct access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42df844d-c65c-4fcd-9b51-a17b3ccebb5e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perception involves an immediate experience of external objects. However, due to the finite speed of light and sound, there is a time lag in perception, which means we are perceiving past states of objects.**, **This time-lag problem implies that perception may not be direct access to the objects as they currently exist.**
