# Is knowledge genuinely sensitive to the stakes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ce118f3-f675-492b-9d02-fd1ff70e4ec4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-a38d2af6-3ec4-421c-a558-c594d0cbc8b7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition to be true, the proposition is indeed true, but the stakes for the person are minimal. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would still judge that the person knows the proposition.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge does not fluctuate solely based on practical stakes. This principle suggests that if a person truly knows something when stakes are low, raising the stakes does not diminish that knowledge.**
