# Are there too many potential contexts complicating the application of contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d800aed-0678-41ac-b5a3-924a2a6f0ae4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c8c66eb7-99be-427a-b717-b1ecb74e1290.md)

## Content

**In principle, every conversational exchange or slight change in emphasis could create a new context with different standards for knowledge.**, **An excessive number of contexts leads to infinite variability, making it practically impossible to track which standards are applicable at any moment.**, **The overabundance of potential contexts diminishes the predictability and usability of knowledge claims.**
