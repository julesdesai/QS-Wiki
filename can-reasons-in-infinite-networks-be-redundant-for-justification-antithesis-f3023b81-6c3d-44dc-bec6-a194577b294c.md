# Can reasons in infinite networks be redundant for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f3023b81-6c3d-44dc-bec6-a194577b294c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherent Infinitism Summary](coherent-infinitism-summary-synthesis-eca6bdeb-868b-4e1e-9a63-23b9880f21ff.md)

## Content

**An infinite network could include numerous redundant reasons that do not contribute new or independent justification for the belief.**, **Justification might be more effective with a finite set of strong, independent reasons rather than an infinite set with redundancy.**, **Redundancies in justification networks suggest they are inefficient and potentially unnecessary for robust justification.**
