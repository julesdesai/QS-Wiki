# Does cognitive success always result from intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2ddfda5-5727-41de-b108-b19111283ab4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a person consistently arrives at true beliefs purely by luck or accident, rather than through intellectual effort or skill. Intuitively, we might deny that this is genuine knowledge**, **It is implausible that cognitive success consistently due to luck can fulfil criteria for intellectual virtue as necessary for knowledge**, **Intellectual virtue implies a systematic and reliable ability or skill in forming true beliefs, not just a coincidental or haphazard success**
