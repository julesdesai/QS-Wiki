# Does knowledge depend on reliable belief formation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bf1e6bc6-4c05-4e5d-b3c0-82d6cf8d5e3d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**A scenario in which a person uses a faulty process to arrive at a belief that happens to be true by chance raises questions about the reliability of belief formation. Intuitively, we would not consider this to be knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that emphasizes the reliability of belief-forming processes for knowledge. This principle, along with causally connected belief requirements, implies unreliability challenges the status of knowledge.**, **It is implausible that unreliable processes can lead to genuine knowledge, given the necessity of causally truthful belief.**
