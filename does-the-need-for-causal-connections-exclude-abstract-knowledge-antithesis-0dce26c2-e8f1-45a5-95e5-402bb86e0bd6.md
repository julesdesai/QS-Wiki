# Does the need for causal connections exclude abstract knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0dce26c2-e8f1-45a5-95e5-402bb86e0bd6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c3b94507-0858-4bfa-a251-208b7829ae1f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge of abstract concepts, like mathematics or ethics, cannot have causal connections in the same way empirical facts do.**, **Without a causal connection similar to empirical knowledge, it is implausible under the causal theory to say one knows abstract concepts in the same way.**
