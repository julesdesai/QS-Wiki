# Are prior probabilities always justified?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99fa8576-8857-45cf-a007-3443715f7795
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Bayesian Epistemology](bayesian-epistemology-synthesis-4576807d-29a3-407d-a965-1c884989f7fe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person relies on a particular prior probability without sufficient justification for why that prior should be accepted. Intuitively, we either have to say that prior probabilities need additional justification or accept unjustified prior beliefs. If we say prior probabilities need justification, then Bayesian updates alone do not lead to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that prior probabilities require independent grounding to truly contribute to knowledge.**
