# Can beliefs formed indirectly through intellectual virtue but unsafe be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1f3dfab-6dc2-4845-8c48-dd4b1fa8f3bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Consider a scientist who develops a theory based on indirect evidence and prior knowledge (an exercise of intellectual virtue). In nearby possible worlds, the same evidence could have led to different conclusions. Intuitively, the scientist possesses knowledge despite these possibilities.**, **Intellectual virtue suggests that knowledge can originate from indirect yet virtuous processes, even if the resulting belief is unsafe in other potential scenarios.**
