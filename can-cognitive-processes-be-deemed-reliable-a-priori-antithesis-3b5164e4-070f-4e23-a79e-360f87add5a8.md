# Can cognitive processes be deemed reliable a priori?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3b5164e4-070f-4e23-a79e-360f87add5a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with a finite chain of reasons](reliabilism-with-a-finite-chain-of-reasons-synthesis-a3f7a107-c974-4a8e-a7f2-d20f4743454a.md)

## Content

**It is implausible to a priori determine the reliability of a cognitive process without empirical investigation.**, **Reliabilism suggests that knowledge can be acquired through reliability, bypassing the need for external validation.**, **Without external validation, the designation of cognitive processes as reliable might be questionably circular or baseless.**
