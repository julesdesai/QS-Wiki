# Is context playing too large a role in justification assignments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9e248d2b-419e-40c6-b1f0-9820b2f186cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism With Context-Dependent Justification Standards](contextualism-with-context-dependent-justification-standards-synthesis-a8126985-f485-4c51-b545-16d8a583ea1e.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a change in the context itself, rather than a change in a person's beliefs or evidence, causes a shift in whether they know a proposition. Intuitively, we might question the appropriateness of allowing context to play such a significant role in defining knowledge.**, **It is implausible that context, an external factor to the person's internal cognitive state, should be the primary determinant of knowledge acquisition or loss.**
