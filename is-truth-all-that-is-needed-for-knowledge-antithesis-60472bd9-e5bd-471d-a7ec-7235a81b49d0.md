# Is truth all that is needed for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60472bd9-e5bd-471d-a7ec-7235a81b49d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person asserts a true proposition accidentally, without any understanding or evidence. Intuitively, we do not consider saying the person knows the proposition.**, **A plausible principle in epistemology holds that knowledge requires not only truth but also understanding or justified belief, making merely true assertions insufficient for knowing.**, **It is implausible to consider that mere accidental truth can elevate a belief to the status of knowledge without further justification.**
