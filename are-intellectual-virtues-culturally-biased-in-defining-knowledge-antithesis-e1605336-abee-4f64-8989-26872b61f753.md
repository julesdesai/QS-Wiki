# Are intellectual virtues culturally biased in defining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1605336-abee-4f64-8989-26872b61f753
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that standards for intellectual virtue can vary widely across cultures, making the criterion relative rather than universal. This principle challenges the objectivity of using virtue as a condition for knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where one culture values different intellectual traits than another, yet both have individuals who claim knowledge. Intuitively, both sets of claims seem valid, questioning uniform virtue standards in knowledge definitions.**
