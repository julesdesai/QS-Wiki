# Does the tracking theory account for changes in belief over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dabbe19d-7b63-4dba-81f9-a720bcbc6563
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-dda35d00-5b10-4342-b443-f197350806e5.md)

## Content

**Consider an example where a person forms a belief based on reliable evidence, but over time the evidence becomes outdated and unreliable. Intuitively, we would judge that the person's belief, even if retained, no longer counts as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires that the justification for a belief remains stable over time. This principle together with a change in the reliability of evidence leads to the conclusion that retained belief may cease to be knowledge.**
