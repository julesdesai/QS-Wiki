# Does causal theory account for perceptual errors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3a8e0b3-53a6-4eaf-a71e-7812e6b670da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where multiple people see a stick submerged in water and report it as bent, though it is straight. Intuitively, these mistaken beliefs are not knowledge, though caused by perceiving a reliable causal effect of light refraction.**, **A principle suggests that if a belief is caused in the right way by perception, it should count as knowledge. This principle is challenged in cases where perceptual conditions reliably produce error.**
