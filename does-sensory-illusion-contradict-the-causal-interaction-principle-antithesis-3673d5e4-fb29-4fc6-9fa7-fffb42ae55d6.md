# Does sensory illusion contradict the causal interaction principle?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3673d5e4-fb29-4fc6-9fa7-fffb42ae55d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-aa743ec0-e3e4-4aae-9408-fa20370276b8.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone is looking at a bent straw in a glass of water, which appears broken due to the refractive properties of water. The intuitive judgment might be that the perception is incorrect despite being caused by the straw's presence.**, **Sensory illusions suggest perception can be misleading even when there is a causal interaction with external objects.**
