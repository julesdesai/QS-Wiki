# Does the absence of justification challenge the concept of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd966739-eefc-45b5-b1e3-d99111a2e1ec
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Approach](virtue-epistemology-approach-synthesis-b14f243d-0108-46b0-abbb-b26bd31de791.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a true belief through intellectual virtue but lacks access to any justification for that belief. Intuitively, it seems doubtful that the person has knowledge without some accessible justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves not only true belief but also involves justification. This principle suggests that justification is essential for knowledge.**
