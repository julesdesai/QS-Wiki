# Does usefulness of a belief ensure its correspondence to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23c3be16-a978-4b31-8a15-3917a3df8f49
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person wrongly believes a placebo will heal them, perceiving it as useful and actionable. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not know that the placebo has healing properties.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief can be useful without being true. This principle suggests that actionable perceptions do not necessarily correspond to reality in a functional way.**
