# Is it possible for cognitive faculties to be unreliable yet yield knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72657db6-e81a-4db8-909a-849fd43e5c82
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Reliability](virtue-epistemology-with-reliability-synthesis-6944a8d9-8cde-45e2-a167-ffb34caa5f33.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has unreliable cognitive faculties due to cognitive degeneration, but they still arrive at true beliefs through chance. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might judge that the person has knowledge, challenging the necessity of reliable cognitive faculties.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can sometimes arise even from unreliable cognitive faculties when true belief is coincidentally attained.**
