# Is avoiding infinite regress purely by reliability considerations plausible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1203a5c-9146-4d04-b032-12c4087a4b68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**A case in which cognitive processes are deemed reliable but lack independent justification illustrates a disconnect between reliability and regress cessation. Intuitively, it seems that independent justification is required to end a regress, where reliability alone appears inadequate.**, **There is a plausible principle that terminating a regress requires foundational justification, not merely deeming cognitive processes reliable. This principle together with the inadequacy of reliability alone entails that infinite regress is not necessarily avoided by reliability.**
