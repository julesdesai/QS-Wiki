# Does necessary absence of justification eliminate the qualitative difference between knowledge and belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 313b0e8e-b750-4b56-b6cb-f417be017d84
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scientist forms a correct hypothesis based upon a flawed search algorithm that coincidentally identifies the right correlations. The hypothesis, although true, emerges from unsubstantiated justification. Intuitively, this doesn't represent scientific knowledge.**, **The lack of accessible justification when forming true beliefs can erode the distinction between mere true belief and recognized knowledge.**
