# Does knowledge always depend on the context of the knower?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 08a80853-ed80-425c-9a34-9a9cea6cbf12
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism Summary](moderate-contextualism-summary-synthesis-54895291-30c8-42cf-8322-baea34575bd1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a mathematician is solving a complex proof alone in a room and a student is watching a demonstration of this proof in a classroom. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the mathematician knows the proof regardless of the student's context.**, **Suppose we have a case in which two people believe the same true proposition under similar conditions, but one is in a different context requiring higher standards for justification. Intuitively, it seems odd to say one knows while the other does not simply due to context.**
