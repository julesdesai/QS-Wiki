# Do innate conceptual categories fail to account for perceptual learning and development?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee14a6ef-e061-4eaf-8906-24240917af63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**Consider children who learn to recognize novel stimuli over time through repeated exposure and learning. Intuitively, this suggests that not all perceptual categories are innate, since some are developed through experience, which challenges the idea that perceptual experiences are wholly structured by innate categories.**
