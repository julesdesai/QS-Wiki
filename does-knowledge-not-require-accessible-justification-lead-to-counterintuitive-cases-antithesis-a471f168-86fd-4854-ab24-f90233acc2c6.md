# Does knowledge not require accessible justification lead to counterintuitive cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a471f168-86fd-4854-ab24-f90233acc2c6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Externalism Summary](safety-condition-externalism-summary-synthesis-29bd2257-ee31-4256-8506-f8628368ab8d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief about a proposition because of sheer luck. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not have knowledge, even though their belief might be true in nearby possible worlds.**, **The claim that knowledge does not require accessible justification could allow for situations where a person believes something true but cannot provide any reasons or justification for their belief.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge implies an ability to provide reasons or justifications for a belief, which contradicts the idea that justification need not be accessible.**
