# How does Proper Functionalism address the possibility of malfunction that still yields true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c60e5c0-4fb9-4449-8926-36291d383060
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-17c9fccb-5f3e-4011-91c3-5213c66550ac.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person's cognitive faculties malfunction but incidentally produce true beliefs. Intuitively, we either have to say that such beliefs are knowledge or they are not. If we say such beliefs are knowledge, then the proper functioning condition is not necessary. If we say such beliefs are not knowledge, then it seems we discount the truth component's importance.**
