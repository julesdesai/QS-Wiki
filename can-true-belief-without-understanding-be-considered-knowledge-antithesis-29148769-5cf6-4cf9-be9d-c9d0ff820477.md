# Can true belief without understanding be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 29148769-5cf6-4cf9-be9d-c9d0ff820477
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherence Theory of Knowledge](pragmatic-coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a3c73806-e84d-41ba-8091-ad822ea4b799.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a mathematical theorem is true based purely on testimony without understanding the proof.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the theorem or that they do not.**, **If we say they know it, it conflicts with the intuition that understanding plays a crucial part in knowing.**
