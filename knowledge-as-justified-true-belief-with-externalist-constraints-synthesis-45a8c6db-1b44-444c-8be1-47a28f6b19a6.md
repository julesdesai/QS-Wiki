# Knowledge as Justified True Belief with Externalist Constraints

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 45a8c6db-1b44-444c-8be1-47a28f6b19a6
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can intellectual virtues account for knowledge in all domains?](can-intellectual-virtues-account-for-knowledge-in-all-domains-antithesis-d93a0559-ae01-4706-8f58-87951b929bfc.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that external factors ensure the justification connects the belief to the truth**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and external factors ensure the justification connects the belief to the truth**
