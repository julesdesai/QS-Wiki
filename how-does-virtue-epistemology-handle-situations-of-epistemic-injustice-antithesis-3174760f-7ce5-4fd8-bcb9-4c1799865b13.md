# How does virtue epistemology handle situations of epistemic injustice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3174760f-7ce5-4fd8-bcb9-4c1799865b13
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual with intellectual virtue provides a true statement, but due to societal biases, others discredit them; intuitively, their statement lacks recognition as knowledge although it is true.**, **A belief can be formed and sustained through intellectual virtue, yet not recognized or accepted as knowledge in biased contexts, indicating a gap in virtue epistemology's account of knowledge.**, **Knowledge acquisition can be unjustly dismissed based on factors unrelated to an agent's intellectual virtue, questioning the comprehensive nature of crediting knowledge to cognitive traits alone.**
