# Can knowledge exist without justification and accessible reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1fa649b9-34e0-4f4c-ba6d-bdb98aa1c071
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism as an externalist account of knowledge](reliabilism-as-an-externalist-account-of-knowledge-synthesis-f53d2eeb-6036-46fc-bee7-531fa51577e9.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a person can have knowledge without some form of justification for their belief.**, **Suppose someone believes a true proposition based solely on a reliable cognitive process with no accessible justification; intuitively, we might resist calling this "knowledge" without some form of justification present.**, **A person who cannot reflect on or access the reliability of their belief-forming process may lack an understanding of why their belief is true, which challenges the notion of knowledge.**
