# Can knowledge exist without direct cognitive access?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0d72bf6-1224-4ba4-8de1-0596ff4d83ea
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Foundationalism](externalist-foundationalism-synthesis-3e0224db-6723-4288-8a1b-8819cbac7570.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person has a true belief about the weather based purely on a meteorologist's reliable report, but the individual has no idea about how the report is generated, suggests that cognitive access may be necessary for justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification requires at least some cognitive understanding of the process or basis of that justification. This principle together with external justification implies that mere external factors may not be sufficient for justification.**
