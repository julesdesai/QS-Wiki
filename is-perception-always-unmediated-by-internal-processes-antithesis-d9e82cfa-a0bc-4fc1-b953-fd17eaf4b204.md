# Is perception always unmediated by internal processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9e82cfa-a0bc-4fc1-b953-fd17eaf4b204
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that any knowledge acquisition involves some form of internal interpretation of sensory data. This principle together with the claim that knowledge is directly acquired from external objects without mediation entails a contradiction.**, **Consider a scenario where two individuals perceive the same object differently due to differing health conditions. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception is mediated by subjective conditions.**
