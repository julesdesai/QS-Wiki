# Are intellectually virtuous intentions sufficient for ensuring knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8adc316-b8f2-40da-bd69-775826662f26
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue-Responsibilist Epistemology](virtue-responsibilist-epistemology-synthesis-ad635627-9cca-4d73-9dab-ad2a12c77e90.md)

## Content

**A person holds a belief with an intellectually virtuous intention, but the belief is based on faulty reasoning; it intuitively seems that the intention alone does not yield knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectually virtuous intentions alone, without correct reasoning, cannot assure knowledge.**
