# Can justification truly require an infinite chain of reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c18447d5-bcd3-4d5e-b41d-c8380ca0d6f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-db00e7c1-6a13-4ca5-8f09-6fae7cd29e0f.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that human cognitive capacity is finite. This principle entails that humans cannot comprehend or manage an infinite chain of reasons.**, **It is implausible that a justified belief, which requires infinite reasons, can ever be practically obtained by a human being due to their cognitive limitations.**
