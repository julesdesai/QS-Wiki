# How does the Galileo case interfere with sensitivity being necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 705ba023-8727-4b73-b0d7-a6fd11709c3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Entailment](nozicks-conditional-entailment-synthesis-db761f27-9caf-47b5-bb68-5ac892feba1b.md)

## Content

**Consider a historical scenario in which Galileo believed Earth orbited the Sun due to compelling calculations available at the time.**, **If, counterfactually, the Earth's orbiting was false due to simulations available, he would still have believed it based on those calculations.**, **It is implausible that sensitivity as a necessary condition of knowledge works in cases where technical understanding outweighs actual belief discrepancies.**
