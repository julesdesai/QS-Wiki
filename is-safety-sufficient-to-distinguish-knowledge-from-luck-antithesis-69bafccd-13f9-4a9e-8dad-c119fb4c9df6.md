# Is safety sufficient to distinguish knowledge from luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69bafccd-13f9-4a9e-8dad-c119fb4c9df6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Intellectual Virtue Epistemology](safe-intellectual-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-f848b9b7-8a1e-463c-8e96-66e43c041eac.md)

## Content

**A person knows a proposition if their belief arises from an intellectual virtue and is safe from error in nearby possible worlds.**, **Consider a case where a belief is safe and true but only accidentally so due to the specifics of the situation.**, **If safety does not rule out accidental truths, it may not be sufficient to distinguish knowledge from mere luck.**
