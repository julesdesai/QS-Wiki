# Does externalism confront the problem of cognitive luck adequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 567aef88-149b-4587-bfea-d6cd7392b0aa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-5b44df35-6663-4278-91dc-65b3fcbf870c.md)

## Content

**Consider a student who accidentally guesses the correct answer on a multiple-choice test without any preparation. Intuitively, we hesitate to call this knowledge as it stems from cognitive luck rather than understanding.**, **If a belief turning out correctly by reliable accident is enough for knowledge, the conception of knowledge misaligns with our intellectual values.**
