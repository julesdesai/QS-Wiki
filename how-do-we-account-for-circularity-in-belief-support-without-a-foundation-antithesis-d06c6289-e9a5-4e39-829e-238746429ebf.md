# How do we account for circularity in belief support without a foundation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d06c6289-e9a5-4e39-829e-238746429ebf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism](coherentism-synthesis-47013a86-4e6f-4466-86d9-11bbc22ccdaf.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a system of beliefs requires a non-circular foundation to avoid infinite regress. This principle together with the idea that knowledge is a system of beliefs supporting each other suggests that coherentism is circular and lacks a foundational basis.**, **Consider a scenario where belief A supports belief B, belief B supports belief C, and belief C supports belief A. Intuitively, this circular support system does not provide genuine justification for any of the beliefs involved.**
