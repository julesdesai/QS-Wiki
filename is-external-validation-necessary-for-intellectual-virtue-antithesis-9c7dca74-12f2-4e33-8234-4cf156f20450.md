# Is external validation necessary for intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c7dca74-12f2-4e33-8234-4cf156f20450
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Process Reliabilism](virtue-epistemology-with-process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b2ea367-af5a-4f0a-a73e-9afbc8cf5818.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a belief arises from a process considered virtuous or reliable by self-assessment but lacks external validation or consensus. Intuitively, we would judge that external validation may be necessary for considering a belief as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that consensus or external verification ensures the robustness of intellectual virtue. This principle suggests that without such verification, the reliability of cognitive processes is questionable.**
