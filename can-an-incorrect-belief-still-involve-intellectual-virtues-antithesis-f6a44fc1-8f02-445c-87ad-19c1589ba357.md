# Can an incorrect belief still involve intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f6a44fc1-8f02-445c-87ad-19c1589ba357
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology combines knowledge with intellectual virtues](virtue-epistemology-combines-knowledge-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-5b32e3fe-f322-4af8-b790-236786e70562.md)

## Content

**A person can exercise intellectual virtues and yet hold a false belief due to no fault of their own, such as being misled by faulty data.**, **There is a plausible principle that care and open-mindedness do not always lead to truth, as seen when historians scrupulously investigate yet reach incorrect historical conclusions due to incomplete evidence.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a scientist uses all relevant virtues but mistakenly interprets data due to an undetectable error, they seem to have fulfilled the conditions for knowledge under virtue epistemology except for truth, questioning the necessity of truth in the presence of virtue.**
