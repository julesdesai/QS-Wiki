# Is the exercise of skill always an indicator of understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4321090a-8d48-40c6-9693-b278108250e7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**Consider an example where an individual expertly handles a task but cannot teach it to others or adapt it to new situations. Intuitively, we would judge this as lacking full understanding, thus questioning skill as an indicator of true knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding involves adaptability and transferability of knowledge. This principle, alongside the inability to teach or adapt, implies the lack of complete understanding associated with skill.**
