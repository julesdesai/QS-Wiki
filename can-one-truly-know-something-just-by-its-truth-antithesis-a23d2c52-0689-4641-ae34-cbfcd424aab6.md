# Can one truly know something just by its truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a23d2c52-0689-4641-ae34-cbfcd424aab6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-9835edc1-dd3b-4e9d-81fa-8a065de25b95.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowing implies understanding in addition to true belief. This principle suggests that merely having a true belief, without understanding why it is true, does not amount to knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where a person accidentally guesses the result of a complex equation correctly. Intuitively, knowing such a result requires more than just truth.**
