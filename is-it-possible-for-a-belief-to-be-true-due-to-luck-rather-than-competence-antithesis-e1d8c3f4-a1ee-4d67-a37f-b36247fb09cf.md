# Is it possible for a belief to be true due to luck rather than competence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e1d8c3f4-a1ee-4d67-a37f-b36247fb09cf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Apt Belief](knowledge-as-apt-belief-synthesis-6ac0cb6c-f2b3-4636-9c8a-2a793aab4e4b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a belief about a proposition purely by guessing, and the belief happens to be true by chance. In such a scenario, intuitively, the person's belief is not due to their intellectual competence.**, **A belief can be true due to sheer luck rather than stemming from one's skill or competence.**, **It is plausible that a true belief arising from luck or chance is not a case of knowledge, even if it turns out to be correct.**
