# Can knowledge be equated with usefulness without accuracy?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5d132795-ec15-4bf9-9452-46c7a8aab76d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person believes a placebo will cure them, and due to a strong placebo effect, it does. Intuitively, we judge that the belief's usefulness does not equate to knowledge.**, **A claim that knowledge equates to usefulness would mean any effective placebo-belief counts as knowledge, which seems implausible.**
