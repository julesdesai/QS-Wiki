# Can malfunctioning cognitive faculties still produce knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 937c1163-1f3f-4140-87c7-1ad286c70d33
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-bef513bb-4bbb-4139-be10-8a3648bc05d3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a brain lesion that causes them to form beliefs inaccurately. In this scenario, the person forms a belief that turns out to be true. Intuitively, we would judge that the person has knowledge despite the lesion affecting proper functioning.**, **It is plausible to think that sometimes a degraded or malfunctioning cognitive system can still yield true beliefs that count as knowledge due to other compensatory mechanisms.**, **It is implausible that knowledge should always depend on perfect cognitive functioning, since many real-life examples show knowledge acquired in less-than-ideal cognitive states.**
