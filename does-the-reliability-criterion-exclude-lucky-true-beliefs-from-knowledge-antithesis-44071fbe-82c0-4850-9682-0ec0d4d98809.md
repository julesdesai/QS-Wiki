# Does the reliability criterion exclude lucky true beliefs from knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44071fbe-82c0-4850-9682-0ec0d4d98809
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f16b250f-5f50-4247-9620-ff9329c04fe6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person has a true belief due to a process that is usually unreliable, but they were lucky this time. Intuitively, we are inclined to say the person does not truly know.**, **A plausible principle suggests that knowledge is incompatible with lucky guesses, regardless of the accidental reliability in a particular instance.**
