# Can intellectual virtue coexist with epistemic injustice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7b799b17-f8a3-4192-93c0-e69362b9d114
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a situation where a person forms a belief based on sound reasoning and intellectual virtue, but their testimony is not given credence due to social prejudice, such as being part of a marginalized group. In such a situation, intuitively, we would say that the person has intellectual virtue, yet their resulting belief might not be recognized as knowledge due to external bias.**, **It is implausible that knowledge should require recognition solely due to intellectual virtue if epistemic injustice can prevent some beliefs from being acknowledged as knowledge despite being virtuously formed.**
