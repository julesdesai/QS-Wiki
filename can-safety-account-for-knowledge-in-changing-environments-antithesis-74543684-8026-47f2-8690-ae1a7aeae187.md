# Can safety account for knowledge in changing environments?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74543684-8026-47f2-8690-ae1a7aeae187
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-81d482ee-e38b-4fc2-a4fb-45ddef61fa34.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a proposition based on reliable visual perception, but unknown to them, their environment has suddenly changed such that in most cases their perception would now result in a false belief. Intuitively, if their belief remains true, we are hesitant to say they possess knowledge, given the environmental threat.**, **A belief is safe if in most nearby possible worlds where the belief is formed in the same way, it is true. Sudden environmental changes can disrupt the safety of a belief despite its truth in the actual world.**
