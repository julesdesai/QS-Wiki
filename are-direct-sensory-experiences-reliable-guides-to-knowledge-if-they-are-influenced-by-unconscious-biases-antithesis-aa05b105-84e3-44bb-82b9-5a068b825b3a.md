# Are direct sensory experiences reliable guides to knowledge if they are influenced by unconscious biases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aa05b105-84e3-44bb-82b9-5a068b825b3a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Unconscious Processes](phenomenalism-with-unconscious-processes-synthesis-53c9374d-552a-49b3-b121-1513a04b6dab.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person's sensory experiences are heavily influenced by unconscious biases and yet are felt directly is a case in which intuitively the person is misled rather than informed.**, **It is implausible that direct awareness of sensory experiences, if distorted by unconscious biases, qualifies as knowledge.**
