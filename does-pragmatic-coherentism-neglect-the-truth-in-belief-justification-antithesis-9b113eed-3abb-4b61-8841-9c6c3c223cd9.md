# Does pragmatic coherentism neglect the truth in belief justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9b113eed-3abb-4b61-8841-9c6c3c223cd9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Coherentism](pragmatic-coherentism-synthesis-37d6c6a9-68e2-4800-8f97-9338457a8af1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that truth should be an essential component of belief justification, independent of practical success. This principle, together with the fact that practical success can be achieved through false beliefs, suggests that truth is not adequately accounted for in pragmatic coherentism.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone holds a false belief that, by chance, leads to practical success. Intuitively, we would judge that the truth value of the belief impacts its justification, regardless of the outcome.**
