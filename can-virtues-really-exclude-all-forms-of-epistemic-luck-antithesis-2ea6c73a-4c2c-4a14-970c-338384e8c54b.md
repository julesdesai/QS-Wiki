# Can virtues really exclude all forms of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2ea6c73a-4c2c-4a14-970c-338384e8c54b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**A person guesses the outcome of a random coin flip correctly and believes it confidently, resulting in a true belief. Intuitively, such a belief is not knowledge, indicating that some forms of epistemic luck are not eliminated by intellectual virtues.**, **The requirement that knowledge must exclude epistemic luck through virtues seems challenged by scenarios where luck plays a crucial role in a true belief.**
