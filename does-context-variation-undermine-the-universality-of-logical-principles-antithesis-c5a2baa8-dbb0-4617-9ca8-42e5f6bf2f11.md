# Does context variation undermine the universality of logical principles?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5a2baa8-dbb0-4617-9ca8-42e5f6bf2f11
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which the context of two individuals leads one to know a true proposition and the other not to know it. Intuitively, this undermines principles of non-contradiction if both their claims are treated as knowledge**, **There is a plausible principle that logical truths should not depend on contextual factors. This principle suggests that universally applicable knowledge should not be contextually bound**
