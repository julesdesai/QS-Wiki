# Can foundationalism account for infinite regress in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1db62e16-d3d1-4705-85b3-616a58d716df
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism: Basic Beliefs](foundationalism-basic-beliefs-synthesis-fa10f2ed-646f-4b84-85d3-ce2c6a58459d.md)

## Content

**Infinite regress of justification occurs when each belief requires support from another belief, leading to an endless chain.**, **A plausible principle in epistemology is that a belief must be supported by another justified belief to be considered justified.**, **If foundationalism holds that some beliefs need no further justification, then an infinite regress should not occur.**, **The existence of an infinite regress challenges the idea that foundationalism can adequately stop the regress through basic beliefs alone.**
