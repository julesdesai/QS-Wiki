# Can infants or non-human animals have knowledge without intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 87e7b48f-3fde-4d9e-9a92-348d98195fed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-83c48565-5b77-4330-ac3e-7b5984f59b6e.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that infants and some non-human animals can possess knowledge without possessing intellectual virtues. This principle suggests that intellectual virtue is not a requisite for certain forms of knowledge.**, **Consider if a cat successfully navigates its environment based on true beliefs about its surroundings without exercising intellectual virtues. Intuitively, we might say the cat has knowledge of its environment.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should extend beyond creatures capable of intellectual virtue, supporting the claim that intellectual virtue is not a universal requirement for knowledge.**
