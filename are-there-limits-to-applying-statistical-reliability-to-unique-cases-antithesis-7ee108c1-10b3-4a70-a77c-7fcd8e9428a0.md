# Are there limits to applying statistical reliability to unique cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ee108c1-10b3-4a70-a77c-7fcd8e9428a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-094c2ddc-bd9c-46c2-87b1-e486aaff8cb4.md)

## Content

**Statistical reliability suggests a cognitive process is reliable if it produces true beliefs in the majority of instances. However, individual beliefs based on this process can still be false.**, **Suppose a person relies on a news source that usually reports accurately but can still report a false story. Intuitively, one would be cautious to label every belief from this source as knowledge.**
