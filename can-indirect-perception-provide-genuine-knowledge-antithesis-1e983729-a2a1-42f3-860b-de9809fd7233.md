# Can indirect perception provide genuine knowledge?

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cognitive Affordances Summary](direct-realism-with-cognitive-affordances-summary-synthesis-daf75c37-de5d-4669-a0c7-0f33d766507a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person sees an object in a mirror and believes it is behind them based on this perception. Intuitively, they still know the object is there despite the perception being indirect.**, **Knowledge involving perception should not necessarily require direct perception, as indirect perception can provide genuine knowledge.**
