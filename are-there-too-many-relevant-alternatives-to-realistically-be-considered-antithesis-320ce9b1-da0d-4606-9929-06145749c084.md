# Are there too many relevant alternatives to realistically be considered?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 320ce9b1-da0d-4606-9929-06145749c084
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relevant Alternatives Theory](relevant-alternatives-theory-synthesis-7ae3d625-f182-4192-aa96-6af239e87847.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which person R attempts to know whether the wine is Merlot by tasting it. Intuitively, R cannot consider every conceivable relevant alternative such as the wine being poisoned, swapped secretly, or their memory failing**, **There is a plausible principle that attempting to rule out all relevant alternatives can render the process of knowing impractical or impossible.**
