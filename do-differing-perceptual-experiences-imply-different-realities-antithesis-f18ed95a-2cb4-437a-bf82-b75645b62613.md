# Do differing perceptual experiences imply different realities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f18ed95a-2cb4-437a-bf82-b75645b62613
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perception should yield consistent results for it to provide immediate access to external reality. This principle together with differing perceptual experiences of the same object by different people entails a contradiction.**, **Consider a scenario where one person perceived a dress as blue and black, while another perceived it as white and gold. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perceptual experiences vary without implicating differing realities.**
