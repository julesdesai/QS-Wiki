# Does the potential for deception in inferential processes negate knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f55feef7-a0fb-497f-b2f3-30cee7e13a5c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Evidence Theory](inferential-evidence-theory-synthesis-888e1fca-9103-4ede-a19f-61db12f550f6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a magician convincing an audience a trick is real magic rather than sleight of hand, using a series of misleading, yet internally consistent premises. Intuitively, though the audience's belief fits their inferential conclusion, it's not knowledge due to the deceptive foundation.**, **Inferential evidence requires sound reasoning from known premises, but if these premises are manipulatively misleading, the derived belief fails to meet the standard for knowledge.**
