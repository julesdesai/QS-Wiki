# How does epistemic luck pose a problem for knowledge as external alignment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 54b601b7-7a2b-46dd-8cfe-9ecba478a0c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-a9c372ef-1ae7-460b-b26c-8a82dc6a1ee1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an agent's belief aligns with truth due to luck, not due to reliable processes.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we feel that while the belief is true, it is not knowledge because it depends too heavily on chance.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be largely dependent on luck, even if it meets external truth conditions.**
