# Can the focus on intellectual virtue misallocate the emphasis in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a2e74115-a79e-4c83-8fdc-e9a4fb8ab1a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-4a6881dd-2a28-464f-be5f-471a2c9842f0.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that emphasizing intellectual virtue neglects other crucial sources of knowledge such as empirical evidence or deduction. This plausibility suggests that virtue isn't the only or primary aspect of understanding knowledge.**, **Consider instances where empirical evidence strongly supports a belief's truth without recourse to virtues. In such instances, intuitively, knowledge appears complete even without virtue's role.**
