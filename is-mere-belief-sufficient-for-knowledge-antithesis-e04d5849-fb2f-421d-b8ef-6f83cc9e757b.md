# Is mere belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e04d5849-fb2f-421d-b8ef-6f83cc9e757b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism about Knowledge](pragmatism-about-knowledge-synthesis-d6358f85-abbd-4b14-8f12-af989e14cf1c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition without any evidence or justification. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not know the proposition, even if they happen to be right.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than mere belief; it requires justified belief. This principle suggests that belief alone cannot constitute knowledge.**
