# Could knowledge exist without variation in belief across possible worlds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7a5163c2-9257-4648-9b44-ce5df83d5d7a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where a person maintains belief in a true proposition in the actual and all possible worlds, yet this constancy reveals nothing about the person's responsiveness to truth. Intuitively, the lack of change across worlds questions whether such uniform belief constitutes genuine knowledge.**, **It is implausible to equate mere constancy of belief with knowledge, suggesting truth-tracking's necessity is questionable.**
