# Can coherence theory effectively differentiate between true and false coherent belief systems?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d06950bb-f705-4f0d-87b1-be0f5aeda362
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism without Infinite Regress](coherentism-without-infinite-regress-synthesis-1802290e-23e3-475c-be0d-a870496d2311.md)

## Content

**Imagine two systems of belief that are internally coherent but one represents true information about the world and the other false. Intuitively, coherence alone cannot be the criterion for truth if both are equally coherent.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth must involve correspondence with reality beyond mere internal consistency.**, **A coherence theory that cannot distinguish between equally coherent but factually disparate systems struggles to provide an effective account of knowledge.**
