## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **Knowledge can be derived from innate cognitive faculties without requiring access to external evidence**, **Innate cognitive faculties can provide immediate insight into certain kinds of truths, such as mathematical truths**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief in the proposition is justified by these faculties' reliability**
