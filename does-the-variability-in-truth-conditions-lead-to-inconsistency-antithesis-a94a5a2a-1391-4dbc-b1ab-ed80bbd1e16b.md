# Does the variability in truth conditions lead to inconsistency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a94a5a2a-1391-4dbc-b1ab-ed80bbd1e16b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two different contexts lead to contradictory truth conditions for the same knowledge claim. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the knowledge claim cannot be both true and false simultaneously.**, **The principle of non-contradiction asserts that contradictory propositions cannot both be true in the same sense at the same time. This principle together with the scenario entails that varying truth conditions by context could lead to inconsistencies.**
