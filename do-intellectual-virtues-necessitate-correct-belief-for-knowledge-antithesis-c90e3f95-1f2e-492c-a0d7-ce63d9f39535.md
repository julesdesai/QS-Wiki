# Do intellectual virtues necessitate correct belief for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c90e3f95-1f2e-492c-a0d7-ce63d9f39535
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue epistemology view on knowledge](virtue-epistemology-view-on-knowledge-synthesis-e49b38ea-0e37-4416-ac67-ff35516a157e.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that exercising intellectual virtues should lead to correct belief for it to result in knowledge. This principle, together with the allowance for belief alone to constitute knowledge, suggests a gap between virtues and belief.**, **Consider a scenario where an intellectually virtuous person is systematically misinformed; intuitively, even in such a case, they do not have knowledge.**
