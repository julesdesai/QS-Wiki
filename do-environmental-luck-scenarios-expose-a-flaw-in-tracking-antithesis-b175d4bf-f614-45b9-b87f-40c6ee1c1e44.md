# Do environmental luck scenarios expose a flaw in tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b175d4bf-f614-45b9-b87f-40c6ee1c1e44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3749df9e-e568-414c-88ec-cc6b23580530.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief about the presence of a feature based on their environment, which usually supports the belief. Intuitively, either the belief is luckily true due to environmental factors, or it is not knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where the presence of decoys or misleading similar features would lead to a false belief under slightly altered conditions. Intuitively, this undermines the claim to knowledge.**
