# Is the truth requirement alone sufficient for knowledge without additional factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5567aa01-2dc0-4a07-a63e-e422b75c9641
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozickian Tracking Theory Summary](nozickian-tracking-theory-summary-synthesis-44760ad8-dc58-4f04-9662-a1508a0ab6d4.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a belief is true and believed, but the belief is formed entirely by unreliable methods or guesswork. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible that the mere truth and belief of a proposition are sufficient for someone to have knowledge. Without additional justificatory conditions, true belief falls short of what is traditionally considered knowledge.**
