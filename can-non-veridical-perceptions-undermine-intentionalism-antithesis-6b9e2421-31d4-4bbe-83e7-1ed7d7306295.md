# Can non-veridical perceptions undermine intentionalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6b9e2421-31d4-4bbe-83e7-1ed7d7306295
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Intentionalism Summary](intentionalism-summary-synthesis-3678fe22-b4a0-4fa7-aad8-5dfa98b1a709.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person experiences a mirage in the desert, perceiving water where there is none. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception represents something that does not exist in reality.**, **A person knows a proposition if its representation through intentional content corresponds with reality implies that knowledge through perception requires correspondence with reality, which is lacking in the case of mirages.**
