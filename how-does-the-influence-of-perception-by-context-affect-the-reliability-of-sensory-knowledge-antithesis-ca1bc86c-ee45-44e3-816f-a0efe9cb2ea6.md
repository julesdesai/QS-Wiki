# How does the influence of perception by context affect the reliability of sensory knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca1bc86c-ee45-44e3-816f-a0efe9cb2ea6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**Suppose that perception is influenced by context to the point where two individuals perceive the same situation differently due to differing contexts. Intuitively, this suggests that perception cannot serve reliably as a foundation for knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptual evidence should yield consistent knowledge if it is to be trusted, and yet the variability implied by context influence seems to challenge this.**
