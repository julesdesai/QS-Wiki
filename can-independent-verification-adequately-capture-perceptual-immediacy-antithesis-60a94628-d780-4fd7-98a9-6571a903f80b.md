# Can independent verification adequately capture perceptual immediacy?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60a94628-d780-4fd7-98a9-6571a903f80b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perceptual experiences provide direct and immediate knowledge without the need for independent verification. This principle together with the requirement for independent confirmation implies that perceptual immediacy is compromised.**, **Suppose a person sees a tree in a garden and recognizes it immediately as an oak. Intuitively, we would judge that the person has knowledge of the tree being an oak. If independent verification were required, the immediacy of this knowledge would be undermined.**, **It is implausible that all perceptual knowledge must be mediated by additional verification processes, as this would render the directness of perception ineffective.**
