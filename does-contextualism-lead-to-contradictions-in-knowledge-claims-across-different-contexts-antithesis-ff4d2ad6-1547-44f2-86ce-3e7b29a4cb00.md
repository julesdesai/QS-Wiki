# Does contextualism lead to contradictions in knowledge claims across different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ff4d2ad6-1547-44f2-86ce-3e7b29a4cb00
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c8c66eb7-99be-427a-b717-b1ecb74e1290.md)

## Content

**Suppose in one context, a person claims to know a certain proposition based on evidence deemed sufficient within that context.**, **In another context, the same evidence might not meet the stricter standards, and the person would not be said to know the same proposition.**, **It is intuitively contradictory for a person to be both said to "know" and "not know" the same proposition simply because of a change in context.**
