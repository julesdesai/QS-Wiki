# Does the lack of a direct causal link affect the strength of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9091ee5b-d9b2-4d60-95cc-b50801555e7e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a370e7ec-6345-4df3-bbad-68ec4fda2c8e.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person uses a horoscope to predict stock market changes, which coincidentally tracks the truth due to an unrelated economic trend. Intuitively, the lack of direct causal evidence weakens the knowledge claim despite truth tracking.**, **A belief that tracks the truth without a causal link seems weaker probabilistically and less intuitively robust in a knowledge claim than one with evidence-based causation.**
