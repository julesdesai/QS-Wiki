# Do all reliable traits qualify as epistemic virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3152448a-671c-4c78-baaf-b7a1f53433b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**There are traits that reliably lead to true beliefs but do not seem like virtues, such as stubbornness in a fortunate context.**, **The definition of an epistemic virtue as a trait leading to reliable success in forming true beliefs might wrongly include traits that lack the normative endorsement typical of virtues.**, **If a person is stubborn and this trait reliably results in true beliefs in certain situations, it challenges the notion that only virtuous traits qualify as epistemic virtues.**
