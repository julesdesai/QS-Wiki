# Does knowledge require checkable, real-world justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 40d9503c-720b-46cf-89ca-80d9332fda63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Finite Justifications](infinitism-with-finite-justifications-synthesis-69ad7e03-7f20-4a4f-85ef-ebc999d85fe8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is said to know a proposition based on an indefinitely long series of reasons. Intuitively, any claim of knowledge should allow for verification or justification through checkable means in the real world.**, **A plausible principle that epistemic claims require rational, real-world verification seems to be challenged by an infinite chain of justification.**
