# Are all knowledgeable beliefs necessarily sensitive to counterfactual scenarios?

## Content

**Imagine a situation where a person believes there is water in the glass based on sight. Even if it were juice, they would still believe it due to similar appearance. Yet, it seems reasonable to say they know it is water.**, **Many everyday claims of knowledge do not change when subject to minor hypotheticals, reflecting common use of the term 'know.'**
