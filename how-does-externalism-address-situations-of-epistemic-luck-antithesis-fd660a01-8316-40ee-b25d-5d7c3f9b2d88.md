# How does externalism address situations of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fd660a01-8316-40ee-b25d-5d7c3f9b2d88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-5b44df35-6663-4278-91dc-65b3fcbf870c.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person driving through an area filled with papier-mâché barns sees a real barn and believes it is real. Intuitively, we would not consider this knowledge even though the belief is both true and formed from a normally reliable process.**, **Knowledge cannot be attributable to sheer luck; hence reliability-based knowledge through an unreliable-looking environment falters in epistemic justification.**
