# Can knowledge exist independently of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 113f2662-6162-4fc4-8cbb-d80700e673fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Empirical Direct Realism](empirical-direct-realism-synthesis-6aae1d50-8ed2-43cf-bf53-0de920bdf07f.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that all knowledge must be tied to direct perception, as mathematical truths are known independently of direct sensory experience.**, **Consider a scenario where a person knows a mathematical proof without ever perceiving any empirical instance of the proof. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge the person knows the proof, despite the absence of direct perception and empirical testing.**
