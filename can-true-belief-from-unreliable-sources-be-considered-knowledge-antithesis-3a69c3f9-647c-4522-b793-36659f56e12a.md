# Can true belief from unreliable sources be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a69c3f9-647c-4522-b793-36659f56e12a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person accurately predicts weather conditions because of a causal connection to a consistently unreliable news outlet. Intuitively, we would have to say this belief is true but not knowledge, due to the unreliability of the source.**, **It is implausible that a belief formed from an unreliable source can be considered knowledge, even if causality is present.**
