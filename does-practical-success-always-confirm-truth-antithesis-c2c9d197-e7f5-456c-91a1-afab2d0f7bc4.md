# Does practical success always confirm truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2c9d197-e7f5-456c-91a1-afab2d0f7bc4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Verifactive Pragmatism](verifactive-pragmatism-synthesis-4eab892d-8662-4622-835b-cae1a39a2d29.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes they have taken medication that will cure their illness, and they subsequently feel better. Intuitively, we would judge that the practical success of feeling better doesn't confirm the truth of their belief if the medication was actually a placebo.**, **Truth can be confirmed by independent factors irrespective of practical outcomes, such as scientific testing or logical reasoning without immediate practical application.**
