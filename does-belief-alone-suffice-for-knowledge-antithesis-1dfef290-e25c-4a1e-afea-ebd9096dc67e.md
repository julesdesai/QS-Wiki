# Does belief alone suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1dfef290-e25c-4a1e-afea-ebd9096dc67e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Revised](coherentism-revised-synthesis-de283b0a-5fd8-4f4c-bf89-d22a34799ca8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a delusional belief that a celebrity is sending them personal messages through television broadcasts. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite claiming belief.**, **It is implausible that merely believing a proposition suffices for knowledge because belief can be formed through unreliable processes.**
