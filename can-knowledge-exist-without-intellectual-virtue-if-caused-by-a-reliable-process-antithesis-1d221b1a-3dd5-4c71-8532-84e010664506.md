# Can knowledge exist without intellectual virtue if caused by a reliable process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d221b1a-3dd5-4c71-8532-84e010664506
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit-based Virtue Epistemology](credit-based-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-b8bc04b8-9f33-4496-9274-056c4b04b89c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has a true belief because they rely on a reliable but non-intellectually virtuous process like a computer algorithm. It is plausible that we might still consider this belief to be knowledge.**, **Knowledge could be seen as not requiring the explicit credit of an intellectual virtue when it results from reliable cognitive processes.**
