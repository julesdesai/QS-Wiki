# Is intersubjective agreement truly necessary for objective knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74a29394-4a3c-4713-aa61-a13294e39f08
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Objective Phenomenalism](objective-phenomenalism-synthesis-91c0e065-6d6c-4b9a-9519-a161a622cd2d.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a scientist discovers a new phenomenon that no others have yet observed. Intuitively, we would judge that the scientist possesses objective knowledge even in the absence of intersubjective agreement.**, **There is a plausible principle that novel insights can constitute objective knowledge even before they gain widespread recognition or verification by others.**
