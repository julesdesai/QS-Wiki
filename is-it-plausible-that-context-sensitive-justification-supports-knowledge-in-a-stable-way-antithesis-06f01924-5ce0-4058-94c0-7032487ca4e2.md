# Is it plausible that context-sensitive justification supports knowledge in a stable way?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06f01924-5ce0-4058-94c0-7032487ca4e2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemological Disjunctivism](epistemological-disjunctivism-synthesis-82cbae05-c2e1-4b4d-af67-987574976695.md)

## Content

**Suppose that the justification of knowledge varies with different types of perception, such as seeing a tree in bright daylight or in foggy conditions. It is implausible to claim that such shifting justifications provide a stable grounding for knowledge across different contexts.**
