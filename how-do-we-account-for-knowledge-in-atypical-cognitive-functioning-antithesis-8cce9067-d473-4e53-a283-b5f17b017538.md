# How do we account for knowledge in atypical cognitive functioning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8cce9067-d473-4e53-a283-b5f17b017538
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-ea617099-7596-484d-aca6-1b318b0033ac.md)

## Content

**Consider cases where individuals with atypical cognitive functioning, such as savants, know complex mathematical truths without knowing how they arrived at them. Intuitively, it seems they possess knowledge despite their cognitive processes deviating from normal functioning.**, **It is implausible that only standard cognitive functioning according to a design plan can lead to knowledge, as atypical processes can also yield correct insights.**
