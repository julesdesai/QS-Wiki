# Is belief justification too fluid in contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 40ddcf2e-2952-435b-975a-64fca4a302fd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism Summary](contextualism-summary-synthesis-70123d28-f52a-42a2-8d85-6f92ac2f5bd1.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that belief justification should have some degree of consistency. This suggests that heavily context-sensitive justifications might undermine the stability and reliability of knowledge claims.**, **Consider a philosopher who is confident about a philosophical argument in academic circles but unsure in a more casual context. It seems counterintuitive that the justification wavers without new evidence.**
