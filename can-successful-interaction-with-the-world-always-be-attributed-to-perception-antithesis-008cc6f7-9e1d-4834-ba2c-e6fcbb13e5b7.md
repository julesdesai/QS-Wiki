# Can successful interaction with the world always be attributed to perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 008cc6f7-9e1d-4834-ba2c-e6fcbb13e5b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-08f9dcda-197c-4176-877f-438b2d186f9d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a blind person successfully navigates the environment using a cane. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that successful interaction is achieved through non-perceptual means rather than direct perception.**, **It is implausible that perception alone provides all the useful information needed for successful interaction with the world when other senses and tools play a significant role.**
