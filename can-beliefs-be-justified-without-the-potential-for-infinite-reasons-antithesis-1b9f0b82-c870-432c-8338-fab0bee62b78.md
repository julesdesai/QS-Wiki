# Can beliefs be justified without the potential for infinite reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b9f0b82-c870-432c-8338-fab0bee62b78
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Finite Infinitism Summary](finite-infinitism-summary-synthesis-cf1727db-af05-4f36-9758-13f464b5dd32.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a proposition based on a coherent set of evidence. Intuitively, we would judge this belief to be justified even if it does not involve the prospect of infinite further justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that coherence among beliefs can suffice for justification. This principle, combined with the coherence of a belief set, implies that infinite potential reasons are unnecessary.**
