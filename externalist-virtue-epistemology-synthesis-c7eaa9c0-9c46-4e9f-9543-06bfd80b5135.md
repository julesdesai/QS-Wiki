## Content

**Knowledge is a true belief arising externally through reliable methods beyond the individual's own intellectual virtues**, **A person knows a proposition if their belief arises from external conditions or methods that are reliable**, **For a belief to constitute knowledge, it must come from an external process that reliably produces true beliefs**, **Knowledge does not necessarily require the truth to be directly creditable to the knower's cognitive abilities**
