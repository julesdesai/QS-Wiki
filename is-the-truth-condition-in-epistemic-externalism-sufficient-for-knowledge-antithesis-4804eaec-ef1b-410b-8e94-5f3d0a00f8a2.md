# Is the truth condition in epistemic externalism sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4804eaec-ef1b-410b-8e94-5f3d0a00f8a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where a belief is true and justified by external factors, yet formed by sheer chance. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief does not qualify as knowledge.**, **Merely having the truth and external justification conditions met is insufficient if the belief formation is due to chance.**, **It is unreasonable to consider truth alone, even if externally justified, as sufficient for a belief to be considered knowledge.**
