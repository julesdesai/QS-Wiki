# Can truth alone suffice for knowledge in some cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3aabfbfb-699a-4bc7-b6f8-527847fe57d3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**In cases where a proposition is true due to luck rather than justification, it seems counterintuitive to call this knowledge, even if the proposition is indeed true.**, **Consider a scenario where a gambler predicts an outcome correctly purely by chance; the proposition becomes true without the gambler's knowledge.**, **Truth without appropriate justification or belief doesn't align with our intuitive understanding of knowledge, as knowledge is expected to reliably guide actions.**
