# Can knowledge exist without robust evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d42e27c3-21ab-4b02-baf1-25831e261e24
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidence-Based Theory](evidence-based-theory-synthesis-3a180c4f-30b0-4e6f-87b5-c20a77cbb8ff.md)

## Content

**A scenario in which a person forms a belief based on a lucky guess rather than evidence can still result in a true belief. Intuitively, this belief might still be considered knowledge if it happens to be correct despite the lack of robust evidence.**, **A person might come to know that it is raining outside solely based on an immediate perception rather than through gathering additional evidence. Immediate perception in such a scenario intuitively does not involve robust evidence.**
