# Could constant shifts in context lead to contradictions in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5ebaa45-c68e-40e8-a834-3293ecd75418
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-cec2b7f9-8a52-4ab7-9dd4-6f75547db097.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone claims to know a proposition in one context but denies knowing it in another, although all factual aspects remain constant. Intuitively, this leads to a contradiction if we accept the proposition's truth does not depend on context.**
