# Can a belief be known when reliable processes are coincidentally reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1a73a6f-d377-4b2a-a817-6c8489383493
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person correctly believes a proposition through a process that, by mere coincidence, happens to be reliable in this instance. Intuitively, the belief seems more like a lucky guess than knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be attributed to mere coincidences; reliability by coincidence does not suffice for genuine knowledge.**
