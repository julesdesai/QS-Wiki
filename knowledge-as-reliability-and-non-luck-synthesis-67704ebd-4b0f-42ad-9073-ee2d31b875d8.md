# Knowledge as Reliability and Non-Luck

**Node Type:** synthesis

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can baseless but fortuitously true beliefs count as knowledge?](can-baseless-but-fortuitously-true-beliefs-count-as-knowledge-antithesis-94dc72d9-b000-469d-a37a-58d07118d8b2.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief reliably produces true beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the truth of the belief is not due to luck**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief reliably produces true beliefs, and the truth of the belief is not due to luck**
