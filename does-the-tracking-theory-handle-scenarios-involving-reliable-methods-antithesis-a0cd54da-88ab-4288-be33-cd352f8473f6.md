# Does the Tracking Theory handle scenarios involving reliable methods?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a0cd54da-88ab-4288-be33-cd352f8473f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3fc68b12-ca29-40d7-8341-51e40d71c5e5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scientist using a highly accurate thermometer reading 100°C when measuring boiling water at sea level. They truly believe the water is boiling.**, **If the boiling point were different under these conditions (say different atmospheric pressure), they would not believe the water is boiling based on this method.**, **In slightly different environments or with minor calibration differences, their belief would hold given reliability of instrumentation.**
