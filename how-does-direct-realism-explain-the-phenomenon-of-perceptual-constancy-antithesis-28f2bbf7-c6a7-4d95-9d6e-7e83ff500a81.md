# How does Direct Realism explain the phenomenon of perceptual constancy?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 28f2bbf7-c6a7-4d95-9d6e-7e83ff500a81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person views a door as rectangular whether it is open or closed. Intuitively, we would judge that perceptual constancy relies on mental processing beyond direct access to external objects.**, **A plausible principle is that direct perception would involve registering the changing sensory data directly. This principle together with the occurrence of perceptual constancy entails that there must be interpretation involved.**
