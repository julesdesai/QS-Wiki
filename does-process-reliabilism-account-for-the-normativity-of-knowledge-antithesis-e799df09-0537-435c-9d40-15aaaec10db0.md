# Does process reliabilism account for the normativity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e799df09-0537-435c-9d40-15aaaec10db0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**There is a commonly held intuition that knowledge not only requires true belief but carries a normative aspect, meaning it is associated with being in an epistemically good or appropriate state. This principle creates tension with a purely process-based account that does not inherently include normativity.**, **Imagine an individual who habitually forms true beliefs by sheer coincidence or luck, meeting reliabilism's criteria superficially but lacking an appropriate epistemic demeanor or rationale.**, **The lack of normative assessment within strictly process-focused accounts raises issues about its adequacy in defining knowledge.**
