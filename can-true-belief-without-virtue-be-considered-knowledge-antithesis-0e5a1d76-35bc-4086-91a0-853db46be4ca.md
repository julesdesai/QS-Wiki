# Can true belief without virtue be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e5a1d76-35bc-4086-91a0-853db46be4ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a true belief, but the belief is not formed through any intellectual virtue or reliable method. Intuitively, such a belief would not qualify as knowledge because it lacks the necessary connection to intellectual virtues.**, **A belief is justified if it is produced by cognitive faculties or habits that constitute intellectual virtues. If the belief lacks this connection, it appears unjustified as knowledge, even if true.**
