# Does invariantism explain less about epistemic standards than contextualism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 359569be-e886-45ff-9810-ef7b4ec23b20
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-bf708b38-e467-425c-8744-5d490abcb982.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people witness the same event but draw different knowledge conclusions based on personal biases.**, **Intuitively, one might assume differing epistemic standards explain their disagreement.**, **If invariantism were true, differing conclusions would need alternative explanations, thus challenging the presumption that context is not the primary factor.**
