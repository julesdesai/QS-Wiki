# Does Process Reliabilism adequately account for the clairvoyance problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7de3673c-867e-4856-baa7-3fef9d71565e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-54c291ff-d1f5-45c3-8197-657cbffa4347.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person has a clairvoyant ability causing them to believe the president is in New York, and this belief is true. They have no reason to trust this ability and are unaware of its reliability. Intuitively, this person's belief seems unjustified despite it stemming from a reliable process.**, **A plausible principle states that justified beliefs require some recognition or awareness of the reliability of the process behind them. Therefore, a belief being reliably formed does not ensure justification when the person is unaware of the process's reliability.**
