# Knowledge as Justified True Belief with No Defeaters

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** 5c6afe9d-4ae1-4856-949d-0221bdebd662
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can beliefs supported by misleading evidence still constitute knowledge?](can-beliefs-supported-by-misleading-evidence-still-constitute-knowledge-antithesis-8aefb9fe-516b-4af9-bda0-b4db8a8bd9bb.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is justified**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that no overriding evidence or reasons would defeat their belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they have a justified true belief and no defeaters are present**
