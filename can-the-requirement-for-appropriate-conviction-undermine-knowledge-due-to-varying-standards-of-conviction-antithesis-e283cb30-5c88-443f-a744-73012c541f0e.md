# Can the requirement for appropriate conviction undermine knowledge due to varying standards of conviction?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e283cb30-5c88-443f-a744-73012c541f0e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist View with Doxastic Involvement](evidentialist-view-with-doxastic-involvement-synthesis-75fddb9e-288b-457e-8f92-4c216b30a2a5.md)

## Content

**The standard for what constitutes an "appropriate" degree of conviction can be subjective and vary greatly among different individuals, leading to inconsistencies in what is considered knowledge.**, **It is implausible that subjective standards, which differ from person to person, should determine whether a true belief based on evidence is knowledge.**
