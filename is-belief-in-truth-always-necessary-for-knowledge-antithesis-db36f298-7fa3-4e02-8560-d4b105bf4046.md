# Is belief in truth always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db36f298-7fa3-4e02-8560-d4b105bf4046
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Proper Functionalism](knowledge-as-proper-functionalism-synthesis-95adcceb-ce7f-44ef-aea6-bebac418915b.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge does not always require the believer’s awareness of the truth condition.**, **Consider a case where a mathematical prodigy intuitively solves complex equations without belief in the process, yet achieves correct results. Intuitively, we might still attribute knowledge to such actions.**
