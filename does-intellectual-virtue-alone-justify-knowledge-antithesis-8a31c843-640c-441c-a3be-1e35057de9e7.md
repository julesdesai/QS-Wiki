# Does intellectual virtue alone justify knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8a31c843-640c-441c-a3be-1e35057de9e7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Virtue Epistemology](revised-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-e97d218b-557b-43e8-afcd-587c60b67e41.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has been educated in a rigorous way and believes a true proposition, yet lacks actual reliability in arriving at truth. Intuitively, having intellectual virtue alone without reliability does not seem to justify that the person has knowledge.**, **Reliability in reaching truth appears necessary alongside intellectual virtue to ensure the presence of knowledge.**
