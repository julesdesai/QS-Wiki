# Do mental representations allow for direct knowledge of external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 88b487b6-8e17-44b1-8f05-1f6d8667d1bf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-e49b01dd-7eac-432b-990b-a606f4616c7d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person with aphantasia, a condition where one cannot visualize images, still possesses factual knowledge about objects.**, **If knowledge requires mental representations of the external world, aphantasic individuals should be unable to know about objects without mental imagery.**, **This suggests the possibility that mental representations might not be necessary for all forms of knowledge about external objects.**
