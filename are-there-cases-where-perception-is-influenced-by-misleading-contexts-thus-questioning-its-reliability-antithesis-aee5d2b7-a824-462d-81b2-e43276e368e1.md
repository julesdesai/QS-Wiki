# Are there cases where perception is influenced by misleading contexts, thus questioning its reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aee5d2b7-a824-462d-81b2-e43276e368e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Contextualism Summary](perceptual-contextualism-summary-synthesis-b9fb6be1-a4e1-46bf-91f2-7fc63c3abb0c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a magician creates an illusion making an object appear to vanish, the perceiver's context suggests the object is gone.**, **If a person claims to know the object is gone based on perception modified by the misleading context, it shows the context can distort reality.**, **If perception modified by context can be so easily influenced to be misleading, its role in knowledge acquisition is questionable.**
