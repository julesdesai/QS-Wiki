# Can someone have true belief without justification and still be considered to have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 789bb434-7c2c-44c4-87fb-706007c24948
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-ebb76c32-727a-4eba-8f42-21c2ee3c5622.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification. This principle together with the idea that someone could have a belief that is true but unjustified (e.g., guessing the outcome of a random event) entails that they do not have knowledge.**
