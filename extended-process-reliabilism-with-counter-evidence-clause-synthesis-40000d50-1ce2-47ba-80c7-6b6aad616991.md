## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief results from a reliable cognitive process that yields true beliefs more often than not**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and their belief results from a reliable cognitive process**, **The reliability of the cognitive process must remain undefeated by any known counter-evidence**
