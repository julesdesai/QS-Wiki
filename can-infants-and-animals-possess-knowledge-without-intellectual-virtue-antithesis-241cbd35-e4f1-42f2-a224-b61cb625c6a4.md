# Can infants and animals possess knowledge without intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 241cbd35-e4f1-42f2-a224-b61cb625c6a4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-6b17452f-daf9-4e36-a5b5-c7d2a1d34c57.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario of an infant or a non-human animal who seems to know basic facts, such as locations or dangers, without evidence of intellectual virtue. We intuitively judge that they have some form of knowledge.**, **Knowledge as requiring intellectual virtue implies that infants and animals, who lack developed cognitive virtuosity, cannot possess knowledge, challenging the comprehensiveness of virtue epistemology.**
