# Does the necessity of truth for knowledge clash with demonstrated actions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85257586-dd7a-4e16-a6b3-42e6e852af0a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Non-Reductive Physicalism on Implicit Knowledge](non-reductive-physicalism-on-implicit-knowledge-synthesis-6ae3dfb7-99c7-43ae-97da-3cb03e8b87cf.md)

## Content

**A person can perform actions that demonstrate skill or competence, even holding incomplete or false beliefs about their actions.**, **A chef who consistently cooks perfect dishes while misunderstanding some culinary principles still satisfies the conditions for skill demonstration.**, **This challenges the necessity of truth since incorrect propositional beliefs support successful action.**
