# Can Knowledge Be Easily Lost Due to Slight Changes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dcd807f9-9926-45d2-af77-ce3777860f7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Theory](safety-condition-theory-synthesis-55c1a70f-0977-44f8-b96b-763b1bae91c4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person knows it is raining because they see rain through the window. Intuitively, if the rain stopped a moment earlier, they would no longer know it is raining.**, **If small changes in the world can easily lead to a belief being false, then our knowledge seems too fragile and unreliable.**, **A belief that is too easily lost due to small changes cannot be considered knowledge because it lacks stability.**
