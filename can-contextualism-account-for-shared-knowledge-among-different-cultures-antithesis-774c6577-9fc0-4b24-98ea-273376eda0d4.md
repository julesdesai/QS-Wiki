# Can Contextualism Account for Shared Knowledge Among Different Cultures?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 774c6577-9fc0-4b24-98ea-273376eda0d4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-6d1ded2e-2e81-4f10-b6c8-db699d1664cc.md)

## Content

**Suppose two cultures have distinctly different forms of epistemic practices but reach the same conclusion about a scientific fact. Intuitively, such shared knowledge should confirm the objectivity of the conclusion.**, **Contextualism implies that knowledge is heavily tied to cultural contexts, challenging the notion that such universally agreed-upon knowledge is actually objective.**, **It is implausible to suggest that the same knowledge claim can emerge independently in different contexts without an underlying objective truth guiding it.**
