# Does stability ignore the need for external validation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a995e217-4b2b-42b7-b8eb-fede5524a6b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scientist perceives a chemical reaction and consistently sees a color change due to faulty equipment. Although these perceptual experiences are stable, intuitively we would judge that external validation is necessary to convert stable perception into knowledge.**, **It is implausible that internal perceptions alone, without external correlation or validation, can always constitute knowledge because they might consistently be misled by instrumental errors.**
