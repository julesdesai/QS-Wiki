# Can temporal coherentism address conflicting evidence effectively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84be0a5d-63fe-41bc-87dc-4ca3d1dc641a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Temporal Coherentism](temporal-coherentism-synthesis-f4dfebfd-2f02-4b67-a5dd-1a5ab136482a.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which new evidence conflicts with a previously coherent belief system. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person should update their beliefs or ignore the evidence. If we say the person should update their beliefs, then coherence is not the primary criterion. If we say coherence should override evidence, then genuine knowledge might be missed.**
