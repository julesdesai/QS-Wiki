# Can knowledge exist without internally accessible evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 49891438-0825-41e8-baef-76d3f9b44f74
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Externalism](evidential-externalism-synthesis-1f3be30d-cfb3-46d2-9d1a-294e056d7ea0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires evidence that is accessible to the knower through introspection. This principle, together with the idea that knowledge must withstand scrutiny from the knower's perspective, entails that knowledge should involve internally accessible evidence.**, **Consider a scenario where a person truly believes a proposition based on evidence only available externally and not introspectively. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person lacks knowledge because they cannot internally justify their belief.**
