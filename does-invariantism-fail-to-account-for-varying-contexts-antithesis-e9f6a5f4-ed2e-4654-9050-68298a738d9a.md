# Does invariantism fail to account for varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9f6a5f4-ed2e-4654-9050-68298a738d9a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-2877eecc-8388-4e01-b87d-ffe7f20b2619.md)

## Content

**People often change their standards for what counts as knowing based on different contexts such as stakes or conversational settings.**, **Intuitively, if a person's life depends on a fact, they might not claim to know it based on the same evidence as in a casual setting.**, **If knowledge is independent of context, it should not change with the significance of the outcome when it clearly seems to do so.**
