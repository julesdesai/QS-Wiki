# How can one distinguish between skill as knowledge and mere habit?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 47a9a305-e851-4fe9-a5b2-b9c00dd0b35c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Skills](virtue-epistemology-with-skills-synthesis-458da717-0537-4f5a-8a52-aec5d63f6438.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that for something to qualify as knowledge, it must involve conscious awareness and intentionality. This principle together with the idea that skill is a form of knowledge implies that skills should involve conscious awareness and intentionality.**, **Consider a scenario where someone drives a familiar route on autopilot without conscious awareness. Intuitively, we would judge the driving skill as more of a habit than reflective knowledge.**
