# Is Reliabilism overly permissive with intrinsic unreliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d7e94a04-aa8f-4879-b8d3-ef8863658aa4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-e69ee86a-1c0a-4d0e-840e-f25c67fd8e9a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person consistently uses an unreliable method, such as guessing, which coincidentally produces true beliefs regularly. Intuitively, a method known for being unreliable should not be considered to confer knowledge, despite sporadic accuracy.**, **An intrinsically unreliable method that occasionally aligns with truth by coincidence challenges the idea of reliability as a basis for defining knowledge.**
