# Can knowledge be entirely based on shared agreement?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 76521db7-18eb-413a-ba29-9510ee7e29c3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-c00af758-5073-4931-9585-2f560dddd897.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be independent of external consensus when an individual has verifiable evidence. This principle together with claiming that knowledge involves inter-subjective agreement on perceptual experiences implies that an individual cannot know something solely on their own evidence without external agreement.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person has direct and clear perceptual access to an external object without others present. Intuitively, we would have to say that the person knows about the object based on their perception alone, or that they do not know it without others verifying it. If we say they do not know, this undermines the independence of personal verification. If we say they do know, this conflicts with the requirement for inter-subjective agreement.**
