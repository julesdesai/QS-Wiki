# Do variable standards undermine the objective nature of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9853dbbb-a2af-48fb-8ec7-cb882f3ab45c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8d1d3277-a6f4-453c-8402-a170b25d1ed4.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge is an objective state, meaning it should not vary with subjective factors like conversational context.**, **If a person’s knowledge claim is subject to contextual factors, then knowledge becomes a shifting and subjective state, which conflicts with the principle of objective knowledge.**
