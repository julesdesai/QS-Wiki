# Does double-checking and its implications present a challenge for the tracking theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d21932da-375b-4ae4-88cc-473887a08045
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Consider a person double-checking a result with different methods. If the proposition is false, the person would abandon belief upon verification failure from both methods. If the proposition is true, they would continue to believe it across methods. Double-checking may inherently result in actions separated from mere belief judgments.**, **The continued belief regardless of result from one method but not another reflects situational human methodical practice, raising questions on its standing in knowledge characterization.**
