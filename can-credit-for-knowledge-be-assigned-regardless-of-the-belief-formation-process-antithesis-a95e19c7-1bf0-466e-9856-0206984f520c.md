# Can credit for knowledge be assigned regardless of the belief formation process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a95e19c7-1bf0-466e-9856-0206984f520c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Credit Theory of Knowledge](credit-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f65ebe79-7fd3-4c3c-9fb3-cd47f9656422.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that credit should be given only if the true belief results directly from the use of the agent's cognitive faculties. This principle together with the fact that belief formation can involve non-intellectual influences entails that not all processes can rightly attribute knowledge credit to the agent.**
