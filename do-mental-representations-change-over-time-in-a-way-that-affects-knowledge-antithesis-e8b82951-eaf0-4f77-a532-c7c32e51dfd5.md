# Do mental representations change over time in a way that affects knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e8b82951-eaf0-4f77-a532-c7c32e51dfd5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Representationalism](representationalism-synthesis-c6ad7a19-c6b2-4a71-b6f5-a8020ca1b42c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a scientist forms an accurate mental representation of a phenomenon based on current evidence, but new evidence later changes the phenomenon. Intuitively, we would judge that the scientist had knowledge initially, despite the change.**, **There is a plausible principle that current knowledge can be valid even if future changes alter the representation. This principle together with the idea of immutable mental representations suggests knowledge is not entirely dependent on static representations.**
