# Can a finitely presented reason truly be infinite?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca82ac74-5416-4514-8e80-d95408680ff3

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Finite Infinitism](finite-infinitism-synthesis-850cc2ed-3e98-4cd8-ae6d-eaa04150a0f8.md)

## Content

**A reason chain that is finitely presented cannot in practical terms be infinite, as each reason must be expressible in finite terms.**, **Infinite justification implies a complexity that typically extends beyond finite presentation, challenging its coherence.**
