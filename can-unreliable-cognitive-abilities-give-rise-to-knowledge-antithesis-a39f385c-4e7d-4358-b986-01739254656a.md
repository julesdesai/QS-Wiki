# Can unreliable cognitive abilities give rise to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a39f385c-4e7d-4358-b986-01739254656a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person frequently arrives at true beliefs through an unreliable cognitive ability, such as superstitious reasoning, but occasionally forms a true belief that coincides with the truth. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the cognitive ability was not aligned with intellectual virtue, even though it occasionally results in truth.**, **It is implausible that knowledge, which requires intellectual virtue, could be derived from unreliable cognitive abilities, as this would not consistently lead to true beliefs.**
