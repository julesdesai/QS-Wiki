# Can an infinite regress provide genuine justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0ecb9268-b19f-49a2-8153-b12f24da2f5b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Coherentism](infinitist-coherentism-synthesis-c961c647-ce7e-4676-a333-4ee929ef8887.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should be achievable, at least in an ideal sense. An infinite regress of reasons may never be fully accomplished, making justification seem unattainable.**, **It is implausible that a process never concluded can offer real support or grounding to a belief. Infinite regresses, by their nature, cannot be completed.**
