# Does contextualism allow for too much latitude in defining 'truth'?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b769444d-36f9-4780-85fa-0ec616c65557
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist approach to knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-a36ecad8-b4cc-4b9e-8a50-84a5c5d3051f.md)

## Content

**If justification standards vary with context, what counts as a 'true' belief may shift as well.**, **This could imply that truth, like justification, is not stable but context-dependent.**, **Such a view undermines the traditional objective understanding of truth.**
