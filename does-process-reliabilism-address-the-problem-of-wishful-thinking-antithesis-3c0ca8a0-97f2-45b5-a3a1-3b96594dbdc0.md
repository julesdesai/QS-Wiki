# Does process reliabilism address the problem of wishful thinking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3c0ca8a0-97f2-45b5-a3a1-3b96594dbdc0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**Suppose a confirmed optimist, Laura, often experiences truth in her beliefs that 'things will work out' simply because in her environment, people rally to prove her right, establishing a 'reliable' process. Intuitively, we suspect her optimism isn't truly knowledge but possibly self-deceptive.**, **Wishful thinking under abnormal reliable conditions provides the right proportion of truth but lacks depth of understanding normally associated with knowledge.**, **The compatibility of such psychological biases within the framework of process reliabilism questions its robustness in the face of peculiar reliability.**
