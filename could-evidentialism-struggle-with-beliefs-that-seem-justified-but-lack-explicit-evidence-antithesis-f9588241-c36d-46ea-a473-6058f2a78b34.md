# Could Evidentialism struggle with beliefs that seem justified but lack explicit evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f9588241-c36d-46ea-a473-6058f2a78b34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism Summary](evidentialism-summary-synthesis-a76d832e-447a-4168-b9be-6c867a1a7af1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person inherently trusts their close friend’s honesty without specific evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the person's belief in their friend’s honesty is justified, even if not explicitly supported by identifiable evidence.**
