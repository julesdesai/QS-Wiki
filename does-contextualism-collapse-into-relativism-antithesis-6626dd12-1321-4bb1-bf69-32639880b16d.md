# Does contextualism collapse into relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6626dd12-1321-4bb1-bf69-32639880b16d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fc8174e2-112e-4e1b-9915-e4d5070db211.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which knowledge depends entirely on context, leading to conflicting truth claims by different individuals. Intuitively, this suggests a move toward relativism where truth itself becomes subject to varying personal perspectives.**, **There is a plausible principle that if standards for justification are set by context, then truth claims could be equally valid yet conflicting across contexts, undermining objective truth. This principle together with contextualism implies a potential collapse into relativism.**
