# Does sensitivity always distinguish knowledge from mere belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20f3b2de-4e4a-43c0-b99f-df12c05c6fba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds a belief that a coin will land heads after flipping it and it does land heads by chance. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, despite their belief being true.**, **In the coin flip scenario, the belief that the coin will land heads is sensitive because if it were false and the coin landed tails, the person would not hold the belief it landed heads.**
