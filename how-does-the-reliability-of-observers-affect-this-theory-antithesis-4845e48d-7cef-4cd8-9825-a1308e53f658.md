# How does the reliability of observers affect this theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4845e48d-7cef-4cd8-9825-a1308e53f658
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Intersubjective Phenomenalism](intersubjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-0a89d160-2bee-479f-b8d3-046de6da5ef6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which perceptual agreement is reached among observers with poor vision. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge their agreement might not lead to true knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge derived from reliable sources is more credible. This principle together with the variable reliability of observers suggests that intersubjective agreement may not guarantee knowledge.**
