# Can knowledge exist without intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c97e60a6-fc47-46cd-8330-ef7414477e71
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-01a05f11-5124-46fb-a178-b1b3f721bc68.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief that is true and believes it, but does so purely by accident, such as guessing. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite the truth and belief being present.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than just true belief; it requires justification or a reliable method of belief formation.**, **It is implausible that mere possession of virtues like open-mindedness and intellectual courage, without further reasons or evidence, necessarily leads to knowledge.**
