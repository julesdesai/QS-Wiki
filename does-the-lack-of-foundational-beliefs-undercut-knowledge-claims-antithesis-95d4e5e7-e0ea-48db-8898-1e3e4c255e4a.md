# Does the lack of foundational beliefs undercut knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95d4e5e7-e0ea-48db-8898-1e3e4c255e4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-c54e9ff1-39e1-4224-b3be-b0979177f411.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person believes a proposition to be true because of a chain of reasoning linked by beliefs, each formed by a distinct reliable process. If none of these beliefs is foundational, intuitively, we struggle to find an ultimate grounding for the whole chain.**, **Without foundational beliefs, there is a risk of an infinite regress of reliability checks, challenging the feasibility of achieving absolute justification or knowledge.**
