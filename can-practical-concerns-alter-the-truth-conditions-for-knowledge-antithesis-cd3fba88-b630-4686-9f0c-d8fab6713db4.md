# Can practical concerns alter the truth conditions for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cd3fba88-b630-4686-9f0c-d8fab6713db4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-f39134a9-444f-41a7-8dde-6cf57fa28070.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a doctor must decide quickly whether a patient needs surgery based purely on a glance at symptoms. The high stakes make the truth condition more sensitive and require higher scrutiny of knowledge judgments. Intuitively, we would judge that the stakes alter the appropriateness of asserting knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge's truth conditions should be objective and invariant to situational practical concerns. This principle together with varying stakes suggests that practical concerns should not change truth conditions.**
