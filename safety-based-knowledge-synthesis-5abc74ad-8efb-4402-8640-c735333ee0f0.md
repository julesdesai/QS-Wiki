**Parent:** [Can knowledge arise from an incorrect causal explanation?](can-knowledge-arise-from-an-incorrect-causal-explanation-antithesis-22f7ae3f-4235-4717-9b83-2c880f567e29.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that their belief could not easily have been false**, **A belief could not easily have been false if in nearby possible worlds, the person does not believe a falsehood**
