# Can justification be subjective and still result in genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 69f764ae-3719-426a-b0cf-557a0be5e6a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Justificationism](contextual-justificationism-synthesis-1b60e9a8-d801-480b-8adb-e726a7af0440.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person justifies their belief in a proposition based on local cultural standards; intuitively, this should not constitute universal knowledge if the justification is subjective.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must rest on intersubjectively valid justification, contradicting the idea that justification can vary with context.**
