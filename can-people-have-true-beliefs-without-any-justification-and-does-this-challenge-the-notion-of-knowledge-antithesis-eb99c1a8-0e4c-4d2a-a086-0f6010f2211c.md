# Can people have true beliefs without any justification, and does this challenge the notion of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eb99c1a8-0e4c-4d2a-a086-0f6010f2211c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-90a3baa8-9102-43d8-b9cc-d77493986c7c.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who dreams about a specific event occurring, and it later does occur exactly as in the dream. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows the event will occur, even if their belief was correct.**, **The existence of true beliefs that arise without any justification challenges whether a genuine connection to reality can account as knowledge, even if the belief is true.**
