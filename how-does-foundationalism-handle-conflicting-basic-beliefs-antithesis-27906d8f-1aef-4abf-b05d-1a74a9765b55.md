# How does foundationalism handle conflicting basic beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 27906d8f-1aef-4abf-b05d-1a74a9765b55
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as an alternative to Coherentism](foundationalism-as-an-alternative-to-coherentism-synthesis-459d7634-bf33-408f-b245-ac7a68c56f44.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two people with equally self-evident basic beliefs that contradict each other. Intuitively, at least one of these beliefs must be unjustified since they cannot both be true.**, **It is implausible that basic beliefs, which are supposed to be infallible or self-evident, can logically contradict each other and still both be justified.**, **Consider a scenario where one person believes a basic belief is self-evident due to cultural conditioning, while another from a different culture has a conflicting self-evident basic belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we must question the justification of at least one of these basic beliefs.**
