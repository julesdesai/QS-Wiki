# Does process reliabilism neglect the role of justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 775c53d1-8c08-4258-b1f8-a280da6f7b29
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-094c2ddc-bd9c-46c2-87b1-e486aaff8cb4.md)

## Content

**Consider a principle where knowledge requires justification in addition to truth and belief. Under process reliabilism, a belief is considered knowledge if it is reliably produced, potentially lacking individual justification.**, **Imagine a belief forming in a person due to a machine's manipulation, producing true beliefs. Without individual justification, this belief might lack the status of knowledge, even if true.**
