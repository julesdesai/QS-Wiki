# Can knowledge occur without the active application of a virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c1b09853-077f-4177-9fd9-3bd63d879c68
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-8faa0d3d-af6f-4054-8090-da1e790293ff.md)

## Content

**In certain cases, a person may have true belief without actively applying any identifiable epistemic virtue.**, **Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on a lucky guess, and the belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, this does not count as knowledge.**, **The requirement that knowledge involves the successful application of cognitive virtues seems to exclude cases where true belief is acquired without such application, even if by reliable means.**
