# Does reliability without consciousness align with our intuitions about knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f10bccf0-3e0b-4aec-8758-3e2e56a2108b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-094c2ddc-bd9c-46c2-87b1-e486aaff8cb4.md)

## Content

**A principle suggests that knowledge often involves a conscious awareness of the foundation of beliefs. If a cognitive process is reliable yet entirely subconscious, there may be a gap between reliability and knowledge.**, **Consider someone who accurately identifies colors while sleepwalking, guided by a reliable yet unconscious process. Intuitively, it seems debatable whether such beliefs qualify as knowledge.**
