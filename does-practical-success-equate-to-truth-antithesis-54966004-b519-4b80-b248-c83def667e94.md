# Does practical success equate to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 54966004-b519-4b80-b248-c83def667e94
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Adequacy](pragmatism-with-practical-adequacy-synthesis-7de50134-9521-4bfe-88f0-682c548d4774.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where an individual predicts the outcomes of complex events using methods with no basis in truth, such as astrology, and achieves accurate results by chance. In such a case, intuitively, we need to separate the truth from recurrent practical success achieved without proper justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is independent from practical success, and accurate predictions achieved by chance do not equate to truth. This principle alongside examples of accidental success suggests the need to differentiate between success by chance and truth.**
