# Can one have knowledge if direct perception is absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e28a47a0-396b-4ebe-b843-951944d02eff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Direct Realism](pragmatic-direct-realism-synthesis-4173acef-5348-4f34-a95b-b6ba164e9e84.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person P uses instruments to perceive ultraviolet light, which is not directly perceivable by human senses, and forms a true belief about it. Intuitively, one might still consider P to have knowledge despite the lack of direct perception.**, **A case involving indirect tool-assisted observations, resulting in correct predictions, suggests that indirect perception can still constitute knowledge.**
