# Knowledge as Formal Causation Theory

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does indirect belief formation invalidate knowledge?](does-indirect-belief-formation-invalidate-knowledge-antithesis-19b22ef1-2a08-46d9-8439-7c4bad646200.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the facts constituting the belief formally cause the fact that makes the proposition true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the facts constituting the belief formally cause the fact that makes the proposition true**
