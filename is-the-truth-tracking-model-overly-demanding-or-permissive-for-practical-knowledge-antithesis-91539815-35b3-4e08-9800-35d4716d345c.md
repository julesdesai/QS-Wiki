# Is the truth-tracking model overly demanding or permissive for practical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 91539815-35b3-4e08-9800-35d4716d345c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1b212c88-0c4c-4dd5-88f4-3ea31a716dd5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person commonly affirms a general proposition based on everyday reliable observation. Intuitively, either the belief satisfies stringent truth-tracking conditions without oppressive demands or it acknowledges simplicity without overgeneralization. If we demand strict conditions, practical common knowledge becomes elusive. If simplification is required, then the theory becomes too permissive.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves practical engagement without requiring exhaustive truth-tracking apparatus unnecessarily. This principle, coupled with common-sense approaches, challenges the wisdom of overly stringent truth-tracking requirements.**
