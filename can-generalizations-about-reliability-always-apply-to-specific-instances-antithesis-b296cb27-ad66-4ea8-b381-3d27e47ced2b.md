# Can generalizations about reliability always apply to specific instances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b296cb27-ad66-4ea8-b381-3d27e47ced2b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-b27c6d4b-ac60-4e95-8268-38e201ff9a4c.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a generalization about the reliability of a method can always justify a belief in a specific instance without exception.**, **A method could be generally reliable, but yield a false belief in a specific instance due to unique circumstances.**
