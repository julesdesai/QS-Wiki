# How might indirect realism deal with the problem of skepticism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b653517d-bfb5-4525-b2a7-d1fedc7dbab1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-801f6a7d-c909-434a-bdd9-effe8ebd5964.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where sensory representations are inherently fallible and not verifiable. Intuitively, this leads to doubt about the reliability of any knowledge claims based on perception.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires certainty or justified true belief. This principle together with the idea that perception is mediated by potentially inaccurate representations implies that skepticism about knowledge claims is inevitable.**
