# Is fallibilism inconsistent with knowledge requiring certainty?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2e3af5f5-ea95-41f6-84b0-968cf8a44dd8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Fallibilist Foundationalism](fallibilist-foundationalism-synthesis-ed3484e7-a0b3-47a4-939c-ec83c0f7bb7c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a true belief about the result of a dice roll based on a reliable source, but the source has a small, known chance of being wrong. In this scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person cannot be certain of their belief despite it being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a level of certainty that excludes the possibility of error. This principle, together with the fallibilist notion that foundational beliefs can be fallible yet generally reliable, entails that knowledge cannot be based on merely generally reliable beliefs without certainty.**
