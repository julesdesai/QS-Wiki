# Can the truth condition alone establish knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aed5ec3f-fd8f-430e-a145-56794071bd63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Externalism Summary](safety-condition-externalism-summary-synthesis-29bd2257-ee31-4256-8506-f8628368ab8d.md)

## Content

**Suppose a situation where a person believes a true proposition but only due to a lucky guess without any evidence. Intuitively, this does not seem to constitute knowledge.**, **A principle of epistemology is that knowledge requires more than mere truth; it also requires some form of justification or reason beyond luck.**, **It is implausible that truth alone is sufficient for knowledge, as luck does not equate with justification.**
