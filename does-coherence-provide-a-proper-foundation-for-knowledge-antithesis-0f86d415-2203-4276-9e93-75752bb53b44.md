# Does coherence provide a proper foundation for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0f86d415-2203-4276-9e93-75752bb53b44
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Non-Conscious Elements](coherentism-with-non-conscious-elements-synthesis-025108b6-66b5-4784-9945-6373af308ffa.md)

## Content

**A case in which all beliefs within a system are coherent with each other, but none of them are consciously accessed, challenges the idea that coherence alone provides a foundation for knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that such coherence does not lead to knowledge without conscious access.**, **There is a plausible principle that states knowledge requires not just coherence but an accessible understanding of the coherence.**
