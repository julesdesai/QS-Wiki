# Can a practically useful but false belief still be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8044830a-b100-4e05-9b01-207ca1ea6313
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Reliabilism](pragmatism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-504b62f9-b2fa-4831-be00-0daf59bd2488.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person falsely believes a harmless substance is toxic, leading them to avoid actual toxins excessively. Intuitively, usefulness of the false belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **A premise that associates knowledge strictly with truth contradicts the practicality of such a belief being considered knowledge.**
