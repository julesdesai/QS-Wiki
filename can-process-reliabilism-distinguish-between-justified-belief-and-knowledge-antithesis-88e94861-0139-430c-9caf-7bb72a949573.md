# Can process reliabilism distinguish between justified belief and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 88e94861-0139-430c-9caf-7bb72a949573
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-0b742bab-1646-43b8-9078-24258a3aaaf7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we consider the situation where Toby, an average person, feels pain and thus believes he is in pain. The feeling of pain is a process that could be seen as reliable for identifying one's own pain. Intuitively, being in pain and recognizing it seems like it should guarantee knowledge, yet process reliabilism does not clearly distinguish why not every belief formed in a reliable process qualifies as knowledge.**, **The belief-forming process, grounded in direct sensation, yields a high proportion of true beliefs about one's own mental states.**, **The inability to separate what is merely justified from what is known in such direct cases poses a problem for a process-oriented account.**
