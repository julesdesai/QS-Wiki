# Is practical success an unreliable guide for knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dc0e9595-54dd-4d89-9b1b-9a2cc4683406
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a novice chess player consistently wins games by relying on intuition rather than strategic knowledge. Intuitively, the player's victories do not imply knowledge of chess strategy, challenging the reliability of practical success as a guide for knowledge claims.**, **If practical success were always sufficient for knowledge, we would then be forced to attribute knowledge to beliefs that are not systematically reliable or repeatable, which seems implausible.**
