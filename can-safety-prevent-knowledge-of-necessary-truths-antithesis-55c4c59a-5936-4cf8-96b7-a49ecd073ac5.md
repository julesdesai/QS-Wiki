# Can safety prevent knowledge of necessary truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55c4c59a-5936-4cf8-96b7-a49ecd073ac5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Consider a mathematician who believes, through proof, in a necessary mathematical truth. In all possible worlds, this truth holds. If their belief doesn't stem from intellectual virtue but is safe, we intuitively accept it as knowledge.**, **It is plausible that safety applies differently to necessary truths, where the concept of nearby possible worlds becomes irrelevant due to the necessary nature of the truth. Thus, knowledge of necessary truths might challenge the conventional application of safety.**
