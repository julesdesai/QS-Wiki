# Can knowledge be purely context-dependent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 575a4721-9835-4170-b3d4-7d4e4a612a38
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology](contextualist-epistemology-synthesis-771a8ab1-0860-48e1-822c-9b3b2cda1c63.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people are in different contexts with varying knowledge standards. Intuitively, it seems strange that one person knows a proposition while other does not, despite both having the same truth and belief conditions.**, **It is implausible that knowledge can vary so significantly based solely on contextual factors without leading to relativism about truth.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person on vacation believes it will rain because they read it on a local website. Intuitively, it seems problematic if their knowledge claim is valid on vacation but not at home.**
