# Does every true belief from intellectual virtue constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a4718dc0-42ef-490d-85b3-c8f73b49033d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-40b3d691-a5bb-479c-ada3-1931521a86e5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person has a true belief that their friend is guilty of a crime based solely on a reliable ability to read body language. Intuitively, one may still hesitate to declare that as knowledge without more evidence.**, **The mere presence of intellectual virtue does not guarantee the absence of defeaters or additional considerations that can undermine the claim to knowledge.**, **True beliefs derived from intellectual virtue might still lack the full justification traditionally required for knowledge, indicating a potential gap in the concept.**
