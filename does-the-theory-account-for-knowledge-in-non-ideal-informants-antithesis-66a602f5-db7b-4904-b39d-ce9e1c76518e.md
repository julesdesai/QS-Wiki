# Does the theory account for knowledge in non-ideal informants?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 66a602f5-db7b-4904-b39d-ce9e1c76518e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1a215329-c53d-4dbe-9509-6b517d1bb8ad.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual is in an environment with misleading but authoritative misinformation, yet by chance holds a true belief due to personal evidence. Intuitively, this true belief appears not to satisfy knowledge due to the truth-tracking premise.**, **It is implausible that agents amid non-ideal environments who achieve true beliefs are necessarily excluded from knowledge.**
