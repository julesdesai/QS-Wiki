# How could an agent verify knowledge without internal access to their knowledge conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 44079bc7-8bf7-4ee1-857e-12f8e1374b79
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-a9c372ef-1ae7-460b-b26c-8a82dc6a1ee1.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an agent's belief meets external conditions for truth, but the agent has no way to internally verify these conditions.**, **In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the agent cannot distinguish between cases of true belief and lucky guesses.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be knowledge, the believer must be able to distinguish it from mere luck.**
