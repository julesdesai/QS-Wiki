# Can a lucky guess align with proper functionalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0ec2638-73fb-474e-ba95-bf90620b0c4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism](proper-functionalism-synthesis-252aa5eb-1857-4367-af60-24f7afe1c9be.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person makes a lucky guess that happens to be true, and their cognitive faculties are functioning according to a defective design plan that mistakenly supports such guesses. Intuitively, this is not a situation where the person knows the proposition despite true belief.**, **Lucky guesses that align with a malfunctioning design plan question whether proper functioning aimed at truth can account for knowledge, suggesting that more than proper functioning is required.**
