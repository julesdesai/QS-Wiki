# Is knowledge possible if beliefs are justified without being true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bde2680d-e4c4-423c-9e09-41bec0c1b370
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Coherentism](pragmatism-with-coherentism-synthesis-7996efa8-d25e-4d54-acc0-6469819e4077.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief is seen as justified due to being practical, yet it is not true. Intuitively, it is evident that such a justified belief cannot constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible that justification based on practicality alone can act as sufficient grounds for a belief to be considered knowledge without the involvement of truth.**
