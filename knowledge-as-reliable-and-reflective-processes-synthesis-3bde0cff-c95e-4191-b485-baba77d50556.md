# Knowledge as Reliable and Reflective Processes

**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Is reliability without awareness sufficient for knowledge?](is-reliability-without-awareness-sufficient-for-knowledge-antithesis-e7f1fc86-af1a-44e7-98ef-018fa7653f03.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they are aware of the reliability of their cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process, and the person is aware of this reliability**, **A reliable cognitive process is one that produces a high proportion of true beliefs over false ones**
