# Can externalism account for the internal perspective on justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b0849f0b-06a4-4efb-a509-8af72ddc20ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-9fb6627e-b63e-4d81-a81a-128eb1da4172.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification for belief requires awareness of qualifying conditions. This principle suggests that for an individual to be justified in holding a belief, they need to have some awareness or access to the conditions that justify it.**, **In cases where individuals are unaware of external factors that justify their beliefs, it is implausible that their beliefs are justified from their own perspective.**
