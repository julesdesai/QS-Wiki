# Is disjunctivism consistent with the continuity of perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 58e1ece8-9fd6-40dd-b0a7-b840df1f07d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-6c9ba281-6911-4c85-9826-b83f47b211cf.md)

## Content

**Consider an experience where a person watches a tree swaying. The transition between seeing the tree and imagining it swaying differently occurs smoothly.**, **The continuity in perceptual experience, whether veridical or hallucinatory, suggests a similarity that is not accounted for by asserting a fundamental difference.**, **If perceptual transitions are seamless between veridical and hallucinatory, then the claim of fundamental difference between them is problematic.**
