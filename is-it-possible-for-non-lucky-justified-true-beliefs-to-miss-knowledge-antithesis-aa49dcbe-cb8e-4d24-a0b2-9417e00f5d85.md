## Content

**Envision a situation where a scientist’s research on a phenomenon is accidentally validated by an unrelated process that aligns results but isn’t understood by the scientist. Even if the justification seems solid, it doesn’t result in knowledge.**, **Such a case shows that even non-lucky justified beliefs can fail to truly be knowledge without comprehensive understanding.**
