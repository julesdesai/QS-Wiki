## Content

**Knowledge comprises a disjunction between veridical perception and non-veridical cases**, **For a person to know a proposition, it must be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it must be believed**, **In veridical cases, the belief directly results from an accurate perception of the world**, **In non-veridical cases, what appears to be knowledge is merely a semblance**
