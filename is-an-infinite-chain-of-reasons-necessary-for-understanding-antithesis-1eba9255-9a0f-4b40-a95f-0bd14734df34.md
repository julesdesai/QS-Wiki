# Is an infinite chain of reasons necessary for understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1eba9255-9a0f-4b40-a95f-0bd14734df34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-db00e7c1-6a13-4ca5-8f09-6fae7cd29e0f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person reads a theorem in mathematics and understands it immediately. Intuitively, we would judge that having infinite reasons is not necessary for their understanding of the theorem.**, **It is implausible that understanding necessarily requires infinite justification, casting doubt on the necessity of Infinitism for comprehension.**
