# Does the theory adequately handle the problem of induction?

**Node ID:** 388e8ef1-793d-412e-839b-5d4afb73ca37
**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Reliable Belief Theory](knowledge-as-reliable-belief-theory-synthesis-0ce3523e-2d9b-49ae-a456-12121a68d7c6.md)

## Content

**Inductive reasoning processes are generally considered reliable, yet they can lead to conclusions that may be revised upon new evidence. Intuitively, this seems inconsistent with a fixed notion of knowledge.**, **The adaptability required in inductive reasoning seems at odds with the static properties that might be inferred from calling a process reliable.**
