# What if a person believes a proposition but lacks understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 113b84e9-94b8-40f2-a657-838c400ad409
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition but does not understand the proposition or its implications. Intuitively, we would judge that such a person does not truly know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that understanding is a critical component of knowing, which conflicts with the notion that belief alone is sufficient for knowledge.**
