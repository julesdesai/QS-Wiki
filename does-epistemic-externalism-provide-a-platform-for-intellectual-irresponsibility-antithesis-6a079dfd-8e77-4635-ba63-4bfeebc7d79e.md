# Does epistemic externalism provide a platform for intellectual irresponsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a079dfd-8e77-4635-ba63-4bfeebc7d79e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a belief is justified by external factors, but the person holding it behaves irresponsibly, ignoring vast counterevidence. Intuitively, we would judge that such carelessness disqualifies the belief from being knowledge.**, **Knowledge depends on external factors might permit irresponsibly held beliefs to qualify as knowledge.**, **It is unlikely that a theory endorsing beliefs held in disregard of counterevidence captures a robust understanding of knowledge.**
