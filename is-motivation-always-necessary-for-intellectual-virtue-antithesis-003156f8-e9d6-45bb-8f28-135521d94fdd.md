# Is motivation always necessary for intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 003156f8-e9d6-45bb-8f28-135521d94fdd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-c1013ff7-a681-40e2-8790-e6dea793296f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person develops a true belief through highly skilled analysis but lacks personal interest or motivation. In such a scenario, intuitively we might still credit the person with knowledge due to their expertise.**, **There is a plausible principle suggesting that motivation, while beneficial, may not be essential if cognitive skills themselves suffice for forming a true belief.**, **It is implausible that motivation is an indispensable component of intellectual virtues if expertise alone can account for belief formation.**
