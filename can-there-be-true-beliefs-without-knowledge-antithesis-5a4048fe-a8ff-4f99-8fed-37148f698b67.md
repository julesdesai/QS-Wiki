# Can there be true beliefs without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a4048fe-a8ff-4f99-8fed-37148f698b67
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalism](internalism-thesis-697c012e-ac77-4c5b-935c-0bf2d4d5ce83.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who has a lucky guess about the winner of a sports game without any evidence or reason. The person's belief turns out to be true, but intuitively, we would say that the person does not know who the winner is.**, **The idea that knowledge requires more than just true belief challenges the notion that merely having a true proposition understood by the knower suffices for knowledge.**
