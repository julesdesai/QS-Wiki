# Can cognitive faculties devoid of reliability criteria contribute to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 950ee6b2-d210-4b0e-bef8-2790f34d3898
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-23f2e823-6756-4d05-9cb6-b00f2dd07c18.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a situation where an unreliable cognitive faculty coincidentally produces a true belief about a specific event, such as correctly guessing a future outcome of a random event. Here, we might question if the reliability of the cognitive faculty is essential for considering the belief as knowledge.**, **A person's belief being the result of an unreliable cognitive faculty does not necessarily exclude the possibility of the belief being true and correctly justified.**
