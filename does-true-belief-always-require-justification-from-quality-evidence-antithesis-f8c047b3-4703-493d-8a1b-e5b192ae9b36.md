# Does true belief always require justification from quality evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8c047b3-4703-493d-8a1b-e5b192ae9b36
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-79c62cab-dbc0-4dae-ad91-54d5bcae1188.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms a belief about the weather based on a very unreliable source, but the belief turns out to be true. Intuitively, we might judge that the person does not have knowledge due to poor evidence quality.**, **A belief that happens to be true despite unreliable evidence suggests that true belief does not always require high-quality evidence for justification.**
