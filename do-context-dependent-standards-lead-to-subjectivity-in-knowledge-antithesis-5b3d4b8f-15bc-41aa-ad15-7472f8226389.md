# Do context-dependent standards lead to subjectivity in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b3d4b8f-15bc-41aa-ad15-7472f8226389
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-605b5e27-4923-4bc2-b3cf-30db710f9ba8.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is true belief that aligns with contextually variable standards of justification.**, **Contextual standards for justification allow different individuals to apply different standards based on personal understanding of the context.**, **Subjectivity in the application of standards can result in inconsistencies in what is considered knowledge across individuals.**
