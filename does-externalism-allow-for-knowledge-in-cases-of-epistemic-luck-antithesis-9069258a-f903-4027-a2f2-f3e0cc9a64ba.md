# Does externalism allow for knowledge in cases of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9069258a-f903-4027-a2f2-f3e0cc9a64ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Theory of Knowledge](externalist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-918f8091-da25-4453-bab0-a36fb277f3d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person's true belief results from luck, such as guessing correctly without any reliable process leading to the truth. Intuitively, we would not say that the person knows the proposition.**, **It is implausible that epistemic luck should allow for knowledge, as knowledge requires more than just accidentally true beliefs.**
