# Does individual perceptual variation challenge the inference of knowledge from objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dd63301a-746c-4db8-ad2b-9d3dafa20730
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people perceive the same event differently due to varying sensory inputs or attention. Intuitively, this makes the inference of objective knowledge from perceptual experience questionable.**, **It is implausible that knowledge claims about external objects remain valid if individual perceptual experiences vary significantly.**
