# Can the argument from hallucination refute Direct Realism's claim on hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 09549fc6-08af-4169-bf6c-d3412e346d1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-63f38132-cda9-42dc-9c71-38670cc01e77.md)

## Content

**Hallucinations are experiences where one perceives something without the presence of an external object.**, **In hallucinations, perceptions occur without corresponding external stimuli, resembling perception but without contact with external reality.**, **If hallucinations can mimic perceptions of reality, then it challenges the notion that direct perception always provides knowledge of external objects.**
