# Can context-dependence in pragmatic contextualism lead to contradictions in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1143e181-0260-4f3f-9b80-df59db73d43b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**Two people in different contexts may hold opposing beliefs about the same proposition, each achieving practical success, and thus, both would be considered to know the proposition.**, **This situation would suggest that conflicting beliefs could both qualify as knowledge solely due to varied contexts and practical success, which seems contradictory.**, **Traditional views of knowledge emphasize truth consistency, raising concerns when pragmatic contextualism allows for contradictory beliefs to be considered knowledge.**
