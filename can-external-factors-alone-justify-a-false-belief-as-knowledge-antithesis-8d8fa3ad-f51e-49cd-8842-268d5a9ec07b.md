# Can external factors alone justify a false belief as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8d8fa3ad-f51e-49cd-8842-268d5a9ec07b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person reads a scientific article stating a proposition P, which they believe, although P is later proven false. The article was based on inaccurate data but presented as legitimate evidence. Intuitively, the person was justified in their belief, yet they did not have knowledge.**, **It is implausible that external factors can justify a belief as knowledge when those factors are based on false or misleading information.**
