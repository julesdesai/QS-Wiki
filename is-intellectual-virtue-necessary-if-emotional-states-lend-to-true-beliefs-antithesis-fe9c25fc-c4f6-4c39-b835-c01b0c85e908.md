# Is intellectual virtue necessary if emotional states lend to true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe9c25fc-c4f6-4c39-b835-c01b0c85e908
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where emotions or feelings drive accurate beliefs, such as a person's gut feeling correctly predicting an event. Intuitively, we might say they know the truth via emotional insight or deny knowledge since it lacks virtue. This stresses intellectual virtue's necessity when alternate sources seem sufficient.**
