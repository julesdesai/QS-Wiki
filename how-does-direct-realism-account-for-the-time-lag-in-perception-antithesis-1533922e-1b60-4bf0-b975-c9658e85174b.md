# How does Direct Realism account for the time lag in perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1533922e-1b60-4bf0-b975-c9658e85174b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider an event like a distant star exploding, which we perceive many years after it occurs due to the time light takes to travel. Intuitively, we would judge that our perception is not direct because it involves a temporal delay from the external event.**, **A plausible principle is that direct access implies immediate knowledge of external objects. This principle together with the existence of perceptual time lags entails a mediated rather than direct perception.**
