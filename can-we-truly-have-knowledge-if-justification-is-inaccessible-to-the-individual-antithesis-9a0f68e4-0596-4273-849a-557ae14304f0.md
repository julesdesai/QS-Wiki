# Can we truly have knowledge if justification is inaccessible to the individual?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a0f68e4-0596-4273-849a-557ae14304f0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Knowledge without Belief](externalist-knowledge-without-belief-synthesis-c20b0285-a7bd-4197-bc3d-7153e215190d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that having justification requires the ability to access one’s reasons for believing something. This principle suggests that if justification is not accessible, we lack essential grounding for claiming knowledge.**, **In a case where someone knows a truth but cannot articulate any reason for it, intuitively, it would seem they lack full understanding or knowledge in a significant sense.**
