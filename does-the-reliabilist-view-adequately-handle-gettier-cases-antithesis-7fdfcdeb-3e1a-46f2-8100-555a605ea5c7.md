# Does the reliabilist view adequately handle Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7fdfcdeb-3e1a-46f2-8100-555a605ea5c7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with a finite chain of reasons](reliabilism-with-a-finite-chain-of-reasons-synthesis-a3f7a107-c974-4a8e-a7f2-d20f4743454a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person has a justified true belief that is true solely due to luck rather than the reliability of the cognitive process.**, **In such scenarios, the intuition is that even though the belief is true and justified, the person does not have knowledge.**, **If reliabilism cannot rule out knowledge in such cases, it might not sufficiently address the Gettier problem.**
