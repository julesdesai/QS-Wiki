# Do perceptual differences imply that reality might be subjective rather than objective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a1c51b6d-0c58-4487-a8d2-0fd14abce69e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-de08aecd-5e9b-4f9b-874a-d2dc97cb1697.md)

## Content

**Consider one person perceiving a sculpture as a rabbit while another perceives it as a duck. Intuitively, differing perceptions suggest subjectivity in interpretation.**, **If interpretations differ greatly, it implies that reality may not be objectively accessible.**, **The subjectivity present in differing perceptions calls into question the claim of an objective reality independent of our representations.**
