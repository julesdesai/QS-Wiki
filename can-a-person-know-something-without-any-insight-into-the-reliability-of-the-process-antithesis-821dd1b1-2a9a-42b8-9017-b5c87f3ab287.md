# Can a person know something without any insight into the reliability of the process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 821dd1b1-2a9a-42b8-9017-b5c87f3ab287
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-74a39329-93a9-479f-b8d3-ff182da0738f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a student who accurately solves a math problem by mechanically following a memorized algorithm, without understanding why the algorithm works. Intuitively, we question whether they genuinely know the solution.**, **The student's cognitive process is reliable and typically yields correct solutions, suggesting true belief.**, **However, the student's lack of insight into the process’s reliability questions the depth and authenticity of their knowledge.**, **Knowledge might require some understanding of what makes the belief-forming process reliable, which reliability theory may miss.**
