# Can coherence account for perceptual beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e92388bc-6def-4f05-9bc8-2b538e425da7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Justified Belief](coherentism-with-justified-belief-synthesis-01e10c3b-52a8-4097-b02c-3f010fc56355.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that perceptual beliefs are justified by immediate sensory experience rather than coherence with other beliefs.**, **Consider a scenario in which someone sees a tree in front of them and believes it is there. Intuitively, this belief is justified by seeing the tree rather than its coherence with other beliefs.**
