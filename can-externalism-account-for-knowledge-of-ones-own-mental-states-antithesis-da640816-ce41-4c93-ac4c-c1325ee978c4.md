# Can externalism account for knowledge of one's own mental states?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da640816-ce41-4c93-ac4c-c1325ee978c4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Foundationalism](externalist-foundationalism-synthesis-3e0224db-6723-4288-8a1b-8819cbac7570.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms beliefs about their own pain based on external observation rather than direct introspection. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that this method of forming beliefs about one's mental states is flawed, suggesting internal access is crucial for such knowledge.**
