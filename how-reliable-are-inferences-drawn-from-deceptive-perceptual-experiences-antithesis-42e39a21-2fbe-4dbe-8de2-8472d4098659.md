# How reliable are inferences drawn from deceptive perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 42e39a21-2fbe-4dbe-8de2-8472d4098659
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person experiences an optical illusion that appears to indicate movement where there is none. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the inferences about movement are unreliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that deceptive perceptual experiences do not provide a reliable foundation for inferential reasoning.**, **A person who relies on such inferences cannot have knowledge about the actual presence of movement.**
