# Can a person know a false proposition if they believe it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b71cbf79-df7c-4861-a097-927c5864deb4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth. This principle together with the idea that if a person believes a proposition, they know it, entails that people could know false propositions.**, **It is implausible that someone could know a false proposition because knowledge is typically thought to require truth.**
