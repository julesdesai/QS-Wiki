# Is purely practical perception sufficient for genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 65c1362c-679c-444f-ac39-93243fe3cc07
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perceptual Pragmatism Summary](perceptual-pragmatism-summary-synthesis-6450a6b1-86fa-4b81-99b4-df7ae7bc6aea.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge requires more than practical considerations. This principle suggests that perceptions based solely on practicality can be misleading.**, **Consider a scenario where a person perceives a dangerous animal as harmless due to its immediate usefulness. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not constitute knowledge of the animal’s nature.**
