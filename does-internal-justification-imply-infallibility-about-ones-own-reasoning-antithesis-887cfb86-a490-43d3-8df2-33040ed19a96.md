# Does internal justification imply infallibility about one's own reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 887cfb86-a490-43d3-8df2-33040ed19a96
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Coherentist-Internalist View](hybrid-coherentist-internalist-view-synthesis-ffa44d27-7377-4f6d-8451-515975b92108.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that being justified by internal reasons alone implies infallibility about the correctness of one's reasoning.**, **Consider a situation where a person internally reasons to a conclusion but makes a logical error. Intuitively, we cannot say the person has knowledge, even if they believe their reasoning is justified.**
