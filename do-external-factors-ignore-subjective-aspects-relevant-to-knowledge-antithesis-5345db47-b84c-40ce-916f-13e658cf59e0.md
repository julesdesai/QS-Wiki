# Do external factors ignore subjective aspects relevant to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5345db47-b84c-40ce-916f-13e658cf59e0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism about Justification](epistemic-externalism-about-justification-synthesis-ef2753e9-b07e-44a0-bb88-245ae31dae48.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person feels a strong emotion affecting their belief but is unable to articulate the feeling. Intuitively, the subjective experience contributes significantly to the person's belief justification, challenging the need for external factors.**, **There is a plausible principle that subjective and internal experiences considerably influence what counts as justified belief. This principle suggests that purely external justification might overlook essential subjective aspects.**
