# Does Coherentism adequately address cases of isolated beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d7517646-fcc9-436e-a2e7-dfaa555b2af9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism about Knowledge](coherentism-about-knowledge-synthesis-39987724-1a23-4547-a925-9e75bbe8b421.md)

## Content

**A coherent set of beliefs might include an isolated belief that is not connected to other beliefs in the system.**, **In a scenario where a person has such an isolated belief, intuitively this belief seems unjustified despite being part of a coherent set.**, **A belief that is not supported by evidence or other beliefs seems unjustified, challenging the notion that mutual support in a coherent system provides justification.**
