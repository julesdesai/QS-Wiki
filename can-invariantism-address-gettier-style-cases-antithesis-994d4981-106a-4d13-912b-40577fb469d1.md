# Can invariantism address Gettier-style cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 994d4981-106a-4d13-912b-40577fb469d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-2877eecc-8388-4e01-b87d-ffe7f20b2619.md)

## Content

**Gettier cases show situations where someone has a true and justified belief, but most agree they do not have knowledge.**, **Imagine someone who believes a true proposition based on a falsehood, such as mistaking a lifelike hologram for a real object.**, **Invariantism with fixed standards would fail to classify Gettier cases as non-knowledge, but intuitively they should not count as knowledge.**
