# Does the internalist view adequately handle the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a5e7db64-de1f-45f7-bb33-fe4562c65c35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Consider a Gettier case where a person has a justified true belief accidentally aligned with reality due to unexpected factors. Intuitively, this scenario challenges the idea that justified true belief suffices for knowledge.**, **Added conditions to justified true belief, such as the absence of defeaters, are not sufficient to address all Gettier-type issues where justification and truth align accidentally.**, **If defeaters cannot account for all Gettier cases, supplementing justified true belief with internal defeat conditions may be inadequate for capturing true knowledge.**
