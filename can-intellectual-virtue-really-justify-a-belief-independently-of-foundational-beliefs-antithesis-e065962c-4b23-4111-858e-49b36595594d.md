# Can intellectual virtue really justify a belief independently of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e065962c-4b23-4111-858e-49b36595594d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-460b628b-7c65-45e3-9b14-3e9060c40e11.md)

## Content

**It is plausible that knowledge must be structured, relying on foundational beliefs that underpin others, contrary to the idea that intellectual virtue can independently justify any belief.**, **Suppose we have a case in which an agent consistently uses intellectual virtues but is disconnected from foundational beliefs, yielding beliefs that conflict with widely accepted scientific facts. Intuitively, we would hesitate to consider these beliefs justified.**
