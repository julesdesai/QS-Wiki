# How does coherentism explain the necessity of empirical input?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 79c879fa-4fc8-4e3d-bf38-c5a4df403af3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Epistemology](coherentism-in-epistemology-synthesis-03124e37-be18-4868-b2cf-e370b5186105.md)

## Content

**A purely coherent system of beliefs can exist in absence of empirical input, as long as internal consistency and support are maintained.**, **Consider a case where a mathematician builds a set of abstract beliefs that do not rely on empirical observation.**, **It is implausible that such a system provides adequate grounds for knowledge about the empirical world, raising questions about the necessity of empirical input in justification.**
