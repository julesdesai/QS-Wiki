# Can we meaningfully distinguish between justified and unjustified beliefs if justification is infinitely deferred?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1eb4e077-8cc9-45ad-a9ac-1f04f840d0d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism in Epistemology](infinitism-in-epistemology-synthesis-3dfd51b6-00d8-4b2a-a651-5af96def07b3.md)

## Content

**A meaningful distinction between justified and unjustified beliefs requires some criteria of completion or adequacy.**, **Without a terminal point or finite understanding, it becomes unclear how one determines when a series of justifications is adequate.**, **If justification is infinitely deferred, then distinguishing epistemically between two beliefs becomes practically challenging.**
