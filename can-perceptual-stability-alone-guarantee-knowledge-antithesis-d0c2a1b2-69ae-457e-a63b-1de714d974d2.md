# Can perceptual stability alone guarantee knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0c2a1b2-69ae-457e-a63b-1de714d974d2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Stability View](phenomenal-stability-view-synthesis-fa414e6c-0cf2-4c6a-a8f2-9164813ceb3c.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person observes a stationary object and perceives it as red under normal lighting, but in unusual lighting the object appears green. Intuitively, stability in perception across different lighting conditions is not enough to claim knowledge that the object is red.**, **In a case where a person has stable perceptual experiences of an object as red across different times and contexts, it is still plausible that external factors like color blindness could undermine true knowledge of the object's color.**
