# Can virtue epistemology exclude all luck in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d963ebcf-6ea3-42c8-ace9-481f1628eecb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Anti-Luck Condition](virtue-epistemology-with-anti-luck-condition-synthesis-d2e613e4-56dd-4edb-a19a-a70d714eba0b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief about the time because they look at a stopped clock that by coincidence correctly displays the current time. Intuitively, this is not a case of knowledge, suggesting luck is present.**, **In the previous scenario, the presence of luck challenges the notion that intellectual virtues alone can eliminate epistemic luck.**
