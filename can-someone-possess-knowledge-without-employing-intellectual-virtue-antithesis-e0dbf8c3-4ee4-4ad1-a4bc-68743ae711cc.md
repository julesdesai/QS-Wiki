# Can someone possess knowledge without employing intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e0dbf8c3-4ee4-4ad1-a4bc-68743ae711cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if a person reliably forms true beliefs, they may possess knowledge. This principle together with the notion that intellectual virtues are necessary negates cases where reliable belief processes lack intellectual virtues.**, **Consider a scenario where a person uses a highly reliable but non-virtuous method to form true beliefs, like a gambler accurately predicting a dice roll repeatedly. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person knows the outcome despite not using intellectual virtue.**
