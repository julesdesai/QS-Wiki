# Can temporarily stable beliefs from unreliable processes count as knowledge?

**Node ID:** 4b741467-18fc-47ee-bce1-2d9943e8dd79

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Stable Belief](knowledge-as-stable-belief-synthesis-76e45399-845a-4199-bf57-b0cd04ba6e58.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone's stable belief arises from an unreliable process but temporarily aligns with truth. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not constitute knowledge, as reliability is missing.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliability of the process by which one arrives at a belief is crucial for knowledge.**
