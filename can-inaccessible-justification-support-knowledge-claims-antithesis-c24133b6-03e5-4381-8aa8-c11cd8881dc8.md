# Can inaccessible justification support knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c24133b6-03e5-4381-8aa8-c11cd8881dc8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-45579dd6-32de-43f3-ae7a-76b3bb7de09b.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based on evidence they cannot introspectively access. Intuitively, this would make us question whether the person truly has knowledge of the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires justification accessible to the individual. This principle together with the assumption that knowledge does not require accessible justification entails a contradiction in the conception of knowledge.**
