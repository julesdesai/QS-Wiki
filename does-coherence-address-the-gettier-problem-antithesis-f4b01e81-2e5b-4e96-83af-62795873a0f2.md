# Does coherence address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4b01e81-2e5b-4e96-83af-62795873a0f2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitist Coherentism](infinitist-coherentism-synthesis-c961c647-ce7e-4676-a333-4ee929ef8887.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where one's belief is both true and coherent but is held for reasons that are coincidentally correct, as in a classic Gettier case. Intuitively, this would not constitute knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that justification should distinguish between knowledge and accidentally true beliefs, which coherence may not adequately address.**
