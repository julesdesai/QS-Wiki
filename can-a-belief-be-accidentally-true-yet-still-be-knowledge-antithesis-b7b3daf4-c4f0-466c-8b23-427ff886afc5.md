# Can a belief be accidentally true yet still be knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b7b3daf4-c4f0-466c-8b23-427ff886afc5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-383ba069-e0f1-4291-bdc7-eacefc701040.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone flips a coin and believes it will land heads based purely on hope, and it does land heads. Intuitively, we would judge that such a belief being accidentally true does not constitute knowledge.**, **If a person holds a belief due to unreliable methods, even if the belief is true, the process's chance nature undermines it being considered genuine knowledge. This challenges the link between mere truth and reliability equating to knowledge.**
