# Is consensus necessary for knowledge if no one perceives the same experience equally?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca25d344-cb95-40fa-96e8-a65718db2746
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose individuals perceive colors differently due to variations in vision. In such instances, achieving a consensus on an objective experience becomes implausible due to subjective perception differences.**, **It is implausible to claim that knowledge relies on collective perception if perceptual differences exist. These differences challenge the ability to establish a shared perception as a basis for knowledge.**
