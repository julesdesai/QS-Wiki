# Is the Safety Principle for knowledge threatened by the problem of deception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5385deda-0087-4db5-bef2-4965525e6a9c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge with Safety Principle](knowledge-with-safety-principle-synthesis-36cb0228-2d5a-4aa3-87ba-01c1622dfdc5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person believes they are seeing a red apple, but in reality, they are deceived by a skillful illusionist. Intuitively, they do not know there is a red apple present.**, **The belief that a red apple is present does not track the truth across relevant possible situations if it is caused by an illusion.**, **In nearby possible worlds, the belief could easily have been false if the illusionist makes minor changes.**
