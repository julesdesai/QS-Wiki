# Does a hidden causal factor present a threat to the safety of belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 758ca14e-5b8b-4876-9194-67e56419026a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Imagine a farmer who regularly sees migrating birds at a lake and believes migrations occur at this time based on this recurring observation. Unbeknownst to them, a hidden cause like local climate has recently disrupted this pattern without their knowledge. Intuitively, we would not attribute knowledge in this situation given the break in pattern.**, **When hidden causes undermine an expected outcome like bird migrations, belief can become false in nearby possible worlds, questioning the belief's safety.**
