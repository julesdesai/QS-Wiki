# Is it possible to have knowledge without sufficient evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 438ff2a4-ead9-48f9-bd51-9f6cf789c9f9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a person can have strong intuitions about truths without having sufficient evidence.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person intuits a mathematical truth without formal proof. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person has knowledge of the truth.**, **A case in which a person firmly believes a proposition due to an intuitive grasp rather than evidence challenges the idea that knowledge requires sufficient evidence for belief.**
