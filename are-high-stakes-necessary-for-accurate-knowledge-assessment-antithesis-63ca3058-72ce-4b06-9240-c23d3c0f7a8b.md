# Are high stakes necessary for accurate knowledge assessment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 63ca3058-72ce-4b06-9240-c23d3c0f7a8b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Consider two identical scenarios where a person holds a belief justified by evidence, one with high stakes and one without. Intuitively, we would judge their knowledge status to be the same irrespective of the stakes involved.**, **There is a plausible principle that the evidence and truth are ample to assess knowledge, without requiring consideration of personal stakes.**, **It is implausible to claim that changes in external stakes can alter the intrinsic properties of knowledge, which depend on evidence and truth.**
