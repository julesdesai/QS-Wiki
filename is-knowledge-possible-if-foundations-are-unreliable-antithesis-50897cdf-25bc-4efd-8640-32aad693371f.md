# Is knowledge possible if foundations are unreliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 50897cdf-25bc-4efd-8640-32aad693371f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person holds a true belief justified by foundational beliefs that have a history of occasional inaccuracies. The person’s reliance on these beliefs casts doubt on the reliability of the knowledge claim despite the current belief being true.**, **A belief is justified if it rests on foundational beliefs that are reliable but not infallible, yet such reliance weakens the assurance typically required for knowledge.**
