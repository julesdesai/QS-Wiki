# Does the Gettier problem undermine the necessity of the truth requirement in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3614335-0ed5-45f4-877f-28479317c983
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-132e7866-a7f4-4b60-9b04-315848d6db8a.md)

## Content

**Consider a case similar to those proposed by Edmund Gettier, where a person has a justified true belief due to circumstances that make the belief true by accident rather than through an intellectual virtue. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge, even though the belief is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that suggests knowledge requires more than justified true belief due to scenarios like the Gettier problem, which suggests that intellectual virtue alone cannot resolve the issue without additional criteria.**
