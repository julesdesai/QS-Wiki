# Can belief's practical consequences always determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 063adf9f-9f97-4c90-b6aa-86f9829ea115
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism](pragmatism-synthesis-08f9dcda-197c-4176-877f-438b2d186f9d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes in a flat earth because it does not impact their daily life negatively. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge this belief not to be knowledge despite any practical utility it might have.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires truth, which a belief in a flat earth lacks.**
