**Parent:** [Does contextualism provide an adequate account of knowledge transmission?](does-contextualism-provide-an-adequate-account-of-knowledge-transmission-antithesis-d97f29ad-026d-46ce-9a77-82afece34922.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that minimal safeguarding standards of justification are met across contexts**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have minimal safeguarding justification, and the proposition is true**
