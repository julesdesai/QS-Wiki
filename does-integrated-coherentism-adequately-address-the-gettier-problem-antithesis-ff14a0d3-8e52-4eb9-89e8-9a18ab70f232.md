# Does Integrated Coherentism adequately address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Integrated Coherentism](integrated-coherentism-synthesis-026ba0da-a1c0-49d0-b737-4a448975b203.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone, Smith, believes the job candidate with ten coins in his pocket will get the job. Smith's belief is based on seeing the employer flip a coin and say they will hire whoever has ten coins. Unbeknownst to Smith, Jones, the other candidate with ten coins, gets hired for unrelated reasons. This is a scenario where Smith's belief is true and coherent but not knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires more than true belief and coherence. This principle implies that coherence alone cannot address Gettier style cases.**
