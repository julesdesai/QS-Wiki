# Do unperceived objects exist at all?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0069bb6c-c935-471e-be16-22566dac1772
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-ab29b70f-4a42-463b-b303-b38517d8aa22.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a case in which an object falls in a forest without anyone to perceive it or its effects. Intuitively, we have to say that the object existed there even though it was unperceived. If we say it doesn’t exist, that contradicts our experience that we can find its effects later, like a fallen log.**
