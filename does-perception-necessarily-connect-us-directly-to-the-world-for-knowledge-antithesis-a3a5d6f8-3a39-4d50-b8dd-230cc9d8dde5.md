# Does perception necessarily connect us directly to the world for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a3a5d6f8-3a39-4d50-b8dd-230cc9d8dde5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4b61ef48-0e05-458a-ab35-9c24d16d77b9.md)

## Content

**Imagine someone perceives a tree but receives this perception through a series of mirrors and cameras that accurately relay visual information to them. Despite the indirect interaction, we still claim they "see" the tree.**, **Perceptual evidence that leads to true belief about the external world does not always require a direct connection to the object of perception.**
