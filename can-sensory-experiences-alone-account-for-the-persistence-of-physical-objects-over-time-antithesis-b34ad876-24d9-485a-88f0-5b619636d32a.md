# Can sensory experiences alone account for the persistence of physical objects over time?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b34ad876-24d9-485a-88f0-5b619636d32a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual leaves a room with a chair inside and returns to find the chair in the same place. The intuition is that the existence of the chair persists independently of the sensory experiences**, **There is a plausible principle that objects have an existence and continuity that is not wholly contingent upon being perceived. This principle would suggest a need for more than sensory reductionism to account for continuity**
