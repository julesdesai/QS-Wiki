# Can misconceptions lead to true belief without genuine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 601e41f4-f3fb-4e69-8c92-0e6a35bae756
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialist Theory of Knowledge](evidentialist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-1034ea09-f6fc-4c03-a110-072015f4c221.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a false belief that coincidentally leads them to form a true belief about an unrelated proposition.**, **Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the proposition or that they do not. If we say they know, then it challenges the requirement for evidence for knowledge. If we say they do not know, then a true belief based on coincidence contradicts the evidentialist view.**
