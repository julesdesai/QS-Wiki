# Can nearby possible worlds ever be irrelevant to the assessment of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ebf97ed8-7050-470c-99b9-785519cb1a8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a mathematician deduces a true theorem and believes it, but in a nearby world makes an accidental calculation error leading to a false belief. In such a scenario, intuitively we might still judge that the mathematician knows the theorem in the actual world due to reasoning accuracy.**, **It is implausible that the existence of errors in such nearby worlds diminishes the mathematician's knowledge, as knowledge seems to hinge on actual deductive accuracy, not hypothetical mistakes.**
