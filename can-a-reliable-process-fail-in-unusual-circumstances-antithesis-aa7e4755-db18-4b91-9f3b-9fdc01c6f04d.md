# Can a reliable process fail in unusual circumstances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aa7e4755-db18-4b91-9f3b-9fdc01c6f04d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Process Reliabilism](knowledge-as-process-reliabilism-synthesis-85beaa94-5271-4ac5-82e2-8d29f5dec194.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that a cognitive process can be generally reliable but fail in certain unusual circumstances. This principle together with the idea of process reliabilism entails that someone might lack knowledge even when their belief arises from a reliable process, if the circumstances are outliers.**
