# Is the requirement of belief sensitivity too demanding for practical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 20c5a87c-e2a8-412a-bccd-ccf464489086
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Consider the case of a person, Irene, who forms generally accurate beliefs through ordinary means. Her method does not involve constant verification, yet intuitively we accept that she possesses knowledge of everyday truths.**, **There is a plausible principle that practical knowledge is unreasonably restricted by imposing constant sensitivity to the truth. Practical knowledge acknowledges the reality of forming beliefs through standard, rather than always scrutinizing, methods.**
