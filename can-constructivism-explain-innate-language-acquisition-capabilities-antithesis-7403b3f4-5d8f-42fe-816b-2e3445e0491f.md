## Content

**Suppose we consider the case of children acquiring language patterns naturally and systematically across different cultures, despite varying perceptual experiences and cognitive development. Intuitively, this suggests that certain aspects of language knowledge may be innate and not entirely constructed from perceived and cognitive interactions.**
