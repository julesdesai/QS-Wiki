# Does knowledge really vary with context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67b68f7e-6ddf-4ca9-a261-16896c685479
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-ed8715f4-7106-486f-8992-736e64ae52d2.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that the truth value of knowledge claims is invariant across contexts. This principle together with the idea of contextual variability in knowledge entails inconsistent knowledge claims can be true.**, **It is implausible that the same proposition can be known in one context and not known in another given identical evidence.**
