# Is truth alone insufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a4821fa-9e32-495c-9e65-ca1bebc03a85
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Virtue Epistemology](pragmatism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-118b1e93-c300-45eb-b419-aeebe3edd4e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person reads an accurate weather report but misinterprets it due to hasty reading. Their resulting belief happens to be true. Intuitively, this does not seem like knowledge due to the improper basis for the belief.**, **There is a plausible principle stating that knowledge requires true belief with appropriate justification rather than accidental correctness. This implies truth alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
