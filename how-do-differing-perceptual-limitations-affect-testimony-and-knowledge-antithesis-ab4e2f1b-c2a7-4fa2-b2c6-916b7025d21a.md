# How do differing perceptual limitations affect testimony and knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab4e2f1b-c2a7-4fa2-b2c6-916b7025d21a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which one person is colorblind and another is not, and both provide testimony about the color of an object. Intuitively, we either have to say that one testimony is incorrect, or that the knowledge acquired is limited by perceptual discrepancies. If we say one testimony is incorrect, then not all testimony can be reliable. If the knowledge is limited, then it cannot fully account for shared perceptual experiences.**
