# Can perceptual beliefs be justified without coherence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b30dc10e-32fd-4663-99dd-bba87cf38960
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism Summary](coherentism-summary-synthesis-14364920-f24e-48ed-b988-c7d3265113e2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person experiences immediate perceptual awareness, such as feeling pain when touching a hot surface. Intuitively, the person seems justified in believing they are in pain without reference to other beliefs.**, **It is implausible that justification for perceptual beliefs requires coherence with other beliefs, as they seem justified in virtue of direct sensory experience.**
