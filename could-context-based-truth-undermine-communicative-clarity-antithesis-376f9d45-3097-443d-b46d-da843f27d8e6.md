# Could context-based truth undermine communicative clarity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 376f9d45-3097-443d-b46d-da843f27d8e6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-7bcec917-3cbe-434b-9e35-2ffc56cc7b2f.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have two speakers communicating a knowledge claim in different contexts that dictate different truth conditions. Intuitively, we would judge that this leads to misunderstanding or lack of communicative clarity.**, **Effective communication implies shared and consistent understanding of knowledge claims. If context can alter truth conditions, then communicative clarity may be undermined when contexts differ.**
