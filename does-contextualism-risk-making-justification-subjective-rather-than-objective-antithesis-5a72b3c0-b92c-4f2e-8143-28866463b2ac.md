# Does contextualism risk making justification subjective rather than objective?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5a72b3c0-b92c-4f2e-8143-28866463b2ac
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-55297f9c-50a2-4f24-b1b0-6029f7bef7b2.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person's mood or temporary psychological state constitutes part of the context may lead to a subjective variation in the perceived adequacy of justification. Intuitively, this suggests that knowledge might be dependent on subjective factors that vary significantly.**, **It is implausible to claim that knowledge, meant to be objective, could be grounded on someone's personal, subjective context, undermining its reliability.**
