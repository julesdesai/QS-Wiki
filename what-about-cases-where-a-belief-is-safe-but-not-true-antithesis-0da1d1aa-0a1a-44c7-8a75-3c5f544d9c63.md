# What about cases where a belief is safe but not true?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0da1d1aa-0a1a-44c7-8a75-3c5f544d9c63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes a proposition that is not true, yet their belief would not easily be false in similar situations because those situations also involve the same falsehood. Intuitively, one might agree that such a belief is not knowledge.**, **For someone to have knowledge, the believed proposition must actually be true. A belief that is false, regardless of its safety in similar situations, does not satisfy the truth condition.**
