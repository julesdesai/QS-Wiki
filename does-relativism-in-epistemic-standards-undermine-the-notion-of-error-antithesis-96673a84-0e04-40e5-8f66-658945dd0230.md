# Does relativism in epistemic standards undermine the notion of error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 96673a84-0e04-40e5-8f66-658945dd0230
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person claims to know a proposition that is demonstrably false according to a more reliable epistemic standard. If standards are personal, intuitively, it becomes challenging to identify and correct errors.**, **A plausible principle is that an objective mechanism for identifying errors relies on universal or intersubjectively accessible standards. Without these, error correction is undermined.**, **If there are no universal standards to identify epistemic errors, it is implausible to maintain a coherent notion of error which is essential for knowledge development.**
