# Can disjunctivism handle the problem of fallible perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 17c147fa-1a85-4467-ad18-631ff7903275
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4a1da673-a89c-4ba0-b895-eeed98d21cef.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a witness observes an incident under poor lighting and identifies someone they believe to see clearly, yet they are mistaken. Intuitively, this person feels justified about their identification.**, **If perceptual experiences only justify beliefs when veridically connected, then the witness’s mistaken belief lacks justification.**, **It seems implausible for a fallible yet sincere perception to offer no justification at all.**
