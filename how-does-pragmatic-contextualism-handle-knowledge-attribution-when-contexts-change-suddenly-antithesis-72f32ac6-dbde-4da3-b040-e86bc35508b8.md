# How does pragmatic contextualism handle knowledge attribution when contexts change suddenly?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 72f32ac6-dbde-4da3-b040-e86bc35508b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Contextualism](pragmatic-contextualism-synthesis-eb83c85f-dd41-4bfc-8e5a-553d55565568.md)

## Content

**A person may hold a belief that is considered knowledge in one context but loses its status if the context shifts abruptly, even if the belief remains true.**, **Sudden changes in context may undermine the stability of knowledge attribution, making it contingent on factors other than the believer's cognitive state.**, **This shift suggests that knowledge should not be so easily influenced by external contextual changes when the true value of the belief remains constant.**
