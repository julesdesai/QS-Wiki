# Does perception always provide reliable access to reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6110a81b-6c11-4ea3-ab11-94520b741f05
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that veridical perception requires reliable mechanisms. This principle together with perception providing immediate access to external reality entails that perception must be infallible.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person sees a straight stick partially submerged in water as bent. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception can be misleading.**
