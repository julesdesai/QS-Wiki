# Can contextualism account for disagreements between different contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 806fdc78-4e0b-4b58-b24e-3ef43279f583
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-8d92b68a-d96e-49d3-9b29-39a25430afac.md)

## Content

**Consider two individuals from differing contexts discussing the same proposition but reaching different justification standards about its truth. Intuitively, it seems this might inhibit meaningful epistemic discourse.**, **A situation where disagreement arises solely from context-based justification challenges the idea of meaningful interaction and understanding across different contexts.**
