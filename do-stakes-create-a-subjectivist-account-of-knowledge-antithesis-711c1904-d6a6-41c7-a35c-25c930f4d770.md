# Do stakes create a subjectivist account of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 711c1904-d6a6-41c7-a35c-25c930f4d770
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-a38d2af6-3ec4-421c-a558-c594d0cbc8b7.md)

## Content

**If knowledge requires meeting personal stakes, then two individuals may know differently based on subjective stakes rather than objective truth. Intuitively, such subjectivism contrasts with the idea of knowledge as an objective fact.**, **A principle of knowledge as an objective state, independent of subjective stakes, suggests that knowledge should not vary with personal stakes, which may lead to relativism.**
