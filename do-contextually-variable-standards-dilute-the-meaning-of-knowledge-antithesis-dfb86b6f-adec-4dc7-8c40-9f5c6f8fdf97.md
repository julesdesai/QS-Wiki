# Do contextually variable standards dilute the meaning of 'knowledge'?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dfb86b6f-adec-4dc7-8c40-9f5c6f8fdf97
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory](contextualist-theory-synthesis-57f2b09b-3c03-4991-b6b3-1cfd3022a37a.md)

## Content

**Imagine varied discourse contexts with differing standards, leading to the term 'knowledge' being applied inconsistently to the same proposition. Intuitively, this dilutes the meaningfulness or significance of the term 'knowledge'.**, **A semantic principle maintains that meaningful terms should have consistent application across instances. With context-specific standards, the term 'knowledge' risks losing its stable meaning and shared understanding.**
