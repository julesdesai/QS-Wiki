# Does knowledge require counterfactual sensitivity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fadf3e49-e67b-4227-952b-ed13f7d115bd
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Justified True Belief](knowledge-as-justified-true-belief-thesis-6b1a1e1e-c03c-4585-b27d-30f3ad56177c.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle suggesting that knowledge requires sensitivity to counterfactual situations where the proposition would not be believed if false.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition that remains true in all nearby situations. Intuitively, we would judge this does not constitute knowledge if the belief persists even when the proposition would not be true in some situations.**
