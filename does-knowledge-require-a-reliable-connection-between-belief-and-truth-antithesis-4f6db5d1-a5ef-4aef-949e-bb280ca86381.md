# Does knowledge require a reliable connection between belief and truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f6db5d1-a5ef-4aef-949e-bb280ca86381
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-50ed68ac-8c7e-4392-b286-f68915df73b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone, Bob, consistently forms beliefs using a method that is generally unreliable, but in this instance, leads to a true belief. Intuitively, we would say that Bob does not have knowledge despite his true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a reliable method of forming beliefs. A method that only occasionally leads to true beliefs, or leads to them by chance, does not seem to confer knowledge.**
