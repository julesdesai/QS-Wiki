# Does externalist reliabilism sufficiently account for the relevance of evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ae2c50e-13e0-46c4-9810-cb19e04e052b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has ample evidence against the truth of a belief formed by a reliable process, yet continues to hold onto the belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the presence of such counterevidence undermines the justification provided by reliability alone.**, **There is a plausible principle that evidence must play a crucial role in determining whether a belief is justified, beyond the mere reliability of the process.**
