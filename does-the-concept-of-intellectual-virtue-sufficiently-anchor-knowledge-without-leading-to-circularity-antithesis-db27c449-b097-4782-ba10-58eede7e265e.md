# Does the concept of intellectual virtue sufficiently anchor knowledge without leading to circularity?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db27c449-b097-4782-ba10-58eede7e265e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Virtue Contextualism](externalist-virtue-contextualism-synthesis-69e2f760-46ad-465f-a31e-67e150de071d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that claims must be informative and avoid assuming what they attempt to prove.**, **Evaluating intellectual virtue by the context could involve using contextual standards to determine what counts as intellectual virtue.**, **Using contextual standards to determine what counts as intellectual virtue might involve assuming that those standards are justified, which could be circular.**
