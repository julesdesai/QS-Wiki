# Does contextualism create a problematic subjectivity in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0a0631f0-90f1-4717-8383-d669b3193d80
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-f4c5fe35-58d0-4960-a84a-81d2bc69d122.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two individuals claim knowledge of the same fact in the same situation, but based on differing personal contextual criteria. Intuitively, such subjectivity seems to undermine the objective nature of knowledge claims.**, **It is implausible that knowledge claims can maintain objectivity if they fundamentally depend on subjective or context-specific criteria.**
