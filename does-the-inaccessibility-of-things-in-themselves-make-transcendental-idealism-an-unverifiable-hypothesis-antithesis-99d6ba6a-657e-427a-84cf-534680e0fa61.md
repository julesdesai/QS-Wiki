# Does the inaccessibility of things-in-themselves make transcendental idealism an unverifiable hypothesis?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 99d6ba6a-657e-427a-84cf-534680e0fa61
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a theoretical view posits the existence of entities that are inherently unknowable. Intuitively, this either means that such entities impart no evidence of their existence or they cannot play any role in our epistemic framework. If they impart no evidence, then we cannot have any reasons to assert their existence. If they cannot play a role in our epistemic framework, then the assertion of their existence becomes epistemically irrelevant.**
