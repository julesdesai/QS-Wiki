# Do self-justifying beliefs effectively halt the infinite regress without requiring an appeal to circular reasoning?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5fa2df47-96d2-4c90-867d-bdb1b93fc15e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism with Basic Belief Justification](foundationalism-with-basic-belief-justification-synthesis-36983d87-c369-411c-807c-6c854afc89c3.md)

## Content

**Consider the scenario where a belief is declared self-justifying, such as "I am in pain." Intuitively, just declaring the belief self-justifying seems to halt the regress.**, **It is implausible that simply labeling a belief as self-justifying, without demonstrating why it does not involve circular reasoning, effectively halts the regress.**
