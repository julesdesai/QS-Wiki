# Can naturalized epistemology adequately account for traditional epistemic norms?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60d97f99-00e4-4f61-a4db-7ec61df71753
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification requires more than merely believing a true proposition. This principle together with the claim that a person knows a proposition if they believe it and it is true entails contradictions with traditional epistemic notions.**, **Suppose we have a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based on a lucky guess. Intuitively, we would say that they do not know the proposition despite it being true and them believing it.**, **It is implausible that natural scientific processes can fully capture the normative aspects of justification.**
