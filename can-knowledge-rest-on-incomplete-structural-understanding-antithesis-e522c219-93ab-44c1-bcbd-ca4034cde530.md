# Can knowledge rest on incomplete structural understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e522c219-93ab-44c1-bcbd-ca4034cde530
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Structural Realism](epistemic-structural-realism-synthesis-4cc8fed2-62df-47a3-af1a-0c0e49fc23b5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a vague understanding of the solar system's structure but lacks precise data about the planets. Intuitively, we would judge that such incomplete understanding falls short of full knowledge.**, **A plausible principle is that complete knowledge requires both structural understanding and detailed facts. If this principle holds, then structural understanding alone might not suffice for knowledge.**
