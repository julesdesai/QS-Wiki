# Can belief formed via reliable processes that lack reflexivity constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7830839-a34c-407a-b505-4cf010163671
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Expanded Virtue Reliabilism](expanded-virtue-reliabilism-synthesis-be1f6636-aeca-4898-8b76-a37fb9c31f3b.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a belief is produced reliably through a process that the agent never reflects upon or critically assesses. Intuitively, we would have to say that critical reflection is a component of intellectual virtues, yet it is absent in this reliable process.**, **It is implausible that reliable processes without reflexivity fulfill the requirement of intellectual virtues necessary for knowledge.**
