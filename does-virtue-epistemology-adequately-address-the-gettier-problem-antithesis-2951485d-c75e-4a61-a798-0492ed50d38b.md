# Does virtue epistemology adequately address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2951485d-c75e-4a61-a798-0492ed50d38b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person has a justified true belief based on evidence that is generally reliable, but unknown to them, the evidence is, in this instance, misleading, and their belief is true due to an unrelated piece of evidence. Intuitively, we judge that they do not truly have knowledge in this case.**, **Being justified in one's belief and having the belief be true due to unrelated factors does not align with knowledge arising from intellectual virtues.**
