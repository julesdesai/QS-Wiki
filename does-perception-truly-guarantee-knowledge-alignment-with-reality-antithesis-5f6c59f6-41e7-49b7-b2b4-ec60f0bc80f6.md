# Does perception truly guarantee knowledge alignment with reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f6c59f6-41e7-49b7-b2b4-ec60f0bc80f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-b8a53bd2-d0b8-44ba-a658-8cb1f890a45a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a perception aligns with a cultural myth believed in a specific context rather than with objective reality. Intuitively, we might judge that such perception does not qualify as knowledge if it aligns with myth rather than reality.**, **A plausible principle is that for perception to lead to knowledge, it must correlate with objective truths rather than subjective myths. This principle suggests that contextualism may inadequately differentiate between myth and knowledge when both seem equally valid in a given context.**
