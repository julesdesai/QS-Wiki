# Can knowledge be undermined by a lucky coincidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6228aa7-64cc-4b8b-ac72-7f8864972ec8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist View](reliabilist-view-synthesis-9b4935ff-e1a0-4d23-aeca-7dfa716ad7fb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a lottery ticket will lose because the odds are low, and it indeed loses. Intuitively, the person does not have knowledge even though their belief is true and based on probabilities.**, **A belief produced by assuming low odds is a belief generated by a reliable process in the sense that odds often align with outcomes, suggesting general reliability in probability-based reasoning.**
