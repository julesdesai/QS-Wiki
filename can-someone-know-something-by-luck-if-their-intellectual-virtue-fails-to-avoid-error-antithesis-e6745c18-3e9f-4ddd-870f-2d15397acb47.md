# Can someone know something by luck if their intellectual virtue fails to avoid error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e6745c18-3e9f-4ddd-870f-2d15397acb47
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Enhanced Virtue Reliabilism with Error Avoidance](enhanced-virtue-reliabilism-with-error-avoidance-synthesis-bae843f7-47b1-4791-af4f-a08b903c892d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief by guessing. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires avoiding error through reliability. This principle together with the success due to guessing entails that guessing does not count as knowledge.**
