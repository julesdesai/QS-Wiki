# Is sensitivity too restrictive for contingent truths?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c55ce1ed-083d-481b-908e-a6d3f43bf48c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-c636dae2-8d6f-478b-827f-65895cd50362.md)

## Content

**For beliefs about contingent truths, nearby possible worlds may differ significantly, making it difficult for the belief to track the truth consistently.**, **Consider a scenario where a person believes a friend will attend a party based on a recent conversation, and this is true in the actual world.**, **In a nearby world where the friend unexpectedly changes plans, the belief would not track the truth, yet we might still consider it knowledge given the supporting evidence.**
