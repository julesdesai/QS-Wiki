# How does contextualist epistemology handle the principle of closure under known entailment?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4604dd92-2c80-4dcb-8fd7-2d9902115e09
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**Suppose one knows a proposition P and knows that P entails Q, but contextually might not know Q. Intuitively, this challenges the closure principle, which suggests that if we know P and P entails Q, we should know Q.**, **It is implausible for a context-dependent view to account for closure without creating contradictions in knowledge claims.**, **Closure under known entailment is an essential feature of a coherent theory of knowledge, conflicting with contextual variability.**
