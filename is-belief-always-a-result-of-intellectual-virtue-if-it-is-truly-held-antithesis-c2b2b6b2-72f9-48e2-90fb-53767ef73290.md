# Is belief always a result of intellectual virtue if it is truly held?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c2b2b6b2-72f9-48e2-90fb-53767ef73290
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-72984f03-1ce3-46fd-9fd6-13203e340ace.md)

## Content

**A person may have a true belief about a proposition due to a testimony they trust. It is implausible to assume this true belief solely arises from the exercise of an intellectual virtue on the part of the believer.**, **Sometimes true beliefs are adopted based on the credibility of the source rather than the intellectual virtues of the person holding the belief.**
