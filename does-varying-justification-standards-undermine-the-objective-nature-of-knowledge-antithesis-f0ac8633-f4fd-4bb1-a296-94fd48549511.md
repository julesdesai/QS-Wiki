# Does varying justification standards undermine the objective nature of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0ac8633-f4fd-4bb1-a296-94fd48549511
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Variable Justification](contextualism-with-variable-justification-synthesis-53c5a1dc-1391-4651-950b-f21901e771b6.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be an objective status independent of subjective factors. This principle together with varying justification standards entails that knowledge becomes subjective rather than objective.**, **Consider a scenario where changes in conversational context lead to changes in whether a belief counts as knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge should not depend on conversational circumstances or perceptions.**
