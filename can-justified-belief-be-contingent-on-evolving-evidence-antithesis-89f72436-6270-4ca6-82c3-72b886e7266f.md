# Can justified belief be contingent on evolving evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 89f72436-6270-4ca6-82c3-72b886e7266f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario in which a person believes a political candidate will win based on early poll results. As new polling data emerges, contradicting the initial results, the person's belief remains unchanged. Intuitively, we might find their belief less justified due to the evolving evidence.**, **A plausible principle is that justified beliefs should adapt to new evidence, which challenges the static nature of evidence alignment for justification.**
