# Can knowledge exist without considering context-sensitive truth conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8bbe264-2eb5-4126-860f-12140e1dc9ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Knowledge View](contextualist-knowledge-view-synthesis-6f4516f3-106e-45ee-95d6-c69e7d21cad0.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should be based on stable and consistent truth conditions rather than context-sensitive ones.**, **This principle together with varying contexts create scenarios where truth conditions fluctuate unpredictably.**, **Fluctuating conditions challenge the idea that they can serve as the basis for what suffices for knowledge.**
