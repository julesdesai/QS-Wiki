# Can perceptual errors invalidate the necessity of causal connections for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d897414e-662e-4d62-abb4-ea98affa553d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person looks at a distant barn and believes it is a real barn, despite the fact that the area is full of deceptive barn facades that appear identical from a distance. Intuitively, if we discover that the specific barn they looked at is real, we hesitate to attribute knowledge due to the presence of potential perceptual error.**, **It is implausible that the necessity of a causal connection can account for knowledge in scenarios with high potential for perceptual deception.**
