# How does the regress problem challenge infinitism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d4e1357c-679a-451e-8ecb-1725b2a36d94
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism View](infinitism-view-synthesis-bddda30f-213c-4b95-bb22-45afdb1e886a.md)

## Content

**The regress problem in epistemology involves the requirement for every reason to be justified by another, leading to an endless chain.**, **An endless chain of finite justifications seems practically unsustainable and unresolvable.**, **As each reason in the chain requires another reason ad infinitum, justification doesn't conclude, rendering beliefs seemingly unjustifiable in practical terms.**
