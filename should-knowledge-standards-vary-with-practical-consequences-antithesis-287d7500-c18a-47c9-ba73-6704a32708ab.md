# Should knowledge standards vary with practical consequences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 287d7500-c18a-47c9-ba73-6704a32708ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-98e3a79c-8fed-46a8-a97f-357c8246f324.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is told with evidence that they are about to miss their bus. If they miss it, they face missing an essential interview. Intuitively, the knowledge required seems heightened due to the importance of catching the bus.**, **Contrast this with finding out about the bus when no consequence follows missing it, suggesting knowledge should change minimally.**, **It is implausible to claim practical consequences alone determine the shifts in knowledge standards when the evidence remains constant.**
