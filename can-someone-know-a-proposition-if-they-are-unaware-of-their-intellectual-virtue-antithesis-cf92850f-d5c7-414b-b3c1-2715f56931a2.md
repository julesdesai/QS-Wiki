# Can someone know a proposition if they are unaware of their intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cf92850f-d5c7-414b-b3c1-2715f56931a2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Virtue Epistemology](reliabilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-a48fe91b-846b-4740-a801-397d1be1c53f.md)

## Content

**If a person holds a belief without awareness of their intellectual virtue, it is possible their belief might arise from a reliable process without them knowing it.**, **Intuitively, not knowing one’s own intellectual virtue challenges the idea that true belief constitutes knowledge.**
