# Do perceptual errors imply non-direct awareness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 194f069c-9dc5-4b02-aa48-c12eb1ea6901
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naive Realism with Direct Cross-Modal Sensing](naive-realism-with-direct-cross-modal-sensing-synthesis-96f2c374-253a-4f49-a9c2-1ee8405679ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person mistakes a rope on the ground for a snake. Intuitively, we would judge that the awareness of the object is not direct because it leads to an error in recognition.**, **It is implausible that direct awareness would consistently result in perceptual errors about objects.**
