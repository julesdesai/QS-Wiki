# Can there be knowledge without belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c4e8ce99-d6e4-466a-a3d4-a5ebfd754238
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-1814de97-222c-437f-bd26-a9ce4edff4f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief that results from a reliable process but does not consciously endorse or accept the belief. Intuitively, we might judge that the person possesses knowledge despite the lack of conscious belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge can exist independently of the agent's conscious endorsement of a belief. This principle suggests that knowledge might not require explicit belief.**
