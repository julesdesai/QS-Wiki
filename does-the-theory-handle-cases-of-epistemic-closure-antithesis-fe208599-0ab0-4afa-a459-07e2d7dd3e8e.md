# Does the theory handle cases of epistemic closure?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe208599-0ab0-4afa-a459-07e2d7dd3e8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Conditional Reliabilism](nozicks-conditional-reliabilism-synthesis-29b3c3e0-b3ff-4b45-a994-893f15be46b2.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone knows that "I am in Chicago." Intuitively, if this person knows "I am in Chicago," and also the fact that being in Chicago implies being on Earth, then they know "I am on Earth."**, **Nozick's theory suggests that knowledge doesn't necessarily transfer if the belief that one is on Earth doesn't track the truth in a counterfactual scenario where one is not on Earth.**, **If knowledge isn't closed under implication, then the principle that knowing p and knowing "if p, then q" does not necessarily lead to knowing q is counterintuitive.**
