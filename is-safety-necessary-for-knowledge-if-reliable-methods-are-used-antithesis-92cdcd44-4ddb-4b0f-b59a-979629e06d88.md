# Is safety necessary for knowledge if reliable methods are used?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 92cdcd44-4ddb-4b0f-b59a-979629e06d88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a skilled weatherman predicts rain accurately using reliable methods, but the prediction could easily have been incorrect due to unpredictable atmospheric changes in nearby possible worlds. Intuitively, we would judge that the weatherman has knowledge despite the lack of safety.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliability in belief formation should contribute to knowledge. This principle together with the presence of reliable methods entails that safety is not always necessary for knowledge.**
