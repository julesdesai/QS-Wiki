# Can external defeaters invalidate knowledge without the person's awareness?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c77ff7cf-c54c-4dce-86da-3f5009614c81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Defeasibility Theory](defeasibility-theory-synthesis-bec695a8-d154-4145-af72-ab6869088e07.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a diligent student accurately answers a test question based on standard materials, yet unknown to them, a typo in textbook implies different learning content. Intuitively, we accept the student possesses knowledge and is justified despite external content inaccuracies they cannot access or anticipate.**, **There exists a principle that knowledge entails reliability in belief-forming processes a person can control but not random external discrepancies. When defeaters are completely external and inaccessible, they do not undermine knowledge.**
