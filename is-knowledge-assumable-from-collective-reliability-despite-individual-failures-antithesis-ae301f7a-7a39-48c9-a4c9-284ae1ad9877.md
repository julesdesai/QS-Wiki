# Is knowledge assumable from collective reliability despite individual failures?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae301f7a-7a39-48c9-a4c9-284ae1ad9877
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief is formed in a large group where most members use a reliable process, but an individual member forms their belief unreliably. Intuitively, we may not count the individual's belief as knowledge despite the group's reliability.**, **The assumption that collective reliability translates to all individuals conflicts with cases of individual errors amidst reliable groups.**
