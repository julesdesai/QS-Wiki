# Is externalism compatible with error possibilities?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ab4d8426-d203-453a-a76f-708efdfb446e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a reliable cognitive process is affected by external factors leading typically to truth but occasionally results in error. Intuitively, we would not regard beliefs formed during the error-inducing periods as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be attributed when the same process leads to significant risk of error. This principle together with the scenario of unreliably reliable processes casts doubt on the externalist view.**
