# Does individual perceptual error undermine the reliability of testimony in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 429fe3f6-a358-473b-807e-d1f34248e1dd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P witnesses an event but interprets it erroneously due to personal biases. Intuitively, we would judge that P's testimony about the event may not reliably contribute to accurate knowledge for others.**, **There is a plausible principle that testimony should be reliable to contribute to perception-based knowledge. This principle together with individual perceptual error entails that testimony may not always be a reliable source of knowledge.**
