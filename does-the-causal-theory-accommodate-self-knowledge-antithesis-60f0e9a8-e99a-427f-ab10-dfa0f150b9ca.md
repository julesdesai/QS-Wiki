# Does the causal theory accommodate self-knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60f0e9a8-e99a-427f-ab10-dfa0f150b9ca
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2b44e1eb-9f6a-4c4d-97b0-9b782ad8c2af.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that knowledge of one’s own mental states, like feeling pain, can be captured by a causal connection between belief and truth.**, **Consider a scenario where someone sincerely states they believe they are in pain; intuitively, this knowledge seems immediate and does not require any external causal relationship.**
