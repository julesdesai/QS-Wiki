# Are all intellectual virtues necessary for justification in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b650f574-6321-4b05-b344-efab849eb67c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ad239dac-f508-456d-984a-1344bc3c3ad8.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation in which a person possesses a true belief based on reliable external evidence but lacks certain intellectual virtues. Intuitively, this person still seems justified in their belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that external factors can justify a belief without relying on the agent's intellectual virtues. This challenges the idea that justification strictly depends on those virtues.**
