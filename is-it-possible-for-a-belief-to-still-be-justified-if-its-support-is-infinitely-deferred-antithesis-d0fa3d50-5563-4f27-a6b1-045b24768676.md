# Is it possible for a belief to still be justified if its support is infinitely deferred?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d0fa3d50-5563-4f27-a6b1-045b24768676
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with An Emphasis on the Chain of Justifications](infinitism-with-an-emphasis-on-the-chain-of-justifications-synthesis-fbcbcc46-9003-42db-8878-9fc62d8799d4.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that a belief loses relevance if its justifying reasons are infinitely deferred. Premature justification should not be pragmatically valuable if it depends on unobtainable future knowledge.**, **In everyday examples, beliefs held on infinitely deferred grounds often fail to serve meaningful purposes since practical reasons for belief must be tangible.**
