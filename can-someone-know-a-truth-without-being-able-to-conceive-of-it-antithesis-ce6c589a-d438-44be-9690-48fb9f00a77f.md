# Can someone know a truth without being able to conceive of it?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce6c589a-d438-44be-9690-48fb9f00a77f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c5c60481-0513-43f2-8ed6-61505f94790c.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowing a proposition requires the ability to conceive of the proposition.**, **There can be cases where a person cannot even conceive of a true proposition they believe, such as highly theoretical scientific truths.**, **It is implausible to say someone knows a proposition if they have no conception of it.**
