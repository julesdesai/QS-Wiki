# Are trivial truths a counterexample to process reliability justifying knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7c35b89e-6843-47dd-955f-a75b25ad6142
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Imagine a belief is formed about a trivial truth through a reliable process, such as believing that the number of cups on the table is the same as the number of cups perceived. Intuitively, such cases do not seem to carry the weight of what we typically consider knowledge.**, **It is implausible to grant knowledge status to trivial truths, as knowledge often requires some significance; reliable processes producing trivialities may not fulfill substantive knowledge criteria.**
