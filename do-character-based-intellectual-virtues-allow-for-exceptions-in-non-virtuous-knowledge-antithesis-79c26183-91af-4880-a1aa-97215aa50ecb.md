# Do character-based intellectual virtues allow for exceptions in non-virtuous knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 79c26183-91af-4880-a1aa-97215aa50ecb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-5ebb7897-3428-494f-bec1-94f934eebf77.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a true belief arises accidentally, such as guessing correctly without basis. Intuitively, we must say they know the truth by chance or not. If we say they know, it challenges the necessity of virtues; if we deny knowledge, it undermines intuitively clear instances of known truths.**
