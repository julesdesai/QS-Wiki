# Can coherentism respond to the isolation objection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 12e2f121-65ac-49a5-acd9-dd2418ec410c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism in Epistemology](coherentism-in-epistemology-synthesis-03124e37-be18-4868-b2cf-e370b5186105.md)

## Content

**A coherent system of beliefs may be entirely disconnected from reality as long as it is internally consistent and supportive.**, **A person might hold a set of beliefs that are coherent but all false, for example, a perfectly coherent set of beliefs about a fictional world.**, **Intuitively, having coherent yet false beliefs does not seem to provide genuine knowledge or justification.**
