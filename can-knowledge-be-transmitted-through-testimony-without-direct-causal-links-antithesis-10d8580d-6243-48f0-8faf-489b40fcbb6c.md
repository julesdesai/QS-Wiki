# Can knowledge be transmitted through testimony without direct causal links?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 10d8580d-6243-48f0-8faf-489b40fcbb6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-68931138-dab6-4042-8f78-fffb10d21878.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person gains their introduction to historical facts from a credible history teacher's testimony. Intuitively, this person is granted knowledge through the teacher's reliable conveyance of truth, even if the person does not have a direct causal connection to the original events.**, **It is implausible that second-hand knowledge always requires a direct causal connection to the events described, suggesting that testimony can adequately convey knowledge.**
