# Is knowledge truly context-dependent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** aa40c35f-07f9-4668-9af3-e7b6de6706cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Reliabilism](contextualism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c7c3d2a5-9a25-4e3f-9e1f-65010441e024.md)

## Content

**Knowledge is often considered to be objective and thus should not vary with different contexts.**, **If the standards for knowing a proposition vary with different contexts, then the truth conditions or criteria for knowledge are not stable.**, **Inconsistent standards for knowledge would mean that a person might know something in one context but not in another, which seems counterintuitive.**
