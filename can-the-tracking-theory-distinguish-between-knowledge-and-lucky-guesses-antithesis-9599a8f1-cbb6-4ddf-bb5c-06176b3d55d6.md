# Can the Tracking Theory distinguish between knowledge and lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9599a8f1-cbb6-4ddf-bb5c-06176b3d55d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3fc68b12-ca29-40d7-8341-51e40d71c5e5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person guesses correctly the outcome of a coin flip based on a hunch. Their belief about the outcome is true by chance. Intuitively, we would not say they know the outcome.**, **If the belief were false (the coin landed on the other side), the person would still have held the belief until the flip.**, **In slightly different situations, like a differently weighted coin, the belief prior to seeing the outcome would persist unchanged.**
