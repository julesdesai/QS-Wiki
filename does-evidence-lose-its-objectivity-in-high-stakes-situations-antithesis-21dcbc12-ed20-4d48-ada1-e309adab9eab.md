# Does evidence lose its objectivity in high stakes situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 21dcbc12-ed20-4d48-ada1-e309adab9eab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment Summary](pragmatic-encroachment-summary-synthesis-e897bf05-5a21-418d-ae35-b563f51919c6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where two people have the same evidence for a true belief, but one faces higher practical stakes than the other. Intuitively, we might say that the person with lower stakes knows the proposition while the other does not.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is grounded in objective evidence rather than subjective factors. This principle together with the scenario entails that knowledge attribution should not be influenced by the stakes involved.**
