# Do mistaken causal attributions undermine the causal theory?

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which someone attributes the cause of their belief to a factor that turns out to be false. Intuitively, if the belief remains true, the mistaken causal attribution challenges the necessity of correct causal connections to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that recognizes the persistence of justified true beliefs despite incorrect causal understandings, questioning causal necessity.**
