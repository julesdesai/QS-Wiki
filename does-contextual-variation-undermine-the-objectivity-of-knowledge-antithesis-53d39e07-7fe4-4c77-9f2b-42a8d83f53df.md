# Does contextual variation undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 53d39e07-7fe4-4c77-9f2b-42a8d83f53df
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge claims should maintain objectivity, meaning they should be invariant from person to person.**, **The idea that the standards for knowing vary with different contexts implies that knowledge claims may change person to person, challenging objectivity.**, **If objectivity is undermined, then the reliability of knowledge as something stable and communicable is threatened.**
