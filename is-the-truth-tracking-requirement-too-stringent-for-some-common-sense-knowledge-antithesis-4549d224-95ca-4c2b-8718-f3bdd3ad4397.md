# Is the truth-tracking requirement too stringent for some common-sense knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4549d224-95ca-4c2b-8718-f3bdd3ad4397
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick’s Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-5a62ad60-20de-4329-b52b-21f59b8da1c1.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person confidently knows a commonplace truth based on habitual or routine evidence, like knowing there is milk in the fridge. If the circumstances in close possible worlds could unexpectedly alter such small truths (such as someone spontaneously using the milk), intuitively one might argue that common-sense knowledge of everyday facts may not meet the formal truth-tracking standard due to their contingent nature.**
