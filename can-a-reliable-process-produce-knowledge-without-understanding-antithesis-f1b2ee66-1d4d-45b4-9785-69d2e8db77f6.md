# Can a reliable process produce knowledge without understanding?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f1b2ee66-1d4d-45b4-9785-69d2e8db77f6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Summary](reliabilism-summary-synthesis-2bc1a227-81a9-4aef-a155-aa259d89de26.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person uses a complex machine to predict the outcomes of horse races reliably but lacks understanding of how the machine works. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows the outcomes or they do not. If we say they do not know, the emphasis on understanding highlights a gap in the reliability account. If we say they do know, it seems odd that understanding is peripheral to knowledge.**
