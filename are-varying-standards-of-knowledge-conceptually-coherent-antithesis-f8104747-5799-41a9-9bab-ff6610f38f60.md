# Are varying standards of knowledge conceptually coherent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f8104747-5799-41a9-9bab-ff6610f38f60
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where someone needs different amounts of evidence to know the same proposition based on different contexts in which they find themselves. Intuitively, it seems problematic that epistemic standards can float so freely.**, **It is implausible that the criteria for knowledge can vary significantly without leading to inconsistencies in what knowledge is.**
