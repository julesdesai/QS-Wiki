# Does knowledge from logical reasoning require causality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55f3e92a-9e16-473e-bdbc-8779e669abfc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that logical reasoning can provide knowledge without requiring a direct causal link between the belief and an external fact. The knowledge arises from the application of logical rules and principles.**, **For instance, consider a person concluding that all bachelors are unmarried through logical reasoning. Intuitively, this conclusion seems to constitute knowledge despite the lack of causal interactions.**
