# Can knowledge arise from unreliable intuition?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93856093-f770-42c9-bc19-1217b34cd157
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Refined Virtue Epistemology](refined-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed8e6ab8-d432-4955-9aec-982a4b148be7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a true belief that arises from a lucky guess rather than an intellectual virtue. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite the belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a reliable process of belief formation. This principle together with the view that knowledge is true belief arising from intellectual virtue entails that an unreliable process due to pure intuition does not constitute knowledge.**
