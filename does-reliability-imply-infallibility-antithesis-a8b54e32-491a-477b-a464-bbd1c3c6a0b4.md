# Does reliability imply infallibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a8b54e32-491a-477b-a464-bbd1c3c6a0b4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-4db07ebc-4cb3-402b-b335-2b01121c3e1a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a very reliable process produces a false belief in an individual case. Intuitively, this suggests the person does not have knowledge because the belief is false, despite the overall reliability**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires at least true belief, and a reliable process cannot compensate for falsehood in any specific instance**
