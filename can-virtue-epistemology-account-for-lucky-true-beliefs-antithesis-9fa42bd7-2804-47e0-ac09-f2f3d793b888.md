# Can virtue epistemology account for lucky true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9fa42bd7-2804-47e0-ac09-f2f3d793b888
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-df7f6b25-43d9-4bf2-9d94-240d68c6e5be.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief simply by chance, such as guessing the correct answer on a test without knowledge of the subject matter. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite having a true belief.**, **A belief that is true solely by luck does not appear to result from intellectual virtue.**
