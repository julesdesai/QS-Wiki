# Is justified true belief really sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8ff1a75a-40ff-4f78-8ab3-39b61d9779cd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Theory of Justification](pragmatic-theory-of-justification-synthesis-cd0f39d2-c604-4de4-bb81-1900aa8a1a8f.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes a proposition, the proposition is true, and the justification relies on practical success, but the person lacks understanding of why the proposition is true, challenges the sufficiency of justified true belief for knowledge.**, **A case in which a person wins a lottery by chance without knowing that their ticket is the winner, yet has justified true belief, suggests that such belief is insufficient for knowledge.**
