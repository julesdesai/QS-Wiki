# How do we account for the knowledge of other minds?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c728182-5bdc-4430-ae1b-4cb1e0d3ad6f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Conceptual Empiricism](conceptual-empiricism-synthesis-7b58485d-3ea5-4513-ba7e-4d4d19b630bf.md)

## Content

**A person can know another is angry or joyous without directly observing anger or joy in perceptual experiences.**, **Knowledge of another's mental state does not always pertain to concepts derived from one's own perceptual experiences.**, **We seem to know facts about other minds that cannot be known solely through the application of concepts generated by perceptual experiences.**
