# Can perceptual illusions undermine immediate knowledge through experience?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 532ac0e1-fb2d-45e2-9710-b1652a0464b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-2e1488cf-3c3f-4cc1-b449-47fe37a86624.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a stick appears bent when half-submerged in water, but is actually straight. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception provides accurate knowledge or acknowledge that perception can mislead. If we say perception provides accurate knowledge about the stick's appearance, then it undermines the claim that perception is direct access to reality.**
