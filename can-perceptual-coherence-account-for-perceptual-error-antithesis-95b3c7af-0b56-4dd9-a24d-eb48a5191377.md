# Can perceptual coherence account for perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95b3c7af-0b56-4dd9-a24d-eb48a5191377
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Constructive Coherence](phenomenalism-with-constructive-coherence-synthesis-649780d2-d21b-4c5f-9b9f-162351fea396.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person sees a mirage that consistently appears to be a lake. In this case, we would intuitively judge that perceptual coherence alone does not prevent errors about the existence of a lake.**, **It is implausible that coherence alone can account for distinguishing between accurate perceptions and perceptual errors without an external reality check.**
