# Does phenomenalism account for the consistent behavior of objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2b11499b-325e-4390-9ae5-c3a65e33baff
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an ice cube is placed on a table and left there. Intuitively, we would judge that the ice cube melts over time due to consistent physical laws.**, **It is implausible that these laws are merely patterns of sensory experiences as they persist without constant observation.**
