# Are there examples where context fails to explain knowledge differences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b2b18063-70d6-4860-8dbb-9f23728cfc42
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person in a high-stakes context and a person in a low-stakes context both claim to know the same proposition. Intuitively, the truth of the proposition should dictate knowledge, not the stakes involved.**, **It is implausible that the truth of a proposition is affected merely by the contextual stakes.**
