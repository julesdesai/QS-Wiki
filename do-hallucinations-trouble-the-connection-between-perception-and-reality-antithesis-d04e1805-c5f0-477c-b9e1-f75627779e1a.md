# Do hallucinations trouble the connection between perception and reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d04e1805-c5f0-477c-b9e1-f75627779e1a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cognitive Affordances Summary](direct-realism-with-cognitive-affordances-summary-synthesis-daf75c37-de5d-4669-a0c7-0f33d766507a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates a vividly realistic event. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not know the event has happened, despite their direct perceptual experience.**, **The claim that a person knows a proposition if perception and cognitive aids together correspond to reality implies that hallucinations that don't correspond to reality cannot constitute knowledge.**
