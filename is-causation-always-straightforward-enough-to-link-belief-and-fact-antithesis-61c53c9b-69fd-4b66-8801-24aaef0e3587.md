# Is causation always straightforward enough to link belief and fact?

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causal Theory](knowledge-as-causal-theory-synthesis-6ad482a2-7fa2-4d67-b89c-e38da44e4516.md)

## Content

**A person can have a true belief that is only indirectly related to the fact that makes it true, such as believing it will rain because they have joint pain**, **In such cases, the belief's causation is not a direct link from fact to belief but a correlation due to an indirect process**
