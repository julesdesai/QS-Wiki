# How does coherentism account for perceptual beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a020bb3c-9d8c-4327-8289-a3be1428166d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism View](coherentism-view-synthesis-4dcf0483-85cc-4ed6-8a5e-86aaec2b7626.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a perceptual belief directly formed from sensory experience, such as seeing a tree in front of them. Intuitively, we would judge that this perceptual belief has some form of justification even if it does not neatly cohere with other beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptual beliefs can have non-inferential justification, which is independent of coherence with other beliefs.**
