# Are there situations where sensitivity leads to counterintuitive knowledge attributions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 774a05ab-d407-4f03-9524-d0d57aeb8c38
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory](sensitivity-theory-synthesis-3d534a12-140c-4311-b926-a2f37f38e945.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person correctly believes their friend is home because they see their friend's car in the driveway, which is usually parked there when the friend is home. Intuitively, we might say the person knows their friend is home.**, **If in a close possible world, the friend's car is in the driveway but they are not home, then the person's belief lacks sensitivity because they would still believe their friend is home, despite it being false in that world.**
