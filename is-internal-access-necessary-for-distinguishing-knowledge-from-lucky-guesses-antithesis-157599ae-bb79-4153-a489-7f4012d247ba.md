# Is internal access necessary for distinguishing knowledge from lucky guesses?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 157599ae-bb79-4153-a489-7f4012d247ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where multiple identical-looking keys hang on a wall, and a person randomly picks a key that happens to unlock a door. The belief that this key is the correct one coincides with truth purely by luck. Intuitively, this belief seems insufficient for knowledge due to the lack of accessible justification.**, **Even if a person's belief aligns with reality by chance, the absence of accessible justification makes it resemble guesswork rather than knowledge.**
