# What about cases where reliability fails due to luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cda80b99-9c79-461f-bcf2-990bad09c8ef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ed1f1377-7007-46b3-89c9-5ab38981916f.md)

## Content

**A situation where a reliable process leads to a true belief merely due to luck illustrates doubt on whether knowledge has been achieved.**, **Imagine a person inferring the winner of a sports game from a usual, but malfunctioning, radio station, yet happening to get the correct result; such luck seems insufficient for genuine knowledge.**
