# Can intellectual virtue guarantee knowledge without accessible justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3af6302e-886c-4e20-b847-0216f119bc4a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Virtue Epistemology](externalism-with-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-ed14dd30-ddd0-424e-b9cf-cae595511d82.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtue should guide belief towards truth, yet this process should be somewhat accessible to the agent in order to constitute knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person's belief is true and formed by intellectual virtue, but the person cannot access the virtue's operation consciously. Intuitively, we would doubt whether the belief truly counts as knowledge without this access.**
