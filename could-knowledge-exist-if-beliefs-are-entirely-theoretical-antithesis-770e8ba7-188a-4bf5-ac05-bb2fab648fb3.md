# Could knowledge exist if beliefs are entirely theoretical?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 770e8ba7-188a-4bf5-ac05-bb2fab648fb3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextual Pragmatism](knowledge-as-contextual-pragmatism-synthesis-58b0d4c4-4eeb-46a9-99af-766901c4c5df.md)

## Content

**Consider a mathematician who believes a complex theorem which has been proven true but holds no known practical consequence. Intuitively, many would regard this belief as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that purely theoretical knowledge without practical application can still count as knowledge, suggesting that practical consequences are not necessary.**
