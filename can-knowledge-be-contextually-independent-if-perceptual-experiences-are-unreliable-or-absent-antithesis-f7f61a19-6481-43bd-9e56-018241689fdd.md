# Can knowledge be contextually independent if perceptual experiences are unreliable or absent?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7f61a19-6481-43bd-9e56-018241689fdd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Phenomenalism](contextual-phenomenalism-synthesis-8ade45c1-54b8-462e-aef7-204b002692b3.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a situation in which all perceptual cues are temporarily absent, such as hearing a sound in a completely dark room. In such a situation, intuitively, one might claim to know the direction of the sound without contextual visual verification.**, **If knowledge can exist without direct perceptual input or verification, then the requirement that perceptual experiences substantiate knowledge in context becomes questionable.**, **A scenario where a person recognizes a song purely by its melody despite being visually and situationally isolated from confirming elements questions whether perceptual experience is always necessary in contexts for knowledge.**
