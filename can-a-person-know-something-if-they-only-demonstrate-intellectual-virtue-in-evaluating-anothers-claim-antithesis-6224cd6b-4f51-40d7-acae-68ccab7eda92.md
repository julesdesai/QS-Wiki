# Can a person know something if they only demonstrate intellectual virtue in evaluating another's claim?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6224cd6b-4f51-40d7-acae-68ccab7eda92
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person's belief is true, but they mainly demonstrate intellectual virtue through critically evaluating someone else's testimony, rather than forming the belief themselves. Intuitively, we might still say they have knowledge, even if their cognitive abilities were not the primary source of the belief.**, **The idea that knowledge requires intellectual virtue coming from one's own cognitive abilities seems to exclude cases where evaluation, not formation, is the key activity.**
