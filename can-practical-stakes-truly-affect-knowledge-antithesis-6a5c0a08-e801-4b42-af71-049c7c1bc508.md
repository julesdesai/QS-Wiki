# Can practical stakes truly affect knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6a5c0a08-e801-4b42-af71-049c7c1bc508
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-829538ce-ebfd-41f1-81ea-e2a3bdbbc235.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people, Alice and Bob, have the same belief based on the same evidence, but Alice has much more to lose if their belief is false. It is implausible that the practical stakes for Alice should affect whether she possesses knowledge while leaving Bob's knowledge unaffected.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should depend solely on truth-related factors such as evidence, justification, and truth, and not on practical circumstances. This principle together with the observation that evidence and truth do not change between Alice and Bob entails that practical stakes should be irrelevant to knowledge.**
