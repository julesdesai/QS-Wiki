# Does the notion of sensitivity fail with deeply entrenched beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c0bf78b-a4ef-440e-bfd9-d155201ece9d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-102d682a-8eb5-4983-bf10-f287ee1765f9.md)

## Content

**Consider someone who has a deep religious faith such that, even if all evidence disappeared, they would still believe. Intuitively, this is considered a form of knowledge for the believer.**, **The relationship between belief persistence and knowledge is complex and not solely dependent on counterfactual sensitivity.**
