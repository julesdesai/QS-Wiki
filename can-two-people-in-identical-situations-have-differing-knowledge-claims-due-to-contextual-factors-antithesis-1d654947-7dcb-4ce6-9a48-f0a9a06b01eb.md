# Can two people in identical situations have differing knowledge claims due to contextual factors?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d654947-7dcb-4ce6-9a48-f0a9a06b01eb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist View](contextualist-view-synthesis-55297f9c-50a2-4f24-b1b0-6029f7bef7b2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals in identical situations are assessed by different communities, each with distinct justification standards. Intuitively, we would judge that the propositions they both believe should or shouldn't qualify as knowledge uniformly, but differing standards might result in opposing knowledge claims.**, **Suppose justification depends on the perceiving community. If so, knowledge attribution becomes relative, making cross-contextual disagreements possible and threatening the idea of universal knowledge.**
