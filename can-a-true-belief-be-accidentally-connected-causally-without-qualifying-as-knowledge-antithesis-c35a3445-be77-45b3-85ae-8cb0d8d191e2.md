# Can a true belief be accidentally connected causally without qualifying as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c35a3445-be77-45b3-85ae-8cb0d8d191e2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-70e14214-ee3d-466b-a7c5-3345f844cfd7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person becomes aware of a fact through a deceptive mechanism that nonetheless accidentally causes a correct belief. Intuitively, this true belief does not qualify as knowledge due to its reliance on deception.**, **A simply accidental true causation does not intuitively meet the criteria of knowledge despite causality.**
