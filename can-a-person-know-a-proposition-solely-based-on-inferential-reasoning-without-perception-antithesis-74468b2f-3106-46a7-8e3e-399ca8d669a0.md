# Can a person know a proposition solely based on inferential reasoning without perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 74468b2f-3106-46a7-8e3e-399ca8d669a0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inferential Phenomenalism](inferential-phenomenalism-synthesis-2adfcff5-a571-43dd-87fa-6a59a7ba9de4.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that perception is necessary for the inferential reasoning that leads to knowledge.**, **Suppose we have a case in which a person in a sensory deprivation chamber tries to infer truths about the external world. Intuitively, we would judge that this person does not know the propositions they infer.**, **Inferential reasoning without perceptual input can result in conclusions that lack the basis for knowledge about the external world.**
