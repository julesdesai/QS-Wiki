# Does probabilistic reasoning overlook the nature of evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da414ad6-177c-4f2a-ae58-8fc7ce06a874
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Bayesian Epistemology](bayesian-epistemology-synthesis-4576807d-29a3-407d-a965-1c884989f7fe.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a person updates their belief using prior probabilities and new evidence, yet the new evidence is not truly indicative of the proposition's truth. Intuitively, we would judge that such evidence does not enhance knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires evidence that is genuinely indicative of the proposition's truth, not just any probabilistic update. This principle alongside the situation suggests that Bayesian reasoning might not fully accommodate the quality and relevance of evidence.**
