# Does basing knowledge solely on true beliefs exclude scenarios of epistemic luck that do not involve false premises?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 966788bb-c6d1-493e-99b8-60465ebf8353
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as No False Lemmas](knowledge-as-no-false-lemmas-synthesis-297c85de-4f84-45f8-a496-f3afa53ad459.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who forms a true belief about a complex fact purely by chance, without any reliance on false lemmas. Intuitively, this seems like luck rather than knowledge.**, **The role of epistemic luck in forming true beliefs questions whether absence of false lemmas alone can capture the essence of knowledge.**
