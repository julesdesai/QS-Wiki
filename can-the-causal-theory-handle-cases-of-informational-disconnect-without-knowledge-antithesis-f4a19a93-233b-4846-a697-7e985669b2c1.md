# Can the causal theory handle cases of informational disconnect without knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f4a19a93-233b-4846-a697-7e985669b2c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism with Causal Theory](externalism-with-causal-theory-synthesis-67f241d3-d718-4f9e-97b2-c0aff473bbc3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a detective logically deduces the location of a suspect based solely on word-of-mouth with groundless origins, yet fortunately aligns this location with the true one. This deduction is causally connected to external fact but rests on misinformation. Intuitively, such conclusions lack knowledge due to insufficient informational integrity.**, **Instances where causal links connect belief to truth, but roots are corrupt or disconnected, challenge the thoroughness of causality in defining knowledge.**
