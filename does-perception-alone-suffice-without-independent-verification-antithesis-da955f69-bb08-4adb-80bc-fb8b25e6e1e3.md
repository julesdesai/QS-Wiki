# Does perception alone suffice without independent verification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da955f69-bb08-4adb-80bc-fb8b25e6e1e3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Perception as Phenomenal-Independence](perception-as-phenomenal-independence-synthesis-76d6e707-cdc6-44d9-83d6-555caf4d40eb.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person perceives water in a glass and drinks it without any verification. Intuitively, we would say that the person knows the liquid is water based on direct perception alone.**, **There is a plausible principle that direct perception without additional verification can be sufficient for certain knowledge. This principle implies that additional verification might be unnecessary or redundant in straightforward perceptual situations.**, **It is implausible to require independent verification when perceptual conditions clearly and directly ascertain an object or event.**
