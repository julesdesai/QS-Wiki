# Does varying contextual justification support epistemic relativism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f003a917-93de-4727-a9f7-89bffab00d37
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Foundationalism](contextualist-foundationalism-synthesis-b757e054-5c50-4073-a4ee-c1ee757cbdbb.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if beliefs can vary significantly with context, then knowledge becomes relative to these contexts.**, **Epistemic relativism implies that conflicting knowledge claims can both be true in their respective contexts.**, **Suppose we have a case where one cultural context supports belief in a deity, while another does not. Both cannot be true in a unified knowledge system, leading to potential contradictions.**
