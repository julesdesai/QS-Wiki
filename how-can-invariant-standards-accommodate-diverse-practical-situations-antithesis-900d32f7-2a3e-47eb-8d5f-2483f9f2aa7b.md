# How can invariant standards accommodate diverse practical situations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 900d32f7-2a3e-47eb-8d5f-2483f9f2aa7b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-1a7f6ba4-b667-492e-9754-ad3a76a8b666.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a single, invariant standard of knowledge can accommodate the wide diversity of practical situations people encounter. If standards are truly invariant, they should apply uniformly across all situations, regardless of practical stakes.**, **Suppose we consider two individuals with different levels of risk involved in acting on the same knowledge claim. Intuitively, the adequacy of their knowledge seems to change with their respective stakes, which challenges the feasibility of a single invariant standard.**
