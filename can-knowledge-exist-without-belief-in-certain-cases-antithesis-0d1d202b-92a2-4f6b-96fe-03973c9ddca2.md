# Can knowledge exist without belief in certain cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0d1d202b-92a2-4f6b-96fe-03973c9ddca2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Theory of Knowledge](disjunctivist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-eb3c40bf-59e8-47a3-98db-de4d9568dbcc.md)

## Content

**There are scenarios in which a person arrives at a true conclusion out of sheer luck without genuinely holding a belief in that conclusion, such as guessing the answer to a complex question correctly. Intuitively, we would not ascribe knowledge in such cases despite the belief being true.**, **A plausible principle is that the presence of belief does not suffice for knowledge. This principle implies that knowledge requires more than just a happenstance true belief.**
