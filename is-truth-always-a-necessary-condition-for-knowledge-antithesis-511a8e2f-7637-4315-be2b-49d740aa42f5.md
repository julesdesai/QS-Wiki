# Is truth always a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 511a8e2f-7637-4315-be2b-49d740aa42f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**In some cases, people operate under justified beliefs that turn out to be false, yet these beliefs guide successful action.**, **There is a plausible principle that true beliefs are necessary for knowledge because knowledge should accurately reflect reality. If individuals can act successfully under false yet justified beliefs, then truth might not be strictly necessary for knowledge.**
