# Does the problem of epistemic luck challenge reliable belief formation as a source of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7ebd3e81-e57e-4a2e-b21d-7b98b1bb9323
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism With Reliable Belief Formation](externalism-with-reliable-belief-formation-synthesis-2a34c494-e349-4b40-95af-530f8f0219c4.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a belief formed from a reliable process coincidentally aligns with reality due to chance factors beyond the process itself. Intuitively, knowledge seems compromised by the role of luck.**, **It is implausible that a belief can be considered knowledge if its truth stems from luck rather than the reliability of the process alone.**
