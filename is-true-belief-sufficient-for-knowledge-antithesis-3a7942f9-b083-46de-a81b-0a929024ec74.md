# Is true belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3a7942f9-b083-46de-a81b-0a929024ec74
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism: Contexts Influence Justification, Not Knowledge](moderate-contextualism-contexts-influence-justification-not-knowledge-synthesis-be3d790f-5519-485d-8f0d-32864e4a3e9e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two people have a true belief about a proposition but one arrived at the belief through unreliable means, such as a lucky guess; intuitively, we would judge that the latter does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires reliability in forming true beliefs.**, **The principle that knowledge standards are fixed and only justification varies does not accommodate cases where justification involves reliable processes.**
