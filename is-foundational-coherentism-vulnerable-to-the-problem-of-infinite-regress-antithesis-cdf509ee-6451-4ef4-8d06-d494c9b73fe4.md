# Is Foundational Coherentism vulnerable to the Problem of Infinite Regress?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cdf509ee-6451-4ef4-8d06-d494c9b73fe4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundational Coherentism Summary](foundational-coherentism-summary-synthesis-fd8d6734-3211-4dc7-85ab-4f7da95f6cb7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where beliefs require justification by other beliefs indefinitely. Intuitively, an infinite regress of belief justifications seems practically unmanageable and theoretically troubling.**, **If foundational empirical input is supposed to stop the regress, it must itself be justified through some means that avoids leading back into an infinite regress or circular justification.**
