# Is there knowledge if the belief involves a lucky guess?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d150a0df-d773-418c-b3e6-260aeb2be23c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory](safety-theory-synthesis-a867b6c0-f011-4cc2-a9c9-8c3a93b64de5.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person makes a guess about a fact and the guess is true in all nearby possible worlds, such as correctly guessing the time without looking at any clock**, **Intuitively, we would not attribute knowledge to this person because the correctness of their belief hinges heavily on luck rather than a reliable process**, **Even if the belief is formed in a manner that is true across nearby possible worlds, it lacks the epistemic justification often associated with knowledge**
