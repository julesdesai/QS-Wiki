# Can the causal theory of knowledge handle scenarios with false intermediate causal links?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f0c709f-f75e-4ff5-b159-bb0fd5ef9d34
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-336c94a4-e4b0-4a1c-b1db-218c9b8f6add.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a scenario where a person believes a news report falsely claiming a bridge is closed, drives a different route, and discovers the bridge is indeed closed for another reason. The belief that the bridge is closed is true, but is not based on the appropriate cause.**, **Intuitively, having a belief based on a false intermediary does not amount to knowledge even if the belief itself is true and causally connected to the fact.**
