# Can an unlikely risk scenario challenge the safety of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e58e67f8-c111-4236-916e-df66d6ca849f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-ddb0d759-5c3e-4616-85ba-00e34fffe012.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person forms a true belief about the current weather by looking at a weather app that is usually accurate, except in rare instances where it fails dramatically due to unforeseen server errors. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows the current weather.**, **A belief is not safe if, in nearby possible worlds, minor changes such as server malfunctions would lead to a false belief. In this scenario, the belief may not be safe due to the chance of server error.**
