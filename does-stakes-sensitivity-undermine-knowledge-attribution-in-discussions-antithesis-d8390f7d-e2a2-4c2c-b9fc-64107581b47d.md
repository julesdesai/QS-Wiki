# Does stakes-sensitivity undermine knowledge attribution in discussions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d8390f7d-e2a2-4c2c-b9fc-64107581b47d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-a38d2af6-3ec4-421c-a558-c594d0cbc8b7.md)

## Content

**In debates or discussions, attributing knowledge based on stakes could lead to inconsistent claims about what different people know. Intuitively, this inconsistency seems problematic for a stable understanding of knowledge.**, **A plausible principle asserts that public and social discourse requires stable knowledge attribution, which stakes-sensitivity can disrupt. Knowledge attribution should be consistent across contexts, irrespective of stakes.**
