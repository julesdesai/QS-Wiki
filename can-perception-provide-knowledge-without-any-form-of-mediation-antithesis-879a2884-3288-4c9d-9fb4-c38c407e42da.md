# Can perception provide knowledge without any form of mediation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 879a2884-3288-4c9d-9fb4-c38c407e42da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Suppose that a person observes a mirage in a desert and perceives water. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows there is water or they do not. If we say the person knows there is water about this case, then they are misled. If we say the person does not know, then perception requires something further to mediate true knowledge.**, **It is implausible that perception alone, unmediated, can distinguish between real and illusory objects.**
