# Do sensory experiences vary too much between individuals to base knowledge on them?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 83efdf83-33f5-4fd0-8e8a-41a00103b90d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-2166f961-34d5-4334-bf8f-39dfb647eb18.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where one person senses a room as warm while another experiences it as cold; intuitively, this variability challenges sensory experiences as a sole basis for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that personal variances in sensory perception can consistently lead to objective knowledge.**
