# How does virtue epistemology handle non-human animals as knowers?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0662db8b-91fb-4b9c-8035-075fe7b84b89
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**In the case of a dog trained to find true food sources reliably, intuitively we can consider the dog knowledgeable despite lacking traditionally defined intellectual virtues associated with humans.**, **If intellectual virtue requires distinctly human cognitive abilities, non-human knowers exhibiting knowledge challenge the normative scope of virtue epistemology.**, **Crediting truth to human-centered cognitive traits may exclude valid knowers from other species, questioning the consistency of the truth-crediting principle in diverse ontological domains.**
