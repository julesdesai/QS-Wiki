# Does disagreement invalidate knowledge based on shared perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 314cfc16-dfa6-4075-87f1-b9ddbf19e534
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a group of people observes a phenomenon and disagrees on its characteristics. In such a scenario, it is implausible to determine a single collective perception that confirms knowledge of the phenomenon.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge must be consistent and reliable. If perceptual disagreements occur, this principle is undermined because collective perceptions lose consistency and reliability.**
