# Can we truly claim to have objective knowledge if it's limited to appearances shaped by our mind's categories?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e697e68-56ec-4cac-8480-2de7531d21a5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that objective knowledge should not be limited by subjective cognitive structures. This principle together with the idea that knowledge is shaped by innate mental categories entails that objective knowledge, as understood in a commonsense way, cannot exist.**
