# Can successful action determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e3a28d12-197e-4439-a576-28a36c518e57
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Practical Adequacy](pragmatism-with-practical-adequacy-synthesis-7de50134-9521-4bfe-88f0-682c548d4774.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person acts on a false belief but achieves their intended goal due to luck. In such a situation, intuitively, we would not judge that the person possesses knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a true belief. This principle, together with the occurrence of achieving successful action based on a false belief, suggests that successful action alone cannot determine knowledge.**
