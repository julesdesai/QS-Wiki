# Does context-dependence lead to contradictions in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2fa37809-c5c3-40b0-bbc7-f3b6097f50ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Theory of Knowledge](contextualist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-bce49363-1810-4900-8ccf-1e421dbc62ca.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where Alice is in a high-stakes context and requires very stringent evidence to claim knowledge that her flight leaves at 9 PM, whereas Bob is in a low-stakes context and accepts much less evidence for the same belief.**, **In this situation, intuitively, Alice does not know her flight leaves at 9 PM, while Bob does know this.**, **It is implausible for both Alice and Bob to hold contradictory knowledge states about the same proposition in an absolute sense because knowledge should be consistent.**
