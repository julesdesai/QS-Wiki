# Can knowledge arise from environmental luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8ce63d7d-d062-4a2a-b2f1-6297a0ff00ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Causally Connected True Belief](knowledge-as-causally-connected-true-belief-synthesis-39ec215f-3805-4011-8837-040b18d21351.md)

## Content

**Consider a person walking through a fake barn county, where many fake but only one real barn exists. They correctly identify the real barn by sheer luck without any discerning process. Intuitively, we question if this counts as knowledge despite belief being true.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not arise merely from environmental luck. This principle implies true belief causally connected by luck challenges the notion of holding knowledge.**, **It is implausible to accepted knowledge if it heavily relies on luck rather than a grounded causal connection, questioning the sufficiency of truth linked by chance.**
