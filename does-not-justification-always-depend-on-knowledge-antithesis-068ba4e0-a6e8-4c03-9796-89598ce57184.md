# Does not justification always depend on knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 068ba4e0-a6e8-4c03-9796-89598ce57184
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism: Contexts Influence Justification, Not Knowledge](moderate-contextualism-contexts-influence-justification-not-knowledge-synthesis-be3d790f-5519-485d-8f0d-32864e4a3e9e.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person is justified in believing a proposition only if they possess knowledge about related information.**, **A plausible principle holds that the justification for a belief cannot be entirely separated from the knowledge of related facts.**, **If justification varies with context while knowledge standards are fixed, the relationship between justification and knowledge becomes problematic.**
