# Can varying contexts for knowing lead to contradictions?

**Node ID:** 610aff91-1eee-465a-a948-d54f130d9f6d

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Suppose an individual is considered to know a proposition in one context but not in another, leading different communities to contradict each other. Intuitively, this seems to create an incoherent understanding of what it means to know.**, **It is plausible that the context-dependent view of knowledge might generate contradictions across different epistemic communities.**
