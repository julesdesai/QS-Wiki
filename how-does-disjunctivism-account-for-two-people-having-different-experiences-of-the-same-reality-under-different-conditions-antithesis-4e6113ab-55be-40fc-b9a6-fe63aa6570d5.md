# How does disjunctivism account for two people having different experiences of the same reality under different conditions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4e6113ab-55be-40fc-b9a6-fe63aa6570d5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism](disjunctivism-synthesis-8f6e7bad-fa5f-47b7-8d7c-d5f7ea4e2a2a.md)

## Content

**Consider two people observing a tree in a foggy environment where one sees it clearly and the other perceives it unclearly. Intuitively, if perception directly reflects reality, then both should ideally experience it similarly if the tree itself does not change.**, **There is a plausible principle that perceptions reflecting reality should be consistent across similar subjects, but individual perceptual conditions reveal subjectivity. This principle challenges the notion that reality alone dictates the veridical nature of perception.**
