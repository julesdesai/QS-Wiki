# To what extent can anonymous expert validation substitute for personal justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95f50378-f658-4763-bf31-cca52cdee8b1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [No-False-Grounds Theory](no-false-grounds-theory-synthesis-00c0ff61-646c-4d63-9780-b055a1d82c9f.md)

## Content

**A person encounters an anonymous but genuinely expert claim online, believes it, and it turns out to be true. However, they have no direct access to the justification for this claim.**, **It is plausible to argue that knowledge requires more than just belief in true claims made by others; it necessitates some form of personal engagement or verification.**, **Intuitive suspicion arises when people claim knowledge based solely on expert assertions without personal grasp of the justification.**
