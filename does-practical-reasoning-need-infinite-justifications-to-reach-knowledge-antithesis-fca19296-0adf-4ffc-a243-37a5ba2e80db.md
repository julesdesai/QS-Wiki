# Does practical reasoning need infinite justifications to reach knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fca19296-0adf-4ffc-a243-37a5ba2e80db
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism](infinitism-synthesis-c0072d0f-5658-4115-88b4-8385b90f0067.md)

## Content

**In a practical case where a person justifies their action based on a belief, intuitively it is recognized that the justification must be compact and understandable rather than extended infinitely.**, **Our practices of law, science, and moral reasoning require finite sets of reasons to progress and settle matters, indicating a discrepancy with the requirement for an infinite chain.**
