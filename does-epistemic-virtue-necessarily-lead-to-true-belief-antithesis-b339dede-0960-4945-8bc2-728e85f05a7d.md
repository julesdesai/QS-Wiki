# Does epistemic virtue necessarily lead to true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b339dede-0960-4945-8bc2-728e85f05a7d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-dbddd6f3-a688-44af-bb01-dd29b6f7b5c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person employs rigorous scientific methods to predict outcomes, yet due to unforeseen variables, their predictions consistently fail. Intuitively, the person exercises cognitive skill without the result of true belief.**, **It is implausible that the presence of epistemic virtue guarantees truth, as external factors can lead to failure despite skillful efforts.**
