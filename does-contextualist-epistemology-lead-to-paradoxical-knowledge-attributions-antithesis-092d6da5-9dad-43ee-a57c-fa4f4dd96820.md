# Does contextualist epistemology lead to paradoxical knowledge attributions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 092d6da5-9dad-43ee-a57c-fa4f4dd96820
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a situation where the same information is asserted as known in one context but not in another, leading to paradoxical claims about the same agent's knowledge status.**, **The shifting standards for knowledge result in paradoxes when two contexts overlap, causing contradictory attributions and challenging our intuitive grasp of knowledge.**, **Intuitively, knowledge should not be subject to paradoxical shifts merely due to contextual differences.**
