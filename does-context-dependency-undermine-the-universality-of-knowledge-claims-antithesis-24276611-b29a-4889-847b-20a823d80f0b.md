# Does context dependency undermine the universality of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 24276611-b29a-4889-847b-20a823d80f0b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity Condition](contextualism-with-sensitivity-condition-synthesis-dd26ba8e-d20d-4379-a4ae-bc06eaa28734.md)

## Content

**Suppose two knowers, P and Q, who share a belief but are in radically different contexts, one with rigorous academic standards and the other informal**, **P's rigorous context recognizes their knowledge, while Q's informal context does not**, **If knowledge depends on context, then any universal claim to knowledge is undermined by variable standards**
