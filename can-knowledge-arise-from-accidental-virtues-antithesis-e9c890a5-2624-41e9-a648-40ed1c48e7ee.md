# Can knowledge arise from accidental virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e9c890a5-2624-41e9-a648-40ed1c48e7ee
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which someone possesses a belief that is true only through accidental reliability of their trait, like a lucky guess that turns out correct. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that they do not possess genuine knowledge despite having a true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not be attributed to beliefs that are true due to accidental traits. This principle together with the idea that some traits may lead to true beliefs accidentally entails that knowledge cannot always be sufficiently grounded in intellectual virtues.**
