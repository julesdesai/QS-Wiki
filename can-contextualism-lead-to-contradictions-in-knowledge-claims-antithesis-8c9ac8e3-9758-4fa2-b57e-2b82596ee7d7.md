# Can contextualism lead to contradictions in knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8c9ac8e3-9758-4fa2-b57e-2b82596ee7d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which one person claims to know based on a certain context, while another, in a different context, claims non-knowledge of the same proposition. Intuitively, both claims cannot coexist without contradiction if they refer to the same epistemic state.**, **There is a plausible principle that if one knows a proposition, then it is true that they know it regardless of different contexts producing contradictory claims.**
