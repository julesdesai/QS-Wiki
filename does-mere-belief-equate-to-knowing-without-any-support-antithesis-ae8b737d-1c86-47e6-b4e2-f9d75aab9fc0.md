# Does mere belief equate to knowing without any support?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ae8b737d-1c86-47e6-b4e2-f9d75aab9fc0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Agent-Centric Justification](virtue-epistemology-with-agent-centric-justification-synthesis-7d83e915-f335-4c77-b1f8-3aa2f1497b9b.md)

## Content

**Consider person G who believes the Earth revolves around the Sun just because they happened to read it in an unreliable source, like a fictional story. While the belief is true, intuitively, mere belief doesn't seem to constitute knowing.**, **The idea that a person knows a proposition simply because they believe it invites the question of whether evidence or justification plays an essential role, implying mere belief might lack the needed support for knowledge.**
