# How can we reconcile fallible foundational beliefs with the notion of certain knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7109032a-1bb8-4800-bbdf-455fddb0a61a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Suppose a foundational belief is contextually reliable but fallible, and a true belief is based on it. Intuitively, we do not regard such fallible beliefs as capable of forming the basis for certain knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires a degree of certainty that cannot be provided by fallible foundational beliefs. This principle implies a potential conflict with contextual foundationalism.**
