# How does justification alone interact with the causal theory of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1baa2f69-ea46-4dc2-ba9b-987e265964de
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-916d423a-1723-4911-b4ab-5dfda1d6916a.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where Sarah justifiably believes a bridge can hold her weight based on engineering reports, and indeed the bridge holds her weight. However, if the bridge was about to collapse but was unknowingly repaired just before her crossing, her justification is disconnected from the actual state that makes the proposition true. Intuitively, one might judge that her justification does not suffice for knowledge due to the lack of causal connection.**
