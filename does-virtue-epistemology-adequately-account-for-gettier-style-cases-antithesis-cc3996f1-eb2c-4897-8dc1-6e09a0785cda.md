# Does virtue epistemology adequately account for Gettier-style cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** cc3996f1-eb2c-4897-8dc1-6e09a0785cda
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-1adc9ad4-a545-4f75-9435-0709a79c765f.md)

## Content

**In a scenario where someone forms a belief that their friend is in Paris because they see them there, while the friend is actually at home and the person in Paris is a look-alike, the belief is accidentally true because the friend decided last minute to fly to Paris, such a scenario raises the intuition that knowledge was not present despite intellectual virtue.**, **A belief can be true due to luck rather than the exercise of intellectual virtue, which suggests that intellectual virtue alone cannot account for true belief.**, **Even if a belief is formed through a reliable cognitive trait, truth can result from factors unrelated to the knower's cognitive abilities, challenging the sufficiency of crediting truth to such abilities.**
