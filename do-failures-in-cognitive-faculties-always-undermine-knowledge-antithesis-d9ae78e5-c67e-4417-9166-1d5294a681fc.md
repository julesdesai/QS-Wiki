# Do failures in cognitive faculties always undermine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d9ae78e5-c67e-4417-9166-1d5294a681fc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Summary](virtue-epistemology-summary-synthesis-6b17452f-daf9-4e36-a5b5-c7d2a1d34c57.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that individuals can know something despite minor lapses in cognitive faculties functioning properly. This principle together with the assertion that justification requires proper cognitive function suggests that some knowledge may exist without fully proper cognitive functioning.**
