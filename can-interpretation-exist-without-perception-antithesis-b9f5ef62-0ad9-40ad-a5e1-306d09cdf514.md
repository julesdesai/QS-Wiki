# Can interpretation exist without perception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b9f5ef62-0ad9-40ad-a5e1-306d09cdf514
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-04d59a43-df5d-4ff1-bc30-b363f66f9a03.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person imagines a complex social interaction and predicts an outcome without seeing it. In this scenario, intuitively, the act of prediction and imagination constitutes a form of interpretation without direct perceptual input.**, **There is plausibility in the idea that interpretative processes function independently when dealing with abstract concepts or foresight.**
