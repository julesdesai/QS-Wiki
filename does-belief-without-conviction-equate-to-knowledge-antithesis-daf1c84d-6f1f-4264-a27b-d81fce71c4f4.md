# Does belief without conviction equate to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** daf1c84d-6f1f-4264-a27b-d81fce71c4f4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Justification](contextualism-with-justification-synthesis-0a61842b-49a8-480a-aa14-aeb970bf43b5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a person casually believes a proposition without firm conviction, due to lack of strong evidence or contemplation. Intuitively, we would judge that mere belief does not amount to knowing without deeper conviction or commitment.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should stem from a deeply held conviction, challenging the view that mere belief suffices for knowledge.**
