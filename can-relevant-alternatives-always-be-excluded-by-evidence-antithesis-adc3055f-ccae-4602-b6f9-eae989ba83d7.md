# Can relevant alternatives always be excluded by evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** adc3055f-ccae-4602-b6f9-eae989ba83d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidence-Based Theory](evidence-based-theory-synthesis-3a180c4f-30b0-4e6f-87b5-c20a77cbb8ff.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person has strong evidence for a belief but cannot account for all hypothetical scenarios that could arise as alternatives. It intuitively seems unreasonable to expect any amount of evidence to exclude every conceivable relevant alternative.**, **Consider a person who has evidence their dog is in the backyard, yet cannot rule out the alternative that someone secretly swapped their dog with an identical one. The evidence may not suffice to exclude such unlikely alternatives.**
