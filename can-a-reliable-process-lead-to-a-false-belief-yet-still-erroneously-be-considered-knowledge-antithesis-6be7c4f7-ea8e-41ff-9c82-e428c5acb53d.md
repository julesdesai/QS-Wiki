# Can a reliable process lead to a false belief yet still erroneously be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6be7c4f7-ea8e-41ff-9c82-e428c5acb53d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-e2d53466-acc3-45a1-95cc-71c95728df76.md)

## Content

**A driver receives faulty weather predictions from a trusted weather app, forming beliefs about weather that align with the process but prove false. The presence of a seemingly reliable process suggests knowledge despite inaccuracy.**, **Consider a medical diagnosis based on a previously reliable test that has now become defective. Intuitively, trust in this process does not yield knowledge despite resembling prior reliable outcomes.**
