# Does externalism allow for epistemic luck to challenge justified belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9de313a7-6d41-4467-88bd-674286a6c6f8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-3f203114-9a4a-4d29-9451-dfb5538c3c76.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a meteorologist predicts the weather correctly by chance and not due to reliable weather data. Intuitively, we either have to say they are justified or lucky. If we say justified, then chance should count as knowledge. If we say lucky, then externalism fails to account for non-justified accurate prediction.**, **It is implausible that what we deem as luck should be equated with justified knowledge.**
