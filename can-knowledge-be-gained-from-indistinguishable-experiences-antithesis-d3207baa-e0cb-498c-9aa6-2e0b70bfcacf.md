# Can knowledge be gained from indistinguishable experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3207baa-e0cb-498c-9aa6-2e0b70bfcacf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-72a215c5-4d26-4e6e-86ab-383aabdf6290.md)

## Content

**There is a scenario where a person perceives a tree and simultaneously has an indistinguishable hallucination of the same tree. Intuitively, we would find it difficult to claim knowledge of the tree when both experiences are epistemically indistinguishable. This raises concerns on whether perception truly provides knowledge if indistinguishable experiences are possible.**
