# Can contextualism lead to contradictions in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 13406412-d1da-47da-949c-47798c16de27
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism adapts justification to situations](contextualism-adapts-justification-to-situations-synthesis-6de50ec6-d692-4487-9677-ea468fdc8bbe.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition p is true in context A, and they are justified in this belief. Intuitively, we might say the person knows p is true in context A.**, **Suppose in context B, the same person believes p but the justification from context A no longer holds. Intuitively, it seems the person does not know p in context B.**, **If justification varies by context, then it could lead to a situation where p is known to be both true and not true, violating the principle of non-contradiction.**
