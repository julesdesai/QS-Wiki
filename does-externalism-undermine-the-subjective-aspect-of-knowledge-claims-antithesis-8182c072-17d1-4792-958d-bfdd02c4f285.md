# Does externalism undermine the subjective aspect of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8182c072-17d1-4792-958d-bfdd02c4f285
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-3f203114-9a4a-4d29-9451-dfb5538c3c76.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals possess the same belief, justified externally, but only one has internal awareness of the justification. Intuitively, we would judge that only the one with awareness truly knows.**, **There is a plausible principle that subjective awareness is relevant for knowledge claims. This principle together with the lack of internal awareness for one individual entails that subjective elements are crucial.**
