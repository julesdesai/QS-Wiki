# Do unconscious processes undermine the reliability of perception in gaining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7e22c3e9-95b2-44b6-9484-1c9415f03d7e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism with Indirect Perception](critical-realism-with-indirect-perception-synthesis-e835ac77-9565-4215-bec3-25c08b025df9.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where unconscious biases significantly distort a person's perception of an event. Intuitively, we would judge that their knowledge of the event is unreliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliable perception is a necessary condition for knowledge. This principle, together with the influence of unconscious biases, entails that knowledge based on such perceptions is compromised.**
