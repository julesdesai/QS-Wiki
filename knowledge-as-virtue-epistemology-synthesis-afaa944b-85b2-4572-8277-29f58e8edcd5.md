# Knowledge as Virtue Epistemology

**Parent:** [Does availability of alternative evidence affect knowledge?](does-availability-of-alternative-evidence-affect-knowledge-antithesis-336914e3-d1c0-4568-9dd5-c3b1bb9f17a4.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a belief to count as knowledge, it is necessary for the true belief to be a result of the person's cognitive virtues or intellectual character traits**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief arises from an exercise of intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief manifests an intellectual virtue**
