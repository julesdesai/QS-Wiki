# Does the safety condition excessively restrict the scope of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3934c017-adb0-418e-90fc-4480ca805413
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-2598d7a5-1a67-4226-8c47-f390974be9e7.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person possesses extensive empirical data confirming a true scientific hypothesis, but in a nearby world, a freak anomaly creates false data, leading to belief in the false hypothesis. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows in the actual world because of their overwhelming evidence or that they don't know. If we say the person doesn't know about this case, then the safety condition severely limits instances of knowledge unduly. If we say the person knows despite near-world anomalies, then the safety condition adds little value.**
