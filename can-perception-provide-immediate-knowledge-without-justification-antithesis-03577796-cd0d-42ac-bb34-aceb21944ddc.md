# Can perception provide immediate knowledge without justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 03577796-cd0d-42ac-bb34-aceb21944ddc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism With Direct Perception](disjunctivism-with-direct-perception-synthesis-d8a83b95-9da3-4dc1-8003-0de7d598eb64.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that genuine knowledge requires some form of justification. This principle together with the claim that perception provides immediate knowledge without justification entails a contradiction with the idea that all knowledge must be justified.**, **Suppose we have a case in which someone sees a red apple and believes it is red. Without any inferential process, they claim to know it is red. Intuitively, we would judge that without justification, one cannot have knowledge in this case.**
