# Is private knowledge impossible in inter-subjective phenomenalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** dba1dbe5-ea63-4078-bd53-52b609a5d889
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Inter-subjective Phenomenalism](inter-subjective-phenomenalism-synthesis-367c4cba-19af-4964-9bd7-aba768d0b0b6.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an individual experiences a rare perceptual event that no one else observes. If knowledge requires confirmation by collective perceptions, then it is implausible for this individual to know anything about the event, even if it is veridical.**, **There is a plausible principle that personal perceptual experience can lead to knowledge. If validation through others is always necessary, this principle is violated, dismissing the possibility of privately acquired knowledge.**
