# Can knowledge originate without the exercise of cognitive virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06ac94ae-ba76-4e36-85c5-61657383fbf3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7681d6b8-18d3-4c19-95f1-d061b70d0c81.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person arrives at a true belief about a proposition through a lucky guess without exercising any cognitive virtues. In such a scenario, intuitively, we might judge this person does not know the proposition.**, **Cognitive virtues are character traits that reliably lead to truth, such as open-mindedness, attentiveness, and intellectual courage. A person can arrive at true beliefs through means other than these virtues, such as chance or luck.**
