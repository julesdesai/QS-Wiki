# Does contextualist epistemology adequately explain the stability of knowledge across contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 06b3e464-efad-41d5-8825-1d09219b46c5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Epistemology View](contextualist-epistemology-view-synthesis-0388cbe7-3574-492e-a015-951a4534f98e.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that whether someone knows a fact changes solely depending on the conversational context.**, **A plausible principle is that knowledge should be stable across different contexts without changing due to external conversational factors.**, **Contextual variability in knowledge attribution suggests that knowledge is unreliable and subject to external manipulation, which undermines its reliability.**
