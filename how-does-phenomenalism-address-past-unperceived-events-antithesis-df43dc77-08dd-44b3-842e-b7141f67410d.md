# How does phenomenalism address past unperceived events?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** df43dc77-08dd-44b3-842e-b7141f67410d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism Summary](phenomenalism-summary-synthesis-1942349a-6235-4079-9822-15fd8cc43040.md)

## Content

**Suppose paleontologists uncover fossils of creatures never observed by humans. Intuitively, we would judge that these creatures existed in the past.**, **If the reality of objects is reducible to possible perceptual experiences, there is no basis for claiming the existence of these creatures without prior perception.**
