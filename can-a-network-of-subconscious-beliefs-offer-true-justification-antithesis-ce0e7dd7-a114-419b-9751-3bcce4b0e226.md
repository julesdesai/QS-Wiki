# Can a network of subconscious beliefs offer true justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce0e7dd7-a114-419b-9751-3bcce4b0e226
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentism with Non-Conscious Elements](coherentism-with-non-conscious-elements-synthesis-025108b6-66b5-4784-9945-6373af308ffa.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person’s belief coheres perfectly with a network of subconscious beliefs, none of which are consciously held or evaluated. Intuitively, we would judge that such coherence does not provide true justification as understood through conscious reasoning.**, **There is a plausible principle that justifiable belief requires conscious interaction with and comprehension of belief networks.**
