# Can easy knowledge objections complicate the theory?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 686930b0-b2a0-467b-97f9-c5398dc6c5cc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Truth-Tracking Theory](nozicks-truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3749df9e-e568-414c-88ec-cc6b23580530.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person forms a belief about a proposition and also forms beliefs about related propositions in a similar manner. Intuitively, the ease with which these secondary beliefs are formed questions the robustness of the original claim to knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge claims should be critically scrutinized rather than easily multiplied. This principle, when considered alongside truth-tracking, might not hold up under scrutiny.**
