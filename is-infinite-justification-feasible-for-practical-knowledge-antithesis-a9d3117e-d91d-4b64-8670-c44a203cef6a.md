# Is infinite justification feasible for practical knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a9d3117e-d91d-4b64-8670-c44a203cef6a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Dynamic Infinitism Summary](dynamic-infinitism-summary-synthesis-65698d6e-d9bc-439d-bd28-3a216caa0bfd.md)

## Content

**Most practical beliefs are justified by bounded reasons, as people typically use finite evidence to justify their beliefs before acting.**, **In daily life, beliefs are often held with confidence based on finite justifications, not endless sequences that are impractically complex to evaluate.**, **An evolving sequence of infinite reasons would be too time-consuming to consider in real-world decision-making scenarios, where decisions must be made quickly.**
