# Is 'seeming' susceptible to illusions and hallucinations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 409cff23-1f33-41ff-b969-0d54dbeec946
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Conservatism supports knowledge based on the immediate 'seeming' of experiences.](phenomenal-conservatism-supports-knowledge-based-on-the-immediate-seeming-of-experiences-synthesis-043a7de6-376c-4af5-aacf-49b8c8b72c50.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone under the effect of a hallucinogenic substance experiences a 'seeming' that their friend is present when the friend is not there. Intuitively, these faulty seemings should not provide prima facie justification for their belief in the friend's presence.**
