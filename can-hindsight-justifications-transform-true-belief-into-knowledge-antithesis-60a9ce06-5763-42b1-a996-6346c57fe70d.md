# Can hindsight justifications transform true belief into knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 60a9ce06-5763-42b1-a996-6346c57fe70d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-71b6710d-dd35-475a-8146-f2431fcd7269.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person adopts a belief based on a false premise, but later finds justification that supports the now true belief. Intuitively, we do not judge that hindsight justifications transform it into knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification must be concurrent with belief formation for knowledge. This principle and the idea that after-the-fact justifications cannot retroactively turn true belief into knowledge entail that temporal alignment between belief and justification is essential.**
