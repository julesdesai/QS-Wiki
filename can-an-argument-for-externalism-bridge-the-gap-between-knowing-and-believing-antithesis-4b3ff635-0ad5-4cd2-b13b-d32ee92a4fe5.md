# Can an argument for externalism bridge the gap between knowing and believing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4b3ff635-0ad5-4cd2-b13b-d32ee92a4fe5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Knowledge without Belief](externalist-knowledge-without-belief-synthesis-c20b0285-a7bd-4197-bc3d-7153e215190d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual has knowledge of an event they experienced but cannot translate this knowledge into expressible belief without external evidence. Intuitively, this suggests that external evidence plays a critical role in forming belief.**, **It is implausible that pure cognitive processing, without integration with belief, satisfies the requirements for knowledge.**
