# Does varying justification lead to knowledge inconsistency?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** da2a4251-e3f3-4d00-9151-63810a1902e7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Standards-Based Contextualism](standards-based-contextualism-synthesis-00ac39b8-e543-4e05-a594-bb8273954856.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that justification should provide consistent support for knowledge across contexts. This principle together with varying justification standards implies potential inconsistency in identifying what counts as knowledge**, **A case where justification varies drastically between contexts provides intuitively inconsistent results about whether the same proposition is known or not, challenging stable application of knowledge**
