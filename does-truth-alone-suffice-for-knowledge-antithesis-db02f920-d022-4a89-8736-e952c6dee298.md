# Does truth alone suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** db02f920-d022-4a89-8736-e952c6dee298
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7b73a914-ea0c-4f4b-b314-725a0c878daa.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person happens to guess the correct answer to a question. Intuitively, we would argue that this does not equate to knowing the answer**, **Truth alone, detangled from reasoning or understanding, does not traditionally suffice for knowledge because it lacks the justification component**, **An accurate guess lacks an intellectual basis that connects the belief to the truth in a substantial way, thus undercutting the notion of knowledge**
