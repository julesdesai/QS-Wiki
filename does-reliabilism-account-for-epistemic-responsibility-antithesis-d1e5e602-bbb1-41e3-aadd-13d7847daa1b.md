# Does Reliabilism account for epistemic responsibility?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d1e5e602-bbb1-41e3-aadd-13d7847daa1b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-e69ee86a-1c0a-4d0e-840e-f25c67fd8e9a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person forms true beliefs without understanding the reasons why their belief-forming processes are reliable. If cognitive responsibility requires awareness and understanding of why one's methods are reliable, then merely reliably produced beliefs may fail to constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing requires an understanding or awareness of the reliability of one's cognitive process in forming beliefs.**
