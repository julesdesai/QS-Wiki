# Can misidentified causation still lead to a true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0232dea4-d54a-4bae-9af8-5e987193f52f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-c3b94507-0858-4bfa-a251-208b7829ae1f.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person believes the barometer falling causes bad weather and observes a storm, believing they have knowledge. In reality, the storm and the barometer are both effects of a common cause (low atmospheric pressure).**, **Intuitively, mistaken causation does not provide genuine causal knowledge even if the belief about the weather is true.**
