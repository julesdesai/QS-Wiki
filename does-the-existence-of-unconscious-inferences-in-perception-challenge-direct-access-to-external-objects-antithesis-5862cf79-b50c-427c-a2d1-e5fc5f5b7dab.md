# Does the existence of unconscious inferences in perception challenge direct access to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5862cf79-b50c-427c-a2d1-e5fc5f5b7dab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-0cfff75d-06de-4513-8b4e-49a340c018a9.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person looks at a picture of a cube and perceives it in three dimensions even though it is a two-dimensional image. Intuitively, we either have to say that perception involves unconscious inferences or question the reliability of direct perception. If we say perception involves unconscious inferences, then perception is mediated by cognitive processing. If we question the reliability, then we challenge the claim of direct access.**, **It is implausible that unconscious inferences align with the claim that perception is not mediated by representations.**
