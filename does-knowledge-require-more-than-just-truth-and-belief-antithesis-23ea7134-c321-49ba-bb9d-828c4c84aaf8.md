# Does knowledge require more than just truth and belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 23ea7134-c321-49ba-bb9d-828c4c84aaf8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism Summary](invariantism-summary-synthesis-0c8c051d-f89d-468b-87e6-c5a1fb54f910.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a doctor believes a patient has a certain illness based solely on a guess, and it turns out to be true. Intuitively, we either have to say that the doctor knows the diagnosis or that they don't really know it. If we say the doctor knows, then it seems we allow knowledge based on lucky guesses. If we say the doctor does not know, then knowledge requires more than just true belief.**, **This scenario suggests that there ought to be a requirement beyond truth and belief for a state to qualify as knowledge to account for proper justification.**, **A desirable principle is that for someone to know something, the truth of the proposition believed must be connected in an appropriate way to the reasons for the belief.**
