# Can someone know a truth without having complete evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35a6e005-fdae-4a86-b854-b7a3773c19ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Evidence Requirement](contextualism-with-evidence-requirement-synthesis-8260fba2-b1af-49c2-a4cf-403608671469.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone has a justified true belief about a proposition due to partial but supporting evidence. Intuitively, we might still consider this person to know the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that being justified about a true proposition can suffice for knowledge even if the evidence does not entirely secure the truth of the proposition.**
