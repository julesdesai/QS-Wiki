# Can intellectual virtues produce false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 071b63c3-9f91-450e-abca-bb426f8cd112
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cfcf3dee-48b2-4ff8-855e-aba5061f4a51.md)

## Content

**Consider the case of a person who exercises intellectual virtues like careful reasoning and evidence evaluation but concludes with a false belief due to deceptive evidence. Intuitively, we may judge that the presence of intellectual virtue doesn't necessarily guarantee true belief.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to count as knowledge, it must result in truth, which is not guaranteed by virtue alone. This principle together with deceptive evidence scenarios challenges the sufficiency of virtue for knowledge.**
