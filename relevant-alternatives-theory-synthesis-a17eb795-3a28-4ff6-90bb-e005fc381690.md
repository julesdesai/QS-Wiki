# Relevant Alternatives Theory

**Parent:** [Are there instances where knowledge is acquired without continuous tracking of truth?](are-there-instances-where-knowledge-is-acquired-without-continuous-tracking-of-truth-antithesis-f45c7712-72ea-4b33-afa2-41117ea29e38.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person believes the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the person can rule out relevant alternatives in which the proposition is false**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that these relevant alternatives conditions hold**
