# Can epistemic externalism handle Gettier-style cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5caa06db-66e2-423d-a3f9-cd301727788b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-da0a1d28-64c8-4b8c-8417-38bce9b3007e.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person believes a true proposition due to an external factor coincidentally aligned with a false intermediary belief challenges our intuitions about knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know the proposition.**, **External factors justifying beliefs do not resolve issues presented by Gettier-style cases, where true beliefs arise through luck.**, **It is implausible that a theory unable to address luck-based true beliefs accurately delineates knowledge.**
