# Can naturalized epistemology address the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e5d8c3b-031e-4fcd-92e1-70b77d969dbd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Naturalized Epistemology](naturalized-epistemology-synthesis-99d5c2d6-f993-4109-8adb-f973b72c0335.md)

## Content

**There is a scenario where a belief is both true and justified by scientific processes but involves an element of luck. Intuitively, we judge that the person does not have knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be accounted for when luck plays a crucial role in the truth of the belief.**, **The Gettier problem suggests that knowing requires more than truth, belief, and scientific processes for justification.**
