# Are interpretations and corrections genuinely capable of aligning perceptions with objective reality?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 08cf1af2-de1c-4372-aff8-3cddedffbc77
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism Summary](critical-realism-summary-synthesis-de08aecd-5e9b-4f9b-874a-d2dc97cb1697.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an individual initially perceives a distant mountain as a small hill due to fog, but later realizes it is a mountain when the fog clears. Initially, intuitive perception fails, requiring interpretation.**, **The subsequent correction requires external information that confirms the falsity of the initial perception.**, **These external corrections introduce the challenge of whether initial perceptions or interpretations could themselves be flawed.**
