# Does the safety condition wrongly exclude some true beliefs from being knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 04ae0b43-c55a-4239-912b-0e56bc89dea4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8bfe37cc-0831-4bcb-9f14-728b24c81cc0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person T forms a true belief in proposition S based on reliable reasoning and evidence.**, **In a slightly altered scenario, T's reasoning would lead to a false belief in S, due to factors outside of T's control.**, **There is a plausible principle that reliable belief-formation processes should typically suffice for knowledge.**, **This principle, together with the fact that T's belief could be false in nearby worlds, suggests that the safety condition may wrongly exclude T's true belief from being considered knowledge.**
