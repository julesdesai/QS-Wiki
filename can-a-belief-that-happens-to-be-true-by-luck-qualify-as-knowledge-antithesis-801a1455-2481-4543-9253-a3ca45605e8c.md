# Can a belief that happens to be true by luck qualify as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 801a1455-2481-4543-9253-a3ca45605e8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8e9332b1-e101-417c-8080-59c95e445ed8.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based on a false premise but the proposition happens to be true by luck. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge of the proposition.**, **For a belief to not easily be false in similar situations, the belief must be formed on a reliable basis. A belief formed purely by luck does not meet this reliability criterion.**
