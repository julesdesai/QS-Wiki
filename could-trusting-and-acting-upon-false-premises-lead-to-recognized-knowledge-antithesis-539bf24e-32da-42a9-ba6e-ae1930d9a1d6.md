# Could trusting and acting upon false premises lead to recognized knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 539bf24e-32da-42a9-ba6e-ae1930d9a1d6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [True Belief as Primary in Knowledge](true-belief-as-primary-in-knowledge-synthesis-f83d0a87-6cde-4f50-a75a-0024fde50cce.md)

## Content

**There are scenarios where people trust and act upon false premises yet consistently achieve desirable outcomes.**, **Acknowledging such outcomes as knowledge challenges the centrality of truth when other factors take precedence.**, **Truth's importance might diminish when false beliefs are practically reliable and lead to positive ends.**
