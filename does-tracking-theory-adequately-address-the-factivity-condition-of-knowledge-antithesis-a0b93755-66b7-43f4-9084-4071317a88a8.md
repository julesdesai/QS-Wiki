# Does tracking theory adequately address the factivity condition of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a0b93755-66b7-43f4-9084-4071317a88a8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-c73695dc-3706-4b4d-aea8-5e0696c46f82.md)

## Content

**The factivity condition states knowledge must be a true belief, yet people can have true beliefs without meeting the tracking requirement across close worlds, presenting potential conflicts**, **Imagine someone who accidentally bumps into truth due to anomalies in the environment, which challenges the condition that beliefs need to track truth to count as knowledge**, **The idea that knowledge is fundamentally about holding a true belief does not necessarily extend to the consistency principle of tracking across alternate circumstances**
