# Is practical sensitivity consistent with traditional views on truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 00069359-c664-4a17-9714-8715bd8d8a6d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism Summary](subject-sensitive-invariantism-summary-synthesis-1a7f6ba4-b667-492e-9754-ad3a76a8b666.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should primarily be concerned with truth and evidence, rather than with practical considerations. If practical stakes become a defining factor in knowledge, it could lead to inconsistencies with traditional conceptions where truth is paramount.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person possesses compelling evidence for a true proposition, yet the practical stakes fluctuate. Intuitively, this person's standing in terms of knowledge should remain intact if truth is the primary concern.**
