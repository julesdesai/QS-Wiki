# Does knowledge demand that intellectual capabilities be exercised actively?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fa45c03a-ba24-4271-b5d5-ad9f2aead2d0
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-5b73def0-7386-47a1-9b12-ba7bdc34b62a.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where someone has a true belief but acquired it passively, such as through subconscious processing or habit. Intuitively, we may still consider this as knowledge, even if they did not actively exercise intellectual virtue.**, **The requirement for knowledge to result from one's own intellectual capabilities seems to demand active engagement, challenging cases of passive true belief formation.**
