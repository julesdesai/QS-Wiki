# Does phenomenalism address perceptual adaptation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0e3445ab-5f2b-4bf4-8e03-13830ecc5ef8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person adjusts to seeing the world upside down due to wearing inverting glasses. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perceptual experiences initially lack coherence but gradually align across senses.**, **Perceptual adaptation, where initial experiences are incoherent but later become consistent, presents a problem for the claim that knowledge of the external world is based solely on coherent perceptual experiences.**
