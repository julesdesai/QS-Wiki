# Can indirect realism adequately address the problem of perceptual error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5b39984b-79f3-42fc-8d46-18dc1b980545
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-801f6a7d-c909-434a-bdd9-effe8ebd5964.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person hallucinates a non-existent object, such as an oasis in the desert. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person's mental representation does not correspond to reality.**, **There is a plausible principle that if perception is mediated by sensory representations, these representations can sometimes mislead us. This principle together with the idea that mental representations often fail to match reality entails that perception might not always reliably lead to knowledge.**
