# Does aptness account for beliefs formed in collaborative or distributed processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bc5e560a-5c35-4933-bd81-c998e74b1197
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Apt Belief](knowledge-as-apt-belief-synthesis-6ac0cb6c-f2b3-4636-9c8a-2a793aab4e4b.md)

## Content

**Some beliefs are formed within a collaborative or distributed setting, where individual competence may not directly yield the truth. Intuitively, in such cases, knowledge seems distributed or shared.**, **It is possible for a belief to be true in a group context due to the collective competence of many agents, rather than the sole intellectual competence of one person.**, **Knowledge attribution in collaborative settings challenges the idea that aptness must arise from an individual's competence alone.**
