# Knowledge as Proper Function Reliabilism

**Parent:** [Can a true belief formed by a reliable process still fall short of knowledge?](can-a-true-belief-formed-by-a-reliable-process-still-fall-short-of-knowledge-antithesis-f8a2e63d-ab0d-4bba-a74c-077056179bc9.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the cognitive faculties function properly in the environment for which they were designed**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief is produced by a reliable cognitive process, and the cognitive faculties function properly in the environment for which they were designed**
