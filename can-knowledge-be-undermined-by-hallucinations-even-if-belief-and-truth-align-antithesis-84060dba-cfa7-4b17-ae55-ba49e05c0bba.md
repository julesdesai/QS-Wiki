# Can knowledge be undermined by hallucinations even if belief and truth align?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 84060dba-cfa7-4b17-ae55-ba49e05c0bba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Function Theory](proper-function-theory-synthesis-66a6c079-b861-44cf-ac2d-ed1fae76b934.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on a vivid hallucination, yet this belief coincidentally aligns with the truth. Intuitively, we would not say the person knows the proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that hallucinations invalidate the reliability required for knowledge. This principle together with the occurrence of coincidentally true beliefs due to hallucinations questions the sufficiency of conducive environments for knowledge.**
