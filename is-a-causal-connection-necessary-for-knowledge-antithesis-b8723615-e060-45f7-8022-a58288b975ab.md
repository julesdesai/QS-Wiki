# Is a causal connection necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b8723615-e060-45f7-8022-a58288b975ab
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism in Epistemology](disjunctivism-in-epistemology-synthesis-4b61ef48-0e05-458a-ab35-9c24d16d77b9.md)

## Content

**Suppose someone learns a fact from a reliable source but where unbeknownst to them, the source is wrong and the person accidentally draws the correct conclusion for unrelated reasons. It seems they do not really "know" it in the deep sense implying a causal connection.**, **Knowledge typically involves a true belief that is sufficiently causally connected to justify the belief in ordinary, robust circumstances.**
