# Does coherence alone suffice to address the problem of infinite regress in justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5de17c69-e772-41cc-a1fa-63933fe88f0d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Constructivist Coherentism](constructivist-coherentism-synthesis-df66a247-3331-4fc3-9810-0103a6210363.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that coherence alone can prevent an infinite regress of justifications, as each belief in the system would require separate justification that relies on further beliefs, potentially leading to an endless cycle.**, **There is a plausible principle that any effective epistemological theory should provide a definitive stopping point to avoid infinite regression. This principle challenges the notion that coherence is enough to secure justification.**
