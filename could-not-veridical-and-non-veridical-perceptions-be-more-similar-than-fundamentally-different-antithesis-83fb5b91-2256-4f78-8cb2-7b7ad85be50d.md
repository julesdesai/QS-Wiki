# Could not veridical and non-veridical perceptions be more similar than fundamentally different?

**Node ID:** 83fb5b91-2256-4f78-8cb2-7b7ad85be50d
**Depth:** 4

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism resolves skepticism by distinguishing between veridical and non-veridical perception.](disjunctivism-resolves-skepticism-by-distinguishing-between-veridical-and-non-veridical-perception-synthesis-7789fcba-70c5-4267-a68d-acdfc3ec9b9e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person experiences a realistic hallucination that they cannot distinguish from reality based on sensory experience alone.**, **In this scenario, intuitively, if the experience is indistinguishable from veridical perception, then veridical and non-veridical perceptions might share significant similarities.**, **The premise that veridical and non-veridical perceptions are fundamentally different in nature might not hold if they can be indistinguishable in practice.**
