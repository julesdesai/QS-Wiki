# Does accessible justification account for the Gettier problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95dd2fc4-95a1-4466-89ed-90e74507086f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Hybrid Internalism](hybrid-internalism-synthesis-3d9ce0d9-6ba2-4665-a054-ed06708d6ee0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person has a justified belief that is true purely by accident, such as believing there is a sheep in a field because they see a convincing sheep-shaped rock, while there is a real sheep hidden from view. Intuitively, we either have to say that the person knows there is a sheep there, or that they do not. If we say that they know, this contradicts our intuition that knowledge cannot arise from luck. If we say they do not know, then justification and truth alone cannot suffice for knowledge without considering the source's accuracy.**
