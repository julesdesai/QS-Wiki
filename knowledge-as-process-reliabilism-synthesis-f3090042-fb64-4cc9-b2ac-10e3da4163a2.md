## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed by a process that is reliably truth-conducive**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed by a process deemed reliable by external standards**, **The reliability of belief processes is determined by objective criteria rather than the subject's own understanding**
