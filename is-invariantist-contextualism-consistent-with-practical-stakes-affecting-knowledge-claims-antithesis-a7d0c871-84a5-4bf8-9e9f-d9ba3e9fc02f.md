# Is invariantist contextualism consistent with practical stakes affecting knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a7d0c871-84a5-4bf8-9e9f-d9ba3e9fc02f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantist Contextualism](invariantist-contextualism-synthesis-72e0f6e6-e984-47a7-9872-225586edec54.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person is said to know a proposition when the stakes are low but not when the stakes are high, intuitively, such factors seem to affect knowledge.**, **Invariantist contextualism posits that the standards for knowing do not vary with different contexts, which implies stakes should not affect claims to know.**, **There seems to be a conflict between invariant standards and the intuitive influence of practical stakes on knowledge attribution.**
