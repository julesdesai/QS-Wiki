# Can perception operate without cognitive processing?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22abb9e3-795f-4f94-9437-f624d2e16050
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-dd7d3552-f5e8-48f4-9a89-f17b9eced87a.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that cognition is involved in interpreting perceptual data. This principle together with perception providing immediate access to external reality entails a contradiction.**, **Consider a scenario in which a person quickly identifies a complex scene, drawing on memory and prior knowledge. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that cognitive processing mediates perception.**
