# Does belief plus truth equate to knowledge without additional criterion?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 632e9583-6c23-47df-95ed-40aabcb6bd17
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Internalism](contextualist-internalism-synthesis-130e5ac9-b512-486c-b0ba-4156d7ff0e7d.md)

## Content

**If someone believes there is milk in the fridge and it is true because they saw it there yesterday, this does not ensure current knowledge.**, **This is because the current status of the milk requires present observation, not past belief, to establish knowledge.**
