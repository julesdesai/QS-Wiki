# Can a belief track truth in an epistemically inaccessible world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39a9d459-8009-4d77-972c-9d6774ff04fe
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory](nozicks-tracking-theory-synthesis-92e582e4-32a5-4017-b62b-ea7485c8afb8.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that tracking should occur in worlds accessible to the believer's epistemic perspective, but the tracking theory does not require the believer to be aware of such worlds.**, **Consider a scenario in which a belief tracks truth across worlds that are theoretically possible but completely inaccessible to any epistemic agent. It is implausible to claim that this is knowledge, as the agent cannot possibly know about these worlds.**
