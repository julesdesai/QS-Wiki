# Can someone believe a proposition and not know it if it is neither true nor useful?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5f9520c2-548b-4dec-bd92-e6ec96f2fb8e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatism with Coherentism](pragmatism-with-coherentism-synthesis-7996efa8-d25e-4d54-acc0-6469819e4077.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person believes a proposition that is neither true nor useful. Intuitively, it is clear that this belief does not constitute knowledge.**, **It is implausible that belief alone suffices for knowledge if the belief lacks truth and practical benefit.**
