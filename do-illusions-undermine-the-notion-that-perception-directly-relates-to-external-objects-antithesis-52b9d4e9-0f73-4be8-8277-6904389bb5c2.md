# Do illusions undermine the notion that perception directly relates to external objects?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52b9d4e9-0f73-4be8-8277-6904389bb5c2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism with Cross-Modal Integration](direct-realism-with-cross-modal-integration-synthesis-1a006bbb-612c-4e5a-ba7b-987e18fe12d2.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person sees a stick submerged in water, and it appears bent. Intuitively, despite the perception of the stick as bent, external knowledge reveals it is straight.**, **This scenario illustrates that direct sensory experience can conflict with the actual properties of the external object.**, **Illusions indicate that sensory experience can be misleading, challenging the idea that knowledge of the world is directly obtained through perception.**
