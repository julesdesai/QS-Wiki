# How does phenomenalism address the problem of indistinguishable perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5345ba87-b63f-4c3e-b74e-28b503371f97
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Cross-Modal Coherence](phenomenalism-with-cross-modal-coherence-synthesis-83c3e50e-d940-4bde-b547-d7163870772c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two identical objects are perceived visually, yet one is felt to be warm and the other cold without any visual indication. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perceptual experiences are not solely based on or explained by visual experiences only.**, **A situation in which visual perception cannot distinguish between identical objects in terms of temperature and touch senses creates incoherency in cross-modal perception.**
