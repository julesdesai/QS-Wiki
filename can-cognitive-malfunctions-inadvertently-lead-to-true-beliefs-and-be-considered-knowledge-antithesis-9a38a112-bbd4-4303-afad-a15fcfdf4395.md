# Can cognitive malfunctions inadvertently lead to true beliefs and be considered knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9a38a112-bbd4-4303-afad-a15fcfdf4395
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Functionalism Summary](proper-functionalism-summary-synthesis-5b044e3f-97d9-4386-9c2f-bd333212a4a8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a cognitive malfunction leads to a true belief, such as someone with a fever-induced hallucination who correctly believes it is raining. Intuitively, we would not deem this correct guess as knowledge.**, **A proper functioning theory requires cognitive faculties to operate as intended, and an unintended malfunction leading to a true belief underscores the failure of the design plan criteria for knowledge.**
