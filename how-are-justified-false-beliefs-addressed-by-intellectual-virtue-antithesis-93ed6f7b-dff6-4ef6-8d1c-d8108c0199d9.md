# How are justified false beliefs addressed by intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 93ed6f7b-dff6-4ef6-8d1c-d8108c0199d9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-31f81092-b7b3-48dd-9f17-87af421f7694.md)

## Content

**A person can exercise intellectual virtue and arrive at a justified belief that turns out to be false due to unforeseen factors.**, **An intellectual virtue can reliably lead to justified beliefs but sometimes those beliefs are defeated by new or hidden evidence.**, **The fallibility of justification through intellectual virtues shows that not all justified beliefs actually result in truth.**
