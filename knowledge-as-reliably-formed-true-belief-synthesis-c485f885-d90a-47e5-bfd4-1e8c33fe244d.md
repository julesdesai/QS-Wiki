# Knowledge as Reliably-Formed True Belief

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** c485f885-d90a-47e5-bfd4-1e8c33fe244d
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can intellectual virtues account for knowledge in all domains?](can-intellectual-virtues-account-for-knowledge-in-all-domains-antithesis-d93a0559-ae01-4706-8f58-87951b929bfc.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is formed through reliable processes**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed through reliable processes**
