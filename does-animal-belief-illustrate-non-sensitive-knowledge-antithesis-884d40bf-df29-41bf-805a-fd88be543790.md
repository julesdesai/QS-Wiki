# Does animal belief illustrate non-sensitive knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 884d40bf-df29-41bf-805a-fd88be543790
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitive belief theory of knowledge](sensitive-belief-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a04ea135-b51c-4804-8a79-c6b4af4a4910.md)

## Content

**Consider an animal which instinctively reacts to environmental cues, leading it to form true beliefs about danger but would not alter its reaction if circumstances negated danger. Intuitively, while the animal's belief assists survival, it lacks sensitivity to actual or false cues.**, **Non-human belief systems effectively leading to true outcomes, yet lacking counterfactual sensitivity, question the role of sensitivity in knowledge claims.**
