# Can a shift in context negate previously acknowledged knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d3b0b69c-88d2-4541-ba85-e7a5b728f548
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-25340e84-3972-4eff-b5ab-6d3b7cc92f60.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person knows a proposition in one context but does not know it in another due to contextual shifts. Intuitively, knowledge should not be so easily negated by context changes.**, **The idea that context changes alone can invalidate knowledge seems implausible.**
