# Can finite chains sometimes suffice for justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 62973325-1196-42c9-8f59-27a23d844040
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Pragmatic Constraint](infinitism-with-pragmatic-constraint-synthesis-44356aca-8ef0-4341-a1b2-865cb5949b96.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a belief with a finite and coherent chain of reasons supporting it. Intuitively, such a chain should suffice for the person to have justified belief.**, **It is implausible that having an infinite chain of reasons is always necessary for justification given the limited cognitive capacities of humans.**
