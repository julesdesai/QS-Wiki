# What about cases of epistemic injustice?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 33cdd3d9-af20-4f1c-97ad-527a852cd1bd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Responsibilist Virtue Epistemology](responsibilist-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d65854c6-8623-4bd4-aed3-f2a4bfae69c9.md)

## Content

**In cases of epistemic injustice, a person's responsible cognitive efforts are undermined by prejudice or bias.**, **Their beliefs might be true and responsibly held, but are dismissed or undervalued by others.**, **This raises doubts about whether they truly have knowledge.**
