# Does the variability of truth undermine objective evaluation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1879cc4-df5b-4529-9594-d1a613906f28
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Suppose a proposition is deemed true relative to one individual’s epistemic standpoint but false according to another’s. Intuitively, it suggests a lack of objective means to evaluate and resolve the disagreement.**, **If truth is dependent on the individual's standpoint, then it becomes implausible to maintain that any claim can be universally evaluated or adjudicated.**, **An objective framework is needed to address conflicts, but this framework is undermined if truth itself is always relative to personal perspective.**
