# Does accidental truth due to a misleading but normally reliable process result in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9c6d8c7e-1841-4ebe-bd98-1757c89431ce
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism about Knowledge](reliabilism-about-knowledge-synthesis-262d9553-ebaf-45bd-b20f-bae6bfba05f7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person reads a newspaper containing true information as part of a credible reporting process but reports on a topic manipulated by a government misinformation campaign. Intuitively, we question whether the person truly knows the information given the misleading origins despite the truth.**, **Reliability is assigned to general processes but does not resolve discrepancies caused by exceptional misinformation.]
