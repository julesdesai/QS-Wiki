# Can knowledge exist without the need for safety in socially shared beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c716eb3b-bed7-4ba5-81ef-3c53fc687232
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a community collectively holds a belief based on shared cultural practices that could easily be false individually but remains true in the communal sense. Intuitively, we either have to say that communal belief doesn't need individual safety or that communal trust modifies the impact of safety requirements. If we say communal belief doesn't require individual safety, then shared beliefs redefine safety importance. If we say communal trust affects safety, then social factors influence knowledge beyond individual belief safety.**
