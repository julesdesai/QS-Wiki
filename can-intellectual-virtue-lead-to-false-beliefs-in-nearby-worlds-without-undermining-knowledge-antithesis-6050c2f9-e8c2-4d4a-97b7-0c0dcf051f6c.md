# Can intellectual virtue lead to false beliefs in nearby worlds without undermining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6050c2f9-e8c2-4d4a-97b7-0c0dcf051f6c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safe Virtue Epistemology](safe-virtue-epistemology-synthesis-3c276837-d198-44b5-af3b-bceb9a7db592.md)

## Content

**Imagine a skilled chess player believing in a strategic move; she bases this belief on her skill, which is her intellectual virtue. In nearby scenarios, she could misinterpret her opponent’s strategy leading to a wrong move. Intuitively, the knowledge claim remains due to her virtue.**, **The emphasis on intellectual virtue suggests that knowledge can withstand errors in nearby worlds as long as the belief formation process is virtuous. This implies that safety might not be as critical in all instances of knowledge as intellectual virtue is.**
