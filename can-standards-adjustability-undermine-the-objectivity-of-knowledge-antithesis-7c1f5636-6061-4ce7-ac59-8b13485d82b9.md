# Can standards' adjustability undermine the objectivity of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7c1f5636-6061-4ce7-ac59-8b13485d82b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where two individuals evaluate the same evidence under different practical needs, resulting in different standards for knowledge. Intuitively, we might judge that knowledge claims become subjective rather than objective.**, **There is a plausible principle that objective knowledge claims should not be swayed by personal or situational factors. This principle, together with the adjustability of knowledge standards based on practical needs, suggests knowledge claims may lose objective standing.**
