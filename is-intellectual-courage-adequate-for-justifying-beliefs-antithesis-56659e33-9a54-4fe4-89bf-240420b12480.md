# Is intellectual courage adequate for justifying beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56659e33-9a54-4fe4-89bf-240420b12480
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology Coherentism](virtue-epistemology-coherentism-synthesis-fa375fb7-6e26-497f-b63d-bbaec5305ff8.md)

## Content

**Imagine intellectual courage leading to the endorsement of radical or minority beliefs without substantial evidential support.**, **Intellectual courage by itself might prompt engagement with risky or controversial topics, but it may not render those beliefs justified if lacking empirical foundation.**, **Exercising courage in intellectual pursuits might showcase the willing consideration of unconventional ideas, yet fall short of providing justification if those ideas are unsubstantiated.**
