# How does one epistemically distinguish between a knowledge claim and an arbitrary assertion in an infinite chain?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c0e38fae-445a-4c7a-bd3e-ed27c6b75938
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism and Non-Foundational Justification](infinitism-and-non-foundational-justification-synthesis-6eeef37c-3f39-4c5c-91c9-b90fbd483157.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a chain where each belief depends on a previous one without an endpoint. Intuitively, we must be able to distinguish a legitimate knowledge claim from arbitrary assertions in this chain.**, **Without distinguishable endpoints that serve as foundational beliefs, it is implausible to resolve which beliefs in the chain are knowledge claims versus mere assumptions.**
