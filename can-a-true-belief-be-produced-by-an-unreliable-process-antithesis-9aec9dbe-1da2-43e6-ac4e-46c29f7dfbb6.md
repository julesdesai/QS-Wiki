# Can a true belief be produced by an unreliable process?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9aec9dbe-1da2-43e6-ac4e-46c29f7dfbb6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-f1c9c7cf-a360-4007-8370-716223f3575c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person randomly guesses answers on a multiple-choice test and happens to get all answers correct. In such a scenario, intuitively, we would judge that the person does not have knowledge despite their beliefs being true.**, **A case exists in which a person holds a true belief because of a lucky guess and not because of a reliable cognitive process.**
