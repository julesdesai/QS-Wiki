# Are contextual justifications too subjective for consistent epistemological application?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4d17c61-2ef3-4493-80ba-8014c9da181c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**Consider two individuals who hold the same belief but in different cultural contexts, leading to different justifications.**, **A plausible principle is that epistemic justification should be objectively evaluable, but if too subjective, it would undermine the common epistemological standard for assessing knowledge claims objectively.**
