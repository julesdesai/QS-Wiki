# Are there cases where a person knows despite failing truth-tracking?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b4404a3e-26d5-441f-84d8-31e87b499a87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Truth-Tracking Theory](truth-tracking-theory-synthesis-3c1e7956-3dab-458a-877c-dcdbcd3fe2cd.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a person knows a mathematical truth that remains unchanged across all possible worlds but fails to fulfill the truth-tracking requirement due to disbelief under hypothetical scenarios. In such a case, intuitively, the person still possesses knowledge despite the belief not being counterfactually robust.**, **It is implausible to suggest that knowledge of immutable truths should depend on hypothetical changes, questioning the necessity imposed by truth-tracking.**
