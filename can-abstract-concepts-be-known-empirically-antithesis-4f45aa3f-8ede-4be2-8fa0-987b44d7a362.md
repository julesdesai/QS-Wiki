# Can abstract concepts be known empirically?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4f45aa3f-8ede-4be2-8fa0-987b44d7a362
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Conceptual Empiricism](conceptual-empiricism-synthesis-7b58485d-3ea5-4513-ba7e-4d4d19b630bf.md)

## Content

**Abstract concepts such as "justice" or "freedom" appear to be understood independently of any singular perceptual experience.**, **The understanding of abstract concepts like "justice" does not pertain to concepts derived from specific perceptual experiences.**, **Knowledge of abstract objects can exist without the application of concepts generated strictly from perceptual experiences.**
