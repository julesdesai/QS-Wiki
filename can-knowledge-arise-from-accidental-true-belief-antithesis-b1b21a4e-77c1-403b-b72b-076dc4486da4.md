# Can knowledge arise from accidental true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1b21a4e-77c1-403b-b72b-076dc4486da4
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology with Intellectual Virtues](virtue-epistemology-with-intellectual-virtues-synthesis-df6fcfd5-84ab-43e0-96af-1596992b4384.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on a guess, and the proposition turns out to be true. Such a scenario suggests that an accidental true belief is not sufficient for knowledge, as it lacks a connection to intellectual virtues.**, **Intellectual virtues require more than mere accuracy; they demand a reliable connection between the belief and the truth of the proposition. Without this reliable connection, a true belief seems to lack justification as knowledge.**
