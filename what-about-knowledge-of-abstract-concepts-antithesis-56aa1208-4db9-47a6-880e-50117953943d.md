# What about knowledge of abstract concepts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 56aa1208-4db9-47a6-880e-50117953943d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-628ba6c3-810c-40bb-af25-92e29ab1c990.md)

## Content

**Consider abstract concepts like numbers, moral ideas, or logical principles. Intuitively, we might judge that knowledge of such things does not derive from sensory experiences since they have no physical form.**, **Suppose we have a case in which someone grasps the concept of justice or infinity. Intuitively, we either have to say that their knowledge is derived from sensory experiences or from rational insight. If we say it is derived from sensory experiences, the abstraction lacks explanation. If we say it is from rational insight, then not all knowledge is sensory-based.**
