# Are indirect perceptual experiences sufficient for comprehensive knowledge of external objects and their properties?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 955a000d-2280-407d-9afb-b73240059f67
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone observes a bird through a telescope without hearing it sing. Intuitively, we would judge that this person lacks comprehensive knowledge of the bird, specifically its song, because their perception is indirect.**, **There is a plausible principle that comprehensive knowledge requires direct acquaintance with all relevant aspects. This principle together with relying on external objects inferred from perceptual experiences entails possible gaps in knowledge.**
