# Can invariantism account for moral epistemic responsibilities?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Epistemic Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-epistemic-standards-synthesis-0f962817-db98-42be-9977-f6a5e2bd4d1d.md)

## Content

**People possess a moral responsibility to discern truth in high-stakes moral scenarios. Intuitively, this suggests their epistemic standards might fluctuate with moral significance.**, **A principle posits that moral implications and responsibilities enhance the threshold for knowing.**, **If invariant standards apply, moral stakes should not alter epistemic duties, which opposes common moral intuitions.**
