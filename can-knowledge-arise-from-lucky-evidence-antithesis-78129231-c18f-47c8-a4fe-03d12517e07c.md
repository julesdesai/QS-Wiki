# Can knowledge arise from lucky evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 78129231-c18f-47c8-a4fe-03d12517e07c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Grounding Knowledge in Evidence](grounding-knowledge-in-evidence-synthesis-000c43a5-6d20-4fdc-9093-7ec5ca2a4c8d.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that if evidence leads to the truth of a belief by mere luck, it does not constitute knowledge.**, **Consider a scenario where a person forms a belief based on evidence that accidentally aligns with the truth, such as picking a number in a sufficiently shuffled lottery and then finding out it's the winning number. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that the person does not have knowledge.**
