# Does contextual support ensure the truth of a belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a65330d6-4fa5-466a-b6d6-1f8c25197aa7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-7feed83d-0716-412e-8434-831a7f45f379.md)

## Content

**A belief can be contextually supported despite being false, as different contexts might employ incompatible standards of justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that truth is an essential component of knowledge, implying that context-based support alone may not secure truth.**, **In cases where a belief is justified based on its context but is false in reality, the justification fails to align with the truth requirement.**
