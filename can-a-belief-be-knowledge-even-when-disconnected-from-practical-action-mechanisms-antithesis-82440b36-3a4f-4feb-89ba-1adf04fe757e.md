# Can a belief be knowledge even when disconnected from practical action mechanisms?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 82440b36-3a4f-4feb-89ba-1adf04fe757e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person can never act on a belief due to external restraints but still holds the belief that aligns with the truth across worlds. Even if the belief doesn’t manifest in actions due to restraints, it can still be classified coherently as knowledge because it tracks the truth accurately.**, **If knowledge requires acting on truth-tracking beliefs, then people under constraints can never know propositions they believe rightly, which seems counterintuitive.**
