# Does high stakes influence accessibility to justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d147049-5d6f-4901-907d-5562e6faf91f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-98e3a79c-8fed-46a8-a97f-357c8246f324.md)

## Content

**Imagine a student being told that passing an exam requires knowing every detail of the syllabus and that failing carries significant consequences. Intuitively, knowing every detail feels beyond accessible justification, hinting at altered standards due to high stakes.**, **Consider a similar student aiming simply to enjoy learning, with minimal consequence tied to their grasp of the syllabus content. Here, the standards of justification seem more accessible and less influenced by outcomes. It seems implausible to suggest justification changes with stakes when it comes to the accessibility of knowledge.**
