# Does our capacity to predict and influence events imply some knowledge of things-in-themselves?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7888c611-59a4-4c74-9082-4aed58ab3be1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Transcendental Idealism](transcendental-idealism-synthesis-de14e49b-50e4-409a-b85a-983076811c68.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that successful prediction and manipulation of phenomena indicate some understanding of the underlying reality. This principle, taken together with our practical successes in science and technology, suggests that we may have access to truths about things-in-themselves, contrary to the claim that external objects are unknowable.**
