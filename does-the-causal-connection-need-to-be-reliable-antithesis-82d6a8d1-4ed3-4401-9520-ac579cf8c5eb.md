# Does the causal connection need to be reliable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 82d6a8d1-4ed3-4401-9520-ac579cf8c5eb
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-83958a5b-d735-4d00-9b23-ae4f36a61779.md)

## Content

**A person receives information through unreliable means, such as a broken clock showing the correct time by chance, but still seems to possess knowledge.**, **The truth of the belief is coincidentally aligned with an actual state of affairs, but the causal process is unreliable.**, **Intuitively, it seems a person can 'know' something even if the causal link is compromised, as long as the belief is true.**
