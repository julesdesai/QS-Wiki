# Can the tracking theory overlook the strength of certain empirical practices leading to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2fbec55-1255-4549-be39-0fce11de3273
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Nozick's Tracking Theory of Knowledge](nozicks-tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-47fa7ee7-d3b9-4071-a8f0-ae9193194fb8.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that people know propositions based on highly corroborated scientific Theories. Belief persists in such theories across different worlds with true propositions aligning. When predictions fail, science evolves, not disqualifying knowledge but refining it.**, **If knowledge needs to track truth counterfactually, much of scientific praxis feeding into empirical knowledge becomes questionable, despite progressing through adaptive theory models.**
