# Is reliabilism susceptible to the new evil demon problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** fe0d159d-6219-4d6c-95ef-62003bb31ec8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism View of Knowledge](reliabilism-view-of-knowledge-synthesis-6d3edad4-2324-422d-a821-a6440e2e445e.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person is deceived by an evil demon, leading them to hold false beliefs. Intuitively, we still might regard their reasoning as rational if they use the same cognitive processes we take to be reliable within our reality.**, **A principle of reliabilism is that knowledge depends on the reliability of the cognitive process, which should be truth-conducive.**, **The new evil demon scenario suggests that reliability conditioned by truth is not sufficient to account for rational beliefs formed under deceptive conditions.**
