# Does immediate 'seeming' always provide justification?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 85b91906-7eb7-4d08-9cef-cb15e12e6b6a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenal Conservatism supports knowledge based on the immediate 'seeming' of experiences.](phenomenal-conservatism-supports-knowledge-based-on-the-immediate-seeming-of-experiences-synthesis-043a7de6-376c-4af5-aacf-49b8c8b72c50.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person feels very strongly that it is raining outside simply because they heard a faint sound of water, but it is actually sunny. Intuitively, the mere seeming does not provide genuine justification for the belief that it is raining.**
