# Can competing reliable processes result in different beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 67e0f9ea-b687-4502-b4b7-8021ddc7146a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism](process-reliabilism-synthesis-094c2ddc-bd9c-46c2-87b1-e486aaff8cb4.md)

## Content

**Suppose two different reliable cognitive processes are available, each leading to a different true belief from the same initial information. Intuitively, this situation raises the question of which belief constitutes knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if reliability leads to truth, contradictory truths from reliable processes cannot coexist. This suggests a challenge in determining which belief is knowledge.**
