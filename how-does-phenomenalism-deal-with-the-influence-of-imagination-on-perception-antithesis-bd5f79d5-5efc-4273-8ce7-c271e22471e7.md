# How does phenomenalism deal with the influence of imagination on perception?

## Content

**A situation in which a person imagines eating a lemon and experiences salivation and a sour taste without any real lemon is not a case where cross-modal perception is coherently explained by current perceptual experiences.**, **Imagined experiences produce sensory responses that are not explained by current perceptual experiences, challenging the coherence of cross-modal perception.**
