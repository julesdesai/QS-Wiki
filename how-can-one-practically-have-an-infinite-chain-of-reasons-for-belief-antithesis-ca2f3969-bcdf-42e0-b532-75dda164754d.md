# How can one practically have an infinite chain of reasons for belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ca2f3969-bcdf-42e0-b532-75dda164754d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism Summary](infinitism-summary-synthesis-a48a630f-d4e8-4dc2-b25a-518a64dbd1fb.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person claims to know a simple fact, like "the sky is blue." If each belief requires an infinite chain of reasons, it becomes practically impossible to possess or articulate such an infinite chain.**, **An infinite chain of reasons for any given belief presumes that humans can comprehend and manage infinite series, which is contrary to our finite cognitive capacities.**
