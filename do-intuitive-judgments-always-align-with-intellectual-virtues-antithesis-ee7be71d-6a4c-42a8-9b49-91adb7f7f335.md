# Do intuitive judgments always align with intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee7be71d-6a4c-42a8-9b49-91adb7f7f335
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-d110ebcb-cebc-4b41-b013-8e3cca593b6d.md)

## Content

**In moral dilemmas, people can solve problems with strong intuitive understanding without requiring complex reasoning or other intellectual virtues**, **Solving puzzles using insight can result in true beliefs, yet it might not align with other intellectual virtues like detailed reasoning or analysis, which questions the necessity of multiple virtues for a belief to be knowledge**, **When perceiving someone’s facial expression accurately without conscious reasoning, this insight-based belief can be true without overt cognitive effort, raising questions on how insights qualify as intellectual virtues**
