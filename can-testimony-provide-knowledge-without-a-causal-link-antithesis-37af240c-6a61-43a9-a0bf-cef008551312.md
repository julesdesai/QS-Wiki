# Can testimony provide knowledge without a causal link?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 37af240c-6a61-43a9-a0bf-cef008551312
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal theory of knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-cf478e0f-32fe-4979-ba4a-203ad9c242c7.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible scenario where testimony provides knowledge without a direct causal connection between the fact and the listener's belief. For example, a person can gain knowledge of historical events purely through reading trustworthy accounts.**, **Consider a person who believes that Abraham Lincoln was the 16th President of the United States based on history books. Intuitively, this person knows this fact despite lacking a direct causal link to the historical events themselves.**
