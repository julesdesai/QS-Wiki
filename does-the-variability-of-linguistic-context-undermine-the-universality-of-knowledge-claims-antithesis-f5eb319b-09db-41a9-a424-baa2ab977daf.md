# Does the variability of linguistic context undermine the universality of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f5eb319b-09db-41a9-a424-baa2ab977daf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-1e9d0e24-887b-4fad-bf5c-71b82495d573.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge claims should have a universal validity irrespective of linguistic variations. This principle together with the idea that linguistic context can alter knowledge suggests that knowledge claims lack universality.**, **Consider a case where two individuals claim to know the same fact, but linguistic context differences lead to one being seen as not knowing. Intuitively, it is counterintuitive to accept that one person's knowledge is legitimate while the other's is not without an evidential difference.**
