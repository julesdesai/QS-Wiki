# Could a person know something if their belief was easily false in a slightly different situation?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55ad0b94-932d-4e9c-8140-53b78bc27427
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-8bfe37cc-0831-4bcb-9f14-728b24c81cc0.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person P believes a proposition Q based on strong evidence, but in a slightly different situation with nearly the same evidence, Q would be false.**, **Intuitively, we would judge that P knows Q in the actual scenario due to the strong evidence, even though their belief could easily be false in a slightly different situation.**, **If we say knowledge requires that belief could not have easily been false in similar scenarios, then P would not know Q, which seems counterintuitive.**
