# How does phenomenalism handle non-sensory cognitive states?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c9ecdbd7-4157-489b-a6a4-775049c09637
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-eea6cb23-77a9-47a4-87da-736833e1953e.md)

## Content

**Cognitive states, such as beliefs or desires, do not necessarily involve direct sensory experiences.**, **A person might believe in the law of non-contradiction without any sensory experience of contradiction, suggesting cognitive states can be independent of perception.**, **Intuitively, cognitive states about abstract ideas or logical principles transcend simple sensory appearances.**
