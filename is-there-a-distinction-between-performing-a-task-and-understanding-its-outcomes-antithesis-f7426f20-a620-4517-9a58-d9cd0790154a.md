# Is there a distinction between performing a task and understanding its outcomes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f7426f20-a620-4517-9a58-d9cd0790154a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Functionalism with Cognitive Task Fulfillment](epistemic-functionalism-with-cognitive-task-fulfillment-synthesis-3edd3165-e9fd-4e05-81df-6b63dbbb89eb.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scenario where a person correctly solves a complex mathematical problem using tools they do not understand. Intuitively, we might hesitate to say they know the solution.**, **A plausible principle is that understanding is essential to knowledge, challenging the idea that mere task fulfillment is adequate.**
