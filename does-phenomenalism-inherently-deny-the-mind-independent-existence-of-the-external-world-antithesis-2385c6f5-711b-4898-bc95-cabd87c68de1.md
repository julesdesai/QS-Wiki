# Does phenomenalism inherently deny the mind-independent existence of the external world?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2385c6f5-711b-4898-bc95-cabd87c68de1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism](phenomenalism-synthesis-9cd8e1c9-9ac3-4393-aab4-24acb9d44183.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a tree falls in a forest with no one to hear it fall. Intuitively, the tree's fall should be said to happen regardless of sensory experiences of it**, **It is implausible that external events or objects do not occur if unperceived, challenging the reducibility of physical objects and events to sensory insights**
