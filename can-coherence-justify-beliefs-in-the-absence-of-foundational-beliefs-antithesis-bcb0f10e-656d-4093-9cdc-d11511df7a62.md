# Can coherence justify beliefs in the absence of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bcb0f10e-656d-4093-9cdc-d11511df7a62
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Foundationalism](coherentist-foundationalism-synthesis-815fa62f-5e40-4962-8349-ea400fc3f5ce.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person's belief system is entirely coherent but lacks any clearly identifiable foundational beliefs that serve as the bedrock of their belief structure. Intuitively, we would judge that coherence might still justify beliefs.**, **There is a plausible principle that maintaining coherence does not necessarily entail clearly identifiable foundational beliefs, for coherence can sometimes be sustained by mutually reinforcing beliefs.**
