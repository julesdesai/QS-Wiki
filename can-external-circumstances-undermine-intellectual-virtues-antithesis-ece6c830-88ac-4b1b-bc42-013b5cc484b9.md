# Can external circumstances undermine intellectual virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ece6c830-88ac-4b1b-bc42-013b5cc484b9
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology View](virtue-epistemology-view-synthesis-28ac8895-184f-4b0d-8051-e8e35e6bae56.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a person has a trait usually deemed virtuous, like perception, but external circumstances systematically mislead the person, such as optical illusions. Intuitively, we would judge that their mistaken beliefs do not constitute knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is undermined by conditions causing systematic unreliability. This principle together with external circumstances that hinder reliability suggests that virtues are not always sufficient to confer knowledge.**
