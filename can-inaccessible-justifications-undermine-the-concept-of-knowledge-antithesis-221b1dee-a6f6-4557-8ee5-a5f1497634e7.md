# Can inaccessible justifications undermine the concept of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 221b1dee-a6f6-4557-8ee5-a5f1497634e7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism about Justification](contextualism-about-justification-synthesis-1dd27965-c99f-4b62-ae25-50f61d9bceba.md)

## Content

**A case involves a person having a belief with proper contextual justifications that are not accessible to them consciously.**, **It is plausible to say that if justifications are not consciously accessible, the belief does not genuinely satisfy the conditions for knowledge, as knowledge traditionally requires the knower's awareness of the justification.**
