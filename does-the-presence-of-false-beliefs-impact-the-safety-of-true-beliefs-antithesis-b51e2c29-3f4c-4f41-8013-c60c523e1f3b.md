# Does the presence of false beliefs impact the safety of true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b51e2c29-3f4c-4f41-8013-c60c523e1f3b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Theory of Knowledge](safety-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-a0cd1fa5-c1bb-4980-9053-18fe84acbd7d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person holds several beliefs, some true and some false, with the true beliefs being accidentally rather than systematically confirmed in nearby possible worlds. Intuitively, we would judge that the presence of many false beliefs could impact the safety of the true belief.**, **It is plausible that the coexistence of false beliefs alongside true ones may undermine the safety condition required for knowing the truth.**
