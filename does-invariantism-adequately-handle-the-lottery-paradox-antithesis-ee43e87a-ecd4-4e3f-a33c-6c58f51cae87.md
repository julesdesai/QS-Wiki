# Does invariantism adequately handle the lottery paradox?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ee43e87a-ecd4-4e3f-a33c-6c58f51cae87
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Invariantism with Fixed Standards](invariantism-with-fixed-standards-synthesis-ae6b0836-0ebf-42ff-aea0-150b72c8cdbe.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes they will not win a large, fair lottery based on statistical probability. Despite the proposition being true, intuitively, the person does not know they will lose.**, **The claim that a person knows a proposition if they believe it and the proposition is true implies knowledge is attributed here.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowing something requires more than just statistical likelihood, such as certainty or near-certainty.**
