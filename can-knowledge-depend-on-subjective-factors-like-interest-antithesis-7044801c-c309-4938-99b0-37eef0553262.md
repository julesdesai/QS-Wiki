# Can knowledge depend on subjective factors like interest?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7044801c-c309-4938-99b0-37eef0553262
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-9a2045c2-a10d-4a36-b3ae-c0c629913ced.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where a student knows a mathematical formula while uninterested in applying it, and another student is keen on applying it but lacks understanding. Intuitively, both their knowledge statuses should rely on their understanding, not their interest levels.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is an objective state that depends on epistemic factors rather than subjective ones like interest or motivation.**, **It is implausible to concede that subjective factors such as interest could determine the presence of knowledge, given its traditionally objective nature.**
