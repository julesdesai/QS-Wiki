# Is coherence sufficient for knowledge if beliefs are indoctrinated?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ccc6d6c6-8f94-4ff6-90ae-c9e9656170d7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherentist Knowledge Summary](coherentist-knowledge-summary-synthesis-1fa3c944-9677-491a-8edd-ea154ce998c7.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a coherent set of beliefs is formed through indoctrination. Intuitively, these beliefs might be justified but manufactured.**, **There is a plausible principle that genuine justification involves critical evaluation, which indoctrinated beliefs lack. Coherence derived this way could fail to provide robust grounds.**
