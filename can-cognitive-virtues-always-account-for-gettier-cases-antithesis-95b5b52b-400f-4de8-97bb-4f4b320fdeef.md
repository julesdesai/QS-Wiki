# Can cognitive virtues always account for Gettier cases?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 95b5b52b-400f-4de8-97bb-4f4b320fdeef
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-db8e4632-83fc-4e14-a2b9-f64cf1d5bb50.md)

## Content

**A Gettier case in which a person believes a true proposition based on evidence that is true, but the connection to the truth is coincidental, undermines the reliability of cognitive virtues.**, **Such cases highlight that cognitive virtues as dispositions do not adequately distinguish between justified true belief and knowledge.**
