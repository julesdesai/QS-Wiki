# Are there cases where stable belief doesn't imply reliability?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 189baddd-11d7-4880-ae4b-ad95f97b7567
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliability Theory of Knowledge](reliability-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-fa98d716-d2d9-4374-84c7-532726e6d89c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has held the belief that the world is flat based on unreliable historical texts despite contrary evidence now known. The person has consistently held this belief without wavering over time.**, **There is a plausible principle that a stable belief over time should reflect reliability. This principle together with the fact that the belief in a flat Earth contradicts established evidence challenges the definition of reliability in terms of stability.**
