# Can testimony always serve as a sufficient basis for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a6ebdce3-7dae-4643-86f4-68179d7762c1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Testimonial Justification Theory](testimonial-justification-theory-synthesis-a0572f9e-ea0a-45f6-9bf1-a16e71038692.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition solely because someone else told them. In this scenario, intuitively we would judge that their belief lacks sufficient basis for knowledge if the other's testimony is unreliable.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only true belief but also reliable justification. This principle together with reliance solely on another's testimony entails that testimonial justification may be insufficient if the source is untrustworthy.**
