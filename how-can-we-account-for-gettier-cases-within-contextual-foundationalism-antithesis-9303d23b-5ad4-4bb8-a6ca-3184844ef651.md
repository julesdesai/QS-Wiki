# How can we account for Gettier cases within contextual foundationalism?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 9303d23b-5ad4-4bb8-a6ca-3184844ef651
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism Summary](contextual-foundationalism-summary-synthesis-82677206-c7b6-4da5-9f54-81e898121db3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person believes a proposition based on a justified, contextually reliable foundational belief, yet the belief turns out to be true by luck. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not truly know the proposition.**, **It is implausible that having a true belief based on contextually reliable foundational beliefs is sufficient for knowledge in such cases.**
