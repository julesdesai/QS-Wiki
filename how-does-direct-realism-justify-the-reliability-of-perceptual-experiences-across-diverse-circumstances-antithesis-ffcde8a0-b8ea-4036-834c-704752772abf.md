# How does Direct Realism justify the reliability of perceptual experiences across diverse circumstances?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ffcde8a0-b8ea-4036-834c-704752772abf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism](direct-realism-synthesis-871ccd9f-a5fb-4a37-9e5c-b4e161a3482d.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person with altered sensory abilities, such as colorblindness, perceives colors differently from others. Intuitively, we would judge the reliability of their perception as context-dependent rather than universally direct.**, **A plausible principle is that factual knowledge should be uniformly accessible if perceptual experiences provide direct access. This principle together with varying reliability in perception challenges the uniformity claim.**
