# Is accessibility of justification crucial for recognizing knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 7071f216-5b7f-403b-b1b2-ea3b51174431
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Justification with Causal Connection](externalist-justification-with-causal-connection-synthesis-0c02280c-1b97-4965-a35c-599f5b7ce400.md)

## Content

**Suppose there is a true belief formed by an intuitive but inaccessible mental faculty, like a subconscious computation. Intuitively, we would either have to say that the belief is not knowledge because of inaccessibility or deny that accessibility is required for knowledge.**, **It is implausible that inaccessible justifications can qualify beliefs as knowledge.**
