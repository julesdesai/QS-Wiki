# Does contextualism address disagreements over knowledge assertions adequately?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 64555107-1e02-4c56-ba01-80a2e9e92edf
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contexualism](contexualism-synthesis-bf8a09d9-c931-48c5-bb27-11fdcf496a81.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where one person asserts knowledge of a proposition and another denies it based on differing contexts. Intuitively, this raises the problem of whether there can be genuine disagreement about knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that disputes over knowledge claims should represent genuine disagreements rather than mere misunderstandings.**, **If contextualism allows both parties to be correct, it could trivialize disagreements about knowledge.**
