# Do perceptions of hallucinations question the reliability of all indirect perceptual experiences?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5150f81a-04d4-49d6-9ed3-e51b158cdb85
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism](indirect-realism-synthesis-197f3805-c8e1-415f-87ee-9ac547e71c62.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person hallucinates seeing someone who is not present. Intuitively, either this experience must be deemed unreliable, or indirect as a source for truth. If we claim it is unreliable, then this undermines the general reliability of experiences. If we claim it still holds some truth, it questions the value of indirect experiences.**, **It is implausible that all indirect perceptual experiences are reliable if hallucinations can lead to incorrect conclusions about external reality.**
