# Do habitual expectations limit the scope of possible knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** a59ae35b-e54e-4624-957a-dccc0868608a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism and Habitual Expectation](phenomenalism-and-habitual-expectation-synthesis-b8f38042-2386-4875-a954-8efd563e1c6e.md)

## Content

**Imagine an individual who, through repeated exposure, develops a strong expectation that the sun rises in the east. However, this expectation prevents them from exploring other possibilities, such as alternative cosmic phenomena. Intuitively, this limits their scope of understanding.**, **A plausible principle is that habitual expectations can constrain open-ended inquiry and exploration. This principle suggests that knowledge limited by habitual expectation may be incomplete.**
