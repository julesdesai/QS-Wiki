# Is coherence sufficient for distinguishing between real and fictional scenarios?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c5280b98-7bfb-4f14-a860-d1b3d29bc0da
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Coherence Theory of Knowledge](coherence-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-3d6de9a5-e71e-464f-88a2-666531bfab5e.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where a detective has a coherent set of beliefs about a crime that is based entirely on fictional events. Intuitively, we recognize that coherence alone does not move the beliefs closer to truth unless grounded in factual evidence.**, **It is implausible that coherence without a connection to empirical evidence can ensure knowledge, as fictional narratives often exhibit high degrees of internal consistency.**
