# Does requiring an infinite chain create an infinite regress problem?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 587469ed-048a-42cd-96ca-8f059dbf6fab
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Infinitism](knowledge-as-infinitism-thesis-1ccb10a4-65b9-4ca2-911c-7c3a5e2f3c55.md)

## Content

**An infinite regress occurs when a proposition requires further justification ad infinitum, causing the justification to never be completed.**, **Requiring an infinite chain of reasons for a justification inherently leads to an infinite regress, implying that knowledge claims are permanently in process and never truly finalized as knowledge, which is implausible.**
