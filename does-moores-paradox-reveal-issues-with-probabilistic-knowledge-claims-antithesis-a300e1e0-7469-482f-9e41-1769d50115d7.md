# Does Moore's paradox reveal issues with probabilistic knowledge claims?

## Content

**Moore's paradox involves asserting "It is raining, but I don't believe it is," illustrating a disconnect in belief and justification.**, **Probabilistic foundations allow for justified belief about states of the world, yet Moorean statements seem to indicate an absence of complete knowledge despite justified, true belief.**, **The ability to rationally declare a true belief without complete convergence of belief and justification reveals that probabilistic models might miss integral knowledge components.**
