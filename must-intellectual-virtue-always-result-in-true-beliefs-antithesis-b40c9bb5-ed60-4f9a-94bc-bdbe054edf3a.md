# Must intellectual virtue always result in true beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b40c9bb5-ed60-4f9a-94bc-bdbe054edf3a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-35af9163-7530-4447-9474-ccb688222939.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person exercises intellectual virtue through rigorous analysis but ends up with a false belief due to deception. In such a scenario, intuitively we might judge that the intellectual virtue did not guarantee truth.**, **There is a plausible principle that intellectual virtue should consistently lead to truth, yet deception can cause the opposite, questioning the relationship between virtue and truth.**
