# Can justification truly depend on what is at stake?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** bed50ac7-b807-4e62-96c3-dd069399ff81
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Encroachment](pragmatic-encroachment-synthesis-98e3a79c-8fed-46a8-a97f-357c8246f324.md)

## Content

**Suppose a medical professional confirms with evidence that a patient needs a particular operation, facing significant risk if withheld. Intuitively, the stakes raise justification standards.**, **Now consider if the procedure is for a minor condition with minimal future impact, where evidence suffices despite low stakes. Intuitive correlation between stakes and knowledge justification feels unwarranted.**, **Justification must fundamentally depend on evidence, not stake-related consequences, challenging the assertion that stakes influence justification accessibly.**
