# Is a belief purely motivated by authority still countable as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 364855de-b87a-4241-9dab-9d6dcf34b0d1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatically Useful True Belief](pragmatically-useful-true-belief-synthesis-e72da4db-a488-4cc6-b424-04d735c0cd87.md)

## Content

**A student believes a historical date is accurate solely based on a teacher's assertion, without independent verification.**, **Despite its truth and potential ramifications for the student's academic success, reliance solely on authority problematizes its categorization as personal knowledge.**, **It's plausible that independent verification or understanding is a necessary component of authentic knowledge.**
