# Is there a problem with reliable false theories leading to reliable beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5806005f-c36d-4a7e-a47f-1034ff40bf3f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism with Emphasis on Reliable Cognitive Processes](reliabilism-with-emphasis-on-reliable-cognitive-processes-synthesis-2f8f29dd-ab81-4b6d-be7e-c16b4daa0bb6.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scientific theory, later found false, was reliably used to produce beliefs that were consistently true. Intuitively, one might question whether such beliefs can be considered as knowledge since the foundational theory was incorrect.**, **It is implausible to consider the output of a false theory as knowledge, even when the outputs happen to be true. Process reliability should align with a true foundational understanding to justify knowledge.**
