# Does reliability guarantee knowledge if accidentally true beliefs are involved?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 391fe1a3-5155-4286-8905-1a5de8f6873b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism Approach to Knowledge](reliabilism-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-96775f82-86aa-4964-9f14-0e86944bf355.md)

## Content

**Imagine a case where someone randomly guesses a true answer and guesses are generally reliable because they often guess correctly. Intuitively, this guessing does not constitute knowledge.**, **A true belief produced by a reliable guessing process may fail to be knowledge due to the accidental nature of the belief's truth.**
