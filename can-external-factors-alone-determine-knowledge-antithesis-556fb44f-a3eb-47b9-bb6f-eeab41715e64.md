# Can external factors alone determine knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 556fb44f-a3eb-47b9-bb6f-eeab41715e64
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Epistemic Externalism](epistemic-externalism-synthesis-539989cd-f86f-4268-aeb1-80f5fff257d5.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that conditions external to an individual's awareness alone can fully determine the possession of knowledge.**, **Consider a situation in which two people have identical beliefs, one formed reliably and one unreliably, but they both lack knowledge of the reliability of their processes. Intuitively, we might judge that their epistemic status should not differ solely based on external factors beyond their awareness.**
