# Knowledge as Virtue Reliabilism with Understanding

**Parent:** [How can we distinguish between reliable processes and epistemically virtuous processes?](how-can-we-distinguish-between-reliable-processes-and-epistemically-virtuous-processes-antithesis-47c86b76-eeb0-463a-8b47-de02eba2cd92.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief results from a reliable cognitive process**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief includes understanding of the truth**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, their belief results from a reliable cognitive process, and they have understanding of the truth**
