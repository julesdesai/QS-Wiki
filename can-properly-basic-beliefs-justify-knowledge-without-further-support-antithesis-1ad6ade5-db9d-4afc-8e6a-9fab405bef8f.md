# Can properly basic beliefs justify knowledge without further support?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ad6ade5-db9d-4afc-8e6a-9fab405bef8f
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Proper Foundationalism](proper-foundationalism-synthesis-115cfa71-603e-48b1-bce9-39f7dced12e3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person has a properly basic belief that does not align with any further evidence or reasoning. Intuitively, we would not judge this belief as justified knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a belief requires supporting reasons or evidence beyond itself to count as genuine knowledge. This principle together with the idea that justification solely relies on properly basic foundational beliefs entails that other support is not necessary.**, **It is implausible that beliefs without further confirmation or reconciliation with reality could constitute foundational knowledge when they remain unchallenged or isolated.**
