# Virtue Epistemology with Allowance for Future Overturning

**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Can intellectual virtues lead to false beliefs yet still constitute knowledge?](can-intellectual-virtues-lead-to-false-beliefs-yet-still-constitute-knowledge-antithesis-e545ae97-73aa-4684-a1ff-a9667e26a312.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true at the time of belief**, **For a person to know a proposition, their belief must be the result of an exercise of their intellectual virtues**, **An intellectual virtue is a trait that reliably yields true beliefs**, **The proposition may be overturned by future evidence without negating past knowledge**
