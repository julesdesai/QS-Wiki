# Contextualism in knowledge ascriptions

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** a9ff3654-89b5-4565-83c0-49bbc0106814
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Does misleading evidence invalidate knowledge?](does-misleading-evidence-invalidate-knowledge-antithesis-2e5691f3-9d39-49da-9645-45c1d989d160.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that knowledge attributions depend on the context in which they are expressed**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, and the knowledge standards set by the context are met**
