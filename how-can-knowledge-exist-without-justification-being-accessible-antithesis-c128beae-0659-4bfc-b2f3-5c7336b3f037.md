# How can knowledge exist without justification being accessible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c128beae-0659-4bfc-b2f3-5c7336b3f037
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-4b0c5095-8474-46c5-ad2b-26df0561fddc.md)

## Content

**Intuitively, knowledge requires that a person can access the justification for their beliefs.**, **A person might have a true belief without knowing why it is true if justification is not accessible to the knower.**, **Cases can be imagined where there is a true belief caused by a reliable process, but it is implausible that these are cases of knowledge without accessible justification.**
