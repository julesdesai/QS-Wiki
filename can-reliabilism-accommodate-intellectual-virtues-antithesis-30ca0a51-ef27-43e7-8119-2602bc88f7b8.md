# Can Reliabilism Accommodate Intellectual Virtues?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 30ca0a51-ef27-43e7-8119-2602bc88f7b8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilist Theory of Knowledge](reliabilist-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-7464bdee-3927-47a4-ac75-4e52dcea682f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a belief is reliably generated but the believer lacks intellectual virtues such as open-mindedness or diligence. It is implausible that such beliefs constitute genuine knowledge if intellectual virtues are entirely absent.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge involves not just reliable belief production but also intellectual virtues which reliabilism overlooks.**
