# Could an appeal to contextual standards obscure the importance of truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4c436852-2aa4-4f3b-9ca4-6361b0b68baa
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge as Contextualism](knowledge-as-contextualism-synthesis-98d1787d-f33b-4a95-b2e8-91902f0e81be.md)

## Content

**If contextualism places a heavy emphasis on the role of context, it might overshadow the role of truth in constituting knowledge.**, **In focusing on the contextual standards, the necessary component of truth could be inadvertently downplayed or relegated to secondary status.**, **An obscured emphasis on truth might lead to accepting beliefs as knowledge that are merely contextually justified but not true.**
