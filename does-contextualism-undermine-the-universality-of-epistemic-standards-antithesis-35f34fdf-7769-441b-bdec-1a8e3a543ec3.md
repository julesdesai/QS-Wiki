# Does contextualism undermine the universality of epistemic standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 35f34fdf-7769-441b-bdec-1a8e3a543ec3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge Justification](contextualism-in-knowledge-justification-synthesis-d6d15e12-9924-4e45-93dc-655c9b790af5.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that universality should apply to epistemic standards across different contexts to ensure consistent attribution of knowledge.**, **In a scientific context, high evidential standards are required for a claim to constitute knowledge, such as multiple reproducible experiments.**, **In a casual conversation, much lower evidential standards might be accepted for a claim to constitute knowledge, such as someone’s personal assertion.**, **If the standards for justification vary with context, then it is unclear how any consistent principle about knowledge could hold universally.**
