# Is sufficient evidence always quantifiable?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 22b6f917-588f-4a9c-b26c-e38a6a7649ed
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidentialism](evidentialism-synthesis-fd1a4d8b-5b56-45a2-920f-de86623a4098.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where a historian believes a certain historical event occurred based on numerous but incomplete documentary sources. Intuitively, the historian's belief could be seen as justified despite the difficulty in quantifying the level of evidence sufficiency.**, **The principle that evidence sufficiency might not always be quantifiable presents a challenge to the requirement for evidence sufficiency for justification.**
