# Can belief based solely on probabilistic foundations constitute knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1ec0e44b-cfd9-4426-ba1b-b06eb41c7fa2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Probabilistic Foundationalism](probabilistic-foundationalism-synthesis-4f173a54-3943-4ddb-a2cd-a6bb34479be2.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition based entirely on probabilistically evaluated foundational beliefs, but the proposition turns out to be coincidentally true. Intuitively, this would not amount to knowledge, because the truth is accidental.**, **For knowledge, it is implausible to require only probabilistically evaluated foundational beliefs without any further consideration of how the belief relates to its truth.**
