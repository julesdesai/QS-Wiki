# Is justification always necessary for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f0c1102b-a4f8-4e08-b45f-8887cf56d0b3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Multi-Standard Contextualism](multi-standard-contextualism-synthesis-9865cf66-a535-4334-b5b4-390c78d28080.md)

## Content

**There is a principle that asserts a person needs justification for a belief to constitute knowledge.**, **Suppose we have an instance where someone accurately guesses the outcome of an event without any justification. Intuitively, we would not consider this an instance of knowledge.**
