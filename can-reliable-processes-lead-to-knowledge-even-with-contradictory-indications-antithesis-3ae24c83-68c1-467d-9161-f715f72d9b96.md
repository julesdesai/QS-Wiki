# Can reliable processes lead to knowledge even with contradictory indications?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3ae24c83-68c1-467d-9161-f715f72d9b96
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-a142f10a-5d37-4074-9f91-84a5b31f1b2a.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person uses a reliable thermometer which consistently reads accurately except it is currently in an environment causing it to display two conflicting temperatures. Intuitively, it is unclear if knowledge is possible with contradictory inputs.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should not rely on processes yielding contradictory outputs. This principle suggests that reliability alone cannot resolve scenarios with conflicting evidence.**
