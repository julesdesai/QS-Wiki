# Does intellectual virtue always lead to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 2780d505-c1ef-4c00-91a9-22b002766986
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-cc6efa59-ea17-4bfb-aaf3-bd4d3ed4481f.md)

## Content

**Consider a specific instance where a historian correctly guesses an ancient event timeline due to an error in interpreting data, showing intellectual diligence and broader virtue knowledge. Intuitively, we would judge that the historian does not know the timeline despite correctness.**, **A principle is plausible that true beliefs arrived at from an intellectual virtue when mixed with incorrect data doesn’t ensure knowledge. Thus, intellectual virtue without correct foundational data cannot be a guarantee of knowledge.**
