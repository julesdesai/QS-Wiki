# Is true belief sufficient for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1a7d7ba3-f870-45e2-ba0d-18550e04b75e
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person believes a proposition, and the proposition happens to be true, but the person's belief is based on a guess rather than any foundational belief. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not know the proposition, although the belief is true.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to constitute knowledge, it must be based on more than just mere truth; it requires a trustworthy method or process in forming the belief.**
