# Do all justified true beliefs have the potential to be defeasible?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 3d64408c-8036-48c6-a47d-1bd33c758910
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Suppose a scenario where each justified true belief can potentially be undermined by new information not presently available. Intuitively, this suggests that the concept of a defeater is inherently applicable to all beliefs.**, **If all justified true beliefs are potentially defeasible, implementing the internalist requirement that knowledge must be free from defeaters could lead to skepticism.**, **Knowledge, if it must be indefeasible, could be nearly impossible to achieve if defeaters are always possible.**
