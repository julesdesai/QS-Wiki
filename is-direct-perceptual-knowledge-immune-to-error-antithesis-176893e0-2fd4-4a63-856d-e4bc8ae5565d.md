# Is direct perceptual knowledge immune to error?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 176893e0-2fd4-4a63-856d-e4bc8ae5565d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivism Summary](disjunctivism-summary-synthesis-e9744a1e-a377-4989-9c30-b08459f11a98.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person sees an object clearly and has what is considered a veridical experience, yet due to a rare optical illusion, the object is not as it seems, challenging their perceived knowledge. Intuitively, we might judge this instance as one lacking true knowledge.**, **The fallibility of direct perceptual experiences shows that they might not always guarantee knowledge aligned with reality.**
