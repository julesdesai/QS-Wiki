# Does indirect realism permit non-human animals to have knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 39c61892-54dc-4049-af34-bd8ddefd1d91
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Indirect Realism Summary](indirect-realism-summary-synthesis-8a578877-1ef2-4cd7-a175-3a668ae7b8a0.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a dog recognizes its owner without apparent complex cognitive processing or representational modeling. Intuitively, we would say the dog knows its owner.**, **It is implausible to claim that all beings require cognitive intermediaries to have knowledge. This raises questions about the accessibility of knowledge to non-human animals who may not process information in the same way humans do.**
