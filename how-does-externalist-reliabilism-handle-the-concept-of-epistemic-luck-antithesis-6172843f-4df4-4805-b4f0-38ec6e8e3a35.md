# How does externalist reliabilism handle the concept of epistemic luck?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6172843f-4df4-4805-b4f0-38ec6e8e3a35
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person forms a belief based on a reliable process and the belief turns out to be true purely by chance, such as just happening to guess correctly. Intuitively, we might be inclined to say that while the belief is true, it is not knowledge due to the element of luck involved.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge should exclude elements of luck, suggesting that a reliable process alone is insufficient for knowledge.**
