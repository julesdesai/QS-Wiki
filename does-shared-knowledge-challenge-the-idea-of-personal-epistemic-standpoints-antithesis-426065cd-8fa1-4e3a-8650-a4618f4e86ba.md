# Does shared knowledge challenge the idea of personal epistemic standpoints?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 426065cd-8fa1-4e3a-8650-a4618f4e86ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Relativism Summary](relativism-summary-synthesis-2e206e35-286d-4605-af4c-93a45cafcac5.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where two individuals both claim to know the same proposition but based on radically different epistemic standpoints. Intuitively, it seems impossible for both to possess true knowledge if their justifications are entirely subjective and contradictory.**, **Shared propositional knowledge implies there must be some common epistemic standards to validate its truth across different perspectives.**, **If epistemic standards vary completely by individual, then it becomes implausible to explain how any shared or communal knowledge is possible.**
