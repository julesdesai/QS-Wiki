# Is perception always reliable for knowing propositions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 128567db-fc94-441f-bb47-d249c7f22026
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Direct Realism Summary](direct-realism-summary-synthesis-455e7e73-d4a0-4c39-a9a4-a91c64bb25a3.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an eyewitness perceives a suspect fleeing from a crime scene, but due to poor lighting, misidentifies an innocent person. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that perception led to a false proposition about the suspect's identity.**, **There is a plausible principle that perception should be reliable for it to justify a proposition about reality. This principle together with instances of misperception entails that perception is not always reliable for knowing propositions.**
