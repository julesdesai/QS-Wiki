# Does Contextual Foundationalism account for the fallibility of foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1d1ab9a4-9bb0-41b8-af1c-325338bb70dc
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextual Foundationalism](contextual-foundationalism-synthesis-3cd555af-8ec3-406f-b70a-64f89852bc49.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which foundational beliefs are contextually secure yet still incorrect due to new evidence. Intuitively, we either have to say that foundational beliefs are infallible or accept that they can be mistaken.**, **It is implausible that foundational beliefs can never be subject to error. This creates tension with the idea of knowledge always being based on these beliefs.**
