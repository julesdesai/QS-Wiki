# Knowledge as Virtue-Driven True Belief

**Parent:** [Can a belief that is safe from error nonetheless fail to constitute knowledge if formed through unreliable processes?](can-a-belief-that-is-safe-from-error-nonetheless-fail-to-constitute-knowledge-if-formed-through-unreliable-processes-antithesis-959274bc-c0f4-4480-87d8-606908828828.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the belief is formed through intellectual virtues**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, the proposition is true, and the belief is formed through intellectual virtues**
