# Is it possible to identify non-arbitrary infinite reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 55c176e9-c014-4fd0-a47f-92ed00be3d5d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with Non-arbitrary Reasons](infinitism-with-non-arbitrary-reasons-synthesis-11967ecc-f2b8-44cb-a381-b7e79afac297.md)

## Content

**A reason is arbitrary if it lacks a justified basis or relevance to the proposition it supports.**, **An infinite chain of non-arbitrary reasons would require endless relevant and justified connections.**, **Identifying non-arbitrary reasons infinitely is practically impossible due to the inherent ambiguity in determining relevance.**
