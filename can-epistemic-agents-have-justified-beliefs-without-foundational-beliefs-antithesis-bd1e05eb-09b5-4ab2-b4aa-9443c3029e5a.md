# Can epistemic agents have justified beliefs without foundational beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis

**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology as a Modified Foundationalism](virtue-epistemology-as-a-modified-foundationalism-synthesis-d4605a61-d4b7-4ba0-a676-f0e4515228ee.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where an agent holds justified beliefs derived from previously held foundational beliefs. In such a scenario, intuitively we would judge that foundational beliefs were initially instrumental in justification.**, **There is a plausible principle that current justified beliefs often rest on past foundational beliefs. This principle together with the role of foundational beliefs in historical justification entails that foundational beliefs are integral to the justification process.**
