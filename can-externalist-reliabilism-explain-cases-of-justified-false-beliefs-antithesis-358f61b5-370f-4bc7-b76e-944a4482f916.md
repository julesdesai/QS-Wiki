# Can externalist reliabilism explain cases of justified false beliefs?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 358f61b5-370f-4bc7-b76e-944a4482f916
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalist Reliabilism](externalist-reliabilism-synthesis-a51e876d-808b-45d8-97dd-3bdec299c925.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case where a person forms a belief using a reliable process that, due to atypical circumstances, produces a false belief. Intuitively, this belief might still be considered justified despite being false.**, **There is a plausible principle that justification can be present even if the belief is false, posing a challenge to the alignment of reliability strictly with true beliefs.**
