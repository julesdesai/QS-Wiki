# Does the reliance on safety condition ignore internal factors of knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** f57ab80f-07b2-450e-b120-a1d9ba7321c8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Safety Condition Externalism Summary](safety-condition-externalism-summary-synthesis-29bd2257-ee31-4256-8506-f8628368ab8d.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who believes a true proposition but is unaware of the conditions under which their belief would be false. Intuitively, it seems this person lacks knowledge because their understanding is not well-grounded.**, **The notion that safety plays a crucial role in justification seemingly overlooks the importance of a person's understanding or awareness of potential false conditions.**, **A plausible principle is that knowing involves some internal awareness or understanding, which seems absent if only the safety of the belief is considered.**
