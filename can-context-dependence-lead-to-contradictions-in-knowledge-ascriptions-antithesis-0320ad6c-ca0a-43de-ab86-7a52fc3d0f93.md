# Can context-dependence lead to contradictions in knowledge ascriptions?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 0320ad6c-ca0a-43de-ab86-7a52fc3d0f93
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Variable Justification](contextualism-with-variable-justification-synthesis-53c5a1dc-1391-4651-950b-f21901e771b6.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which a person asserts they know a fact in a relaxed context and another claims they do not know it in a strict context. Intuitively, either both should be correct, or one must be wrong, which suggests a contradiction.**, **It is implausible that both conflicting knowledge claims about the same proposition could be true simultaneously.**
