# How do hallucinations challenge the reliability of shared perceptual accounts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 4309fc98-b7f2-4d67-887d-cf7fd219912b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Phenomenalism with Reliance on Testimony](phenomenalism-with-reliance-on-testimony-synthesis-6d144448-4d6b-4388-8669-d0f45e936182.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a person P frequently experiences vivid hallucinations that they mistake for reality and report as testimony. Intuitively, we would judge that P's testimony, based on hallucinations, cannot contribute to accurate perception-based knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that if hallucinations are indistinguishable from real perceptions to the observer, then testimony influenced by them cannot reliably support shared perceptual accounts.**
