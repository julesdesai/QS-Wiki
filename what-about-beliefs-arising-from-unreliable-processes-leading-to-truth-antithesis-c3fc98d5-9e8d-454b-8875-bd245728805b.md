# What about beliefs arising from unreliable processes leading to truth?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c3fc98d5-9e8d-454b-8875-bd245728805b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-7601e066-11d5-4f0c-8c42-44a4cc1f650d.md)

## Content

**A person could use an unreliable cognitive faculty, such as a faulty memory, to form a belief that ends up being true. Intuitively, this true belief is not knowledge.**, **It is possible for a belief formed by unreliable cognitive faculties to be true, but such beliefs are not considered knowledge.**
