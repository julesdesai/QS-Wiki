# Is sensitivity to error a necessary condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 264ce125-a88f-4acd-a6c0-2309c7532af2
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Externalism](externalism-synthesis-9fb6627e-b63e-4d81-a81a-128eb1da4172.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief held is true in the actual world, but in a close possible world, the belief is false due to slightly changing circumstances. Intuitively, we would judge that possessing knowledge requires sensitivity to errors that could occur in close possible worlds.**, **A belief process that justifies knowledge without sensitivity to possible errors fails to secure knowledge of the condition being true.**
