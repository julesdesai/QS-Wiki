# Can knowledge exist without a causal connection?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8b16001d-513b-4d43-88d3-a35808f258ad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Causal Theory of Knowledge](causal-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-83958a5b-d735-4d00-9b23-ae4f36a61779.md)

## Content

**There are cases where a person seems to have knowledge without a clear causal connection, such as a person correctly guessing the outcome of a random event.**, **In situations where a person dreams about an event and it coincidentally occurs, it seems they have some form of awareness despite no causal link.**, **Intuitively, we sometimes attribute knowledge in cases of lucky guesses, which lack a causal process linking belief to truth.**
