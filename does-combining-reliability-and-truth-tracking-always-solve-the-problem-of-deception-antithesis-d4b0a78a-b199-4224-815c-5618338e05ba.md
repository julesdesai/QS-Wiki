# Does combining reliability and truth-tracking always solve the problem of deception?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d4b0a78a-b199-4224-815c-5618338e05ba
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Process Reliabilism with Nozick's Condition](process-reliabilism-with-nozicks-condition-synthesis-5d350bc0-f5e6-4b33-b9c5-feeee7d84d21.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which a belief is formed reliably and truth-tracks, yet is based on deceptive evidence tailored to mimic true evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that knowledge is absent.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge cannot be founded on deceit, even with reliability and truth-tracking. This principle together with the scenario suggests an insufficiency in the view.**
