# Can knowledge be considered objective if perceptions vary?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d2831213-f6d9-4fec-8e78-6e545140ba63
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Critical Realism](critical-realism-synthesis-aa743ec0-e3e4-4aae-9408-fa20370276b8.md)

## Content

**Consider a situation where cultural background affects one's interpretation of a physical object, such as seeing red as a sign of danger. Intuitively, such interpretations indicate variability in perception based on cultural contexts.**, **Cultural variations in perception suggest that what is perceived can be subjective, challenging the objectivity of knowledge about causal structures.**
