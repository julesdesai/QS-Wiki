# How does tracking theory address the closure principle in epistemology?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 081d7b58-e7b5-494f-9c58-4fcd27069d3d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory](tracking-theory-synthesis-c73695dc-3706-4b4d-aea8-5e0696c46f82.md)

## Content

**The closure principle suggests if someone knows a proposition and knows that it entails another proposition, the second must be known as well, challenging tracking as it would require belief tracking across all entailments and worlds**, **A person might know a proposition but fail to pervasively track all related necessary propositions, thereby questioning knowledge constituted by tracking**, **Instance of knowing one fact and just assuming another due to entailment without proper belief tracking highlights a gap in the principle's application**
