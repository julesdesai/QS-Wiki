# Is it possible for belief and truth to coexist without culminating in knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 966ddc63-3bde-410b-a6f9-a2593c8b40b7
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Revised Foundationalism with Fallible Foundations](revised-foundationalism-with-fallible-foundations-synthesis-eea8b3fa-1186-4e04-a7da-360421394f6f.md)

## Content

**Consider a case in which a person believes a proposition and the proposition is true, yet the belief is supported by an unreliable cognitive process known for yielding both true and false beliefs historically. Intuitively, such a belief, although true, would not be regarded as knowledge.**, **There is a plausible principle that a justified belief must be supported by a process or method that ensures reliability beyond marginal fallibility to count as knowledge.**
