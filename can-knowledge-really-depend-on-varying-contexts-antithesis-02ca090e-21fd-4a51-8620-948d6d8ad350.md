# Can knowledge really depend on varying contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 02ca090e-21fd-4a51-8620-948d6d8ad350
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-876f0403-5dcc-4837-9485-46808d567bde.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge should have a stable and consistent nature regardless of situational context.**, **Suppose two individuals are in different contexts but have access to the same evidence and conditions. Intuitively, it seems implausible that one knows a proposition and the other does not based solely on contextual differences.**
