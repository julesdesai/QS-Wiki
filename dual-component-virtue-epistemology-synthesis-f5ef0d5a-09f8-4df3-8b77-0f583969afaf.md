# Dual-Component Virtue Epistemology

**Node Type:** synthesis
**Node ID:** f5ef0d5a-09f8-4df3-8b77-0f583969afaf
**Depth:** 5
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Should intellectual virtue always lead to truth?](should-intellectual-virtue-always-lead-to-truth-antithesis-2612182e-b45f-40cb-bba3-bebcd4434974.md)

## Content

**For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the belief to result from an intellectual virtue**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the belief process to reliably lead to true beliefs**, **For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, the proposition is true, the belief results from an intellectual virtue, and the belief process reliably leads to true beliefs**
