**Suppose we have a case in which scientific communities maintain stringent knowledge standards; intuitively, these standards seem not to shift easily across contexts.**, **Consider a scenario in which scientific discourse relies on non-contextual standards of evidence and proof; intuitively, this suggests contextualism struggles to account for such knowledge practices.**, **It is implausible that context can accommodate the fixed epistemic demands of scientific inquiry without loss of rigor.**
