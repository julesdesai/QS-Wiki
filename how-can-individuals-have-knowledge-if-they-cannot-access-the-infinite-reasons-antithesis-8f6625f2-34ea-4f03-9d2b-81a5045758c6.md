# How can individuals have knowledge if they cannot access the infinite reasons?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8f6625f2-34ea-4f03-9d2b-81a5045758c6
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Infinitism with An Emphasis on the Chain of Justifications](infinitism-with-an-emphasis-on-the-chain-of-justifications-synthesis-fbcbcc46-9003-42db-8878-9fc62d8799d4.md)

## Content

**A plausible principle is that knowledge requires justification that an individual can access. This principle, in conjunction with inaccessible infinite chains, implies that knowledge is elusive for practical purposes.**, **Infinite chains of reasons are practically unreachable for individuals due to cognitive limitations. This limits the usability of knowledge if justifications can't be realistically accessed.**
