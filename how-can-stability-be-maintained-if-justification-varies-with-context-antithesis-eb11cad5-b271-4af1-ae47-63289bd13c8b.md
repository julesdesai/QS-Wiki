# How can stability be maintained if justification varies with context?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** eb11cad5-b271-4af1-ae47-63289bd13c8b
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualist Approach to Knowledge](contextualist-approach-to-knowledge-synthesis-2e0c1d55-19f7-4e12-a786-a9e840e9798b.md)

## Content

**A case where a legal advisor asserts knowledge of a law's interpretation based on courtroom standards but in a casual setting outside the court, the justification standard differs, leading to a potential reassessment of the knowledge claim. The knowledge stability based on justification is compromised. **, **It is implausible that a knowledge framework that allows variations in justification based on context can maintain stable and reliable knowledge claims. Stability of knowledge demands consistent justification standards.**
