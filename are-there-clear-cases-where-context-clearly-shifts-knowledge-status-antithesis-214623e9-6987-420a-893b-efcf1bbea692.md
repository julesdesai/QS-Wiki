# Are there clear cases where context clearly shifts knowledge status?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 214623e9-6987-420a-893b-efcf1bbea692
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Reliabilism](contextualism-with-reliabilism-synthesis-c7c3d2a5-9a25-4e3f-9e1f-65010441e024.md)

## Content

**Suppose a person is in a low-stakes environment where they claim to know a trivia fact by memory. Intuitively, we say they know it. In a high-stakes environment, if one relies on the same memory, we might say they do not know it.**, **The intuition that the context can shift knowledge status in such a way is not uniform or universally accepted, pointing to potential weaknesses in contextualism.**
