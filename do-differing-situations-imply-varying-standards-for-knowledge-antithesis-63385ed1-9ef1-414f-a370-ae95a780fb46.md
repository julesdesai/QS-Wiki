# Do differing situations imply varying standards for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 63385ed1-9ef1-414f-a370-ae95a780fb46
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Subject-Sensitive Invariantism](subject-sensitive-invariantism-synthesis-ef027452-1300-45f1-9d7c-023fdb6cd119.md)

## Content

**Suppose Alice and Bob are both asked if they know how to open a particular lock. Alice needs to open the lock to save someone, while Bob needs to open it casually. Intuitively, Alice's higher stakes situation demands higher certainty, challenging the idea that the standards for knowing remain constant.**
