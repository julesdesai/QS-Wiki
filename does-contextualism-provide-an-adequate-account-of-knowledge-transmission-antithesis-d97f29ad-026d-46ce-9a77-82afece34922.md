# Does contextualism provide an adequate account of knowledge transmission?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** d97f29ad-026d-46ce-9a77-82afece34922
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Knowledge](contextualism-in-knowledge-synthesis-faf8f039-bcfa-455d-bf03-63fe321db5bc.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which an expert imparts a justified true belief to a non-expert in a different context. Intuitively, we would judge that the non-expert should gain knowledge from the expert's testimony.**, **Under contextual standards, variance in context might imply that what is knowledge for the expert is not easily transferrable as knowledge to the non-expert.**
