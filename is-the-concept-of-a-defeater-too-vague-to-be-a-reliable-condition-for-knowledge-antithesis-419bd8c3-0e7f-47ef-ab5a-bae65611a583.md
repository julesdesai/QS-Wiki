# Is the concept of a defeater too vague to be a reliable condition for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 419bd8c3-0e7f-47ef-ab5a-bae65611a583
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Internalist Defeasibility View](internalist-defeasibility-view-synthesis-fd143e9a-1c88-44de-a8f1-4ab4ab5bc60e.md)

## Content

**Consider a case where two people disagree on what constitutes a defeater, with one person considering certain information undermining and another not. Intuitively, the subjective nature of what counts as a defeater can complicate its role in defining knowledge.**, **If people differ on what constitutes a defeater, the concept may be too vague to reliably define when knowledge is present.**, **A clear and consistent criterion for what counts as a defeater might be necessary for defeaters to restrict knowledge, but no such criterion seems readily available.**
