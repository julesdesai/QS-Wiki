# Do practical decision-making needs lead to contradictory standards?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c476e43e-8668-41da-9e1e-6935dbe06ed8
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Moderate Contextualism](moderate-contextualism-synthesis-4577cf68-8dec-49df-bf7d-5b1d9d39d213.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two decision-makers apply different standards for knowing because of differing stakes involved in their decision-making processes. Intuitively, we might think that this results in contradictory knowledge claims about the same proposition.**, **There is a plausible principle that the standards to assess knowledge should lead to consistent judgments about whether one knows a proposition. This principle, together with differing standards due to practical needs, implies the possibility of contradictions in knowledge claims.**
