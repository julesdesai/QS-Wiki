# Can Reliabilism explain why true beliefs formed by unreliable processes seem accidental?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 79fa44db-ab66-4c96-aba0-a75b96217407
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Reliabilism](reliabilism-synthesis-24b421a0-08f3-4c66-b0fb-2acc7f5da514.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where someone forms a true belief by using an unreliable cognitive process, such as flipping a coin to decide a fact. Intuitively, this belief seems to lack knowledge because its truth seems accidental.**, **There is a plausible principle that knowledge is incompatible with beliefs that are true merely by chance, even if the chance process could be described as contextually reliable. This principle indicates a challenge to defining reliable processes without accounting for incidental correctness.**
