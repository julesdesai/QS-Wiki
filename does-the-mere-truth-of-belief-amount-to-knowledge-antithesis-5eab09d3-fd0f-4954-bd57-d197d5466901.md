# Does the mere truth of belief amount to knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 5eab09d3-fd0f-4954-bd57-d197d5466901
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Disjunctivist Externalism Summary](disjunctivist-externalism-summary-synthesis-838ba4d9-493f-4109-9c69-34614a236108.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that a belief could constitute knowledge merely by being true without any justification or reliable belief formation process.**, **Consider a scenario where someone believes a true statement based purely on hearsay without any verification or evidence. Intuitively, we would judge that the person does not actually know the statement just because it happens to be true.**, **There is a plausible principle that for a belief to be knowledge, it must be supported by more than mere truth, such as some form of justification or reliability in its formation.**
