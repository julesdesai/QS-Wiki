# Can justification exist without strong evidence?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b1f549be-2e0e-4d6d-83cb-426390d66290
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Pragmatic Justification Theory](pragmatic-justification-theory-synthesis-c04257c0-23ff-4055-ad22-e7f5ba02d666.md)

## Content

**There is a plausible principle that knowledge requires not only true belief but also strong justification supported by evidence. This principle, together with the notion of justification involving practical success, suggests that knowledge might be attributed in cases where real evidence is lacking or insufficient, which seems counterintuitive.**, **Consider a scenario in which a gambler believes they will win based on a lucky streak rather than statistical odds. Intuitively, this belief lacks proper evidence-based justification even if the gambler wins, questioning whether practical success alone provides justification.**
