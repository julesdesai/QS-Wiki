# Can emotions serve as evidence for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1898bd8c-eafa-49b8-83d5-cb2404398e2a
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Grounding Knowledge in Evidence](grounding-knowledge-in-evidence-synthesis-000c43a5-6d20-4fdc-9093-7ec5ca2a4c8d.md)

## Content

**It is implausible that emotions alone constitute sufficient evidence for knowledge.**, **Consider a case where a person feels strongly that a friend is upset but lacks any concrete evidence. In such a case, intuitively we would judge that emotions do not provide a strong enough evidential basis for knowledge.**
