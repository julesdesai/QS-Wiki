# Can practical conditions alter the truth-value of knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b6936dcc-fbea-4ddb-805d-accaa16703f3
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism with Sensitivity to Practical Conditions](contextualism-with-sensitivity-to-practical-conditions-synthesis-e44527bc-e319-461c-896c-272879d9a91c.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which two people are inquiring separately about the time: one is a casual inquiry at home, and the other is a high-stakes inquiry before catching a flight. Intuitively, the high-stakes context may demand more stringent justification than the low-stakes context, raising the question of whether practical conditions should alter the truth-value of knowledge claims.**, **If knowledge depends on the context of inquiry, a situation where one has different epistemic standards for the same proposition in different contexts seems to imply relative rather than objective truth, challenging the absolute nature of truth in knowledge claims.**
