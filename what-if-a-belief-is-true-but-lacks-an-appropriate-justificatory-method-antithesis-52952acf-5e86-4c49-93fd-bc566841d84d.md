# What if a belief is true but lacks an appropriate justificatory method?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 52952acf-5e86-4c49-93fd-bc566841d84d
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism in Epistemology](contextualism-in-epistemology-synthesis-8d92b68a-d96e-49d3-9b29-39a25430afac.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario where a person comes to believe a true proposition through unreliable methods, like a horoscope. Intuitively, we would judge that this does not count as knowledge.**, **A belief being true is not sufficient for knowledge if the means of forming the belief are irrational or unreliable, even if coincidentally correct.**
