# Does possessing intellectual virtues suffice for knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** e4334122-a525-4bae-b5e6-0bb392c61b88
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-synthesis-39ebccb5-104c-44e1-841f-55d82b6e37fc.md)

## Content

**Suppose we have a case in which an individual has developed keen intellectual virtues over time through study and practice. Even with these virtues, they sometimes encounter highly deceptive evidence that leads them to false beliefs. Intuitively, we might say possessing virtues alone is not sufficient for knowledge if the resulting belief is false.**, **Consider a scenario in which a detective with brilliant deductive skills believes a suspect is guilty based on misleading clues. Despite their virtue in logical reasoning, they have arrived at a false conclusion, highlighting that intellectual virtues might not be sufficient for knowledge.**
