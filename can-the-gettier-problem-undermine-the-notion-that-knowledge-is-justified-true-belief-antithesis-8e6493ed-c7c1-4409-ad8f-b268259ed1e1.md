# Can the Gettier problem undermine the notion that knowledge is justified true belief?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 8e6493ed-c7c1-4409-ad8f-b268259ed1e1
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Foundationalism as a basis for justification](foundationalism-as-a-basis-for-justification-synthesis-96d7dd5d-5a4f-4ab7-a168-41aac5038abd.md)

## Content

**Consider a scenario in which Smith has strong evidence for the belief that Jones owns a Ford, but unbeknownst to Smith, the evidence is not correct. Coincidentally, Smith forms the true belief that Brown is in Barcelona. Intuitively, we would judge that Smith does not have knowledge of Brown's location.**, **In this scenario, the belief that Brown is in Barcelona is true and justified yet does not constitute knowledge.**, **The principle that knowledge is justified true belief does not hold if there are cases where justified true belief does not equate to knowledge.**
