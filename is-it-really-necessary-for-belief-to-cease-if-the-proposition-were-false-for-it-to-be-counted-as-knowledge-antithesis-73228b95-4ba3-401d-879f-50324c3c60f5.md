# Is it really necessary for belief to cease if the proposition were false for it to be counted as knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 73228b95-4ba3-401d-879f-50324c3c60f5
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Sensitivity Theory of Knowledge](sensitivity-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-102d682a-8eb5-4983-bf10-f287ee1765f9.md)

## Content

**Consider a person who knows their friend is in the living room because they see them, yet if the person wasn't there, they would still believe it due to habit. Intuitively, we would judge that the person knows their friend is in the living room.**, **It is plausible that knowledge depends more on the reliability of people’s previous experiences rather than sensitivity to counterfactuals.**
