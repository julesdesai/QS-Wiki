# Is contextualism able to explain retrospective changes in knowledge status based on past contexts?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** b244a0fe-d0cc-445c-a6f3-8302706b8794
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-c8874149-f3cc-4119-8896-6d48b3a50470.md)

## Content

**Imagine a scientist who believed a theory that was widely accepted as true in the past but is now disproven. Intuitively, it seems questionable whether the scientist "knew" the theory at the time.**, **Knowledge claims might be judged differently when viewing past contexts in light of present knowledge, creating a tension between contextual relativism and temporal constancy of knowledge.**, **Contextualism must address whether knowledge truly changes over time with shifts in context and widely accepted standards.**
