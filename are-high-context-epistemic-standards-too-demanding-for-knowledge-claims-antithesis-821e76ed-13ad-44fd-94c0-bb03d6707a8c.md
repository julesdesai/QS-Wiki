# Are high-context epistemic standards too demanding for knowledge claims?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 821e76ed-13ad-44fd-94c0-bb03d6707a8c
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Contextualism](contextualism-synthesis-bf708b38-e467-425c-8744-5d490abcb982.md)

## Content

**Suppose high-context epistemic standards demand extraordinary evidence for claims as mundane as "I know the earth orbits the sun."**, **These demands often exceed what is attainable or expected in everyday discourse or scientific consensus.**, **If contextualism requires meeting excessively high standards, this renders most common knowledge claims unjustifiably unattainable.**
