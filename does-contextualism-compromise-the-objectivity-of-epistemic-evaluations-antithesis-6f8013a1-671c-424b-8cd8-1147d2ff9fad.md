# Does contextualism compromise the objectivity of epistemic evaluations?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 6f8013a1-671c-424b-8cd8-1147d2ff9fad
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Evidential Contextualism](evidential-contextualism-synthesis-b6d3d34f-f424-4888-9297-84b78c2655db.md)

## Content

**If knowledge is context-dependent, then what is taken to be known is partly determined by subjective or local factors unique to specific situations.**, **These factors can lead to knowledge claims that are not objective, as they rely on contextual parameters rather than universal criteria.**, **This context dependency challenges the object's neutrality, central to many traditional epistemic evaluations.**
