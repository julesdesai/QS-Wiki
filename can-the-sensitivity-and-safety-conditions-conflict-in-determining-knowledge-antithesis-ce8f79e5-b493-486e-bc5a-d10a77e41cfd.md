# Can the sensitivity and safety conditions conflict in determining knowledge?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** ce8f79e5-b493-486e-bc5a-d10a77e41cfd
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Tracking Theory of Knowledge](tracking-theory-of-knowledge-synthesis-34b45a3c-9960-48d7-bb61-82e72864432f.md)

## Content

**A belief can be sensitive, where it wouldn’t be held if false, but it might not be safe, where it could easily be false in a nearby world.**, **In certain cases, a belief might satisfy sensitivity but fail the safety condition because, in slightly altered circumstances, it could easily have been false. Intuitively, this undermines the belief's status as knowledge even if it tracks the truth.**
