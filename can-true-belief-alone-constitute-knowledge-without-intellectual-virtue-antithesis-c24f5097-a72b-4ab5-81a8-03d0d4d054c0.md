# Can true belief alone constitute knowledge without intellectual virtue?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** c24f5097-a72b-4ab5-81a8-03d0d4d054c0
**Depth:** 2
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Virtue Epistemology](virtue-epistemology-thesis-bdb11636-09c0-49d0-87da-f8f93d023ea2.md)

## Content

**A case in which a person holds a belief that is true but arises purely from luck does not demonstrate intellectual virtue.**, **It is implausible to say that knowledge can be constituted merely by a true belief without the exercise of intellectual virtue.**
