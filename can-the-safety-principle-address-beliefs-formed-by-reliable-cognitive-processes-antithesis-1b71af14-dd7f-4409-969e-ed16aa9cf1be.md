# Can the Safety Principle address beliefs formed by reliable cognitive processes?

**Node Type:** antithesis
**Node ID:** 1b71af14-dd7f-4409-969e-ed16aa9cf1be
**Depth:** 4
**Terminal:** No

**Parent:** [Knowledge with Safety Principle](knowledge-with-safety-principle-synthesis-36cb0228-2d5a-4aa3-87ba-01c1622dfdc5.md)

## Content

**Imagine a reliable clairvoyant who has an unshakeable true belief that a distant city is experiencing rain without justification. Intuitively, one might argue that they do not truly know this fact.**, **The belief seems to track the truth across possible situations given the clairvoyant's reliability, without traditional justification.**, **In nearby possible worlds, the belief could not easily have been false if the clairvoyant's powers remain consistent.**
